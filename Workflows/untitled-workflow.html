What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of manually sharing content from your Tiny tiny RSS feed to Mastodon. Many content curators and publishers maintain RSS feeds but struggle to consistently cross-post updates to their Mastodon audience. This workflow eliminates that manual process by automatically detecting new items in your RSS feed and posting them as toots.

The system checks your feed every 10 minutes, ensuring timely content sharing while preventing duplicate posts. It handles all the technical details of parsing the RSS feed, formatting the Mastodon post, and managing the API connection - letting you focus on creating great content rather than distribution logistics.

How It Works

1. RSS Feed Monitoring

The workflow begins by connecting to your Tiny tiny RSS public feed URL. It checks for new items at regular 10-minute intervals, using the feed's publication dates and unique identifiers to detect updates.

2. Content Processing

Each new RSS item is processed to extract the title, description, link, and other relevant metadata. The workflow can be customized to include specific elements or reformat the content for Mastodon's character limits.

3. Mastodon API Connection

Using your Mastodon instance URL and access token, the workflow authenticates with the Mastodon API. It prepares the post content according to your preferred format, including optional hashtags or content warnings.

4. Post Creation & Deduplication

Before posting, the system checks against previously shared items to prevent duplicates. Only verified new content gets posted to your Mastodon account, with success/failure notifications available.
