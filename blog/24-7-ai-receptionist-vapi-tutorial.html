5-Minute Vapi Setup (Step-by-Step)

Creating your first AI receptionist in Vapi requires no technical skills. Here's the exact process shown in the video tutorial (timestamp 2:15):

Step 1: Create Your Assistant

After signing up at vapi.ai, navigate to the Assistants tab and click "Create New." Name your assistant (e.g., "Lisa") and select a blank template. This gives you full control over the configuration.

Step 2: Configure the AI Model

Vapi offers multiple AI models with different price/performance tradeoffs. For most receptionist use cases, GPT-4.1 Nano provides the best balance at 9 cents per minute with 960ms latency.

Step 3: Set the First Message

This is what callers hear when they connect. A professional greeting like "Hello, this is Lisa from [Your Business]. How can I help you?" establishes credibility immediately.

Step 4: Craft the System Prompt

The system prompt defines your assistant's personality, capabilities, and limitations. Include:

  • Company information and services
  • Call handling instructions
  • Compliance requirements
  • Personality traits

Pro Tip: Use Vapi's "First Message Mode" to make your assistant speak first, creating a more natural conversation flow compared to traditional IVR systems.

Connecting to Your Business Phone Number

Vapi offers three ways to connect your AI receptionist to callers (timestamp 12:30):

Option 1: Free Vapi Number (US Only)

For US businesses, Vapi provides free phone numbers with unlimited inbound calls. Simply select a number from their dashboard and assign your assistant.

Option 2: Twilio Integration (Global)

International businesses can connect Vapi to Twilio numbers. You'll need your Twilio Account SID and Auth Token, which takes about 5 minutes to set up.

Option 3: Existing Number Forwarding

Already have a business number? Forward calls to your Vapi assistant while keeping your current provider. This maintains brand consistency while adding AI capabilities.

Important: Enable "End Call Function" in Vapi settings so your assistant can gracefully conclude conversations rather than waiting for the caller to hang up.

Advanced CRM Integration with n8n

The real power comes when connecting your AI receptionist to business tools (timestamp 18:45). Using n8n or Make.com, you can:

  • Pull caller data from your CRM to personalize conversations
  • Log call details and transcripts automatically
  • Schedule follow-ups and appointments
  • Trigger workflows based on call outcomes

The tutorial shows a complete n8n workflow that:

  1. Receives incoming call webhook from Vapi
  2. Checks the caller's number against your CRM
  3. Dynamically updates the AI's knowledge with relevant customer data
  4. Personalizes the greeting using the caller's name
  5. Logs the call details back to your CRM

Transient Assistants: This advanced approach creates a new AI instance for each call, pre-loaded with that caller's history. It's like having a receptionist who remembers every past interaction.

Static vs. Transient Assistants: Which is Right For You?

Vapi offers two architectural approaches for AI receptionists (timestamp 25:10):

Static Assistants

Best for: Basic call answering, FAQs, and simple routing

Pros: Simple setup, lower cost, works immediately

Cons: No personalization or CRM integration

Transient Assistants

Best for: Businesses with existing CRMs that want personalized interactions

Pros: Remembers callers, accesses business data, handles complex tasks

Cons: Requires n8n/Make.com setup, slightly higher latency

Most businesses start with a static assistant and upgrade to transient once they see the value. The tutorial includes templates for both approaches.
