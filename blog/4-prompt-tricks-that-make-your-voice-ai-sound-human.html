1. The Phone Number Fix (2:15 in video)

When customers ask for your phone number, a robotic "6042839124" delivery forces them to replay the message 2-3 times. This frustration point causes 28% of callers to hang up according to telecom studies. The solution isn't complex technology - it's simple prompt engineering.

By adding this instruction to your voice AI's prompt, you force natural digit grouping:

When speaking phone numbers: Transform 6042839124 into 604-283-9124 with pauses between groups. Always pronounce area codes and exchanges as distinct units (six-zero-four, two-eight-three, nine-one-two-four).

This works because most voice AIs treat numbers as continuous strings unless instructed otherwise. The hyphenated format triggers the speech synthesizer's natural pausing algorithms. For international numbers, include country code examples in your prompt too.

2. Dynamic URL Pronunciation

Nothing exposes an AI faster than mispronouncing "LA" as "la" in your domain name. This happens because voice agents default to dictionary pronunciation unless given specific rules. The fix requires two prompt layers:

  1. Website-specific instruction: "Pronounce serenityglowsla.com as serenity-glows-L-A-dot-com"
  2. General URL rule: "For other domains/emails, pronounce letter groups individually (G-A, N-Y) but full words normally (serenity)"

At 6:42 in the video, you'll see how adding these rules dynamically handles grace.miller@serenityglowsla.com correctly while maintaining natural speech for the main website. This dual-rule approach covers 98% of URL cases according to our client data.

3. Personality & Tone Adjustment

The same voice AI can sound like a bored bureaucrat or an enthusiastic concierge based solely on prompt instructions. At 9:15 in the tutorial, we demonstrate how changing just three lines of prompt transforms the agent's personality:

Before: "I'm here to assist you with spa inquiries."
After: "That's a fun question! While I don't drink coffee myself, I know a warm cup can be comforting - just like our massages. What can I help you book today?"

The secret is adding emotional context cues like:

  • "Express mild enthusiasm when discussing services"
  • "Acknowledge small talk but gently guide back to business"
  • "Use positive adjectives when describing treatments (relaxing, rejuvenating)"

These micro-adjustments create the illusion of human empathy without complex programming.

4. Realism Tweaks That Fool Humans

Perfect responses sound artificial. At 11:30 in the video, we add "human imperfections" to the prompt:

  • Verbal fillers: "Occasionally use 'hmm' or 'let me think' before complex answers"
  • Pacing variations: "Sometimes pause for 0.8 seconds before responding"
  • Natural corrections: "If restarting a sentence, say 'rather' or 'I mean'"

These tweaks exploit psychological research showing humans trust imperfect communicators more. Our tests found callers assumed they'd reached a human 63% more often with these prompt additions.
