1. Lead Capture & Follow-Up System

Every business struggles with following up on leads consistently. You collect contact information through forms, but then what? This simple automation ensures no lead falls through the cracks by automatically acknowledging submissions and adding contacts to your CRM.

The automation triggers when someone submits a form (like Google Forms or Typeform). It then sends an immediate acknowledgment message (email or text) and creates their contact in your CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.). This eliminates manual data entry and ensures prompt follow-up.

Time saved: This automation can save 5-10 hours per month by eliminating manual lead processing. Businesses using this system report a 30% increase in lead response rates.

2. Lead Capture with Slack Notifications

Building on the first automation, this version adds real-time Slack notifications when new leads come in. It also intelligently categorizes leads based on their responses, helping your team prioritize follow-ups.

The workflow checks if the lead is sales-ready (based on form responses). If yes, it creates them as a "Hot Lead" in your system and notifies your sales team via Slack. If not, it adds them as a "Subscriber" for nurturing. This simple categorization helps focus sales efforts where they're most likely to convert.

3. Automated Meeting Summaries

How many hours do you spend reviewing meeting notes or writing summaries? This automation connects Fireflies.ai (meeting transcription) to your CRM, automatically creating summarized notes after each call.

The process starts when Fireflies detects a meeting has ended. Make.com fetches the transcript, uses AI to extract key points, and creates a structured note in your CRM. This ensures consistent documentation without manual work. At the 4:30 mark in the video tutorial, you can see how the AI prompt structures the summary output.

Implementation tip: Start with simple summary templates, then customize the AI instructions to match your business needs. The example in the video focuses on coach-client meetings but can be adapted for any use case.

4. RSS to LinkedIn Automation

Consistent social media posting drives engagement but takes valuable time. This automation monitors your blog's RSS feed (or any content source) and automatically shares new posts to LinkedIn with custom summaries.

When new content appears in the RSS feed, Make.com extracts the key details, generates an engaging summary using AI, and posts it to your LinkedIn profile. This maintains an active social presence without daily manual posting. The video shows how to configure the RSS trigger and customize the AI-generated post.

5. Website Form to CRM Capture

Website contact forms are lead goldmines, but manually transferring data to your CRM wastes time and risks errors. This automation instantly creates or updates CRM contacts whenever someone submits your website form.

The workflow checks if the contact already exists in your CRM. If yes, it updates their record with the new information. If not, it creates a new contact. This ensures your CRM always has the latest information without spreadsheet exports or manual updates.
