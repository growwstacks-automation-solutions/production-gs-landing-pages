Step-by-Step: Recording a Client Meeting

At 1:30 in the video, the narrator demonstrates the complete user flow:

Step 1: Open Agentforce Chat

From the client's account record in the Salesforce mobile app, the banker taps the Agentforce chat window. The system displays a pre-configured prompt ready to receive the meeting summary.

Step 2: Dictate Meeting Notes

Using the phone's microphone button, the banker verbally describes the meeting details, participants, discussion points, and any action items - just as they would debrief a colleague.

Step 3: Send & Automate

After stopping the recording, the banker taps send. Behind the scenes, Agentforce processes the text through a Salesforce Flow that structures the data and creates all necessary records.

Key advantage: The banker captures the meeting details while they're fresh, without switching contexts to manually type notes. This maintains flow state and improves accuracy.

Behind the Scenes: How the Automation Works

At 2:10 in the demo, the narrator explains the technical architecture powering this solution:

The user's voice message is converted to text by the phone's native speech recognition, then passed to a Salesforce Flow. This Flow sends the text to a custom prompt template that performs two critical functions:

  1. Generates a concise meeting summary using natural language processing
  2. Extracts structured data points into a JSON format

The JSON output enables the Flow to map specific values directly to Salesforce fields - meeting purpose, key discussion points, outcomes, and participant details all populate automatically.

Automatic Task Creation From Action Items

The most powerful feature appears at 3:15 in the video. When the prompt detects action items in the meeting text (phrases like "need to", "follow up", or specific deadlines), the Flow automatically:

  • Creates individual Task records
  • Populates due dates based on mentioned timelines
  • Assigns tasks back to the relationship manager
  • Includes relevant context in the task description

In the demo, the banker's mention of "send her information about our managed services packages by tomorrow" generates a task with tomorrow's due date. Similarly, scheduling "a demo of the enterprise management platform for next Thursday" creates a second task with that future date.

This automation ensures no action item gets lost in notes. Tasks appear in the banker's queue immediately with all necessary context, ready for execution.
