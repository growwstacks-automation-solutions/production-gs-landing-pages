The Browser Revolution We've Been Waiting For

For years, browsers have remained essentially the same - glorified document viewers with some added tabs and extensions. While companies like Microsoft and Google are now bolting on AI assistants, Comet represents a fundamental shift: the first browser designed from the ground up as an AI agent.

Created by the team behind Perplexity AI, Comet looks deceptively normal at first glance. But its built-in assistant doesn't just answer questions - it takes actions. During testing, I watched it automatically summarize my emails, compare products across websites, schedule meetings, and even plan an entire trip - all from simple natural language prompts.

The key difference: While Chrome with Gemini can tell you about a product, Comet will research it, compare prices across retailers, and add the best deal to your cart - all without you ever visiting the stores yourself.

How It Planned My Goa Trip in 2 Minutes

The travel planning demonstration (7:30 timestamp) revealed Comet's ability to synthesize information across multiple sources. With dozens of tabs open about Goa travel, I issued one command to summarize, organize and plan.

Comet analyzed all open travel-related tabs, extracted key information, closed irrelevant pages, and generated a clean 2-day itinerary complete with hotel suggestions, dining recommendations and activity planning. This complex cognitive task - which would typically require hours of manual research and note-taking - was completed in under two minutes.

Business application: Imagine applying this to market research, competitive analysis or academic literature reviews - Comet could read and synthesize dozens of sources automatically.

Addressing Security Concerns

With great power comes great responsibility. Granting a browser this level of access to accounts and data naturally raises security questions. Comet employs several safeguards:

  • End-to-end encryption for all sensitive operations
  • Local AI processing when possible (reducing cloud data exposure)
  • Granular permission controls for each connected service
  • No credential storage - requires re-authentication for sensitive actions

While no system is completely risk-free, Comet's architecture demonstrates thoughtful attention to privacy concerns that often plague AI assistants.
