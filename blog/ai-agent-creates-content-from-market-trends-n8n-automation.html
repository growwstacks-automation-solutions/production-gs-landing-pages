Workflow Overview: From Pain Points to Content

The complete system consists of 15 interconnected nodes in n8n that transform market signals into ready-to-create content ideas. The process begins with scheduled triggers and parameter injection, moves through AI-powered market analysis, and culminates in automated task creation and team notifications.

Key components include the market discovery AI agent (which identifies pain points) and the content ideation AI agent (which transforms those insights into platform-specific content ideas). Between them, a public search connector ensures all insights are grounded in real user discussions.

Step-by-Step: Building the AI Content Generator

Step 1: Schedule Market Discovery Trigger

Configure the workflow to run automatically on your preferred schedule (daily, weekly, etc.). This ensures fresh content ideas are generated consistently without manual intervention.

Step 2: Inject Niche and Keyword Parameters

Define your focus areas by injecting specific niche topics and research keywords. These parameters guide the AI's research to ensure relevance to your business.

Step 3: Extract Raw User Pain Points

The first AI agent scans public discussions to identify repeated complaints, questions, and challenges related to your niche. It extracts these verbatim without adding interpretation.

Step 4: Public Search and Social Intelligence

A connector node fetches data from multiple platforms to feed the AI analysis. This ensures insights are based on actual user conversations across the web.

Step 5: OpenAI Market Discovery

The reasoning engine analyzes the raw data to identify patterns and cluster related pain points. This structured output becomes the foundation for content ideation.

The Two AI Agents Powering This System

This workflow leverages two specialized AI agents working in sequence. The market discovery agent (Steps 3-5) is analytical, focused solely on identifying and categorizing pain points. The content ideation agent (Steps 6-8) is creative, transforming those insights into actionable content ideas.

By separating these functions, the system maintains focus at each stage - first understanding the market need, then brainstorming ways to address it through content. This division of labor mimics how high-performing content teams operate, but with AI handling the heavy lifting.
