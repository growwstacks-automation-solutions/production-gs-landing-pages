The Problem With Manual Price Tracking

Most businesses know they should monitor competitor pricing, but few do it consistently. Manual checking involves:

  • Visiting multiple websites daily
  • Recording prices in spreadsheets
  • Spotting patterns across competitors
  • Reacting to changes days after they occur

This process consumes 5-10 hours per week for most ecommerce teams, yet still misses crucial price changes during peak shopping hours or flash sales. The alternative - expensive enterprise price tracking software - often costs thousands per month with complex setups.

84% of retailers admit they react to competitor price changes too slowly, losing an average of 12% in potential revenue during price wars according to retail analytics data.

How the Automation Works

This n8n and Airtop solution creates a complete price tracking system in 5 steps:

  1. Schedule Trigger: Runs the workflow daily (or hourly)
  2. Google Sheets: Pulls competitor URLs from a spreadsheet
  3. Airtop Agent: Visits each site and extracts pricing data
  4. JavaScript: Converts raw data into readable HTML tables
  5. Gmail: Sends formatted price reports to your team

The magic happens in Airtop's AI browser automation, which can navigate complex ecommerce sites, interpret dynamic pricing displays, and extract data exactly like a human would - but at scale and without fatigue.

The workflow begins with n8n's schedule trigger, configured to run at your preferred frequency:

Key configuration points:

The Google Sheets integration then pulls your predefined list of competitor URLs and product pages. This centralized management means you can add or remove tracking targets without touching the automation code.

Configuring Airtop for Price Extraction

Airtop's query page action does the heavy lifting with a simple natural language prompt:

 "Extract prices of up to 30 different products from the web page. Include product name, original price, discount price, and product URL for each item."

The AI handles:

  • Product identification across page sections
  • Price interpretation (including strikethrough originals)
  • URL collection for quick verification
  • Pagination if products span multiple pages

At 3:22 in the tutorial video, you'll see how the raw JSON output contains all product data - perfect for analysis but needing formatting for human consumption.

Formatting Results for Business Use

The provided JavaScript code transforms Airtop's JSON into clean HTML tables with:

  • Sortable columns by price or discount percentage

This step bridges the gap between raw data and actionable insights - your team gets presentation-ready reports without manual reformatting.

Customization Tip: Modify the JavaScript to add your logo, brand colors, or additional analysis like average discount rates across competitors.
