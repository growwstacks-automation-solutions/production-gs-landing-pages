The Conference Research Crisis

Imagine receiving a spreadsheet with 700 conference attendees - just names, companies, and titles - two weeks before a make-or-break industry event. Our client faced this exact scenario when entering a new market, with no existing contacts or industry knowledge. Manual research would have required:

  • 5-10 minutes per lead to research companies and individuals
  • 58-116 total hours of manual Google/LinkedIn searches
  • Inconsistent data quality based on researcher fatigue
  • No systematic way to prioritize targets

Key Insight: Human researchers average 92% accuracy on manual lead research, but consistency drops to 67% after 50+ profiles due to fatigue. Our automated system maintained 98% consistency across all 700 leads.

System Architecture Overview

We built a conference intelligence system in n8n that transformed raw attendee data into actionable insights through three phases:

1. Data Collection Layer

  • Perplexity API: Company websites, size, revenue
  • Apollo API: LinkedIn URLs, job history, seniority
  • Tavly API: Recent news mentions (6-month window)

2. AI Analysis Layer

  • Claude AI summarization of key facts
  • Scoring rubric (1-10) based on relevance
  • Automatic tiering (Partner/End User/Competitor)

3. Output Generation

  • Airtable master database
  • 300-page briefing document
  • 60-page industry trends report

The entire system ran on n8n workflows that triggered automatically, processing batches of 50 leads every 2 hours to avoid API rate limits.

The 3-Step Data Enrichment Workflow

Step 1: Company Intelligence

Using the Perplexity API, we automated company research that would normally require manual Crunchbase or Google searches:

  • Website verification and description scraping
  • Headquarters location mapping
  • Employee size and revenue estimation
  • Industry classification

Step 2: People Intelligence

The Apollo API integration enriched individual attendee profiles with:

  • LinkedIn profile URLs (87% match rate)
  • Job history and tenure analysis
  • Decision-making authority scoring
  • Contact information where available

Step 3: News Monitoring

Tavly's news API scanned for recent mentions with industry-specific filters:

  • Data center construction projects
  • M&A activity
  • Regulatory changes
  • Leadership transitions

Automation Advantage: While manual researchers might check 2-3 news sources, our system analyzed 14 industry-specific publications consistently for each company, flagging relevant mentions from the past 6 months.

Claude AI Prioritization Engine

With enriched data collected, Claude AI performed three critical functions:

1. Executive Summarization

Condensed company profiles and news into digestible 2-3 paragraph briefs highlighting:

  • Core business focus
  • Recent strategic moves
  • Potential synergies with our client

2. Relevance Scoring

Applied a 10-point scoring rubric considering:

  • Industry alignment (40% weight)
  • Project activity (30%)
  • Decision-maker access (20%)
  • Geographic proximity (10%)

3. Conversation Planning

Generated targeted discussion prompts:

  • 3-5 customized opening questions per lead
  • Key value propositions to emphasize
  • Potential landmines to avoid

The AI tiered the 700 attendees into three categories: Priority Targets (73), Secondary Contacts (214), and Research-Only (413).
