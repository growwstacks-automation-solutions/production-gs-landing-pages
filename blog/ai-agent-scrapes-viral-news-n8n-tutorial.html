Complete Workflow Overview

The end-to-end process mirrors what content teams do manually, but with perfect consistency:

  1. Scrape source site (Hacker News in the demo)
  2. Extract metadata: Titles, URLs, and engagement metrics
  3. Filter for virality: Only articles meeting your threshold
  4. Scrape full content: Pull article text from source URLs
  5. AI summarization: GPT generates concise briefs
  6. Email composition: Professional HTML formatting
  7. Distribution: Send to your list or CRM

Scraping Technique: CSS Selectors Explained

The magic happens in the HTML Extract node where we identify specific CSS selectors for each data point. As shown at 12:45, inspecting element reveals:

  • span.titleline > a for article titles
  • href attribute for direct URLs
  • span.score for engagement points

The tutorial demonstrates how to test selectors live, with the crucial step being the transformation of "138 points" into a pure number for filtering. This pattern works across most modern websites with consistent DOM structures.

AI Summarization Process

Raw scraped content gets messy fast. At 18:20, you'll see the critical HTML-to-markdown conversion that creates clean text for AI processing. The OpenAI node then receives this prompt:

This produces executive-style briefs rather than generic summaries. For legal/financial niches, you'd modify the prompt to emphasize compliance implications or market impacts.
