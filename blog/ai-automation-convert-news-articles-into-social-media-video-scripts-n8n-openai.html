The Daily Content Crisis Every Business Faces

Most businesses know they should post daily on social media - but between running operations and serving clients, content creation often falls to the bottom of the priority list. The result? Either inconsistent posting or hours wasted manually rewriting news articles.

This workflow solves both problems by automating the entire content pipeline - from monitoring industry news sources to generating video-ready scripts in your brand voice.

Content creation consumes 15+ hours weekly for the average small business owner who manually researches and rewrites articles. This automation reclaims that time while improving consistency.

How This Automated Content Workflow Solves It

The system monitors RSS feeds from your chosen news sources, extracts the key points using OpenAI, formats them into social-ready scripts, and posts them to Notion for scheduling - all with human approval checkpoints.

At 1:15 in the video, you'll see the complete workflow diagram showing how these components connect:

  2. AI summarization via OpenAI

Step 1: Automated RSS Feed Monitoring

The workflow begins by continuously monitoring RSS feeds from your selected industry news sources. You can add as many sources as needed - the system checks for new articles every hour.

Key features of this stage:

  • Filters articles by keywords to ensure relevance
  • Extracts full article text (not just headlines)
  • Stores raw content for AI processing

Step 2: AI-Powered Article Summarization

Each new article gets processed through OpenAI to extract the core insights and reformat them into social media-friendly scripts. The AI:

  • Identifies the 3-5 key points from each article
  • Rewrites them in your brand voice (casual, professional, etc.)
  • Formats them with timestamps for video narration

Pro Tip: You can customize the OpenAI prompt to match your exact content style - whether you prefer bullet points, Q&A format, or narrative storytelling.

Step 3: Human-In-The-Loop Approval

Before anything gets published, the system posts drafts to a Google Drive folder for human review. This critical step ensures:

  • Quality control before public posting
  • Opportunity to add personal commentary
  • Final approval before automation continues

At 2:30 in the video, you'll see how moving a file to the approval folder triggers the next automation stage.

Step 4: Seamless Notion Integration

Approved scripts automatically post to your Notion content calendar with:

  • Article title and publication date
  • Formatted video script
  • Original source link
  • Scheduling metadata

This creates a ready-to-use content library where you can batch-record videos or send scripts directly to your video creation team.

Built-In Duplicate Protection

The workflow includes smart duplicate detection that:

  • Prevents reposting the same article
  • Identifies similar content across sources
  • Flags potential copyright issues
