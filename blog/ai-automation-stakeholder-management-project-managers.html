Step-by-Step Workflow Setup

The beauty of this solution is its simplicity - no coding required. We'll use a no-code automation platform (like Make or Zapier) with three key components: 1) A trigger watching your meeting transcripts folder, 2) An AI module to analyze the content, and 3) A Google Sheets integration to log the results.

Step 1: Set Up the Trigger

Configure the automation to watch a specific Google Drive folder where you'll store meeting transcripts. The workflow activates whenever a new file appears, whether it's a direct upload or from tools like Otter.ai or Fireflies.ai.

Step 2: Connect the AI Analysis

The AI module needs two key inputs: 1) The transcript file itself, and 2) A carefully crafted prompt that guides the analysis. At 7:30 in the video, you'll see how the prompt specifies exactly what outputs we want (sentiment, engagement level, summary).

Step 3: Configure the Output

Map the AI's outputs to specific columns in your Google Sheet. The system automatically creates a timestamped record for each stakeholder mentioned in the transcript, including their name, sentiment score, engagement level, and a concise summary.

Pro Tip: Always test each step individually before connecting the full workflow. At 4:45 in the video, you'll see how testing the trigger with a sample file helps verify everything works before going live.

3 Common Implementation Mistakes to Avoid

While the workflow is straightforward, these pitfalls can undermine your results: 1) Using unclear meeting transcripts (always ensure speaker identification), 2) Overlooking the prompt refinement phase (test with 5-10 sample transcripts), and 3) Not setting up trend analysis from the start.

The video at 15:45 shows a critical mindset shift - don't limit the system to just "important" meetings. The more data it processes, the better it understands your project's stakeholder landscape. What seems like noise today might reveal important patterns over time.

Maintenance Reality: Plan to spend 30 minutes weekly for the first month reviewing and correcting analyses - this trains the AI to better understand your specific project context and stakeholder dynamics.
