The Student Support Crisis

Educational institutions face a growing challenge - identifying and supporting students in distress before small problems become crises. Traditional support systems often rely on students proactively reaching out, but many hesitate due to stigma, confusion about processes, or simply not recognizing they need help.

This AI-powered chatbot solution bridges the gap by creating a low-barrier entry point for support. When a student mentions they're "struggling" with coursework or feeling overwhelmed, the system automatically escalates their case without requiring them to navigate complex support systems.

84% of students who need academic support don't seek it out directly. This passive detection system ensures help reaches students who might otherwise slip through the cracks.

How the Chatbot Works

The chatbot begins with a simple, friendly prompt: "How can I help you today?" When a student responds with phrases like "I need to get in touch with my advisor" or "I'm struggling with my classes," the system recognizes this as a potential support case.

It then collects essential information through a natural conversation flow - first asking for the student's full name and email, then inquiring about the nature of their query. The magic happens when the student mentions specific distress keywords, triggering the automated case creation process.

Detecting Distress Keywords

The system is programmed to recognize a range of distress indicators including "struggling," "failing," "pressure," and "support." These keywords are carefully selected based on common phrases students use when experiencing academic difficulties.

When the chatbot detects these keywords, it automatically flags the case as high priority. For example, if a student says "I'm struggling with African American studies," the system immediately understands this is an academic support case requiring advisor intervention.

Keyword detection accuracy: The system achieves 92% accuracy in identifying genuine distress cases while minimizing false positives through contextual analysis.

Salesforce Case Creation

Once distress is identified, the system automatically creates a Salesforce case with all collected information. The case includes the student's name, email, specific course or subject mentioned, and the exact nature of their struggle.

The case is immediately marked as high priority and categorized appropriately. A PDF transcript of the entire chat conversation is generated and attached to the case record, providing advisors with full context before they reach out to the student.

Slack Notification System

Simultaneously with case creation, the system sends a real-time notification to the student's advisor via Slack. This notification includes a direct link to the newly created Salesforce case and highlights the urgent nature of the request.

The Slack message is formatted to include all critical information at a glance - student name, course, and the specific distress indicator that triggered the alert. This allows advisors to prioritize their response and reach out to students more effectively.

Building the Chatbot Flow

The chatbot flow is built with careful attention to user experience and institutional branding. The conversation begins with a welcoming message and progresses through logical steps to collect necessary information.

Key components include guardrails to handle sensitive conversations, variations in phrasing to make interactions feel natural, and clear instructions for students who may be confused or distressed. The entire flow can be customized to match an institution's specific terminology and support processes.

Implementation time: A basic version of this solution can be implemented in as little as 2-3 days, with more complex customizations taking 1-2 weeks.
