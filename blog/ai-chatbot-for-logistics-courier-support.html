The Logistics Support Challenge

Every logistics manager knows the pattern: the same shipment status questions arrive dozens of times daily across email, chat, and phone. Customers want to know where their package is, when it will arrive, and what to do if plans change. Field teams need instant access to rate cards and service guides without calling dispatch.

When handled manually, this creates three costly problems: inconsistent answers (agents interpret policies differently), delayed responses (especially during peak periods), and frustrated staff who spend their days repeating basic information. 72% of logistics support time is spent answering questions that are already documented in company manuals and FAQs. This repetitive work costs mid-sized couriers $18,000-$25,000 monthly in avoidable labor expenses.

Building Your Logistics Assistant

Configuration follows a simple three-step process tailored for shipping operations:

Step 1: Define the Scope

Identify which queries to automate first - typically shipment status (85% of volume), service areas, and basic rate calculations. Leave complex claims and exceptions for human staff.

Step 2: Train the System

Upload your existing PDFs, knowledge base articles, and policy manuals. The AI structures this information and learns how to retrieve it contextually.

Step 3: Deploy Across Channels

Embed the assistant on your website, WhatsApp, SMS, and internal apps. It maintains consistent answers everywhere by referencing the same source materials.

Implementation takes 2-3 weeks for most logistics companies. The heaviest lift is often discovering and organizing existing documents - work that pays dividends beyond the AI implementation by cleaning up internal knowledge systems.
