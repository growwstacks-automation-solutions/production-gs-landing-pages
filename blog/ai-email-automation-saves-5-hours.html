Why Existing Email Solutions Fail

Traditional email management tools promise relief but deliver frustration. Sanebox ($7/month) requires weeks of manual training and still makes mistakes. Superhuman ($30/month) offers speed but no automatic categorization. Gmail's built-in filters only work for known senders.

The fundamental flaw? These tools can't actually understand email content. They rely on rules and keywords, forcing you to manually create filters for each new sender. When an email contains both a newsletter and sales pitch, they fail completely.

The breakthrough realization: If AI can write essays and explain quantum physics, why can't it read an email and tell you what it is? This insight led to building an automation that thinks like a human secretary - but works 24/7 for pennies.

How the Automation Works

The system uses just five Make.com modules in a simple but powerful sequence:

Step 1: Gmail Watcher

Constantly monitors your inbox for new emails, triggering the workflow the moment a message arrives.

Step 2: OpenAI Analysis

Sends the email's subject, sender, and body to ChatGPT with this prompt: "Read this email and categorize it as: newsletter, sales pitch, invoice, or other."

Step 3: Smart Router

Directs the email down different paths based on the AI's categorization decision.

Step 4: Label Application

Applies the appropriate Gmail label automatically, sorting the email before you even see it.

In summary: 1) Detect new email → 2) Have AI read it → 3) Route by category → 4) Apply label. The entire process takes 3-4 seconds per email and runs continuously.
