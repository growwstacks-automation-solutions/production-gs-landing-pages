How the LinkedIn Content Scraper Works

This n8n workflow automates what top content marketers do manually - research high-performing posts, analyze engagement patterns, and adapt those insights for their own content. The difference? Instead of spending hours each week scrolling LinkedIn, the process happens automatically in the background.

The workflow has five key components that work together:

  1. A schedule trigger that runs the scraper automatically (daily, weekly, etc.)
  2. A LinkedIn scraping node that collects posts based on your keywords
  3. Filter nodes that identify high-quality content (minimum 20 likes in our example)
  4. An Airtable integration that stores and organizes the post data
  5. Optional AI nodes that can generate similar post ideas based on the scraped content

Step 1: Setting Up the Schedule Trigger

The workflow begins with n8n's schedule trigger, which determines how often your scraper runs. For most businesses, weekly is ideal - enough to stay current without overwhelming your database.

In the trigger settings (shown at 0:45 in the video), you can specify:

  • Frequency: Daily, weekly, or custom intervals
  • Time of day to run (early mornings work well for fresh data)
  • Timezone settings

Pro Tip: Set your scraper to run Monday mornings to analyze weekend engagement patterns when business content often performs differently.

Step 2: Scraping LinkedIn Posts

The core of the workflow is the LinkedIn scraping node, which collects posts based on your configured search parameters. At 1:10 in the tutorial, you'll see how we target:

  • Specific keywords (like "AI" or "Amazon" in our example)
  • Post types (articles, native posts, etc.)
  • Date ranges (last week, last month, etc.)

This node uses your exported LinkedIn cookies (we'll cover prerequisites later) to authenticate and avoid rate limits. The cost is surprisingly low - about $1 per 1,000 posts scraped.

Step 3: Filtering for High-Quality Content

Not all scraped posts are worth analyzing. At 1:35 in the video, we add a filter node that only keeps posts with:

  • Minimum 20 likes (adjustable based on your industry standards)
  • At least 3 comments (shows genuine engagement)
  • Relevant keywords in the post text

In our test run, this filter kept 6 high-quality posts out of 50 scraped - automatically discarding the 44 that didn't meet our engagement threshold.

Why 20 likes? This threshold ensures you're only learning from posts that actually resonated, not just random content. For niche industries, you might lower this to 10; for competitive spaces, raise it to 50.

Step 4: Storing Data in Airtable

The filtered posts then flow into Airtable (shown at 2:05), creating a searchable database of high-performing content. Each record includes:

  • Full post text
  • Engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares)
  • Author information
  • Post date and time
  • Any media attachments

This becomes your personal content research library. Over time, you'll spot patterns in what works - specific phrases, post lengths, or topics that consistently get engagement in your industry.

Step 5: Generating Content Ideas

The final step (optional but powerful) uses AI to generate new post ideas based on your scraped content. By feeding the top-performing posts into an LLM like GPT-4, you can:

  • Get variations on proven post structures
  • Identify common themes across successful posts
  • Generate similar content in your brand voice

This creates a complete content flywheel - research → analysis → creation - all automated through n8n.

Key Prerequisites You Need

Before building this workflow, you'll need two key components (covered at 2:50 in the video):

  1. LinkedIn Cookies: Use the Cookie Editor Chrome extension to export your LinkedIn session cookies. This authenticates your scraper to avoid rate limits.
  2. Keyword JSON File: Create a simple JSON file with your target search terms (like "AI" or "Amazon" in our example). This tells the scraper what content to collect.
