The $78,000 Problem Every Dental Practice Faces

Dental clinics lose an average of 18 calls per week - that's 936 missed opportunities annually. At $150-$300 per new patient visit, this translates to $78,000-$156,000 in lost revenue per year. The problem compounds when staff are juggling in-person patients, insurance verifications, and administrative tasks.

Traditional IVR systems frustrate callers with endless menus. Answering services lack the nuance to qualify patients or book appointments. Most dental teams resort to overtime or additional hires just to manage call volume - an expensive solution that still can't guarantee 100% coverage.

23% of potential new patients don't call back after reaching voicemail, according to Dental Economics research. They simply move on to the next practice in their Google search results.
