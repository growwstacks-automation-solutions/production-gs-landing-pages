The Booking Nightmare (Before Automation)

Picture this: It's February half-term in the UK, and one of the country's largest ski schools is turning off their Google Ads - not because they lack demand, but because they physically can't handle more customers. The owner's wife works from 8am to 11pm daily during peak season, manually coordinating lessons across 30 instructors at multiple resorts. Each of their 60 daily initial inquiries spawns 4-5 follow-up emails as customers ask about availability, pricing, and instructor specialties.

The breaking point came when they realized their highest-revenue week was actually their least profitable. The manual labor required to handle bookings - cross-referencing dozens of calendars, calculating travel times between resorts, and drafting personalized responses - consumed all their margins. They had tried hiring assistants and buying off-the-shelf solutions, only to be burned by unreliable staff and overpromised technology.

32 hours weekly - that's how much time the ski school was spending solely on email admin before automation. The equivalent of nearly one full-time employee dedicated just to handling booking inquiries and follow-ups.

Overcoming the Legacy System Challenge

The first major hurdle was their outdated Timely booking system - a legacy platform with no API access. This meant our AI couldn't directly check instructor availability or create new bookings. Rather than forcing a disruptive system change, we engineered a clever workaround using Google Calendar as middleware.

Every night at midnight, a custom workflow syncs all 30 instructors' schedules from Timely to individual Google Calendars. The system handles three scenarios: new bookings (adds to Google Calendar), updated bookings (modifies existing events), and cancellations (removes events). This gives the AI clean, API-accessible availability data without requiring changes to their established Timely workflow.

Technical Insight: We batch-process the 30 instructor syncs in parallel groups of 10 to optimize performance. The entire sync completes in under 15 minutes despite the volume.

How the AI Classifies Booking Emails

Not every email to the ski school requires the full booking workflow. Marketing inquiries, general questions, and spam would waste processing power if handled by the AI receptionist. Our solution starts with a classification layer that analyzes each incoming email to determine if it's a genuine booking request.

The classifier uses natural language processing to identify key phrases and intent. Genuine inquiries typically include date ranges, group sizes, and skill levels. The system returns a confidence score and reasoning behind its classification - crucial for debugging when (rarely) it makes an incorrect determination. Only verified booking inquiries proceed to the full workflow.

Smart Instructor Matching System

The heart of the solution is its ability to match customers with the ideal instructor based on multiple factors. We built a comprehensive instructor database in Supabase containing:

  • Specializations (children, nervous skiers, German speakers etc.)
  • Location assignments (which resorts they work at)
  • Availability windows (when they're working each season)
  • Google Calendar IDs for real-time schedule checks

When a request comes in for "a patient instructor for our anxious 6-year-old," the AI cross-references instructor specialties, location availability, and actual calendar openings to suggest the best matches. It even factors in business rules like minimum booking durations during peak times.

The Midnight Calendar Sync Solution

The secret sauce enabling this entire system is the nightly sync between their legacy Timely system and Google Calendar. Here's how it works technically:

  1. Webhook trigger: A Timely webhook initiates the sync process at midnight
  2. Data comparison: Custom code compares yesterday's Google Calendar state with new Timely events
  3. Three-way processing: Adds new bookings, updates changed ones, and removes cancellations
  4. Parallel execution: Runs simultaneously for all 30 instructors in batched groups

This approach gives us the best of both worlds - maintaining their existing Timely workflow while providing the AI with modern API access to availability data.

Why We Kept a Human in the Loop

While the AI handles the heavy lifting of calendar checks and initial response drafting, we intentionally kept the ski school owners in the approval chain. Here's why:

  • Brand voice preservation: Their personalized hospitality built their reputation
  • Exception handling: They know which instructors work well together for group bookings
  • Trust building: After previous automation failures, they needed to verify outputs

The system creates email drafts with all available options, which the owners can quickly review and tweak before sending. This hybrid approach maintains their signature service quality while eliminating 90% of the manual work.
