At $100/hour labor costs, that's $200 spent chasing a $12,000 average contract.

The installer who delivers the first complete proposal gains an anchoring advantage—their numbers become the mental benchmark, regardless of being the cheapest. Yet most solar companies still treat proposal generation like a bespoke artisanal process instead of an automated system.

Key insight: Prospects don't compare absolute prices—they compare the perceived value of your proposal against whatever number they saw first. Speed creates the anchor.

Building the 90-Second Qualification Form

The automation starts with an optimized intake form that balances thoroughness with conversion rates. Unlike traditional solar forms with 50+ fields, this version collects just 11 critical data points in under 90 seconds:

  1. Contact info (name, email, phone)
  2. Service address (for satellite imagery pull)
  3. Average monthly electric bill (estimates system size)
  4. Property type (single/multi-family, commercial)
  5. Ownership status (flags landlord approval needs)
  6. Roof type/material (affects installation costs)
  7. Roof age (auto-flags potential replacements)
  8. Shading conditions (impacts production estimates)
  9. Battery backup interest (shapes proposal options)
  10. Optional roof photos (not required for initial quote)

The form uses Tally for its native n8n integration, but works with any form builder. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how the dropdowns and conditional logic prevent invalid entries that could break the automation.

n8n Workflow Architecture

The 8-node n8n workflow transforms form submissions into polished proposals through this sequence:

Step 1: Tally Trigger

Listens for new form submissions and passes raw data to the next node. Requires your Tally API key and form ID.

Step 2: Data Normalization

Converts messy field IDs ("question_ABC123") into clean variables ("electric_bill_range"), formats phone numbers for SMS, and structures the AI instructions.

Step 3: AI Proposal Generation

GPT-4-turbo analyzes the inputs against your pricing rules to generate system specs, production estimates, pricing ranges, and narrative explanations in JSON format.

Step 4: Validation Guardrails

Checks for pricing outliers, missing disclosures, and compliance requirements before merging with customer data.

Step 5: Email Template Builder

Transforms the structured data into branded HTML with clear ROI calculations and optional add-ons.

Step 6-7: Multi-Channel Delivery

Sends the full proposal via Gmail (for detail) and a shortened version via Twilio SMS (for immediacy).

Pro tip: The workflow processes 98% of submissions in under 10 seconds—faster than most CRMs can send a confirmation email.

Solar-Specific AI Prompt Engineering

The magic happens in the prompt engineering that makes GPT-4-turbo behave like an experienced solar estimator:

  • Role definition: "You are a senior solar estimator with 12 years of residential and commercial installation experience..."
  • Output structure: Requires strict JSON formatting with nested arrays for system options, incentives, and disclosures
  • Pricing rules: Embeds your $/watt targets, minimum margins, and typical labor hours
  • Production estimates: Uses NREL PVWatts data for the prospect's region
  • Legal safeguards: Auto-includes FTC solar rights disclosures and tax credit explanations

At 4:50 in the video, you'll see how the prompt dynamically adjusts for commercial vs. residential properties, older roofs needing replacement, and battery backup configurations—all while maintaining your brand voice.

Pricing & Compliance Guardrails

The validation node acts as a quality control checkpoint with these automated checks:

  • Price floor enforcement: Rejects proposals below your $2.50/watt minimum
  • Roof age alerts: Flags proposals for roofs older than 15 years with a recommended replacement addendum
  • Rental property rules: Requires landlord approval language when ownership_status = "renting"
  • Battery upsell: Promotes storage more aggressively when battery_interest = "yes"
  • Tax credit math: Verifies the 30% federal credit calculation matches system pricing

If any check fails, the workflow either auto-corrects the proposal or routes it to a human review queue—preventing embarrassing or non-compliant quotes from reaching prospects.

Email + SMS Delivery Strategy

The dual-channel delivery system accounts for how solar shoppers consume information:

Email (Gmail): Contains the full proposal with:

  • Branded HTML template
  • System sizing and production estimates
  • 3 pricing tiers with ROI timelines
  • Federal/state incentive breakdowns
  • Next steps for site survey scheduling

SMS (Twilio): Sends a 120-character teaser:

"Hi [Name], your solar estimate is ready! View your $[X]-$[Y] price range and 25-year savings projection here: [Link]. Reply BOOK to schedule your site survey."

This combination achieves a 93% open rate (SMS) and 47% detailed review rate (email)—far higher than email alone.
