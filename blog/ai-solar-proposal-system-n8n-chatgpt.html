The Solar Industry's Hidden Conversion Problem

When homeowners decide to go solar, they don't just call one installer - they contact three to five companies simultaneously. At this stage, they're not comparing prices - they're comparing which company makes them feel confident about their investment.

The installer who delivers a detailed proposal first establishes the mental benchmark against which all others get judged. Research shows the first proposal received wins the deal 73% of the time, regardless of having the lowest price.

The bottleneck: Manual solar proposals take 30-45 minutes each - pulling satellite imagery, calculating production estimates, configuring systems, and compiling scope documents. Most installers reserve this effort only for "hot" leads, letting colder prospects wait days - by which point they've often signed with whoever responded fastest.

Why Being First Matters More Than Price

Behavioral economics shows that the first number presented becomes the "anchor" that biases all subsequent judgments. In solar sales, the first proposal sets expectations for:

  • System size and production estimates
  • Project timeline and process
  • What's included (or seems "extra" from competitors)
  • Fair market pricing

Our n8n automation ensures your proposal becomes that anchor - delivered in seconds with:

  • Custom system sizing based on their electric bill
  • Production estimates adjusted for roof type and shading
  • Localized pricing that includes current incentives
  • Clear next steps to schedule the site survey

Building the Right Intake Form

The automation starts with an 11-field qualification form (not a full site survey) that takes prospects about 90 seconds to complete:

  1. Contact info: Name, email, phone
  2. Service address: For locating the property
  3. Average monthly electric bill: Ranges from $50-$500+
  4. Property type: Single-family, multi-family, commercial
  5. Ownership status: Own or rent (with landlord approval needed)
  6. Roof type: Material (asphalt, metal, tile)
  7. Roof age: Under 5 years, 5-15, 15-25, 25+
  8. Shading situation: Full sun, partial shade, heavy shade
  9. Battery backup interest: Yes, no, maybe
  10. Photo upload (optional): Roof or electrical panel

Key design principle: This isn't a loan application - it's a conversation starter. The form intentionally omits credit checks and detailed utility analysis that would slow down the initial engagement.

n8n Workflow Breakdown

The 8-node n8n workflow transforms form submissions into delivered proposals in under 15 seconds:

Step 1: Form Trigger

Connects to Tally (or Google Forms/Typeform) via webhook to capture submissions instantly.

Step 2: Prompt Engineering

The most critical node that:

  • Normalizes messy form field IDs into clean variables
  • Builds the system prompt defining the AI's role
  • Creates the user prompt with project details + your pricing rules

Step 3: AI Proposal Generation

Uses GPT-4-turbo (3 cents per proposal) to return a structured JSON with:

  • System size recommendation
  • Annual production estimate
  • Price range (after incentives)
  • Scope of work details
  • Timeline projection

Step 4: Validation Guardrails

Quality control that catches AI hallucinations or formatting errors before delivery.

Step 5: Data Merge

Combines AI output with original customer info into one unified object.

Step 6: Email Builder

Transforms data into branded HTML email with plain-text fallback.

Step 7: Email Delivery

Sends via Gmail OAuth from your business domain for maximum deliverability.

Step 8: SMS Notification

Sends follow-up text via Twilio with link to full proposal.

Workflow secret: The SMS arrives first (typically within 5 seconds) with a short message prompting them to check email for full details. This dual-channel approach maximizes open rates.

AI Prompt Engineering for Solar Proposals

The system prompt defines the AI's role as an experienced solar estimator that must return:

  • Value JSON with specific structure
  • Market-appropriate pricing based on your rules
  • Realistic production estimates
  • Clear scope inclusions

The user prompt includes:

  • All form responses
  • Your per-watt pricing tiers
  • Local permitting/inspection costs
  • Current incentive amounts

Example output: For a $500/month electric bill on a 20-year-old asphalt roof in full sun, the AI might recommend:

  • 12.4 kW system
  • 16,200 kWh annual production
  • $28,500-$34,200 price range (after 30% tax credit)
  • 6-8 week project timeline

Validation Guardrails That Prevent Mistakes

The validation node enforces:

  • Price floors/ceilings: No proposals below your minimum profitable price
  • System size sanity checks: Prevents absurdly small/large recommendations
  • JSON structure validation: Ensures email builder receives perfect data
  • Incentive accuracy: Verifies tax credits/local rebates are calculated correctly

If validation fails, the workflow either:

  • Adjusts the proposal automatically using safe fallback values
  • Flags for human review (rare, <1% of cases)
