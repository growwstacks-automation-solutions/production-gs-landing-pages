How Fliper Works

The process mirrors how professional writers work, but automated. After logging in at flip.workflow.io, you create a "column" (project) and specify:

  1. Input language (12 supported)
  2. Output language (can differ from input)
  3. Writing style (6 professional options)

At 3:48 in the tutorial, you'll see how selecting "Simple and Clear" style transformed bullet points about university electricity solutions into a journalistic article with proper transitions and narrative flow.

3 Input Options Explained

Fliper accepts content in whatever form you naturally produce it:

1. Live Audio Recording

Speak directly into your device's microphone (great for brainstorming). The AI removes filler words ("um", "like") while preserving your core message.

2. Audio File Upload

Import recorded meetings, interviews, or voice memos. At 6:12, the demo shows how a rough audio clip about campus energy became a structured article.

3. Text Snippets

Paste disjointed notes or bullet points. Fliper analyzes relationships between ideas to create coherent transitions.
