The Unconventional Start (Selling Before Building)

Most aspiring agency owners waste months perfecting their offering before talking to a single client. You Found AI flipped this approach - they started cold calling businesses about voice AI solutions before they even had a product.

"We were all working full-time jobs in the beginning," explains co-founder Aal. "After 5 PM, we'd cold call local businesses from Google Maps listings. We booked our first meeting on day one and closed a client within weeks - that's when we knew this could work."

Key insight: Selling first creates immediate market validation. The team used early client projects to refine their voice AI offering rather than building in isolation.

This approach solved two critical problems: it provided real-world feedback to shape their product, and generated social proof before they had case studies. Their first clients understood they were early adopters working with a new agency.

The Cold Calling Breakthrough

Early on, You Found AI received game-changing advice from an established agency owner: "Just cold call. Put your head down and cold call." While most agencies focus on email or social media, they committed to phone outreach.

Their system was simple but effective:

  1. Identify local businesses in target industries (initially plumbers, therapists, home services)
  2. Call the main business number listed on Google Maps
  3. Ask for the owner or decision-maker
  4. Deliver a 15-second pitch focused on lead capture

Result: Cold calling converted 3-5x better than other outreach methods, with some weeks seeing 30% of calls converting to booked demos.

The team quickly realized most business numbers routed to receptionists or call centers. This led them to develop their breakthrough solution for reaching owners directly.

The Google Maps Owner Phone Hack

After exhausting traditional lead sources, You Found AI's technical co-founder built a custom Google Maps scraper with a crucial enhancement - it extracted owner mobile numbers from business listings.

"We were getting receptionists 80% of the time with regular numbers," Aal explains. "Our system pulls the owner's actual mobile number from Google Maps data - these are local businesses who often list personally."

The impact was immediate:

  • Call connection rates jumped from 20% to 65%
  • Average calls per booked demo dropped from 50 to 15
  • They scaled from 100 to 600 calls/day with the same team

This became so valuable they packaged it as "Owner Phones" - now sold to other agencies struggling with gatekeepers.

Why Niching Down 3X'd Their Results

Early on, You Found AI made the common mistake of selling to "any business owner." After struggling with inconsistent messaging, they niched down to home services and trades.

The transformation was dramatic:

  • Demo-to-close rate increased from 25% to 42%
  • Average deal size grew by 60%
  • Client retention improved significantly

Epiphany: Specializing allowed them to develop industry-specific voice AI solutions that solved concrete problems rather than generic lead capture.

Their fulfillment became more efficient too - they could reuse and adapt components across similar clients rather than building from scratch each time.

The Recurring Revenue Fulfillment Model

While many AI agencies focus on one-time implementations, You Found AI structured 90% of engagements as monthly retainers. This created predictable revenue and aligned incentives.

Their standard offering includes:

  • Initial setup fee ($1,500-$3,000)
  • Monthly management ($997-$2,500/month)
  • Performance-based bonuses

"Recurring revenue lets us invest in long-term client success," notes Aal. "We're not just handing off an AI agent - we're continuously optimizing it based on real usage data."

This model also smoothed cash flow fluctuations and reduced the constant pressure to acquire new clients.

Operations & Scaling Systems

As volume grew, You Found AI implemented several key systems:

1. Automated CRM Population

Their dialer automatically logs calls and scheduled demos into their CRM, eliminating manual entry. This saved 2+ hours daily in admin work.

2. Make.com Workflows

They connected their dialer, CRM, and scheduling tools using Make.com (formerly Integromat), creating seamless data flow between systems.

3. Documentation Standards

Every client interaction and technical component is documented in Notion using standardized templates. This enabled them to train new team members quickly.

Result: These systems allowed them to triple client volume without adding overhead proportionally.

The 70% Reinvestment Strategy

Rather than taking profits early, You Found AI reinvested heavily in growth levers:

  • Dialer software: Upgraded to a premium dialer that increased daily call capacity from 100 to 600
  • Lead generation: Built their Owner Phones scraper to solve their own outreach bottleneck
  • Talent: Hired a dedicated sales closer and technical support specialist

"Early-stage agencies should reinvest 50-70% of profits," advises Aal. "Every dollar we put back into the right systems generated 3-5x returns within months."

This disciplined reinvestment allowed them to scale rapidly while maintaining healthy margins.

3 Key Lessons for AI Agencies

1. Do What Works - Then Do More of It

When they focused exclusively on cold calling (their highest-converting channel), revenue tripled in a month. Many agencies spread efforts across multiple mediocre channels instead of doubling down on what works.

2. Care Deeply About Client Outcomes

Their best growth came from referrals and testimonials - only possible through exceptional client results. This requires building with the client's business needs first, not just chasing revenue.

3. Avoid Shiny Object Syndrome

Committing to their proven systems for 6+ months created compounding results. Many agencies constantly pivot to new strategies before seeing full potential.

Final takeaway: "Everything is hard at first. Stick with what works, care about your clients more than your paycheck, and the results will come."
