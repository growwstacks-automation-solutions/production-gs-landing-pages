Implementation: What to Expect

Deploying an AI voice agent like Lindy involves four key phases:

Phase 1: Call Type Analysis (1-2 weeks)

Identify the 20% of call types that account for 80% of volume - these become your automation priorities. In the demo, workflow errors like WF1023 would clearly qualify.

Phase 2: Knowledge Integration (2-3 weeks)

Connect the AI to your documentation, CRM, and ticketing system. The demo agent pulled solution steps directly from the company's knowledge base.

Phase 3: Conversation Design (2 weeks)

Map out ideal dialog flows for each call type. Notice how the demo agent asked specific questions about error codes before providing solutions.

Phase 4: Pilot & Refinement (4 weeks)

Launch with a subset of calls, monitor resolution rates, and continuously improve the AI's responses based on real interactions.

Pro tip: Start with clearly defined technical issues like the workflow error in the demo before tackling more subjective inquiries. These yield the fastest ROI.

AI Voice Agent Best Practices

Based on successful implementations like the one shown in the Lindy demo, follow these guidelines:

1. Maintain a Human Handoff Option

Even the best AI can't handle every issue. Ensure seamless transfer to human agents when needed (though the demo showed this wasn't necessary for the workflow error).

2. Train on Real Call Recordings

Use actual customer support calls - not hypothetical scenarios - to teach the AI how people naturally describe problems.

3. Set Clear Performance Metrics

Track first-call resolution rate (aim for 85%+), average handle time (target <4 minutes), and customer satisfaction (target 90%+ CSAT).

4. Start With Low-Risk Scenarios

Technical troubleshooting like the workflow error in the demo presents less risk than sensitive account or billing issues.

Critical success factor: Continuously update the AI's knowledge base as your product evolves to maintain high resolution rates.
