The Plumbing Call Crisis

62% of business calls go unanswered according to industry research - and for plumbers, this statistic represents a revenue hemorrhage. When a pipe bursts at 2 AM or a water heater fails on Sunday afternoon, customers facing water damage won't leave a voicemail and wait patiently. They'll immediately call the next plumber in their Google search results.

Traditional solutions like hiring additional reception staff or using answering services come with significant drawbacks. Receptionists require salaries, benefits, and can't provide 24/7 coverage without expensive shift rotations. Answering services often provide poor customer experiences and can't actually book appointments - they just take messages.

Key insight: Each unanswered plumbing call represents $250-$500 in lost revenue on average, based on typical service call pricing. For a busy plumbing company missing 10 calls per week, that's $130,000-$260,000 in lost annual revenue.
