The $120 Billion Problem Restaurants Can't Solve

Every missed phone call represents lost revenue for restaurants - whether it's an unplaced order, missed reservation, or delivery request. Gurveer Singh, CEO of Certus AI, grew up in his family's 11-restaurant business and experienced this pain firsthand. "At nine years old, my job was answering phones because my parents couldn't afford to miss a single call," he recalls in the interview.

The numbers are staggering: US restaurants lose between $100-120 billion annually from unanswered calls and order errors. With the restaurant market growing from $1 trillion to $1.5 trillion post-COVID, this problem is only accelerating. Traditional solutions - hiring more staff or outsourcing to call centers - are expensive and often ineffective.

Key Insight: Phone calls represent the highest-margin revenue stream for restaurants. Third-party platforms like Uber Eats charge 25-30% per order, while direct phone orders keep that profit with the restaurant.

How Voice Agents Actually Work in Restaurants

Certus AI's system functions as a complete replacement for the restaurant phone line. When a customer calls, the AI agent answers and handles the entire interaction - whether it's taking a food order, booking a reservation, or arranging delivery. The system integrates directly with the restaurant's POS (like Toast or Square), delivery partners (Uber Eats, DoorDash), and reservation systems.

This end-to-end automation means orders flow directly to the kitchen for preparation, deliveries are dispatched to drivers, and reservations are logged - all without staff intervention. "Our tagline is to replace the restaurant phone line," Singh explains. "We handle everything so staff can focus on in-person customers."

Why Customers Actually Prefer AI Over Human Staff

One might assume customers would resist interacting with AI, especially in hospitality. However, Certus has found the opposite to be true. Singh shares an insight from Jeff Bezos: "When you're speaking to AI, it doesn't matter if it's AI or human as long as it gets the job done well."

The system includes subtle touches like restaurant background noise (clanging plates, faint chatter) to create authenticity. More importantly, it eliminates the frustration of rushed staff who can't devote full attention to phone orders. Elderly customers - the demographic least expected to embrace the technology - have become some of its biggest fans because the AI patiently guides them through the ordering process.

Conversion Secret: Unlike basic AI receptionists that redirect customers to websites, Certus completes the entire transaction during the call - exactly what the customer called to accomplish.

The POS Integration Challenge Solved

The true innovation lies in Certus's deep integration with restaurant tech stacks. Most AI solutions falter at the critical point of actually inputting orders into POS systems. Certus built proprietary connectors that translate conversational orders into structured data that flows directly into:

  • Kitchen display systems for food preparation
  • Delivery dispatch platforms for driver assignment
  • Reservation books for table management
  • Customer databases for order history

This eliminates the need for staff to manually re-enter phone orders, reducing errors and saving significant time during peak hours.

The Surprising Revenue Impact Beyond Just Answering Calls

While capturing previously missed calls obviously increases revenue, Certus discovered an unexpected secondary benefit. After implementation, restaurants see customers shifting orders from third-party platforms (which charge 25-30% fees) back to direct phone orders.

Singh shares data from an early adopter: "Within six weeks, his third-party platform revenue decreased while his overall revenue increased. Customers realized they could get the same delivery service directly from the restaurant at lower prices." This creates a double win - higher order volume plus improved margins.

Handling 400-Item Menus With Nested Modifiers

One of Certus's key technical breakthroughs was handling complex menus common in ethnic cuisines. Indian and Chinese restaurants often have 100-400 items with intricate customization options ("half tomato/half garlic base, left side mushrooms, right side jalapeños").

The system uses advanced context window management to track these details conversationally. Rather than relying solely on large language models, Certus developed a hybrid approach that combines:

  • Real-time database queries for menu accuracy
  • Conversational memory to maintain order context
  • Error detection algorithms to flag potential mistakes

For the rare errors (under 0.5% of orders), Certus proactively contacts customers to correct mistakes before food preparation.

YC Insights That Changed Their Trajectory

As a Y Combinator-backed company, Certus benefited from counterintuitive advice that shaped their approach. Group partner Tyler Bosmani (co-founder of Clever) advised: "To go fast, you need to go slow." This meant pausing customer acquisition for six weeks to perfect their onboarding system.

The result? After this intensive product focus, Certus achieved 100% week-over-week growth for four consecutive weeks. The lesson: Sometimes the fastest path to scale requires temporary slowdowns to build proper infrastructure.
