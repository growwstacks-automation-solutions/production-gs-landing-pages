The High-Volume Conversation Challenge

Real estate agents and debt collectors face the same fundamental problem: too many conversations that follow predictable patterns, yet require immediate human attention. A single open house weekend can generate 100+ leads needing follow-up. Debt collection teams must balance high contact volumes with strict compliance requirements.

Traditional solutions either sacrifice speed (human teams can't scale) or quality (generic IVRs frustrate callers). RunGo's founder Adrian Smith discovered this pain point while working with nonprofits in 2017, where SMS fundraising campaigns had 99% open rates but no capacity to handle responses at scale.

Key insight: The magic happens when you combine AI's scalability with human-like conversation quality. Modern voice agents can now handle 50-80 calls per hour (vs 10-15 for humans) while maintaining natural flow and compliance.

Real Estate Use Cases That Scale

At 12:35 in the interview, Adrian explains how real estate agents use AI voice agents throughout the transaction lifecycle. The most impactful applications include:

1. Open Home Follow-Ups

Agents can automatically call all attendees within minutes of an open house ending, qualifying interest levels and booking appointments while the property is top of mind. This cuts the typical 24-48 hour response time down to under 30 minutes.

2. Neighborhood Outreach

When listing a property, agents can target entire streets with personalized calls: "We're selling 42 Maple Street - when would you like us to appraise your home?" This generates off-market listings that never hit public portals.

3. Rental Management

Automated reminders for late rent, maintenance scheduling, and lease renewals handle 80% of routine landlord-tenant communications while maintaining detailed audit trails.

Implementation tip: Start with your highest-volume, most repetitive conversations. For most real estate teams, this means open home follow-ups and appointment scheduling.

Debt Collection & Compliance

Debt collection presents unique challenges - conversations must adhere to strict regulations while maintaining high contact rates. AI voice agents solve this through:

  • Scripted compliance: Built-in disclosures and prohibited phrase detection
  • Sentiment analysis: Automatic escalation when detecting distress
  • Payment flexibility: Secure payment processing during the call

Adrian's team spent 18 months perfecting their debt collection voice agent (now replicable in weeks with modern tools). The system handles delicate payment negotiations while generating detailed call logs for compliance audits.

From SMS to AI Voice: The Tech Evolution

RunGo's journey mirrors the rapid advancement of conversational AI:

  1. 2017: SMS fundraising for nonprofits with simple decision trees
  2. 2019: Added MMS and voicemail drops to campaigns
  3. 2021: First voice agents using Dialogflow (2000+ audio files per conversation)
  4. 2023: LLM-powered agents with voice cloning (weeks to build new flows)
  5. 2025: Platform handles 100,000+ calls monthly across real estate and debt collection

The key breakthrough came when voice cloning and LLMs reduced development time from 18 months to 1 week for complex implementations.

Implementation Process & Timelines

Deploying AI voice agents involves three key phases:

1. Conversation Mapping (1-2 Weeks)

Document all possible conversation paths, compliance requirements, and escalation triggers. Real estate implementations typically have 5-7 main conversation flows.

2. Voice Cloning & Integration (1-2 Weeks)

Record agent voices (or use stock voices) and connect to CRM systems like Salesforce or HubSpot. Debt collection implementations often require custom payment system integrations.

3. Testing & Refinement (2 Weeks)

Rigorous testing ensures natural flow and compliance. RunGo's team makes hundreds of test calls to identify edge cases before go-live.

Pro tip: Start with a pilot on one high-volume use case before expanding. Most teams begin with open home follow-ups or late payment reminders.

The Future of Voice AI (3-Year Outlook)

At 24:12 in the interview, Adrian predicts voice AI will become commoditized within three years. The competitive advantage will shift from technology to:

  • Vertical expertise: Deep understanding of industry-specific workflows
  • Compliance frameworks: Pre-built regulatory controls for sensitive sectors
  • Implementation speed: Rapid deployment of new conversation flows

Early adopters who embed voice AI into their core processes today will maintain an edge even as the technology becomes more accessible.
