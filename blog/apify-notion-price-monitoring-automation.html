How the Automation Works

The system runs on a daily or weekly schedule you control. Each cycle begins by fetching all product records from your Notion database, including current prices and competitor URLs. Apify then scrapes the latest pricing data directly from Amazon product pages, just like a human visitor would see it.

A comparison engine analyzes each product individually, calculating price differences and determining whether changes are significant enough to trigger alerts. The workflow updates your Notion database with fresh pricing data, marks status changes, and sends formatted Slack notifications with all relevant details.

Setting Up the Notion Database

Your Notion database serves as the central repository for all product and pricing information. The workflow requires these key fields: Product Name, SKU Code, Last Known Price, Currency, Last Check Date, Status (Higher/Lower/Equal), Tracking Notes, and Comparison Details.

At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how the database structure enables efficient data processing. Each product becomes a record that the automation can read, update, and reference. The system maintains a complete history of price changes directly in Notion, creating an auditable trail of competitor pricing strategies over time.

Configuring the Apify Scraper

Apify's Amazon scraping actors extract product data with near-perfect accuracy. The workflow passes each competitor URL to the scraper, which returns the current price, rating, review count, and product page URL. This happens without loading Amazon in a browser or requiring manual intervention.

The video demonstrates two approaches at 3:40 — using pre-scraped data sets for testing or making live API calls to scrape fresh data on demand. For production use, we recommend the live scraping option to ensure you're always working with the most current pricing information available.

Building the Comparison Logic

The magic happens in the function node that compares scraped prices against your Notion records. At 4:55 in the tutorial, you'll see how it calculates price differences, determines whether changes are significant (based on thresholds you set), and prepares all data for database updates and notifications.

The comparison handles edge cases like temporary discounts, out-of-stock items, and price fluctuations below your alert threshold. You can customize the logic to ignore changes under a certain percentage or dollar amount, preventing notification fatigue for minor adjustments.

Pro Tip: Add a 5% buffer to your comparison logic to avoid reacting to temporary price fluctuations while still catching meaningful market shifts.

Automated Alerts and Notifications

When the system detects a significant price change, it sends a Slack notification with all relevant details: product name, old price, new price, percentage change, and direct link to the product page. The notification format is fully customizable — you can add your logo, include additional context, or format the message however your team prefers.

The workflow updates your Notion database simultaneously, ensuring your records always reflect the latest market data. At 6:20 in the video, you'll see how the system marks status changes (Higher/Lower) and timestamps each update, creating a complete pricing history for analysis and reporting.
