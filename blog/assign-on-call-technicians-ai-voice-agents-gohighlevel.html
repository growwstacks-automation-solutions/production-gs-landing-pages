The After-Hours Emergency Call Problem

Service businesses lose an average of $3,700 per month in emergency call revenue due to disorganized on-call systems. When a pipe bursts at 8 PM, customers expect immediate help - not voicemails or frantic group texts between technicians. Traditional on-call rotations create three painful problems:

First, managers waste 90 minutes weekly coordinating schedules manually. Second, 68% of emergency calls get delayed responses because the wrong technician receives the alert. Third, businesses forget to collect emergency fees 43% of the time, leaving money on the table.

The hidden cost: Every unanswered emergency call damages your reputation. 82% of customers will call a competitor next time if you miss their after-hours emergency.

Daily Technician Selection System

GoHighLevel solves this with a foolproof daily selection workflow. At 4 PM each afternoon, the manager receives an email with a simple form link (timestamp 2:15 in the video). The form shows all available technicians in a dropdown, plus a "None Available Tonight" option.

When a selection is made, GoHighLevel updates a custom contact field called "On-Call Technician." This field powers three critical automations: your voice AI checks it during after-hours calls, your notification system uses it to alert the right person, and your billing system references it for emergency fee tracking.

Automatic Emergency Fee Handling

The system doesn't just route calls - it protects your revenue. When the AI detects an emergency situation (like the plumbing leak example at 14:30 in the video), it follows a specific protocol:

  1. Explains the $149 emergency service fee that applies to after-hours calls
  2. Confirms the customer verbally agrees to the charge
  3. Logs the agreement in the customer record before connecting the call

This eliminates awkward money conversations with technicians and ensures proper documentation for every emergency service.

Real-Time Technician Alerts

When an emergency call comes through, the assigned technician receives three simultaneous notifications (demonstrated at 16:45 in the video):

1. SMS with customer details - Name, address, nature of emergency, and fee agreement status

2. Calendar invite - Blocks off 2 hours for the service window with map link

3. Whisper alert - A pre-call briefing that plays before connecting to the customer

The system waits 15 minutes for confirmation. If the technician doesn't respond, it escalates to your backup contact or the business owner.

Fallback Scenarios When Unavailable

For nights when no technician is available (or if someone forgets to make the daily selection), the system has graceful fallbacks. The voice AI immediately informs callers that emergency service isn't available that night, but offers to:

  • Schedule first-morning appointment
  • Provide temporary mitigation tips (like shutting off water valves)
  • Send a callback reminder for when your team arrives

This maintains customer trust even when you can't provide immediate service.

Implementation Step-by-Step

Step 1: Create Custom Fields

In GoHighLevel, create two company-wide custom fields: "On-Call Technician" (dropdown) and "Emergency Fee Agreed" (checkbox). Add all technicians to the dropdown options.

Step 2: Build the Daily Selection Form

Create a simple form with just one field - the technician dropdown. Set it to update the "On-Call Technician" field on submission.

Step 3: Configure the Reminder Workflow

Set up a daily automation that emails your manager at 4 PM with the form link. Include a reminder that selections lock at 5 PM.

Step 4: Program Voice AI Emergency Path

In your voice AI settings, create an emergency call path that: 1) Detects emergency keywords 2) Explains the fee 3) Updates records 4) Routes to the technician.

Step 5: Setup Technician Notifications

Configure SMS, calendar, and whisper alerts to fire when the emergency call path activates.

Implementation tip: Test with fake emergency calls after hours to verify the entire chain works before going live.
