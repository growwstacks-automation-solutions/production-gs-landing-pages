Workflow Breakdown: From Telegram to LinkedIn

The complete system consists of five seamless steps that transform your rough ideas into published posts:

Step 1: Idea Capture via Telegram

Whenever inspiration strikes - during a client call, while commuting, or over coffee - you send your raw idea to a Telegram bot. These messages get stored in a Google Sheet with minimal formatting.

Step 2: AI Polish in n8n

The workflow pulls the oldest unpublished idea and sends it to an LLM with specific instructions to enhance while preserving your voice (demonstrated at 12:38).

Step 3: Automated Image Creation

A Python app using Pillow generates branded images based on the post content (shown at 15:20), incorporating your company colors and visual style.

Step 4: LinkedIn Publishing

The polished post and generated image get published to your personal profile, company page, or both - based on your settings in the Google Sheet.

Step 5: Queue Management

A Telegram reminder (set up at 22:10) notifies you when your post queue runs low, ensuring you never miss a day.

In summary: Capture ideas → AI polish → Auto-image → Scheduled posting → Low-content alerts. This creates a complete content engine that runs on autopilot while keeping you in control.

The Scheduling System That Never Runs Dry

Consistency is key on LinkedIn - but running out of content is every business owner's nightmare. This workflow includes a smart scheduling system that:

  • Pulls the oldest unpublished post each day
  • Tracks which posts have been published
  • Sends Telegram reminders when your queue drops below 2 posts (shown at 22:10)

The result? You always have content scheduled, without the stress of last-minute creation. The system even accounts for weekends and holidays by only posting on business days.
