The Manual Reposting Problem

Content curation accounts and social media managers spend up to 5 hours per week manually finding, downloading, and reposting content from blogs, news sites, and other Instagram accounts. This tedious process often leads to inconsistent posting schedules and missed opportunities when trending content appears.

The automation solution emerged when we realized RSS feeds contain all the necessary components for Instagram posts - images, captions, and links - but lacked a direct publishing method. By combining RSS feed parsing with Make.com's Instagram integration, we created a hands-off content pipeline.

Time savings: What used to take 5 hours per week now runs automatically, freeing up social media managers to focus on engagement and strategy rather than manual reposting tasks.

RSS Feed Setup

RSS.app transforms any webpage or social media account into a customizable feed. Simply paste the URL of the content source you want to follow into their generator. The platform offers powerful filtering options that ensure only relevant content gets posted to your Instagram.

Key customization options include hiding duplicate posts, excluding items containing specific keywords, and even translating content into your preferred language before it reaches Instagram. Once configured, copy your unique RSS feed URL - this becomes the content source for your Make.com automation.

Instagram Account Preparation

Instagram's API requires Business or Creator accounts for automated posting. Switch your profile type in the Edit Profile section, then connect it to your Facebook account through the Meta Account Center. This one-time setup unlocks the publishing capabilities needed for the automation.

At the 1:15 mark in the video tutorial, you'll see how to verify your account has the correct permissions. The automation won't work with personal Instagram accounts, as they lack the necessary API access for scheduled posting.

Make.com Automation Steps

Create a new scenario in Make.com with two simple modules. The first retrieves items from your RSS feed URL, while the second posts them to Instagram as photo posts. The magic happens in the field mapping between these modules.

Step 1: Configure the RSS Module

Add the "Retrieve Items" module and paste your RSS feed URL. Set how many new items to fetch each cycle (we recommend 1-3 to avoid content flooding).

Step 2: Set Up Instagram Posting

Add the "Create Photo Post" module under Instagram Business. Log in once to authenticate, then map these key fields:

  • Image: Use the "enclosure.url" from RSS (contains the image)
  • Caption: Map to the item title or description
  • Link: Include the original content URL

Pro tip: Add a filter before the Instagram module to skip items without images, preventing failed posts when the RSS feed includes text-only content.

Testing and Activation

Run a test with just 1-2 items to verify the posts appear correctly on Instagram. Check that images display properly, captions include the intended text, and links direct to the original content. The video tutorial shows exactly what to look for during this quality check.

Once confirmed, activate the scenario to run automatically every 15 minutes. Make.com will check your RSS feed at this interval, posting new items as they appear without any manual intervention needed.

Advanced Customizations

For businesses with specific content needs, add these powerful enhancements to your automation:

  • Content scheduling: Add a delay module to post at optimal engagement times
  • Branded captions: Prepend/append your hashtags or brand messaging
  • Multi-feed merging: Combine several RSS sources into one Instagram account
  • Approval workflows: Insert manual review steps before posting

These customizations require additional Make.com modules but can transform a basic reposting tool into a sophisticated content distribution system.
