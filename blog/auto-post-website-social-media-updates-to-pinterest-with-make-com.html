The Pinterest Content Problem

Pinterest remains one of the most powerful platforms for driving website traffic, with pins continuing to generate clicks months or even years after posting. But most businesses struggle with consistency - the blank pin creation screen stares back at you, demanding fresh content daily.

The solution isn't creating more original content (that's time-consuming), but intelligently repurposing content you already have. This automation solves three core problems:

Content sourcing: Finding quality content to repin takes time. This workflow automatically pulls from your own website or curated sources.

Consistency: Manual posting leads to irregular activity. Automation ensures daily pins without fail.

Time drain: The 10-15 minutes spent crafting each pin adds up. This system reduces pin creation to zero-touch.

How RSS.app Solves the Content Sourcing Challenge

RSS.app converts virtually any webpage into a standardized RSS feed that automation tools like Make.com can process. Here's why this matters:

Most websites don't provide clean RSS feeds anymore, and scraping content directly is unreliable (website layouts change frequently). RSS.app acts as a universal translator, creating a stable feed from:

  • Blog posts from your company website
  • Product pages from e-commerce stores
  • Social media profiles (Instagram, Twitter/X, etc.)
  • News sites in your industry
  • YouTube channels (posts video thumbnails and descriptions)

The process is simple: paste any URL into RSS.app, and it generates an XML feed URL you'll use in Make.com. At 1:15 in the video tutorial, you can see how to customize these feeds to exclude certain content or combine multiple sources.

Step-by-Step: Connecting RSS to Pinterest with Make.com

Make.com (formerly Integromat) serves as the bridge between your RSS feed and Pinterest. Here's how to build the automation:

Step 1: Set Up the RSS Trigger

Create a new scenario in Make.com and add the "RSS" module. Select "Watch RSS feed items" and paste your RSS.app URL. Set how many new items should be fetched each cycle (we recommend 1-3 to avoid flooding your board).

Step 2: Connect Pinterest

Add the Pinterest module and choose "Create a pin." Authenticate with your Pinterest account and select the board where pins should appear. Set "Image URL" as the source type since we're pulling images from the RSS feed.

Step 3: Map the Data Fields

This is where the magic happens. Connect elements from your RSS feed to Pinterest fields:

  • Image: Use the path "media content properties URL" (where RSS.app stores the article image)
  • Title: Map to the pin name
  • Description: Use the article summary or first paragraph
  • Link: The original article URL

Pro Tip: At 2:10 in the video, you can see how to test this mapping with a "Run once" before activating the full automation.

Advanced Content Filtering Options

Not all content from your source website may be suitable for Pinterest. RSS.app provides several filtering options to ensure only high-quality content gets posted:

Image Requirement: Automatically exclude posts that don't contain images (since pins without images perform poorly).

Keyword Filters: Block posts containing certain words or phrases that aren't relevant to your audience.

Content Translation: If reposting from foreign language sources, automatically translate to your audience's language before pinning.

Feed Bundling: Combine multiple RSS feeds into one master feed that Make.com monitors, allowing you to post content from several sources to the same board.

Important: These filters happen at the RSS.app level before content reaches Make.com, making your automation more efficient.

Testing and Activating Your Automation

Before letting your automation run continuously, follow this testing protocol:

1. Manual Test: Use "Run once" in Make.com to post one item and verify all fields mapped correctly.

2. Image Check: Confirm the pin uses the intended image from the article (sometimes websites have multiple images).

3. Link Verification: Click the pin to ensure it links to the correct URL.

4. Scheduling: Set Make.com to check your RSS feed every 15-60 minutes depending on how frequently your source publishes.

Once verified, activate the scenario. From now on, Make.com will automatically detect new content in your RSS feed and post it to Pinterest without any manual intervention.
