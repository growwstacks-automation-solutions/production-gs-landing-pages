The Calendar Invite Problem

When clients book appointments through Acuity Scheduling, they receive a confirmation email - but not an actual calendar invitation. Without that calendar event, appointments are easily forgotten. Studies show that no-show rates decrease by 25-30% when clients receive automatic calendar invites.

Manually sending calendar invites after each booking is time-consuming and prone to human error. Service businesses typically spend 2-3 hours per week just managing calendar invites - time that could be spent serving clients or growing the business.

The hidden cost: Every no-show represents lost revenue and wasted time. For a business charging $100 per session with 5 no-shows per month, that's $6,000 in lost revenue annually - all from a problem that can be solved with a simple automation.

Zapier Automation Solution

Zapier acts as the bridge between Acuity Scheduling and Google Calendar, automatically creating calendar events and sending invites whenever a new appointment is booked. This happens instantly in the background, with no manual work required from you or your team.

The automation works by triggering whenever Acuity detects a new appointment. Zapier then takes all the appointment details (time, client email, service type) and uses them to create a detailed Google Calendar event with the client automatically invited.

Key benefit: Clients receive their calendar invitation within minutes of booking, while the appointment details are still fresh in their mind. This dramatically improves attendance rates while eliminating hours of administrative work.

Step-by-Step Setup

Setting up this automation takes about 15 minutes and requires no coding. Here's exactly how to connect Acuity Scheduling to Google Calendar using Zapier:

Step 1: Create a New Zap

Log in to your Zapier account and click "Make a Zap." Name it something clear like "Acuity to Google Calendar Invites."

Step 2: Set Up the Trigger

Search for and select "Acuity Scheduling" as your trigger app. Choose "New Appointment" as the trigger event. Connect your Acuity account when prompted.

Step 3: Test the Trigger

Zapier will pull a sample appointment from Acuity. Verify it contains all the fields you need (client email, appointment time, etc.).

Step 4: Add the Action

Search for and select "Google Calendar" as your action app. Choose "Create Detailed Event" as the action event. Connect your Google account.

Step 5: Map the Fields

This is the crucial step where you connect Acuity data to calendar fields:

  • Map the appointment title to the event summary
  • Set start/end times using the Acuity appointment times
  • Add the client email to the attendees field
  • Include any relevant notes in the description

Pro tip: At 2:45 in the video tutorial, we show exactly how to map the client email field - the most important step that ensures invites get sent.

Testing Your Automation

Before turning on your Zap, thorough testing is essential. Zapier makes this easy with their test mode:

Run a test by creating a dummy appointment in Acuity. Within minutes, check your Google Calendar to verify:

  • The event appears with correct details
  • The client email shows as an attendee
  • The invitation email was sent (check your client's inbox)

If anything is incorrect, return to your Zap and adjust the field mappings. It's much easier to fix issues during testing than after the Zap is live.

Advanced Customizations

Once your basic automation is working, consider these enhancements:

  • Add buffer time: Set events to start 10 minutes early so clients aren't late
  • Include preparation instructions: Add to the description field
  • Set reminders: Configure Google Calendar to send reminders 1 day and 1 hour before
  • Add video conferencing: Automatically generate Google Meet links

These small tweaks can further improve client experience and reduce last-minute cancellations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

After implementing this for dozens of clients, we've identified these frequent pitfalls:

  • Missing client email field: The most common error - if the attendee field isn't mapped, no invite gets sent
  • Timezone mismatches: Ensure both Acuity and Google Calendar use the same timezone
  • Overwriting existing events: Be careful with update triggers that might duplicate events
  • Forgetting to turn on the Zap: It sounds obvious, but many people complete setup then forget to activate
