Healthcare Recruitment Case Study

Healthcare recruiters face a brutal challenge - they need to contact hundreds of qualified nurses quickly, but manual calling is time-consuming and inefficient. Our nursing recruitment client was making 120 calls per day with only 8 booked appointments - a 6.7% conversion rate that left most leads untouched.

After implementing this Retell AI + n8n system, they achieved:

3x more appointments booked (24/day) while reducing call time by 65%. The AI handles initial screening conversations, qualifies leads, and only transfers the hottest prospects to human recruiters.

The system works by pulling nurse leads from a Google Sheet where each row contains:

  • Name and contact information
  • Specialty and years of experience
  • Preferred shift times and locations
  • Current employment status

At 3:02 PM in the tutorial video, you'll see how the AI agent references specific details from the Sheet during calls - "Hi Sarah, I see you have 7 years of ICU experience - would you be interested in hearing about a day shift position at Toronto General paying 15% above market rate?"

Retell AI Configuration

Retell AI's conversational engine requires careful tuning to sound natural while staying on-message. These settings proved most effective for outbound healthcare recruitment calls:

Global Parameters

  • Speech responsiveness: 0.95 (allows brief natural pauses)
  • Interruption sensitivity: 0.85 (prevents cutting off leads mid-sentence)
  • Voicemail detection: "Please leave a message after the tone"
  • Max call duration: 12 minutes (prevents rambling conversations)

Agent Personality

The opening message sets the tone - we use a warm but professional female voice named "Sarah":

"Hi {first_name}, this is Sarah with Nursing Recruitment Partners. We help ICU nurses find positions that offer better pay, schedules, and growth opportunities. I came across your profile and wanted to share an opening that might interest you - do you have a quick minute to chat?"

Notice the {first_name} variable - this gets replaced with the lead's actual name from Google Sheets during each call. At 7:15 in the video, you'll see how we configure these dynamic variables in Retell's interface.

n8n Workflow Breakdown

This automation runs on a schedule (daily at 9 AM) and handles the entire call process:

Step 1: Pull Fresh Leads

The Google Sheets node queries rows where last_contact is empty, filtering for qualified leads ready to be called.

Step 2: Initiate Retell AI Call

An HTTP request node triggers Retell's API with the lead's phone number and dynamic variables mapped from the Sheet.

Step 3: Handle Call Outcomes

Based on Retell's webhook response, the workflow updates the Sheet with call results (e.g., "Not Interested", "Callback Requested", or "Meeting Booked").

Key Insight: The "Meeting Booked" trigger at 18:45 automatically sends calendar invites via Cal.com integration while texting the lead confirmation details - eliminating manual coordinator work.

Dynamic Variable Mapping

The magic happens when your AI agent references specific lead details during calls. These variables from Sheets get injected into Retell's conversation flow:

  • {first_name} - Personalizes greetings
  • {hospital} - "I see you worked at {hospital} for 3 years"
  • {shift_preference} - "This position offers {shift_preference} schedules"
  • {salary_boost} - "Pays {salary_boost} above your current rate"

At 22:10 in the tutorial, we demonstrate how the n8n "Function" node transforms raw Sheet data into conversational phrases. For example, converting "10%+" in the Sheet to "10 percent higher than market rate" in the AI's speech.

SMS Integration

Using Twilio with the same number for calls and SMS creates a seamless experience. The system sends:

  1. Pre-call text: "Hi {first_name}, this is Sarah with Nursing Recruiters. I'll be calling shortly about an opportunity that matches your {specialty} background."
  2. Post-call summary: "Thanks for chatting! As promised, here's the link to schedule your interview: {booking_link}"
  3. Follow-up: "Just checking in - did you get a chance to review the benefits package I sent?"

The n8n Twilio node at 27:35 shows how we conditionally trigger different SMS flows based on call outcomes stored in Google Sheets.

Testing & Optimization

Initial call recordings revealed three key improvement areas:

1. Timezone Awareness

Early calls at inappropriate times were marked "Do Not Call". We added timezone validation using {timezone} variables from Sheets.

2. Objection Handling

Analyzing failed calls showed 68% of rejections came from three specific objections we then programmed responses for.

3. Call Length

Calls under 90 seconds had 12% conversion vs 43% for 2.5-4 minute calls. We adjusted the AI's pacing accordingly.

Pro Tip: Use Retell's conversation analytics to identify drop-off points (shown at 32:20). Our "salary concern" detection improved bookings by 22% after refinement.
