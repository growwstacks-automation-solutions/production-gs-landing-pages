How the Automation Works

The system triggers whenever a new appointment appears in your booking platform (Google Sheets, Calendly, etc.). Make.com calculates the time remaining until the appointment and schedules two reminders:

  1. 24-hour notice - Sent the day before with preparation instructions
  2. 1-hour notice - Final reminder to prevent last-minute cancellations

At the 3:15 mark in the video, you'll see how the time difference calculation works. The magic happens through Make.com's date manipulation tools that automatically account for timezones and formatting quirks.

Setting Up Your Trigger

The foundation is connecting your appointment data source. While the video demonstrates Google Sheets (ideal for beginners), you can use any system that exports calendar data:

  • Google Sheets: Simple spreadsheet with client name, email/phone, and appointment time
  • Booking platforms: Calendly, Acuity, Square Appointments via native integrations
  • CRMs: HubSpot, Salesforce, or Zoho with scheduled exports

At 1:45 in the tutorial, watch how to configure the "Watch Rows" trigger in Make.com. This monitors your data source for new appointments and initiates the reminder sequence.

Mastering Time Calculations

The most technical part (shown at 5:30) involves calculating the hours until each appointment. Make.com handles this through:

  1. Date parsing: Converting your timestamp into a standardized format
  2. Time subtraction: Finding the difference between now and the appointment
  3. Conversion: Changing milliseconds into hours for easy conditional routing

Pro tip: Always test your time calculations with known values first. The video shows a troubleshooting moment at 7:10 where timestamp formatting caused initial errors.

Conditional Routing for Perfect Timing

At 9:45, you'll see how to create two reminder paths using routers:

  • Path 1: Triggers when the appointment is 24-48 hours away
  • Path 2: Activates when the appointment is 0.5-1 hour away

This ensures clients receive reminders at the most effective times without duplicate messages. The conditional logic uses simple numeric comparisons against your calculated time difference.

Crafting Effective Reminder Messages

The video demonstrates basic email templates at 12:20, but you can customize these extensively:

  • SMS: Short, actionable reminders with cancellation links
  • Email: Detailed instructions, preparation checklists, and directions
  • WhatsApp: Interactive messages with quick reply buttons

Personalization tokens (like {{client_name}} and {{appointment_time}}) automatically insert the correct details for each recipient.

Testing & Deployment

Before going live (14:50 in video), always:

  1. Run test scenarios with known future dates
  2. Verify timezone handling for remote clients
  3. Check spam folders for automated messages
  4. Monitor the first 5-10 real reminders closely

The system becomes completely hands-off once validated. Make.com's error handling will alert you if any reminders fail to send.
