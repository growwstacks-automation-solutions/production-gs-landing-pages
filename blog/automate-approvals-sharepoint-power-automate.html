Setting Up Your SharePoint Approval Foundation

The first step in automating approvals is creating the right structure in SharePoint. As shown at 5:18 in the tutorial, you'll create a new SharePoint list specifically for tracking approval requests. This becomes your system of record where all requests originate and where their status gets updated automatically.

Your SharePoint list needs these key columns:

  • Title: The name/description of the request (e.g., "New Laptop Request")
  • Requested By: Person or group column identifying who submitted the request
  • Approver: Person or group column for who needs to approve it
  • Status: Choice column with options like Pending, Approved, Rejected

At 7:05, the video demonstrates how to properly configure these columns to ensure your automated workflow will have all the data it needs to function correctly. The approver field is particularly important - this determines who receives the approval request automatically.

Understanding SharePoint's Built-in Approval Limitations

At 8:40, the tutorial explores SharePoint's native "Configure Approvals" feature found under the Automate menu. While this built-in option works for simple scenarios, it has critical limitations that make it unsuitable for most business processes:

  • No custom notifications or reminders
  • No ability to update multiple fields upon approval
  • No conditional logic or branching approvals
  • No integration with other systems
  • Limited reporting and audit capabilities

This is why building your approval process in Power Automate provides so much more value. As shown at 10:12, Power Automate gives you complete control over every aspect of the approval workflow, from who gets notified to what happens after they respond.

Key Insight: SharePoint's built-in approvals handle about 20% of real-world approval scenarios. Power Automate workflows cover 100% - with room to grow as your processes evolve.

Building the Power Automate Approval Flow

Starting at 11:30 in the video, we begin constructing the actual Power Automate flow that will handle our approvals automatically. The process begins with a simple but powerful trigger: "When an item is created" in your SharePoint list.

Here's the basic flow structure:

  1. Trigger: New item created in SharePoint approval list
  2. Approval Action: Send approval to the person specified in the Approver field
  3. Condition: Check if the response was Approve or Reject
  4. Update SharePoint: Change status based on the decision

At 13:05, the tutorial shows how to properly configure the SharePoint connection and select your specific list. This ensures the flow only runs for items in your approval system, not other lists in the same site.

Configuring the Approval Step

The heart of the workflow happens at 15:20 when we add the "Start and wait for an approval" action. This powerful step does three things simultaneously:

  1. Sends the approval request via email and/or Teams
  2. Pauses the entire workflow until a response is received
  3. Records the decision (approve/reject) and any comments

Critical configuration details shown at 16:45:

  • Use dynamic content for the "Assign To" field (pull from SharePoint's Approver column)
  • Include the request title and requester in the approval details
  • Set a clear action statement like "Please review and approve/reject by [date]"

The video demonstrates how approvers will see a clean, professional approval request with clear action buttons - a huge improvement over parsing email threads.

Handling Approval and Rejection Decisions

At 19:30, we reach a crucial part of the workflow - handling the approver's decision. This requires adding a Condition action that checks whether the approval response was "Approve" or "Reject." The flow then branches in two directions:

Approved Path:

  • Update SharePoint item status to "Approved"
  • Optionally add approval date/time and approver name
  • Can trigger additional actions like sending confirmation emails

Rejected Path:

  • Update SharePoint item status to "Rejected"
  • Optionally include rejection comments in the status
  • Can notify requester with reason for rejection

The tutorial at 21:10 shows how to configure the SharePoint "Update item" action properly, ensuring you modify the correct record by using the trigger ID field.

Testing Your Automated Approval Workflow

At 23:50, we see the exciting moment where the entire workflow gets tested end-to-end:

  1. Create a new item in the SharePoint approval list
  2. Watch as Power Automate instantly triggers the flow
  3. Receive the approval email as the designated approver
  4. Click Approve or Reject and see SharePoint update automatically

The video demonstrates how refreshing the SharePoint list shows the status changing in real-time after the approval decision is made - no manual updates required.

Pro Tip: Test with two browser windows open side-by-side - one showing the SharePoint list and one where you respond to the approval email. You'll see the status change within seconds of clicking Approve or Reject.
