Why ChatGPT Projects Transform Campaign Creation

Marketers know the frustration of reinventing the wheel with each campaign. You start fresh every time, pasting brand guidelines into each new ChatGPT session, hoping the AI remembers your tone from last time. By the third email, consistency drifts, and by week's end, your campaign feels disjointed.

ChatGPT projects solve this by creating persistent workspaces for each campaign. For our Black Friday example, we set up a "Nailed It Hardware Black Friday" project containing all brand files, promotional calendars, and email templates. The AI references this context for every interaction, maintaining perfect voice consistency across all seven days of emails.

Campaigns generated in ChatGPT projects see 23% higher open rates compared to one-off prompts because the AI maintains deeper context about your brand, audience, and campaign goals throughout the entire workflow.

Preparing Your Brand Files for AI Consistency

The secret to AI-generated content that actually sounds like your brand? Treat ChatGPT like a new hire who needs thorough onboarding. We prepare five key text files before creating our project:

  1. Brand Story - The origin and personality of your business
  2. Mission Statement - Your core purpose and values
  3. Campaign Guidelines - Promotional calendar and daily themes
  4. Voice & Tone - Writing style dos and don'ts
  5. Persona Instructions - How ChatGPT should "act" when writing

Uploading these to the project means any team member can generate on-brand content without retraining the AI each time. At 4:23 in the video, you'll see how we test ChatGPT's understanding by asking it to summarize our brand - a crucial verification step before generating content.

Generating 7 Days of Email Copy in One Session

With our brand files loaded, creating the actual email content becomes surprisingly efficient. For our hardware store's Black Friday week, we prompt ChatGPT to generate:

  • Kickoff Email (Nov 23): 50% off all tools
  • Mega Monday (Nov 24): Power tool specials
  • Toolbox Tuesday (Nov 25): Hand tool discounts
  • Workshop Wednesday (Nov 26): DIY class promotions
  • Thankful Thursday (Nov 27): Loyalty rewards
  • Blowout Friday (Nov 28): Doorbuster deals
  • Small Business Saturday (Nov 29): Local partnerships

The key is iterative refinement. We generate all emails in one session so ChatGPT maintains context between days. When we notice unwanted emojis in the first draft (a brand no-no), we provide that feedback once, and ChatGPT applies it to all subsequent emails automatically.

Structuring Your Campaign in Google Sheets

The bridge between ChatGPT and Make.com is a well-organized Google Sheet. We structure ours with:

  • Column A: Send Date (formatted as YYYY-MM-DD)
  • Column B: Email Subject Line
  • Column C: Full Email Body (HTML supported)
  • Column D: Target Segment (Optional)

This simple structure allows Make.com to treat each row as a complete email package. At 12:17 in the tutorial, you'll see how we export the ChatGPT content directly into this format, creating a campaign management system that's both human-readable and automation-friendly.

Pro Tip: Add a "Test Mode" column that toggles between real customers and your team's email addresses for safe campaign testing before going live.

Make.com Integration: Connecting the Dots

Make.com becomes the campaign conductor, orchestrating three key systems:

  1. Google Sheets: Reads our email content and scheduling
  2. CRM/ESP: Pulls customer lists and segments
  3. Gmail/GSuite: Handles actual email sending

The magic happens in the filters. We set up Make.com to only process rows where the send date equals today's date, ensuring each email goes out precisely when intended. Automated Scheduling & Send Date Logic

Timing is everything in email campaigns. Our Make.com scenario includes these smart scheduling features:

  • Time Zone Awareness: Sends at 7 AM in each recipient's local time
  • Day-of-Week Logic: Skips Thanksgiving Day automatically
  • Send Limits: Caps at 500 emails/hour to avoid spam filters

These rules are configured once in Make.com, then work automatically for every campaign going forward. The system even handles date formatting conversions between Google Sheets' display format and the technical format required by email APIs.
