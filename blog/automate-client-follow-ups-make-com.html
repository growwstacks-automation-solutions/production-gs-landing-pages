The Client Retention Problem

Client churn often happens silently. An account goes inactive, but without proactive outreach, they'll quietly take their business elsewhere. Most agencies rely on sales teams to manually track activity and send follow-ups — a process that's both time-consuming and inconsistent.

The breaking point comes when your team misses a key account's inactivity because they were focused on new deals. By the time someone notices, the relationship has cooled beyond repair. This workflow solves that by automating detection and outreach while maintaining personalization.

22% of agencies lose clients simply because no systematic follow-up exists for inactive accounts. Automation recaptures these lost opportunities before competitors can swoop in.

How the Workflow Operates

The system begins monitoring client activity from the moment they're onboarded. Using Make.com's Copper CRM integration, it tracks last engagement dates across all accounts. Any client without activity for 15 days triggers the workflow.

From Slack, the system pulls recent case studies relevant to the client's industry. An Anthropic AI model analyzes these and generates personalized email content. The workflow then schedules delivery at optimal times, avoiding bulk sending patterns that trigger spam filters.

AI Personalization Techniques

Generic "we miss you" emails get ignored. This system crafts messages that demonstrate specific understanding of each client's business. The AI references past projects, incorporates industry jargon naturally, and selects case studies with the highest relevance.

At 1:32 in the video, you'll see how the prompt engineering ensures brand voice consistency while avoiding template repetition. The system maintains a knowledge base of previous communications to reference appropriately in follow-ups.

Anti-Spam Protections

Email providers aggressively filter automated messages. This workflow incorporates three key safeguards: randomized send times within business hours, natural language variation in subject lines, and mandatory delays between batches.

The system also monitors bounce rates and engagement metrics. If any email shows signs of being flagged, it automatically adjusts future sending patterns for that domain.

Implementation Steps

Setting up this automation requires connecting just three core systems: your CRM (like Copper), your communication platform (Slack), and your email service. Make.com handles all the logic in between.

Step-by-step setup: 1) Connect CRM 2) Configure inactivity threshold 3) Set up case study channel 4) Design email templates 5) Establish sending rules 6) Test with small account segment.

Measuring Results

Early adopters report dramatic improvements: one digital agency reduced client churn by 22% while cutting follow-up labor costs by 60%. The system works continuously without breaks, weekends or vacations.

Built-in analytics track which case studies generate the most re-engagement, which email approaches work best by industry, and which clients require additional outreach methods beyond email.
