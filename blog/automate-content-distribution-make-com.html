The Content Distribution Problem

Marketing teams create valuable content - whitepapers, case studies, product updates - only to see it languish because distribution becomes a bottleneck. Manually sending each email with attachments requires:

  • Downloading the latest file version
  • Updating contact spreadsheets
  • Personalizing each message
  • Attaching files individually
  • Tracking who received what

This process often takes 5-10 minutes per contact. For a list of 100 people, that's 8-16 hours of repetitive work per campaign - time that could be spent creating more content or analyzing results.

68% of marketers say manual content distribution prevents them from sending updates as frequently as they should, according to Content Marketing Institute research.

How the Automation Works

This Make.com workflow eliminates the manual grind by automating the entire content distribution process from file selection to personalized delivery. The system:

  1. Automatically fetches the newest file from your Google Drive folder
  2. Pulls your current contact list from Google Sheets
  3. Generates personalized emails for each recipient
  4. Sends messages with attachments or Drive links
  5. Runs on your schedule (daily/weekly/monthly)

At 1:15 in the video demo, you can see how the automation checks Google Drive for the most recent file, ensuring contacts always receive your latest content without manual version checks.

Step 1: Fetching Your Latest Content

The automation begins by connecting to your designated Google Drive folder where you store content for distribution. Instead of manually selecting files, the workflow:

  • Sorts files by "Last Modified" date
  • Selects the most recent document
  • Passes it forward in the workflow

This ensures your audience always receives the current version of your content without requiring someone to manually check and attach files. The system can handle PDFs, Word docs, PowerPoint files, and most other common formats.

Pro Tip: Name your files consistently (e.g. "2025-10-Content-Update.pdf") to make version tracking even clearer in your Drive history.

Step 2: Processing Your Contact List

Your contact list lives in Google Sheets, where you can easily add, remove, or segment recipients. The automation:

  1. Connects to your specified Google Sheet
  2. Processes each row as a separate contact
  3. Extracts name, email, and any custom fields
  4. Filters contacts based on your criteria

As shown at 1:45 in the video, you can add filters to only send to specific segments - like customers who haven't received an update in 30 days or those in particular industries. This prevents over-emailing while ensuring relevant distribution.

Step 3: Sending Personalized Emails

The final step creates and sends personalized emails through Gmail. For each contact, the automation:

  • Inserts their name and other details into your template
  • Attaches the content file (or adds a Drive link)
  • Sends from your branded email address
  • Optionally CCs team members for visibility

At 2:20 in the demo, you'll see how the system dynamically inserts contact details while maintaining your professional email formatting. The result looks hand-crafted but requires zero manual effort per recipient.

Attachment vs Link Options

The workflow offers two content delivery methods through a simple router:

Attachments

Best for: Small files under 25MB

Benefits:

  • Higher open rates (20-30% better)
  • Content stays with the email
  • No extra clicks required

Drive Links

Best for: Large files or frequent updates

Benefits:

  • No size limitations
  • Single source of truth
  • View tracking available

You can configure different rules for different content types - sending small PDFs as attachments while linking to large video files, for example.
