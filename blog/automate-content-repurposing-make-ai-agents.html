Step 1: Setting Up Your OpenAI Connection

Every AI Agent needs a brain - in this case, we're using OpenAI's models through their API. At 6:15 in the video, you'll see how to:

  1. Visit platform.openai.com (separate from your ChatGPT account)
  2. Create a new API key under "API Keys"
  3. Set up billing (API usage typically costs $10-20/month for this workflow)

This key lets your Make Agent access GPT-4 or other models to analyze and rewrite your content. The creator recommends GPT-3.5-turbo for this workflow - it's fast, affordable, and produces excellent results for structured content repurposing.

Pro Tip: Always name your API keys descriptively (e.g., "Make-Content-Repurposing"). When you eventually have multiple automations, this helps track which key is used where for security and billing purposes.

Step 2: Creating Your First AI Agent

In Make.com, navigate to "AI Agents" and click "Create Agent." You'll need to:

  1. Name your agent descriptively (e.g., "Script Repurposing Agent")
  2. Select OpenAI as your AI provider
  3. Paste your API key when prompted
  4. Choose GPT-3.5-turbo as your model (optimal balance of cost/speed/quality)

The magic happens in the System Prompt - this tells the AI exactly what to do with your content. We'll dive deep into crafting this in the next section. For now, know that this is where you define:

  • The input format (Google Docs scripts)
  • The transformation rules (YouTube Short structure, LinkedIn post style)
  • The output destinations (new Google Doc, LinkedIn post, Slack notification)

Crafting the Perfect System Prompt

Your System Prompt is the instruction manual for your AI Agent. The example shown at 11:30 includes:

You are a Script Repurposing Agent. Your goal is to transform YouTube scripts from Google Docs into: 1. 60-second YouTube Short scripts with:    - Strong hook in first 3 seconds    - 3 quick steps/points    - Clear CTA 2. LinkedIn posts that:    - Start with engaging question    - Include 1 key insight    - End with discussion prompt After creating these, ping me on Slack and post the LinkedIn content automatically.

This level of specificity ensures consistent output quality. Notice how it defines not just what to create, but the structural requirements for each format. The creator emphasizes using the "Improve" button in Make - it analyzes your prompt and suggests enhancements for clarity and effectiveness.

Building Your Brand Voice Context Document

At 15:45, the tutorial shows a game-changing technique: creating a style guide document using ChatGPT. By feeding it your existing content, ChatGPT can analyze and document your:

  • Typical sentence length and structure
  • Word choice preferences
  • Tone (conversational, authoritative, etc.)
  • Common phrases and transitions

Upload this document in the "Context" section of your Make AI Agent. Now when repurposing content, the AI will mimic your natural voice rather than generating generic text. This is especially powerful for teams - new writers can reference the same document to maintain brand consistency.

Real-world result: After implementing this, one creator saw their LinkedIn engagement rise 37% because the automated posts sounded more authentically "them" than their previous manual attempts.

Connecting Google Docs, LinkedIn & Slack

The real power comes from connecting your tools. At 22:10, you'll see how to:

  1. Set up Google Drive monitoring: Create a trigger that detects new scripts in your designated folder
  2. Build LinkedIn posting: Configure the agent to publish directly to your company page

Make's visual interface makes this surprisingly simple - just search for each app and follow the connection prompts. The key is organizing your Google Drive with separate folders for source scripts and AI-generated content to prevent infinite loops.
