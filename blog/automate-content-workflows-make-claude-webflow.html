The Content Bottleneck Nobody Talks About

Publishing content is only half the battle. The real time sink comes after hitting "publish" - generating FAQs for SEO, proofreading for errors, updating internal systems, and coordinating these tasks across teams. Most content creators spend 30-60 minutes per article on these repetitive post-publishing tasks.

This bottleneck becomes especially painful when scaling content production. At 5 articles per week, that's 4-5 hours wasted on administrative work rather than creating new content. The solution? Automating the entire post-publishing workflow using Make.com, Claude AI, and Webflow.

Content teams waste 20-30% of their time on post-publishing tasks that could be automated. This workflow recaptures that time while improving consistency and reducing human error.

How Make.com Solves the Post-Publishing Grind

Make.com (formerly Integromat) acts as the central nervous system of this automation. For just $10/month, you get 10,000 operations - more than enough for most content teams. The platform connects to Webflow's API and monitors for new publications.

When you publish an article in Webflow, Make.com immediately springs into action. It extracts the article content, then splits the workflow into two parallel processes: FAQ generation and spell checking. This happens automatically, without any manual intervention required from your team.

Claude AI FAQ Generation in Action

The FAQ generation arm of the workflow sends your article content to Claude AI with specific instructions. Claude analyzes the text and generates three natural, SEO-optimized questions that readers might have about the topic. These aren't generic questions - they're tailored to your specific content.

Claude returns the FAQs in JSON format, which Make.com then converts to plain text using a simple pre-built module (no coding required). The formatted FAQs are automatically pushed back to your Webflow CMS as draft content, ready for your review. As shown at 2:15 in the video, this entire process happens in seconds.

Automated FAQ generation improves SEO by ensuring schema markup is always populated with relevant questions. Google uses this data to better understand and rank your content.

Automated Spell Checking & Quality Control

The second parallel process handles quality control. Claude AI scans your published content for spelling errors, grammatical issues, and stylistic inconsistencies. Unlike basic spell checkers, Claude understands context - it won't flag industry terms or brand names as errors.

Any potential issues are compiled into a report and sent to your Slack channel. This gives your team visibility into content quality without requiring manual proofreading. The system could even be extended to automatically correct minor issues while flagging only substantive changes for human review.

Real-Time Slack Notifications

Transparency is key in automated workflows. Every action - from FAQ generation to spell check completion - triggers a detailed Slack notification. These messages include:

  • The three generated FAQs (with links to edit them in Webflow)
  • Any spelling/grammar issues detected
  • Confirmation that updates were pushed to your CMS

This means your team stays informed without needing to constantly check multiple systems. At a glance, you can see everything the automation has handled since your last publication.

The Scaling Benefits for Content Teams

This workflow demonstrates how automation can transform content operations. For a team publishing 20 articles per month, that's 15-30 hours saved - nearly an entire work week reclaimed. But the benefits multiply as you scale:

  • Consistency: Every article gets the same post-publishing treatment
  • Quality: AI proofreading catches errors humans might miss
  • Speed: FAQs are ready immediately after publication
  • Visibility: Slack notifications keep everyone informed

The same principles can be applied to social media posting, email newsletters, and content repurposing - creating a comprehensive automation strategy for your entire content operation.
