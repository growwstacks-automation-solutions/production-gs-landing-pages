How the Automation Works

The system follows a simple but powerful three-step process that runs completely autonomously once set up. Each morning at your specified time (9 AM by default), Make.com triggers the sequence that delivers inspiration to your network.

First, it searches your Airtable base for the next "to do" quote. Then it sends that quote via email to your designated recipients. Finally, it marks that quote as "done" in Airtable so it won't repeat. The entire process happens without any manual intervention, yet feels personal to recipients.

Setting Up Your Airtable Base

Your quote database is the foundation of this automation. As shown at 0:45 in the video, the Airtable base needs just three essential columns: Quote (text), Author (text), and Status (single select with "to do" and "done" options). This simple structure gives you everything needed to power the automation.

You can enhance your base with additional fields like categories, languages (notice the Norwegian quote mentioned at 1:00), or sentiment tags. These optional fields let you create more sophisticated filtering logic later. Start simple, then expand as needed.

Configuring the Make.com Workflow

The Make.com workflow consists of three key modules that mirror the automation's three-step process. At 1:05 in the video, you can see these modules in action: the Airtable search, email send, and Airtable update.

The Airtable search module looks for records where status equals "to do," sorted by creation date. The email module uses the quote and author fields to compose the message. Finally, the update module changes the status to "done" so the quote won't repeat. Each module takes just minutes to configure.

Pro Tip: Add a delay module if you want quotes to send at a specific time each day rather than immediately when the automation runs.

Personalization Options

While the basic automation sends the same quote to all recipients, you can easily add personalization. The video mentions at 1:20 how you could filter quotes by language - sending English quotes to some contacts and Norwegian to others.

More advanced personalization could include: adding the recipient's name to the email subject or body, selecting quotes based on the recipient's interests (if stored in Airtable), or varying send times based on the recipient's timezone. The possibilities are endless with Make.com's flexible modules.

Scaling to Multiple Recipients

The demo shows sending to a fixed list of emails, but the system easily scales to larger audiences. You can store recipient emails in Airtable with preferences or categories, then use Make.com's iterator to send personalized versions to each contact.

For very large lists, consider adding rate limiting to stay within email service limits. You could also implement A/B testing by sending different quotes to segments of your list and tracking open rates back to Airtable for optimization.

Alternative Use Cases
