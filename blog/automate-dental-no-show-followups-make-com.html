The $200,000 Problem Most Dentists Ignore

Dental practices lose an average of 15-20% of scheduled production to no-shows - that's $150,000-$200,000 annually for a $1M practice. Even worse, 42% of no-shows never reschedule when not contacted promptly, according to Dental Economics research.

The traditional solution - having front desk staff manually call and email - creates three problems: 1) It's time-consuming (5-7 minutes per patient), 2) Follow-ups get delayed during busy periods, and 3) Staff often use inconsistent messaging that doesn't reflect your practice's brand.

Key insight: Patients contacted within 30 minutes of a missed appointment are 47% more likely to reschedule than those contacted the next day. Automation ensures immediate follow-up every single time.

How the No-Show Automation Works

This Make.com workflow creates a seamless connection between your front desk's Google Sheet and your practice's Gmail account. When a patient doesn't show up:

  1. Front desk enters the patient's email in Column A
  2. Leaves Column B blank (the "signal" column)
  3. Make.com detects the new entry
  4. Sends your pre-written email template
  5. Marks the row as "sent" in Column B

The entire process happens in seconds, with zero manual effort beyond the initial email entry. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you can see the exact moment the test email sends after marking a no-show.

Step 1: Setting Up Google Sheets

Your spreadsheet needs just two columns: "Email" (Column A) and "Signal" (Column B). The automation will only act on rows where:

  • Column A (Email) contains an address
  • Column B (Signal) is blank

This simple structure means your staff doesn't need to learn any new software - they just enter emails in the same spreadsheet they already use for tracking appointments. At 3:45 in the video, you'll see how the filter criteria ensures only new no-shows trigger emails.

Step 2: Configuring Make.com

After creating a free Make.com account, you'll set up a new "scenario" with these modules:

Module 1: Google Sheets - Search Rows

This checks your spreadsheet every 15 minutes (or on demand) for new no-shows. Key settings:

  • Spreadsheet ID: The name from your Google Sheets URL
  • Sheet name: Typically "Sheet1"
  • Filter: "Signal does not exist AND Email exists"

At 5:20 in the tutorial, watch how running this module tests the connection and shows which rows meet the criteria.

Step 3: Connecting Gmail

The second module sends emails through your practice's Gmail account (or Outlook if preferred):

Module 2: Gmail - Send Email

Configuration options:

  • To: Use the "Email" variable from Google Sheets
  • Subject: Professional but friendly (e.g., "We missed you today!")
  • Body: Your customized template (see examples below)

Pro tip: Include a direct booking link in your email template. Practices that do this see 2-3x higher rescheduling rates compared to generic "call us" requests.

Step 4: Testing & Refining

Before going live, test with your team's emails:

  1. Add test emails to your sheet (leave Signal blank)
  2. Run the scenario manually
  3. Verify emails arrive as expected
  4. Check that Signal column updates to "sent"
