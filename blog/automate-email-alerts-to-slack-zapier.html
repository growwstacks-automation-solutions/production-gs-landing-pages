How Zapier Solves This in 4 Simple Steps

Zapier creates automated workflows (called Zaps) that connect your apps and services. For our email-to-Slack alert, the entire setup takes just four steps that anyone can follow - no technical skills required:

Step 1: Create a New Zap

Start by clicking "Create Zap" in your Zapier dashboard. This begins a new automation workflow that we'll build step by step.

Step 2: Set Up the Trigger

Choose Gmail as your trigger app and select "New Email Matching Search" as the trigger event. This tells Zapier to watch for specific emails.

Step 3: Configure the Action

Select Slack as your action app and choose "Send Direct Message" as the action. This determines what happens when your trigger email arrives.

Step 4: Test and Activate

Zapier will guide you through testing the connection between Gmail and Slack before turning on your automation permanently.

In summary: Create Zap → Set email trigger → Configure Slack action → Test workflow. The entire process takes less than 4 minutes for basic alerts.

Setting Up Your Email Trigger

The trigger is the event that starts your Zap. For email alerts, we'll use Gmail's search functionality to identify specific messages worth alerting your team about.

After selecting Gmail as your trigger app and connecting your account (1:15 in the video), you'll configure the search criteria. The most common options include:

  • Subject line keywords (e.g., "urgent" or "client request")
  • Specific senders (from:important@client.com)
  • Labels or categories (label:follow-up)
  • Attachment presence (has:attachment)

In our example workflow, we're watching for emails with "Zapia video" in the subject line. You can make your search as broad or specific as needed for your use case.

Configuring the Slack Action

Once your trigger is set, it's time to tell Zapier what to do when that email arrives. The action step sends a customized message to your Slack channel or team members.

After connecting your Slack account (2:30 in the video), you'll configure:

  • Recipient: Choose a channel (@channel) or specific user (@username)
  • Message content: Include email details like subject, sender, or snippets
  • Formatting: Add emoji, links, or attachments as needed

The video shows how to create a simple message like "Hey team, we received an email about Zapia video" that appears instantly in Slack when the matching email arrives.

Pro tip: Use Zapier's "Find Text" tool to extract specific information from the email body and include it in your Slack alert for context.

Testing and Refining Your Workflow

Before activating your Zap permanently, Zapier guides you through a test to ensure everything works as expected (3:45 in the video). This crucial step helps you:

  • Verify the trigger correctly identifies your target emails
  • Confirm the Slack message contains all needed information
  • Adjust formatting or content before going live

If the test fails, Zapier provides clear troubleshooting steps. Once successful, simply toggle your Zap to "On" and it will run automatically moving forward.

The video demonstrates checking Slack to confirm the test message was received exactly as configured - proving your automation is ready for real-world use.

Advanced Options for Power Users

While our basic alert takes just minutes to set up, Zapier offers powerful advanced features for more sophisticated workflows:

  • Multi-step Zaps: Add additional actions like creating Trello cards or logging to Google Sheets

  • Delay: Wait before sending the Slack alert (useful for after-hours emails)
  • Custom formatting: Use Slack's block kit builder for rich, interactive messages
