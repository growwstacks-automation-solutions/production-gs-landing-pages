The Email Automation Problem

Every sales team and business owner knows the frustration: personalized emails get 3-5x better response rates than generic templates, but crafting truly custom messages for each lead takes hours of research and writing. At 5-10 minutes per email, a 100-lead campaign becomes an all-day project.

Most automation tools solve the wrong problem - they help you send more emails faster, but still sound robotic and impersonal. The breakthrough comes when you combine Make.com's automation power with AI's ability to research and write personalized content at scale.

Key stat: Businesses using this automated personalization approach report 42% higher open rates and 28% more replies compared to template-based email automation.

How the Workflow Works

The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence: research → personalize → send. For each lead in your spreadsheet, the system first gathers relevant information about their location and industry, then uses those details to craft a genuinely personalized email.

Here's the complete flow:

  1. Pull lead data from your Google Sheet (name, industry, location)
  2. Research interesting facts about their location/industry using Perplexity AI
  3. Generate a personalized email incorporating these details with GPT
  4. Format the email as HTML and send via Gmail
  5. Log all activity back to your spreadsheet

The magic happens in steps 2 and 3 - where AI does the heavy lifting of personalization that would normally take you hours per lead.

Step 1: Set Up Your Lead Data

Your automation starts with a simple Google Sheet containing lead information. At minimum, you'll want:

  • First name (essential for personalization)
  • Industry (for relevant messaging)
  • Location (city/state/country for local references)
  • Email address (for sending)

In Make.com, you'll create a "Search Rows" module connected to this spreadsheet. This pulls one lead at a time for processing (you can adjust batch size later). The module extracts key variables like first name, industry, and location to use in subsequent steps.

Pro tip: Start with a small test batch of 5-10 leads to refine your workflow before scaling up. This helps catch any formatting issues early.

Step 2: Research with Perplexity AI

This is where the personalization magic begins. The workflow sends the lead's location and industry to Perplexity AI with a prompt like:

"Research some interesting facts about [location] and appreciated facts about the [industry] industry."

Perplexity returns 3-5 relevant facts that make your email stand out. For example, for a software executive in Ireland, it might find:

  • Ireland's nickname as the "Emerald Isle"
  • Notable software companies based in Dublin
  • Recent tech industry growth statistics

These authentic details transform your email from generic to genuinely personalized - without you spending hours on research.

Step 3: Generate Personalized Emails

With research complete, the workflow passes all details to GPT with instructions to craft a friendly, personalized email. A well-structured prompt ensures natural-sounding output:

"Create a cold email for [first name]. After greeting, include 1-2 interesting facts about [location] and appreciate their work in [industry]. Keep tone conversational like a friend, not too formal. Use only the best facts from this research: [Perplexity output]."

The AI generates emails like this example:

"Hi [Name], hope you're doing great! I've always been fascinated by Ireland - did you know it's called the Emerald Isle because of its lush green landscapes? As someone in software, you're part of an industry that's grown 40% in Dublin just last year. I'd love to connect and learn more about..."

This approach creates emails that feel hand-written, not automated.

Step 4: Automate Email Sending

The final steps format and send the email automatically:

  1. Convert to HTML: A second GPT module transforms the plain text email into properly formatted HTML (preserving line breaks and structure)
  2. Send via Gmail: The Make.com Gmail module sends the email with your chosen subject line (e.g., "Hi [First Name] - Quick Question")
  3. Log activity: The system records sent emails back to your spreadsheet for tracking
