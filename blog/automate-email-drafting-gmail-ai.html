How AI Email Drafting Works

The system operates on a simple but powerful four-step process that mimics how you would manually respond to emails, just much faster:

Step 1: Listen for New Emails

The workflow monitors your Gmail inbox for new messages, specifically targeting unread emails that match your predefined filters.

Step 2: Apply Smart Filters

Before drafting a response, the system evaluates the sender, subject line, and content to determine the appropriate response strategy.

Step 3: Generate AI Draft

Using ChatGPT's API, the workflow analyzes the email content and generates a professional, context-aware response in your preferred style.

Step 4: Create Draft Reply

The system composes a draft email with the AI-generated response, including the original message for context, and places it in your drafts folder for review.

In summary: The workflow listens for new emails, filters them intelligently, generates professional responses using AI, and creates draft replies for your review - cutting response time from minutes to seconds.

Setting Up Smart Email Filters

The secret to effective email automation lies in precise filtering. You don't want the system drafting responses to newsletters, spam, or highly sensitive messages.

We recommend creating three filter tiers:

  • Priority Contacts: Clients, partners, and key colleagues who always get fast responses
  • General Inquiries: Common questions that follow predictable patterns
  • Exclusions: Senders and subjects that should never trigger auto-responses

At 2:30 in the video, you'll see how to configure these filters in Make.com to ensure only appropriate emails trigger the drafting workflow.

Configuring AI Responses

The magic happens in the AI prompt engineering. We configure ChatGPT to act as your "helpful assistant" with specific instructions:

  • Maintain your professional tone and brand voice
  • Keep responses concise but thorough
  • Reference specific details from the original email
  • Use markdown formatting for clean presentation

The system passes the entire email thread to the AI for context, ensuring each draft feels personalized rather than generic. You can provide sample responses to train the AI on your preferred style.

Testing and Refining the Workflow

Before going live, we recommend a two-phase testing process:

  1. Internal Testing: Send test emails from different accounts to verify filtering and response quality
  2. Shadow Mode: Run the workflow in parallel with your normal routine for a week, comparing AI drafts to your manual responses

During testing, you'll identify opportunities to refine the AI prompts and filter criteria. Most users achieve 85% accuracy after just 3-5 days of adjustments.
