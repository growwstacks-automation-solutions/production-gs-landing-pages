How the Automation Works

This Make.com workflow acts as a bridge between your Facebook ads and email system. When a new lead comes in from your ads, Make.com instantly:

  1. Captures the lead data (name, email, phone, etc.)
  2. Stores it in your chosen database (Airtable, Google Sheets, etc.)
  3. Generates a personalized email using your template
  4. Sends the email through your connected service (Outlook, Gmail, etc.)
  5. Updates the database with the sent status

The entire process happens in under two minutes, with zero manual intervention. As shown at 3:40 in the video, you can even use HTML templates to create beautifully formatted emails that maintain proper spacing and styling.

Setting Up Your Database

Your database is the foundation of this automation. Whether you choose Airtable, Google Sheets, or another system, you'll need these core fields:

  • Lead name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Lead source (which Facebook ad)
  • Email template selection
  • Email sent status
  • Timestamp

The beauty of this system is its modularity. At 1:55 in the tutorial, you'll see how easily you can switch between Airtable and Google Sheets without changing the core automation logic. This future-proofs your workflow as your business grows.

Creating Email Templates

Your email templates should balance personalization with automation efficiency. Include merge fields for the lead's name and other key details, but keep the core message consistent.

For Outlook integration (shown at 4:20 in the video), you'll need to:

  1. Write your template in HTML format
  2. Include placeholders for dynamic fields (e.g., {{first_name}})
  3. Test the spacing and formatting
  4. Add error handling for missing data

Pro tip: Create multiple templates for different ad campaigns or lead types, then have Make.com select the appropriate one based on the lead source field.

Make.com Configuration

The Make.com scenario connects all the pieces. You'll set up these modules in sequence:

  1. Trigger: New record in your database
  2. Filter: Only proceed if email hasn't been sent
  3. Template processing: Insert lead data into your email template
  4. Email sending: Connect to your email service (Outlook shown at 5:10)
  5. Status update: Mark record as "email sent" in your database

The entire scenario takes about 15 minutes to configure once you understand the flow. The video demonstrates each step clearly, with special attention to the Outlook API requirements at 6:30.

Testing and Debugging

Before going live, test your automation with these steps:

  1. Create test records in your database
  2. Run the scenario manually in Make.com
  3. Verify the email content and formatting
  4. Check the database status update
  5. Monitor for any errors in the Make.com logs

At 7:45 in the tutorial, you'll see how to handle common issues like HTML formatting errors in Outlook or missing data fields. The key is building robust error handling from the start.
