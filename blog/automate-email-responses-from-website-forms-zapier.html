What You Need to Get Started

Setting up automated form responses requires just three simple components that most businesses already have:

  1. A website with a form - This could be Squarespace (as shown in our tutorial), WordPress, Wix, Webflow, or any other platform with form functionality
  2. An email account - Gmail works perfectly (and is what we'll demonstrate with), but Outlook, Yahoo Mail, or any SMTP-compatible email service will work
  3. A free Zapier account - Zapier is the automation platform that connects your form to your email

The entire setup takes less than 15 minutes and requires no coding or technical expertise. At 3:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see just how simple the initial Zapier setup is.

Setting Up the Zapier Trigger

The first step is creating what Zapier calls a "Zap" - their term for an automation workflow. This will tell Zapier to watch for new form submissions on your website.

Here's how to set up the trigger:

Step 1: Create a new Zap

After logging into Zapier, click "Create Zap" in the top navigation. This starts a new automation workflow.

Step 2: Set the trigger

Search for and select your website platform (like Squarespace Forms). Choose "New Form Submission" as the trigger event.

Step 3: Connect your account

Follow Zapier's prompts to connect your website account. For Squarespace, this involves getting an API key from your site settings (shown at 4:50 in the video).

Pro Tip: If you're not sure what your form is called in your website platform, check the form settings. In Squarespace, you can rename forms to make them easier to identify in Zapier.

Connecting Your Website Form

Once your website account is connected, Zapier will show you all the forms available on your site. This is where you'll select the specific contact form you want to automate responses for.

Key points when connecting your form:

  • Select the correct form from the dropdown list
  • Test the connection with a sample submission to ensure Zapier can see your form data
  • Continue to the action step once you've confirmed the connection works

At 6:20 in the video, you'll see how to test that Zapier is properly receiving form submission data before moving forward with the automation setup.

Configuring the Email Action

Now comes the powerful part - setting up the automated email response. This is where you'll connect your email account and craft the message that will be sent automatically to everyone who submits your form.

Step 1: Choose your email service

Select your email provider (like Gmail) and choose "Send Email" as the action.

Step 2: Connect your email account

Authorize Zapier to access your email account following the on-screen prompts.

Step 3: Set up the email template

This is where you'll configure:

  • Who the email goes to (typically the email address from the form)
  • The "From" name and address
  • The email subject line
  • The body content of your response

At 8:45 in the video, you'll see how to pull information from the form submission into your email template for personalization.

Personalizing Your Response

The magic of this automation comes from personalizing each response using data from the form submission. Here's how to make your automated emails feel hand-crafted:

Include the sender's name

Reference the form submitter's name in your greeting ("Hi [First Name]") by pulling it from the form fields.

Reference specific form answers

If your form asks about their needs or interests, mention those in your response to show you've read their submission.

Add your signature

Include your standard email signature to make the message look like it came directly from you.

Conversion Tip: Personalized automated emails have 26% higher open rates than generic responses. Taking the extra minute to customize your template pays off in better engagement.

At 10:30 in the video, you'll see a complete example of a personalized response template that references specific form fields.

Testing Your Automation

Before turning your Zap live, it's crucial to test that everything works as expected. Here's the testing process:

Step 1: Send a test email

Use Zapier's "Test" feature to send a sample email through your automation.

Step 2: Check your inbox

Verify that the test email arrived with all personalization and formatting correct.

Step 3: Submit a real form

After publishing your Zap, fill out your actual website form to confirm the end-to-end automation works.
