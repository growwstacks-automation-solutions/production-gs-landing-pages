The Lead Response Crisis

Service businesses lose 78% of potential customers to competitors when they don't respond within 30 minutes of a lead inquiry. Yet most contractors and tradespeople check their Facebook leads just 2-3 times per day - often hours after the customer has already called someone else.

Key insight: SMS alerts have a 98% open rate compared to 20% for emails, and are typically read within 3 minutes. By routing Facebook leads directly to text messages, service businesses can achieve near-instant response times without changing their workflow.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When setting up Facebook-to-SMS alerts, these pitfalls can derail your automation:

1. Sending to the Lead's Number

At 4:18 in the video, you'll see the AI catch this critical error during testing. The system initially tried to send the alert to the lead's phone number (notifying them instead of you). Always verify the recipient number is yours during setup.

2. Overloading the SMS

While you can include all form fields, we recommend just 3-4 key details. A 160-character message ensures deliverability and quick scanning.

3. Ignoring Time Zones

If you serve multiple regions, add the lead's time zone to your SMS template. Calling a customer at 3 AM because you didn't realize they're in a different time zone creates poor experiences.
