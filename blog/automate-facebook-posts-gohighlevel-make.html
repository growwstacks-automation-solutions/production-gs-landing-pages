The Problem With Manual Facebook Posting

Every business owner knows they should post consistently on Facebook - but between running operations, serving customers, and managing teams, content creation often falls by the wayside. The blank post editor stares back at you each morning, and before you know it, weeks have passed without an update.

Manual posting creates three major pain points:

  • Time drain: Crafting quality posts takes 30-45 minutes each - time you don't have
  • Inconsistency: Erratic posting hurts your algorithm visibility and engagement
  • Creative fatigue: Coming up with fresh ideas daily leads to burnout

The hidden cost: For most small businesses, manual Facebook management consumes 5-7 hours weekly while delivering suboptimal results.

How the Automation Works

This automation solves the Facebook content dilemma by connecting GoHighLevel (your CRM and marketing hub) with Make.com (formerly Integromat) for AI-powered content generation. The system handles everything from idea generation to publishing without your direct involvement.

Here's the high-level flow:

  1. Trigger: An event in GoHighLevel (like a contact status change) initiates the workflow
  2. AI Generation: Make.com sends context to OpenAI's GPT to create the post
  3. Formatting: The content gets structured for Facebook with your branding
  4. Publishing: GoHighLevel posts automatically to your Facebook page

Key benefit: Set it up once and your Facebook page stays active with quality content while you focus on running your business.

Setting Up Your GoHighLevel Triggers

The automation starts with triggers in GoHighLevel that determine when and why to create posts. Common triggers include:

  • Contact status changes: When a lead becomes a customer
  • Scheduled times: Daily/weekly at specific hours
  • Campaign milestones: Product launches or promotions

To set up a trigger (as shown at 0:45 in the video):

  1. In GoHighLevel, navigate to Automations
  2. Click "Add Trigger" and select "Contact Changed"
  3. Configure what change should activate it (like status updates)
  4. Add filters to specify exact conditions
  5. Save the trigger to activate it

You can create multiple triggers for different post types, allowing varied content based on different business events.

AI Content Generation with Make.com

The magic happens when Make.com connects to OpenAI's GPT models to generate your posts. At 1:20 in the tutorial, you'll see how to:

  1. Add an "OpenAI" action in Make.com
  2. Select GPT-3.5 Turbo (the most cost-effective model)
  3. Input your custom prompt that defines post style
  4. Map fields from GoHighLevel to provide context

Cost note: At ~1 cent per execution, AI generation costs less than $3/month for daily posting - a fraction of what manual creation would cost in your time.

Your prompt is crucial - it should specify tone, length, and content focus. Many businesses create different prompts for:

  • Educational content
  • Promotional posts
  • Engagement questions
  • Customer success stories

Connecting GoHighLevel and Make.com

The critical link happens through webhooks (shown at 2:15 in the video). Here's how to connect the systems:

  1. In Make.com, create a new webhook
  2. Name it descriptively (like "Social Media Wizard")
  3. Copy the unique webhook URL
  4. Paste it into your GoHighLevel automation
  5. Configure the data structure so Make.com understands the incoming information

The webhook acts as a bridge - when your GoHighLevel trigger activates, it sends data to Make.com, which processes it through GPT, then returns the generated post back to GoHighLevel for publishing.

Pro tip: Start with simple test posts to verify the connection works before implementing your full prompt library.

Testing and Optimizing Your Posts

Like any automation, testing ensures quality before full deployment. Recommended steps:

  1. Sandbox testing: Run initial posts to a private page or draft folder
  2. Human review: Check the first 10-20 posts for brand alignment
  3. Prompt refinement: Adjust your GPT prompts based on results
  4. Performance tracking: Monitor engagement metrics to identify top-performing content types

Most businesses find the AI content exceeds expectations after prompt refinement. The system learns from your feedback - when you see posts you like, analyze what made them successful and incorporate those elements into your prompts.
