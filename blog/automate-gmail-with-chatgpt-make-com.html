How the Automation Works

The system operates through four connected components working in sequence:

  1. Gmail Watcher: Monitors your inbox for new emails matching specific criteria (skipping spam and newsletters)
  2. Google Sheets: Logs each email's details including sender, subject, and content
  3. ChatGPT: Generates context-aware responses using your custom instructions
  4. Gmail Sender: Replies automatically with the AI-generated message

What makes this powerful is the customization. Unlike generic autoresponders, your ChatGPT prompt includes specific details about your services, pricing, and preferred communication style - enabling responses that sound authentically like you.

Step 1: Setting Up the Gmail Trigger

The workflow begins by configuring Make.com to watch your Gmail inbox. At 3:15 in the tutorial video, you'll see how to:

  • Connect your Gmail account to Make.com
  • Set filters to only process important emails (skipping spam and promotions)
  • Configure the system to mark emails as read after processing
  • Limit processing to 5 emails at a time to avoid API overloads

This trigger runs every 15 minutes in production, though the demo shows manual triggering for testing purposes. The key is ensuring it only processes unread emails from specified folders/labels to maintain control.

Step 2: Connecting Google Sheets

Each processed email gets logged to Google Sheets for record-keeping and optional human review. The setup involves:

  1. Creating a sheet with headers for: Date, Sender Name, Sender Email, Subject, Email Content
  2. Connecting Make.com to your Google Drive
  3. Mapping Gmail fields to the appropriate spreadsheet columns

Why use Google Sheets? The spreadsheet serves as both an audit trail and potential moderation point. You could add a manual approval step before sending responses if desired, though the tutorial shows fully automated operation.

Step 3: Configuring ChatGPT Responses

The heart of the system is the ChatGPT prompt that crafts responses. At 8:30 in the video, you'll see the carefully constructed prompt that:

  • Establishes the AI's role as your email assistant
  • Includes your specific services and pricing information
  • Provides guidelines for handling different email types (inquiries, complaints, scheduling requests)
  • Specifies response formatting and tone preferences

The prompt dynamically inserts the sender's name and email content to personalize each reply. You'll also see how the system skips spam emails entirely based on sender address patterns.

Step 4: Sending Automated Replies

The final step configures Gmail to send the AI-generated responses. Key configuration points:

  1. Connecting your sending Gmail account (can be same as receiving)
  2. Mapping the ChatGPT response to the email body
  3. Setting the recipient to the original sender
  4. Using the original subject line prefixed with "Re:"

The system formats responses in HTML for professional presentation, including proper spacing and structure. At 12:45 in the video, you'll see a test response demonstrating how naturally the AI handles different email types.
