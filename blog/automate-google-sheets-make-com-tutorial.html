What is Make.com? (Formerly Integromat)

Make.com (previously known as Integromat) is a powerful no-code automation platform that connects different apps and services. It solves the common problem of data being trapped in silos across multiple tools, forcing teams to manually copy information between systems.

With Make.com, you can create visual workflows called "scenarios" that automatically transfer and transform data between applications. The platform supports over 1,000 integrations including Google Workspace, Slack, CRMs, and marketing tools.

Key benefit: Make.com's free plan includes 1,000 operations per month - enough to automate basic workflows without cost. Each action (like adding a row to Sheets or sending an email) counts as one operation.

Client Onboarding Workflow Overview

We'll build a complete client onboarding system that automatically:

  1. Captures new project details from a Google Form
  2. Adds the submission to your Google Sheets CRM
  3. Sends a personalized thank-you email via Gmail
  4. Notifies your team in Slack about the new project

This eliminates manual data entry while ensuring no client submission falls through the cracks. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you can see the complete workflow diagram in Make.com.

Step 1: Setting Up Your Make.com Account

Getting started with Make.com takes less than 2 minutes:

  1. Visit make.com and click "Get Started for Free"
  2. Sign up using your Google account or email
  3. Verify your email address if required

Once logged in, you'll see your dashboard showing available credits (1,000/month free) and active scenarios (2 max on free plan). Scenarios are what Make.com calls your automation workflows.

Step 2: Connecting Google Forms as Trigger

The first step in any automation is setting up the trigger - the event that starts your workflow. For our onboarding system, we'll use new Google Form submissions as the trigger:

  1. Click "Create a new scenario" then "Build from scratch"
  2. Search for "Google Forms" and select "Watch responses" module
  3. Connect your Google account and paste your form ID
  4. Set the limit to 5 responses per execution cycle
  5. Save and test the connection by submitting a test form

Pro tip: Always test each connection as you build it. At 5:30 in the video, you can see how to verify the Google Forms trigger is working before adding more steps.

Step 3: Mapping Data to Google Sheets

Now we'll automatically add each form submission to your Google Sheets CRM:

  1. Add a Google Sheets module after the Forms trigger
  2. Select "Add a row" as the action
  3. Connect your Google account and paste your Sheet ID
  4. Select the specific worksheet/tab name
  5. Map each form field to the corresponding Sheet column

The key is properly mapping the data fields between Forms and Sheets. At 8:45 in the tutorial, you can see exactly how to connect the field values to ensure clean data transfer.

Step 4: Adding Gmail Confirmation Emails

Next, we'll automatically send a personalized email to each new client:

  1. Add a Gmail module after the Sheets step
  2. Select "Send an email" as the action
  3. Connect your Gmail account
  4. Set the recipient to the form submitter's email
  5. Create a template with personalized fields from the form

At 12:20 in the video, you can see how to insert dynamic fields (like the client's name and project details) directly into the email template for personalization.

Step 5: Setting Up Slack Notifications

Finally, we'll notify your team in Slack about new projects:

  1. Add a Slack module connected via router (parallel path)
  2. Select "Send a message" as the action
  3. Connect your Slack account
  4. Choose the channel for notifications
  5. Create a message template with key project details

Using a router allows both the Gmail and Slack actions to happen simultaneously after the Sheets update, rather than waiting for one to complete before starting the other.

Step 6: Testing the Complete Workflow

Before going live, thoroughly test your automation:

  1. Submit a test form with realistic data
  2. Run the scenario once manually in Make.com
  3. Verify the data appears correctly in Sheets
  4. Check the test email was sent properly
  5. Confirm the Slack notification posted

At 15:50 in the tutorial, you can see the complete test of all connected systems working together automatically.

Step 7: Scheduling Your Automation

Once tested, set your scenario to run automatically:

  1. Click the schedule icon in your scenario
  2. Choose how often to check for new form submissions
  3. Free plan: Minimum 15-minute intervals
  4. Paid plans: As frequently as every minute
  5. Save and activate your scenario

Remember: More frequent checks use more operations. For most onboarding workflows, checking every 1-4 hours provides timely processing without wasting credits.
