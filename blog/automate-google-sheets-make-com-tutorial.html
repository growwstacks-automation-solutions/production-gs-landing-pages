What is Make.com?

Most small businesses waste countless hours manually transferring data between apps - copying form responses to spreadsheets, sending confirmation emails, and notifying team members. Make.com (formerly Integromat) solves this by automating these repetitive workflows.

Make.com is a visual automation platform that connects your apps and services. You create "scenarios" (workflows) that automatically move and transform data between tools whenever specific triggers occur. The platform supports over 1,000 apps including Google Workspace, Slack, CRMs, and payment processors.

Key benefit: The Google Sheets automation shown in this tutorial typically saves businesses 5-15 hours per week by eliminating manual data entry and follow-up tasks.

Workflow Overview

We'll build a complete automation that connects four apps:

  1. Google Forms - Captures new project submissions from clients
  2. Google Sheets - Stores all project data in a centralized CRM
  3. Gmail - Sends personalized thank you emails to clients
  4. Slack - Notifies your team about new projects

This creates a seamless workflow where form submissions automatically update your spreadsheet, trigger emails, and alert your team - with zero manual intervention.

Step 1: Connect Google Forms

The first module in our scenario watches for new Google Form submissions. At 5:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how to:

  1. Create a new scenario in Make.com
  2. Add the Google Forms "Watch Responses" trigger
  3. Connect your Google account
  4. Paste your form ID (found in the form URL)

Testing is crucial - submit a test form response and use Make.com's "Run once" feature to verify the connection works before proceeding.

Step 2: Connect Google Sheets

Next, we'll add form responses to a Google Sheet. The key steps shown at 7:30 in the video:

  1. Add a Google Sheets "Add a Row" module
  2. Connect your Google account
  3. Paste your spreadsheet ID (from the sheet URL)
  4. Select the correct worksheet tab
  5. Map form fields to spreadsheet columns

Pro tip: Always include column headers in your sheet and set "Does your table contain headers?" to Yes. This makes field mapping much easier.

Step 3: Add Gmail Module

At 12:45 in the tutorial, we add automated email notifications:

  1. Add a Gmail "Send an Email" module
  2. Connect your Gmail account
  3. Set the recipient to the form submitter's email
  4. Create a personalized email template
  5. Insert dynamic fields from the form (name, project details)

The email template can include any information about your process, next steps, or links to resources. Personalization tokens pull data directly from the form submission.

Step 4: Add Slack Notification

At 15:20, we complete the workflow with team notifications:

  1. Add a Slack "Send a Message" module
  2. Connect your Slack account
  3. Select the channel for project notifications
  4. Compose the message with key project details
  5. Use a router to run both email and Slack actions

The Slack message should include all information your team needs to act on the new project. You can format it with emojis, sections, or @mentions for specific team members.

Testing the Workflow

Before activating your automation:

  1. Submit a test form response
  2. Run the scenario once in Make.com
  3. Verify the data appears correctly in Sheets
  4. Check the test email was sent
  5. Confirm the Slack notification posted

At 17:00 in the video, you'll see how to test each component and troubleshoot any mapping errors before setting the scenario to run automatically.

Scaling Your Automations

This basic workflow can be expanded in powerful ways:

  • Add conditional logic to route different form responses
  • Connect to your CRM to create new contacts
  • Generate documents or contracts automatically
  • Schedule follow-up tasks in your project management tool

The same principles apply when connecting Google Sheets to other apps like Shopify, QuickBooks, or social media platforms. Once you master the basic pattern, you can automate nearly any repetitive data task.
