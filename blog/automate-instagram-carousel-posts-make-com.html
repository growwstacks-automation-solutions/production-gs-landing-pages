The Carousel Content Bottleneck

Creating Instagram carousels is one of the most time-consuming tasks in social media management. Between gathering images, writing captions, manually uploading each slide, and ensuring proper sequencing - what should be a 5-minute task often balloons to 30+ minutes per post.

The frustration compounds when you realize this manual process must be repeated multiple times per week. At 3 carousels weekly, that's over 75 hours annually spent just on upload mechanics rather than content strategy.

Content teams report: 68% of social media professionals say carousel creation is their least efficient workflow, with 42% postponing carousels despite knowing they drive 3x more engagement than single-image posts.

How Make.com Solves This

Make.com (formerly Integromat) provides visual workflow automation that connects Google Sheets directly to Instagram's API. Instead of manual uploads:

  1. Your team adds carousel content to a shared Google Sheet
  2. Make.com checks for new entries hourly/daily
  3. The automation handles all image processing
  4. Posts publish automatically as polished carousels

This turns carousel creation from a multi-step chore into a simple spreadsheet update. At 2:15 in the tutorial video, you'll see how the status column prevents duplicate posts while ensuring every image gets published.

Google Sheets Setup

The automation relies on a properly formatted Google Sheet with three key columns:

  • Column A: Your caption text (supports emojis and hashtags)
  • Column C: Google Drive URLs for each carousel image
  • Column D: Status field (blank=unpublished, "done"=posted)

When building your sheet:

  • Keep image URLs in sequential order - this becomes your carousel sequence
  • Use one row per carousel set (all images for one post in columns)
  • Include header row as shown at 3:45 in the video

Building the Make.com Workflow

The automation uses six connected modules:

  1. Google Sheets: Checks for unpublished carousels
  2. Get File: Downloads images from Drive URLs
  3. ImageBB: Hosts images for Instagram compatibility
  4. Sheets Update: Marks rows as "done" after posting
  5. Array Aggregator: Bundles images into carousel format
  6. Instagram: Publishes the complete carousel

At 7:20 in the video, you'll see how the array aggregator combines up to 10 images while preserving your intended sequence from the spreadsheet.

Image Handling Workaround

Instagram's API won't accept Google Drive links directly. The workflow uses ImageBB (a free image hosting service) as an intermediary:

  1. Make.com downloads each image from Drive
  2. Uploads to ImageBB via their API
  3. Receives a public URL that Instagram accepts

At 9:05, the tutorial shows how to get your free ImageBB API key and connect it to Make.com. This extra step adds about 2 minutes to setup but solves the Instagram/Drive compatibility issue permanently.

Instagram Business Configuration

For automation access, your Instagram must be:

  • A Business account (convert in settings if personal)
  • Connected to a Facebook page (required for API access)
  • Logged into Make.com via Facebook authentication

The connection process at 12:30 shows how to:

  1. Select your Instagram Business profile in Make.com
  2. Map the caption from your Google Sheet
  3. Attach the aggregated image array

Pro Tip: Test with 1-2 images first before scaling to 10-image carousels. The video demonstrates a successful 10-image test at 14:45.

Testing and Deployment

Before scheduling your automation:

  2. Verify the post appears correctly on Instagram
  3. Check that status fields updated to "done"
