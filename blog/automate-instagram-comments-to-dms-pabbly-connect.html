Step-by-Step Setup Process

Setting up this automation requires connecting Pabbly Connect to your Instagram Business account through Facebook's Developer platform. While the process has several steps, none require coding knowledge - just careful following of instructions.

Step 1: Create Facebook Developer App

Navigate to developers.facebook.com and create a new app. Select "Business" as the app type and follow the prompts to connect it to your Instagram Business account. This grants Pabbly Connect the necessary permissions to read comments and send DMs on your behalf.

Step 2: Configure Pabbly Connect Trigger

In Pabbly Connect, create a new workflow with Instagram Developer App as the trigger. Select "Comments" as the event type. At 6:50 in the video, you'll see how to authenticate your account and test the connection by making a test comment.

Step 3: Set Up Lookup Table

Create a lookup table in Pabbly Connect that matches post IDs to the messages you want to send. This allows one automation to handle multiple posts with different links. The tutorial shows how to extract just the post ID from Instagram's response data.

Step 4: Configure DM Action

Add an "Instagram Developer Send Private Reply" action to your workflow. Map the comment ID from the trigger and the message from your lookup table. At 9:20, the creator demonstrates how to include dynamic content in your automated DMs.

Pro Tip: Add a filter step to only trigger the automation when comments contain specific keywords like "link" or "DM me". This prevents sending unwanted messages for generic comments.

Advanced Features for Business Use

While the basic setup handles most use cases, Pabbly Connect offers several powerful extensions for businesses:

Multi-Post Management

Expand your lookup table to include dozens or hundreds of posts. Each can have its own customized DM response with product links, lead magnets, or video URLs. The system automatically selects the right message based on which post received the comment.

Comment Text Filtering

Add router steps to send different DMs based on comment content. For example, comments with "price" could receive a pricing sheet, while "demo" requests get a calendar link. This creates a semi-conversational experience without manual work.

CRM Integration

Connect the automation to your CRM by adding a step that records commenters as leads. You can capture their Instagram handle, the post they commented on, and what link they received - valuable data for follow-up campaigns.

Scalability: This single automation can handle thousands of comments monthly without additional setup. As your Instagram presence grows, your DM system grows with it automatically.
