The Lead Response Crisis in Legal

Legal practices lose more potential clients to response delays than to competitor pricing. A Harvard Law study found that firms responding within 5 minutes convert 8x more leads than those waiting 24 hours. Yet most small practices still rely on manual processes—checking forms, copying data, drafting emails—that inevitably create bottlenecks.

The breaking point comes when you're handling 10+ leads daily. Paralegals waste hours on data entry, attorneys miss urgent inquiries in crowded inboxes, and promising cases slip through the cracks because no one noticed the submission until it was too late.

37% of legal leads choose another firm when responses take more than 24 hours. Automation reduces this to under 5 minutes—giving your practice the first-mover advantage in client acquisition.

How Automation Solves It

This Make.com workflow acts as your 24/7 intake coordinator. When a potential client submits your Typeform, the system:

  1. Instantly adds their details to your CRM (Monday.com, HubSpot, etc.)
  2. Sends a personalized welcome email with practice-area-specific content
  3. Alerts your team via Telegram/Slack with all critical details
  4. Routes to the appropriate attorney based on case type

The entire process completes in under 5 seconds—compared to the 15–45 minutes typically spent manually processing each lead. At 10 leads/day, that's 250+ hours/year regained for billable work.

Step-by-Step Setup

Step 1: Import the Blueprint

In Make.com (formerly Integromat), create a new scenario and import the pre-built lawyer lead automation blueprint. This gives you the complete workflow structure—you'll just need to connect your accounts.

Step 2: Connect Typeform

Create a webhook in Make.com linked to your Typeform account. Select your intake form from the dropdown—this triggers the automation whenever someone submits it.

Step 3: Configure Your CRM

Map the Typeform fields to your CRM columns. The blueprint supports Monday.com out-of-the-box but can adapt to any CRM with API access. Critical fields to include: name, email, phone, practice area, and case details.

Pro Tip: Add a 5-second delay after Typeform to ensure all data loads before CRM processing begins.

Typeform Integration

Your intake form becomes the automation's starting point. Design it to capture:

  • Contact information (name, email, phone)
  • Practice area selection (dropdown)
  • Case details (textarea)
  • Urgency level (radio buttons)

The system parses these responses to route leads appropriately. For example, criminal defense inquiries might go to one attorney while personal injury cases route to another—all automated based on the form selections.

CRM Configuration

Monday.com works seamlessly with this blueprint, but the principles apply to any CRM:

  1. Create a "New Leads" board with columns matching your Typeform fields
  2. Generate an API key in your CRM's settings
  3. In Make.com, map each Typeform response to the corresponding CRM field

The automation will create a new lead record with status "Awaiting Review" and all submitted details—eliminating manual data entry errors.

Email Personalization

The welcome email module lets you:

  • Merge the lead's name into the greeting
  • Include practice-area-specific content
  • Attach relevant intake documents
  • Set expectations for next steps

Templates can vary by case type—a DUI consultation email looks different than a divorce inquiry. The system selects the appropriate version based on the form response.

Team Notifications

Telegram/Slack alerts include:

  1. Lead name and contact info
  2. Practice area and urgency level
  3. Key case details excerpt
  4. Direct link to the CRM record

Configure these to go to specific channels/attorneys based on case type. Criminal defense alerts might ping one group while family law goes to another—all automated.

Conditional Logic for Practice Areas

The blueprint includes routing filters that:

  • Flag high-urgency matters for immediate review
