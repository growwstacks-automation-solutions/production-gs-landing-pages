The Lead Qualification Problem

Healthcare sales teams face a universal challenge: determining which facilities are large enough to qualify for their services. For waste management consulting (as shown in the video), this means identifying facilities with 100+ beds - a threshold where cost-saving services become viable.

Manual research is painfully slow. Checking each facility's website, calling for information, or searching public records takes 5-10 minutes per lead. For a list of 1,000 prospects, that's 80+ hours of wasted effort - especially when 60-70% will be under the qualification threshold.

Key insight: Bed count data exists publicly for most facilities, but it's scattered across websites, directories, and licensing databases. AI can aggregate these signals faster than any human researcher.

How Make.com Solves It

Make.com (formerly Integromat) connects your spreadsheet or CRM with AI services to automate lead enrichment. The workflow shown in the video follows a simple but powerful pattern:

  1. Scan your lead list for missing bed counts
  2. Pass facility details to AI (name, address, website)
  3. Extract bed count estimates from available data
  4. Update your records automatically

This creates a self-maintaining system where leads are qualified as they enter your pipeline - no manual research required. At 1:45 in the video, you'll see how the AI correctly identified Blue Skies of Texas as having ~393 beds by analyzing their website content.

Step-by-Step Automation

Here's how to build this qualification workflow in Make.com:

Step 1: Set Up the Trigger

Configure the Google Sheets module to search for rows where the "Bed Count" field is empty. Start with 1 row for testing (as shown at 3:12), then scale to 5-10 rows per minute in production.

Step 2: Connect AI Enrichment

Use the OpenAI module with a carefully crafted prompt (visible at 4:30) that provides:

  • Example input/output pairs to train the AI
  • Clear instructions to estimate based on available data
  • Formatting requirements for consistent results

Step 3: Update Your Records

Feed the AI's bed count estimate back into your spreadsheet. The video shows this simple but crucial step at 6:08 - ensuring your data stays current without manual entry.

Pro tip: Add a "Last Enriched" timestamp column to track when each record was updated and avoid duplicate processing.

AI Enrichment Process

The magic happens in the AI prompt design. The video demonstrates a proven structure at 4:30:

  1. System message: Defines the AI's role as a healthcare facility researcher
  2. Example pairs: Shows sample inputs and desired outputs
  3. Current lead: Passes the actual facility data for analysis

This approach yields 80-85% accuracy for bed count estimates - more than sufficient for qualification purposes. For the remaining 15-20%, you can either:

  • Flag for manual review
  • Run through a secondary AI pass with different parameters
  • Default to "unknown" and let sales prioritize accordingly

Scaling the Workflow

At 7:20 in the video, the creator explains how to scale from testing to production:
