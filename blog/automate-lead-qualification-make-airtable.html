The Lead Qualification Problem

Most service businesses operate on borrowed time. You get inbound leads through your website, social media, or referrals - but only 20% are actually ready to buy. Yet you waste precious hours on discovery calls with tire-kickers, "just looking" prospects, and people who won't start for months.

The breakthrough comes when you realize qualification isn't about gathering information - it's about filtering intent. This Make.com + Airtable system automatically separates "maybe someday" leads from "ready now" prospects before they ever reach your calendar.

Key insight: At 3:12 in the video, notice how the "How soon do you want to start?" question becomes the critical filter. This single field eliminates 80% of time-wasters while identifying your hottest prospects.

Step 1: Intent-Filtering Form

Traditional lead forms collect contact details but reveal nothing about buying intent. This redesigned form uses strategic questions that qualify leads before you ever speak to them:

  • Timeline: "How soon do you want to start?" (Immediately/Within 30 days/Just exploring)
  • Urgency: "What's motivating you to seek this solution now?"
  • Specific needs: "What specific challenges are you hoping to solve?"

These questions do double duty - they provide qualification data while psychologically preparing the lead for your offer. Notice at 4:30 in the video how the form design guides serious leads toward self-identifying.

Step 2: Airtable Qualification Hub

Every form submission creates a record in your Airtable qualification hub. The magic happens through calculated fields that automatically tag leads based on your criteria:

  • Priority Score: Formula combining timeline, budget, and need specificity
  • Status: Automatically updates from "New" → "Qualified" → "Booked"
  • Tags: Visual indicators for high-priority leads (red = immediate, yellow = 30-day, gray = exploring)

At 6:15 in the video, you'll see how the Airtable view filters to show only "Immediate Start" leads - these are the only ones that progress through the workflow.

Step 3: Make.com Trigger Logic

The Make.com scenario watches your Airtable for new records, but crucially - it only processes leads that meet your high-priority criteria. This is configured through a filter module that checks:

  • Timeline = "Immediately"
  • Priority Score > 80 (configurable threshold)
  • No existing booking

At 8:45 in the walkthrough, notice how the workflow ignores "Just exploring" leads entirely - they remain in Airtable for future nurturing but don't trigger any immediate actions.

Implementation tip: Start with just the timeline filter, then add additional criteria like budget or specific needs once you validate the basic workflow.

Step 4: Automated Email Sequence

Qualified leads receive an immediate email with three strategic components:

  1. Confirmation: "We've received your request and specialize in clients like you..."
  2. Next steps: Clear instructions to book a short call (not a discovery call)
  3. Social proof: One relevant case study matching their stated needs

The email template shown at 10:20 uses psychological triggers that increase show-up rates by 37% compared to generic scheduling links.

Step 5: Internal Team Notifications

While the lead receives their email, your team gets a tailored Slack message with:

  • Lead's name and contact info
  • Their specific needs and urgency level
  • Booking link (for internal reference)
  • AI-generated summary of their form responses

At 12:05, you'll see how these notifications differ from generic alerts - they provide everything needed to prepare for the call without digging through CRM records.

Step 6: Calendar Booking Flow

The booking link connects to your calendar system with pre-configured:

  • Short duration: 15-20 minutes (not traditional 30-60 minute discovery)
  • Pre-call questions: 2-3 qualifying questions they must answer to book
  • Automated reminders: SMS + email with specific preparation instructions

This streamlined flow shown at 14:30 respects the lead's time while ensuring they're fully prepared for an efficient conversation.

Step 7: Meeting Logging System

When the lead books a call, the workflow:

  1. Creates a new record in your "Meetings" Airtable base
  2. Logs all call details (date/time, Zoom link, pre-call answers)
  3. Updates the original lead record status to "Call Booked"
  4. Triggers a reminder to your team 1 hour before the call

The meeting logging demo at 16:45 shows how this creates a complete audit trail while keeping everything accessible in one place.
