Make.com Basics

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a visual automation platform that connects apps without coding. Think of it as the brain between your applications - when something happens in one app (like a form submission), Make.com can trigger actions in other apps (like sending an email or updating a spreadsheet).

The platform operates on a simple concept: triggers and actions. A trigger is the event that starts the automation (like a new form submission), and actions are what happens because of it (sending an email, logging data, etc.). The free plan includes 1,000 operations per month - enough for your first 3-5 client automations with no credit card required.

Step 1: Form Trigger

The foundation of any Make.com automation is the trigger - the event that starts everything. In this case, it's a new submission to your Google Form. Setting this up correctly ensures your automation runs instantly rather than on a delay.

Key configuration points:

  • Set the watch interval to "immediately" for real-time response
  • Limit to 1 submission per check during testing
  • Ensure all form fields are captured as variables

Pro tip: Always test your trigger with actual form submissions before adding actions. At 7:30 in the video, you'll see how to verify data is flowing correctly.

Step 2: Automated Email

The magic happens in the Gmail action - where generic automation becomes personalized communication. Using variables from the form submission, each email includes the lead's name, quotes their message back to them, and sets clear expectations for follow-up.

The email template achieves three psychological wins:

  1. Instant acknowledgment (satisfies the need for quick response)
  2. Personalization (creates connection using their own words)
  3. Clear next steps (reduces anxiety about being forgotten)
