The Lego Social Media Problem

Lego collectors, resellers, and fan accounts face a unique content challenge: building sets takes hours, photography requires perfect lighting, and daily posting demands creative captions—yet algorithms reward consistent, high-quality posts. Most accounts either post sporadically or burn out trying to maintain daily content.

The breakthrough came when AI image generation reached quality levels where artificial Lego builds became indistinguishable from photographs. Combined with Make.com's automation capabilities, we developed a system that:

Saves 5+ hours per week: No more building, staging, or photographing physical sets. The AI generates perfect Lego images on demand while you focus on growing your business or enjoying the hobby.

How the Automation Works

This Make.com workflow connects four key services into a seamless posting machine:

  1. Google Sheets: Acts as your content database with Lego set names, captions, and hashtags
  2. OpenAI/Gemini: Generates photorealistic images of completed Lego sets
  3. Dropbox: Stores and shares the AI-generated images
  4. Make.com: Orchestrates the entire process end-to-end

The magic happens in the scheduling. Once configured, Make.com checks your Google Sheet daily, processes new rows through the workflow, and publishes to both Instagram and Facebook—all while you sleep.

Step 1: Create Your Lego Database

Start by building a Google Sheet with three key columns (as shown at 2:15 in the video):

  • Lego Set Name: "Titanic", "Millennium Falcon", etc.
  • Caption: Engaging description for social posts
  • Hashtags: 5-10 relevant tags per post

Pro tip: Use ChatGPT to generate your initial database. Simply prompt: "I'm making a social media account posting images of completed Lego sets to Instagram. Make me three columns: 25 popular Lego sets, Instagram descriptions, and hashtags."

Example output: For the Lego Titanic, ChatGPT might generate: "Finally completed the legendary Titanic - 7,500 pieces and 4 months in the making! #LegoTitanic #BuildingChallenge #CollectorLife"

Step 2: Generate AI Lego Images

The automation triggers whenever a new row appears in your Google Sheet. Make.com sends the Lego set name to OpenAI's image generation API with this optimized prompt:

"Professional photograph of a completed [Lego Set Name] on display in a well-lit room. The set should be perfectly assembled with no missing pieces. Use natural lighting and include subtle depth-of-field effects to create a premium look."

At current rates (March 2024), each image costs approximately $0.08-$0.15 depending on quality settings. The AI typically generates 4 image options per prompt, giving you variety to choose from.

Step 3: Automate Social Posting

Once the AI generates an image, the workflow:

  1. Uploads the image to a designated Dropbox folder
  2. Creates a shareable link for Instagram's requirements
  3. Posts to your Facebook Page with the caption and hashtags
  4. Publishes to your connected Instagram account simultaneously

Critical note: As mentioned at 8:45 in the video, your Instagram must be connected to a Facebook Page via Meta Business Suite for Make.com to post successfully.

Scheduling and Maintenance

Configure the automation to run:

  • Frequency: Daily, every 12 hours, or custom intervals
  • Image Variations: Modify the AI prompt seasonally (e.g., holiday decorations)
  • Content Refresh: Add new Lego sets to your Google Sheet monthly

The system requires minimal maintenance—just occasional updates to your Google Sheet and monitoring of your OpenAI API usage. For high-volume accounts, consider upgrading to Make.com's paid plans for more frequent posting.
