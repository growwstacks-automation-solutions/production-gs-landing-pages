Why Carousels Dominate LinkedIn Engagement

LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes content that keeps users on platform longer - and nothing achieves this better than carousels. While text posts get quick scans, carousels encourage active engagement through swiping, with each slide counting as a separate interaction. This signals high-quality content to LinkedIn's ranking system.

For knowledge businesses like coaching and consulting, carousels solve two critical problems: they showcase expertise visually while breaking complex ideas into digestible pieces. A well-structured 8-slide carousel can communicate what would take 1,500 words of text - in a format your audience actually consumes.

3-5x more profile views: Professionals using carousels consistently report 300-500% increases in profile visits compared to text-only posting. The visual format makes your content more shareable, extending your reach beyond immediate connections.

The 3-Part Automation Workflow

This automation transforms your raw content ideas into published carousels through three seamless stages. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how the entire process flows from start to finish without manual intervention.

Stage 1: Google Sheets Trigger - Your content ideas live in a simple spreadsheet. When you add a new row with a topic (like "Why Primitive Coaching Centers Fail"), Make.com detects the update and begins processing.

Stage 2: Gamma AI Design - The automation sends your content to Gamma AI with specific instructions about slide count, tone, and format. Gamma applies professional design principles most creators lack, ensuring mobile-friendly readability.

Stage 3: LinkedIn Publishing - The finished carousel exports to Google Drive, then posts directly to your LinkedIn with optimal formatting. The entire process happens while you focus on higher-value work.

Google Sheets: Your Content Command Center

Your automation starts with a dead-simple Google Sheet that becomes your content dashboard. Instead of staring at a blank Canva canvas, you simply fill cells with:

  • Primary topic or question (e.g. "5 Signs Your Coaching Approach Needs Updating")
  • Key points to cover (3-5 bullet points)
  • Desired slide count (typically 6-10)
  • Brand tone (professional, conversational, etc.)

The sheet acts as your content brief - no design skills required. At 3:40 in the tutorial, you'll see how the "Watch Rows" module triggers the workflow the moment you add new content. This means you can batch-create a month's worth of carousel ideas in one sitting, then let the automation handle the rest.
