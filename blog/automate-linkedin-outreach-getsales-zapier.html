The Manual Outreach Problem

The solution emerged when automation platforms like Zapier began integrating with LinkedIn tools like GetSales. Now that initial manual connection request - which used to require opening each profile individually - happens automatically based on your spreadsheet data.

Key stat: Teams using this automation report sending 5x more connection requests per week while saving 15+ hours on manual work.

How Zapier + GetSales Work Together

Zapier acts as the bridge between your lead source (Google Sheets) and GetSales' LinkedIn automation. The workflow follows a simple trigger-action pattern:

  1. Trigger: New row added to your Google Sheet (or CRM)
  2. Action: Zapier sends lead data to GetSales via webhook
  3. Result: GetSales sends personalized LinkedIn request

At 2:15 in the tutorial video, you'll see how the webhook configuration allows these platforms to communicate even without a direct integration. This "glue" approach works with nearly any sales tool combination.

Setting Up Google Sheets Trigger

The Google Sheets trigger is the starting point for your automation. Here's how to configure it:

Step 1: Connect Your Google Account

In Zapier, select Google Sheets as your trigger app and authenticate with your Google account. Make sure to grant access to the specific spreadsheet containing your leads.

Step 2: Configure Trigger Settings

Choose "New Spreadsheet Row" as your trigger event. Select your worksheet and identify which column contains the LinkedIn profile URLs. Zapier will monitor this sheet for new entries.

Step 3: Test With Sample Data

Zapier will pull a test record to verify the connection. Make sure it captures all required fields (name, company, LinkedIn URL) before proceeding.

Pro tip: Use dropdown menus or data validation in your Google Sheet to ensure consistent formatting of LinkedIn URLs. This prevents errors in the automation.

Configuring GetSales Action

With the trigger working, we now configure GetSales to receive and act on the lead data:

Step 1: Set Up Webhook in GetSales

Navigate to GetSales' webhook settings and create a new endpoint. Copy the unique URL provided - this is where Zapier will send your lead data.

Step 2: Add Webhook Action in Zapier

In your Zap, add a "Webhooks by Zapier" action. Select "Custom Request" as the event type and paste your GetSales endpoint URL.

Step 3: Map Data Fields

Configure the webhook to send data in the exact format GetSales expects. Typically this includes:

  • First name
  • Last name
  • Company
  • LinkedIn profile URL
  • Any custom fields for personalization

At 7:30 in the video, you'll see how to handle authorization headers containing your GetSales API key. This security step ensures only your account can trigger actions.

Personalization Tips for Higher Response Rates

Automation works best when it doesn't feel automated. Here's how to make your outreach feel personal:

Connection acceptance rates increase 42% when messages reference a shared connection, mutual interest, or specific aspect of the prospect's profile.

Include these personalization elements in your Google Sheet columns:

  • Common ground: Alumni networks, shared groups, or interests
  • Company mentions: Recent news or achievements
  • Role-specific: Comments on their job title challenges

GetSales can insert these variables into your template messages automatically. The more relevant each request feels, the more likely prospects are to engage.

Scaling Your Outreach Without Getting Flagged

LinkedIn monitors for spammy behavior, so scaling requires strategy:

Safe Volume Guidelines

  • New accounts: Start with ≤20 requests/day
  • Established accounts: Cap at 50-75 requests/day
  • Always: Space requests throughout the day

Diversify Your Approach

Mix connection requests with:

  • Profile views
  • Content engagement
  • Follows (when connections aren't appropriate)
