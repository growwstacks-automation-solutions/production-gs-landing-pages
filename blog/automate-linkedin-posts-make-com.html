How Make.com Connects to LinkedIn (Step-by-Step)

Connecting Make.com to LinkedIn takes less than 2 minutes and requires no technical skills. The process uses LinkedIn's official API through OAuth authentication, meaning your password is never stored by Make.com.

Here's how to set it up:

Step 1: Add the LinkedIn Module

In your Make.com scenario, search for and add the "LinkedIn" module. Choose the "Create a User Text Post" action to start building your automated posting workflow.

Step 2: Authenticate Your Account

Click "Add Connection" and log in to LinkedIn when prompted. You must be logged into LinkedIn in the same browser for this to work seamlessly. The system will request necessary permissions - approve these to establish the connection.

Step 3: Test Your First Post

Write a test post in the module, run the scenario once, and verify it appears on your LinkedIn profile. This confirms everything is working before adding more complex automation.

Pro Tip: Always test with a simple text post first before adding images or complex content flows. This isolates any connection issues early.

Adding Images to Automated LinkedIn Posts

Posts with images receive 2-3x more engagement than text-only updates. Make.com supports multiple methods for automatically including visuals:

Method 1: Upload Directly from Storage

Connect Google Drive, Dropbox, or other cloud storage to access your image library. The workflow can select specific images or choose randomly from a designated folder.

Method 2: AI-Generated Images

Integrate DALL-E, Midjourney, or Stable Diffusion to create custom images based on your post content. The system generates the image, uploads it, and attaches it to your LinkedIn post - all automatically.

Method 3: URL-Based Images

Pull images from approved websites or your own hosted content. This works well for sharing infographics, charts, or other existing visual assets.

Example: A real estate client uses AI to generate neighborhood-specific housing market images that automatically pair with their local market update posts.
