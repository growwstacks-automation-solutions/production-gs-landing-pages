Step 1: Setting Up Your Content Source

The workflow starts with a Google Sheet named "Automation Content" with two columns: "Content Links" and optional "Notes." Here's the magic:

1. Add any article link (industry news, blog posts, competitor updates) to the sheet

2. Make.com watches for new rows via its Google Sheets integration

3. Each new link triggers the workflow without manual intervention

This means you can batch-add a week's worth of content in 5 minutes, and the system processes them one by one on schedule.

Step 2: AI Summarization with Gemini

At 3:40 in the tutorial, we add the Google Gemini module. Here's why Gemini 2.5 Flash outperforms other models for this:

1. 128K context window → Reads entire articles without truncation

2. Free API access → No per-use costs unlike GPT-4

3. Instruction-following → Consistently outputs LinkedIn-optimized summaries

The prompt is simple: "Summarize this article in 3 bullet points highlighting insights for [your industry] professionals." Change the bracketed text to your niche.

Step 3: Brand Voice Personalization

Raw summaries sound robotic. At 5:20, we add a second Gemini step with this transformative prompt:

"Convert this summary into a LinkedIn post using: [Your tone: e.g., 'casual with 1 emoji per paragraph'] + [Your perspective: e.g., 'always end with a question'] + [Word count: 120-150 words].

This is where the system becomes uniquely yours. One client in commercial real estate added: "Include one metaphor comparing the insight to property valuation." Their engagement jumped 37%.

Step 4: Automated LinkedIn Publishing

The final step (7:50 in video) connects to LinkedIn via Make's OAuth integration. Key settings:

1. Post Type: "Text" works best for AI-generated content

2. Visibility: Set to "Public" or "Connections Only"

3. Scheduling: Add a delay module to space posts 8-24 hours apart

After testing with "Run Once," schedule the scenario to trigger daily at 9 AM. The system will check your Google Sheet for new links, process them through AI, and post — all before your first coffee.
