The Problem With Disconnected Apps

Most businesses today use a patchwork of tools - forms for inquiries, spreadsheets for contacts, Notion for project management, and email for communication. This fragmentation creates operational headaches where information exists in silos, requiring manual copying between systems.

The worst part? Customer inquiries often fall through the cracks. A form submission might sit in a spreadsheet until someone manually researches the company, drafts a response, updates the CRM, and notifies the team. This process typically takes hours or days - far too slow for today's expectations.

68% of customers expect a response to inquiries within one hour, yet most small businesses take 12+ hours to reply according to recent surveys.

Why Softr Workflows Are Different

Traditional automation tools like Zapier or Make require complex setups to connect multiple apps. Softr Workflows simplify this by providing native connections between Softr apps and popular tools like Notion, Gmail, and Google Sheets - with built-in AI capabilities you won't find elsewhere.

What sets Softr apart is its AI action that can research companies online. While other tools just move data between apps, Softr can actually enhance that data by finding additional information before passing it along. This enables truly personalized automated responses that feel human-crafted.

Building the Inquiry Workflow

Our example workflow solves the disconnected apps problem by creating a seamless system from initial inquiry to final response. When someone submits a consulting inquiry through a Tally form (connected to Google Sheets), the workflow:

  1. Researches the company using Softr's AI
  2. Composes a personalized email response
  3. Adds the submission to a master Notion database
  4. Sends a Slack notification with all details

At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you can see how we configure the Tally form to feed responses directly into Google Sheets, which serves as our workflow trigger.

Setting Up the Trigger

Every automation begins with a trigger - the event that starts the workflow. For this system, we're using new rows in a Google Sheet as our trigger point. The sheet receives submissions from our Tally form, which has several advantages over using Notion or Airtable directly.

Tally forms provide robust conditional logic, file uploads, and payment processing - features that make them ideal for client inquiries. By connecting to Google Sheets first, we create a reliable data pipeline that's easier to debug than direct database triggers.

Pro Tip: Always test your trigger with sample data before building the rest of the workflow. At 3:30 in the video, we demonstrate how to send a test submission to verify the Google Sheets connection.

Configuring AI Research

The magic happens in Softr's AI action. After the trigger, we add an "AI Research" step that takes the company name from the form submission and searches for relevant information online. This might include:

  • Company size and industry
  • Key executives or contacts
  • Recent news or announcements
  • Competitive differentiators

The AI compiles this information into structured data that subsequent steps can use. At 4:45 in the video, you'll see how we craft the AI prompt to ensure consistent, useful results.

Automating Personalized Emails

With the research complete, we configure the Gmail action to send a personalized response. The email template includes dynamic fields that pull from both the original form submission and the AI research:

  • Recipient's name and email address
  • Company name in the subject line
  • Specific references to the company's business
  • Our standard response template with signature

At 6:20 in the tutorial, we demonstrate how to test the email action and verify that all dynamic fields populate correctly before going live.

Updating Your Database

No automation is complete without proper record-keeping. The next step adds the inquiry to our master Notion database, including:

  • All original form fields
  • The AI research findings
  • Timestamp of the automated response
  • Status tracking for follow-up

This creates a complete audit trail and ensures the sales team has full context when they follow up. The database update happens simultaneously with the email send, eliminating manual data entry.

Slack Notifications

The final piece is team awareness. We configure a Slack notification that posts to our #inquiries channel with:

  • Company name and inquiry type
  • Key details from the AI research
  • Link to the full Notion record
  • Confirmation that the auto-response was sent

This keeps everyone informed without flooding inboxes. At 7:50 in the video, we show how to customize the Slack message formatting for maximum readability.
