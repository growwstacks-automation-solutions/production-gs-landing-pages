How the Zapier Reassigned Booking Trigger Works

The Zapier Booking Reassigned Trigger acts as a bridge between OnceHub and the rest of your tech stack. When any booking changes ownership in OnceHub, this trigger instantly captures the event and pushes the updated details to your chosen destination app.

Unlike manual exports or periodic syncs, this happens in real-time (typically within 1-2 minutes). The automation handles all the field mapping and data transformation, ensuring the receiving app gets information in exactly the format it needs.

Step-by-Step Setup in OnceHub and Zapier

Setting up the reassigned booking automation takes just minutes. At 1:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see the exact steps to connect OnceHub to Zapier and select the trigger.

Step 1: Connect Your OnceHub Account

Navigate to OnceHub's Account Integration page and select Zapier under Automation. If you haven't connected before, you'll authorize the integration. Most businesses only need to do this once.

Step 2: Choose Your Destination App

While the demo uses Google Sheets, you can select any of Zapier's 5,000+ supported apps. Popular choices include CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot), project tools (Asana, Trello), and communication platforms (Slack, Teams).

Step 3: Select the Reassigned Booking Trigger

From Zapier's OnceHub triggers, choose "Manage reassigned OnceHub bookings." This creates the automation foundation that will detect ownership changes.

Pro Tip: At 2:30 in the video, notice how Zapier automatically pulls a test record. This lets you verify the connection works before mapping fields.

Complete Data Mapping Options Available

OnceHub passes a wealth of information through the reassigned booking trigger. At 3:45 in the tutorial, you'll see the key fields being mapped to Google Sheets:

  • Contact Details: Name and email of the person who booked the meeting
  • Meeting Information: Date, time, status, and location (physical or virtual)
  • Ownership Data: New host/owner email (critical for reassignments)
  • Context Fields: Purpose of the meeting and any custom questions

The exact fields available depend on your OnceHub configuration. Most businesses map at least the contact info and new owner details to ensure smooth handoffs.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

After implementing this automation for dozens of clients, we've identified three frequent pitfalls:

1. Overlooking Field Mappings

Teams often map basic info like names and dates but forget critical context fields (meeting purpose, custom answers) that the new host needs.

2. Skipping the Test Step

At 4:20 in the video, you'll see the importance of testing with real data before publishing. Many teams rush past this and encounter formatting issues later.

3. Not Monitoring for Failures

While Zapier is reliable, occasional failures happen. Set up alerts (available on paid plans) to catch any missed reassignments promptly.
