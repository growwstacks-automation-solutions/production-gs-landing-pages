GoHighLevel's New AI Calling Feature

GoHighLevel recently added outbound calling capabilities to its AI agents. This transforms what was previously just an inbound answering service into a complete AI receptionist that can proactively contact your customers.

The system works by triggering calls when specific events occur - typically after a form submission or appointment booking. The AI handles the entire conversation, confirming details, answering common questions, and providing opt-out instructions to maintain compliance.

What makes this particularly powerful is the integration with GoHighLevel's existing automation features. Every call is recorded, transcribed, and summarized automatically, with the results stored in the contact record and emailed to the business owner.

Critical Compliance Requirements

Outbound calling carries significant compliance risks if not handled properly. GoHighLevel's system includes built-in safeguards to maintain TCPA compliance, but you must understand these limitations:

  • US-to-US calls only: Both the calling number and recipient number must be US-based
  • Call frequency limits: Maximum one call per number per day, four calls per number every two weeks
  • Time restrictions: Calls can only be made between 10am-6pm local time
  • Mandatory opt-out: Every call must include clear instructions for opting out of future calls

The system automatically checks these requirements before placing any calls. If a contact doesn't meet all criteria, the call won't proceed, protecting your business from potential violations.

Prerequisites and Initial Setup

Before configuring the AI calling workflow, you need three key components in place:

  1. US Phone Number: A GoHighLevel-provided US number to make the outbound calls
  2. Calendar or Form: The trigger point for calls (appointment calendar or lead form)
  3. Custom Field for Appointment Date: Special field to properly pass appointment times to the AI

The custom field is particularly important due to a current GoHighLevel limitation. While the system has appointment time fields, they don't reliably pass to the AI agent. Creating a custom contact field specifically for this purpose ensures the AI has accurate appointment details during calls.

How to Enable Outbound AI Calls

Activating outbound calling requires navigating to Sub Account → AI Agents → Voice AI and clicking "Enable Outbound Calls." After accepting the terms, you'll see a list of all forms and calendars in your account.

Select the specific calendar or form that should trigger calls, then paste in the required compliance language (available in the video at 6:45). This language must include your business name and clear opt-out instructions.

Important: Without this compliance language, your workflows won't be able to trigger AI calls, even if everything else is configured correctly.

After pasting the text and updating the consent, your selected form/calendar will be marked as compliant and ready to use with outbound calling workflows.

Building the Two-Part Workflow

The complete solution requires two connected workflows in GoHighLevel:

Workflow A: Appointment Trigger

  1. Trigger: Appointment status changes to "confirmed"
  2. Action 1: Update contact field with appointment time (using your custom field)
  3. Action 2: Initiate voice AI outbound call

Workflow B: Call Processing

  1. Trigger: Voice AI transcript generated
  2. Action 1: Process transcript with ChatGPT to create summary
  3. Action 2: Email full transcript and summary to business owner

This two-part structure ensures calls happen immediately after booking while still providing complete reporting on every interaction.

AI Agent Configuration

The final step is configuring your AI agent for outbound calls. Navigate to your voice AI agent and scroll to the Outbound Call section. Key settings include:

  • Initial Message: Brief introduction confirming the appointment and requesting if it's a good time to talk
  • Agent Goals: Set to Advanced mode and connect your knowledge base
  • Prompt: Instructions for handling the conversation and what information to confirm

The knowledge base is particularly important - this is where you provide answers to common customer questions like location, what to bring, business hours, etc. Without this training, the AI won't be able to answer basic customer queries.
