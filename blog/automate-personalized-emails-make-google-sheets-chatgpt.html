How the Automation Works

The workflow follows a simple but powerful sequence: Extract contact details → Generate personalized content → Create draft emails. What makes it special is how it maintains customization while automating the repetitive parts.

At its core, the system:

  1. Pulls structured data from Google Sheets (company name, industry, contact info)
  2. Feeds this to ChatGPT with instructions for personalized email generation
  3. Formats the output and creates drafts in your Gmail for final review

Pro tip: The video at 4:30 shows how adding simple filters prevents duplicate sends and ensures only valid email addresses get processed.

Google Sheets Integration

The automation starts with your contact database in Google Sheets. The tutorial uses columns for:

  • Company name
  • Website URL
  • Business category/industry
  • Contact name
  • Email address
  • Status (blank = ready to email)

The Make.com Google Sheets module pulls this data in batches of 25-50 contacts at a time. Smaller batches prevent API timeouts and allow for quality checks between runs.

Implementation note: At 3:50 in the video, you'll see the exact range configuration (A1:J25) that works reliably without overwhelming the system.

ChatGPT Personalization

The magic happens when structured data meets ChatGPT's natural language generation. The system prompt:

  1. Establishes your brand voice and services
  2. Provides an email template structure
  3. Inserts the variable fields (company name, industry)
  4. Requires specific subject line formatting

This creates emails that feel hand-written because they reference the recipient's actual business context, while maintaining consistency in your messaging.

Example output: "Hi [Company Name], I'm reaching out because I noticed your [Industry] business could benefit from..." - personalized yet scalable.

Gmail Drafting Process

Rather than sending emails directly, the workflow creates drafts in your Gmail account. This hybrid approach gives you:

  • Final quality control before sending
  • Ability to add last-minute personal touches
  • Confidence that automation hasn't introduced errors

The video at 5:40 demonstrates how parsed ChatGPT output flows seamlessly into Gmail drafts with proper formatting intact.

Best practice: Schedule your email review sessions in batches - perhaps every morning to clear that day's drafted emails.

Quality Control Measures

Two critical filters ensure only valid, ready-to-email contacts get processed:

  1. Status check: Only processes rows where the status column is blank (not "completed" or "drafted")
  2. Email validation: Confirms the email field contains an @ symbol to catch formatting errors

These simple checks prevent duplicate sends and ensure your outreach list remains clean and effective.

Implementation tip: Consider adding a third filter for website URLs to enable even deeper personalization in future versions.

Scaling Considerations

While this workflow handles batches of 25-50 contacts efficiently, larger campaigns require some adjustments:

  • Split contact lists into multiple sheets or tabs
  • Schedule runs at intervals to avoid API limits
  • Consider upgrading to ChatGPT 4 for more nuanced writing at scale
