The Manual Referral Problem

Job seekers face a brutal time sink when requesting referrals: researching each company, finding the right contacts, crafting personalized emails, and following up. Even with AI tools like ChatGPT, the manual process still consumes 15-20 minutes per request - making large-scale outreach impractical.

The breakthrough came when analyzing where time was being wasted: 60% on contact research, 30% on email personalization, and 10% on actual sending. By automating these components with n8n, we reduced the time per request from 20 minutes to just 2 minutes - a 90% reduction.

Key insight: The most valuable automation isn't sending bulk emails - it's the personalization at scale. This workflow maintains human-quality customization while handling the repetitive work.

Workflow Overview

This n8n workflow transforms a simple Google Sheet of target companies into personalized outreach emails through five automated steps:

  1. Input: Google Sheet with company URLs and job links
  2. Contact Discovery: Hunter.io finds relevant department contacts
  3. Research: LinkedIn data enriches each contact profile
  4. AI Composition: Gemini generates unique emails referencing the recipient's background
  5. Delivery: Gmail sends with attached resume and tracking

The magic happens in steps 3-4 where the AI cross-references your resume with each recipient's LinkedIn profile to highlight relevant experience. This creates emails that feel hand-written but are generated at scale.

Google Sheets Setup

The workflow starts with a Google Sheet containing three critical columns: Company URL (e.g. google.com), Job Link, and Position Name. This becomes your centralized control panel for all outreach.

To configure the Google Sheets API (timestamp 2:15 in the video):

  1. Create a new project in Google Cloud Console
  2. Enable Sheets API, Drive API, and Gmail API
  3. Create OAuth credentials with authorized redirect URIs from n8n
  4. Add test users to bypass verification during development

Pro Tip: Always use full company URLs (ibm.com) rather than names (IBM) - this ensures Hunter.io can accurately find domain-specific contacts.

Hunter API Configuration

Hunter.io powers the contact discovery with its database of professional emails. The free tier allows 10 emails per company - enough for most referral campaigns (timestamp 8:30).

Key configuration steps:

  1. Sign up at Hunter.io and retrieve your API key
  2. Set department filters matching your target roles (HR + IT for tech jobs)
  3. Configure the n8n Hunter node with your API key
  4. Test with 1-2 companies to verify email quality

The workflow automatically handles rate limits and batches requests to stay within free tier limits while maximizing coverage.

AI Email Generation

Gemini (formerly Bard) transforms basic contact info into compelling emails through a multi-step prompt (timestamp 14:50):

  1. Analyzes the recipient's LinkedIn profile for relevant details
  2. Cross-references with your resume to identify matching experience
  3. Generates a 3-paragraph email with personalized opener
  4. Includes the specific job link and your contact info

The prompt template in the Google Sheet allows easy customization of tone and structure. Each email reads as uniquely handwritten while maintaining consistent branding.

Gmail Integration

The final step automates sending through your Gmail account with tracking (timestamp 19:10):

  1. Configure Gmail API credentials in n8n
  2. Set your LinkedIn profile URL in the Google Sheet
  3. Upload your resume to Google Drive (set to "Anyone with link")
  4. Test with 1-2 emails to verify formatting

Each sent email includes your resume attachment and LinkedIn link while maintaining a human-like "Sent from my inbox" appearance to avoid spam filters.

Execution Options

Choose how automated you want the process to be:

  • Full Automation: Runs end-to-end from sheet to sent emails
  • Semi-Automated: Generates emails in sheet for manual review
  • Discovery Only: Just finds contacts without email generation

To modify, simply delete or disable nodes in the n8n workflow. The Google Sheet serves as the single source of truth for all variations.

For teams: Share the sheet with colleagues to collaborate on target lists while maintaining individual sending accounts through separate n8n credentials.
