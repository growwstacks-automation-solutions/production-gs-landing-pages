The Proposal Email Problem

Every service business faces the same tedious process - after finalizing a proposal, you need to manually draft an email to send it to the client. This involves copying their contact details from your CRM, pasting them into your email client, writing a personalized (but largely templated) message, and attaching the proposal document.

While tools like Inker can send proposals directly, personal emails from your actual account get 27% higher open rates according to recent email marketing studies. The challenge? Manual email creation eats up valuable time that could be spent on billable work or business development.

Time wasted: The average professional spends 2.3 hours per week just drafting proposal emails - time that could be automated while maintaining the personal touch that drives response rates.

How the Automation Works

This Make.com automation solves the proposal email bottleneck by connecting Monday.com to your Gmail (or other email provider). When you change a status in Monday.com - like moving from "Waiting for Meeting" to "Send Proposal" - the workflow automatically:

  1. Pulls the client's contact details from Monday.com
  2. Generates a draft email using your pre-approved template
  3. Inserts all relevant client-specific information
  4. Saves the complete draft in your email account

The result? You get a perfectly formatted, ready-to-send proposal email in your drafts folder - with all the personalization that drives response rates, but without any of the manual work. Setting Up the Trigger

The magic starts with configuring the status change trigger in Monday.com. This is the "when" of your automation - the specific action that tells Make.com to start the workflow. Here's how it works:

Step 1: Identify Your Trigger Column

Choose which status column in Monday.com will initiate the workflow. Most businesses use either a "Proposal Status" or "Project Stage" column with values like "Draft," "Ready to Send," and "Sent."

Step 2: Configure the Make.com Watch Trigger

In Make.com, set up a Monday.com trigger that monitors for changes to your specified column. The trigger should activate only when the status changes to your "send proposal" value (like "Ready to Send").

Step 3: Map the Client Data

Connect the Monday.com item fields to variables in your Make.com scenario. This ensures the client's name, email, company, and any custom fields flow correctly into your email template.

Pro tip: Add a 2-minute delay after the status change before creating the draft. This accounts for any lag in Monday.com's API and prevents duplicate emails if you accidentally change the status multiple times.

Designing Your Email Template

The heart of this automation is your email template - the structured message that gets personalized for each client. An effective proposal email template includes:

  • Dynamic fields: {Client Name}, {Company Name}, etc. that auto-fill from Monday.com
  • Branded greeting: Maintains your company's voice and tone
  • Clear next steps: What the client should do after reviewing the proposal
  • Attachment handling: Automatic inclusion of the proposal document

In Make.com, you can create multiple templates for different proposal types or client segments. The system will use the appropriate template based on rules you set - for example, sending a different version to enterprise clients versus small businesses.

Testing the Workflow

Before going live, thoroughly test your automation with these steps:

Step 1: Create Test Records

Add sample clients to your Monday.com board with all required fields populated. Mark them clearly as test records.

Step 2: Run Manual Triggers

Change the status on your test records and verify the draft emails appear correctly in your designated email folder.

Step 3: Check All Variables

Ensure every dynamic field (names, dates, amounts) populates correctly in the email body and subject line.

Step 4: Refine Timing

Adjust any delays to ensure the draft appears within 10-15 seconds of changing the status - fast enough to feel instant but slow enough to avoid API rate limits.

Testing checklist: Verify the email subject, body, attachments, recipient address, and sender name all match your requirements before deploying the workflow to your team.
