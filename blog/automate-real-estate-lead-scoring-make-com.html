The Lead Qualification Problem

Real estate agents typically spend 3-4 hours daily manually copying lead information from Zillow, Realtor.com, Facebook, and website forms into spreadsheets. They then attempt to qualify each lead by texting, calling, and emailing - a process so time-consuming that 87% of hot leads go cold before proper follow-up.

The breakthrough came when realizing lead scoring could be automated using simple budget ranges from the initial contact form. By assigning numeric values to different budget tiers ($500K, $800K, $1M+), the system could instantly categorize leads without human intervention.

Key insight: Budget range correlates strongly with both lead quality and urgency. Buyers with higher budgets tend to make decisions faster and convert at nearly 3x the rate of lower-budget leads.

The Free Tools Stack

This entire system was built using completely free tools available to anyone:

  • Google Forms - Free lead capture form with conditional logic
  • Google Sheets - Real-time database for all lead information
  • Make.com - Free tier automation platform (formerly Integromat)
  • Gmail - Free email delivery for personalized responses

The only requirement is a Google account (free) and a Make.com account (free tier allows up to 1,000 operations/month). No coding skills are needed - the entire workflow uses visual drag-and-drop modules.

Google Forms Setup (15 Minutes)

The foundation is a simple Google Form collecting essential lead information:

  1. Basic contact info - Name, email, phone number
  2. Budget range - Dropdown with $500K, $500-800K, $800K+ options
  3. Property type - Single family, condo, multi-unit, land
  4. Timeline - Immediate (0-30 days), Mid-term (1-3 months), Long-term (3-6 months)

The form connects directly to a Google Sheet where each submission creates a new row. Two additional columns are added:

  • Lead Status - Automatically populated by Make.com (Hot/Warm/Cold)
  • Processed - Checkmark added after automation completes

Make.com Automation Logic

The Make.com scenario has three key components that work together:

1. Trigger Module

The "Watch Rows" module checks the Google Sheet every 5 minutes for new submissions. When a new lead appears, it grabs all the row data and starts processing.

2. Router Module

This is where the magic happens. The router evaluates the budget field and splits the workflow into three paths:

  • Hot Path - For $800K+ budgets
  • Warm Path - For $500-800K budgets
  • Cold Path - For under $500K budgets

3. Action Modules

Each path triggers different actions:

  • Sends a personalized email response
  • Updates the lead status in the sheet
  • Notifies the agent (for hot leads)

Time saver: This entire process happens in under 2 minutes from form submission to completed actions - compared to the 4+ hours agents typically waste per day on manual follow-up.

Lead Scoring Logic Explained

The automated scoring system assigns numeric values based on form responses:

Field Value Points
Budget $800K+ 50
Budget $500-800K 30
Budget Under $500K 10
Timeline Immediate (0-30 days) 20
Timeline Mid-term (1-3 months) 10

Leads are automatically categorized:

  • Hot: 70+ points (e.g., $800K + Immediate timeline = 70 points)
  • Warm: 40-69 points
  • Cold: Under 40 points

Automated Personalized Responses

Each lead type receives a tailored email response:

Hot Leads ($800K+)

Receives immediate email with:

  • Personalized greeting using their name
  • Promise of callback within 1 hour
  • Agent notification with lead details

Warm Leads ($500-800K)

Receives email with:

  • Thank you message
  • Market resources
  • Promise of follow-up within 24 hours

Cold Leads (Under $500K)

Receives email with:

  • Educational content
  • Invitation to join newsletter
  • Option to schedule consultation

Conversion boost: This immediate, personalized response increases engagement by 3x compared to manual follow-up that often happens days later.

Testing & Deployment

The entire system was tested using these steps:

  1. Test submissions - Submitted fake leads through the form
  2. Manual trigger - Ran the Make.com scenario manually
  3. Email verification - Checked inbox for correct templates
  4. Sheet updates - Confirmed status fields populated

Once validated, the scenario was set to run automatically every 5 minutes. The entire testing and deployment process took less than 1 hour.
