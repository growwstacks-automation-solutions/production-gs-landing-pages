The Manual Access Problem

If you run a Skool community with valuable Pickaxe tools, you know the frustration. Every new member means another manual task - checking their signup, copying their email, logging into Pickaxe, and granting access. It's repetitive work that steals time from more important tasks.

Worse, delays in granting access create poor member experiences. Someone joins your Skool excited to use your tools, only to wait hours or days before they can actually access them. This manual process doesn't scale as your community grows.

Community builders spend 3-5 hours weekly manually managing access between platforms. Automation eliminates this completely while providing instant access to new members.

Zapier Basics for Automation

Zapier acts as a secure bridge between Skool and Pickaxe. When configured properly, it detects new Skool members and automatically adds them to your Pickaxe studio without any manual intervention.

The setup uses a simple "Zap" - Zapier's term for an automation workflow. This particular Zap has just two steps: 1) Trigger when someone joins your Skool community, and 2) Action to add them to your Pickaxe studio. The free Zapier plan handles this perfectly.

Setting Up the Skool Trigger

The first step is configuring Skool as your trigger app. In Zapier, select Skool and choose "New Paid Member" as your trigger event. This ensures the automation runs whenever someone joins your community.

You'll need to connect your Skool account to Zapier. The process is straightforward - just log in when prompted and authorize the connection. Once connected, Zapier can detect new members in real-time.

Configuring the Pickaxe Action

For the action step, you'll need your Pickaxe API key from your studio settings. As shown at the 2-minute mark in the video, this is found under the Zapier integration section in Pickaxe.

When configuring the action, you can map Skool member data to Pickaxe fields. At minimum, include their name and email. You can also set their access level or assign specific products, as demonstrated around the 4-minute mark in the tutorial.

Pro Tip: Keep your API key secure. If compromised, generate a new one immediately to revoke access from the old key.

Testing Your Automation

Before going live, thoroughly test your Zap. Zapier provides a testing mode that lets you verify each step works correctly. You'll see green check marks confirming successful data transfer between Skool and Pickaxe.

The video shows this testing phase around the 5-minute mark, where sample member data appears in Pickaxe exactly as configured. Testing ensures your automation works flawlessly before real members start coming through.

Advanced Configuration Options

While the basic setup handles most needs, you can expand this automation with Zapier's paid plans. These allow additional actions like sending welcome emails, adding members to your CRM, or triggering other workflows.

For communities with multiple membership levels, you can configure different Pickaxe access based on the Skool plan purchased. The video briefly mentions this capability when discussing premium versus standard access levels.
