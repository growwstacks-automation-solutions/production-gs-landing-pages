The Multilingual Content Challenge

Creating content in multiple languages presents a unique scaling problem. Every video needs to be:

  • Clipped into platform-specific formats (Reels, Shorts, etc.)
  • Given language-appropriate captions
  • Posted to language-matched social accounts
  • Tracked across all platforms

Most creators either limit themselves to one language or spend hours manually repurposing content. At 4:30 in the tutorial video, the creator explains how this workflow solved their specific pain of switching between Arabic, French and English content creation.

Before automation: Each video required 45-60 minutes of manual work to repurpose across platforms. After automation: The same process happens automatically in under 5 minutes.

Workflow Overview

This Make.com automation handles the entire multilingual social media workflow:

  1. Videos are uploaded to a designated Google Drive folder
  2. The system detects new videos and downloads them
  3. AI analyzes the video title to generate engaging captions
  4. A second AI model detects the video's primary language
  5. Content is routed to language-specific posting branches
  6. Videos are posted to all connected social platforms

The entire process runs automatically whenever new videos are added to the Drive folder, requiring zero manual intervention after initial setup.

Step 1: Video Organization

The workflow begins with video organization in Google Drive:

  • Create a "Social Content" folder in Drive
  • Set up Make.com to monitor this folder for new files
  • Configure the "Search Files" module to retrieve only video files
  • Set a limit of 1 file during testing to conserve operations

At 2:45 in the video, the creator demonstrates how they use macOS Automator to automatically organize videos by language before they even reach Drive. This optional step adds another layer of organization but isn't required for the core workflow.

Step 2: AI Caption Generation

The system uses AI to generate engaging captions based on video titles:

Key configuration: The AI is instructed to keep captions under 90 characters, use emojis when appropriate, and exclude any file extensions from the final output.

You can choose between different AI models (xAI, Gemini or GPT) based on your preferences. The tutorial creator prefers xAI for its accuracy with multilingual content, especially for Arabic captions.

The AI prompt includes:

  • System message defining the AI's role as a content writer
  • Clear instructions about length and formatting
  • Example outputs to guide the style

Step 3: Language Detection

A second AI model analyzes video titles to detect the primary language:

  • Configured to respond with only "English", "Arabic" or "French"
  • Routes content to the appropriate posting branches
  • Can be expanded to support additional languages

At 7:20 in the video, the creator shows how this detection works in practice, with the AI correctly identifying an Arabic-language video title and routing it to the Arabic content branch.

Pro tip: Test the language detection with sample titles before connecting to live posting workflows to ensure accurate routing.

Step 4: Platform-Specific Posting

The workflow includes separate branches for each language:

  • English content posts to English-language accounts
  • Arabic content posts to Arabic accounts
  • French content posts to French accounts

Each branch can connect to multiple platforms:

  • LinkedIn (personal and company pages)
  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • TikTok

The creator demonstrates at 9:45 how content flows through the Arabic branch to post to their Arabic social accounts automatically.
