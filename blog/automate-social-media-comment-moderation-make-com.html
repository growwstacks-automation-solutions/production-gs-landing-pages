How Make.com Solves This with AI Classification

The system's secret weapon is its real-time comment classification engine. When a new Instagram comment arrives (00:58 in the video), Make.com routes it through a ChatGPT module that analyzes:

  • Sentiment (positive/neutral/negative)
  • Intent (question/compliment/request)
  • Entity recognition (product mentions, service inquiries)

This happens in under 3 seconds—compared to human teams taking 2-3 minutes per comment. The classifier references your brand's keyword lists and banned phrases to ensure inappropriate content gets flagged for human review.

The 3 Automated Response Paths

At 01:45, the tutorial shows the decision tree that routes comments to appropriate responses. The system uses three primary paths:

1. FAQ Responses

Service-related questions ("How do I return my order?") trigger API calls to your knowledge base. The workflow pulls the exact FAQ answer, formats it for the platform, and posts within minutes.

2. Product Conversion Path

Comments mentioning products ("Love this dress!") receive responses with purchase links or special offers—turning engagement into sales without salesperson involvement.

3. General Appreciation

Simple compliments ("Great post!") get branded thank-you responses that maintain community connection without burdening your team.

Pro Tip: The system improves over time—it logs which automated responses get the most positive engagement and adjusts future replies accordingly.

Implementation: Step-by-Step Setup

Building this workflow requires connecting just five core components in Make.com:

Step 1: Social Platform Connections

Authenticate your brand's Instagram (and other platforms) via OAuth. The system needs read access to comments and write access to post replies.

Step 2: AI Classifier Module

Configure the ChatGPT module with your brand's keywords, product names, and common question phrases. This takes about 30 minutes of training with sample comments.

Step 3: Knowledge Base Integration

Connect your FAQ database, product catalog, or CRM. The system will reference these when crafting responses to technical questions.

Step 4: Response Templates

Create 10-15 base response templates in your brand voice. The AI will adapt these based on comment context.

Step 5: Escalation Rules

Define which comment types should route to human team members (like complaints or complex technical questions).
