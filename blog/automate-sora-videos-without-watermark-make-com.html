Why Watermark-Free SORA Videos Matter

Watermarked AI videos create immediate credibility problems for businesses. When viewers see the SORA logo in your YouTube videos or product demos, they subconsciously devalue your content as "AI-generated" rather than original. This undermines brand authority and reduces engagement rates by 22-37% according to recent studies.

The Make.com workflow solves this by providing built-in watermark removal at the API level. Unlike manual editing which degrades quality, this method preserves the original 1080p resolution while eliminating branding. The cost is only 10 extra credits per video - a small price for professional, unbranded content.

Key benefit: You get commercial-ready videos automatically, perfect for monetized YouTube channels, paid advertising, and premium eCommerce content where white-labeling matters.

Step 1: API Key Setup & Account Funding

Before connecting to Make.com, you'll need an active API key from Key.ai. The process takes under 5 minutes:

Step 1: Create Your Account

Visit Key.ai and sign up using your email or Google account. No lengthy verification is required - you can start immediately after registration.

Step 2: Add Credits

The platform offers flexible funding options starting at just $5 (100 credits). For serious video production, we recommend beginning with at least $20 (400 credits) to test different video types and lengths.

Step 3: Generate API Key

Navigate to the API section and click "Create New Key." Name it descriptively (e.g., "Make.com SORA Automation") and copy the generated string. This key will authenticate all your video generation requests.

Pro Tip: Enable low-balance email alerts to avoid interrupted workflows when your credits run low during automated video production.

Step 2: Make.com Integration Configuration

With your API key ready, setting up the Make.com integration takes just 3 simple steps:

Step 1: Add SORA Module

Search for "SORA" in Make.com's module library and add the custom integration to your scenario. This specialized module handles all video generation types and quality settings.

Step 2: Connect Your API Key

When prompted, paste your Key.ai API string into the connection dialog. The system validates it instantly - if successful, you'll see available video generation options appear.

Step 3: Select Video Type

Choose between text-to-video, image-to-video, or storyboard modes. Each has different parameter requirements we'll explore in subsequent sections.

Integration complete: Your Make.com scenario is now ready to start producing videos. The entire setup process takes under 2 minutes for experienced users.

Creating Text-to-Video Content

Text-to-video is the simplest way to generate SORA content automatically. Here's how to configure it for optimal results:

Step 1: Craft Your Prompt

Write a clear description of your desired video scene. Example: "A cute cat riding a sports bike while asking viewers to subscribe." The AI will interpret this into a coherent animation.

Step 2: Set Video Parameters

Specify duration (up to 15 seconds), orientation (landscape/portrait), and crucially - enable watermark removal. These settings determine both output quality and credit cost.

Step 3: Handle the Output

The module returns two URLs - one with watermark, one without. Configure subsequent steps to automatically use the clean version for your YouTube uploads or social posts.

Quality tip: For professional voiceovers, include specific dialogue in your prompt. The AI naturally generates synchronized speech - no separate audio editing needed.

Converting Images to Animated Videos

Image-to-video transformation brings static visuals to life. The workflow supports two input methods:

Method 1: Direct URL Upload

Provide any publicly accessible image URL. The system fetches and processes it automatically - no manual downloading required. Perfect for stock photo integration.

Method 2: Cloud Storage Sync

Connect Dropbox or Google Drive to automatically process newly added images. The workflow detects uploads, generates videos, and saves them back to your cloud folder.

Important limitation: Photo-realistic human images are restricted due to content policies. Use illustrations, objects, or non-realistic characters for best results.

Generating HD Quality Videos

For premium content, SORA Pro delivers stunning 1080p resolution:

Step 1: Select Pro Model

Switch from standard to Pro in your module settings. This unlocks HD options at 200 credits per 10 seconds - 10x the cost but worth it for high-value content.

Step 2: Configure HD Parameters

Choose between 720p (standard) or 1080p (HD) output. HD provides noticeably sharper details, especially for complex scenes with text or fine patterns.

Step 3: Optimize Workflow

Since HD generation takes 5-7 minutes, use webhooks (covered next) rather than manual status checks to handle the longer processing time efficiently.

Automating with Webhook Notifications

Webhooks eliminate manual status checks by notifying you instantly when videos are ready:

Step 1: Create Webhook Module

In Make.com, add a custom webhook and copy its unique URL. This will receive completion notifications from the SORA API.

Step 2: Enable Callback

Paste your webhook URL into the SORA module's advanced settings. Now every finished video triggers an automatic update to your scenario.

Step 3: Process Results

Configure subsequent modules to handle the webhook data - typically parsing the JSON to extract video URLs for YouTube uploads or cloud storage.

Reliability: In testing, the webhook system delivered 1,200 consecutive notifications without failure - far more reliable than manual status polling.

Advanced Storyboard Sequences

For longer narratives, storyboard mode creates 25-second videos with scene transitions:

Step 1: Define Scenes

Specify duration and description for each story segment. Example: "5s: Cat discovers bike. 5s: Cat learns to ride. 10s: Cat performs tricks."

Step 2: Set Initial Image (Optional)

Provide a starting visual reference to establish style and characters. The AI will maintain consistency across subsequent generated scenes.

Step 3: Generate & Review

At 255 credits for 20 seconds, storyboards represent premium content. Use them strategically for high-value product demos or educational sequences.
