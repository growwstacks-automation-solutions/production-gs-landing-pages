Zapier + Stripe Integration Overview

Zapier acts as a bridge between Stripe and your notification system. When a new payment occurs in Stripe, Zapier detects it instantly and triggers your chosen action - whether that's sending an email, Slack message, or even texting your phone.

The integration requires no coding and takes less than 10 minutes to set up. Once configured, it runs automatically in the background, checking for new payments every few minutes. You'll receive notifications within 1-2 minutes of each transaction completing.

Step 1: Setting Up Your Stripe Trigger

The first step is telling Zapier what event should start the automation. In this case, we want to trigger whenever a new payment appears in Stripe.

Step 1: Create a New Zap

Log into your Zapier account and click "Create Zap." This starts a new automation workflow. Name it something descriptive like "Stripe Payment Notifications."

Step 2: Choose Stripe as Your Trigger App

Search for and select Stripe from Zapier's app directory. Then choose "New Payment" as your specific trigger event.

Step 3: Connect Your Stripe Account

Zapier will prompt you to connect your Stripe account. Follow the authentication steps to grant Zapier read-only access to your payment data.

Step 4: Test the Trigger

Zapier will look for a recent payment in your Stripe account to verify the connection works. If successful, you'll see sample payment data appear.

Pro tip: At the 1:45 mark in the video tutorial, we show how to filter which payments trigger notifications - useful if you only want alerts for transactions over a certain amount.

Step 2: Configuring Email Notifications

With the trigger set up, we now define what happens when a new payment is detected. For most users, email notifications are the simplest solution.

Step 1: Add an Action Step

Click "Add Step" in your Zap and search for Gmail. Select "Send Email" as your action.

Step 2: Connect Your Gmail Account

Authenticate with Google to allow Zapier to send emails on your behalf. You can use any email address hosted by Gmail or Google Workspace.

Step 3: Customize Your Email Template

Compose your notification email using Stripe payment data. Include dynamic fields like:

  • Customer name and email
  • Payment amount and currency
  • Product/service purchased
  • Transaction date and ID

Step 4: Test and Publish

Send a test email to verify everything works, then publish your Zap to activate the automation.

Testing and Troubleshooting

Before relying on your automation, it's crucial to verify it works correctly. Here's how to test and fix common issues:

Trigger Testing

Zapier's test function should return sample payment data from your Stripe account. If empty, check your Stripe connection and ensure you have recent payments.

Email Delivery

Test emails should arrive in your inbox within minutes. Check spam folders if missing and whitelist Zapier's sending address if needed.

Data Accuracy

Compare notification details against actual Stripe transactions to ensure all fields populate correctly.

Monitoring

Zapier provides logs of all automation runs. Check periodically for errors or skipped payments.
