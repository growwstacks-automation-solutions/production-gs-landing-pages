How the Automated SWOT Workflow Works

The workflow follows a simple but powerful sequence: extract company data from Airtable, analyze with OpenAI, and return structured SWOT insights. Here's the step-by-step flow:

Step 1: Search Airtable Records

The Make.com scenario begins by querying your Airtable company directory. It searches across all fields (typically 10-15 per company) to gather comprehensive data for analysis.

Step 2: Parse Individual Records

Each company record is processed individually. The system extracts key details like company description, size, and other available fields to provide context for the SWOT analysis.

Step 3: Send to OpenAI

The company data is sent to GPT-4.1 with a specialized prompt that structures the response into clear Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats sections.

Step 4: Update Airtable

The completed SWOT analysis is matched to the original company record in Airtable and saved automatically, creating a permanent competitive intelligence asset.

In summary: Query records → Analyze with AI → Save insights. The entire process runs unattended once configured.

Optimizing the OpenAI Integration

The secret to high-quality SWOT analyses lies in the GPT-4.1 prompt engineering. Our optimized prompt includes:

  • Clear instructions: Directs the model to organize output into four distinct SWOT sections
  • Format requirements: Specifies bullet points for easy scanning
  • Contextual guidance: Encourages balanced, evidence-based analysis

The prompt transforms raw company data into boardroom-ready strategic analysis. For example, when analyzing Finage Capital, the system identified:
