The Manual Data Transfer Problem

Every time someone submits your Typeform, you face the same tedious task: copying their responses into a spreadsheet. This manual process isn't just time-consuming - it's error-prone. Missed entries, typos, and outdated records cost businesses an average of 15 hours per month in cleanup and verification.

The solution? Automating the entire transfer process with Zapier. By connecting Typeform directly to Rows, you eliminate human error while reclaiming those lost hours for meaningful work.

Manual data entry costs: For a form receiving 30 submissions daily, manual transfer consumes 2.5 hours/week. Over a year, that's 130 hours - more than three full work weeks wasted on copy-paste.

Setting Up Your Rows Spreadsheet

Before connecting your systems, prepare your Rows spreadsheet to receive automated submissions. At 1:15 in the video, you'll see how to create column headers that match your Typeform fields.

Create columns for each data point you want to capture - typically name, email, and any custom questions from your form. Pro tip: Add a "Timestamp" column to track when submissions arrive automatically.

Best practice: Structure your Rows spreadsheet exactly like your Typeform's response data. This 1:1 mapping makes the Zapier setup process smoother and reduces configuration errors.

Connecting Typeform to Zapier

Zapier acts as the bridge between Typeform and Rows. At 2:30 in the tutorial, you'll see how to set up Typeform as your trigger - the event that starts your automation.

Select "New Submission" as your trigger event, then connect your Typeform account. Zapier will test the connection by pulling in a sample submission to verify everything works.

Authorizing the Rows Connector

The crucial step shown at 3:45 is authorizing Rows in Zapier. You'll need your Rows API key, found under Workspace Settings → API.

Once authorized, select your target spreadsheet and worksheet. Zapier will automatically detect your column headers, preparing them for field mapping in the next step.

Mapping Fields from Typeform to Rows

Field mapping determines where each piece of Typeform data lands in Rows. At 4:20, the tutorial shows how to match form fields to spreadsheet columns.

For each Rows column, select the corresponding Typeform field from the dropdown. Test with a sample submission to verify all data transfers correctly before going live.

Testing Your Integration

Always test your Zap before relying on it. Submit a test form response and verify it appears in Rows within minutes (shown at 5:00).

Check that all fields mapped correctly, data formats appear as expected, and no information is missing. Fix any mismatches before activating your automation.

Scaling and Advanced Uses

Once basic submissions work, enhance your integration. Add filters to process only certain responses, or include calculation fields that auto-populate in Rows.

For high-volume forms, consider adding a Google Sheets step to archive all submissions before filtering relevant data to Rows. This creates a complete audit trail.
