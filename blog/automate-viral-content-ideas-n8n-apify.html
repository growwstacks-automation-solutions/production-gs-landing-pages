The Content Research Problem

Creating fresh, engaging content is one of the biggest challenges marketers face. According to recent studies, 63% of marketers struggle with consistently finding high-performing content ideas, while 42% admit to copying competitors because they lack better insights.

The traditional approach - manually checking platforms for inspiration - is time-consuming and often ineffective. Without proper metrics, it's hard to distinguish truly viral content from temporarily popular posts.

Key insight: The most valuable content ideas aren't just recent - they're posts that continue performing weeks or months after publication. This system automatically calculates "likes per day" and other longevity metrics to surface evergreen winners.

Integrating Apify With n8n

The magic happens when you connect Apify's scraping power with n8n's automation capabilities. The workflow:

  1. Form trigger: Clients or team members submit requests specifying niche, quantity, and platforms
  2. Platform filtering: n8n routes the request to the appropriate Apify actors
  3. Data processing: Extracts only the most valuable metrics from the raw scraped data
  4. Google Sheets output: Organizes results by platform with automatic performance scoring

Technical note: The system uses n8n's new Apify node (v1.0+) which supports direct SSO login and credential management, eliminating the need for API keys.

Platform-Specific Scrapers Explained

Each social platform requires slightly different approach based on what metrics are available and how the data structured:

TikTok

Scrapes captions, hashtags, sounds, and engagement metrics. The "likes per day" calculation helps identify content with staying power beyond initial virality.

Instagram

Collects both feed posts and stories data. Tracks saves (when available) to identify high-value content that viewers save for later.

YouTube

Separate scrapers for long-form (watch time metrics) and Shorts (swipe-through rate). The system can filter by duration.

LinkedIn

Focuses on text-based content performance in B2B spaces. Tracks comments and shares which companies are engaging.

Twitter/X

Modified version of the scraper featured in previous tutorials. Now tracks view counts (vs. just likes/RTs).

Reddit

Lightweight scraper tracks post mentions of brands/products plus sentiment analysis where available.

Meta Ads

Specialized scraper shows competitor ad campaigns, duration running, and creative approaches (but not ROAS data).

Analyzing Performance Metrics

The system doesn't just collect data - it processes to highlight the highest potential content ideas:

  • Engagement rate: (Likes+Comments)/Views for video platforms
  • Likes per day: Total likes divided by days since posting
  • Comment sentiment: Positive/negative ratio where available
  • Ad longevity: For Meta Ads, days running duration indicates performing creatives

These metrics help distinguish between flash-in-the-pan virality and content with staying power.

Google Sheets Output Structure

The final output organizes data into a client-ready format:

  1. Separate tabs for each platform
  2. Color-coded rows highlight top 10% performers
  3. Auto-calculated columns for key metrics
  4. Filter views by date range, engagement threshold

The sheet automatically updates when the workflow runs, giving clients fresh data without manual intervention.
