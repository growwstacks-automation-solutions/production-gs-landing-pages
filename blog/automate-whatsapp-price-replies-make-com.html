The WhatsApp Price Reply Problem

Every business owner receiving orders via WhatsApp faces the same exhausting routine: customers constantly asking "How much is this?" or "What's the price of that?" Each inquiry forces you to stop what you're doing, check your price list, then manually type out a response - often for the same products dozens of times per day.

The hidden cost goes beyond just time wasted. Each interruption breaks your focus, making it harder to handle important tasks. Customers waiting for price replies experience delays, potentially losing sales to competitors who respond faster. And human errors in quoting prices can lead to costly mistakes.

83% of customers expect price responses within 10 minutes on WhatsApp. Manual replies average 47 minutes - creating a 37% lost sales opportunity.

How Make.com Solves It

Make.com (formerly Integromat) provides a visual automation platform that connects WhatsApp with your product database. When a customer asks about prices, the system instantly:

  1. Detects price-related keywords ("how much", "price of")
  2. Identifies the product name in their message
  3. Searches your database for current pricing and availability
  4. Sends a perfectly formatted response in seconds

The magic happens through Make.com's AI Agent module, which understands natural language queries and fetches the exact information needed from your connected database (Google Sheets, Airtable, etc.).

Setting Up WhatsApp 360

The first module in your Make.com scenario watches for incoming WhatsApp messages containing price inquiries. Using WhatsApp 360 (formerly 360 Messenger), you'll:

  1. Create a new scenario in Make.com
  2. Add WhatsApp 360 as your first module
  3. Configure it to watch for new messages
  4. Set up your API connection using credentials from WhatsApp

Critical tip: Always test your WhatsApp connection immediately after setup to ensure messages flow correctly into Make.com. At 4:22 in the video tutorial, you'll see exactly how to validate this connection.

Configuring the AI Agent

The AI Agent acts as your virtual sales assistant, processing incoming messages and generating appropriate responses. Key configuration steps include:

  • Selecting your preferred AI model (GPT-4 works best for multilingual support)
  • Writing detailed instructions about your products and response style
  • Setting parameters for how to handle unavailable products

Pro Tip: Your AI instructions should include examples of perfect responses. "When a customer asks about Product X, respond with: 'Product X costs $Y and is currently in stock. Would you like to place an order?'"

Connecting Your Product Database

The system needs real-time access to your product information. Most businesses use Google Sheets for simplicity:

  1. Add the Google Sheets module to your scenario
  2. Configure it to search rows based on product names
  3. Map the spreadsheet columns to price and availability fields

When the AI Agent identifies a product inquiry, it automatically searches your sheet and returns the current data. The system updates instantly when you change prices or inventory levels in your spreadsheet.

Testing the Automation

Before going live, thoroughly test with various message formats:

  • Simple queries ("How much is Product A?")
  • Multiple products in one message
  • Misspelled product names
  • Out-of-stock items

At 7:15 in the video, you'll see a real test where the system perfectly handles a message asking "How much is Bella Vita Luxury perfume?" returning both price and availability information automatically.

Advanced Filtering

To prevent the system from responding to every message, add filters that only trigger on price-related inquiries:

  1. Add a filter module after the WhatsApp trigger
  2. Set conditions for messages containing "price", "how much", or other keywords
  3. Configure fallback options for non-price messages

This ensures your automation only engages when appropriate, maintaining personal touch for other conversations.
