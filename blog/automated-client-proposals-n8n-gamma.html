The Hidden Cost of Manual Proposals

Consultants and sales engineers lose countless hours recreating meeting discussions into proposals. After a 45-minute discovery call, you might spend 2-3 hours drafting slides - only to realize you missed key pain points the client mentioned.

This workflow solves three critical problems: First, it captures every detail from meeting transcripts while the conversation is fresh. Second, it structures the information using proven proposal frameworks. Third, it delivers presentation-ready decks in minutes instead of hours.

Time savings: Early adopters report saving 5-10 hours weekly per consultant - equivalent to $28,000+ annual cost savings per team member by reclaiming billable hours previously spent on administrative work.

Two-Part Workflow Architecture

The system uses a modular n8n design for maximum flexibility. Part one handles meeting logging - capturing dates, attendees, and key discussion points in a Google Sheet. Part two generates the actual Gamma proposal when approved.

Splitting these functions serves two purposes: First, not every meeting requires a proposal (introductory calls vs. deep dives). Second, it allows adding new post-meeting actions without rebuilding the entire workflow. You might later add CRM updates or follow-up email sequences.

Automated Meeting Logging

The workflow begins when Fireflies.ai finishes transcribing a meeting. An n8n webhook receives the completion notice, then polls Fireflies' API until the full transcript is ready (typically 5-15 minutes post-call).

Key technical details: The system extracts and cleans attendee lists using Claude AI to normalize names and titles. It identifies action items and discussion highlights through Fireflies' built-in AI analysis. Everything logs to Google Sheets with a unique meeting ID for tracking.

Pro tip: Adding a "status" column (NA/pending/generated) helps track which meetings have associated proposals and prevents duplicate work.

The Critical Human Approval Step

After logging meeting details, the system sends a Slack message asking: "Would you like to generate a proposal for [Meeting Title]?" This human gate serves three purposes:

  1. Quality control: Not all calls merit formal proposals
  2. Timing flexibility: You might want to discuss internally first
  3. Workflow transparency: Clear audit trail of what was generated when

Clicking "yes" triggers the Gamma generation, while "no" simply updates the sheet status to "declined." The approval rate in production hovers around 60-70% - proving its effectiveness at filtering unnecessary work.

Gamma Deck Generation Process

When approved, the workflow feeds meeting details to an AI agent acting as a "senior solutions consultant." The prompt instructs it to:

  • Analyze transcript insights for business challenges
  • Structure proposals with executive summaries, ROI calculations
  • Make confident assumptions about missing details
  • Use clear placeholders for client-specific numbers

The AI outputs formatted text that n8n sends to Gamma's API. Typical decks include 6-8 slides covering: problem statement, proposed solution, financial impact (average $28K savings in examples), implementation roadmap, and why the client should choose your firm.

Customizing Proposal Templates

Gamma offers multiple template options through its API. The workflow currently uses:

  • Theme: Professional (clean layouts with subtle branding)
  • Model: Consultant (optimized for B2B services)
  • Image source: Generic business illustrations

You can create custom themes matching your brand guidelines. Most businesses maintain 3-5 variations for different service lines or client types. The "preserve" mode ensures your templates maintain consistent styling while allowing dynamic content insertion.

How ROI Gets Calculated Automatically

The system transforms discussion points into financial metrics using a simple formula:

Time savings × hourly rate × weeks affected

For example, if a client mentions spending 10 hours weekly on manual processes, and your solution automates 70% of that work at their $100/hour rate, the deck shows:

7 hours/week × $100 × 52 weeks = $36,400 annual savings

These calculations appear in both the executive summary and dedicated ROI slides. The numbers update automatically based on transcript analysis - no manual spreadsheet work required.

Implementation Roadmap

Every proposal includes a visual 4-week implementation timeline showing:

  • Week 1: Requirements finalization
  • Week 2: System configuration
  • Week 3: Testing and adjustments
  • Week 4: Training and handoff

The workflow automatically adjusts these phases based on project complexity inferred from the meeting. Larger engagements might show 6-8 week plans, while simpler solutions suggest 2-3 week turnarounds.
