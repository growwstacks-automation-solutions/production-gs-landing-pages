The Problem With Manual Follow-ups

Every business owner knows the frustration of manual email outreach. You send an initial email, mark your calendar for follow-ups, and then... life happens. The follow-up gets delayed, or worse, forgotten entirely. Even when you do remember, you're left copying templates, updating spreadsheets, and wasting precious time on repetitive tasks.

Studies show that 80% of sales require 5 follow-ups, yet most businesses stop after 2. Why? Because manual follow-up is tedious, error-prone, and scales poorly. That's where automation comes in.

The hidden cost: For a business sending just 50 outreach emails per week with 3 follow-ups each, that's 200 manual emails monthly - easily 10+ hours of wasted time that could be automated.

Workflow Overview

This n8n workflow solves the manual follow-up problem by automating your entire email outreach sequence. Here's how it works at a high level:

  1. A scheduled trigger runs at your chosen interval (daily, weekly, etc.)
  2. Postgres queries identify contacts due for initial outreach or follow-ups
  3. Resend sends personalized emails via API
  4. Webhooks capture replies and update contact status
  5. Engaged contacts get moved to a "warm" list automatically

The system handles all the timing, sequencing, and tracking - you just focus on conversations with interested prospects.

Setting Up Postgres Database

Configuring Resend Email

Resend provides reliable email delivery with excellent deliverability. At 8:15 in the video, you'll learn how to:

  1. Create a Resend account and verify your domain
  2. Generate API keys for n8n authentication
  3. Structure the HTTP request to send emails via API
  4. Handle rate limits (Resend allows 2 emails/second)

The workflow includes a loop with delays to stay within Resend's limits while processing larger contact batches.

Building the n8n Workflow

The core workflow has three main components (shown at 12:40 in the video):

1. Schedule Trigger

Configure how often the workflow runs - daily, weekly, or custom intervals that match your sales cycle.

2. Postgres Query

Retrieves contacts who are due for either initial outreach or follow-ups based on date fields.

3. Email Sending Loop

Sends emails via Resend API with proper rate limiting and error handling.

Key Feature: The workflow includes filters that prevent sending follow-ups to contacts who have already replied, maintaining professional communication automatically.

Handling Responses

At 18:30 in the video, you'll see how the workflow handles replies:

  • Resend webhooks notify n8n when emails receive replies
  • The workflow updates Postgres to mark contacts as "replied"
  • Engaged contacts can be moved to a separate "warm" list
  • Future follow-ups are automatically suppressed for these contacts

This creates a complete system where outreach, follow-up, and response handling all happen automatically.

Scaling Considerations

As your outreach grows, keep these factors in mind:

  • Rate limiting: The workflow includes delays to stay within Resend's 2 emails/second limit
  • Database performance: Postgres handles thousands of contacts easily, but queries should be optimized
  • List hygiene: Automatically remove bounced emails and invalid addresses
  • Personalization: Use merge tags to keep emails feeling human even at scale

At 22:10 in the video, you'll see how to modify the workflow for larger contact lists while maintaining performance.
