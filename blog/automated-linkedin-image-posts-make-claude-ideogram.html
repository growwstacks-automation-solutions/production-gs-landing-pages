The Problem With LinkedIn Content

Maintaining an active LinkedIn presence is crucial for B2B businesses, but creating quality content consistently is time-consuming. Most marketers and business owners face three core challenges: coming up with fresh ideas daily, writing engaging posts that sound human, and creating professional visuals to accompany them.

This automation solves all three by combining AI content generation with human oversight. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how raw bullet points transform into polished LinkedIn posts complete with hashtags and AI-generated images - all while preserving your brand voice.

Businesses using this system report saving 5-7 hours per week on content creation while actually increasing their posting frequency from 1-2 times per week to daily. The human approval step ensures quality control while eliminating the blank page problem.

How The Automation Works

The system runs on two Make.com scenarios that work together. Scenario 1 handles content generation, while Scenario 2 manages the approval and publishing workflow. Everything starts in a Google Sheet that serves as your content calendar.

When you add a new row with raw notes and select a post type (text, image, or carousel), the automation kicks off. For image posts, Claude AI generates both the post copy and an optimized image prompt. The image prompt goes to Ideogram's API which creates professional visuals suited for LinkedIn.

Image Generation Process

What makes this system unique is how it handles image generation. Unlike manual processes where you'd need design skills or to brief a designer, Claude AI writes the image prompt automatically based on your content. The prompt gets optimized for visual appeal and brand consistency.

Ideogram then generates the image and returns a URL, which gets stored in your Google Sheet. The approval email includes both the post copy and a link to preview the image. At 5:30 in the video, you can see how the generated image appears exactly as it will on LinkedIn - no surprises when it goes live.

The entire image creation process happens in under 90 seconds, yet produces professional results that would take most marketers 30+ minutes to create manually. The system can maintain consistent visual branding across all posts by incorporating your style preferences into the AI prompts.

Approval Workflow

Human approval is the secret sauce that makes this automation work for businesses. When content is generated, you receive an email with the draft post and image preview. A simple reply of "approve" triggers publishing, while any edits you make get incorporated automatically.

The system uses Gmail's thread ID to connect your approval back to the original content in Google Sheets. Scenario 2 in Make.com watches for your reply, then routes the post to the correct publishing path based on its type. At 7:45 in the video, you'll see how seamless the approval process is in action.

Carousel Document Posts

Beyond text and image posts, the system also automates LinkedIn carousels. For these, Claude generates the content which gets merged with a branded HTML template from Google Drive. PDF.co converts this to a professional-looking PDF that gets stored and linked in your content calendar.

At 9:20 in the video, you can see sample carousel pages with clean formatting and branding. While LinkedIn's API restrictions currently require manual upload for carousels (pending partner approval), the automation handles everything up to that final step, saving hours of document creation time.

Platform Requirements

This automation connects several platforms through Make.com. You'll need accounts with: Make.com (for orchestration), Claude API (content generation), Ideogram (image creation), Google Workspace (Sheets, Drive, Gmail), and a LinkedIn Business account with API access.

An important note at 10:30 in the video explains LinkedIn's API tiers - while text and image posts work with standard access, carousel uploads require partner-level approval. The system is designed to accommodate all three post types, with carousel publishing activating automatically once approved.

The entire tech stack costs under $100/month for most businesses, paying for itself many times over in saved content creation time and increased LinkedIn engagement.
