The Payment Problem Women Business Owners Face

Late payments are one of the most frustrating challenges for women entrepreneurs with subscription-based services or recurring clients. You deliver excellent work on time, but when payment day comes, you're left sending awkward follow-up emails or making uncomfortable phone calls.

This isn't just about cash flow - it's about respect for your time and boundaries. Studies show women business owners are 30% more likely to experience late payments than male counterparts, often because clients unconsciously deprioritize their invoices.

The hidden cost: Chasing payments wastes 3-5 hours per week for most solopreneurs - time that could be spent serving clients or growing your business. Worse, the mental energy spent worrying about unpaid invoices creates unnecessary stress.

How Automation Solves Late Payments

The solution isn't working harder to chase payments - it's creating a system that prevents late payments in the first place. Automated reminders sent at strategic intervals keep your invoices top of mind for clients without you having to ask.

Behavioral research shows that reminders sent at 21, 14, and 7 days before a due date improve on-time payment rates by 60-80%. The key is sending them automatically, consistently, and professionally - which is exactly what this Make.com workflow does.

Payment Reminder System Overview

This automated system runs quietly in the background, handling all your payment reminders while you focus on your business. Here's how it works at 3:15 in the video tutorial:

  1. Daily checks your client database for upcoming payments
  2. Calculates how many days remain until each payment is due
  3. Sends personalized email reminders at 21, 14, and 7-day intervals
  4. Updates your records with the last reminder date sent

Key benefit: Your clients receive professional, timely reminders without you lifting a finger. No more forgotten payments or awkward conversations.

Setting Up the Make.com Workflow

The automation begins by creating a new scenario in Make.com (formerly Integromat). At 4:50 in the video, you'll see how to:

  1. Click "Create Scenario" from your Make.com dashboard
  2. Add Google Sheets as your first module to connect your client database
  3. Configure it to search rows in your payment tracking spreadsheet

This connection allows the automation to access all your client payment information - names, email addresses, amounts due, and due dates.

Connecting Your Google Sheets Data

Your spreadsheet needs just a few key columns to make this system work effectively, as shown at 6:30 in the tutorial:

  • Client name and email address
  • Payment amount due
  • Payment due date
  • Payment status (paid/unpaid)
  • Last reminder date (this will auto-update)

The system checks this data daily but only acts when it finds upcoming payments that haven't been marked as paid.

Calculating Days Until Payment is Due

The magic happens when the system calculates exactly how many days remain before each payment is due (demonstrated at 8:15). Here's how it works:

  1. Add a "Set Variable" module from the Tools section
  2. Configure it to subtract today's date from the payment due date
  3. This gives you the number of days remaining (or overdue)

Why this matters: This calculation determines which reminder email to send - whether the payment is 21, 14, or 7 days away. The difference in these numbers triggers different message sequences.

Configuring the Email Reminders

At 11:40 in the video, you'll see how to set up the three reminder emails that go out at different intervals:

  1. 21-day notice: Friendly early reminder about the upcoming payment
  2. 14-day notice: More specific reminder with payment details
  3. 7-day notice: Final reminder with clear instructions

Each email template automatically inserts the client's name, amount due, due date, and days remaining - making every message personalized without manual work.

Automatically Updating Client Records

The final piece (shown at 15:50) ensures your records stay current by:

  1. Adding an "Update Spreadsheet" module after each email
  2. Configuring it to note the date each reminder was sent
  3. This creates an audit trail showing all reminder activity

Now you'll always know when reminders were sent, eliminating guesswork about whether you've followed up with a client.
