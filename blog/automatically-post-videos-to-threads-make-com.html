The Threads Automation Challenge

Most marketers know they should post daily on Threads - but between filming, editing, writing captions, and actually posting, it's easy to fall behind. The platform's lack of native automation tools makes consistent posting a manual, time-consuming process.

This workflow solves three key problems: First, it automatically pulls videos from your Google Drive. Second, it generates engaging captions using AI. Third, it posts to Threads through Buffer - all without you needing to open any apps.

Key stat: Marketers who post daily on Threads see 3.2x more engagement than those posting weekly - but 78% struggle with consistency due to the manual effort required.

Setting Up Google Drive Integration

The foundation of this automation is connecting Make.com to your Google Drive where videos are stored. Unlike simpler integrations, Google Drive requires special permissions to work with automation tools.

At the 2:15 mark in the video, you'll see the critical step of making your Drive folder publicly shareable. This allows Make.com to access your content. The workflow uses the "Watch Files and Folders" module to detect new videos added to your specified folder.

For security, the automation includes a cleanup step that deletes the video from Drive after posting (with a 5-minute delay to ensure successful upload). This prevents accumulating unused files while maintaining privacy.

AI-Powered Caption Generation

Blank posts perform poorly - but writing fresh captions for every video takes time. This workflow solves that by automatically generating captions from your video filenames using Groq 3 Mini AI.

The AI prompt (shown at 4:30 in the video) instructs the system to: 1) Create a title from the filename 2) Add relevant emojis 3) Remove unnecessary text 4) Keep it under 90 characters. For best results, name your videos descriptively (e.g., "How_to_make_sourdough_bread.mp4").

Pro tip: If your videos aren't properly named, you can add an extra AI step to transcribe the audio and generate captions from the content itself - though this uses more credits.

The Buffer Workaround

Here's the clever part: Since Make.com doesn't directly integrate with Threads, we use Buffer as a bridge. At 8:45 in the video, you'll see how to connect your Threads account to Buffer's free tier.

The workflow uploads videos to Buffer instead of Threads directly. Buffer then handles the actual posting to Threads on your behalf. This approach works because Buffer maintains an official Threads integration that Make.com lacks.

Buffer also provides scheduling capabilities, allowing you to space out your automated posts throughout the day for maximum reach. The free plan supports up to 3 social channels.

Zapier as the Missing Link

To connect Make.com to Buffer, we use Google Sheets as a data bridge and Zapier as the trigger. At 10:20 in the video, you'll see how the workflow:

  1. Saves video URLs and AI-generated titles to a Google Sheet
  2. Uses Zapier's free plan to watch for new rows in that sheet
  3. Triggers Buffer to add the video to your queue

This three-step handoff might seem complex, but it solves the critical integration gap between Make.com and Threads. The entire process happens automatically once configured.

Scheduling and Maintenance

The final piece is setting the automation schedule and adding cleanup steps. At 12:30 in the video, you'll see how to:

  • Set the Make.com scenario to run daily (or more frequently)
  • Add a 5-minute delay before deleting source files
  • Monitor for failed uploads in Buffer

The sleep module is crucial - it ensures Buffer has enough time to process the video before Make.com deletes the original file from Drive. For longer videos, you may need to increase this delay.

Scaling to Other Platforms

While this tutorial focuses on Threads, the same workflow can be adapted for other platforms:

  • For Instagram: Use Buffer's Instagram connection
  • For TikTok: Modify the Zapier step to use TikTok's API
  • For YouTube: Use Make.com's native YouTube module

The AI caption system works across all platforms, and you can customize the prompts to generate platform-specific captions (e.g., longer descriptions for YouTube, hashtags for Instagram).
