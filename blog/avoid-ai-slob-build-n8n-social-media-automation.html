The AI Slob Problem

Business owners know they should post daily on social media, but generic AI-generated content often does more harm than good. The telltale signs — unnatural captions, robotic voiceovers, and stock-looking visuals — make brands appear inauthentic.

The breakthrough came when we realized AI works best when augmenting real content rather than creating from scratch. By starting with your existing product photos or service images (which every business has), we maintain authenticity while still automating the repetitive parts.

Key insight: The most effective AI workflows use your real content as the foundation. This workflow transforms your existing library of images into videos while preserving your brand's authentic look and feel.

How the Workflow Works

This n8n automation solves the AI slob problem through a carefully designed 9-step process that maintains human oversight while automating the heavy lifting:

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

The workflow begins with a schedule trigger set to run daily at 9 AM. This ensures consistent posting without requiring manual initiation.

Step 2: Random Image Selection

A JavaScript node randomly selects one image from your Google Drive folder containing product/service photos. Randomization prevents repetitive content.

Step 3: CDN Upload

The selected image gets uploaded to Pallyy's content delivery network (CDN) to create a publicly accessible URL needed for video generation services.

Pro tip: Having at least 10-15 quality source images ensures sufficient variation in your automated posts.

Content Repurposing Process

The magic happens in steps 4-6 where your static images transform into engaging social media content:

Prompt Engineering: An AI agent (GPT-4 in this example) generates detailed video prompts based on your image's filename/description. This ensures the generated video matches your brand style.

Video Generation: The image and prompt get sent to Wavspeed's video generation model (or similar service) which creates an 8-second marketing reel with matching audio.

Caption Overlay: Submagic analyzes the audio and overlays animated captions in your preferred style, crucial for viewers watching without sound.

Why this works: Starting with your real images prevents the "stock photo" look, while AI handles the time-consuming video editing and captioning.

Human Approval System

The workflow includes a critical human approval step before posting:

After content generation completes, the system emails you a preview link to the finished reel with captions. You can:

  • Approve: The post gets scheduled automatically
  • Reject: The workflow selects another image and regenerates content

This guardrail prevents poor-quality AI content from reaching your audience while still saving hours of manual work. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you can see exactly how the approval email appears.

Implementation note: The approval system can be adapted to Slack, Teams, or other platforms if email isn't your preferred channel.

Platform Integrations

The final steps handle cross-platform posting:

CDN Re-upload: Approved videos get uploaded to Pallyy's CDN to ensure reliable access across platforms.

Multi-platform Posting: The workflow demonstrates posting to Instagram but can be extended to TikTok, LinkedIn, and Twitter/X by adding parallel branches in n8n.

Pallyy serves as the central hub for platform connections, handling API complexities so you don't need separate integrations for each social network.

Cost Breakdown

While cheaper than hiring a social media manager, this automation does require some monthly investments:

  • Pallyy: $29/month (auto-posting)
  • Submagic: $23/month (captioning)
  • Wavspeed: ~$1.20 per 8-second video
  • GPT-4: Minimal cost for prompt generation

Total estimated $80-90/month when posting daily — a fraction of the cost for manual content creation while maintaining higher quality than full-AI approaches.

Cost-saving tip: Explore alternative video generation services like Kaiber or Fable for potentially lower per-video costs.
