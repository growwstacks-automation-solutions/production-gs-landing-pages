Why Connect Shopify to Make.com?

Manual data entry between Shopify and other business systems creates bottlenecks, errors, and wasted time. A typical eCommerce store spends 5-10 hours weekly exporting order data, updating inventory spreadsheets, and copying customer information between platforms.

By connecting Shopify to Make.com, you create real-time automated workflows that eliminate these repetitive tasks. The integration uses Shopify's official API with enterprise-grade security, ensuring data flows accurately without manual intervention.

Key benefit: Stores using Shopify-Make.com automations report 80% fewer data errors and save an average of 7 hours per week on administrative tasks.

Step 1: Create Your Make.com Scenario

Every automation in Make.com begins with a scenario - essentially a container for your workflow logic. To start:

  1. Log into your Make.com account
  2. Click "Create a new scenario" at the top of the dashboard
  3. Name your scenario (e.g., "Shopify Order Processing")

The blank scenario canvas will appear, ready for you to add your first Shopify trigger module. This is where the real automation begins.

Step 2: Add Shopify Trigger

Triggers determine what Shopify event will start your automation. Common triggers include new orders, updated products, or customer registrations.

  1. Click the "+" icon to add your first module
  2. Search for "Shopify" in the app selector
  3. Choose your trigger type (e.g., "Watch Orders")

Pro tip: Start with simple triggers like new orders before building complex multi-step workflows. This helps isolate any connection issues.

Step 3: Authenticate Shopify Connection

The first time you add a Shopify module, Make.com will prompt you to create a connection. This secure OAuth process ensures only authorized access to your store data.

  1. Click "Add" next to the connection field
  2. Enter your Shopify store subdomain (e.g., "yourstore" from yourstore.myshopify.com)
  3. Click "Save" then authorize the connection in Shopify's popup

Once authorized, Make.com will remember this connection for future scenarios. You can manage or revoke access anytime in your Shopify admin under "Apps."

Step 4: Test Your Connection

Before building complex workflows, verify your basic connection works:

  1. Run the scenario once manually (click the play icon)
  2. Check if recent Shopify orders appear in the execution data
  3. Add a simple action like sending a test email to confirm the flow

If the test fails, double-check your Shopify subdomain spelling and ensure you've granted all requested permissions during authorization.

Troubleshooting Connection Issues

If your Shopify-Make.com connection isn't working:

  1. Verify subdomain: It should be just the first part of your Shopify URL (e.g., "storename" from storename.myshopify.com)
  2. Check permissions: Re-authenticate if you see "access denied" errors
  3. Test triggers: Create a test order/product in Shopify to trigger the scenario
  4. Review logs: Make.com's execution history shows detailed error messages

Most connection issues resolve by carefully re-entering the subdomain and reauthorizing the app permissions in Shopify.
