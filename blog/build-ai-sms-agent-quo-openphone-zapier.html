Why Automate SMS Responses?

Modern customers prefer texting over calling - 75% of millennials would rather text than call a business. But most small businesses struggle to respond quickly, especially after hours. Missed texts mean missed opportunities and frustrated customers.

An AI SMS agent solves this by providing instant, accurate responses 24/7. It can handle common inquiries like business hours, pricing, appointment booking, and FAQ answers - freeing up your team for more complex customer needs.

Businesses using AI SMS agents report: 40% faster response times, 30% reduction in repetitive inquiries, and 25% higher customer satisfaction scores compared to manual texting.

How the AI SMS Agent Works

The system has three key components: Quo (OpenPhone) provides your business phone number and SMS API, Zapier connects everything together, and the AI chatbot generates intelligent responses.

When someone texts your number, Quo triggers the Zapier workflow. Zapier sends the message to your AI chatbot, which analyzes it against your knowledge base (website, FAQs, product info). The AI formulates a response, which Zapier sends back through Quo - all in seconds.

Setting Up Your Zapier Chatbot

Start by creating a free Zapier account. Navigate to the Chatbots section and click "Create Chatbot." The key configuration happens in two places: the instructions and knowledge base.

In the instructions, clearly state your company name and that this is for SMS responses. For example: "You are [Company]'s SMS support agent. Provide concise, helpful answers to customer questions via text message."

Pro Tip: Set your knowledge base to refresh daily or weekly so your AI stays current with website updates, pricing changes, or new FAQs.

Connecting Quo (OpenPhone)

In Quo (OpenPhone), generate an API key in your account settings. This allows Zapier to monitor your number for incoming messages. You can select specific numbers to monitor or apply the automation to all your business lines.

The connection takes less than 2 minutes. Zapier will test the connection by pulling sample message data to confirm everything works before proceeding to the next steps.

Building the Zapier Workflow

The workflow has just three steps: trigger on incoming Quo messages, process with your Zapier chatbot, then send the AI response back through Quo.

For the trigger, select "Incoming Message Received" from Quo. For the action, choose your configured chatbot. Use the sender's phone number as the conversation key so the AI maintains separate threads for each customer.

Finally, set up the reply action using Quo's "Send Message" function. Map the AI's response to the message field and use the original sender's number as the recipient.

Training Your Knowledge Base

Your AI's effectiveness depends on the quality of your knowledge base. Upload your website URLs, product PDFs, FAQ documents, and any other relevant materials.

Zapier can connect to Google Docs, Notion, and even Airtable bases. For dynamic information like pricing or inventory, use Zapier Tables that update automatically when your data changes.

Best Practice: Start with 5-10 key knowledge sources, then expand based on the questions customers actually ask. Review conversation logs monthly to identify gaps.
