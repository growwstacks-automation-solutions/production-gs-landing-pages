Step 1: Creating Your AI Assistant

We'll use Vapi.ai as our platform for building the voice agent. After signing up, create a new assistant and select the blank template. Name it something like "[Your Business] Receptionist".

For the AI model, select GPT-4 for the best conversational ability. Set the first message mode to "Assistant speaks first" so it greets callers proactively. The initial greeting should match how your business answers calls - for example: "Thanks for calling [Business Name], this is [Agent Name]. How can I help you today?"

Pro Tip: Record your best receptionist handling a few calls, then transcribe those conversations to use as training examples for your AI agent. This helps it learn your business's specific phrasing and workflow.

Step 2: Configuring The AI Personality

The system prompt defines how your AI agent behaves. A good prompt includes:

  1. Role definition: "You are [Name], a friendly professional receptionist at [Business]. Your primary function is to provide a smooth booking experience."
  2. Task breakdown: Outline the 3-step process (greeting, checking availability, booking)
  3. Conversation examples: Show ideal interactions so the AI can mimic them
  4. Business context: Your services, pricing, policies, etc.

For the voice, select one that matches your brand personality. The tutorial uses "Sarah" - a warm, professional female voice - but you can choose from multiple options.

Step 3: Connecting To Your Calendar

Here's where the magic happens - connecting your AI agent to your actual calendar. We'll use n8n as our automation platform to bridge Vapi.ai and Google Calendar.

Create a new n8n workflow called "Check Availability". Add a webhook node to receive requests from Vapi, then connect it to:

  1. An AI node that interprets the customer's requested time
  2. A Google Calendar node that checks real availability
  3. A response node that sends available slots back to Vapi

When a customer asks "Do you have anything tomorrow at 2pm?", your AI will now check actual calendar availability and respond accurately.

Step 4: Setting Up Confirmation Emails

After booking an appointment, customers should receive a confirmation email with all details. In n8n, create a second workflow called "Book Appointment" that:

  1. Receives the booking details from Vapi
  2. Creates a calendar event with the customer's information
  3. Sends a confirmation email via Gmail
  4. Returns a success message to Vapi to confirm to the customer

The email template should include the service type, date/time, business address, cancellation policy, and any preparation instructions. You can customize it to match your existing confirmation emails.

Testing Your AI Agent

Before going live, thoroughly test your agent with various scenarios:

  • Different service types (e.g., "oil change" vs. "tire rotation")
  • Various time requests ("tomorrow at 2pm" vs. "next Tuesday morning")
  • Full vs. unavailable time slots
  • Customer rescheduling or canceling

At 4:32 in the video tutorial, you can see a complete test call where the AI agent handles an oil change booking flawlessly - checking real calendar availability, collecting customer details, sending a confirmation email, and ending the call professionally.

Important: Always include a way for customers to reach a human if needed. Program your AI to say "If you'd prefer to speak with someone, please say 'representative' or call back during our business hours."
