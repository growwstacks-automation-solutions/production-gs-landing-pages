Step 1: Setting Up Your Retail AI Voice Agent

Creating your voice agent begins in the Retail AI dashboard. The single-prompt agent type shown at 3:15 in the video is perfect for most business use cases:

  1. Log in to Retail AI and click "Create Agent"
  2. Select "Voice Agent" then "Single Prompt Agent"
  3. Choose your AI model (GPT-4.1 works well for most cases)
  4. Select a voice that matches your brand personality
  5. Set your timezone for accurate scheduling

Pro tip: Use the chat testing feature (shown at 8:30) to refine your agent's responses before connecting it to a phone number. This lets you quickly iterate on the prompt without customers hearing unfinished versions.

Step 2: Configuring the FAQ Knowledge Base

At 10:45 in the tutorial, you'll see how to upload a PDF with your services and pricing. This becomes the AI's reference for answering common questions:

  1. Prepare a PDF with your services, prices, hours, and policies
  2. In Retail AI, go to Knowledge Base → Add New
  3. Name your knowledge base (e.g., "Auto Shop Services")
  4. Upload your PDF or connect to your website
  5. Test by asking your agent questions from the document

The demo shows the AI correctly answering "What's the price of a basic service?" by pulling the $220 figure from the uploaded PDF. This eliminates the need to hardcode prices in your prompt.

Step 3: Building the n8n Booking Workflow

The most powerful part begins at 14:20 when we build the n8n workflow that connects Retail AI to Google Calendar. Here's the key architecture:

  1. Webhook trigger receives booking requests from Retail AI
  2. Availability check queries your calendar for open slots
  3. Time conversion handles timezone adjustments (critical at 18:45)
  4. Calendar integration creates the appointment if available
  5. Alternative suggestions finds next available slot if booked
  6. Response handler sends confirmation back to the caller

At 22:10, you'll see the complete workflow in action, handling both successful bookings and alternative time suggestions seamlessly.

Step 4: Connecting Your Business Phone Number

The final step at 31:00 shows how to connect your live phone number:

  1. Purchase a local number through Retail AI or bring your own via SIP
  2. Assign your published agent to the number
  3. Configure inbound/outbound call settings
  4. Test with the "Call Agent" button before going live

The demo call at 33:30 shows the complete customer experience - asking about services, checking prices, and booking an appointment with the AI handling everything naturally.

Step 5: Testing and Optimizing Your Agent

After deployment, use these strategies to improve your agent:

  • Review call transcripts to identify common questions you haven't covered
  • Monitor for any misunderstandings and refine your knowledge base
  • Add seasonal promotions or changing availability to your prompt
  • Track which times have the most missed calls to adjust staffing

As mentioned at 28:45, you can build a customer memory system that recognizes repeat callers and references their history for personalized service.
