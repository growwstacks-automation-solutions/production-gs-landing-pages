The Silent Customer Feedback Challenge

Businesses lose valuable insights every day because customers don't proactively share feedback. Traditional methods like email surveys often get ignored, while manual follow-ups consume staff time without guaranteeing responses.

This WhatsApp feedback bot solves both problems by triggering automated, personalized requests immediately after service completion. Customers can respond at their convenience through a familiar messaging platform, while businesses get structured data they can actually use.

72% of customers are more likely to provide feedback if asked through messaging apps rather than email, according to recent CX research.

How the WhatsApp Feedback Bot Works

The automated feedback collection process follows a simple but powerful sequence:

  1. Trigger: Customer sends "completed" after service
  2. Welcome Message: Personalized thank you with rating request
  3. Rating Collection: 3-button quick response (poor/average/excellent)
  4. Comment Prompt: Optional text field for detailed feedback
  5. Confirmation: Thank you message confirming receipt
  6. Data Logging: All responses automatically recorded in Google Sheets

The entire conversation feels natural to customers while generating structured data that's immediately actionable for your team.

Configuring the Trigger

The magic starts when a customer indicates their service is complete. Here's how to set that up:

In your Chatflow dashboard:

  1. Select "Keyword or Regex Match" as the trigger type
  2. Add "completed" as the primary keyword (you can add variants like "done" or "finished")
  3. Save the trigger configuration

Now whenever a customer messages this keyword after service, your feedback sequence will begin automatically.

Designing the Message Flow

The conversation flow makes or breaks response rates. Here's how to build an effective sequence:

1. Welcome Message

Create a friendly opening that:

  • Personalizes with the customer's name (use the $name variable)
  • Thanks them for their business
  • Briefly explains the feedback process

2. Rating Buttons

Add three quick-reply buttons labeled:

  1. Poor
  2. Average
  3. Excellent

3. Comment Prompt

Follow up with a text field asking for optional additional comments about their experience.

4. Closing Message

End with appreciation and information about how their feedback will be used.

Connecting to Google Sheets

The real power comes from automatically logging all responses. Here's the integration setup:

1. Create a Google Sheet
