Step 1: Bolka AI Agent Setup

Creating your first voice agent takes under two minutes. Start by logging into your Bolka AI dashboard (free trial available) and clicking "Create New Agent."

Critical configuration points:

  1. Agent Name: Use something descriptive like "24/7 Sales Qualifier" or "Tech Support Tier 1"
  2. Description: Outline the agent's primary purpose and scope (e.g. "Handles inbound sales calls, qualifies leads, and books consultations")

These details help Bolka's AI understand the context and boundaries for your agent's conversations. At 1:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how these fields affect the agent's initial training.

Step 2: Conversation Configuration

This is where you define how your agent interacts with callers. Three elements make or break the experience:

  1. Call Intent: Specify the primary reason for calls (e.g. "Calling about solar panel installation quotes")
  2. Intro Message: Craft a natural opening like "Thanks for calling Acme Solar! I'm your digital assistant Sam. How can I help you today?"
  3. Fallback Response: Write a graceful handling for misunderstood queries: "Let me connect you with a specialist who can help with that."

Pro tip: Record your best human agents and analyze their most effective phrases. These become templates for your AI's conversational flow.

Step 3: Audio Settings

Bolka provides multiple voice profiles across genders and accents. For US businesses, we recommend:

  • Voice: "Ethan" (natural, mid-range pitch, clear diction)
  • Speaking Rate: 1.1x (slightly faster than average human)
  • Emotion Modulation: Enabled (adds appropriate vocal inflections)

These settings create a perception of competence and efficiency. At 2:30 in the video, you'll hear side-by-side comparisons of different voice configurations.

Step 4: Knowledge Base Integration

Connect your agent to existing resources for accurate, on-brand responses:

  1. Enable Bolka Knowledge Base: Provides general conversation competence
  2. Upload Your FAQ Docs: PDFs, help center articles, product manuals
  3. Connect CRM (Optional): Pull customer data for personalized responses

This step transforms your agent from a generic responder to a specialized representative of your business. The system continuously learns from interactions to improve future calls.
