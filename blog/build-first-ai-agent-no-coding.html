Building a Competitor Monitoring Agent

Let's walk through creating your first practical AI agent - one that automatically tracks competitors and identifies content opportunities for your business.

The process begins with giving your agent an identity. Unlike traditional software, AI agents develop working styles and communication preferences based on how you describe them. When naming your agent and defining its purpose (shown at 3:40 in the video), think of it as onboarding a new team member:

  • Give it a clear name related to its function (e.g., "Competitor Intel")
  • Describe your business context - what you do, who you serve, your priorities
  • Explain the types of competitors you want tracked and why

The more context you provide at this stage, the more tailored and useful the agent's output will be. This onboarding process typically takes just 2-3 minutes but pays dividends in the quality of automated work you'll receive.

Setting Up Scheduled Automation

The real power of AI agents emerges when they work autonomously on your schedule. At 9:50 in the video, you'll see how to transform a one-time task into a recurring automation that delivers value week after week.

Setting up scheduled execution is surprisingly simple:

  1. Navigate to the Tasks tab in your agent dashboard
  2. Click "Create New Task"
  3. Describe what you want the agent to do (e.g., "Monitor my five competitors every week")
  4. Set your preferred schedule (e.g., Every Sunday at 6 PM)
  5. Specify how you want to receive the report (WhatsApp, email, etc.)
  6. Save the task

That's it. Your agent will now run automatically at the scheduled time, whether you remember to initiate it or not. This transforms the agent from a tool you use into an automated team member that consistently delivers value.

As shown at 11:15, you can view the history of all task executions, check for any issues, and adjust the schedule or parameters at any time.
