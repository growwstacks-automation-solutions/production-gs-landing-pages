Step 1: Automated Brand Crawl

The foundation of your design agent is its understanding of your visual identity. Rather than manually entering color codes and font rules, AetherClaw can crawl your website to extract this information automatically.

In the tutorial, we see the agent analyze ather.com to identify core brand elements. It successfully detects the primary color palette but needs manual correction on accent colors - a common issue since many sites use temporary campaign colors. This hybrid approach (automated crawl + manual refinement) creates the most accurate brand profile.

Step 2: Critical Asset Upload

While the web crawl handles general styling, certain assets require direct upload. Your logo files, proprietary illustrations, and any locked brand elements should be provided separately to ensure pixel-perfect reproduction.

The video demonstrates how providing official logo files prevents the AI from attempting to recreate them (which often leads to distorted or simplified versions). At 4:30, you'll see the agent acknowledge receipt of these assets and confirm it will use them as canonical sources rather than generating approximations.

Step 3: Image Model Connection

AetherClaw's true power comes from connecting it to professional-grade image generation models. The tutorial uses Nano Banana for its free access tier, but the system works with any modern image AI through API connections.

At 5:12 in the video, the agent lists its available image generation options. This modular approach lets you upgrade models as your needs grow without retraining the entire system. For teams generating high volumes of assets, we recommend connecting at least one premium model for critical materials.

Pro Tip: Always specify "use the more powerful model" in your prompts when quality matters most. The agent will automatically select the best available option from your connected services.

Step 4: Reference Image Training

The secret to professional-quality outputs lies in reference images. By showing the agent 5-10 examples of your best existing designs, you establish concrete quality benchmarks it can emulate.

In the tutorial at 6:45, we see how reference images correct the AI's tendency toward generic "tech blue" gradients. This visual training is more effective than text instructions alone - the agent develops an intuitive sense of your preferred compositions, spacing, and visual hierarchy.
