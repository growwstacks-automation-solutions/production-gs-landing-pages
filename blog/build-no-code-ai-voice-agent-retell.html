Retell AI Pricing & Getting Started

One of the biggest barriers to adopting AI voice technology is unclear pricing. Retell AI stands out with its straightforward pay-as-you-go model that makes it accessible for businesses of all sizes.

When you sign up, you get $10 in free credits - enough for about 100 minutes of conversation. After that, voice agents cost approximately 7-12 cents per minute depending on the AI model you choose. Phone numbers purchased through Retell cost $2/month, which is competitive with other VoIP providers.

Key Insight: The actual cost per minute varies based on your AI model selection. GPT-4.1 runs about 11-12 cents/minute while the optimized GPT-4.1 Mini brings that down to 8.5 cents with faster response times.

Enterprise plans are available for high-volume users, with custom pricing based on call volume and specific needs. But for most small to medium businesses, the pay-as-you-go model works perfectly - our agency has handled over 400,000 calls using this approach.

Choosing the Right Agent Type

Retell offers four agent architectures, each suited for different use cases:

  1. Conversational Flow: Node-based decision trees ideal for complex inbound scenarios (receptionists, support)
  2. Single Prompt: Simple but effective for outbound calls and basic inbound tasks
  3. Multi-Prompt: Intermediate option with limited practical advantages
  4. Custom LLM: For businesses running their own language models

Pro Tip: Start with single-prompt agents for outbound calls and simple inbound tasks. Use conversational flow for complex inbound scenarios where call paths need precise control.

The 3 Components of an AI Voice Agent

Understanding how voice agents work will help you build better ones. Think of them as having three key components:

  1. Ears (Transcription): Converts speech to text using AI models
  2. Brain (LLM): Processes the text and generates responses
  3. Mouth (TTS): Converts text responses back to speech

Each component impacts call quality. At 12:30 in the tutorial, we show how transcription errors cascade - if the "ears" mishear, the "brain" responds incorrectly, and the "mouth" says the wrong thing. This is why testing with real call recordings is crucial.

You can mix and match models for each component. Retell supports multiple LLMs (GPT-4.1, Claude, Gemini) and voice providers (ElevenLabs, Cartesia). The platform defaults to GPT-4.1 and ElevenLabs voices, which offer the best balance of intelligence and naturalness.

Advanced Prompt Engineering

The prompt is your agent's brain - it determines how it thinks, responds, and behaves. Unlike chat interfaces where you can clarify, voice agents must get it right the first time.

Effective prompts include:

  • Clear instructions on the agent's role and responsibilities
  • Specific guidance on tone and speaking style
  • Rules for when to transfer calls or end conversations
  • References to connected knowledge bases and functions

At 18:45 in the video, we demonstrate prompt adjustments that transformed a robotic-sounding agent into one that customers thought was human. The key was adding conversational markers ("um", "let me think") and emotional cues.

Critical Setting: Always define a welcome message. Choose between dynamic (AI-generated) or custom (fixed) openings based on your needs. Custom messages ensure consistency for branding.

Adding Knowledge Bases

Knowledge bases provide supplemental information your agents can reference during calls. Unlike prompts (instructions), these contain facts about your business, products, or services.

You can add knowledge bases by:

  • Uploading documents (PDFs, Word files)

When we crawled a sample company website at 27:30, the agent gained instant knowledge about services, pricing, and team members - all without prompt modifications.

Implementation Tip: Create separate knowledge bases for different departments or products. This keeps information organized and reduces token usage during calls.

Voice & Call Behavior Settings

Realistic call behavior requires fine-tuning several settings:

  • Voice Selection: Choose from Retell's recommended voices or add custom ElevenLabs voices
  • Background Noise: Add subtle office sounds for authenticity
  • Responsiveness: Controls how quickly the agent responds after the caller stops speaking
  • Interruption Sensitivity: Determines if the agent stops talking when interrupted
  • Backchanneling: Adds verbal nods ("uh-huh", "I see") for natural flow

At 31:45, we demonstrate how adjusting responsiveness from 1.0 to 0.5 seconds made an agent better at handling elderly callers who pause frequently.

Other critical settings include:

  • Voicemail detection (for outbound calls)
  • Max call duration (5-15 minutes recommended)
  • End call on silence (60-90 seconds ideal)
