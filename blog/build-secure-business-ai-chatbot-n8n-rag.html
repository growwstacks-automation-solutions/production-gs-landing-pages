What is RAG and Why Your Business Needs It

Traditional AI chatbots often hallucinate answers or expose sensitive data—two problems that make businesses hesitant to deploy them. Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) solves both issues by grounding responses in your actual business documents while filtering out private information.

The workflow demonstrated here processes insurance documents, but the same approach works for healthcare FAQs, product manuals, or legal references. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how document chunks get stored in a vector database where they can be precisely retrieved to answer specific questions.

Key insight: RAG isn't just about better answers—it's about auditable answers. Every response can be traced back to specific document sections, creating accountability that standalone AI models lack.

Automatic PII Protection That Actually Works

The Guard World filter in this workflow automatically detects and masks sensitive information like policy numbers and phone numbers before they reach the AI model. At 8:30 in the demo, you'll see how input containing "61234123" gets sanitized to simply show "Hong Kong, China" in the output.

This isn't basic keyword matching—the system understands context. It distinguishes between discussing phone numbers versus actually receiving one from a customer. The filtered conversation still flows naturally while keeping PII out of the AI's training data.

Compliance win: All conversations are logged with timestamps and masked PII, creating an automatic audit trail for regulated industries like insurance and healthcare.

The Complete n8n Workflow Breakdown

This solution combines three n8n workflows into one seamless system:

1. Document Processing Pipeline

Google Drive documents are automatically chunked and embedded into a vector database (Weaviate in the demo). The chunking process preserves semantic meaning while breaking content into manageable pieces.

2. Secure Chat Interface

Telegram handles user input (both text and voice), which flows through the PII filter before querying the vector database. Relevant document chunks are retrieved and fed to the AI model alongside the sanitized question.

3. Evaluation System

A separate workflow periodically tests the chatbot's accuracy by comparing its answers against known correct responses. Scores are calculated based on correctness (50%) and completeness (50%).

Implementation tip: Start with just the document processing and chat components—the evaluation system can be added later as your knowledge base matures.

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) combines document retrieval with AI generation for more accurate responses. Unlike standard chatbots that rely solely on their training data, RAG systems first search your business documents for relevant information.

The workflow breaks documents into semantic chunks stored in a vector database. When a question arrives, it finds the most relevant chunks and provides them as context to the AI. This produces answers grounded in your actual content rather than the AI's general knowledge.

  • Eliminates hallucinations by tethering responses to real documents
  • Allows continuous improvement by updating the document database
  • Creates audit trails showing which documents supported each answer

The Guard World filter automatically detects and masks sensitive information before it reaches the AI model. It uses pattern matching for specific data types like policy numbers and phone numbers, combined with contextual understanding.

In testing, a Hong Kong phone number "61234123" was automatically redacted to just show "Hong Kong, China". The original number never reaches the AI's processing, preventing it from being stored in training data or responses.

  • Configurable patterns for different PII types (SSN, credit cards, etc.)
  • Context-aware filtering avoids false positives in general discussions
  • Logged conversations show masked PII for compliance auditing

The n8n evaluation feature scores answers based on correctness (50%) and completeness (50%). Correctness measures factual accuracy against known answers, while completeness assesses whether all aspects of the question were addressed.

The formula is Accuracy = (Correctness × 0.5) + (Completeness × 0.5). Concrete facts from documents typically score 90-100%, while inferred answers often score 0% when the system lacks sufficient reference material.

  • Manual scoring establishes baseline for new question types
  • Automated scoring tracks improvements over time
  • Identifies knowledge gaps where additional documents are needed
