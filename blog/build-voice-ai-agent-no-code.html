The 3-Step Setup Process

Setting up a voice AI agent requires just three simple steps:

Step 1: Create Your Agent

Name your agent (like "Tina" for a dental office) and specify the business name. Write a simple greeting like "You've reached [Business Name], how can I help you today?"

Step 2: Define Information to Collect

Specify what details the agent should gather - typically name, email, and reason for calling. The system automatically logs the caller's number and timestamps each interaction.

Step 3: Connect a Phone Number

Assign a local or toll-free number to the agent. You can forward this to existing business lines or use it as a dedicated line for tracking purposes.

In Summary: Name your agent → Define what to collect → Connect a number. The entire process takes less time than a coffee break.

Handling Common Objections

When pitching this service, you'll encounter three main objections:

"We don't want callers talking to a robot"

Explain that 68% of callers don't care if it's AI as long as their need gets addressed. Offer a trial period to prove the quality.

"We already have staff"

Position it as overflow support for when staff is busy or after hours. Every answered call is potential revenue.

"What if it makes mistakes?"

The system flags complex calls for human follow-up. For routine appointments, it's more accurate than hurried staff.
