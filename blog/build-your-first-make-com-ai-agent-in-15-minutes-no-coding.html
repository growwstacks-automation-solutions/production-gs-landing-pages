What This AI Agent Does (And Why You Need It)

Creating consistent, engaging social media content is one of the biggest challenges for modern businesses. Between researching trending topics, writing platform-specific posts, and finding relevant images, content creation can consume hours each week - time most business owners simply don't have.

This Make.com AI agent solves all three problems automatically. When triggered (either manually or on a schedule), it will:

Complete content package in minutes: Research latest industry news → Analyze top articles → Generate YouTube scripts, Instagram Reels, LinkedIn posts, and suggested images - all formatted and ready to publish.

The system works through two specialized agents working together:

  1. Research Agent: Uses Perplexity AI to find and summarize the 5 most relevant recent articles in your industry
  2. Content Writer: Takes those articles and transforms them into complete social media content packages

Setting Up Your Make.com Account

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a visual automation platform that lets you connect apps and services without coding. While they offer a free forever plan, we recommend starting with their Pro plan which gives you access to AI agent features.

To get started:

  1. Visit Make.com and click "Sign Up"
  2. Create your account using Google, Facebook, or GitHub login
  3. Upgrade to Pro plan (optional but recommended)
  4. Navigate to the "AI Agents" section in the left sidebar

Pro Tip: Make frequently offers 1-month free trials of their Pro plan - check the video description for any current offers.

Creating Your First AI Agent

The foundation of our automation is the AI Agent that will coordinate between the research and writing components. Here's how to set it up:

  1. Click "AI Agents" in the left sidebar
  2. Click "Create Agent" in the top right
  3. Name your agent (e.g., "AI Content Agent")
  4. Select model size (Medium works well for most use cases)
  5. Add the system prompt that defines your agent's behavior

The system prompt is crucial - it tells your agent how to interpret requests and when to use which tools. A good starter prompt includes:

  • Instructions for handling research requests
  • Rules for content creation
  • Formatting guidelines for outputs

At 4:32 in the video tutorial, you can see an example of a well-structured system prompt being added to the agent.

Building the Research Agent

The research agent's job is to find the most relevant recent articles in your industry. We use Perplexity AI for this because of its excellent web search capabilities.

To create the research agent:

  1. Create a new scenario in Make.com
  2. Add the Perplexity AI module (requires API key)
  3. Configure the search parameters (last 3 days, top 5 results)
  4. Connect to Slack for output

Key configuration: Set your scenario to run "On Demand" rather than on a schedule - this allows your main AI agent to trigger it when needed.

When complete, your research agent will:

  • Search for trending articles in your niche
  • Summarize each one with headline, key points, and link
  • Output the results to Slack in an easy-to-read format

Developing the Content Writer Agent

The content writer takes articles from your research agent and transforms them into ready-to-use social media posts. This is the more complex of the two agents, handling:

  • Article analysis and key point extraction
  • Platform-specific content creation
  • Image suggestion generation
  • Output formatting for Slack

At 12:18 in the video, you can see the complete content writer blueprint being imported - we recommend starting with this template rather than building from scratch.

The key components are:

  1. Perplexity AI module to analyze the article
  2. Make.com AI module to generate platform-specific content
  3. Slack integration to deliver the final package

Connecting Your Agents

With both agents built, we need to connect them to work together seamlessly:

  1. Create a folder in Make.com for your AI automation
  2. Move both scenarios into this folder
  3. Edit your main AI agent to add both as system tools
  4. Add clear descriptions explaining when each tool should be used

The final connection happens through Slack (or your preferred messaging platform):

  • User sends research request ("What's new in AI?")
  • AI agent triggers research tool
  • Research results come back to Slack
  • User selects article and requests content
  • AI agent triggers content writer with article link
  • Complete content package arrives in Slack

Automation power: This entire process - from research request to ready-to-publish content - happens automatically in about 5 minutes.

Testing and Refining Your Workflow

Before relying on your AI agent for daily content, it's important to test and refine it:

  1. Run test research requests to verify result quality
  2. Check that content outputs match your brand voice
  3. Adjust system prompts for better formatting
  4. Test different triggering methods (Slack commands vs scheduled)

Common refinements include:

  • Adjusting the research time window (3 days vs 7 days)
  • Modifying content lengths for different platforms
  • Adding/removing social platforms from outputs
  • Customizing the AI's writing style
