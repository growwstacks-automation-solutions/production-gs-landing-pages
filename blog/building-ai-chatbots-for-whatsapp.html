Getting Started with ChatLink

Creating your first AI agent takes just minutes. After signing up for a free account (no credit card required), you'll land on the project dashboard. Here's how to set up your first chatbot:

  1. Click "Create" under "My Agents"
  2. Choose between an AI Chatbot (structured flows) or AI Agent (open conversation)
  3. Name your agent (e.g., "Customer Support Bot")
  4. Select your preferred AI model (GPT-4.1 works well for most use cases)

Training Your AI Agent

The real power comes from teaching your chatbot about your business. ChatLink offers multiple ways to build your knowledge base:

Pro tip: Start with your most common customer questions. The more specific your training data, the better your chatbot will perform.

You can add knowledge sources by:

  • Uploading documents: PDFs, Word files, or text documents containing product info, FAQs, or policies
  • Adding website URLs: The bot will crawl your site to learn about your offerings
  • Direct text input: Manually enter questions and answers you want the bot to know
  • Connecting Zoho/Notion: Sync existing knowledge bases from these platforms

Processing time varies based on document size, but most knowledge bases are ready within 15-30 minutes.

ChatLink gives you control over how your AI agent responds to customers. Three key settings to adjust:

  1. Temperature: Controls response creativity (0.3-0.7 works best for business use)
  2. System prompt: Defines your bot's personality and response guidelines
  3. Model selection: Choose between different AI versions based on your needs

At 18:45 in the video, you can see how to generate a custom system prompt using ChatGPT and paste it into ChatLink. This ensures your bot responds in your brand voice and follows your business rules.

WhatsApp Integration Process

Connecting your AI agent to WhatsApp involves a few straightforward steps:

  1. Create a WhatsApp Business account through Meta
  2. Verify your business phone number via SMS
  3. Connect the account in ChatLink's deployment section
  4. Set up two-factor authentication

The first 1000 messages are free, after which Meta charges per message (this is separate from ChatLink's pricing). Approval can take up to 48 hours, but once connected, your bot will automatically respond to customer messages on WhatsApp.

Note: ChatLink handles all the technical API integration automatically, saving you from dealing with Meta's complex developer documentation.

Testing and Improving Your Bot

Before going live, thoroughly test your chatbot using ChatLink's playground. Ask it the questions your customers typically have and verify the answers are accurate. Key testing strategies:

  • Ask variations of the same question to check consistency
  • Test edge cases and unusual requests
  • Verify the bot directs users appropriately when it doesn't know an answer

ChatLink's conversation history lets you review all interactions and fine-tune responses. If the bot gives an incorrect answer, you can directly edit it and provide feedback to improve future responses.

Analytics and Performance Tracking

ChatLink provides comprehensive analytics to measure your chatbot's effectiveness:

  • Conversation metrics: Volume, duration, and satisfaction
  • Knowledge source usage: Which documents your bot references most
  • Channel performance: Compare WhatsApp vs website vs Instagram
  • Lead capture: Track form completions and contact growth

These insights help you continuously improve your chatbot by identifying knowledge gaps or areas where responses need refinement.
