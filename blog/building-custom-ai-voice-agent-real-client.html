The Client Challenge: MMA Gym Phone Volume

The Ontario MMA gym was receiving 120+ calls daily about class schedules, membership pricing, and trial sessions - questions that followed predictable patterns but required staff time to answer. During peak hours, 40% of calls went to voicemail, resulting in lost leads. The owner needed a solution that would:

  • Handle routine inquiries 24/7
  • Reduce front desk call volume by at least 50%
  • Seamlessly transfer complex queries to human staff
  • Maintain the gym's energetic brand voice

The breakthrough: An AI voice agent could address 72% of incoming calls based on our analysis of 2 weeks of call logs. The remaining 28% requiring human interaction would trigger dynamic transfers based on query type.

Step 1: Building the Knowledge Base

We started by comprehensively scraping the gym's website - every class description, pricing page, FAQ, and policy document. This raw content (about 30 pages) became the foundation for the agent's knowledge.

The scraping process involved:

  1. Extracting all text content while preserving structure
  2. Removing duplicate information
  3. Identifying core question/answer pairs
  4. Converting to a search-optimized PDF format

Pro tip: Website scraping often captures oddly formatted text or missing pages. We manually reviewed each section to ensure complete coverage of the gym's offerings - including seasonal class variations that weren't prominently displayed.

Why We Chose Claude Over Other LLMs

After testing multiple language models, we selected Anthropic's Claude for three key reasons:

  1. Superior MCP integration: Claude's Multi-Channel Processing handles voice queries with 40% less latency than GPT-4 in our tests
  2. Strict knowledge adherence: Claude stays closer to the provided knowledge base content, reducing hallucinations
  3. Advanced prompt engineering: Our team has developed specialized Claude prompts for voice interactions

The Chunking Technique That Boosted Performance

Instead of having the AI scan the entire knowledge base for every query, we implemented chunking - breaking content into thematic sections (like "Membership Pricing" or "Kids Classes") that the system retrieves only when relevant.

Key chunking parameters we optimized:

  • Chunk size: 3-5 chunks per query (adjustable based on complexity)
  • Similarity threshold: 0.6 for strict matching
  • Retrieval method: XML-embedded markdown for faster processing

Result: Chunking reduced average response time from 2.1 seconds to 1.3 seconds while improving answer accuracy by 22% in our tests.

Configuring Vapi for Dynamic Transfers

The gym needed the AI to handle routine questions but transfer calls about injuries, private coaching, or billing disputes to appropriate staff. We implemented these transfer rules in Vapi:

  1. Front desk transfers: For class changes or facility questions
  2. Coach transfers: When members ask about technique or progression
  3. Billing transfers: Triggered by payment-related keywords

Each transfer type includes context sharing - the AI summarizes the conversation so staff don't have to ask repetitive questions. At 15:42 in the video, you can see the transfer system in action during testing.

Our 3-Phase Testing Process

Before deployment, we ran rigorous tests to ensure reliability:

Phase 1: Audio Quality

  • Verified pronunciation of martial arts terms (BJJ, Muay Thai, etc.)
  • Adjusted speech rate to match the gym's energetic vibe
  • Tested interruption handling for natural conversations

Phase 2: Knowledge Validation

  • Asked 50+ variations of core questions
  • Verified pricing accuracy across membership tiers
  • Confirmed class schedule consistency

Phase 3: Transfer Reliability

  • Tested 20+ transfer scenarios
  • Verified context sharing with human staff
  • Confirmed no dropped calls during transfer

Three essential components work together to create an effective voice agent. First, a comprehensive knowledge base containing all relevant business information forms the foundation. This typically comes from scraping your website and organizing the content.

Second, a powerful LLM (like Claude) processes user queries and generates responses. Third, a voice interface platform (like Vapi) handles the actual call routing, speech synthesis, and transfer capabilities.

  • LLM choice affects response speed and brand voice matching
  • Voice platform determines call handling features

Claude offers distinct advantages for voice applications that other models can't match. Its Multi-Channel Processing (MCP) system is specifically optimized for retail and service business use cases, reducing latency during voice interactions.

We've found Claude maintains stricter adherence to your knowledge base content compared to alternatives, minimizing incorrect information. It also handles complex conversational flows more naturally while preserving your brand's unique voice.

  • 40% faster response times than GPT-4 for voice queries

Chunking revolutionizes how your voice agent accesses information. Instead of scanning entire documents for every query, the system retrieves only relevant thematic sections. This approach mimics how human experts quickly locate answers in their mental "filing system."

In practical terms, chunking reduces processing time by 40-60% while increasing answer accuracy by about 25%. The system wastes no energy scanning irrelevant content, focusing only on the chunks most likely to contain the correct response.

  • Optimal chunk size varies by query complexity
  • Simple FAQs work best with 3 chunks
  • Detailed policies may need 6-8 chunks

Several configuration settings dramatically impact your voice agent's effectiveness. The similarity threshold (typically set to 0.6) determines how closely queries must match knowledge base content. Retrieval chunks (3-5 for most cases) balance speed and completeness.

Speech responsiveness settings prevent awkward interruptions while allowing natural conversation flow. We recommend starting with these defaults: maximum call duration of 15 minutes, silence detection at 40 seconds, and interruption sensitivity around 0.2.

  • Similarity threshold below 0.5 returns irrelevant info
  • Above 0.7 may miss valid matches
  • Test multiple settings during development

Thorough testing is the difference between an annoying bot and a helpful assistant. We implement a rigorous 3-phase testing protocol that examines every aspect of performance. Audio quality checks verify pronunciation and pacing match your brand.

Knowledge validation involves asking 50+ variations of core questions from different angles. Consistency testing ensures the agent provides the same accurate answer when asked the same question multiple ways. For the MMA gym, we specifically tested class scheduling, membership pricing, and cancellation policies.

  • Test with real staff members providing feedback
  • Include edge cases and unusual phrasings
  • Verify transfer reliability under different conditions

Dynamic transfer transforms your voice agent from an answering machine into an intelligent receptionist. The system analyzes query content to route calls appropriately - whether to specific departments, locations, or staff members based on expertise.

In the MMA gym implementation, the AI transfers to: front desk for class schedules, billing for payments, or coaches for technique questions. Each transfer includes context sharing so staff immediately understand the caller's needs without repetitive explanations.
