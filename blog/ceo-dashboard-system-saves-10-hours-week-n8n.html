The Manual Reporting Nightmare

Most eCommerce founders start their mornings the same way - logging into Shopify to check sales, opening Meta Ads Manager to review spend, then manually transferring numbers to a spreadsheet to calculate actual profit. This daily ritual consumes 1-2 hours of valuable time that could be spent growing the business.

The problem compounds as stores scale. More sales channels mean more platforms to check. More ad accounts mean more spreadsheets to update. Soon you're spending 10+ hours weekly just to understand your basic financial performance.

Key insight: Manual reporting creates a 24-hour lag in decision-making. By the time you spot a ROAS drop or CPA spike, you've already wasted a full day of ad spend. Real-time automation eliminates this delay.

Dashboard Overview

This n8n-powered CEO dashboard solves the reporting problem by automatically combining data from Shopify and Meta Ads, calculating key metrics, and updating a Google Sheets dashboard every morning. The system handles everything from data collection to visualization without manual intervention.

The workflow pulls up to 250 Shopify orders per API call (handling pagination automatically) and combines them with daily ad spend from Meta. It then calculates:

  • Gross sales and net revenue after refunds
  • COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)
  • Shipping costs and taxes
  • ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
  • CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)
  • Gross and net profit margins

Shopify Integration

The first component pulls order data from Shopify using the Shopify API. At 2:05 in the video, you'll see how to connect your store by entering your API credentials in n8n. The workflow automatically handles:

  • Order totals and line items
  • Shipping costs and taxes
  • Refunds and returns
  • Store timezone for proper date alignment

This data forms the revenue side of your profit equation. The system pulls all historical orders during initial setup, then only new orders during daily updates.

Meta Ad Integration

The second component connects to your Meta Ads account to pull daily spend data. You'll need two pieces of information:

  1. Your Meta Access Token (from Meta for Developers)
  2. Your Ad Account ID (from Meta Ads Manager)

Once connected, the workflow pulls spend data for all active campaigns, allowing it to calculate true advertising efficiency metrics like ROAS and CPA. Unlike manual reporting, this integration captures every dollar spent - no more missed or misallocated ad spend.

Data Transformation

The magic happens in the transform layer where n8n merges and processes the raw data. Key operations include:

  • Matching Shopify orders with ad spend dates
  • Calculating gross and net revenue
  • Computing COGS based on your product costs
  • Determining true profit after all expenses
  • Calculating ROAS, CPA, AOV, and other KPIs

Pro tip: The transform layer is where you can customize calculations to match your unique business model. Add custom logic for subscription revenue, wholesale orders, or other special cases.

Google Sheets Update

The final step pushes all calculated data to your Google Sheets dashboard. The workflow:

  1. Clears the previous day's raw data
  2. Appends the new processed data
  3. Triggers dashboard visualizations to update

Your dashboard includes time period filters (30/60/90 days) that automatically adjust all charts and metrics. A "Refresh Now" button lets you manually trigger updates if needed between scheduled runs.

Setup Walkthrough

Getting started requires just three main steps:

Step 1: Shopify API Setup

Generate API credentials in your Shopify admin and enter them in the n8n Shopify node. The workflow will test the connection and pull your store's timezone.

Step 2: Meta Ads Connection

Get your access token from Meta for Developers and your ad account ID from Meta Ads Manager. Enter these in the Meta nodes to establish the data pipeline.

Step 3: Google Sheets Configuration

Create a new Google Sheet with the proper tab structure and share it with your n8n service account. The workflow handles all subsequent updates automatically.

Implementation time: Most stores can have this dashboard fully operational in under 30 minutes. The video tutorial walks through each step with timestamps for easy reference.
