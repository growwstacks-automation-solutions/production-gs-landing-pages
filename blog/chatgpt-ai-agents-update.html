What Are Workspace Agents?

Most businesses waste countless hours on repetitive tasks - following up with leads, creating content, updating CRMs. OpenAI's new workspace agents solve this by acting as virtual employees that work 24/7 across your business tools.

Unlike regular ChatGPT which responds to individual prompts, these agents execute complete workflows autonomously. At 12:37 in the video, we see an agent researching AI news, drafting SEO blog posts, and publishing to Google Docs - all without human intervention.

Key capability: Workspace agents continue running even when you close your laptop. They're hosted in OpenAI's cloud and execute scheduled tasks like clockwork - whether that's daily content creation, weekly reporting, or real-time lead follow-ups.

Agent vs Custom GPT: Key Differences

Many businesses already use custom GPTs for specific tasks, but workspace agents represent a fundamental shift. At 8:15 in the tutorial, we demonstrate three critical differences:

  1. Scheduled execution: Agents run on fixed schedules (like every Monday morning) rather than waiting for prompts
  2. Tool integration: They connect directly to business apps like Slack, Gmail and Google Drive
  3. Team sharing: Your entire company can access and use the same agent simultaneously

The most powerful agents combine these features. For example, at 22:45 we build a lead outreach agent that monitors your CRM daily, drafts tailored follow-ups, and updates records - all while notifying your sales team in Slack.

Step-by-Step Agent Setup

Creating your first agent takes under 10 minutes. At 9:45 in the tutorial, we walk through the exact process:

Step 1: Access Workspace

Navigate to the new Agents tab in ChatGPT (business plan required). Click "Create Agent" to begin.

Step 2: Describe the Job

Type what you want the agent to do in plain English. Example: "Research AI news daily and turn the most promising topic into an SEO blog post."

Step 3: Review the Plan

ChatGPT generates an agent blueprint including name, schedule, and capabilities. Edit any details before proceeding.

Step 4: Connect Tools

Link business apps like Google Drive (for content storage) or Slack (for notifications). Shown at 17:20.

Step 5: Set Permissions

Control which team members can use or modify the agent. Enterprise plans add approval workflows.

Step 6: Deploy

Click "Start Building" to launch your agent. It will run on the schedule you defined.

Troubleshooting tip: If the agent doesn't appear in your sidebar (18:30), ensure you're on a ChatGPT business plan. Personal accounts lack agent functionality.

Team Collaboration Features

Unlike solo AI tools, workspace agents shine for team use. At 25:40 we explore three collaboration benefits:

Shared Access

Your entire company can use the same agent simultaneously. Sales, marketing and support teams all benefit from centralized automation.

Activity Logs

Track every action agents take across your business tools. Essential for compliance and troubleshooting.

Approval Workflows

Configure agents to request human sign-off before sensitive actions like sending customer emails.

The tutorial at 27:15 shows how admins control permissions - deciding who can build agents, which tools they access, and what actions they perform autonomously versus requiring approval.

Security & Admin Controls

Enterprise teams often hesitate to adopt AI over security concerns. OpenAI addresses this with four safeguards demonstrated at 30:20:

  1. Tool permissions: Granular control over which apps each agent accesses
  2. Approval gates: Require human review before sensitive actions
  3. Audit logs: Detailed records of every agent activity
  4. Access controls: Restrict who can create or modify agents

For regulated industries, you can configure agents to operate in "human-in-the-loop" mode - automating workflow steps while preserving oversight on critical decisions.
