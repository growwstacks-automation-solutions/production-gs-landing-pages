The 3 n8n Hosting Options Compared

When starting with n8n automation, most businesses face the same dilemma: where to host their workflows. The platform offers three main approaches, each with dramatically different cost implications and operational requirements.

At first glance, n8n's own cloud hosting seems convenient - until you see the pricing tiers. Local hosting appears free but creates hidden reliability issues. The third option, VPS hosting, combines the best of both worlds: cloud reliability at local hosting prices.

Key insight: The $7/month VPS solution provides unlimited executions - something n8n's $5,200/month enterprise plan still doesn't offer.

Why n8n Cloud Hosting Gets Expensive Fast

n8n's cloud hosting starts at $24/month for their basic plan, which includes just 2,500 workflow executions and limits you to 5 concurrent executions. While this may seem reasonable for testing, real business automation needs quickly outgrow these constraints.

As shown at 1:15 in the video, scaling to 500,000 monthly executions (a realistic volume for agencies managing multiple clients) costs $5,200/month - and still imposes execution limits. This pricing model makes cloud hosting unsustainable for growing automation needs.

The Hidden Costs of Local Computer Hosting

Many tutorials suggest running n8n locally as a "free" alternative. While technically possible, this approach creates three significant problems that often outweigh the cost savings.

First, your computer must remain powered on 24/7 - impractical for laptops and energy-inefficient for desktops. Second, any internet outage or travel situation disrupts your automations. Third, the electricity costs of running a powerful machine continuously often exceed $7/month anyway.

Before you try local hosting: Calculate your computer's kWh consumption running continuously - you might be surprised how close it gets to VPS costs without any of the reliability.
