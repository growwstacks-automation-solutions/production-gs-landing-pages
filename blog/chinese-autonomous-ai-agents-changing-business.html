The Agentic AI Revolution

The business automation landscape shifted dramatically in late 2025 as Chinese developers released autonomous AI agents that outperform Western chatbots in complex task execution. Unlike conversational AI that requires step-by-step prompting, these agents receive high-level goals and independently determine the steps needed to achieve them.

This represents a fundamental shift from reactive AI tools to proactive digital employees. As demonstrated at the 3:15 mark in the video, these agents don't just respond - they plan, they prioritize, and they execute multi-step workflows without constant supervision. The implications for business productivity are staggering.

Key difference: Traditional chatbots assist with tasks, while autonomous agents own complete processes from start to finish. This distinction will redefine job roles across industries in .

Manis AI: Your Multi-Agent Workforce

Manis AI represents the first commercially available autonomous agent with true multi-agent architecture. Rather than handling all tasks itself, it functions as a project manager that decomposes objectives and assigns subtasks to specialized sub-agents.

For recruitment workflows, Manis can process hundreds of resumes in parallel - one agent extracts education history, another analyzes work experience, while a third scores cultural fit. This division of labor allows Manis to deliver comprehensive candidate assessments in hours rather than days.

Website creation demo: At 4:32 in the video, you'll see Manis build a complete business website in under 4 hours - planning structure, writing copy, coding functionality, and deploying the site without human intervention.

AutoGM Rumination: Free and Fast

Jaip AI's AutoGM Rumination delivers autonomous capabilities at no cost through its web and mobile interfaces. Powered by efficient GLM models, it performs complex tasks like vacation planning and business report generation 8x faster than comparable Western solutions while consuming less computing power.

The "Rumination" in its name refers to the agent's ability to deeply analyze problems from multiple angles before executing solutions. Unlike single-pass chatbots, AutoGM revisits and refines its approach, demonstrating human-like reasoning about business challenges.

Real-world example: AutoGM can transform a simple request like "Plan a 5-day Tokyo business trip under $3,000" into a complete itinerary with booked flights, vetted hotels, meeting venue recommendations, and local dining options - all formatted as a shareable document.

Deepseek NextG: The Future Agent

Scheduled for release by end of 2025, Deepseek NextG promises advanced autonomous capabilities through self-improving architecture. Early demonstrations show the agent remembering user preferences across sessions and optimizing its approach to recurring business tasks.

What sets NextG apart is its minimal input requirement - users describe desired outcomes rather than processes. The agent then determines the optimal path, adjusting its methods based on results. This makes it particularly valuable for complex, variable workflows like customer support routing or inventory management.

Enterprise potential: Deepseek claims NextG will reduce operational decision-making time by 70% while improving consistency across business processes - a combination that could save mid-sized companies over $150,000 annually in labor costs.
