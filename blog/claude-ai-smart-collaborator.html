Crafting Effective Prompts For Business Results

The video emphasizes at 3:30 that the best way to interact with Claude is like you would with a skilled coworker - naturally yet specifically. Effective prompting follows three key components that mirror how you'd brief a human colleague on a project.

First, set the stage by explaining your role and objectives. Second, clearly define the task - what action you want Claude to take. Third, specify any rules about style, tone, or format. The video provides an excellent example at 4:45 where a prompt for an investor pitch deck includes all three elements: context about the streaming app, specific content requests, and formatting requirements.

Pro tip: Attach examples of work you admire to show Claude exactly what you want. This "show don't tell" approach yields better results than abstract descriptions of your preferences.
