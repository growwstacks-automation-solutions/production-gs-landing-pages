What Are MCPs and Why Do They Matter?

Every business leader knows the frustration: critical data locked in different systems, requiring constant switching between tabs and manual copy-pasting. Model Context Protocols (MCPs) solve this by giving your AI assistant secure, natural language access to your entire tech stack.

MCPs act as standardized connectors between large language models like Claude and your business tools. Unlike traditional APIs that require technical implementation, MCPs work conversationally. You simply tell your AI what you need from connected systems using natural language.

Key capability: MCPs enable two-way interaction - your AI can both retrieve information from connected tools and take actions within them based on scoped permissions. This transforms your assistant from an information provider to an active participant in your workflows.

The Power of Progressive Loading

One major advancement in MCP technology is progressive loading (shown at 7:30 in the video). Unlike earlier versions that loaded all connected tools into memory immediately, modern MCPs only activate when needed.

Token efficiency: Progressive loading means a CRM MCP might only consume 1.3K tokens when actually used, rather than occupying that space throughout the entire conversation. This optimization preserves valuable context window space for the actual dialogue.

The practical benefit? You can maintain more MCP connections without worrying about them hogging resources. They sit dormant until specifically referenced, then load just what's needed to fulfill your request.

Security Considerations With MCPs

The video's most important warning comes at 14:20 - MCPs currently operate as black boxes. When you grant an MCP access to sensitive systems like your CRM or email, you're trusting that:

  1. It only accesses what you explicitly request
  2. It doesn't store or transmit your data improperly
  3. The provider maintains proper security controls

Security best practice: Only use official MCPs from verified vendors for sensitive systems. For unofficial tools, consider creating custom skills where you control the integration code directly.

Always review permission scopes carefully and start with read-only access when connecting new MCPs to critical business systems.
