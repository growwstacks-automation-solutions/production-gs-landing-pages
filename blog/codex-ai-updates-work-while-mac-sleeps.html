The Locked Mac Revolution

For years, AI automation hit a frustrating wall - your computer needed to stay awake, unlocked, and actively monitored for tasks to complete. This limitation meant business owners either babysat their machines or risked incomplete work when their Macs inevitably went to sleep.

Codex AI's "locked use" feature changes everything. Now your Mac can:

  • Run tasks while the screen is off and computer locked
  • Wake apps temporarily to complete work
  • Relock automatically when finished

1.8 million views in one day: The announcement video demonstrating locked use went viral, showing how users can now send tasks from their phone to a sleeping Mac and return to completed work.

Implementation requires just two permissions in Codex settings - screen viewing and app control. Once enabled, the AI can click buttons, navigate menus, and interact with applications exactly like a human would - just without needing anyone present.

App Shots: Instant Context Capture

Previously, explaining complex on-screen content to AI required tedious copying, pasting, and describing. App Shots eliminates this friction with a simple double-command-key shortcut.

When you press both command keys:

  • Codex captures the active window's complete content
  • Includes text currently scrolled out of view
  • Preserves formatting and structural relationships

This feature shines when processing:

  • Long email threads needing summarized responses
  • Spreadsheets requiring data extraction
  • Document comparisons or version analysis

Time savings: What previously took 5+ minutes of manual work now happens in one second, making AI assistance practical for quick, context-rich tasks throughout the workday.

Goal Mode: Multi-Hour Autonomy

While earlier AI agents struggled with tasks lasting beyond 30 minutes, Codex's official Goal Mode release enables overnight operation on complex objectives. This represents a 16x duration improvement in just one year.

Key characteristics of Goal Mode:

  • Runs continuously for up to 8 hours per goal
  • Self-verifies and corrects its work
  • Handles multi-app workflows autonomously

The critical success factor is goal clarity. Well-defined objectives like "Compile all customer feedback from the past quarter into categorized insights with priority flags" produce excellent results, while vague instructions lead to wasted cycles.

Overnight productivity: Members of the AI Profit Boardroom report waking to completed market research, drafted client reports, and organized knowledge bases - work that previously consumed entire workdays.

Built-In Safety Measures

Understandably, many users initially hesitate granting an AI control over their locked computer. Codex addresses these concerns with multiple protection layers:

  • Temporary unlocking: Only during active task execution
  • Intrusion detection: Auto-relocks if keyboard/mouse movement occurs
  • Screen protection: Prevents viewing of sensitive content
  • Permission boundaries: Cannot access certain system areas

The system underwent third-party security audits before release, with additional safeguards recommended for high-sensitivity environments:

  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Use separate user accounts for AI tasks
  • Restrict access to particularly sensitive documents

Current Limitations

While revolutionary, these features have some important boundaries:

  • Mac-only: Windows support for locked use and App Shots is planned but not yet available
  • Sleep depth: Works with screen-off sleep but not full shutdown/hibernation
  • Power requirements: Extended Goal Mode tasks need AC power connection
  • App compatibility: Some proprietary or highly secured applications may block automation

Additionally, Goal Mode works best with:

  • Clearly defined success criteria
  • Structured data inputs
  • Repeatable processes

Creative or highly subjective tasks often require human refinement after AI completion.
