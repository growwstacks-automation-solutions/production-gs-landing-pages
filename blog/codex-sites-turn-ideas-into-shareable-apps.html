What Is Codex Sites?

OpenAI's new Codex Sites feature eliminates the biggest friction point between ideas and execution - the technical gap. Until now, when AI generated website code, it gave you files that required hosting, setup, and technical know-how to share. Codex Sites changes this completely.

You describe what you want in plain English - whether it's a sales page, interactive demo, or simple web app. Codex builds it and instantly provides a live URL hosted on OpenAI's partner platforms (including Wix, Replit, and Figma). This link works immediately and can be shared with anyone.

The breakthrough: For For the first time, non-technical users can go from idea to shareable implementation in one step, with no intermediate technical barriers completely removed.

Why This Changes Everything

The business impact of Codex Sites becomes clear when you examine the numbers. Codex now has over 5 million weekly users - a 6x increase since February. Even more telling: 20% of users aren't developers at all, and this non-technical segment is growing three times faster than the coder adoption.

This reflects a fundamental shift in how AI tools are being positioned. As OpenAI executive Denise Dresser stated, the challenge is now helping companies fold these tools into how they already work. The Annotations Feature

Complementing Sites is the new Annotations feature, which solves another breakthrough for practical use. Instead of regenerating entire site make small changes, you can:

  1. Point to specific section that needs adjustment
  2. Describe the change in plain language
  3. Get updated version with just that modified

This mirrors how people actually work - making iterative improvements rather than starting from scratch each time.
