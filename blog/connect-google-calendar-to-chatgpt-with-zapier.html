The Hidden Cost of Unprepared Meetings

Professionals waste an average of 15 minutes before each meeting scrambling to prepare — reviewing attendee backgrounds, drafting discussion points, and trying to remember the meeting's purpose. Multiply that by 10-15 meetings per week, and you're losing 2.5-4 hours weekly just on meeting prep.

The solution isn't scheduling fewer meetings (though that helps). It's automating the prep work so you walk into every discussion fully prepared without spending mental energy on logistics. That's where connecting Google Calendar to ChatGPT creates transformative efficiency.

Key insight: The most valuable meetings automation doesn't schedule your time — it prepares you to use that time effectively.

Step-by-Step Setup Guide

Here's how to recreate the automation from the video (timestamp 0:45 shows the complete workflow):

Step 1: Create a New Zap

Log into Zapier and click "Create Zap." Name it something like "Meeting Prep Generator" for easy reference later.

Step 2: Set the Trigger

Select Google Calendar as your trigger app. Choose "New Event" as the trigger event. Connect your Google account if you haven't already.

Step 3: Test the Trigger

Zapier will pull a sample event from your calendar to confirm the connection works. This ensures the automation will have real meeting data to process.

Step 4: Add ChatGPT Action

Add OpenAI as your action app. Select "Create Completion" as the action event. Configure your prompt to use the meeting details — something like: "Generate 5 relevant discussion questions for a meeting titled: [Event Title]. Meeting description: [Event Description]."

Step 5: Add Slack Action

Connect your Slack account and choose "Send Channel Message" as the action. Map the ChatGPT output to the message content. Set the channel where you want meeting prep to appear.

Step 6: Test and Activate

Run a test to confirm everything works, then turn on your Zap. New calendar events will now automatically generate prep materials.

Pro tip: Add a filter step to only process events where you're listed as an attendee, avoiding notifications for meetings you're just copied on.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When setting up this integration, watch out for these pitfalls:

Overly broad triggers: Without proper filters, you might generate prep for personal calendar events or all-day blocks. Always test with sample data before going live.

Vague ChatGPT prompts: "Prepare me for this meeting" yields worse results than "Generate 3 discussion questions focusing on [specific topic] from the meeting description."

Notification overload: Sending every generated prep to your main Slack channel can create noise. Consider a dedicated #meeting-prep channel or DM delivery.
