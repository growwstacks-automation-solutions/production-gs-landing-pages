Zapier Basics: Triggers vs Actions

Every Zapier automation has two core components. The trigger is the event that starts the automation (like a new Google Sheets row), while actions are what happen afterward (like sending an email or updating your CRM).

This distinction matters because Google Sheets can only serve as the trigger in this integration. While you can both read from and write to Sheets, the initial connection focuses on detecting changes that kick off workflows.

Step-by-Step Connection Guide

Connecting Google Sheets to Zapier takes just 5 minutes when you're logged into both accounts. Here's the exact process:

Step 1: Create a New Zap

In Zapier, click "Create" then choose "Zaps" to start a new automation. Name your Zap something descriptive like "Google Sheets → CRM Update".

Step 2: Set Google Sheets as Trigger

Search for and select Google Sheets as your trigger app. Choose between "New Spreadsheet Row" (simpler) or "New or Updated Spreadsheet Row" (more comprehensive).

Step 3: Connect Your Google Account

Authenticate with the Google account that has access to your spreadsheet. Double-check you're using the correct account if you manage multiple.

Step 4: Select Your Spreadsheet

Select the specific Google Sheet and worksheet you want to monitor. For reliable testing, ensure sheet has column headers and at least one data row.

Pro tip: Use named ranges in your Google Sheet (Format → Named ranges) to make columns easier to reference in subsequent Zap steps.

Testing and Troubleshooting

The "Test trigger" step is where most connections fail initially. Zapier looks for sample data to confirm it can read your sheet. Follow these troubleshooting steps if the test returns no results:

  • Add a fresh row to your sheet with test data
  • Verify your Google account has edit permissions
  • Check for blank columns that might hide data
  • Try reconnecting your Google account
