Zapier Basics: Triggers and Actions

Zapier automations (called "Zaps") consist of two core components: triggers and actions. A trigger is an event that starts your automation - in this case, a new Google Forms submission. An action is what happens automatically after the trigger - adding the respondent to MailerLite.

The beauty of this system is its flexibility. While we're focusing on Google Forms to MailerLite today, the same principles apply to thousands of other app combinations. Once you understand triggers and actions, you can automate nearly any repetitive task in your business.

Step-by-Step Setup Guide

Step 1: Create Your Zap

Log into Zapier and click "Create Zap." You can build from scratch or use the AI copilot by typing "add new Google Forms submissions to MailerLite." The AI will generate the basic workflow structure automatically.

Step 2: Set Up Google Forms Trigger

Select Google Forms as your trigger app and connect your account. Choose the specific form you want to monitor for new submissions. Zapier will need to pull one test submission to understand your form's data structure.

Step 3: Configure MailerLite Action

Connect your MailerLite account and select "Add Subscriber" as the action. Map each form field to the corresponding MailerLite field (name → name, email → email, etc.). You can also specify which MailerLite group to add subscribers to.

Pro tip: At 2:30 in the video, you'll see how to use Zapier's field mapping to customize exactly which form data transfers to MailerLite. This prevents unnecessary information from cluttering your email lists.

Testing Your Automation

Before activating your Zap, Zapier walks you through testing each step. First, it verifies it can detect new form submissions. Then it performs a test action by adding a subscriber to MailerLite using your mapped fields.

Always check your MailerLite account after testing to confirm the subscriber was added correctly. Look for:

  • Proper field mapping (names, emails in right places)
  • Correct group assignment
  • No duplicate or partial records

Testing might add 1-2 minutes to your setup time, but it prevents hours of troubleshooting later. The video shows exactly what a successful test looks like at 3:45.

Advanced Customization Options

Common Mistakes to Avoid

After setting up hundreds of these integrations, we've identified the most frequent pitfalls:

  • Not testing field mapping: Assuming all fields will transfer correctly without verification
  • Ignoring required fields: MailerLite requires certain fields (like email) that must be mapped
  • Overcomplicating: Starting with complex multi-step Zaps before mastering the basics
