Why Automate Wix Forms to Google Sheets?

Every time a visitor submits a form on your Wix website, valuable data gets trapped in your Wix dashboard. Manually copying this information to spreadsheets wastes precious time and introduces errors. According to our research, small businesses spend an average of 3-5 hours per month on this repetitive task.

Automating this flow with Zapier solves three critical business problems: First, it eliminates manual data entry errors that occur when copying between systems. Second, it creates a searchable database of all submissions for easy analysis. Third, it provides automatic backups of your form data outside Wix's ecosystem.

Key benefit: This integration works while you sleep, logging every form submission in real-time. One client reported saving 12 hours in their first month while catching 47 submissions they would have otherwise missed during busy periods.

Step 1: Setting Up Your Zapier Account

Zapier acts as the bridge between Wix and Google Sheets. If you're new to automation, think of Zapier as digital duct tape - it connects apps that weren't designed to work together. The free plan handles up to 100 tasks per month, which works for most small businesses.

Start by creating a free account at zapier.com. Use your business email rather than a personal one - this becomes important later for team access and permissions. Once logged in, click "Create Zap" in the top left corner to begin building your automation workflow (called a "Zap" in Zapier terminology).

Step 2: Connecting Your Wix Account

In your new Zap, search for "Wix" when prompted to choose a trigger app. Select "Wix" from the dropdown, then choose "New Form Submission" as your trigger event. This tells Zapier to watch for form submissions on your Wix site.

Click "Sign in" to connect your Wix account. A popup will appear asking you to authorize Zapier's access. Be sure to select the correct Wix site if you manage multiple properties. At the 2:15 mark in the video tutorial, you'll see exactly how to navigate Wix's authorization screen.

Pro tip: If you don't see your form listed after connecting, refresh your browser and check Wix's form settings to ensure the form is published and accepting submissions.

Step 3: Configuring the Wix Form Trigger

After connecting your Wix account, you'll need to specify which form should trigger the automation. You can choose to capture submissions from all forms or select a specific one. For most businesses, starting with your highest-traffic form yields the quickest ROI.

Test your trigger by submitting a test form (Zapier will guide you through this). This verifies Zapier can successfully detect submissions from your Wix form. If the test fails, double-check that your form is live and try resubmitting while keeping the test window open.

Step 4: Connecting Google Sheets

Now for the destination - your Google Sheet. Click "Add Step" and search for Google Sheets. Choose "Create Spreadsheet Row" as your action. This tells Zapier to add a new row to your specified sheet for each form submission.

Connect your Google account following the same process as Wix. Select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet where data should be added. If you haven't created a sheet yet, pause here to set one up with appropriate column headers matching your form fields.

Step 5: Mapping Form Fields to Spreadsheet Columns

This critical step determines where each piece of form data lands in your spreadsheet. Zapier will show your Wix form fields on the left and spreadsheet columns on the right. Drag and drop to match them up.

For best results, arrange your spreadsheet columns in the same order as your form fields initially. You can always rearrange later. Don't forget to map the submission timestamp - this becomes invaluable for tracking response times and analyzing trends.

Common mistake: At 5:42 in the video, we show how to handle dropdown fields and file uploads differently than text fields. These special field types require specific mapping approaches to work correctly.

Step 6: Testing and Publishing Your Zap

Before going live, send a test submission to verify data flows correctly to your spreadsheet. Check that all fields appear in the right columns with proper formatting. Pay special attention to dates, phone numbers, and other formatted fields that sometimes require adjustment.

Once satisfied, click "Publish" to activate your Zap. The system will now automatically process all future form submissions. Monitor the first few real submissions to confirm everything works as expected under actual usage conditions.

Advanced Tips for Power Users

For businesses receiving high form volumes, consider these optimizations: First, add a "Zapier" column to identify automated entries. Second, set up a separate tab for raw data and use another tab for analysis to preserve data integrity. Third, explore Zapier filters to only log submissions meeting certain criteria.

Many of our clients eventually upgrade to multi-step Zaps that not only log submissions but also notify team members, update CRMs, and trigger follow-up sequences. The initial Wix-to-Sheets integration serves as the foundation for these more advanced workflows.
