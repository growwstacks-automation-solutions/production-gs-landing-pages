Why This Integration Changes Everything

If you've been manually scheduling social media posts across multiple platforms, you know the frustration. The creative drain of staring at a blank post composer. The time wasted copying content between apps. The missed opportunities when great content sits unpublished because you didn't have time to schedule it.

The Lately-Zapier integration solves these problems by connecting your content creation tools directly to your social media scheduling. Now when something happens in one app—a new blog post, a content idea in Notion, AI-generated copy—it can automatically trigger actions in Lately without any manual intervention.

6,000+ app connections: The real power comes from Zapier's ability to connect Lately with thousands of other tools. This creates endless possibilities for automating your unique workflow across all the apps your team already uses.

How the Lately-Zapier Connection Works

At its core, the integration allows Lately to function as either a trigger or an action within Zapier workflows. As an action, Lately can perform tasks like scheduling posts when triggered by events in other apps. As a trigger, Lately can initiate workflows in other apps when certain events occur (like when a post is published).

Setting up the connection is simple. You'll find Lately in Zapier's app directory at zapier.com/apps/lately. Once connected, you can choose whether to use Lately as the starting point (trigger) or the end point (action) in your automation sequences.

For Content Teams: Idea to Published Post

Content teams often struggle with moving ideas through their workflow efficiently. The gap between ideation and publication is where great content often gets stuck or delayed.

With the Lately-Zapier integration, you can create a seamless pipeline from idea to published post. Here's how it works:

Getting Started in 3 Simple Steps

Implementing these automations is easier than you might think. You don't need technical skills—just follow these steps:

Step 1: Connect Your Accounts

Create accounts with both Lately and Zapier if you don't have them already. Then visit zapier.com/apps/lately to establish the connection.

Step 2: Choose Your Trigger

Decide what event should start your workflow—a new blog post, a record in Airtable, AI-generated content, etc.

Step 3: Set Your Lately Action

Configure what should happen in Lately when your trigger occurs—scheduling a post, creating a draft, etc.

Start small, then expand: Begin with one simple workflow, then add more complex automations as you become comfortable with the system.
