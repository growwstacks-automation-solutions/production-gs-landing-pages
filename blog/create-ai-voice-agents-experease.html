Voice Agent Creation Basics

Creating an AI voice agent in ExpertEase begins with defining its core purpose. Unlike generic chatbots, voice agents need specific roles (customer support, sales, etc.) to guide their conversation style. The creation process starts by clicking "New Voice Agent" and naming your bot - choose something descriptive like "24/7 Tech Support" or "Appointment Scheduler".

At 0:45 in the video, we see the critical role selection dropdown. This determines the agent's default behavior patterns - a sales agent will be more proactive in offering solutions, while a support agent focuses on problem resolution. You can optionally upload a logo (useful if the agent will appear in other channels like chat), and toggle call recording preferences based on your compliance requirements.

Pro Tip: Start with recording enabled (default) to to analyze call patterns and improve your agent, then disable selectively once optimized. Recordings help identify where callers get confused or where the AI misunderstands requests.

Voice Customization Options

The human-like quality of an AI voice agent comes from careful voice tuning. ExpertEase provides three key controls shown at 1:20 in the tutorial: language selection (English with regional variants), speaking speed (1.0-1.1x recommended), and accent selection (American, Australian, etc.).

Always use the preview function to test how your agent will sound saying common phrases. The voice should match your brand personality - a financial services agent might prefer a measured 1.0x speed with a neutral accent, while a travel agency could opt for a more energetic 1.1x pace with regional lilt. Avoid speeds above 1.1x as they reduce comprehension.

Conversion Boost: In tests, callers stayed on the line 23% longer with regionally matched accents compared to generic voices, leading to higher satisfaction and issue resolution rates.

Crafting Effective Agent Instructions

At 2:10, the tutorial demonstrates the instruction panel where you define the agent's purpose and call flow. This is where you specify how conversations should begin (e.g., "Begin by confirming the caller's identity" or "Offer the current promotion after resolving their issue").

Well-structured instructions work best. Break down complex processes into discrete steps the AI can follow, and include examples of ideal responses. For customer support, you might write: "When asked about refunds, first verify purchase details, then explain policy, finally offer store credit if eligible." The more specific your instructions, the more consistent the AI sounds like a trained human agent.

Attaching Knowledge Bases

At 2:45, we see how to upload PDFs, websites, or custom instructions that become the agent's reference materials. This is crucial for handling specialized queries - imagine giving your AI the same manuals your human agents use.

For best results, structure knowledge in Q&A format when possible. Product FAQs, product manuals, and policy documents translate well to voice interactions. The AI can directly reference these during calls, saying things like "According to our return policy section 3.2..." - building caller trust through precise information.

Call Scheduling Methods

The tutorial demonstrates three scheduling approaches at 4:20: test calls for quality assurance, manual scheduling with timezone awareness, and bulk uploads via CSV template. Each serves different business needs.

Bulk scheduling shines for outbound campaigns. The provided template lets you set phone numbers, optimal call times, and even personalized variables ("Hi [Name], about your [Product] inquiry..."). This automation can replace hours of manual dialing while maintaining personalization at scale.

Building Conversation Templates

At 5:00, we see template creation - predefined conversation starters that ensure brand consistency. These work like email templates but for voice: "Hi, I'm [Agent] calling from [Company] about your recent [Interaction]."

Templates handle the structured parts of calls, freeing the AI to improvise the middle sections based on caller responses. Include variables (marked with {}) for personalization, and test different openings to see which yield highest engagement rates.

Purchasing Dedicated Numbers

The final step shown at 6:30 is acquiring a local phone number that becomes your agent's caller ID. ExpertEase offers numbers by country, region, and even city - crucial for establishing local presence.

Choose numbers build trust - customers are 47% more likely to answer a local area code than an unknown or international number. The platform handles all telephony infrastructure, letting you focus on conversation quality rather than technical setup.
