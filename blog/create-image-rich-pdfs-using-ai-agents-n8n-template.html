How the AI PDF Generator Works

Educational content creation typically requires hours of research, writing, and design work. This n8n workflow automates the entire process through a coordinated sequence of AI agents that handle each specialized task.

The system begins when you send a simple message with your topic (like "Apollo 11 moon landing") to a Telegram bot. From there, four automated stages transform your request into a professional PDF textbook complete with chapters and AI-generated images.

Complete automation from trigger to delivery: The average PDF takes just 2-3 minutes to generate, with no human intervention required after the initial request. At 1:32 in the video, you can see the Apollo 11 PDF being automatically saved to Google Drive.

Stage 1: Topic Validation & Refinement

The first AI agent acts as a gatekeeper, ensuring incoming requests are valid historical topics suitable for the system. This prevents wasted processing on irrelevant messages.

Using Gemini 2.5 Flash, the agent analyzes the input message to determine if it describes a historical event or concept. Valid topics get refined into professional titles (e.g., "Napoleon's military campaigns"), while off-topic requests receive a polite decline response.

Key validation rules: The agent checks for (1) historical relevance, (2) sufficient specificity, and (3) absence of modern or technical subjects. This ensures only appropriate topics proceed to the research stage.

Stage 2: Automated Research & Chapter Creation

Approved topics trigger the research agent, which breaks the subject into five chronological chapters. For each chapter, the AI:

  1. Conducts web research to verify key dates, figures, and facts
  2. Writes an engaging 3-4 sentence paragraph summarizing events
  3. Creates detailed image prompts for visual representation

The research phase produces structured content ready for image generation and PDF assembly. At 3:15 in the video, you can see the chapter outlines being prepared for the Industrial Revolution topic.

Stage 3: AI Image Generation

Nano Banana Pro generates realistic images for each chapter based on the AI-created prompts. The workflow:

  1. Processes one chapter at a time to avoid API overload
  2. Converts base64 image data into proper file formats
  3. Uploads completed images to Google Drive
  4. Updates the tracking sheet with image URLs

The video at 4:50 shows stunningly realistic images being generated for the Mayflower crossing, including a ship battling Atlantic storms and period-accurate passenger depictions.

Image quality: At $0.01 per image, Nano Banana Pro delivers museum-quality historical visuals that would cost $50+ from stock photo services or require hours of manual creation.

Stage 4: PDF Assembly & Delivery

The final stage combines all content into a professional PDF:

  1. Aggregates all chapters and images into structured HTML
  2. Applies consistent styling for readability
  3. Converts HTML to PDF with adjustable layout settings
  4. Saves the final document to Google Drive
  5. Updates the job tracker with the PDF link

The workflow handles everything from initial research to final delivery. At 6:20 in the video, you can see the completed Apollo 11 PDF with its professional chapter layout and realistic images of the lunar landing.

Built for Scalability

Unlike many tutorial workflows, this system is designed for production use:

  • Job tracking: Google Sheets maintains status for all PDF generations
  • Rate limiting: Controlled pacing prevents API bottlenecks
  • Modular design: Easy to swap AI providers or add processing steps
  • Error handling: Automatic retries and failure notifications

The video at 7:30 demonstrates how the system gracefully handles and recovers from an image generation error by automatically retrying with adjusted parameters.
