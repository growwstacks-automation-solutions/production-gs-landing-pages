How the WhatsApp AI Chatbot Works

The chatbot combines WhatsApp Business API with OpenAI's language model to understand and respond to customer inquiries. When a message arrives, the system analyzes the question, searches your knowledge base, and generates a human-like response tailored to your business.

Key components include:

  • AI Engine: GPT-based model that understands questions and generates responses
  • Knowledge Base: Your business information in document format
  • Configuration Settings: Controls response style, creativity, and behavior
  • WhatsApp Integration: Connects the AI to your WhatsApp Business account

Step 1: Setting Up Your Account

Begin by creating an account on the Pabi Chatflow platform (www.pabi.com/chatflow). New users receive 100 free monthly credits to test the service. The signup process takes less than 2 minutes and doesn't require credit card information.

After signing up, connect your WhatsApp Business account by clicking "Add WhatsApp Number" in the dashboard. You can use either the WhatsApp Connect method (simpler) or manual token method (more technical). The platform provides step-by-step guidance for both options.

Pro Tip: Use the code "shyt" during signup to get maximum benefits if you decide to upgrade to a paid plan later. Paid plans start at $29/month with unlimited messages.

Step 2: Creating Your AI Agent

Navigate to the "AI Assistant" section in the left sidebar and click "Create New." Name your assistant (e.g., "Real Estate Bot") and select "AI Agent" as the instruction type. This determines how the bot processes and responds to messages.

The AI instructions field is where you define the bot's personality and response style. For a real estate business, you might include:

  • Respond professionally but friendly
  • Always mention property details clearly
  • Offer to provide more information if requested
  • Include contact details in every response

These instructions shape how the AI formulates answers to customer questions. You can refine them later based on actual chatbot performance.

Step 3: Configuring the AI Settings

The AI configuration determines the AI's behavior and response quality.

Step 3: Configuring the AI Settings

The AI configuration section controls how your chatbot responds. Key settings include:

Temperature (0-1)

This controls response creativity. 0 means more factual and concise, while 1 allows more creative responses. For business use, 0.5-0.7 works well - providing enough personality while staying professional.

Model Selection

Choose between different AI models. GPT-5 Mini offers the best balance of speed and accuracy for most business applications.

API Key

Connect your OpenAI API key (required for paid plans). The platform provides a link to create one if you don't have it. This allows the chatbot to access advanced language capabilities.

Stop Keywords

Configure words/phrases that transfer the conversation to a human agent (e.g., "speak to representative"). This ensures complex issues get proper attention.

At 6:15 in the video, you can see how adjusting these settings affects the chatbot's responses in real-time.

Step 4: Uploading Your Knowledge Base

The knowledge base is your chatbot's brain - it contains all the information needed to answer customer questions. Create a comprehensive document (DOCX or PDF) with:

  • Company overview and services
  • Product/service details and pricing
  • FAQs and common customer questions
  • Policies and procedures
  • Contact information

For the real estate example shown in the video, the knowledge base included property listings, neighborhood information, pricing details, and financing options. The more detailed your knowledge base, the better your chatbot will perform.

Formatting Tip: Use clear headings and bullet points in your knowledge document. This helps the AI locate and reference information more accurately when answering questions.

Step 5: Customizing the Chat Interface

The assistant interface section lets you brand your chatbot's appearance:

Profile Picture

Upload your business logo or a friendly avatar that represents your brand.

Color Scheme

Match your brand colors for the chat window, buttons, and text.

Welcome Message

Create a friendly greeting that introduces the chatbot and sets expectations.

Footer Text

Include links to your privacy policy or website.

These customizations create a seamless experience that feels like part of your business rather than a third-party tool. At 7:40 in the video, you can see how different styling options affect the chat interface.

Step 6: Activating Your Chatbot

After configuring all settings, toggle the "Active" switch and save your assistant. Then assign it to:

Specific Chats

Manually enable the AI for individual conversations (good for testing).

Contact Lists

Automatically deploy the chatbot for groups of contacts (e.g., new leads).

All Contacts

Enable the AI assistant for all incoming messages (full automation).

The inbox settings (shown at 8:20 in the video) let you create rules for when the chatbot should respond. For example, you might configure it to handle all initial inquiries but transfer to a human for follow-up questions.

Testing Tip: Before full deployment, test your chatbot thoroughly with different question types. Refine the knowledge base and instructions based on any incorrect or incomplete responses.
