The Manual Problem Nobody Has Time For

Every business with international operations eventually needs country data - coordinates for mapping, major cities for logistics planning, or territories for compliance. The manual approach? Endless Google searches, copying coordinates from maps, verifying city populations, and hoping your spreadsheet stays accurate.

At 3 minutes per country (optimistic), processing 200+ countries would take 10+ hours of focused work - the kind of task that perpetually lingers on to-do lists. Even using ChatGPT in batches requires constant copy-pasting and formatting.

The hidden cost: Manual geocoding isn't just time-consuming - it's error-prone. A single mistyped coordinate or misremembered capital city can derail logistics or mapping systems down the line.

At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see the magic moment where entering "Japan" automatically returns Tokyo's precise coordinates plus Osaka as a second major city - with no manual intervention. The AI handles everything from geographic precision to proper city selection.

Make.com Setup in 3 Steps

The entire automation requires just three connected modules in Make.com, making it one of the simplest yet most powerful workflows you can build:

Step 1: Google Sheets Trigger

Configured to find rows where latitude/longitude data is missing. This ensures the automation only processes countries needing data.

Step 2: AI Data Extraction

Using GPT-5 Mini for efficiency, with a precise system prompt that forces JSON output with all required fields: latitude, longitude, city_1, and city_2.

Step 3: Sheet Update

The parsed data automatically updates the original Google Sheet, completing the loop. The workflow then moves to the next empty row.

In summary: 1) Find empty rows → 2) Get AI data → 3) Update sheet. This simple flow processes hundreds of countries with zero manual work.

AI Prompt Engineering for Reliable Data

The secret to consistent results lies in the AI prompt engineering. At 6:30 in the video, you'll see the exact system prompt that guarantees proper JSON output:

System Prompt: "Define the exact latitude, longitude, and two major cities for the given country or territory. Your response must be in this JSON format: {latitude: number, longitude: number, city_1: string, city_2: string}. Be precise."

This prompt does three critical things:

  1. Specifies the exact data points needed
  2. Forces machine-readable JSON output
  3. Handles both countries and special territories

Testing with Mexico at 7:10 proves the reliability - returning Mexico City as city_1 and Cancun as city_2 automatically.
