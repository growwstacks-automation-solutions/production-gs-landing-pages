Enterprise Voice Agent Requirements

Enterprise voice applications demand more than lifelike speech. They require:

  • On-prem deployment: Healthcare and financial services often prohibit cloud processing of sensitive data
  • Deterministic output: No creative variations when saying product names or addresses
  • Sub-300ms latency: Natural conversation pacing prevents caller frustration
  • Vertical specialization: A model that handles tech support calls can't process fast food orders

General purpose models fail these requirements spectacularly. One Rhyme customer lost a seven-figure deal when their agent mispronounced the CEO's name during a demo - a mistake that would never happen with proper vocabulary boosting.

Speech-to-Text's Hidden Challenges

While TTS handles output, speech-to-text processes the input - and its failures cascade through the entire system. Enterprise STT requires:

  • Entity precision: 99% general accuracy means nothing if you miss 10% of product names
  • Demographic adaptation: Models trained on male voices often fail with female speakers
  • Noisy environment handling: Emergency calls with sirens demand different processing than quiet office calls

Public benchmarks mislead by testing on clean datasets. Real-world performance depends on handling edge cases - like distinguishing Ukrainian from Russian during geopolitical crises, where errors carry consequences beyond accuracy metrics.

TTS Quality vs Speed Tradeoffs

The voice agent "triangle of death" forces developers to choose two priorities:

  1. Quality: Lifelike, accurate speech
  2. Speed: Sub-300ms response times
  3. Cost: Economical at scale

An 8-billion parameter model might deliver perfect quality, but becomes prohibitively expensive at millions of daily calls. Successful implementations balance these constraints based on use case:

Use Case Priority 1 Priority 2
Banking outbound Accuracy Quality
Food ordering Speed Accuracy
Marketing demos Quality Cost

Finding Product-Market Fit

The most successful voice agent implementations follow a phased approach:

  1. Triage: Automate just 3-4 initial questions before human handoff
  2. Measure: Track containment rates and customer satisfaction
  3. Expand: Gradually automate more steps as confidence grows

Horizontal platforms promising "build anything" voice agents fail because they optimize for flexibility over specialization. Domino's succeeded by focusing exclusively on pizza orders before expanding to other menu items.
