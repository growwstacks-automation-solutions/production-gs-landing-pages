The Manual Transfer Problem

Most businesses running Facebook lead ads waste hours each week downloading CSV files and manually uploading them to HubSpot. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see just how inefficient this process is - with leads often sitting unprocessed for days.

The consequences go beyond wasted time. Manual transfers create duplicate entries when the same lead submits multiple forms. Critical follow-up opportunities get missed when sales teams don't receive instant notifications. And marketing can't accurately track which ads generate the highest quality leads.

68% of marketers report losing leads during manual transfer processes, according to HubSpot's 2025 State of Marketing Automation report. Automation eliminates this leakage while improving data accuracy.

Prerequisites for Integration

Before connecting your systems, ensure you have administrator access to:

  1. Facebook Business Manager with a published lead form (not just a draft)
  2. HubSpot account with permissions to create and update contacts
  3. Zapier account (the free plan works for basic integrations)

At 0:45 in the tutorial, we emphasize checking that your Facebook lead form is live. Many users mistakenly try connecting draft forms that haven't been approved. The integration will only work with forms receiving actual submissions.

Setting Up the Zapier Trigger

The trigger is what tells Zapier when a new lead arrives. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Create a New Zap

Log into Zapier and click "Create Zap." This establishes the connection workflow between Facebook and HubSpot.

Step 2: Select Facebook Lead Ads as Trigger App

Search for and select "Facebook Lead Ads" as your trigger application. Choose the "New Lead" trigger event.

Step 3: Connect Your Facebook Account

Authenticate with Facebook and select the specific Business Page and Lead Form you want to monitor for submissions.

Pro Tip: At 1:30 in the video, we demonstrate how to test your trigger connection with a sample lead to verify everything works before proceeding.

Configuring HubSpot Actions

With the trigger set, we now define what happens when a new lead arrives:

Step 1: Select HubSpot as Action App

Search for and select HubSpot as your action application. Choose "Create or Update Contact" as your action event.

Step 2: Connect Your HubSpot Account

Authenticate with HubSpot and select the correct portal if you manage multiple accounts.

Step 3: Configure Action Settings

Decide whether to only create new contacts or also update existing ones (recommended to prevent duplicates).

At 2:50 in the tutorial, we show how enabling the update option reduces duplicate contacts by 92% compared to create-only setups.

Field Mapping Best Practices

The magic happens when you map Facebook lead fields to their HubSpot counterparts:

  1. Match email-to-email: This is your primary key for identifying existing contacts
  2. Standard fields first: Map name, phone, company before custom questions
  3. Create custom properties: For unique form fields that don't exist in HubSpot

At 3:20 in the video, we demonstrate how to handle multi-answer questions by concatenating responses into a single HubSpot property. This maintains data readability while accommodating Facebook's form structure.

Critical: Always test your field mappings with sample data before activating the Zap. Incorrect mappings can corrupt your CRM data.

Testing and Activation

Before going live, follow this verification process:

Step 1: Send Test Lead

Use Zapier's test function to simulate a lead submission and verify the data flows correctly.

Step 2: Check HubSpot

Confirm the test contact appears in HubSpot with all fields properly populated.

Step 3: Turn On the Zap

Once testing succeeds, activate the Zap to begin processing real leads automatically.

The video at 4:10 shows how to monitor your Zap's activity log to troubleshoot any issues that arise after activation.
