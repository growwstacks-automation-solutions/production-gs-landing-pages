Zapier Setup Overview

The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence: Facebook Lead Ads trigger → HoneyBook action. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see the exact two-step Zap configuration that makes this possible.

Zapier acts as the bridge between platforms, listening for new Facebook leads and instantly pushing that data to HoneyBook. The entire setup takes under 10 minutes but delivers ongoing time savings that compound daily.

Connecting Facebook Lead Ads

Begin by creating a new Zap in your Zapier account and selecting Facebook Lead Ads as the trigger app. You'll need to authenticate your Facebook account and grant Zapier access to your lead forms.

Key configuration points:

  1. Select the specific Facebook Page containing your lead forms
  2. Choose the exact lead form you want to connect
  3. Set the trigger to "New Lead"

At this stage, Zapier will fetch sample lead data to confirm the connection works. This ensures all your form fields will be available for mapping in the next step.

Configuring HoneyBook Actions

With the trigger set, add HoneyBook as your action app. The most common actions are "Create Contact" or "Create Project" - choose based on your workflow.

After connecting your HoneyBook account, you'll map Facebook form fields to their HoneyBook counterparts. For example:

  • Facebook "First Name" → HoneyBook "Client First Name"
  • Facebook "Email" → HoneyBook "Primary Email"
  • Facebook "Phone" → HoneyBook "Mobile Number"

Pro tip: Use HoneyBook's custom fields to capture any unique information from your Facebook form that doesn't have a default match.

Field Mapping Strategies

Effective field mapping transforms raw lead data into actionable client records. These strategies ensure maximum utility:

  1. Prioritize key identifiers: Always map email and phone first - these enable follow-up
  2. Preserve context: Add the Facebook ad name as a HoneyBook note to track lead source
  3. Standardize formats: Use Zapier's Formatter to clean phone numbers or dates

Hidden benefit: Consistent field mapping creates searchable, filterable HoneyBook data that powers better reporting and segmentation over time.

Testing and Troubleshooting

Before going live, thoroughly test your Zap using Zapier's testing tools. Common issues and fixes:

  • Missing fields: Ensure all required HoneyBook fields are mapped
  • Format mismatches: Use Zapier's Formatter to adjust date/phone formats
  • Duplicate contacts: Add a search step to check for existing clients first
