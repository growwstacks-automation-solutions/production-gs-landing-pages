Step-by-Step Setup

Creating your first voice agent takes just minutes. Here's exactly how to configure an interview screening agent:

Step 1: Create Custom Agent

Select "Custom Agent" in Fireflies and choose "Screening Interview" as the template. Give your agent a name like "Technical Recruiter Assistant" and select the relevant role (e.g., AI Engineer).

Step 2: Configure Questions

Add your screening questions in order. For technical roles, include:

  • "Walk me through your current role and responsibilities"
  • "What tools do you use most in your work?"
  • "Describe a recent project and your contribution"
  • "What challenges do you typically face?"

Step 3: Add Knowledge Base

Upload your company website, job description PDF, and any technical documentation. This helps the agent answer candidate questions about your company and role specifics.

Step 4: Set Instructions

Define the agent's personality and conversation flow. Example:

"Greet candidates warmly, ask how they're doing. Be professional but friendly. Ask questions directly, allowing time for responses. For unclear answers, ask one follow-up for clarification. Thank candidates for their time at conclusion."

Step 5: Choose Voice & Duration

Select from multiple voice options (male/female, various accents) and set session length (10-40 minutes). Preview the voice to ensure it matches your brand tone.

Step 6: Share Interview Link

Copy the unique URL to send to candidates. They'll enter their name/email and join a call with your AI interviewer.

Prompt Engineering Tips

The quality of your voice agent depends heavily on your prompt engineering. These professional techniques create more effective conversations:

1. Define Clear Objectives

Start your prompt by stating the interview's purpose: "The primary goal is to assess technical skills in LLM engineering while evaluating cultural fit." This focuses the agent's analysis.

2. Structure the Flow

Break the conversation into sections: warm introduction, technical assessment, situational questions, and closing. Specify time allocations for each.

3. List Key Competencies

Identify exactly what to evaluate: "Assess for: 1) Hands-on experience with Voiceflow 2) Python API integration skills 3) Client requirement balancing."

4. Provide Evaluation Criteria

Tell the agent what good answers sound like: "Strong candidates will mention specific projects, challenges overcome, and measurable outcomes."

Pro tip: Include example dialogues showing ideal question-answer sequences. The more examples you provide, the better the agent mimics your preferred style.
