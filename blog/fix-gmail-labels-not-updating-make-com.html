Why Gmail Labels Stop Updating in Make.com

You set up the perfect automation - emails matching certain criteria get labeled automatically in Gmail through Make.com. It worked flawlessly for months, then suddenly stopped. Now important messages slip through unlabeled, forcing you to manually check everything.

This breakdown typically happens when Google's authentication tokens expire. Make.com uses OAuth to access your Gmail account, and these connections need periodic refreshing - especially if you've changed your Google password or security settings. The 'Update Email Labels' module is particularly sensitive to these changes.

85% of Gmail label sync failures in Make.com can be resolved by simply re-establishing the connection, as shown at the 0:45 mark in our video tutorial.

Step-by-Step Fix for Gmail Label Updates

Follow these exact steps to get your Gmail labels updating properly in Make.com again. The whole process takes less than 2 minutes but saves hours of manual email management.

Step 1: Re-add the Gmail Module

In your Make.com scenario, click the plus icon (+) to add a new module. Search for "Gmail" and select "Update Email Labels" (not to be confused with similar modules). This forces Make.com to establish a fresh connection.

Step 2: Reauthenticate Your Account

When prompted, choose your Gmail account and go through the authentication flow. A new window will open where you'll need to grant permissions again. This refreshes the OAuth token that Make.com uses to access your labels.

Step 3: Configure the Module Properly

Ensure all fields are populated correctly. The Message ID field cannot be empty - expand it and select an appropriate filter. Choose your label from the dropdown (you must have created it in Gmail first). Set your filter criteria carefully to match your workflow needs.

Pro Tip: At 1:30 in the video, we show how to use the "Has the words" filter to catch emails containing specific phrases like "subscribe" - a common trigger for automation workflows.

Step 4: Save and Test

Click OK to save your module configuration. Run your scenario manually once to verify it works before relying on it for automation. Check both Make.com's operation history and your actual Gmail account to confirm labels are being applied correctly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

While the fix is straightforward, many users stumble on these pitfalls that can prevent the solution from working:

1. Using the wrong module: Make.com has multiple Gmail modules. Only "Update Email Labels" handles label application. The "Watch Emails" module won't solve your label update issue.

2. Empty Message ID field: As shown at 1:15 in the video, you must expand this section and set a filter. An empty ID field will cause the module to fail silently.

3. Label doesn't exist in Gmail: The label must be created in your Gmail account before it appears in Make.com's dropdown. Check your Gmail labels list first if you don't see your label option.

4. Overly broad filters: While you want to catch relevant emails, filters that are too broad may hit Gmail's rate limits. Be as specific as your workflow allows.

Testing Your Fix Works

After implementing the solution, proper testing ensures your automations will run reliably. Don't skip these verification steps:

1. Manual scenario run: Trigger your Make.com scenario manually and watch the execution. Green checkmarks mean success; red icons indicate lingering issues.

2. Check Gmail directly: Open your Gmail account and verify the test email received the correct label. Don't just trust Make.com's operation history.

3. Review operation details: In Make.com, click into the successful operation to see exactly what data was processed. This helps catch mismatches between expected and actual behavior.

Remember: It may take 2-3 minutes for labels to appear in Gmail after the scenario runs, depending on server load. Don't panic if changes aren't instant.

Preventing Future Connection Issues

While some reauthentication is inevitable, these practices will minimize disruptions to your Gmail label automations:

1. Maintain consistent security settings: Frequent changes to your Google account's security settings or passwords will trigger more reauthentication requests.

2. Enable two-factor authentication: Google accounts with 2FA tend to have more stable third-party connections. This adds a layer of security while paradoxically reducing connection issues.

3. Monitor scenario history: Set aside 5 minutes weekly to review your Make.com scenario operations. Catching authentication errors early prevents prolonged workflow breakdowns.

4. Consider connection redundancy: For mission-critical automations, build redundancy by having a backup connection that you can switch to if the primary fails.
