Solving the Data Sovereignty Challenge

One major barrier to enterprise AI adoption has been data security and compliance. As Sachdev explained (at 12:45 in the video), modern platforms address this through three key architectural principles:

1. Zero-Copy Architecture: Data remains in its original location (Snowflake, Databricks, on-prem systems) while AI agents access it without creating copies or moving it to external platforms.

2. Granular Access Controls: Just as HR systems prevent finance teams from viewing employee health data, AI platforms enforce strict data access policies at the agent level. A call center AI has no access to financial systems, even if both exist on the same platform.

3. Geopolitical Flexibility: With increasing data localization laws, solutions must deploy across public clouds, private infrastructure, and on-prem while maintaining consistent functionality. This prevents vendor lock-in to any single cloud provider or jurisdiction.

Why Small Language Models Outperform LLMs

The Davos discussion revealed a surprising trend: enterprises achieving the best results are increasingly using small language models (SLMs) rather than massive LLMs. Here's why:

Factor Large Language Models Small Language Models
Accuracy Broad but shallow knowledge Deep domain expertise
Latency Higher response times Near real-time performance
Cost per Query $0.10-$1.00 $0.001-$0.01 (100x cheaper)
Hallucinations More frequent Rare in domain of expertise

As Sachdev explained (at 18:30), "We take a large model and surgically remove everything not needed for the specific use case. A telecom billing SLM doesn't need to know Shakespeare - it needs to be the world's best billing expert." This focused approach delivers better results at a fraction of the cost.

Making AI Agents Deterministic for Enterprises

Early AI implementations struggled with unpredictable behavior - a non-starter for regulated industries. Modern platforms solve this through three innovations (discussed at 22:15 in the video):

1

Domain-Specific SLMs

By focusing models on narrow domains, irrelevant outputs (hallucinations) drop dramatically. A claims processing SLM won't suddenly start writing poetry.

2

Guardrail Systems

Pre- and post-processing checks ensure outputs comply with business rules before being acted upon. Think spellcheck for business logic.

3

Deterministic Workflows

Breaking processes into small, well-defined tasks prevents agents from "reasoning" their way into unpredictable behaviors.

Together, these approaches make AI behavior predictable enough for even highly regulated industries like insurance and banking.

The Cost Optimization Breakthrough

Many promising AI pilots fail at scale due to exploding costs. The solution? Context-aware inferencing layers that dynamically optimize resources (explained at 16:40 in the video).

This technology automatically routes tasks to appropriate compute resources based on requirements:

High-Priority Tasks (like call center interactions) get GPU acceleration for real-time responses, while Back-Office Processes (contract reviews) run on cost-effective CPUs with longer latency tolerance.

The system continuously monitors performance needs and adjusts resource allocation, preventing the "everything on GPUs" cost spiral that doomed many initiatives.
