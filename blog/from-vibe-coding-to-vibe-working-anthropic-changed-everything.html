Opus 4.6 Upgrade

The coinciding Opus 4.6 release provided critical upgrades for operational work:

  • 1M token context: Maintains coherence across long documents like contracts
  • Agent teams: Specialized sub-agents work parallel tasks
  • Debugging autonomy: Self-corrects errors during extended workflows

These aren't academic improvements. The legal plugin uses the expanded context to compare NDAs against entire case law databases while sales agents maintain prospect-specific threads across weeks of outreach.

Integration vs Intelligence

The fundamental insight from Anthropic's rollout: intelligence without integration is a demo; intelligence with integration reprices industries. Three integration layers matter most:

  1. Tool access: Model Context Protocol enables secure system connections
  2. Process mapping: Agents follow real workflow stages (not just chat)
  3. Output standardization: Delivers work in expected formats (not just text)

This explains why previous AI advances didn't trigger market reactions - they lacked operational pathways.

Vibe Working Explained

Scott White's "vibe working" phrase captures the paradigm shift:

Before: "Write a sales email template for SaaS companies" (vague, requires editing)
After: "Prepare personalized outreach for these 50 enterprise prospects" (executable outcome)

This moves from describing implementations to describing business results. The agent handles tool logins, data gathering, personalization, and delivery while maintaining compliance - exactly what junior staff typically manage.

Why Most AI Agents Fail

Despite the potential, most AI agent projects fail before production due to three gaps:

  • Reliability: Can't maintain consistent performance across real inputs
  • Security: Lack enterprise-grade access controls
  • Trust: No safeguards against catastrophic errors

Anthropic's plugins succeed by constraining scope (NDA review vs all legal work) and building verification layers into each workflow step.
