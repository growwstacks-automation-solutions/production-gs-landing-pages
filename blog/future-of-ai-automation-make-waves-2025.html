Enterprise Automation Breakthroughs

Many businesses still view Make.com as a tool for simple task automation, but the reality revealed at Make Waves 2025 tells a different story. Enterprises with 20,000+ employees are now building comprehensive automation ecosystems that connect departments and streamline complex workflows.

The key insight from conference speakers was that enterprise adoption follows a pattern of starting with departmental pain points before scaling across the organization. As one automation expert shared: "You don't replace your ERP overnight - you identify high-friction processes where automation can demonstrate quick wins."

Enterprise automation tip: Begin with finance or marketing departments where repetitive tasks often create bottlenecks. Document the time savings from initial automations to build internal support for broader implementation.

Maya: The Next-Gen AI Agent

Make's new Maya assistant represents a fundamental shift in how non-technical users can build complex automations. Unlike previous versions that relied on single large language models, Maya coordinates multiple specialized agents that each handle different aspects of workflow creation.

At the 14:30 mark in the conference video, a Make engineer explains: "Maya uses different agents to first understand your intent, then validate the scenario, and finally build the implementation. This agent-team approach makes it far more adaptable to real business needs than previous assistants."

Make's Three Pillar Strategy

The conference revealed Make's strategic focus on three core areas that will shape automation development through :

  1. Build: Faster scenario reuse and one-click sharing of automation components
  2. Accelerate: Next-gen AI agents like Maya that collaborate as you design
  3. Scale: Make Grid visualization for managing complex automation landscapes

This three-pronged approach addresses the complete lifecycle of enterprise automation - from initial creation to ongoing management of sophisticated agent ecosystems.

Practical Implementation Approach

Several speakers emphasized starting small when introducing automation. The coffee shop owner's story (at 22:15 in the video) illustrates this perfectly - they began by automating bean inventory tracking before expanding to customer communications and accounting.

Implementation roadmap: 1) Identify repetitive tasks causing frustration 2) Document current time/cost 3) Build simple automation 4) Measure improvement 5) Scale validated use cases.

This incremental approach helps overcome organizational resistance while demonstrating concrete ROI at each stage.
