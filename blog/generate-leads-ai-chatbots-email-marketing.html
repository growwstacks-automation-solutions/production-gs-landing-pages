The Email Capture Problem Most Businesses Face

Traditional email capture methods are broken. Popup forms convert at just 3-5%, while website banners get ignored completely. Even worse, these passive approaches collect emails without establishing any relationship or delivering immediate value.

The breakthrough comes when you make email collection an active conversation. Chatbots transform this process by offering something valuable (like a discount) in exchange for contact information. Step 1: Setting Up Your Discount Offer Flow

The foundation is creating a chatbot flow that offers value and collects emails. Here's how to build it:

1. Create the Initial Offer

Start with a simple yes/no question: "Would you like a special 5% discount?" This binary choice makes it easy for users to engage.

2. Add Email Capture

For "yes" responses, prompt for their email address. Include clear language about how you'll use their information.

3. Set Up Confirmation

After email submission, send an instant message confirming their coupon is coming. This reassures users the system worked.

Pro tip: Add an email validation step to filter fake addresses. At 5:12 in the video, you'll see how temporary emails can be detected automatically.

Step 2: Creating Your Immediate Response Email

The first email must deliver the promised value instantly. Here's what to include:

1. Clear Subject Line

Use "Your 5% Discount Code Is Here!" so it stands out in crowded inboxes.

2. Visible Coupon Code

Place the code prominently near the top so users don't have to search.

3. Call-to-Action Button

Include a "Shop Now" button linking directly to your product page.

Step 3: Building Your Drip Campaign Sequence

The real power comes from automatic follow-up emails. Here's how to structure them:

Email 1: Discount Delivery (Immediate)

Contains the coupon code and simple redemption instructions.

Email 2: Value Addition (1 Day Later)

Shares helpful content related to their interests or purchase intent.

Email 3: Gentle Reminder (3 Days Later)

Notes the discount expiration and offers additional assistance.

Step 4: Connecting Facebook Comment Triggers

Now let's make the system automatic by triggering it from Facebook interactions:

1. Set Up Comment Automation

Create a rule that detects comments containing "offer" or similar keywords.

2. Connect Your Flow

Link these comments to your discount offer chatbot sequence.

3. Add Public Reply

Configure a public response like "DM sent!" to maintain transparency.
