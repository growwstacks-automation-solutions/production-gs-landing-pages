The Manual PDF Problem

Every business that collects form submissions faces the same tedious challenge: turning raw responses into professional reports. Whether it's client intake forms, survey results, or application submissions, someone inevitably ends up copying data into templates - a process that's both time-consuming and prone to errors.

The solution lies in automation, but most small businesses assume they need expensive software or developer resources. As shown in the video at 1:23 in the video tutorial, this workflow proves you can achieve professional results using completely free tools.

Time savings: What typically takes 15-20 minutes per report manually becomes fully automated, processing submissions in under 30-60 seconds without human intervention.

Weavely Form Setup

Weavely's AI form builder simplifies creating professional data collection forms without design skills. As demonstrated in our example contact form, you can capture all standard information like names, email addresses, phone numbers, and company details.

The magic happens automatically when respondents submit the form - their data gets structured in a way that Make.com can process. No need for complex API setups or webhook configurations that often required with other form builders.

PDF Template Design

The magic happens in your Google Docs template. By placing field names in curly braces (like {name} or {email}), you create placeholders that Make.com will automatically replaces with actual form responses.

Your template can include:

  • Company branding and logos
  • Pre-formatted sections with your desired layout
  • Conditional elements that only appear when certain form fields are provided
  • Automatic date stamps and tracking numbers

Make Automation Flow

Make.com (formerly Integromat) serves as the automation engine that connects all the pieces. The workflow consists of four key modules:

Step 1: Trigger on Form Submission

The automation starts when Weavely detects a new form response. Make.com watches for these events and immediately processes the data.

Step 2: Create Document from Template

Make.com takes your Google Docs template and creates a new document, populated version with the respondent's information inserted all the designated fields.

Step 3: Convert to PDF

The newly created document gets converted to PDF format while preserving all your careful formatting and branding.

Step 4: Email Delivery

Finally, the system attaches the PDF and sends it either to the respondent (using the email address they provided) or to your internal team for processing.

Pro tip: At 3:45 in the video, you'll see how to set up conditional routing - sending different PDF versions based on specific form responses.

Google Docs Integration

The Google Docs integration handles the heavy lifting of document creation and formatting. Make.com's Google Drive module lets you:

  • Store templates in specific Drive folders for organization
  • Control who can access generated reports
  • Maintain version history of all created documents

This integration means you never need to manually upload or download files - the entire process happens seamlessly in the background.

Automated Email Delivery

The final piece configures professional delivery of your reports. The system can:

  • Send to form respondents with personalized messages
  • CC or BCC internal team members
  • Include multiple attachments if needed
  • Add tracking to confirmations

As demonstrated at 5:10 in the video tutorial, the email content can be as simple or detailed as your process requires - from simple attachments to fully branded HTML emails.
