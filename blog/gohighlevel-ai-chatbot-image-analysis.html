What Changed in GoHighLevel's AI

Until now, GoHighLevel chatbots would stop responding when customers sent photos — a major limitation since many service requests begin with visual documentation. Property managers needed to manually review hundreds of photos. Auto shops wasted time estimating damage from blurry images. Service businesses lost leads when they couldn't instantly respond to visual inquiries.

The October 2025 update changes everything. Now your chatbot can analyze image contents, categorize them based on your criteria, and trigger appropriate workflows — all without human intervention.

Key capability: The AI doesn't just recognize that an image exists — it understands what's in the photo and responds accordingly. In tests, it correctly identified property conditions (excellent/good/bad) with 92% accuracy when given clear evaluation criteria.

How to Set Up Image Analysis

Enabling this feature takes just minutes but will transform how your business handles visual requests. Here's the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Create or Edit Your Chatbot

Navigate to Conversations → AI Chatbots in your GoHighLevel dashboard. Either create a new chatbot or edit an existing one where you want to enable image analysis.

Step 2: Enable Image Processing

In the chatbot settings, look for the new "Chatbot can analyze images" toggle and turn it on. This activates the visual recognition capabilities.

Step 3: Configure Your Prompt

This is where the magic happens. Your prompt needs to explicitly tell the AI:

  • What types of images to expect (properties, cars, junk piles, etc.)
  • How to categorize them (excellent/good/bad, minor/major damage, etc.)
  • What response to give based on the analysis

Pro tip: Include examples in your prompt like "When you see peeling paint and broken windows, categorize as 'bad condition'" to improve accuracy.

Auto Shop Damage Estimation

Auto body shops can now provide instant estimates when customers text photos of their damaged vehicles. The chatbot analyzes:

  • Damage location (front bumper, side panel, etc.)
  • Severity (minor dent vs. major collision)
  • Potential repair costs based on your price matrix

This creates a seamless customer experience while freeing up your estimators to focus on complex cases. Connect it to your scheduling system to automatically offer appointment slots for the estimated repair window.

Implementation note: Start with broader categories like "minor/major" damage before attempting precise dollar estimates, as paint colors and lighting can affect the AI's perception.

This reduces quote turnaround from hours to seconds while capturing leads who might otherwise contact competitors during your response delay.

Prompt Engineering Tips

Your prompt is the blueprint for how the AI interprets images. Follow these guidelines for best results:

  • Be specific about categories: "Rate condition as excellent, good, or bad based on these criteria..."
  • Include visual examples: "When you see rust and large dents, classify as 'major damage'"
  • Set response expectations: "For excellent properties, say 'This looks great! When can we schedule a walkthrough?'"
  • Account for unknowns: "If unsure, ask for additional photos of [specific areas]"

Avoid vague prompts like "analyze this image." The more concrete your instructions, the better the AI performs.

Connecting to Workflows

The real power comes from linking image analysis to your automations. Here's how:

  1. Tag contacts based on image analysis results (e.g., "Premium Lead" for excellent properties)
  2. Trigger different sequences (send a repair estimate vs. a congratulations message)
  3. Update deal stages automatically when visual criteria are met
  4. Notify your team only for specific image classifications

For example, a real estate workflow might:

1. Receive property photos via text
2. AI rates condition
3. Excellent: Notify agent, schedule showing
4. Good: Send seller preparation tips
5. Bad: Trigger rehab contractor outreach
