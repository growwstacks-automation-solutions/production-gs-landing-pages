Setting Up Your Test Call

Before deploying AI calling to real leads, it's crucial to test the system thoroughly. The demo shows two methods to trigger a test call: applying a specific tag to a contact or manually adding them to the test workflow.

The most reliable method shown is manually adding a contact to the "AI Outbound Call Test" workflow. This bypasses any tag delays and initiates the call sequence immediately. The key configuration step is ensuring the correct phone number is assigned to your AI agent in the settings.

Critical step: Many users forget to save their AI agent configuration after assigning a phone number. This simple oversight is the most common reason test calls fail to initiate.

Common Issues and Workarounds

As a new feature, the AI calling system has some known quirks. The most frequent is delayed call initiation - sometimes taking several minutes after triggering the workflow. The demo shows this happening around the 6:15 mark.

Other issues include calls getting stuck in the workflow or delays in the call appearing on the recipient's phone. These aren't necessarily failures - the system may just need more time to process. The workaround is to be patient and check the enrollment history to monitor progress.

Pro tip: If a call seems stuck, don't immediately cancel it. Wait at least 10-15 minutes as the system may still be processing. Canceling too soon can create duplicate call attempts.

Production-Ready Workflow

Beyond testing, the demo reveals a complete production workflow for handling real leads. This sophisticated automation handles four distinct call outcomes: interested, unsure, not interested, and no answer.

For interested leads, the system books appointments directly in the calendar. Unsure leads receive follow-up messages. Not interested leads are tagged appropriately to prevent further contact. No answers trigger voicemail delivery and follow-up attempts.

Implementation insight: The most effective workflows combine AI calls with text and email follow-ups, creating multiple touchpoints that increase conversion rates by up to 300% compared to calls alone.

Handling Different Call Outcomes

The demo workflow shows how to configure actions for each potential call outcome. For interested parties, it immediately books the appointment and sends confirmation details. At 8:45, we see how the system handles an "unsure" response with scheduled follow-ups.

For "not interested" responses, the workflow applies a "Do Not Disturb" tag to comply with opt-out requests. No-answer scenarios trigger voicemail delivery and are rescheduled for a follow-up call attempt later.

Best practice: Configure different voicemail messages for first attempts versus follow-ups. The first can be more general, while follow-up voicemails should reference the previous attempts to increase callback rates.

Compliance Considerations

AI calling brings powerful capabilities but also regulatory responsibilities. The demo emphasizes that calls should only be made to opted-in leads, not cold contacts. The AI always begins with an opt-out message as shown at 4:45 in the video.

Other compliance measures include honoring do-not-call requests immediately, maintaining call records, and ensuring your calling hours align with local regulations. The system automatically logs all call attempts and outcomes for compliance reporting.

Compliance must: Always begin calls with the AI identifying itself as an automated assistant and providing a clear opt-out instruction. This satisfies TCPA requirements for artificial voice calls.
