Step 1: Enabling the Beta Feature

GoHighLevel's voice agent is currently in Labs (beta) with gradual rollout. Here's how to activate it:

Step 1.1: Access Labs Settings

Navigate to Settings > Labs in your agency dashboard. Scroll down to the "Voice Calling Features" section.

Step 1.2: Accept Call Recording Terms

Toggle the activation switch and accept the terms for call recording compliance. This step is mandatory before creating your first agent.

Step 1.3: Verify Phone Numbers

Depending on your account type, you may need to verify at least one phone number for outbound capabilities. This prevents spam abuse of the beta feature.

Pro Tip: Enable the feature at the agency level first, then activate for individual sub-accounts as needed. This maintains control while allowing client-specific configurations.

Step 2: Basic Agent Configuration

With the feature enabled, create your first voice agent with these critical settings:

Voice Selection

Choose conversational voices like Dakota or Zuri over synthetic options. These sound more natural during extended interactions.

Initial Message

Keep your greeting under 5 seconds. Our demo used: "Hi there! I'm Jennifer from Lash University. What brings you in today?"

Interruption Sensitivity

Set to "Moderate" so the AI allows natural back-and-forth without waiting for full sentences.

Call Duration Limit

Cap at 5 minutes unless handling complex sales. Our 3:39 demo call achieved its goal (course registration) efficiently.

Configuration Insight: The "Advanced Behavior" tab lets you adjust speech rate and emotional tone. For educational services, we set a slightly slower pace (+15%) with "Encouraging" tone to mirror patient instructors.

Voice Agent Best Practices

After configuring 30+ voice agents, we've identified these success factors:

Conversation Design

  • Script the first 15 seconds, then go organic
  • Build in natural pauses ("Let me check that...")
  • Limit options to 3 choices maximum per prompt

Technical Setup

  • Use noise-canceling microphones for call recording
  • Enable SSL on your website for secure voice streams
  • Whitelist GoHighLevel IPs in your firewall

Performance Monitoring

  • Review call recordings weekly for improvement areas
  • Track "handoff rate" to human agents
  • A/B test different voice personalities
