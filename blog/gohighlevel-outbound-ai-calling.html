What Is Outbound AI Calling?

Every business owner knows the frustration of leads slipping through the cracks because no one followed up fast enough. Research shows that contacting a lead within 5 minutes of inquiry increases conversion rates by 9x compared to waiting just 30 minutes. But maintaining that level of responsiveness is nearly impossible for most businesses.

GoHighLevel's new outbound AI calling feature solves this problem by having an AI agent automatically call leads immediately after they submit a form, text your business, or request information. The agent can answer questions, qualify the lead, and even book appointments - all without human intervention.

Key benefit: This isn't just a simple robocall system. The AI agent understands natural conversation, can answer complex questions based on your business knowledge base, and makes intelligent decisions during the call to move leads through your sales funnel.

How to Enable the Outbound AI Calling Feature

Before you can start using outbound AI calling, you need to enable the feature in your GoHighLevel account. This is currently in beta but available to all users. Here's the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Access Your Agency View

Unlike most GoHighLevel features, outbound AI calling must be enabled from your agency view rather than a sub-account. Click the blue bar at the top of your dashboard to switch to agency view.

Step 2: Navigate to Labs

Once in agency view, go to Settings in the bottom left, then find the Labs section on the left sidebar. This is where all beta features are located.

Step 3: Activate Voice AI Outbound Calling

Scroll down until you find "Voice AI Outbound Calling" and click "Activate Feature." You'll need to select which sub-accounts you want to enable this for.

Important: The first time you activate outbound calling, you'll need to complete a quick verification process. This typically takes less than 2 minutes and is required for compliance reasons.

Creating Your Outbound AI Agent

With the feature enabled, you can now create your first outbound AI agent. This is different from the inbound agents you may have set up previously.

Step 1: Start a New Voice AI Agent

From your sub-account, go to AI Agents in the left sidebar and click "Create Your First Voice AI Agent." Select "Custom Agent" and continue.

Step 2: Configure Basic Settings

Fill out the agent name, your business name, language, and voice preference. For outbound calls, choose a professional but friendly voice that represents your brand well.

Step 3: Select Outbound Calling

This is the critical difference - make sure to select "Outbound Call" rather than inbound. You'll then configure:

  • Disclaimer message: Required legal notice that this is an AI call
  • Agent's intent message: How the agent introduces the call (e.g., "I'm calling about the form you filled out...")

Building Your Knowledge Base

Your AI agent is only as good as the information it has access to. Creating a comprehensive knowledge base ensures it can answer leads' questions accurately.

Option 1: Web Crawler

The easiest way to build your knowledge base is using the web crawler. This extracts information directly from your website:

  1. In your AI agent settings, go to Knowledge Base
  2. Click "Create New" and name your knowledge base
  3. Select "Add Source" → "Web Crawler"
  4. Enter your website URL and click "Extract Data"

Option 2: Google Docs

For more control, create a Google Doc with all your business information, FAQs, and services. Share it with "Anyone with the link" and paste that URL into the web crawler.

Pro tip: Focus on your FAQ page, service descriptions, and pricing information. These are the questions leads ask most frequently when they first contact your business.

Configuring Your AI Agent

With your knowledge base loaded, it's time to fine-tune how your agent handles calls. Switch to Advanced Mode for full control.

Writing the Prompt

The prompt tells your AI agent how to behave during calls. A good prompt includes:

  • Agent's role (e.g., "customer support specialist")
  • Primary goal (e.g., "book appointments")
  • Call flow structure
  • How to handle objections
  • When to transfer to a human

Setting Up Actions

Actions determine what happens after the call. You can configure:

  • Live transfer: Connect to a human agent
  • Workflow trigger: Start follow-up sequences
  • Calendar booking: Schedule appointments directly
  • SMS confirmation: Send text reminders

Connecting a Phone Number

Your AI agent needs a phone number to make calls from. GoHighLevel makes this easy with built-in number provisioning.

Step 1: Add a New Number

Go to Settings → Phone Numbers and click "Add a Number." You can search by area code to get a local number for your business.

Step 2: Complete A2P Verification

Application-to-Person calling requires verification. This is a simple process that typically takes less than 24 hours.

Step 3: Assign to Your AI Agent

Once verified, select this number in your AI agent settings under "Phone Number." This ensures calls come from your business line.

Cost: GoHighLevel numbers cost $1/month. This is easily offset by the increased conversions from immediate follow-up.

Setting Up the Trigger Workflow

The final step is creating the automation that tells your AI agent when to call. This connects your forms and other triggers to the outbound calling system.

Step 1: Create a New Workflow

Go to Automations and create a new workflow from scratch. Name it something descriptive like "Outbound AI Call on Form Submit."

Step 2: Set the Trigger

Choose what action will start the workflow. Common triggers include:

  • Form submission
  • SMS received
  • Website chat started

Step 3: Add Voice AI Outbound Call Action

Search for "Voice AI Outbound Call" in the actions list and add it to your workflow. Select your AI agent and the phone number to use.
