What Makes AntiGravity 2.0 Different

Traditional AI tools operate like advanced chatbots - you type, they respond, requiring constant manual input. AntiGravity 2.0 flips this model entirely. Instead of being the worker, you become the manager of AI agents that operate autonomously.

The desktop app combines four revolutionary capabilities in one platform: running multiple agents simultaneously, having those agents bring in specialized helpers, scheduling work to happen automatically, and controlling everything through voice commands. At 3:42 in the video, you can see how these features work together seamlessly.

Key difference: While other AI tools require you to sit at the keyboard, AntiGravity 2.0 is designed for the agent to do the work while you focus on strategy and approvals.

Getting Started in 3 Steps

Setting up AntiGravity 2.0 takes less than five minutes. The onboarding process guides you through three critical setup choices that determine how your AI team will operate.

After signing in with your Google account and selecting light/dark mode, you'll land on the "Build with Google" page. Here, installing two key add-ons dramatically expands your agents' capabilities:

  • Web Guidance: Teaches agents how to browse and interact with websites properly
  • Developer Tools: Enables agents to test and validate web pages autonomously

Next, you'll create your first project - essentially a container for all related files, settings, and agent activities. Projects can represent clients, products, or workflow streams, with built-in rules and permissions.

Voice Control Changes Everything

The most surprising feature for many users is how effectively AntiGravity 2.0 handles voice commands. At 5:18 in the video, you'll see a demonstration where a simple verbal request ("Build me a one-page landing site for a local pizza shop...") triggers a complete website build process.

Unlike typing, voice input lets you:

  • Brainstorm complex ideas naturally without structuring them first
  • Give multi-part instructions in a single flow
  • Adjust requirements mid-stream as new thoughts emerge

The agent parses your spoken words, identifies the core requirements, and begins executing - often asking clarifying questions through the planning interface before proceeding.

How Agents Plan Before Acting

One of AntiGravity 2.0's most powerful features is its planning phase. Before executing any task, the agent creates a detailed step-by-step plan that appears in a side panel.

These plans typically include:

  • A flowchart of the intended workflow
  • Questions needing your input
  • The proposed order of operations

You provide feedback directly on the plan through comments, just like reviewing a Google Doc. Only after you click "Proceed" does the agent begin the actual work. This prevents surprises and ensures you're always in control of the direction.

Granular Permission Controls

By default, AntiGravity 2.0 operates cautiously - asking permission before taking any action on your computer. However, the settings allow you to customize exactly how much autonomy your agents have.

In project settings, you can:

  • Whitelist specific automatic actions
  • Define which files/folders are accessible
  • Restrict internet access if needed
  • Create sandboxed environments for experimentation

These controls make AntiGravity 2.0 safe for business use while still allowing efficient automation once you've established trust with your agents.

Running Multiple Agents Simultaneously

The real power emerges when you run multiple agents concurrently. Each conversation operates as a separate agent, and you can have a main agent coordinate specialized sub-agents.

At 7:30 in the video, you'll see how:

  • Research agents gather information
  • Writing agents draft content
  • Quality agents review the work

All agents report to the main agent, who consolidates everything for your review. The interface shows all active agents and their current tasks, with one-click options to unblock any that need input.

The Game-Changing Overnight Feature

The most transformative feature is scheduling. You can set agents to work automatically while you sleep - compiling reports, analyzing data, or preparing materials for the next day.

A common setup might instruct agents to:

  • Scan industry news overnight
  • Identify the three most important developments
  • Draft 200-word briefs on each
  • Compile everything into a morning report

The app continues running in the background with a menu bar icon showing active agents. When you reopen it, completed work is waiting - effectively giving you a full night's worth of productivity while you rest.
