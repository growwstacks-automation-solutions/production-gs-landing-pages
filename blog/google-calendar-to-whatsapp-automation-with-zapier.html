Step 1: Set Up The Google Calendar Trigger

The automation starts when Zapier detects a new calendar event. Here's how to configure it:

Step 1: Select Google Calendar as your trigger app

Choose "New Event" as the trigger event. This tells Zapier to watch for newly created calendar entries.

Step 2: Connect your Google account

Zapier uses OAuth to securely access only your calendar data — no passwords are stored.

Step 3: Test with sample data

Zapier pulls example events to verify the connection works before proceeding.

Pro tip: Filter for specific calendars if you maintain multiple (e.g., only trigger from your "Appointments" calendar).

Step 2: Configure The WhatsApp Action

Now we'll set up what happens after Zapier detects a new event:

Step 1: Select WhatsApp as your action app

Search for and select WhatsApp in Zapier's app directory.

Step 2: Choose "Send Message"

This action sends a WhatsApp message whenever the trigger fires.

Step 3: Connect your WhatsApp Business account

You'll need to verify your number via OTP (one-time password).

Step 4: Customize your message template

Insert calendar fields like {Event Title}, {Start Time}, and {Location} using Zapier's dropdown menu.

Example template: "Reminder: Your {Event Title} is scheduled for {Start Time} at {Location}. Reply YES to confirm or RESCHEDULE if needed."
