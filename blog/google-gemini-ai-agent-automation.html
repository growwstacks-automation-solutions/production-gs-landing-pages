What Is Workspace Flows?

Google's Workspace Flows represents a fundamental shift in how professionals can automate their work. Unlike traditional automation tools that require technical setup, Workspace Flows lets you describe what you want to automate in plain English, and Gemini builds the workflow for you.

The system combines AI decision-making with workflow automation across Google's ecosystem and popular third-party apps. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how it can analyze email content, make decisions about how to process it, and take appropriate actions - all without human intervention.

Key differentiator: Where tools like Zapier require you to manually map every step, Workspace Flows uses Gemini's AI to interpret requests and construct appropriate workflows automatically. This reduces setup time from hours to minutes.

How to Access Gemini's AI Agents

Accessing Workspace Flows couldn't be simpler. As shown at 0:45 in the video, you'll find the Flows button right next to the Ask Gemini option in Gmail's interface. Alternatively, visit flows.workspace.google.com directly.

The interface is divided into three main areas: Discover (pre-built templates), Agents (your active automations), and Activity (logs of what your agents have done). The visual workflow builder makes it easy to see exactly how information moves through your automation.

Pro tip: Bookmark flows.workspace.google.com for quick access. The interface continues to evolve with new features added weekly.

Powerful Built-in Templates

Google has pre-loaded Workspace Flows with dozens of templates that solve common business problems. At 1:20 in the video, you'll see templates for email processing, meeting preparation, lead management, and more.

These aren't just simple triggers - they're complete AI-powered workflows. For example, the "Prepare for Meetings" template doesn't just send reminders. It researches attendees, summarizes previous notes, extracts action items, and compiles everything into a briefing document automatically.

Most valuable template: The "Daily Email Summary" (shown at 6:10) filters out promotional emails, categorizes important messages, and delivers an executive summary to your Google Chat each morning. This alone can save 1-2 hours daily for busy professionals.

Creating Custom Flows

At 3:30 in the video, you'll see the powerful visual workflow builder in action. Starters (triggers) can be scheduled times, specific emails, calendar events, or form submissions. From there, you can add decision points powered by Gemini's AI.

The real magic happens in the Actions section (4:00 in the video). Beyond basic Google app integrations, you can have Gemini analyze content, make decisions, and take appropriate actions. For example, automatically labeling sponsorship emails (shown at 5:15) or extracting action items from meeting notes.

Customization tip: Use the "Ask Gemini" action to add AI decision-making anywhere in your flow. At 5:45, you'll see how this allows for complex email processing that would normally require human judgment.

Third-Party Integrations

What sets Workspace Flows apart is its growing list of third-party integrations (shown at 4:45). Currently supported apps include Salesforce, QuickBooks, Mailchimp, Asana, Confluence, and Jira - with more coming soon.

These aren't superficial connections. You can create Salesforce contacts from forms, update QuickBooks records, build Mailchimp campaigns, or manage Asana projects - all triggered by events in Google Workspace. The video shows examples at 10:30 of creating Salesforce leads automatically from form submissions.

Integration power: The QuickBooks connection alone can save accounting teams 5+ hours weekly by automating customer/vendor creation and payment recording from Google Forms submissions.

Workspace Flows uses starters as triggers - these initiate your automations based on specific events or schedules. Common triggers include receiving emails matching certain criteria, calendar events, form submissions, or specific times.

When setting up a trigger, you can specify detailed conditions. For email triggers, this might include specific senders, keywords in subject lines, or attachments. For calendar triggers, you can set offsets (e.g., "1 hour before meeting").
