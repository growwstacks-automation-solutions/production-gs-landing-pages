The 4-Part Directing Formula

After testing hundreds of combinations, we developed a formula that works for any voice or context:

  1. Role: Who is speaking? (e.g., "A tired, defeated old man")
  2. Emotion: Primary feeling (e.g., "Filled with regret")
  3. Delivery: Speaking style (e.g., "Slowly with a raspy whisper")
  4. Atmosphere: Contextual setting (e.g., "As if telling a final secret")

Example: Compare the basic instruction "sad voice" to our full formula: "A tired, defeated old man filled with regret, speaking slowly with a raspy whisper as if telling a final secret." The difference transforms a computer reading into a character performing.

This works because it gives the AI specific acting directions rather than vague emotions. Like a movie director, you're defining both what the character feels and how they express it.

Creating Character Voices

Switch to multi-speaker mode for animations or games. Format your script with character names in CAPS followed by a colon. Assign each a unique voice profile:

  • PILOT: Younger, energetic voice with quick delivery
  • COMMANDER: Stern, authoritative tone with precise diction
  • ENGINEER: Gruff, pragmatic voice with occasional pauses

The AI maintains each character's vocal identity across lines, creating believable dialogue. For RPG games, generate hundreds of unique NPC voices instantly.

Advanced Voice Directing Tips

Take your AI voices to Hollywood levels with these pro techniques:

  1. Layer emotions: Combine primary and secondary feelings ("Mostly confident but with underlying doubt")
  2. Control pacing: Specify sentence rhythm ("Starting slow, building to a rapid climax")
  3. Add imperfections: Real speech has flaws ("Occasional breath sounds and slight stumbles")
  4. Reference styles: Compare to famous voices ("Similar to Morgan Freeman's cadence but higher pitch")

Remember: The AI interprets your directions literally. "Sound like a bad actor" produces stilted delivery, while "Sound like someone trying too hard to act" creates authentic overacting.
