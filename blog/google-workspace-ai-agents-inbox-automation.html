What Are Google Workspace AI Agents?

Email overload isn't just frustrating - it's expensive. A 2024 Stanford study found knowledge workers waste $12,000/year per employee on unnecessary email management. Google's solution? AI Agents powered by Gemini 3.0 that automate your inbox while you sleep.

Unlike basic filters, these agents understand content contextually. They can translate Portuguese emails to English, extract key points from newsletters, and even screen job applicants - all while maintaining your personal workflow preferences.

Key difference: Regular filters move emails based on simple rules. AI Agents read, comprehend, and take intelligent actions like a human assistant would - just faster and available 24/7.

Agent 1: Auto-Translate Foreign Emails

At 7:50 in the tutorial, we see a practical example: automatically translating Portuguese emails to English. Here's how to recreate this time-saver:

Step 1: Set the Trigger

Create a new agent triggered when emails arrive from specific addresses (like your international colleagues).

Step 2: Add Translation

Use Gemini to translate the message body while preserving the original formatting and attachments.

Step 3: Label and Forward

Create a "Translated" label and have the agent email you both versions for reference.

Pro Tip: At 9:20, we see how to include the original message as an attachment so you can verify translations when needed.

Agent 2: Summarize Key Newsletters

The tutorial demonstrates at 11:30 how to process newsletters like "Sasmus" - extracting key points and links while labeling them consistently.

Step 1: Identify Source

Set the trigger to emails containing both the sender address and specific keywords like "Sasmus".

Step 2: Request Summary

Ask Gemini to: "Recap this email and if it's a guide or link, give bullet points on what I need to do."

Step 3: Deliver Digest

Have the agent email you just the summary every Friday, saving hours of reading time.

Agent 3: Screen Job Applicants

At 14:00, we see how to automate HR workflows by processing Google Form job applications:

Step 1: Connect Form

Select your Google Form job application as the trigger point.

Step 2: Analyze Responses

Configure Gemini to: "Review all answers and see if this is a good candidate for [position]."

Step 3: Filter Results

Only forward applications that meet your criteria, with all key details compiled in one email.

Time Saver: This automation can reduce HR screening time by up to 80% according to the demo at 16:45.

Bonus: Weekly Inbox Recap

At 18:30, the tutorial shows how to create a scheduled agent that:

This ensures nothing slips through the cracks while keeping your inbox manageable. The agent even learns which senders and topics are most important to you over time.

While filters just move or label emails based on simple rules, AI Agents use Gemini AI to understand content, make decisions, and take intelligent actions like a human assistant would - just faster and available 24/7.

For example, a filter might label all emails containing "invoice." An AI Agent could extract the invoice amount, due date, and vendor, then create a Google Sheet record and calendar reminder - all from one message.

  • Filters: simple keyword matching
  • AI Agents: contextual understanding and multi-step processing
  • Example: extract data → create record → set reminder

Agents support multi-step workflows with conditional logic. For example: 'If email contains X, translate it then summarize the key points. If it's from Y, save attachments to Drive and create a task. On Fridays, send me a summary of all unread messages.'
