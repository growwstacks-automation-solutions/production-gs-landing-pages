GPT-5.5 Overview

Most businesses using AI face a frustrating trade-off: either pay for expensive, high-performance models that consume excessive tokens, or settle for less capable alternatives. OpenAI's GPT-5.5 shatters this compromise by delivering frontier intelligence at nearly half the cost of competing models.

This revolutionary model represents a paradigm shift in AI capabilities, focusing not just on answering questions but on completing complex, multi-step tasks end-to-end. Unlike previous iterations that required significant hand-holding, GPT-5.5 can independently plan, use tools, check results, and finish the job with minimal intervention.

Key breakthrough: GPT-5.5 uses just 1/4 the tokens of GPT-5.4 for similar tasks while delivering superior results in coding, research, data analysis, and document creation.

Performance Benchmarks

When evaluating AI models, raw benchmark scores often don't tell the full story. GPT-5.5 achieves an impressive 82.7% accuracy on Terminal Bench (testing complex command line workflows), outperforming all other models by a substantial margin.

On the SWAY Bench Verify test (solving real-world GitHub issues end-to-end), GPT-5.5 achieves 58.6% accuracy. While this appears slightly lower than Opus 4.7's performance, the critical difference lies in efficiency. GPT-5.5 reaches correct solutions with significantly fewer steps, retries, and back-and-forth interactions.

The model's tokenizer differences further amplify its advantage. Competitors like Opus 4.7 require more tokens for the same input and output, making GPT-5.5 faster, more consistent, and more cost-efficient in real-world applications.

Token Efficiency Advantage

Token consumption directly impacts AI operational costs, yet most businesses lack visibility into this critical metric. GPT-5.5's breakthrough efficiency comes from its ability to complete tasks with minimal token usage while maintaining exceptional quality.

Compared to GPT-5.4 High, the new model uses just 25% of the tokens for equivalent tasks. Against Opus 4.7, it requires only one-third the tokens. This efficiency translates to substantial cost savings at scale, especially for businesses running numerous AI workflows daily.

Real-world impact: A business processing 10 million tokens daily could save approximately $12,500 per month by switching from Opus 4.7 to GPT-5.5.

Pricing Comparison

While GPT-5.5 delivers superior performance, businesses naturally question whether the benefits justify the cost. The model is priced at $5 per 1 million input tokens and $30 per 1 million output tokens, with $0.50 per 1 million tokens cached.

At first glance, this appears 20% more expensive than Opus 4.7. However, the critical factor is token efficiency. Because GPT-5.5 completes tasks with significantly fewer tokens, most businesses will actually reduce their AI costs while gaining better results.

Cost example: A task requiring 100,000 tokens with Opus 4.7 might need just 33,000 tokens with GPT-5.5, resulting in a 45% cost reduction despite the higher per-token price.

The model is currently available to all paid ChatGPT users (can be enabled in settings) and via API. Developers can also access it through platforms like Kilo, which offers $25 in free credits.
