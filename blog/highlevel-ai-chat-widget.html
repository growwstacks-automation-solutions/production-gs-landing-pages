How the AI Chat Widget Works

The widget embeds directly on your website as a floating button or full chat interface. When clicked, it initiates a voice conversation with your configured AI agent. Unlike traditional chatbots limited to text, this allows natural spoken dialogue.

Key features include:

  • Multiple voice options with natural-sounding speech
  • Customizable avatars and interface colors
  • Automatic transcription of conversations
  • Seamless handoff to human agents when needed

Setting Up Your AI Agent

Creating your first AI agent takes less than 5 minutes. The process involves:

Step 1: Name Your Agent

Give your agent a friendly, approachable name that fits your brand voice. "Sally" in the demo creates a more personal experience than "Support Bot."

Step 2: Select a Voice

Choose from multiple natural-sounding voices in different accents. The British voice shown at 3:45 adds professionalism while remaining warm and engaging.

Step 3: Configure the Welcome Message

Set the initial greeting that plays when visitors start a conversation. This is your chance to set expectations and guide the interaction.

Step 4: Define Goals

Specify what your agent should accomplish - whether it's answering FAQs, scheduling appointments, or processing orders.

Pro Tip: Start with 2-3 clear goals and expand as you see how customers actually use the widget. Overloading your agent with too many functions initially can reduce effectiveness.

The Power of MCP

MCP (Multi-Conversation Protocol) is the intelligent backend that supercharges your AI agents. While the chat widget handles the conversation, MCP can:

  • Make multiple API calls simultaneously
  • Process complex workflows in the background
  • Combine data from different sources
  • Handle conditional logic based on conversation flow

As mentioned at 1:30 in the video, MCP enables scenarios like checking inventory levels while simultaneously scheduling a delivery - tasks that would normally require separate systems and manual work.
