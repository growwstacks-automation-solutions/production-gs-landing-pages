Beta Access Requirements

Getting started with HighLevel's Outbound Voice AI beta isn't as simple as flipping a switch. The feature requires creating a brand new agent - existing agents won't work. This limitation helps HighLevel maintain control during the beta phase while they refine the technology.

When you create a new agent, the system automatically upgrades it to Voice AI capability. You'll see this reflected in your agent list, where Voice AI-enabled agents are clearly marked. This segregation ensures beta testers are using the most up-to-date infrastructure.

Key limitation: Outbound Voice AI calls must be triggered through automations rather than direct dialing. This creates an audit trail and helps enforce calling restrictions programmatically.

Strict Calling Restrictions

HighLevel has implemented some of the most stringent calling limits we've seen in Voice AI to prevent abuse. Calls are rate-limited to one per minute per location, with each location capped at 100 calls per day. Perhaps most restrictive: each phone number can only be called once per day.

The system enforces a maximum of four calls to any number within a two-week period. Calls can only be placed between 10am and 6pm in your time zone. These restrictions make thorough testing challenging but demonstrate HighLevel's commitment to responsible AI calling.

Testing tip: At 9:00am, calls will fail silently due to time restrictions. Always check the time window before troubleshooting.

Setup Process

Configuring Outbound Voice AI begins in the automation builder. You'll find the action at the bottom of the available triggers, labeled "Voice AI, Outbound Voice AI." Selecting this reveals the configuration panel where you'll name your action and select your Voice AI-enabled agent.

The interface allows choosing from any phone numbers associated with your account. Once configured, the action saves like any other automation component. Importantly, it requires contact information to function - meaning you'll need a form or integration that provides phone numbers to your workflow.

Pro tip: HighLevel's "Learn More" feature provides detailed documentation right in the interface, including use cases and validation rules.

Compliance Considerations

Outbound Voice AI carries significant compliance responsibilities. You must have explicit permission to call contacts via AI, similar to TCPA requirements for robocalls. HighLevel's strict calling limits help prevent abuse, but the responsibility ultimately falls on the user.

We recommend adding a dedicated checkbox on forms for Voice AI calling consent. The wording should clearly explain that calls may be made by an AI assistant. This protects your business while maintaining transparency with contacts.

Warning: Calling someone without permission using Voice AI could result in the same legal consequences as robocalling violations.

Inbound vs Outbound Differences

While the underlying technology is similar, Outbound Voice AI requires different messaging than inbound. Your greeting should identify the call's purpose immediately ("This is Jeff calling from...") rather than the more passive inbound approach ("Thanks for calling...").

The AI behaves identically after the greeting - gathering information, triggering workflows, and following your scripted paths. However, the outbound context means contacts may be less prepared for the interaction, requiring clearer explanations of why you're calling.

Best practice: Create separate agents for inbound and outbound calls to maintain distinct greetings and call flows.

Testing Challenges

The calling restrictions create unique testing hurdles. With each number only callable once per day, thorough testing requires either many test numbers or spreading tests across multiple days. This can slow development cycles significantly.

An alternative approach is testing the core functionality using inbound calls, then verifying outbound-specific elements separately. While not perfect, this workaround can help validate most of your workflow without burning through your limited outbound test calls.

Testing limitation: Calling your own number consumes one of your daily calls to that number, making iterative testing difficult.

Best Use Cases

Given the calling limits, Outbound Voice AI shines for low-volume, high-value communications. Appointment reminders, service follow-ups, and scheduled check-ins are ideal candidates where personalization matters but staff time is limited.

The technology is less suited for cold outreach due to the strict calling limits and compliance requirements. Instead, focus on enhancing existing customer relationships where you already have consent to communicate.

Conversion tip: Voice AI follow-ups to form submissions convert 3-5x better than email alone when properly configured.
