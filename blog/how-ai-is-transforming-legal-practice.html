How Large Language Models Actually Work

Many lawyers approach AI with skepticism, imagining complex systems that "think" like humans. The reality is simpler yet equally powerful - large language models (LLMs) are essentially advanced word predictors. At 12:45 in the video demonstration, the presenter shows how even smartphone keyboards predict the next word, just at a vastly smaller scale than AI systems.

Modern LLMs like Claude Opus or GPT-5 analyze trillions of parameters - patterns learned from legal documents, case law, and general text. They don't understand concepts but identify statistical relationships between words. This explains both their strengths (generating fluent legal text) and limitations (potential for "hallucinating" incorrect citations).

Key insight: LLMs trained specifically on legal materials perform better for law tasks. Claude Opus demonstrates approximately 28% higher accuracy on contract drafting than general-purpose models when tested with proper prompting techniques.

Chatbots: The Drafting Revolution

The blank page syndrome plagues every lawyer. Chatbots like Claude and specialized legal versions (Harvey, CoCounsel) solve this by generating first drafts in seconds. At 18:30 in the video, the presenter demonstrates drafting a Florida eviction notice memo - complete with proper formatting and statutory references.

Three strategic uses for legal chatbots:

  1. Contract generation: Input basic terms, receive a formatted draft
  2. Memo drafting: Describe the legal issue, get an organized analysis
  3. Strategy brainstorming: Test arguments and counterarguments

Pro tip: Enable web search in chatbots to verify statutes. At 22:10, the video shows how this catches outdated references - critical for maintaining accuracy.

RAG Systems: Smarter Legal Research

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) combines AI's fluency with database accuracy. These systems (NotebookLM, Westlaw CoCounsel) search case law and statutes, then generate responses grounded in those sources. At 31:45, the demo shows how uploading case PDFs allows instant analysis across hundreds of pages.

RAG excels at:

  • Summarizing deposition transcripts
  • Comparing case law across jurisdictions
  • Identifying relevant passages in lengthy documents

The presenter emphasizes verification - clicking source links (37:20) to confirm the AI's interpretation matches the original text. This hybrid approach combines AI efficiency with lawyer oversight.

Voice AI: Beyond Transcription

Voice tools like Granola (demoed at 41:15) provide three underutilized benefits:

  1. Real-time documentation: Client meetings automatically transcribed
  2. Deposition analysis: Identify key testimony moments
  3. Hands-free drafting: Dictate emails and memos 3x faster than typing

The video shows how asking "What were the main points from this meeting?" (43:30) generates concise summaries. This saves approximately 6 hours weekly in documentation time for busy practitioners.

Security note: Enterprise versions with client confidentiality protections are essential for legal use. The presenter recommends tools with SOC 2 compliance.
