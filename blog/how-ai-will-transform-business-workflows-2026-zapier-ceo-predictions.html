AI Will Create 170M New Jobs by 2030

The greatest misconception about AI automation is that it primarily eliminates jobs. While certain tasks will become automated, Zapier CEO Wade Foster cites World Economic Forum research predicting 170 million net new jobs will be created by 2030 through AI adoption.

This job growth comes from two sources: entirely new roles that manage and optimize AI systems, and existing roles that evolve to incorporate AI collaboration. The key insight? AI excels at repetitive tasks but struggles with complex judgment calls - creating demand for human-AI hybrid roles.

71% of business leaders say AI will reshape teams through redeployment or new hiring rather than reduction. Only 6% of companies in Zapier's survey anticipated workforce reductions from AI adoption.

65% of Companies Plan to Hire AI Specialists by

Zapier's research reveals a massive shift in hiring priorities: nearly two-thirds of businesses plan to add AI automation specialists to their teams this year. These aren't just technical roles - they're hybrid positions that understand both business processes and AI implementation.

What's driving this demand? As Foster explains in the CNN interview, "Most commonly we're eliminating tasks, not jobs." Companies need specialists who can identify which repetitive tasks to automate and how to redesign workflows around these new capabilities.

46% of organizations will tie pay increases and promotions to demonstrated AI fluency in , making this an essential skill for career advancement across all departments.

Everyday AI vs Transformative AI

Foster makes a crucial distinction between two phases of AI adoption: everyday AI (tools like ChatGPT that assist individuals) and transformative AI (systems that reshape entire workflows across teams). While 2025 was the year of everyday AI, 2026 marks the shift toward transformative implementations.

Transformative AI addresses the "last mile" problem in business automation - connecting disparate systems so data flows seamlessly between departments. This eliminates the friction points where customer information typically gets lost in handoffs between teams.

Sales Workflow Transformation Example

Foster provides a concrete example during the 4:30 mark of the interview: traditional sales processes involve multiple handoffs between researchers, account executives, and implementation specialists - with data often falling through the cracks at each transition.

Transformative AI creates an automated "golden thread" of customer context that follows the prospect through every stage. When properly implemented, this means:

  • Researchers automatically enrich lead profiles with relevant data
  • Account executives receive AI-generated briefing documents before calls
  • Implementation teams inherit complete customer histories without manual transfer

The result? Higher conversion rates, faster onboarding, and customers who feel truly understood at every touchpoint.

How Entry-Level Jobs Will Evolve

One common concern is that AI will eliminate traditional entry-level positions like data entry clerks or junior analysts. While these roles will certainly change, Foster emphasizes they'll evolve rather than disappear entirely.

New hybrid positions are emerging that combine:

  • AI oversight and quality control
  • Workflow design and optimization
  • Cross-departmental coordination

Educational institutions are already adapting curricula to prepare graduates for these roles. The key differentiator? While AI handles repetitive tasks, humans provide the strategic oversight and creative problem-solving that algorithms can't replicate.
