The Hidden Cost of Missed Dental Calls

Dental clinics lose an average of 18% of potential new patients when calls go unanswered after hours. Front desk staff juggling incoming calls while checking out patients leads to dropped calls, double bookings, and frustrated patients who take their business elsewhere.

The traditional solution - hiring additional receptionists - creates new problems. Staff costs rise while efficiency drops, since appointment scheduling is inherently bursty with peaks around lunch hours and evenings when patients call.

Key insight: 63% of patients who can't reach a live person on their first call won't call back - they'll search for another provider instead.

Handling Natural Language Requests

Patients don't speak in calendar invites - they say things like "tomorrow afternoon" or "next Thursday morning." The AI handles this through:

  • Relative date conversion: Knows today's date to interpret "tomorrow" correctly
  • Time range mapping: Translates "morning" to your configured opening hours
  • Contextual understanding: Recognizes that "cleanings" take 30 minutes while "crowns" need an hour

The system even handles corrections gracefully - if a patient says "actually make that 4pm instead," it will check the new time's availability and update the booking accordingly.
