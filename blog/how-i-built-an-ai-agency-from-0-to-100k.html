The Critical First Step Most People Get Wrong

When starting an AI agency, most entrepreneurs make one fatal mistake - they try to offer every AI service under the sun. The creator nearly fell into this trap himself when first entering the AI space, overwhelmed by options like web applications, voice agents, chatbots, and workflow automations.

The breakthrough came when he realized: "If you're everything to everyone, you're nothing to no one." This epiphany led him to focus exclusively on workflow automations using Make.com, despite having no prior experience with the platform.

Key insight: Focus beats versatility in the AI services space. By specializing in one area (workflow automations), the creator could develop deep expertise quickly rather than spreading himself thin across multiple disciplines.

He spent just two weeks learning Make.com through YouTube tutorials before taking on his first client. This rapid skill acquisition was possible because he wasn't trying to master multiple platforms simultaneously. The narrow focus allowed him to reach "good enough" competence much faster than if he'd divided his attention.

At 4:32 in the video, he emphasizes: "We are not developers. We are business owners. Our job here is to solve problems, not solve the issue whether make.com is better than n8n." This mindset shift - from technical comparisons to business outcomes - was crucial for early success.

How to Land Your First Client (Without Cold Outreach)

With basic skills in place, the next challenge was finding that elusive first client. Like many beginners, the creator had no existing network of business owners to tap into. His solution? A simple text message sent to everyone in his contacts:

"Hey [Name], hope you're all good. Over the past few months, I've been working a lot with AI automations that help businesses automate repetitive tasks to save time and increase revenue. Do you happen to know anybody who would be interested?"

This message, sent to about 200 contacts, led to his first client - a restaurant owner referred by his sister. The genius of this approach lies in the question format, which removes sales pressure while leveraging second-degree connections.

For that first client, he worked for free in exchange for two things:

  1. A testimonial he could use to build credibility
  2. Two referrals to other potential clients

This created a flywheel effect where each completed project generated new opportunities. As he explains at 8:15: "They're paying you for your lack of experience... I was grateful to get my first client and gain real-world experience."

The Content + Outreach System That Booked 400+ Calls

While warm outreach worked for initial clients, scaling required a more systematic approach. The creator developed a three-part LinkedIn strategy that eventually booked him 400+ calls:

1. Profile Optimization

His LinkedIn profile became his "website," structured to convert visitors into booked calls:

  • Banner: "Double client capacity without hiring" (outcome-focused)
  • Headline: "I help six and seven figure agencies to double client capacity without hiring more people in 30 days" (specific offer)
  • About section: Used PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) framework
  • Featured section: Direct call booking link to reduce friction

2. Content Strategy

He posted three types of content consistently:

  1. Automation videos: Showing actual workflows he built
  2. Lead magnets: Free resources in exchange for engagement
  3. Personal stories: Building human connection and trust

At 14:22, he notes: "One post reached 700,000 people through LinkedIn's network effect when people commented to access a lead magnet."

3. Outreach System

He combined content with targeted outreach:

  • Connected with 20 targeted prospects daily (LinkedIn's limit)
  • Used personalized connection messages referencing their location
  • Sent Loom videos to qualified connections (40/day at peak)
  • Followed up with intent-based DMs when prospects engaged with content

This system generated 3-6 booked calls per day at its peak, all from a single platform.

The 3-Step Sales Process That Converts 30%+ of Calls

Booking calls was only half the battle. The creator developed a sales process that converted at unusually high rates:

Discovery Call (20-30 mins): Qualify the client by assessing their problem, budget, and urgency. Goal is to determine if they should move to the next stage.

Audit Call (1-2 hours): This paid engagement ($300+) served multiple purposes:

  • Filtered unserious clients (if they wouldn't pay for audit, they wouldn't pay for project)
  • Allowed proper scoping to prevent "scope creep"
  • Identified additional opportunities in their business
  • Built trust through extended interaction

Proposal Call: Presenting the solution with clear:

  1. Outcomes
  2. Deliverables
  3. Timeline
  4. Pricing

At 24:50, he explains: "The audit call allowed me to work with better clients, charge more money, and have less stress." This step was the key to moving from small projects to consistent five-figure engagements.

Client Delivery That Leads to Upsells and Referrals

The final piece of the puzzle was a client delivery process designed to maximize satisfaction and future business:

Onboarding

Set clear expectations about:

  • Timeline (intentionally padded by 2-3 weeks)
  • Availability
  • Communication protocols
  • Software access requirements

Weekly Updates

Regular progress reports served two purposes:

  1. Kept clients informed without them needing to follow up
  2. Created perception of significant work being done

Delivery & Documentation

Every automation came with:

  • Internal testing before handoff
  • Clear documentation
  • Formal delivery email

At 29:15, he reveals: "Automations are really easy. We extended timelines to make it seem like more work." This psychological tactic, combined with exceptional communication, led to upsells and referrals that fueled growth.

Key Takeaways

Building a six-figure AI agency in one year requires focus, systems, and a client-first approach. The creator's journey proves you don't need technical expertise or a large network to start - just the willingness to specialize, learn quickly, and execute consistently.
