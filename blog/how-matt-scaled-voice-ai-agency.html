The Accidental AI Agency Founder

Matthew K. never planned to start a voice AI agency. His journey began in a whiskey packaging sales job where he secretly used ChatGPT to write LinkedIn posts for his boss. "I was generating so much business through his LinkedIn that he confronted me after two months," Matt recalls in the interview (12:45 timestamp).

This accidental discovery led to building custom GPTs for local businesses at £250 each. When voice AI platforms like Vapi emerged, Matt recognized the potential to solve a universal business problem: missed phone calls. "I showed my dad a voice agent booking appointments and his reaction told me everything - this was going to be huge."

Key Insight: The best agency ideas often come from solving your own problems first. Matt's packaging sales experience directly informed his AI voice positioning - "helping businesses never waste a lead."

How a Disastrous Webinar Generated First Clients

Most aspiring agency owners wait until they're "ready" before putting themselves out there. Matt did the opposite - he agreed to teach a webinar about voice AI to 500 people before having any paying clients. "I was on holiday, absolutely terrified, and went into insane detail about every Vapi setting," he admits (23:15 timestamp).

Despite running over time and barely finishing his presentation, that webinar delivered his first two clients. "They saw my depth of knowledge and didn't care that I hadn't done this before for others." These initial case studies became the social proof needed to attract higher-ticket clients.

The $30K Niching Mistake

After hitting $10K/month serving multiple industries, Matt made a costly assumption. "I decided to niche down exclusively to personal injury law firms because it seemed perfect - they run ads and miss calls." He turned away other opportunities to focus on this niche.

The result? Three months without a single law firm client. "I didn't validate the market first. These firms weren't ready to pay for voice AI solutions yet." This mistake cost an estimated $30K in lost revenue before he diversified again.

Lesson: Test niches with small projects before fully committing. Matt now recommends running parallel tests with 2-3 industries before specializing.

The 22% Booking Rate Loom Method

Matt's most effective outreach strategy combines LinkedIn lead magnets with personalized Loom videos. Here's how it works:

  1. Post a free AI resource (agent template, prompt library) on LinkedIn
  2. Ask commenters to DM a specific word to receive it
  3. Screen profiles for ideal client fit
  4. Send a 60-second Loom video saying: "Hey [name], saw you wanted our [resource]. Curious what you're looking to solve with AI voice?"

This approach generates a 22% booking rate compared to 3-5% for cold emails. "The personal video makes all the difference - it's not a pitch, just genuine curiosity about their needs."

How to Control Scope Creep

Early on, Matt fell into the classic trap of adding unbilled features. "Clients would ask 'can it also do this?' and I'd say yes to please them." This led to 15-20 extra hours of work per project with no additional pay.

His solution? Phased agreements with clear boundaries. "Now I define exactly what's included in Phase 1. Any additional requests get estimated for Phase 2." This simple change increased his effective hourly rate by 300%.

LinkedIn Content That Actually Converts

While most AI agencies post educational content, Matt focuses on demonstration posts showing real automations in action. His top-performing formats:

  • Screen recordings of voice agents handling calls
  • Before/after metrics from client implementations
  • "Giveaway" posts offering free agent templates

"The template giveaways perform 3x better than tips posts because they attract serious buyers who want to implement solutions now."

Critical Delivery Lessons

After delivering 50+ voice AI projects, Matt emphasizes three often-overlooked steps:

  1. Client immersion: Spend 1-2 hours recording real calls to understand conversation flows
  2. Simple testing: Start with basic call routing before adding complex logic
  3. Change management: Prepare staff for AI adoption with training sessions

"The technology is easy - the human factors make or break implementations."
