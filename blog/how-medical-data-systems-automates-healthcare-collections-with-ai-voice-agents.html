The Healthcare Collections Challenge

Medical Data Systems (MDS), a pioneer in healthcare receivables since 1985, faced mounting challenges in their collections call center. High employee turnover rates exceeding industry averages made consistent patient service nearly impossible. Training new staff was expensive and time-consuming, with each agent requiring weeks to become proficient in handling sensitive billing conversations.

Tim McDonald, MDS's Application Development Manager, explains their breaking point: "In a call center area, the turnover rate is really high and training new employees is expensive. Finding them in the first place is hard to do." The company needed a solution that could handle their growing call volume while maintaining quality patient interactions.

Healthcare call centers face: 45-60% annual turnover rates (nearly double other industries), $4,000-$12,000 per agent in training costs, and 4-6 week ramp-up periods before new hires become fully productive.

How AI Solved Their Call Center Crisis

MDS discovered conversational AI as a potential solution after reading about retail applications in healthcare receivables. They needed technology that could handle sensitive financial discussions while maintaining HIPAA compliance. After evaluating several options, they partnered with retail for its human-like conversational abilities.

"Retail really stood out as having what we considered the most humanlike conversational AI out there in the business," said McDonald. The AI needed to handle complex billing questions, payment processing, and patient concerns - all while maintaining the empathy expected in healthcare interactions.

The implementation process focused on training the AI with thousands of real call recordings, teaching it to navigate the nuances of healthcare billing conversations. This included handling payment disputes, explaining billing codes, and guiding patients through payment plans - scenarios that typically require human judgment.

Implementation Results That Defied Expectations

The AI voice agent now handles approximately 30,000 calls monthly for MDS, collecting an average of $280,000 in payments each month. Perhaps most impressively, it resolves 70% of calls completely without human intervention, transferring only 30% to live agents.

Key metrics after AI implementation: 30,000 calls/month handled by AI, $280K monthly collections, 70% fully automated resolution rate, 0% increase in call abandonment.

These results exceeded MDS's initial expectations. The virtual assistants speak with patients from hospitals and physician practices, answering billing questions, taking payments, and addressing concerns. Contrary to initial fears, the AI's performance actually improved revenue collection by freeing human agents to handle more complex cases.

Patient Acceptance of AI Voice Agents

MDS initially worried patients would reject interacting with an AI for sensitive financial matters. However, their abandoned call rate remained unchanged after implementation - a strong indicator of patient acceptance. Surprisingly, many patients actually prefer the AI interaction for payment processing.

"There was multiple times as we were setting up the AI and we were listening to the recordings," recalls McDonald. "There was many times where I would hear a conversation and I'd think, 'Uh-oh, how's it going to handle that?' But it handled it and it went on and it answered the questions and everything was great."

The AI's ability to maintain natural flow during unexpected patient responses proved crucial. It handles payment disputes, explains billing statements, and guides patients through complex insurance questions - all while maintaining HIPAA compliance throughout the interaction.

How AI Handles Payment Processing

The AI voice agent seamlessly processes payments using major credit cards. Patients can say their card number aloud or enter it via phone keypad. The system confirms payment details securely without human intervention, then provides confirmation and next steps.

Payment processing follows this workflow:

Step 1: Payment Method Selection

The AI offers Mastercard, Visa, American Express, and Discover as payment options, asking the patient which they'd like to use.

Step 2: Secure Card Entry

Patients can either say their card number aloud or enter it using their phone keypad, with pound (#) signaling completion.

Step 3: Transaction Confirmation

The system confirms successful payment processing and informs patients when their account will reflect the payment (typically within 5 business days).

Payment security note: All payment processing occurs through PCI-compliant systems with end-to-end encryption, meeting healthcare industry security standards.

Future AI Expansion in Healthcare

MDS sees AI playing an expanding role in healthcare receivables. "We believe the use of AI at MDS in the future will only expand," says McDonald. "They've been a huge benefit to us and I can only see additional benefits to be gained in the future."

Potential expansions include:

  • Handling more complex billing disputes currently requiring human agents
  • Integrating with patient portals for seamless online-to-phone support
  • Expanding to additional languages to serve diverse patient populations
  • Incorporating predictive analytics to identify payment plan candidates

The success at MDS demonstrates how AI can transform healthcare operations beyond clinical applications. By automating routine but critical financial interactions, healthcare organizations can improve both operational efficiency and patient satisfaction.
