The Website That Was Hurting Enrollment

Nelson College's website had become an enrollment liability. "We did not want people to go to the website," admits one administrator in the case study video. Critical information like program details and campus life essentials were buried 10-20 clicks deep in an outdated architecture.

The college's 6,000-page website contained redundant, outdated content alongside missing critical information. Prospective students would search for basic details like meal plans, only to hit dead ends or incorrect information. The resulting bounce rates were alarmingly high, with many visitors giving up entirely.

Key insight: Many education websites unintentionally create frustration by prioritizing organizational structure over student needs. Nelson's transformation began when they shifted perspective to ask "What do students actually search for?" rather than "How do we want to present information?"

Building an Advisor-Inspired Chatbot

The breakthrough came when Nelson College stopped thinking about their website as a content repository and started designing it as a digital advisor. They built their AI chatbot using actual scripts from phone conversations between admissions staff and prospective students.

This advisor-first approach created several advantages:

  • The chatbot's tone matched real college interactions
  • Responses included helpful hyperlinks like an advisor would provide
  • It could handle variations of the same question just like human staff

"It's really flexible and there's a lot of care that went into it," notes one team member in the video. The system was designed to provide general information while seamlessly directing students to deeper resources when needed.

How Vector Search Transformed Content Discovery

The true game-changer was implementing a vector database for semantic search. Traditional keyword search failed because students phrased questions differently than how content was labeled. The vector database allowed the system to understand intent rather than just match keywords.

Implementation involved:

  1. Content audit: Reducing 6,000 pages to core, relevant information
  2. Vector embedding: Converting all content into numerical representations
  3. Semantic indexing: Creating relationships between conceptually similar content

Pro tip: Nelson's team used AI to generate variations of common questions (e.g., "What's the food like?" vs. "Tell me about meal plans") to train their system. This created robust understanding of student intent regardless of phrasing.

The Art of Prompt Engineering for Education

"James would put a lot of time into massaging that prompt," explains one team member about their prompt engineering process. They needed responses that were:

  • Accurate and factual
  • Aligned with institutional values
  • Helpful without being overwhelming
  • Transparent about limitations

The team started with advisor scripts, then iteratively refined prompts based on real student interactions. They developed guardrails to prevent hallucinations while maintaining the helpful, conversational tone that made the system effective.

Discovering Critical Content Gaps

One unexpected benefit: The chatbot revealed content that students needed but couldn't find. "The meal plans were on the website, but somewhere along the content migration, the actual offering within the meal plan had fallen off the site," recalls a team member.

The chatbot identified these gaps by:

  1. Tracking frequently asked questions
  2. Noticing when multiple users asked similar questions unsuccessfully
  3. Identifying topics where responses lacked supporting links

This allowed Nelson to proactively fix missing content rather than waiting for frustrated calls to the admissions office.

30% Lower Bounce Rates & 25% More Visitors

The results spoke for themselves:

  • 30% reduction in bounce rates as students found what they needed
  • 25% increase in total visitors as the site became more useful
  • Fewer repetitive calls/emails to overworked staff
  • Improved student satisfaction with digital services

"We were quite embarrassed of our old website and now we are quite proud of this one," shares a Nelson administrator. The transformation went beyond metrics - it changed how the college thought about serving students digitally.

How to Implement This at Your Institution

Based on Nelson College's experience, here's a proven implementation framework:

Phase 1: Content Audit
Identify your most searched-for content and fix gaps (1-2 weeks)

Phase 2: Chatbot Training
Collect advisor scripts and FAQ documents (1 week)

Phase 3: Vector Implementation
Set up semantic search across your content (2-3 weeks)

Phase 4: Testing & Refinement
Pilot with real users and iterate (ongoing)

The entire process can be completed in 4-6 weeks with focused effort. As Nelson discovered, the investment pays dividends in student satisfaction and staff time savings.
