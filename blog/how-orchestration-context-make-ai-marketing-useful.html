The Generic AI Problem Every Marketer Faces

You've likely experienced this: ask ChatGPT for marketing advice, and you get textbook responses that sound smart but lack real-world applicability. "Build brand awareness!" "Create engaging content!" "Understand your audience!" The advice isn't wrong - it's just not specific enough to drive meaningful action for your unique business.

The fundamental limitation isn't the AI's capability - it's the context vacuum in which most marketers use these tools. Without access to your CRM data, customer interactions, and campaign history, even the most advanced AI can only offer Marketing 101 platitudes. This creates a dangerous gap between the promise of AI and its practical utility.

68% of marketers report that generic AI outputs require significant editing before being usable, according to a MarTech survey. The missing ingredient? Context about their specific business and customers.

The Context Gap That Makes AI Marketing Fall Flat

Consider what happens when you ask an AI to "create a follow-up email for our new lead." Without context about who that lead is, what content they've engaged with, or their position in the buyer's journey, the AI must default to generic templates. The result feels impersonal and often misses key opportunities to advance the relationship.

Top-performing marketers solve this by building systems that automatically gather and apply contextual data before AI generation begins. They know that an AI writing an email with access to the lead's LinkedIn profile, past support tickets, and recent website behavior will outperform one working in the dark by 3-5x in engagement metrics.

How Data Orchestration Solves the Context Problem

Orchestration creates intelligent workflows that gather and synthesize data from multiple sources before activating AI. When a new lead comes in, an orchestrated system might automatically:

  1. Check your CRM for existing company contacts
  2. Pull enrichment data from LinkedIn or ZoomInfo
  3. Review past support interactions
  4. Analyze content engagement history

Only after compiling this context does it prompt the AI to generate content. This transforms the output from generic to hyper-relevant, as demonstrated at the 2:15 mark in our tutorial video.

Orchestration isn't about replacing human marketers - it's about giving them (and their AI tools) the same contextual awareness they'd have after hours of manual research, delivered instantly.

Real-World Example: From Vanilla to Hyper-Relevant

A B2B SaaS company implemented orchestration for their webinar follow-ups. Previously, AI-generated emails had a 14% open rate. After adding workflow steps that:

  • Pulled the attendee's job title from their registration
  • Checked for past product usage
  • Analyzed which webinar segments they engaged with most

The new context-rich AI emails achieved 42% open rates and 3x more demo bookings. The AI wasn't smarter - it was better informed.

3 Implementation Steps for Context-Rich AI Campaigns

Step 1: Audit Your Data Sources

List every system containing customer or prospect data - CRM, support tools, webinar platforms, etc. Identify which data points would most improve AI outputs if accessible during content generation.

Step 2: Design the Orchestration Flow

Map how data should flow between systems before AI activation. A lead follow-up might first check the CRM, then enrichment tools, then past interactions - all before prompting the AI.

Step 3: Build and Test Workflows

Use tools like Make.com or n8n to create automated workflows that gather and synthesize context. Start with one campaign type, measure improvements, then expand.

Pro Tip: Begin with your highest-value campaigns where personalization matters most - enterprise sales outreach or high-ticket customer onboarding are ideal starting points.

Common Mistakes That Sabotage AI Marketing Results

Three frequent errors undermine AI marketing success:

  1. Starting with AI instead of data: Prompting ChatGPT before gathering context guarantees generic outputs.
  2. Overlooking existing systems: Your CRM and support tools contain goldmines of contextual data - use them.
  3. Expecting human-level intuition: AI needs explicit context - don't assume it can "read between the lines."

The solution? Always ask "What context would a human marketer need to create this?" Then build workflows that provide that data to your AI tools.
