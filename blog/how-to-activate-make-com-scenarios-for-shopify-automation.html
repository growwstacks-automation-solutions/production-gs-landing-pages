Why Automate Shopify Tasks?

Every Shopify store owner knows the frustration of repetitive manual tasks. Updating order statuses, syncing inventory, and sending customer notifications eat up valuable time that could be spent growing your business. At 3:15 in the video, we see how these mundane tasks can consume hours each week.

Automation solves this by handling routine operations automatically. Make.com's Shopify integration lets you create workflows that trigger actions based on events in your store - like automatically updating orders when inventory ships or sending follow-up emails after delivery.

Time-saving impact: The Update Order module featured in this guide can save store owners 5-10 hours weekly on order management alone. For businesses processing 50+ orders daily, this automation pays for itself quickly.

Accessing Make.com's Platform

The first step is logging into your Make.com account (formerly Integromat). If you don't have one, you'll need to sign up for a plan that includes Shopify integrations. The platform offers a visual interface where you'll build your automation scenarios.

Once logged in, navigate to the Scenarios section using the top menu. This is where all your automations live. The interface may seem overwhelming at first, but we'll focus only on the essential elements needed for Shopify automation.

Creating Your First Scenario

Click the "Create Scenario" button to start building your automation. You'll be presented with a blank canvas where you can add and connect modules. The key to efficiency is using the search field effectively - this helps you quickly find the specific Shopify actions you need.

At 1:45 in the video, we demonstrate how to search for "Shopify" to see all available modules. Being precise with your search terms reduces scrolling and helps you find the right modules faster. This becomes especially important as you build more complex workflows.

Selecting Verified Modules

Make.com offers both official and community-created modules. For reliability, always look for the "Verified" badge when selecting Shopify modules. These have been tested by Make.com's team and are guaranteed to work with the latest Shopify API.

The video at 2:30 highlights the Update Order module - one of the most useful verified options for Shopify automation. This module lets you automatically modify order details like status, tags, or notes based on triggers from other apps or schedules.

Pro tip: Verified modules receive priority support from Make.com if issues arise. They're also updated automatically when Shopify makes API changes, ensuring your workflows keep running smoothly.

Connecting Your Shopify Store

After selecting your module, you'll need to connect your Shopify store. Click the "Connection" button in the module settings. Make.com will guide you through authorizing access to your store's data via Shopify's API.

At 4:50 in the tutorial, we show the authorization process where you'll log into your Shopify admin to approve the connection. For added security, you can create a dedicated private app in Shopify specifically for Make.com integrations rather than using your main admin credentials.

Testing and Saving Your Workflow

Before activating your scenario, always run a test using the "Run Once" button. This executes a single cycle of your workflow so you can verify it works as expected. Check your Shopify admin to confirm the test order update was processed correctly.

The final step at 5:40 in the video shows how to save your scenario. Make.com will store all your settings and connection details. Once saved, you can schedule how often the scenario runs or set it to trigger instantly when specific events occur in your store.

Remember: Always revisit your scenarios after major Shopify updates or when changing store settings. While verified modules auto-update, some configurations may need manual adjustments.
