Step 1: Train Your Chatbot Knowledge Base

The foundation of an effective Bitrix24 chatbot is its training data. Unlike basic rule-based bots, AI chatbots learn from your specific business content to provide accurate, contextual responses.

You can train your chatbot using three types of sources:

1. Website URLs

Point the chatbot to your website or specific knowledge base pages. The AI will crawl and learn from this content, enabling it to answer questions about your services, pricing, or policies.

2. Document Uploads

Upload PDFs, Word documents, or other files containing product specifications, FAQs, or training materials. This is particularly useful for complex products or regulated industries where precise wording matters.

3. Sample Questions

Add common customer inquiries along with ideal responses. This direct training helps the chatbot handle specific scenarios exactly how your team would.

Step 2: Configure Chatbot Personality

Your chatbot's tone should match your brand voice and customer expectations. The configuration panel offers multiple personality options:

  • Professional: Formal business language for B2B or corporate environments
  • Friendly: Warm and approachable for consumer-facing brands
  • Casual: Relaxed tone for younger demographics or informal industries
  • Polite: Respectful and considerate for sensitive topics or services

You can also customize the chatbot's name, avatar, and opening message to create a cohesive experience. At 3:40 in the video, notice how changing these elements immediately transforms the user experience.

Step 3: Integrate with Bitrix24 Live Chat

Connecting your trained chatbot to Bitrix24 takes just a few clicks:

1. Navigate to Contact Center

In your Bitrix24 portal, go to Contact Center → Live Chat. Select the open channel where you want the chatbot to appear.

2. Add Chatbot Configuration

Click "Configure Chat Bots" and select your newly created AI chatbot from the dropdown menu.

3. Set Handoff Rules

Define when the chatbot should transfer conversations to human agents (e.g., when a lead is qualified or when specific keywords appear).
