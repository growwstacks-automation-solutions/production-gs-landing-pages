Why Team Access Matters for Automation

Many growing businesses hit a wall when their automation workflows become too complex for one person to manage. Without proper team access, you either bottleneck all automation work through a single person or risk security issues by sharing login credentials.

Make.com's organization features solve this by letting you grant precise access levels to team members. Companies that properly set up team access see 3x faster automation development while maintaining security controls. This system allows your marketing, sales, and operations teams to collaborate on workflows while keeping sensitive data protected.

Key benefit: Proper user management means your finance team can access payment automations while your marketing team works on social media workflows - all without stepping on each other's toes or seeing data they shouldn't.

Step 1: Access Organization Settings

Begin by logging into your Make.com account. If you're the organization owner or admin, you'll see the organization tab at the top of your dashboard. This is your control center for all team management functions.

At the 0:45 mark in the video tutorial, you can see exactly where this tab appears. Clicking it takes you to the organization management area where you can handle billing, users, teams, and security settings all in one place.

Step 2: Navigate to Users Section

Within the organization settings, locate and click on the Users tab. This displays all current members of your Make.com organization along with their roles and team assignments.

Look for the prominent "Invite User" button - this is your gateway to adding new team members. The interface is designed to be intuitive, but we'll walk through each field to ensure you set up permissions correctly.

Step 3: Invite New User

Clicking "Invite User" opens a form where you'll enter the new member's email address. Make.com will validate this address format automatically as you type.

This is also where you'll assign the user's initial role. For most team members, the "Member" role provides the right balance of access - they can create and edit workflows but can't change organization settings. Save "Admin" roles for those who need full control.

Pro tip: Always double-check the email address before sending. A typo here means your team member won't receive their invitation, delaying their onboarding.

Step 4: Set Permissions and Teams

If you've organized your workflows into teams (recommended for companies with multiple departments), this is where you assign the new user to the appropriate team. Team assignments help keep workflows organized and limit access to only relevant automations.

At the 2:10 timestamp in the video, you can see how to create teams if you haven't already. This extra layer of organization pays dividends as your automation catalog grows.

Step 5: Review and Send Invitation

Before finalizing, review all entered information - especially the email address and role assignment. Once satisfied, click "Save" to send the invitation.

The new user will receive an email with activation instructions. They'll have 7 days to accept before the invitation expires. You can resend invitations if needed from the Users section.

Best Practices for Team Collaboration

Simply adding users isn't enough - you need a strategy for effective collaboration. Start by creating teams that mirror your organizational structure (Marketing, Sales, Operations, etc.). This keeps workflows organized and access appropriately limited.

Document your naming conventions for scenarios and modules so team members can easily understand each other's work. Consider holding monthly automation review meetings where teams can share their most effective workflows.

Remember that permission levels can be adjusted as team members' roles evolve. Regular audits of user access help maintain security as your organization grows.

Adding users to your Make.com organization unlocks powerful team collaboration for your automation workflows. By following these steps, you can onboard team members in minutes while maintaining proper security controls through role-based access.
