Zapier Account Setup

Before connecting Whop, you'll need a Zapier account. The platform offers a free tier that includes 100 tasks/month - enough for testing basic automations. Paid plans start at $19.99/month for businesses with higher volume needs.

Follow these steps to prepare your Zapier account:

Step 1: Create or Log In to Your Zapier Account

Visit zapier.com and either sign up for a new account or log in existing credentials. Business accounts have access to all the same features - the main differences are in monthly task limits and premium apps.

Step 2: Navigate to the Zap Editor

Once logged in, click "Create Zap" from the dashboard. This opens the visual workflow editor where you'll build your automation workflow (called a "Zap").

Pro Tip: Bookmark the Zapier editor URL (zapier.com/app/editor) for quick access. You'll return here often to check on your automations' performance.

Configuring Your Whop Trigger

The trigger is the event that starts your automation. For Whop integrations, common triggers include:

  • New membership activation
  • Membership renewal
  • Subscription cancellation
  • Product purchase

Follow this process configure your Whop trigger:

Step 1: Select Whop as Your Trigger App

In the Zap editor, search for "Whop" in the trigger app selection. Whop maintains an official Zapier integration, so you should see it appear in the dropdown.

Step 2: Choose Your Trigger Event

Select the specific Whop event that should start your automation. For membership businesses, "Membership Went Valid" (triggering when a customer's membership becomes active) is most common.

Step 3: Connect Your Whop Account

Click "Sign in to Whop" and follow the OAuth prompts to securely link your account. You'll need admin access to the Whop you want to complete this step.

Important: Keep pop-ups enabled during the connection. The OAuth flow opens in a new window that some browsers may block initially.

Adding Action Steps

Actions what happens after your trigger fires. This where Zapier's real power comes into play - you can connect Whop events to virtually any other business tool.

Step 1: Choose Your Action App

Click the "+" button add an action step after your Whop trigger. Search for the app you want to connect with (Slack, Google Sheets, Mailchimp, etc.) and select it from the dropdown.

Step 2: Select the Specific Action

Each app offers multiple action types. For example, with Slack you might choose "Send Channel Message" or with Google Sheets "Add Row to Spreadsheet." Pick the action that matches your workflow goal.

Step 3: Map Your Data Fields

This is where magic happens. Use Zapier's data picker to insert dynamic fields from your Whop trigger into the action inputs. Instead of static text, you can pull in the member's name, email, purchase details, and more.

Dynamic fields ensure each automation run uses current, accurate data rather than generic placeholder text. This what makes Zapiermations feel personalized and professional.

Testing Your Integration

Before going live, always test your Zap thoroughly. Zapier provides built-in testing tools that let you verify each step works as intended.

Step 1: Test the Trigger

Zapier will attempt to pull a recent example from your Whop account. If no sample appears (common with new businesses), create a test membership in Whop that matches your trigger criteria.

Step 2: Test Action Step

After configuring your action, run a test to confirm data flows correctly to your target app. Check Slack, your Google Sheet, or wherever the action should land verify the output.

Step 3: Review Data Mapping

Pay special attention to how fields are mapped between apps. A common mistake is swapping first/last names or selecting the wrong email field. Correct any mapping errors before publishing.

Testing Tip: Use a test channel in Slack or a dedicated test sheet in Google Drive during development. This prevents accidental notifications to real customers or teammates.

Troubleshooting Tips

Most Whop-Zapier integrations run smoothly once configured, but these common issues occasionally arise:

1. Authentication Errors

Symptom: "Account not connected" messages
Fix: Reauthenticate your Whop connection in Zapier. API tokens occasionally expire after platform updates.

2. Missing Data

Symptom: Blank fields in action steps
Fix: Confirm your Whop trigger is pulling complete data correctly. Some fields require premium Whop plans.

Symptom: Multiple entries for single trigger
Fix: Adjust your Zap's deduplication settings and ensure your Whop webhooks aren't firing repeatedly.

Support Tip: Zapier's execution history shows detailed logs for every automation run - your first stop when diagnosing issues.
