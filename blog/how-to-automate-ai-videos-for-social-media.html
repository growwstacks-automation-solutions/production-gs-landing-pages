The AI Video Cost Reality (And Why It's Worth It)

Every social media manager knows the pain: you need to post daily video content to stay relevant, but creating quality videos manually takes hours of scripting, filming, and editing. The alternative? Paying a video editor $500-$2000 per month for just a few weekly posts.

AI video generation changes everything - but at what cost? Currently, OpenAI's Sora 2 charges 10-50 cents per second depending on model quality, while Google's Veo 3.1 ranges from 15-40 cents per second. A 15-second video could cost $1.50-$7.50 to generate.

The breakthrough: This automation posts 3 AI videos per week for $18-$90 - far less than human editing costs. As models improve and prices drop (which they will), your cost per video decreases while quality increases.

How the Automation Works: 3 Simple Steps

The system transforms news articles into social-ready videos automatically. No filming. No editing. Just set it and forget it.

  1. Content Trigger: RSS feeds monitor industry news sources for fresh article ideas
  2. AI Video Generation: Sora or Veo creates videos from article summaries
  3. Cross-Platform Posting: Videos upload to Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts automatically

At 2:15 in the tutorial video, you'll see how the RSS feed trigger pulls in a tech article about "Yelp's AI receptionist" - which then becomes a fully produced Instagram Reel with no human intervention.

Step 1: Setting Up Your Content Trigger

The automation starts by monitoring RSS feeds from industry news sources. This ensures your videos are always relevant and timely.

For sites without RSS feeds, the workflow uses RSS.app to create custom feeds (shown at 4:30 in the video). Simply paste any website URL, and it generates a feed you can plug into Make.com. This works for:

  • Industry blogs and news sites
  • Competitor social accounts
  • Trending topics in your niche

Pro Tip: Start with 2-3 high-quality sources rather than dozens. The Verge's tech RSS feed (used in the tutorial) consistently provides viral-worthy AI content.

Step 2: AI Video Generation with Sora/Veo

Once the RSS feed detects a new article, the automation:

  1. Generates a video script from the article summary
  2. Sends the prompt to Sora or Veo
  3. Downloads the finished video to Dropbox

At 7:50 in the video, you'll see the critical setting: "Wait for video to be generated" must be enabled. This ensures the automation doesn't proceed until your AI video is complete.

The system includes a proven prompt template for viral-style short videos (portrait orientation, professional tone, captions). You can customize this to match your brand voice.

Step 3: Cross-Platform Social Media Posting

The final step uses Blatato's API (a $29/month tool) to post videos across all platforms simultaneously. This handles:

  • Instagram Reels
  • TikTok
  • YouTube Shorts
  • Facebook
  • LinkedIn

At 14:20 in the tutorial, you'll see the Instagram post created automatically from the "Yelp AI receptionist" video. The system even generates platform-optimized captions using GPT-4.

Testing Strategy: As shown at 15:40, start by posting to your smallest audience first (like Instagram vs TikTok) to refine quality before scaling.
