Zapier Setup Overview

The complete automation requires just 7 steps in Zapier, taking less than 10 minutes to configure. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how simple the initial setup is compared to the hours of manual work it replaces.

Here's the high-level process we'll walk through:

  1. Create new Zap in your Zapier dashboard
  2. Set Amazon Seller Central as the trigger app
  3. Authorize Zapier in Amazon Seller Central
  4. Set Protexting as the action app
  5. Map order data fields between systems
  6. Test the automation with sample orders
  7. Publish your Zap to go live

Pro tip: Have both your Amazon Seller Central and Protexting login credentials ready before starting to streamline the setup process.

Connecting Amazon Seller Central

The first critical step is establishing the connection between Zapier and your Amazon Seller Central account. This grants Zapier permission to monitor for new orders while keeping your account secure.

After creating your new Zap, you'll:

  1. Search for and select "Amazon Seller Central" as your trigger app
  2. Choose "New Order" as the trigger event
  3. Enter your Amazon Seller ID when prompted
  4. Provide the Zapier developer ID: 6,222-904-945

At this point, Zapier will attempt to pull a sample order to verify the connection works. Don't worry if this fails initially - we still need to complete authorization in Amazon Seller Central first.

Authorizing Zapier Developer ID

The most technical part of the setup occurs in Amazon Seller Central where you'll authorize Zapier as a trusted developer. This security measure ensures only approved applications can access your order data.

Follow these steps carefully:

  1. Log in to Amazon Seller Central in a separate tab
  2. Navigate to Apps and Services > Manage Your Apps
  3. Select "Authorize New Developer"
  4. Enter "Zapier" as the developer name
  5. Input the developer ID: 6,222-904-945
  6. Agree to the terms and generate your OAuth token
  7. Copy the token and paste it back into Zapier

Important: The authorization token expires after 90 days. Mark your calendar to renew it before expiration to avoid service interruptions.

Setting Up Protexting Action

With Amazon successfully connected, we now configure what happens when a new order arrives - pushing it automatically to Protexting for verification.

In your Zap:

  1. Click "Add Step" and select "Action"
  2. Search for and choose "Protexting" as your action app
  3. Select "Push Amazon Orders" as the action event
  4. Sign in to your Protexting account when prompted
  5. Map the Amazon order fields to Protexting's requirements

The field mapping determines which order details get sent to Protexting. At minimum, you'll want to include order ID, customer email, product details, and shipping information.

Testing Your Automation

Before going live, thorough testing ensures your automation works flawlessly. Zapier makes this easy with built-in test functionality.

For reliable testing:

  1. Use Zapier's "Test Trigger" to pull a recent sample order
  2. Verify all order details appear correctly in the test data
  3. Run the Protexting action step with the test data
  4. Check your Protexting account to confirm the test order arrived
  5. Review field mappings if any data appears incorrect

Testing tip: Create a test order in Amazon Seller Central if you don't have recent orders available for testing purposes.

Going Live With Your Zap

Once testing confirms everything works correctly, activating your automation takes just one click. But first, complete these final checks:

  1. Confirm both Amazon and Protexting integrations show as connected
  2. Verify field mappings include all required order details
  3. Ensure your Protexting account has the Zapier integration enabled
  4. Click "Publish" to activate your Zap

Your automation is now live! New Amazon orders will flow automatically to Protexting for verification without any manual intervention.

Remember: Monitor your Zap's history tab for the first few days to catch any unexpected issues early.
