The Offboarding Crisis Most Companies Ignore

When an employee leaves your company, you have exactly seven days to complete critical security and compliance tasks. Revoke system access. Retrieve company equipment. Conduct exit interviews. Complete final paperwork. Miss one step, and you're risking a security breach or compliance violation.

Yet most businesses handle this process with spreadsheets and memory. HR teams juggle multiple departments, hoping someone remembers to disable the former sales rep's CRM access or collect the developer's laptop. 20% of companies experience security incidents from incomplete offboarding - often discovering the problem months later during audits.

The hidden cost: Manual offboarding creates invisible risk. Former employees retain access to sensitive systems. Equipment walks out the door untracked. Compliance documentation gets delayed. The automation eliminates these risks by making the process predictable and accountable.

How Automation Solves the Offboarding Problem

The solution connects your employee database (Airtable) with Make.com's automation platform. When an employee's status changes to "offboarding," the system instantly generates seven perfectly timed tasks with clear owners and deadlines.

Unlike manual processes that rely on memory, the automation calculates due dates based on the employee's last working day. Access revocation happens on their final day. Equipment collection occurs within 48 hours. Exit interviews are scheduled during their notice period. The entire workflow completes in 7 days with zero manual intervention after the initial trigger.

Step-by-Step: The 7 Automated Offboarding Tasks

Here's exactly what happens when the automation runs (timestamp 1:15 in the video):

Step 1: System Access Revocation

Scheduled for the employee's last working day. The automation creates tickets in your IT system to disable all accounts - email, Slack, CRM, and any department-specific tools.

Step 2: Equipment Collection

Due within 48 hours of departure. The system notifies facilities to collect laptops, badges, and other hardware, then updates the asset tracking system.

Step 3: Exit Interview

Scheduled during the notice period. HR receives a calendar invite with prepared questions tailored to the employee's role.

Step 4: Knowledge Transfer

Assigned to the employee's manager. The automation generates a checklist of documents and handoffs needed before departure.

Step 5: Benefits Termination

Processed on the final day. The system queues health insurance cancellations and final paycheck calculations.

Step 6: Compliance Documentation

Due within 5 days. Legal receives automated reminders to file non-disclosure acknowledgments and other required paperwork.

Step 7: Alumni Network Invitation

Sent 7 days after departure. The former employee gets added to your alumni database with appropriate communication preferences.

In summary: The automation handles access, equipment, interviews, knowledge transfer, benefits, compliance, and alumni tracking - all with perfect timing based on the employee's departure date.

Airtable Setup for Triggering the Workflow

The automation starts when you update an employee's status in Airtable (timestamp 0:45 in the video). Here's the minimum field structure needed:

  • Employee Name: First and last name
  • Status: Active/Offboarding/Terminated
  • Last Working Day: Date field for departure
  • Department: Sales/Engineering/HR etc.
  • Company Email: For notification purposes

When Status changes to "Offboarding" and Last Working Day is populated, Make.com watches for this change through Airtable's API. The entire automation triggers from these two field updates - no manual intervention required.

Building the Make.com Automation Scenario

The Make.com scenario (shown at 1:30 in the video) follows this logic flow:

  1. Trigger: Watches for Airtable records where Status=Offboarding
  2. Calculate Dates: Determines due dates for all 7 tasks based on Last Working Day
  3. Create Tasks: Adds records to your task management system (or Airtable)
  4. Assign Owners: Routes tasks to the correct teams (IT, HR, Facilities)
  5. Send Notifications: Alerts all stakeholders via email or Slack

The beauty lies in the date calculations. For example, the system knows to:

  • Schedule access revocation ON the last working day
  • Set equipment collection for 2 days AFTER departure
  • Plan exit interviews 5 days BEFORE the end date

Time saved: What normally takes HR 2-3 hours per offboarding now happens automatically in under 60 seconds, with perfect accuracy every time.

Handling Department-Specific Requirements

The automation checks the employee's department field to trigger role-specific steps (timestamp 2:00):

  • Sales: Revokes CRM access and transfers accounts
  • Engineering: Removes code repository permissions
  • Finance: Disables accounting system logins
  • Executive: Secures board portal and sensitive documents

This departmental logic happens automatically through Make.com's router module. The system checks the Airtable department field, then follows the appropriate branch of the workflow. No manual configuration is needed - the automation handles all variations seamlessly.
