The Manual Lead Generation Problem

Most service businesses follow the same painful routine: search Google Maps for potential clients, copy-paste contact details into a spreadsheet, then blast generic emails that get deleted immediately. At 3-5 minutes per lead, that's 15-25 hours per week wasted on outreach that converts at less than 2%.

The breakthrough comes when you realize Google Maps already organizes businesses by location, reviews, and services — the perfect targeting criteria. The manual approach fails because it can't leverage this data for personalization at scale.

Cold email with basic personalization gets 8.5% response rates compared to 1.3% for generic blasts (Backlinko study). Make.com bridges this gap by automating both the data collection and personalization.

How Make.com Solves It

This automation creates a closed-loop system: scrape targeted leads → generate personalized content → deliver automatically. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how the plumbing business example processes all this while you sleep.

The workflow uses four key modules:

  1. Appify scrapes Google Maps with your filters (location, industry, reviews)
  2. Sleep Tool waits for complete data collection
  3. Data Processor extracts key details (business name, website, services)
  4. OpenAI generates tailored lead magnets in seconds

Unlike manual scraping, this system maintains perfect data consistency — no missed fields or formatting errors that break personalization later.

Step 1: Scraping Google Maps

The Appify module (shown at 0:45 in the video) handles the heavy lifting. You configure:

  • Location: Target cities or service areas (London in the example)
  • Search Strings: Industry keywords like "plumbing" or "HVAC"
  • Filters: Minimum reviews, ratings, business hours

For local service businesses, adding a 10-15 mile radius filter prevents wasting effort on unreachable leads. The JSON output contains all visible business details without violating Google's terms.

Pro Tip: Start with 50-100 leads/day to test response rates before scaling. The video shows a single lead for demonstration, but real workflows should process batches.

Step 2: Processing Lead Data

The sleep tool (1:30 in the video) ensures all data arrives before proceeding. This avoids partial records that break personalization. The data processor then extracts:

  • Business name (for document titles)
  • Website URL (for advanced research)
  • Services offered (for content tailoring)
  • Review highlights (for pain point identification)

At this stage, you can add filters to exclude businesses that recently received outreach or match your ideal customer profile more precisely.

Step 3: Creating Personalized Content

The OpenAI module (2:50 timestamp) transforms raw data into tailored lead magnets. The example uses a simple prompt, but best practices include:

  • Content Type: Checklists outperform ebooks 3:1 for local services
  • Length: 3-5 pages maximize engagement
  • Branding: Include your logo/contact in footer

For plumbing businesses, a "5-Point Commercial Plumbing Inspection Checklist" with the recipient's business name in the title converts exceptionally well.

Step 4: Delivering The Lead Magnet

The final stage (3:40 in the video) creates Google Docs with the personalized content and either:

  1. Sends directly as an email attachment
  2. Generates a draft for your review
  3. Stores in a folder for sequenced follow-up

We recommend the draft approach for quality control, then scheduling sends at 15-20 per hour to avoid spam filters. The system can also log responses in your CRM automatically.

Advanced Personalization Techniques

The video shows basic name insertion, but you can layer on:

  • Service Matching: Cross-reference the business website to mention specific services
  • Review Analysis: Identify common complaints to address in the content
  • Local References: Mention neighborhood-specific issues (e.g., "older pipes in Downtown")

One client increased responses from 9% to 23% by adding two sentences referencing each business's top Google review.
