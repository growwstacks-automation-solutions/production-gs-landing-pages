Step 1: Create Your Zap

Every automation begins with creating a "Zap" - Zapier's term for an automated workflow. Navigate to Zapier.com and click "Create Zap" in the top navigation. You'll be greeted with a two-part interface: one side for the trigger (what starts the automation) and one for the action (what happens next).

At the 0:45 mark in our tutorial video, we demonstrate how to name your Zap clearly - like "Monday.com Status Changes → Slack Alerts" - so your team can easily understand each automation's purpose later. This becomes critical as you scale to dozens of interconnected workflows.

Step 2: Set the Monday.com Trigger

Triggers are the heartbeat of your automation. For Monday.com, the most powerful triggers involve changes to specific columns - like when a task moves to "In Progress" or when a deadline date is updated. These discrete events provide the cleanest starting points for reliable automations.

In the trigger section, search for and select "Monday.com". You'll then choose from trigger events like:

  • New item in board
  • Item updated in board
  • Specific column changed
  • Item moved between groups

For first-time users, we recommend starting with "Item updated in board" as it provides the broadest visibility into changes while you learn the platform.

Step 3: Connect Your Monday.com Account

Zapier needs permission to access your Monday.com data. When prompted, click "Connect Account" and enter your Monday.com API credentials (found under your profile picture > Admin > API). This secure connection only needs to be established once - future Zaps will reuse this authenticated link.

At 1:30 in the video, we show how to test the connection by selecting a sample board from your account. This verification step ensures Zapier can actually see your Monday.com data before proceeding with the automation setup.

Pro tip: Create a dedicated "Zapier" user in your Monday.com account with appropriate board permissions rather than using your personal login. This improves security and makes permission management easier as your automations scale.

Step 4: Configure the Action

Now for the magic - deciding what happens when your trigger fires. Common Monday.com actions include:

  • Creating items in other boards (great for cross-team coordination)
  • Updating existing items when dependencies change
  • Sending notifications via Slack, email, or SMS
  • Logging changes in Google Sheets for audit trails

When configuring the action, you'll map Monday.com fields to corresponding fields in the target app. Zapier's interface makes this drag-and-drop simple, but the real power comes in using custom field mappings to transform data along the way (like converting a status change into a formatted Slack message).

Step 5: Test Your Automation

Before going live, always test your Zap using Zapier's built-in testing tools. The platform will show you exactly what data flowed through each step, making it easy to spot mapping errors or logic gaps. We recommend testing with:

  1. A simple change that should trigger the automation
  2. A change that should NOT trigger it (to verify your filters work)
  3. A complex change that tests all field mappings

At 2:50 in the video, we demonstrate how to interpret test results and troubleshoot common issues like missing fields or incorrect data formatting between apps.
