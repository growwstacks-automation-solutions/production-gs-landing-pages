The Manual Email List Problem

Most Shopify stores lose 30-50% of potential email subscribers because they rely on manual processes. Between abandoned carts, new customers, and repeat buyers, store owners juggle multiple CSV exports and imports just to keep their Moosend list current. The friction means many customers never make it onto your list at all.

Automating this flow solves three critical problems: First, it captures every customer immediately after their first interaction. Second, it eliminates human error in data transfers. Third, it frees up 5-10 hours per month that you can reinvest in creating better email content.

Email marketing drives 174% more conversions when subscribers are added within 1 hour of their first store interaction. Manual processes often take days, missing this critical window.

Zapier Setup: Creating Your Automation

Zapier acts as the bridge between Shopify and Moosend, translating customer data between the platforms automatically. The setup takes just four steps that even non-technical users can complete in under 15 minutes.

Start by logging into your Zapier account (or creating one if you don't have one). The free plan supports up to 100 automated tasks per month - enough for most small stores. Click "Create Zap" to begin building your integration between the two platforms.

Step 1: Configuring Your Shopify Trigger

The trigger determines which Shopify events will add customers to your Moosend list. Three options work best for most stores:

Option 1: New Customer

Triggers when someone creates an account on your store. Best for general list building but may include non-buyers.

Option 2: New Paid Order

Only adds customers after they complete a purchase. Creates a buyer-only list with higher engagement.

Option 3: Abandoned Cart

Captures potential customers who didn't complete checkout. Ideal for recovery campaigns.

Pro Tip: Use "New Paid Order" as your trigger if you want to segment based on purchase behavior later. The order data gives Moosend more fields to work with.

Step 2: Setting Up the Moosend Action

After defining your Shopify trigger, search for and select Moosend as your action app. Choose either "Create Subscriber" to add new contacts or "Update Subscriber" to modify existing ones.

Connect your Moosend account by entering your API key (found in Moosend's settings). Then select the specific mailing list where you want subscribers added. If you use multiple lists in Moosend, double-check you've selected the correct one.

Step 3: Perfecting Your Data Mapping

Data mapping determines which Shopify fields transfer to which Moosend fields. At minimum, map the customer email address. For better segmentation, also map:

  • First and last name
  • Phone number (if collected)
  • Order count or total spent
  • Product categories purchased

Pay special attention to consent fields if you operate in GDPR-regulated regions. Map Shopify's consent checkboxes to Moosend's subscription preferences to maintain compliance.

Step 4: Testing and Launching

Before going live, Zapier lets you send a test customer from Shopify to Moosend. Check that:

  1. The test subscriber appears in the correct Moosend list
  2. All mapped fields contain the expected data
  3. No required fields were missed in the mapping

If anything looks incorrect, return to the data mapping step and adjust. Once verified, turn your Zap on. It will now run automatically whenever your trigger condition occurs in Shopify.

Advanced Optimizations

After your basic integration works, consider these upgrades:

1. Add Filters

Exclude wholesale customers or those from certain countries by adding filters before the Moosend action.

2. Multi-Step Zaps

Add customers to different Moosend lists based on their purchase amount or products bought.

3. Tagging

Apply Moosend tags based on Shopify customer tags for better segmentation.

Example: Tag customers who spend over $100 as "VIP" in Moosend to trigger special email sequences.
