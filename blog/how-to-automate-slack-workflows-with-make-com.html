Setting Up Your Make.com Account

Make.com (formerly Integromat) provides a visual workflow builder that connects Slack to hundreds of other apps. Unlike simple Zapier automations, Make.com handles complex multi-step workflows with conditional logic and data transformation.

To get started, create a free Make.com account. The free plan includes 1,000 operations per month - enough for basic Slack automations. For more advanced workflows, the Core plan at $9/month provides 10,000 operations.

Connecting Slack to Make.com

The first technical step is establishing a secure connection between Make.com and your Slack workspace. This uses OAuth 2.0 authentication, the same protocol Slack uses for all third-party integrations.

In Make.com, create a new scenario (their term for workflow) and add the Slack app. You'll be prompted to authenticate with Slack and grant specific permissions. Only authorize the channels and data types your automation needs - this follows the principle of least privilege for security.

Choosing the Right Trigger

Triggers determine what event starts your automation. Slack offers several trigger options in Make.com, each suited for different use cases:

  • New message in channel: Watches a specific channel for any new message
  • Keyword in message: Triggers only when messages contain specific words or phrases
  • Mention of your app: Starts when someone @mentions your integration
  • Reaction added: Activates when users add emoji reactions to messages

At 1:15 in the video tutorial, we demonstrate setting up a channel watch trigger that monitors your #customer-feedback channel for new messages.

Common Slack Automation Actions

Once your trigger is set, you can add actions that execute when the trigger occurs. These are some of the most valuable Slack automations businesses implement:

  • Message forwarding: Automatically send important messages to relevant channels or individuals
  • Task creation: Convert Slack messages into tasks in Asana, Trello, or other project tools
  • CRM updates: Log customer conversations from Slack to your CRM or support system
  • Notification systems: Alert specific teams when keywords like "urgent" appear in channels

Pro tip: Start with one simple automation, test it thoroughly, then expand to more complex workflows as your team becomes comfortable with the process.

Building Advanced Workflows

Make.com's real power emerges when you create multi-step workflows that connect Slack to other business tools. For example, you could build an automation that:

  1. Watches for messages containing "client request" in your #sales channel
  2. Extracts the client name and request details
  3. Creates a task in your project management tool
  4. Posts a confirmation message back to Slack
  5. Logs the interaction in your CRM

These advanced workflows eliminate entire manual processes while ensuring consistency across systems.

Testing and Deploying Your Automation

Before activating any Slack automation, thoroughly test it in Make.com's simulation mode. The platform lets you run workflows with sample data to verify everything works as expected.

For sensitive automations, consider creating a dedicated #automation-testing channel in Slack where you can safely test workflows without affecting real communications. Once validated, gradually roll out automations to production channels while monitoring for any issues.
