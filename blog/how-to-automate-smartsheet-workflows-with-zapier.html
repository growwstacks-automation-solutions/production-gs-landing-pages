Why Automate Smartsheet with Zapier?

Manual data entry between Smartsheet and other business apps creates bottlenecks, errors, and wasted time. Teams often spend 5-10 hours weekly copying information to email systems, CRMs, or project tools - work that could be fully automated.

Zapier solves this by acting as a bridge between Smartsheet and 5,000+ other applications. When configured properly, changes in your sheets automatically trigger actions elsewhere - sending notifications, updating records, or kicking off workflows without human intervention.

Example workflow: When a project status changes to "Approved" in Smartsheet, Zapier can automatically create a task in Asana, notify the team via Slack, and email the client - all without you lifting a finger.

Getting Started with Zapier

Begin by navigating to Zapier.com and logging into your account. If you don't have one, the free plan allows basic automations (called Zaps) with limited runs per month.

From your dashboard, click "Create Zap" to start building your Smartsheet integration. The Zap editor has two main sections: Trigger (what starts the automation) and Action (what happens next).

Setting Up Your Smartsheet Trigger

The trigger defines which Smartsheet event will start your automation. Common options include:

  • New Row - Triggers when data is added
  • Updated Row - Fires when existing data changes
  • New or Updated Row - Combines both events

For most workflows, "Updated Row" provides the best balance of specificity and flexibility. At 1:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how to select this trigger from the dropdown menu.

Connecting Your Smartsheet Account

Zapier needs permission to access your Smartsheet data. Click "Connect Account" and follow the prompts to authorize the connection. This is a one-time setup for each Smartsheet account you want to automate.

Security note: Zapier uses OAuth for these connections, meaning your Smartsheet password is never shared directly with Zapier. You can revoke access at any time from your Smartsheet account settings.

Configuring Your Trigger

After connecting your account, specify which sheet and columns to monitor:

  1. Select Sheet: Choose from your connected Smartsheet account
  2. Column to Monitor: Pick the specific data point that matters (like Status or Due Date)
  3. Include Created: Set to "True" if you want new rows to also trigger the automation

Pro Tip: For complex workflows, use Zapier's filters to only trigger when specific conditions are met (like when Status equals "Approved"). This prevents unnecessary automation runs.

Setting Up Your Action

Now define what happens after your trigger fires. Common Smartsheet actions include:

  • Sending email notifications (Gmail, Outlook)
  • Creating tasks (Asana, Trello, ClickUp)
  • Updating records (Salesforce, HubSpot)
  • Posting messages (Slack, Teams)

Connect your action app just like you did with Smartsheet, then customize the details. You can insert Smartsheet data directly into your action using Zapier's merge fields.

Testing and Activating Your Zap

Before going live, test your automation with sample data. Zapier will show you exactly what data flows between Smartsheet and your action app. Verify everything looks correct, then click "Publish" to activate.

Your integration is now live! Zapier will automatically monitor your specified Smartsheet changes and trigger your defined actions in real-time. At 3:40 in the video, you'll see the testing process in action.
