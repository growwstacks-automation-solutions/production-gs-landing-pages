What Is Make.com?

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a visual automation platform that connects your apps to work together. Unlike traditional social media schedulers, Make.com doesn't just post at set times - it intelligently repurposes content based on triggers from your other platforms.

The platform uses "scenarios" - visual workflows where a trigger (like a new YouTube video) automatically performs actions (like creating LinkedIn posts). These scenarios can include conditional logic, data transformation, and even AI-powered content adaptation.

Key difference: While tools like Buffer schedule posts, Make.com creates dynamic content workflows that respond to your business activity in real-time.

Setting Up Your First Automation

Creating your first social media automation in Make.com follows a simple three-step process: trigger, action, and activation. Let's walk through automating LinkedIn posts from YouTube uploads.

Step 1: Choose Your Trigger

In Make.com's scenario builder, select YouTube as your trigger app. Choose "New Video in Channel" as the trigger event. This will watch your YouTube channel for new uploads.

Step 2: Configure Connection

Authorize Make.com to access your YouTube account. You'll need to log in and grant permissions, similar to connecting any third-party app. Make.com only requests the minimum permissions needed.

Step 3: Set Execution Schedule

Determine how often Make.com should check for new videos. The free plan allows checks every 15 minutes, while paid plans can check as often as every minute. For most businesses, checking every 2-4 hours is sufficient.

Pro tip: Set your scenario to only process videos uploaded after the automation's creation date to avoid reposting your entire back catalog.

Connecting Social Media Platforms

With your YouTube trigger set, the next step is connecting your destination platforms. Make.com supports all major social networks through their official integrations.

For our LinkedIn example, you'll add an action module after the YouTube trigger. Search for and select LinkedIn, then choose "Create a Post" as your action. The platform will guide you through authorizing your LinkedIn account.

In the post creation module, you can map fields from your YouTube video to the LinkedIn post:

  • Use the video title as your post headline
  • Pull the description for the post body
  • Include the video URL as the link
  • Add relevant hashtags from your video tags

This creates a basic cross-posting workflow. At 2:15 in the tutorial video, you can see how to enhance this with custom formatting and platform-specific optimizations.

Adding AI for Smarter Posts

The real power comes when you add AI to transform content for each platform's audience. Make.com integrates with ChatGPT and other AI tools through its AI modules.

Between your YouTube trigger and LinkedIn action, add an "OpenAI ChatGPT" module. Configure it to:

  1. Take the YouTube video description as input
  2. Rewrite it in a professional yet conversational tone
  3. Optimize the length for LinkedIn's algorithm
  4. Include a call-to-action to watch the video

You can create parallel paths for other platforms. For example, the same YouTube video could generate:

  • A short, punchy Instagram caption with emojis
  • A threaded Twitter/X post with key takeaways
  • A Pinterest pin description with relevant keywords

AI prompt example: "Transform this YouTube video description into a 150-word LinkedIn post that highlights three key insights. Maintain a professional but approachable tone and end with a question to spark discussion."

Testing and Activating Your Scenario

Before going live, always test your automation with the "Run Once" feature. This executes your scenario manually so you can verify each step works as intended.

Check the execution logs to see:

  • Did the YouTube trigger correctly identify your video?
  • Did the AI module generate appropriate post content?
  • Did LinkedIn receive and publish the post correctly?

Once testing is complete, activate your scenario. Make.com will now automatically run it according to your schedule. The platform provides detailed logs of every execution, including any errors that occur.

For the first week, monitor your automations closely. Check that posts appear as expected and adjust any AI prompts or formatting as needed. After this initial period, the system should run smoothly with minimal oversight.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

While Make.com is relatively easy to use, beginners often make these avoidable errors:

1. Overlooking Platform Limits

Each social platform has API rate limits. Posting too frequently through automation can temporarily suspend your access. Space out posts and stay within platform guidelines.

2. Skipping Error Handling

Always add error handling routes in your scenarios. If LinkedIn rejects a post, your automation should notify you rather than failing silently.

3. Using Generic AI Prompts

Vague prompts produce generic content. Include specific details about your brand voice, audience, and content goals in every AI instruction.

4. Forgetting to Update Connections

Platform API tokens expire periodically. Set calendar reminders to check and refresh your connections every 3-6 months.

Remember: Automation handles the repetitive work, but human creativity still drives your best content. Use the time savings to focus on strategic storytelling.
