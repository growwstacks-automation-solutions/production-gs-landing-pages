Connecting Zapier to Squarespace

The integration requires just two components: a Squarespace API key and a Zapier account. Many business owners hesitate at the term "API key," thinking it sounds technical—but generating one is as simple as clicking "Create Key" in your Squarespace settings (demonstrated at 3:42 in the video).

Here's the step-by-step process to establish the connection:

Step 1: Generate Your Squarespace API Key

Navigate to Settings → Advanced → Developer API Keys in your Squarespace dashboard. Create a new key specifically for Zapier with "Forms" permissions—this limits access to only form submission data for security.

Step 2: Connect to Zapier

In Zapier, search for Squarespace when setting up a new Zap. Paste your API key when prompted—it only takes seconds to authenticate. The connection remains active until you revoke the key in Squarespace.

Pro Tip: Name your API key "Zapier" in Squarespace so you can easily identify and manage it later among other integrations.

Setting Up Your First Trigger

Triggers are the "when this happens" part of your automation. For Squarespace, the most common trigger is "New Form Submission"—which activates your Zap whenever someone completes any form on your site.

At 5:18 in the tutorial, you'll see how to select your specific form from a dropdown menu in Zapier. This ensures the automation only runs for relevant submissions (like newsletter signups rather than contact inquiries). The setup includes:

  • Choosing between Squarespace Forms or Commerce triggers
  • Selecting your connected Squarespace site
  • Picking the exact form to monitor
  • Testing the trigger with a sample submission

This trigger configuration takes most users under 3 minutes to complete, yet handles hundreds of future form submissions automatically.

Configuring Automation Actions

Actions determine what happens after your trigger activates. The tutorial at 7:30 shows a simple email notification action, but Zapier offers 5,000+ possible actions across apps like:

Each action can include data from the form submission—like inserting the respondent's email address into both your spreadsheet and CRM simultaneously. The 9:15 mark demonstrates how to map form fields to action fields for perfect data transfer every time.

Testing and Publishing Your Zap

Before going live, Zapier lets you test each step with real data (shown at 11:20). Submit a test form on your Squarespace site to verify:

  1. The trigger correctly detects the submission
  2. All form fields appear in Zapier
  3. Your actions execute properly
  4. Data appears correctly in destination apps

Once testing succeeds, click "Publish" to activate your Zap. It will now run automatically for all matching submissions—with no ongoing effort required. The tutorial's final demo at 12:45 shows the complete workflow from form fill to automated spreadsheet update in under 10 seconds.

Advanced Integration Tips

For businesses ready to level up their automations, these pro techniques unlock additional value:

Multi-Step Zaps

Chain multiple actions together—like adding a contact to your CRM, then scheduling a follow-up task in Asana, then sending the sales team a Slack alert—all from one form submission.

Conditional Logic

Use filters to only run actions for specific submissions. For example, only notify the team about leads from your "Enterprise Plan" contact form, not general inquiries.

Data Formatting

Zapier's Formatter tool can modify submitted data—like converting a phone number to international format before saving to your database.

These advanced features require no coding—just strategic planning about your ideal workflow. The tutorial's bonus section at 14:30 demonstrates conditional logic in action.
