Setting Up the Trigger

The trigger is what tells Zapier when to send a notification. For Stripe payments, you'll select one of these events:

  • Charge succeeded - For successful payments
  • Charge failed - When a payment fails
  • Charge refunded - For refunds issued
  • Charge created - Any new payment attempt

At 2:08 in the video, we demonstrate connecting your Stripe account to Zapier using OAuth. This secure connection only grants read access to payment events - your actual payment processing remains completely within Stripe.

Configuring Notifications

Once the trigger is set, you'll choose where to send notifications. Zapier supports:

  • Email - Direct to your inbox or team distribution list
  • Slack - Instant messages to specific channels
  • Google Sheets - Permanent record of all payment events
  • SMS - Critical alerts via text message

The video at 3:42 shows how to set up Slack notifications that include the customer's name and payment amount - creating actionable alerts rather than generic notifications.

Customizing Alert Content

Generic "payment received" notifications aren't helpful. At 4:15 in the tutorial, we customize the message to include:

  • Customer name and email
  • Payment amount and currency
  • Payment status (success/failure/refund)

  • Link to the Stripe dashboard for details

This level of detail means you can take immediate action - following up on failed payments, thanking customers for purchases, or verifying refund requests.
