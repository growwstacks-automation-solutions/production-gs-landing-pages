Periscope + Zapier Solution

Periscope bridges this gap by making WhatsApp data available to Zapier's automation platform. Instead of manual copying, conversations automatically trigger workflows that push data to your business tools.

This integration supports multiple trigger events including new chats, incoming messages, message updates, reactions, and ticket activity (created, updated, or deleted). Each event contains rich data about the conversation that you can map to actions in thousands of connected apps.

Step 1: Generate Your API Key

The foundation of the integration is your unique Periscope API key. This secure token authenticates the connection between Periscope and Zapier.

To generate your key:

  1. Log into your Periscope account
  2. Navigate to Settings → Integrations → Zapier
  3. Click "Generate API Key"

Important: The API key is only shown once during generation. Copy it immediately and store it securely - you'll need it to authenticate in Zapier.

Step 2: Zapier Setup

Currently, the Periscope integration on Zapier is in private beta. You'll need to:

  1. Use the invite link in the video description to unlock access
  2. Accept the invite in your Zapier account
  3. Periscope will then appear as an available app

Once enabled, create a new Zap in your Zapier dashboard. Click "Add Trigger" and search for Periscope to begin configuring your automation.

Step 3: Trigger Configuration

With Periscope selected as your trigger app, choose the specific event that will start your automation. Options include:

  • New chats
  • Incoming messages
  • Message updates
  • Reactions
  • Ticket activity (created, updated, or deleted)

For this example, we'll select "New ticket created" - triggering whenever a ticket is raised from a WhatsApp conversation in Periscope.

Pro Tip: Always click "Test your trigger" to see sample data before proceeding. This shows exactly what fields will be available for mapping in your action steps.

Step 4: Action Steps

After configuring your trigger, add any action you need. Zapier's 5,000+ app connections mean virtually unlimited possibilities:

  • Send ticket data to Slack for team notifications
  • Create a row in Notion with conversation details
  • Log messages in Airtable for reporting
  • Fire off an email with ticket details
  • Update a CRM like Salesforce or HubSpot

Map the fields from your Periscope trigger to the corresponding fields in your action app. Test each step thoroughly before publishing your Zap.
