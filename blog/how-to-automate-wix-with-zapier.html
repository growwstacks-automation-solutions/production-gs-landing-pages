Why Automate Wix with Zapier?

Wix websites handle crucial business functions like lead capture, content publishing, and ecommerce transactions. Yet most site owners manually process every form submission, order notification, and content update. This creates bottlenecks where simple tasks consume disproportionate time.

Zapier acts as a bridge between Wix and hundreds of other business tools. By connecting them, you create workflows that automatically handle repetitive tasks. For example, when someone submits a Wix contact form, Zapier can instantly add them to your CRM and send a personalized thank you email - without you lifting a finger.

Time savings: Businesses using Wix-Zapier automations report saving 5-15 hours weekly on administrative tasks. That's 20-60 hours monthly regained for strategic work.

Zapier-Wix Setup Process

The integration begins in your Zapier dashboard. Navigate to "Make a Zap" and select Wix as your trigger app. You'll need to authenticate your Wix account by following the connection prompts.

At the 1:15 mark in our tutorial video, you'll see the exact steps for establishing this connection. The process involves clicking the Wix icon in Zapier's app directory, then authorizing the integration through Wix's permission screen.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated automation email (like automations@yourdomain.com) for these connections rather than your personal Wix login. This maintains access if team members change.

Choosing the Right Trigger

Triggers are events that start your automation. Wix offers several trigger options through Zapier, including new form submissions, blog comments, store orders, and contact additions.

For first-time users, we recommend starting with the "New Form Submission" trigger. This is one of the most reliable and universally applicable options. When configuring, you'll select which specific Wix form should initiate the workflow.

Action Configuration

After setting your Wix trigger, you'll choose what happens next. Zapier offers thousands of possible actions across apps like Gmail, Slack, Google Sheets, and CRMs.

A simple but powerful first automation sends form responses to a Google Sheet. When configuring this action, you'll map each Wix form field to a corresponding spreadsheet column. Zapier handles the data transfer automatically whenever the form is submitted.

Testing Your Workflow

Before activating your Zap, test it thoroughly. Zapier provides a testing console where you can simulate trigger events and verify the action executes correctly.

For form submissions, test with real data (like test@yourdomain.com) rather than generic placeholders. This ensures all field mappings work as intended. Watch for common issues like mismatched field types or authorization errors.

Common Wix-Zapier Use Cases

Beyond basic form processing, businesses use this integration for:

  • Ecommerce automation: Sync new orders to accounting software or inventory systems
  • Content distribution: Auto-post new blog content to social media
  • CRM updates: Add new contacts to HubSpot or Salesforce
  • Internal alerts: Notify teams via Slack about high-priority form submissions

Each of these follows the same fundamental pattern: Wix event → Zapier automation → business result.
