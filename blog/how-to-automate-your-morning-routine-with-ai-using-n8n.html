The Morning Routine Problem

Most professionals start their day drowning in information overload. You wake up to hundreds of emails, dozens of news alerts, and endless social media updates - all competing for your attention before your first coffee. The average knowledge worker spends 47 minutes each morning just catching up on industry news and updates.

The challenge? Critical information gets buried in noise. You waste time separating signal from noise across multiple platforms. Even worse, you often miss important developments because they get lost in the flood.

The hidden cost: This daily catch-up routine costs businesses an estimated $8,200 per employee annually in lost productivity. The mental fatigue from constant context-switching before your workday even begins impacts decision quality all morning.

How n8n Solves It

n8n (pronounced "n-eight-n") is an open-source workflow automation platform that connects apps and services without coding. Unlike simpler tools like Zapier, n8n gives you granular control over every step of an automation while remaining accessible to non-technical users.

For our morning routine automation, n8n acts as the central nervous system that:

  • Triggers at a set time each morning
  • Pulls data from multiple RSS feeds
  • Processes content through AI summarization
  • Formats the output for easy reading
  • Delivers it to your inbox automatically

The result? You wake up to one curated digest containing only what matters to you - no noise, no distractions, just signal.

Building the Workflow Step-by-Step

Our automation has five core components that work together seamlessly:

Step 1: The Schedule Trigger

This initiates the workflow daily at your chosen time (we use 7 AM):

  • Add a "Schedule Trigger" node
  • Set to run every 1 day
  • Configure your preferred time (e.g., 07:00)

Step 2: RSS Feed Configuration

Define your news sources in JSON format:

 [   "https://techcrunch.com/feed/",   "https://www.engadget.com/rss.xml",   "https://yournicheblog.com/feed" ]

Use the "Edit Fields" node to store this array of RSS URLs.

Step 3: Processing the Feeds

The workflow then:

  • Splits the URLs into individual feeds
  • Reads each RSS feed's content
  • Aggregates all articles into one dataset

At 2:45 in the video, you'll see how the "Split Out" and "RSS Read" nodes handle this process.

Integrating AI for Summarization

The magic happens when we connect OpenAI to process the aggregated content:

  1. Add an "AI Agent" node
  2. Configure with your OpenAI API key
  3. Set the system message to: 
     "Only summarize news from past 24 hours.  Write in plain English with no jargon.  Here is the news list: {{aggregatedContent}}"
  4. Select GPT-4 or your preferred model

Customization Tip: Adjust the prompt to specify your preferred summary style - bullet points, executive summary format, or even humorous takes on the news.

The AI processes hundreds of articles down to one concise digest containing only the most relevant updates.

Delivering the Final Digest

Before sending, we convert the AI's markdown output to email-friendly HTML using the "Markdown to HTML" node. Then configure the Gmail node:

  • Connect your Gmail account (requires OAuth setup)
  • Set recipient (your email or team distribution list)
  • Define subject line (e.g., "Your Morning Digest")
  • Insert the HTML content

The entire process - from trigger to delivery - runs in about 3-5 minutes each morning, saving you hours weekly.
