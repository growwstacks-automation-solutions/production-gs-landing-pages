GHL Call Recording Setup

Before creating your demo, you need high-quality call recordings. GHL makes this easy with built-in call recording features, but there are critical settings most users miss:

Step 1: Enable Call Recording

Navigate to your phone number settings in GHL and click "Edit Configuration." Toggle on "Call Recording" but crucially, turn OFF the "Call Recording Message" option. This prevents an announcement from interrupting your demo audio.

Step 2: Optimize Agent Settings

In your voice agent settings, set the "Agent Speed" to Normal (not Fast). The upgraded GHL AI handles normal speed perfectly, while fast settings can make the agent interrupt callers. Each voice may require slightly different speed adjustments.

Pro tip: Record 5-10 calls and select the best one. Don't settle for the first attempt - slight variations in caller responses can dramatically affect demo quality.

Creating Branded Templates

Your demo template establishes credibility and brand recognition. While you can use Photoshop, Canva provides an accessible option for most users:

Step 1: Design the Layout

Create a square or vertical rectangle canvas (1080x1080 or 1080x1920 pixels). Include your logo, brand colors, and a relevant background image. Leave 20% blank space at the bottom for the animated sound waves.

Step 2: Add UI Elements

Include phone mockup elements like call duration, signal bars, and contact name. These subtle details make the demo feel authentic. Use your brand's primary color for the call button and other interactive elements.

Export the template as a high-quality JPEG or PNG. Having 3-5 variations allows you to showcase different industries or use cases.

Audio Editing in Descript

Descript's AI-powered editing tools transform raw call recordings into polished demos. Here's the exact workflow:

Step 1: Import Assets

Drag your template image into the video track and your call recording into the audio track. Descript will automatically transcribe the audio (takes about 10 minutes for a 1-minute call).

Step 2: Clean Up the Audio

Use these essential Descript features:

  • Remove Filler Words: Automatically eliminates "ums" and "ahs"
  • Shorten Word Gaps: Reduces pauses longer than 3 seconds
  • Studio Sound: Enhances audio quality if recording conditions weren't ideal

Trim any unnecessary sections to keep the demo under 60 seconds. Focus on the most impressive parts of the conversation.

Adding Animated Sound Waves

The animated sound waves are what make your demo stand out. Here's how to implement them:

Step 1: Add Waveform Element

In Descript, navigate to Elements → Waveforms → Lines. Drag the waveform onto your template, positioning it in the blank space at the bottom.

Step 2: Customize the Appearance

Adjust these settings for optimal results:

  • Line thickness: 9 points
  • Color: Use your brand's accent color
  • Height: About 20% of the total template height

Preview the animation to ensure the waves sync perfectly with the audio. The peaks should correspond to speech moments.

Conversion tip: Demos with properly synced sound waves convert 37% better than static images. The animation helps viewers follow the conversation flow intuitively.

Exporting and Sharing

Your final demo should be versatile enough for multiple platforms:

Step 1: Export Options

In Descript, choose "Local Export" for maximum quality. Recommended settings:

  • Format: MP4
  • Resolution: 1080p
  • Frame rate: 30fps

Step 2: Distribution Channels

Deploy your demo across these high-impact locations:

  • Website: Embed above the fold on your voice AI service page
  • Email: Include as a linked thumbnail in proposals
  • Social Media: Share as a native video (not link) for better reach
  • QR Codes: Print on trade show materials linking to the demo

Create 3-5 variations showcasing different industries or use cases to appeal to diverse clients.
