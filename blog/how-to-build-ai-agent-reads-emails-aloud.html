The Morning Email Problem

Most professionals begin their day the same way - bleary-eyed, reaching for their phone to check overnight emails. This ritual creates immediate stress, strains your eyes first thing in the morning, and often means important messages get lost in the visual clutter.

The cognitive load of processing written information is highest when you're not fully awake. Studies show we retain 28% less information from reading when fatigued compared to listening. This explains why so many people find themselves re-reading the same emails multiple times before fully comprehending them.

Key insight: Converting email content to audio leverages our natural listening comprehension abilities, which remain strong even when we're not fully alert. This matches how humans evolved to process information - through speech long before written language.

How AI Transforms Email Review

Modern AI models now solve three critical problems that made email-to-audio systems impractical in the past. First, they can accurately identify urgent messages from routine ones. Second, they summarize lengthy threads without losing key details. Third, text-to-speech technology now produces natural-sounding voices indistinguishable from humans.

The workflow combines Claude Sonnet for intelligent summarization with Cartisia's Sonic model for lifelike speech. Together they create a briefing that sounds like a personal assistant read your emails and is telling you what you need to know - not just reading them verbatim.

Workflow Overview

This six-step automation runs completely autonomously every morning, requiring no manual intervention after setup. It connects your email provider, AI models, and preferred delivery method into a seamless system.

At 6:00 AM (or your preferred time), the workflow: retrieves your overnight emails, has Claude analyze and summarize them, converts the summary to audio using Cartisia's Sonic, stores the recording in Google Drive, and sends you a Slack message with the audio file link - all before you've finished your first cup of coffee.

Step 1: Setting the Trigger

The daily trigger is what tells your AI agent when to begin working. This acts as your digital alarm clock - but instead of waking you, it wakes your automation to start processing your emails.

In relay.app, you'll create a scheduled trigger set to run daily at your preferred morning time. The example uses 6:00 AM Pacific, but you can set this to whatever time matches when you typically begin reviewing emails. This ensures the briefing is ready when you are.

Step 2: Retrieving Overnight Emails

The workflow needs to fetch only relevant emails - those that arrived while you were sleeping. By setting a 10-hour lookback window from the trigger time, it captures all messages since your last evening email check without including older ones you've already addressed.

The email retrieval step filters for messages in your inbox (not archived or categorized) sent within the specified timeframe. Additional filters can exclude newsletters or other low-priority senders if desired. This typically results in 20-50 emails for most professionals.

Step 3: AI Summarization

Claude Sonnet 4.5 analyzes all retrieved emails and produces a concise written briefing. The prompt instructs it to prioritize emails needing responses, highlight urgent requests, and group related messages together - exactly how a human assistant would prepare you for the day.

The AI doesn't just concatenate email text. It understands relationships between messages, recognizes action items, and presents information in logical order. For example, it might say: "You have three emails about the Acme project - the client approved the proposal but has questions about implementation timelines. These require your response today."

Pro tip: You can customize the summarization prompt to match your priorities. Add instructions like "emphasize emails from my manager" or "flag any messages mentioning budget changes" to tailor the briefing to your role.

Step 4: Text-to-Speech Conversion

Cartisia's Sonic model transforms Claude's written summary into natural-sounding speech. Unlike older text-to-speech systems that sounded robotic, Sonic produces fluid speech with appropriate pauses, emphasis, and intonation.

You can select from multiple voice options to find one that's pleasant to listen to first thing in the morning. The example uses "Tessa" - a clear, calm female voice - but you might prefer a deeper tone or different accent based on personal preference.

Step 5: Delivering the Briefing

The final steps store the audio file in Google Drive (creating a searchable archive of your daily briefings) and send you a Slack message with the file link. The workflow could alternatively deliver via email, SMS, or other messaging platforms.

The Slack notification includes a consistent message format ("Your daily briefing for [date]") and direct link to the audio file. This creates a predictable routine - you know exactly where to find your briefing each morning without searching through multiple apps.
