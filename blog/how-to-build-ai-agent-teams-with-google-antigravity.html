The AI Agent Team Framework

Most business owners struggle with the same problem: there's never enough time or specialized talent to execute all their ideas. You might have brilliant marketing concepts, product ideas, or growth strategies - but turning them into reality requires skills you don't have and hours you can't spare.

Google's Antigravity solves this by letting you act as the director of specialized AI teams. Imagine having a marketing manager who researches your ideal customers, a developer who builds landing pages, and a content creator who develops lead magnets - all working in parallel while you focus on high-level strategy.

Key insight: Antigravity isn't just another chatbot - it's an orchestration layer that manages multiple AI agents with different specialties, memory, and access to your tools. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how these agents can even access your logged-in browser sessions to complete real work on your behalf.

Getting Started with Antigravity

The beauty of Antigravity is its simplicity. Unlike traditional automation tools that require complex setups, you can be up and running in minutes:

Step 1: Access the Platform

Simply visit antigravity.google (currently in beta) and download the application. No special permissions or invites required during this free beta period.

Step 2: Navigate the Interface

At first glance, the interface might look technical - but the magic happens when you click "Open Agent Manager" (shown at 3:42 in the video). This reveals the inbox-style control center where you'll direct your AI teams.

Step 3: Create Your First Agent

The playground area lets you prototype ideas quickly. As demonstrated at 4:30, you can simply type a natural language instruction like "Research my primary customers at bookton.ai and develop lead magnets they'd like" - and an agent immediately begins working.

Pro tip: Start small with single-task agents before scaling to complex multi-agent workflows. The platform's true power emerges when you have specialized agents working in parallel on different aspects of the same project.

Running Parallel Workflows

The true power of Antigravity emerges when you run multiple specialized agents simultaneously. At 8:20, the video shows how you can have:

  • One agent researching competitors
  • Another building website components

This parallel processing capability is what enables one person to achieve what previously required an entire team. The platform manages context switching and resource allocation automatically.

Cost and Performance Considerations

Currently free during beta, Antigravity represents an incredible value because it's built on Gemini 3 - which at 10:30 we see is 8-16x more expensive than competing models when accessed via API.

Key points about the economics:

  • Free access during beta (no announced pricing yet)
  • Enterprise-grade AI at no cost
  • Likely to transition to paid tiers post-beta
  • Current performance would be prohibitively expensive via API

This creates a unique window where businesses can access cutting-edge AI capabilities without the typical cost barriers - making now the ideal time to experiment and develop workflows.

AI Model Comparisons

While Antigravity uses Gemini 3, it's important to understand how different models compare for various tasks (as analyzed at 11:15):

Task Type Best Model Why
General Coding Claude Sonnet 4.5 Faster and more cost-effective
Current Events Gemini 3 Google's real-time data advantage
Creative Writing GPT-5.1 More natural language flow
Deep Research Gemini 3 Superior reasoning and synthesis

The key takeaway? While Antigravity is revolutionary for certain workflows, it's not always the best tool for every AI task. Smart businesses will develop a "model stack" strategy.
