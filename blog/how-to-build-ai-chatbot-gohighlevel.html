Understanding GoHighLevel's Chatbot Options

GoHighLevel offers three distinct chatbot approaches, each suited for different business needs. Many users make the mistake of choosing the wrong type initially, leading to frustrating limitations down the road.

1. Guided Form Setup: Best for simple FAQ bots with predetermined answers. Works well for basic information delivery but lacks flexibility.

2. Prompt-Based Bots: Our recommended approach for most businesses. These use AI to generate dynamic responses based on your training data, handling unpredictable questions while maintaining brand voice.

3. Flow-Based Builder: Ideal for standardized processes like onboarding sequences. Creates branching conversation paths but requires more upfront setup.

Pro Tip: For fitness coaches, consultants, and service businesses, the prompt-based approach delivers the best balance of automation and personalization. It adapts to customer questions rather than forcing them down rigid paths.

Step 1: Initial Chatbot Setup

Navigate to the "Conversation AI" section in your GoHighLevel dashboard and click "Create New Agent." This begins the process of building your automated messaging assistant.

Name your bot something recognizable (like "Prime Coaching Assistant") and select "Prompt-Based Bot" as your type. For most businesses, choosing "General Q&A" as the primary purpose works well - this handles the broadest range of inquiries while still allowing for appointment booking functionality.

Set the status to "Autopilot" if you want the bot to reply automatically without human review. For testing phases, "Suggestive" mode lets you approve responses before sending.

Step 2: Channel Configuration

The true power of this system comes from connecting all your customer communication channels to one AI assistant. In the Channels section, enable:

  • Instagram Direct Messages
  • Facebook Messenger
  • Website Chat Widget
  • SMS Text Messages
  • WhatsApp (if applicable)

For the website widget, customize the greeting message and hours of availability. Even if your bot runs 24/7, setting "typical response time" expectations manages customer expectations.

Critical setting: Adjust the "Wait before reply" to 30 seconds rather than the default 2. Instant responses feel robotic, while a slight delay mimics human typing speed for more natural conversations.

Step 3: Training Your Bot to Sound Human

The difference between a generic AI and your brand's voice comes down to training. GoHighLevel's "Training 2.0" feature lets you upload documents, FAQs, and even crawl your website to teach the bot your specific knowledge base.

Create a new knowledge base and add:

  1. Your services PDF or pricing page
  2. FAQs from your website or internal documents
  3. A "Brand Voice Guide" document with examples of how you want the bot to respond
  4. Sample conversations showing ideal customer interactions

For fitness coaches (as shown in the video), include transformation stories, program details, and common client questions. The more niche-specific information you provide, the more authoritative your bot sounds.

Step 4: Optimizing Response Settings

Under "Bot Settings," several key adjustments dramatically improve conversation quality:

  • Maximum Conversation Length: Set to 10 messages before requiring human takeover
  • Voice Notes: Disable unless your industry commonly uses audio messages
  • Pricing: Monitor your token usage (about 2 cents per message)

Most importantly, craft your "Fallback Message" - what the bot says when it doesn't know an answer. A good template: "I don't have enough information to answer that perfectly. Let me connect you with [Name], our [Role] who can help personally."

Step 5: Enabling Appointment Booking

For service businesses, the real magic happens when your bot can schedule appointments directly. Connect your GoHighLevel calendar and configure:

  1. Available meeting types (consultation, follow-up, etc.)
  2. Duration options
  3. Buffer times between appointments
  4. Automatic confirmation messages

When a customer asks about booking, the bot checks real-time availability and offers specific time slots. Once selected, it sends calendar invites to both parties and can even process payments if you've integrated Stripe or PayPal.

Conversion tip: Program your bot to ask qualifying questions before offering appointment times. For fitness coaches: "To suggest the best session type, may I ask what results you're hoping to achieve?" This screens tire-kickers while warming up serious leads.

Step 6: Monitoring Performance

GoHighLevel's analytics dashboard shows exactly how your bot performs across all channels. Key metrics to track:

  • Messages Handled: Total conversations managed without human intervention
  • Appointments Booked: Direct revenue attributed to bot interactions
  • Escalation Rate: Percentage of conversations transferred to humans

Review chat transcripts weekly to identify gaps in your training data. Notice which questions trigger fallback responses, then add that information to your knowledge base. Over time, your bot will handle an increasing percentage of inquiries autonomously.
