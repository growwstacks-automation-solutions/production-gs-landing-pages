Creating Your First AI Agent

The term "agent" in Botpress refers to your chatbot instance. Creating one is as simple as clicking "New Agent" and answering a few questions about your intended use case. These answers help customize the starter templates and suggested workflows.

At the 2:30 timestamp in our video, we demonstrate selecting "Customer Support" as the primary goal. This automatically configures helpful defaults like:

  • Pre-built escalation workflows
  • FAQ suggestion templates
  • Customer satisfaction tracking

Pro tip: Name your agent something descriptive but simple. Avoid special characters as this name will appear in URLs and integration settings.

Training Your Chatbot With Knowledge Sources

The real magic happens when you train your Botpress agent on your specific business knowledge. At the 4:15 mark, we upload a PDF of product documentation - but you can also connect websites, help centers, or manually enter Q&A pairs.

Botpress uses this information to build a comprehensive knowledge base. The system supports:

  • Automatic content extraction from documents
  • Smart categorization of information
  • Continuous learning from real conversations

For best results, start with your most frequently asked questions. The AI will learn to recognize similar phrasings and provide consistent answers.

Customizing Your Bot's Personality

At 6:50 in the tutorial, we dive into personality settings. This is where you define whether your chatbot should be formal or casual, humorous or strictly professional. The tone should match your brand voice.

Key customization options include:

  • Response length (brief vs. detailed)
  • Formality level
  • Use of emojis
  • Default greeting message
  • Error handling style

Remember: Personality isn't just about being friendly - it's about being appropriately helpful while maintaining professionalism when needed.

Building Conversation Flows in the Workflow Editor

The visual workflow editor (shown at 8:20) is where you design your chatbot's decision logic. Each "node" represents a step in the conversation, connected by arrows that show possible paths.

Common node types include:

  • Message nodes: Send text, images, or buttons
  • Question nodes: Collect user input
  • Action nodes: Integrate with external systems
  • Condition nodes: Create branching logic

To create a basic FAQ flow: Add a question node to detect the user's intent, connect it to a message node with the answer, then add follow-up options. The editor includes templates for common patterns.

Testing and Deploying Your Chatbot

Before going live, thoroughly test your chatbot using the built-in simulator (demonstrated at 9:45). Try various phrasings of the same question to ensure consistent responses.

When ready, deployment options include:

  • Website embed: Copy a script tag to any webpage
  • WhatsApp/Messenger: Connect via official channels
  • API access: For custom integrations

Launch strategy: Start with a limited rollout to gather real conversation data, then refine before full deployment. Botpress makes it easy to update your chatbot without downtime.
