The Client Audit Challenge

Service businesses often operate in the dark about where their teams spend the most time. Manual processes get repeated daily without anyone questioning if they could be automated. A typical agency wastes 15-20% of billable hours on tasks that could be streamlined with simple automation.

The breakthrough comes when you systematically track operations. By capturing what tasks are performed, how often, and how long they take, patterns emerge. At 2:15 in the video, we demonstrate how one client's manual report generation was consuming 6 hours monthly - a prime automation candidate.

Key insight: You can't automate what you don't measure. This workflow creates the data foundation to make strategic automation decisions.

Tally Form Setup (Free Tier)

Tally's free form builder serves as the data collection point for your client audit system. We configure it with four strategic fields that reveal automation opportunities:

Step 1: Create the Form

After signing up at tally.so, create a new form titled "Client Process Tracker". This will be the interface your team or clients use to log operations.

Step 2: Add Key Fields

  • Process Title: Short description of the task
  • Department: Which team handles this
  • Current Process: How it's performed now
  • Frequency: How often it occurs

Pro tip: Add a "Time Estimate" field later to quantify hours spent. This becomes crucial for calculating automation ROI.

Structuring Data in Airtable

Raw form submissions need organization to reveal insights. Airtable transforms the Tally data into a structured database with powerful filtering and AI analysis capabilities.

Step 1: Create the Base

Name your base "Client Audit System" and match the field headers to your Tally form. Ensure each column uses the appropriate data type (text, number, etc.).

Step 2: Set Up Views

Create filtered views by department (Sales, Ops, etc.) and priority level. This lets different teams focus on their relevant automation opportunities.

Time saver: Airtable's built-in AI summarizer (shown at 7:42 in the video) can automatically analyze entries and suggest priority scores.

Automated Priority Scoring

The real magic happens when Airtable's AI evaluates each process for automation potential based on:

  • Time consumption: Hours spent monthly
  • Frequency: How often it occurs
  • Complexity: Implementation difficulty

At 9:15 in the tutorial, we demonstrate how the system scored a sales reporting process as "Priority 7" - indicating it could save 6 hours monthly with medium implementation effort.

Make.com Notification System

Make.com completes the workflow by monitoring Airtable for high-priority entries and automatically notifying your team.

Step 1: Connect the Services

Create a Make.com scenario that watches your Airtable base. Use the API key from Airtable's developer settings to establish the connection.

Step 2: Configure Triggers

Set the scenario to activate when new records are added or existing ones are modified with a priority score above your threshold (we recommend 5+).

Step 3: Automate Alerts

Connect to your email system (Gmail, Outlook etc.) to send formatted notifications containing the process details and AI-generated recommendations.
