FastBots solves this by combining AI conversation with direct calendar integration. Step 1: Create Your Chatbot in 90 Seconds

The fastest way to build a chatbot is by training it on your existing website content. FastBots' crawler automatically indexes all text from your site pages to create the chatbot's knowledge base.

In the demo at 4:50, they:

  1. Clicked "Create Chatbot"
  2. Named the bot (e.g., "MicroGrant Support")
  3. Entered the website URL to crawl
  4. Let the system index 2,700+ characters of content

Pro Tip: Add common questions as suggested prompts in the Appearance settings. This increases engagement by letting visitors click instead of type.

Step 2: Connect to Your Calendar

The magic happens when connecting your chatbot to scheduling systems. FastBots uses Zapier AI Actions as the bridge between conversation and calendar bookings.

At 6:30 in the video, they:

  1. Went to Integrations → Zapier AI Actions
  2. Clicked "Add Action" and selected Google Calendar
  3. Configured the meeting details (duration, title, etc.)
  4. Set AI to dynamically collect attendee email

This creates a "tool" the chatbot can access when booking requests occur. The same method works for Outlook, Calendly, or any Zapier-connected calendar system.

Step 3: Configure Booking Logic

The AI instructions determine how booking conversations flow. At 8:15, they edited the prompt in the "Tune AI" section to:

You are a customer service chatbot for [Business Name].  When users request to book a meeting: 1. Ask for their name and email 2. Ask what date and time works best 3. Use the Schedule Google Meet action to book a 30-minute "Sales Funnel Meeting" 4. Confirm details and provide the meeting link

This natural language "training" works like instructing a human employee. The AI follows this script whenever booking intent is detected.

Step 4: Test Your Booking Chatbot

Testing ensures the chatbot collects the right information and successfully creates calendar events. At 9:40, they demonstrated a live test:

  1. Clicked "Can I book a meeting?" suggested question
  2. Provided name, email, date (October 19), and time (11 AM)
  3. The chatbot confirmed details and triggered the Zapier action
  4. Verified the meeting appeared in Google Calendar

The entire process from question to confirmed booking took under 30 seconds. The video at 10:15 shows the calendar event appearing in real-time.
