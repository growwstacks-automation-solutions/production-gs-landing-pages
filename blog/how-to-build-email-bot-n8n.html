Step-by-Step: Building Your Email Bot

Step 1: Set Up the Schedule Trigger

Begin by creating a new workflow in n8n and adding a Schedule Trigger node. This determines when your automated emails will send. Configure it to run daily by setting "Days Between Triggers" to 1.

Step 2: Add the Gmail Action

Connect a Gmail node to your trigger. At 1:45 in the tutorial, you'll see the simple OAuth process to connect your Google account. n8n handles all the authentication complexity behind the scenes.

Step 3: Compose Your Email

Fill in the recipient, subject line, and message body just like you would in Gmail. The workflow shown uses static content, but you can later enhance it with variables for personalization.

Pro Tip: Always test with your own email address first before adding client or customer addresses to avoid accidental sends during development.

Testing and Activating Your Workflow

Before going live, use n8n's manual execution feature to test your email. Check your inbox to verify the message arrived as expected. At 2:30 in the video, you'll see how to execute individual nodes during testing.

Once confirmed working, toggle the workflow to active. n8n will now automatically send your scheduled emails without any manual intervention. The platform provides execution logs so you can monitor success and troubleshoot any issues.
