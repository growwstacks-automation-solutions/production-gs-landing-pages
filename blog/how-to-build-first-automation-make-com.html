How Automation Solves This Daily Frustration

Make.com (formerly Integromat) connects your Google Sheets data with email notifications through a visual workflow builder. No coding required - just drag, drop, and configure modules that represent different apps and functions.

This particular automation checks your spreadsheet daily for active trials with end dates approaching within 3 days. When it finds one, it sends you a personalized email reminder with the service name and expiration date - all automatically.

Setting Up Your Google Sheet

Start by creating a simple spreadsheet with these columns: Service Name, Trial End Date, Status (Active/Inactive), and any notes. This becomes your central hub for tracking all subscriptions and trials.

At 1:15 in the video, you'll see how to name your sheet and structure the data for optimal automation. The key is using consistent status labels ("Active" for current trials you want to track) and proper date formatting so Make.com can interpret the information correctly.

Configuring Make.com

The Make.com workflow uses just three modules: Google Sheets (trigger), OpenAI (decision), and Email (action). The Sheets module checks your spreadsheet daily for rows where Status equals "Active" and the end date is within 3 days of today.

At 3:50 in the tutorial, notice how the filter ensures only relevant trials trigger notifications. This prevents false alarms for expired or canceled services while catching every upcoming deadline that matters.

Adding the AI Check

While optional, the OpenAI module adds intelligence to your automation. For about $0.02 per check, it analyzes the trial data and confirms whether a reminder is needed. This extra layer prevents errors if your spreadsheet contains unusual date formats or ambiguous statuses.

The video at 5:30 shows how to configure GPT-3.5-turbo (the most affordable model) with a simple prompt that outputs "yes" or "no" based on the dates. The temperature setting at 0.2 ensures consistent, predictable responses rather than creative variations.

Sending Email Reminders

When both the spreadsheet filter and AI check confirm a trial is ending soon, the email module sends your reminder. You can customize the message template with variables like {ServiceName} and {EndDate} that pull directly from your spreadsheet.

At 7:10 in the tutorial, you'll see how to format these dynamic fields so each email feels personal. A well-crafted reminder might say: "Your Canva Pro trial ends on December 13 - cancel before then to avoid the $12.99 monthly charge."

Testing Your Workflow

Before relying on the automation, test it with fake trial dates that trigger immediately. This verifies each component works as expected without waiting days for real deadlines. Make.com's scenario testing lets you run the workflow manually with sample data.

The tutorial at 8:20 demonstrates how to force a test run and check the resulting email. Once confirmed, schedule the automation to run daily - it will handle the rest automatically while you focus on more important work.
