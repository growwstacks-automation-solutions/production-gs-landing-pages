Zapier Basics: What You Need to Know

Zapier connects over 7,000 apps through automated workflows called "Zaps." Each Zap has two main components: a trigger (the event that starts the automation) and an action (what happens after the trigger).

The free plan allows you to create single-step Zaps with limited runs per month - perfect for learning and small projects. As shown at 2:15 in the video, signing up is simple and doesn't require a credit card. This makes Zapier an ideal platform to start your automation freelancing journey with zero upfront costs.

Setting Up Your First Zap

Creating your first money-making automation follows a simple 4-step process:

Step 1: Choose Your Trigger App

In our example, we select Trello as the trigger app. The specific trigger is "when a new card is created" in a particular board and list. This establishes when our automation will run.

Step 2: Connect Your Accounts

Zapier makes it easy to connect your apps through OAuth. As shown at 4:30 in the video, you simply authorize Zapier to access your Trello account with a few clicks.

Step 3: Set Up the Action

For our action, we choose Slack and select "send channel message." This determines what happens after our trigger event occurs.

Step 4: Test and Publish

Zapier lets you test each step before going live. Once confirmed working, you publish the Zap to start automating.

Pro Tip: Always test with real data before publishing. Create an actual Trello card during testing (as shown at 8:45) to ensure your Slack message includes all the right information.

Trello to Slack Workflow Walkthrough

The Trello-to-Slack automation demonstrated in the video solves a specific business problem: eliminating the manual work of notifying team members about new tasks. Here's how it works in detail:

When you create a new card in a specific Trello list (like "Tasks"), Zapier automatically sends a formatted message to your chosen Slack channel. The message can include the card name, description, due date - any information that would normally require manual copying.

At 6:20 in the video, you can see how to customize the Slack message format. This is where you add value by including the most relevant information for the team. A well-structured automated message often works better than manual notifications because it's consistent every time.

Testing and Publishing Your Automation

Thorough testing is crucial before delivering automations to clients. The video shows three key testing phases:

1. Trigger Testing: Verify Zapier can detect new Trello cards (shown at 7:10). This confirms your connection works and the right events will start the automation.

2. Action Testing: Check that the Slack message contains all needed information (8:00 mark). You can customize which Trello fields appear in Slack.

3. Live Testing: After publishing, create a real Trello card to see the full workflow in action (10:45). This final confirmation ensures everything works as expected.

Zapier's history feature (demonstrated at 11:20) lets you monitor all automation runs - essential for troubleshooting and proving value to clients.
