What This Plumbing AI Agent Actually Does

Every plumbing business owner knows the frustration of missed calls - potential customers calling when you're on a job, emergencies going unanswered after hours, and the administrative nightmare of manual scheduling. Traditional answering services cost $200-$500/month and still require callbacks.

This AI voice agent solves all three problems simultaneously:

24/7 call answering: Answers calls in a natural human voice, qualifies leads, checks real-time technician availability from your Google Calendar, and books appointments automatically - with zero human intervention needed from you or your team.

The system specifically handles three core scenarios:

  • Emergency calls: Detects active leaks/flooding situations and follows escalation protocols
  • Standard bookings: Finds next available slot based on real technician locations
  • Qualification: Collects key details (issue type, water valve status) before dispatch

Step 1: Configuring Your Vapi Assistant

Vapi provides the voice interface that callers interact with - think of it as your AI receptionist's ears and mouth. The setup takes under 5 minutes:

  1. Create free account at vapi.ai
  2. Click "New Assistant" and name it (e.g. "Plumbing Agent")
  3. Select GPT-4-turbo as the model (best for service businesses)
  4. Enable "Assistant speaks first" (recommended plumbing calls)

Pro tip: The system prompt (AI's brain) contains all your protocols - emergency handling, qualification questions to ask, and how to transfer to human when needed. We provide this pre-written in the templates.

How the Availability Checking Works

The smart scheduling logic (shown at 11:05) prevents overbooking by:

Dynamic time buffers: 15 minutes before/after each appointment accounts for LA traffic. Adjust based on your service area density.

The system:

  1. Generates all possible time slots for requested date
  2. Pulls existing appointments from Google Calendar
  3. Filters out slots overlapping with busy intervals (+buffers)
  4. Returns only truly available times in human-readable format

This means when a customer asks "Can someone come Thursday at 2pm?" - the AI knows with 100% accuracy whether that slot is actually available.
