Voice Agent Anatomy: How the Tech Stack Works

Every voice AI agent has four core components working together in under one second per response:

  1. Speech-to-text - Transcribes the caller's words (Vapi uses whisper-grade transcription)
  2. LLM reasoning - Processes the text and determines response (GPT-4.1 recommended)
  3. Text-to-speech - Converts the response to natural voice (11Labs cloned voices)
  4. Tool integration - Connects to CRM, calendar, payment systems (via n8n automation)

The magic happens in the prompt engineering - where you define the agent's personality, call flow logic, and integration points. A well-structured prompt includes:

  • Role & objective (e.g. "You are Grace, a digital assistant for Amplify Family Law")
  • Personality guidelines ("Warm and reassuring without being exaggerated")
  • Call stages (Greeting → Qualification → Booking → Closing)
  • Example interactions (Show don't tell how to handle common scenarios)

Inbound vs Outbound: Key Differences

Voice AI agents handle two fundamentally different call types - each requiring unique approaches:

Inbound calls (user initiates) excel at appointment booking, qualification, and customer support. The challenge? You don't know the caller initially and must gracefully collect information.

Outbound calls (AI initiates) shine for payment reminders, follow-ups, and lead reactivation. The challenge? Avoiding spam flags and delivering value in the first 5 seconds. Our law firm implementation uses both:

  • Inbound: Qualifies new clients, books consultations (saves 15hrs/week in staff time)
  • Outbound: Collects overdue invoices (generates $15k+/month in recovered payments)

Critical technical difference: Outbound requires number cleaning (to avoid spam flags) and stricter timing controls (to prevent calling outside business hours).

Prompt Engineering Secrets for Reliable Agents

After deploying 30+ voice agents, we've identified the prompt structures that deliver 90%+ consistency:

Critical rule: Keep system prompts under 3000 tokens. Longer prompts increase costs and decrease reliability.

Prompt Structure That Works

  1. Role & Objective: "You are Grace, a digital assistant for Amplify Family Law. Your primary objective is to qualify incoming calls..."
  2. Personality: "Warm and reassuring without being exaggerated. Emphasize warmth when the user expresses emotional distress."
  3. Context: Business hours, disclaimer ("Not a lawyer"), current time (using dynamic variables)
  4. Instructions: Communication style, tool usage rules, qualification criteria
  5. Stages: Greeting → Qualification → Booking → Closing
  6. Examples: 3-5 sample conversations showing ideal flows

Pro tip: Use the "regenerate 10 times" test - if 9/10 responses follow your structure correctly, the prompt is solid.
