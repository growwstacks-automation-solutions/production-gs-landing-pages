Industry Targeting Guide (What Actually Sells)

The biggest mistake new voice AI agencies make? Targeting the wrong industries. After analyzing hundreds of clients and $1.3M in revenue, we identified clear patterns in what converts.

Voice AI solutions fall into two categories: AI inbound (receptionist replacement) and AI lead follow-up (converting form submissions). The most profitable industries have:

Top 5 Performing Industries: 1) Trade services (plumbers, electricians) 2) Law firms 3) Therapists 4) Real estate agents 5) Locksmiths. These share high call volumes, service-based models, and owners who feel the pain of missed calls directly.

We prioritize industries where:

  • Service information is clearly listed on their website (speeds up agent setup)
  • Customers primarily call to book (not dominated by booking apps)
  • Owners are the primary call handlers (they feel the pain directly)

Avoid industries like hair salons or photography studios where visual portfolios dominate booking decisions. These businesses typically use Instagram or dedicated booking apps, making voice AI less critical.

The Conversion Playbook (Objection Handling)

Closing voice AI deals requires understanding three distinct buyer personas and their objections.

Prosumers (solopreneurs): "I can't afford $1,500" → Offer $200/month payment plans. Calculate their missed revenue: 10 missed calls/day x $1,500 average job = $15,000 daily risk.

SMBs (small teams): Position as receptionist support, not replacement. Typical objections:

  • "Our receptionist handles this" → "What happens when they're sick?"
  • "We use a booking app" → "How many calls still come in after hours?"

Enterprise: 6-8 month sales cycles focused on compliance and scale. Key requirements:

  • On-prem deployment options
  • 99.9% uptime SLAs
  • Detailed call analytics

Contract Templates That Protect You

The #1 reason agencies lose money? Poor contract structure. After reviewing 150+ client agreements, we developed a bulletproof template.

Never accept "50% on completion" terms. Clients determine completion, which can delay payments indefinitely. Instead, use milestone payments with 100% upfront per phase.

Essential contract clauses:

  1. Detailed scope of work: Exactly what's included (and excluded)
  2. Client responsibilities: API access, content approval timelines
  3. Change management: How scope changes are handled (and billed)
  4. Payment terms: Net 7 or Net 15 to maintain cash flow

Sample pricing structures:

  • Prosumer: $1,500 setup + $100-200/month
  • SMB: $3,000-6,000 setup with CRM integration
  • Enterprise: $15,000+ with custom development

Monitoring & Alert Systems

The fastest way to lose clients? Letting them discover issues before you do. Our monitoring system catches 99% of problems proactively.

Critical alerts: 1) Failed calls 2) Calendar sync errors 3) CRM integration failures. Receive alerts via SMS, email, and Slack before clients notice.

Monitoring setup:

  • Ping test every 5 minutes
  • Call simulation 2x/day
  • Calendar sync verification hourly

Response protocol:

  1. Acknowledge alert within 15 minutes
  2. Diagnose root cause
  3. Implement fix or fallback
  4. Notify client if outage exceeded 30 minutes
