Getting Started With Zapier

Begin by navigating to zapier.com in your browser and logging into your account. If you're new to Zapier, the free plan provides more than enough functionality for most Adalo integrations. At the 2:15 mark in the video tutorial, we demonstrate how to select the right workspace if you're part of a team.

From your dashboard, click "Create Zap" to start building your automation. Zapier calls these automations "Zaps" - each consists of a trigger (the event that starts the automation) and an action (what happens after the trigger). Give your Zap a clear, descriptive name that will help you identify it later among other automations.

Setting Up Your Adalo Trigger

The trigger is the foundation of your automation - it tells Zapier when to start moving data from Adalo. Search for and select "Adalo" as your trigger app, then choose "New Record" as your trigger event. This means your Zap will activate whenever a new item is added to a specific collection in your Adalo app.

Connect your Adalo account by following Zapier's secure authorization prompts. You'll need to select which Adalo app and collection to monitor. Pro tip: Create at least one sample record in your chosen collection beforehand - this helps Zapier understand your data structure during setup.

Configuring Your Action App

After setting up the Adalo trigger, choose which app should receive the data. Popular choices include Google Sheets, Slack, Mailchimp, or CRMs like HubSpot. Select your desired action event - for example, "Create Spreadsheet Row" if sending to Google Sheets.

Connect your action app account using Zapier's authorization flow. Pay special attention to workspace or folder selection - this ensures your data lands in the correct location. The video demonstrates this critical step at the 4:30 timestamp.

Field Mapping Best Practices

Field mapping is where you define how data flows from Adalo to your destination app. Zapier will display fields from both systems side by side. Match each Adalo field (like "Customer Name" or "Order Date") to its corresponding field in your action app.

Use the sample data from your test record to verify mappings are correct. For important fields like email addresses or IDs, double-check that the data formats match between systems. The video shows advanced mapping techniques at 6:45 that can handle complex data transformations.

Testing Your Workflow

Before going live, thoroughly test your Zap. Run a test trigger to pull sample data from Adalo, then review how Zapier processes it through to your action app. Check the destination app to confirm the test record appears exactly as expected.

Common testing scenarios include verifying that:

  • All required fields are mapped
  • Data formats are preserved (dates, currencies, etc.)
  • No sensitive data is being shared unintentionally

Common Issues and Troubleshooting

Even well-designed automations can encounter issues. The most frequent problems include authorization errors (when app connections expire), field mapping mismatches, and rate limits on free plans. At 8:20 in the video, we demonstrate how to diagnose and fix these issues.

For persistent problems, try these steps:

  1. Reconnect the affected app in your Zap
  2. Verify field mappings still match current app structures
  3. Check Zapier's status page for known service outages
  4. Upgrade your plan if hitting usage limits
