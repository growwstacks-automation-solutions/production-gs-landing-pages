Why Automate Calendly with Make.com

Every new meeting booking creates a cascade of manual tasks - sending confirmations, updating calendars, notifying team members, and logging details in your CRM. These repetitive tasks eat up valuable time that could be spent on higher-value work.

By connecting Calendly to Make.com, you create an automated pipeline that handles these routine follow-ups instantly. When someone books or cancels an appointment, the webhook trigger kicks off your predefined workflow without any human intervention.

Time savings: Businesses using this integration report saving 5-7 hours per week on scheduling coordination. That's 20-30 hours monthly recovered just by automating meeting follow-ups.

Step 1: Prepare Your Calendly Account

Before connecting to Make.com, ensure your Calendly account is properly configured. Log into Calendly and verify you're in the correct organization workspace if you manage multiple accounts. This ensures the integration accesses the right event types and settings.

Review the specific event types you want to automate. Make note of any custom questions you've added to your booking forms, as these responses will be valuable data points for your automated workflows. At 1:15 in the video tutorial, we demonstrate how to locate your organization URI - keep this handy for the connection setup.

Step 2: Create Your Make.com Scenario

Log into your Make.com account (or sign up if you're new to the platform). Navigate to the Scenarios section and click "Create a new scenario." This will serve as your automation blueprint where you'll define what happens when bookings occur.

Name your scenario something descriptive like "Calendly Booking Automation" so it's easily identifiable later. The scenario editor provides a blank canvas where you'll build your workflow by adding and connecting modules.

Step 3: Set Up the Webhook Trigger

In your Make.com scenario, click the "+" button to add your first module. Search for "Calendly" in the app directory and select the verified Calendly module. This ensures you're connecting through the official API for maximum reliability and security.

Choose "Watch Events" as your trigger - this sets up the webhook that will listen for booking activity. The configuration window will guide you through creating the webhook connection. At this stage, you'll select which event types to monitor (new bookings, cancellations, or both).

Pro tip: Start by monitoring just new bookings to test your workflow, then add cancellation tracking once the initial automation is working smoothly.

Step 4: Authenticate the Connection

When prompted, click "Create a Connection" to link your Calendly account. This opens a secure authentication window where you'll log into Calendly and grant Make.com permission to access your booking data. The process uses OAuth 2.0, so your credentials remain secure.

After authenticating, you'll need to enter your organization URI (from Step 1) and user URI. These identifiers tell Make.com exactly which Calendly resources to monitor. Double-check these values match your setup to ensure the webhook captures the correct events.

Step 5: Configure Webhook Settings

With authentication complete, finalize your webhook configuration. Select the specific event types you want to trigger your workflow (invitee created, canceled, or both). You can also filter by particular Calendly event types if you only want to automate certain meeting categories.

Review all settings carefully before saving. Once activated, your webhook will immediately begin listening for booking activity. Test the connection by scheduling a dummy appointment in Calendly and verifying Make.com receives the webhook data (visible in the scenario execution history).

Common Use Cases for This Integration

Now that your Calendly-Make.com connection is live, consider these powerful automation opportunities:

  • Instant CRM updates: Push booking details to Salesforce, HubSpot, or other CRMs automatically
  • Personalized confirmations: Send customized confirmation emails with meeting specifics
  • Team notifications: Alert internal teams via Slack when key meetings get scheduled
  • Payment processing: Trigger invoices or payment collection for paid appointments
  • Calendar sync: Add events to secondary calendars beyond your primary scheduling tool

The real power comes from chaining multiple actions together in your Make.com scenario. For example: When a sales demo gets booked → update CRM → notify sales rep → send prep materials → add to team calendar → schedule follow-up task.
