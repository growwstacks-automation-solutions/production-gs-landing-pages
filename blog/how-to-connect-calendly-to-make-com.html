Why Connect Calendly to Make.com?

Manual scheduling creates invisible productivity drains that cost businesses hundreds of hours annually. Each appointment booked through Calendly typically requires 3-5 manual follow-up actions: sending confirmations, updating CRMs, processing payments, and syncing with team calendars. These repetitive tasks add up quickly.

By connecting Calendly to Make.com, you create an automated workflow that handles these tasks instantly. When someone books an appointment, Make.com can trigger a sequence of actions across multiple apps without any manual intervention. Businesses using this integration report saving 3-5 hours per week on average.

Key benefit: The Calendly-Make.com integration turns scheduling from a time-consuming chore into a fully automated system that works while you sleep. No more missed follow-ups or calendar conflicts.

Getting Started with Make.com

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a visual automation platform that connects apps without coding. Unlike simpler tools, Make allows complex multi-step workflows with conditional logic, data transformations, and error handling.

To begin, navigate to make.com and sign up for a free account. The platform offers a generous free tier that's perfect for testing basic automations. Once logged in, you'll see the dashboard where all your automation scenarios (workflows) are managed.

Creating Your First Automation Scenario

Scenarios are Make.com's term for automation workflows. Each scenario begins with a trigger (like a new Calendly appointment) followed by one or more actions (like sending an email or updating a CRM).

Click "Create a new scenario" from your Make.com dashboard. This opens a visual builder where you'll construct your automation. The interface may seem complex at first glance, but we'll walk through each step methodically.

Step 1: Naming Your Scenario

Give your scenario a descriptive name like "Calendly Appointment Automation" so you can easily identify it later. Good naming becomes crucial as you build multiple automations.

Step 2: Adding the First Module

Click the "+" button to add your first module. This will be your trigger - the event that starts the automation. We'll select Calendly in the next section.

Adding Calendly as a Trigger

In the module search bar, type "Calendly" and select it from the results. Make.com supports multiple Calendly trigger types, but we'll use "New Scheduled Event" which fires whenever someone books an appointment.

This trigger will capture all the appointment details: the invitee's name, email, scheduled time, event type, and any custom questions they answered during booking. These data points become available to use in subsequent automation steps.

Pro tip: At 1:32 in the video tutorial, we demonstrate how to filter for specific Calendly event types if you only want to trigger automations for certain appointment types.

Authenticating the Connection

Before Make.com can access your Calendly data, you need to establish a secure connection between the two services. Click the "Add" button next to the connection field in the Calendly module.

You'll be redirected to Calendly's OAuth screen to authorize the connection. Make sure you're logged into the correct Calendly account, then click "Allow" to grant permissions. This process typically takes less than 30 seconds.

Once authenticated, return to Make.com and select your Calendly organization from the dropdown. The connection is now established and will remain active unless manually revoked.

Testing Your Workflow

With the Calendly trigger configured, it's time to test the connection. Click the "Run once" button in Make.com, then book a test appointment in Calendly. Within seconds, you should see the appointment data appear in Make.com's execution panel.

This test confirms that Make.com is successfully receiving events from Calendly. While we haven't added any actions yet (like sending emails or updating CRMs), this foundational step is critical for all subsequent automation.

Advanced Automation Possibilities

With the basic connection established, the real power comes from adding actions after the Calendly trigger. Here are three powerful automations you can build:

1. CRM Updates

Automatically create or update records in Salesforce, HubSpot, or other CRMs when appointments are booked, including all attendee details and meeting notes.

2. Custom Confirmation Emails

Send personalized confirmation emails with attachments (contracts, intake forms) tailored to the specific appointment type booked.

3. Payment Processing

For paid appointments, automatically charge the client's card via Stripe or PayPal and send receipts.

Time savings: These automations typically save 8-12 minutes per appointment. For businesses booking 20+ meetings weekly, that's 3-4 hours saved every week.
