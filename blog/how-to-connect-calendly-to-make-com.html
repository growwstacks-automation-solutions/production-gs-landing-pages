Why Connect Calendly to Make.com?

By connecting Calendly to Make.com, you create a system where every appointment automatically triggers the exact right sequence of actions. Step 1: Create Your Make.com Webhook

The connection between Calendly and Make.com begins with a webhook - a special URL that allows applications to communicate with each other. In Make.com, you'll create this webhook endpoint that will receive data from Calendly whenever an appointment-related event occurs.

Log into your Make.com account and click "Create a new scenario." Search for the "Webhooks" module and select "Custom Webhook." Make.com will generate a unique URL that serves as your connection point with Calendly. Copy this URL - you'll need it in the next step when configuring Calendly.

Pro Tip: Name your webhook clearly (like "Calendly Appointments") so you can easily identify it later among other integrations.

Step 2: Configure Calendly Webhook Settings

With your Make.com webhook URL copied, switch to your Calendly account. Navigate to Integrations → Webhooks in your Calendly settings (the exact path may vary slightly depending on your Calendly plan). Here you'll add a new webhook by pasting the URL from Make.com.

Calendly allows you to select which events should trigger the webhook. The most common options are "Invitee Created" (new appointment booked) and "Invitee Canceled" (appointment cancelled). Select the events relevant to your workflow, then save your webhook configuration.

Step 3: Test the Connection

Before relying on this integration for business operations, you must verify it's working correctly. The simplest test is to book a new appointment in Calendly (using a test email if needed), then check if the data appears in your Make.com scenario.

In Make.com, you should see the webhook module light up with data from your test booking. This confirms the connection is working. At this point, you can start building out the rest of your automation by adding modules after the webhook to process the appointment data.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

While connecting Calendly to Make.com is straightforward, there are a few pitfalls that can derail your automation:

  • Not testing with real data: Always test with actual appointment scenarios that match your real use cases.
  • Forgetting to handle cancellations: Build separate workflows for canceled appointments to keep your systems accurate.
  • Overlooking data mapping: Verify all fields from Calendly are correctly mapped to their destinations in your workflow.

The most successful implementations start simple, then expand functionality once the basic connection is proven reliable.
