Step-by-Step Connection Guide

Connecting Calendly to Zapier takes less than 10 minutes but will save you countless hours. Follow these exact steps to set up your integration:

Step 1: Log in to Your Zapier Account

Navigate to zapier.com and sign in to your account. If you don't have one yet, create a free account - you can upgrade later if you need more advanced features.

Step 2: Create a New Zap

Click the "Create Zap" button in the top navigation. This starts a new automation workflow that will connect Calendly to your other apps.

Step 3: Set Calendly as Your Trigger App

Search for and select "Calendly" as your trigger app. Choose the specific event type that will start your automation, like "New Scheduled Event" or "New Invitee".

Step 4: Connect Your Calendly Account

Authenticate with your Calendly credentials to give Zapier permission to access your scheduling data. Make sure you're logged into the correct Calendly account if you manage multiple.

Step 5: Test Your Trigger

Zapier will prompt you to book a test appointment through Calendly to verify the connection works. This ensures your real appointments will trigger the automation correctly.

Step 6: Add Your Action Apps

Now choose what should happen when someone books through Calendly. Popular actions include creating Google Calendar events, adding CRM records, or sending confirmation emails.

Step 7: Customize Your Data Mapping

Map Calendly fields (like name, email, and meeting time) to the corresponding fields in your action apps. This ensures information flows correctly between systems.

Step 8: Turn On Your Zap

Once everything is configured, activate your Zap. It will now run automatically whenever the trigger event occurs in Calendly.

Pro Tip: Start with one simple Zap to get comfortable with the integration, then gradually add more complex automations as you see opportunities to streamline additional scheduling tasks.

3 Common Setup Mistakes to Avoid

After implementing hundreds of these integrations, we've identified the most frequent pitfalls:

1. Not Testing with Real Bookings

Many users only test with their own Calendly bookings, which doesn't reveal how the Zap handles external invitees. Always have someone outside your organization book a test meeting.

2. Overlooking Time Zone Conversions

Calendly handles time zones beautifully, but some apps don't automatically adjust. Always confirm time data appears correctly in destination systems.

3. Forgetting to Update Changed Event Types

If you modify your Calendly event types after creating Zaps, you may need to update your automation workflows to recognize the new versions.

Save hours of troubleshooting: These three mistakes account for 80% of support tickets about Calendly-Zapier integrations. Address them proactively during setup.
