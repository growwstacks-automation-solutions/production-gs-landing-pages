Example impact: A marketing team reduced content creation time by 70% by automating first drafts of blog posts using DeepSeek through Make.com, while maintaining their brand voice and quality standards.

Step 1: Setting Up Your Make.com Scenario

Begin by logging into your Make.com account and navigating to the Scenarios section. Click "Create a new scenario" to start building your automation workflow. This blank canvas is where you'll connect DeepSeek with your other business applications.

Name your scenario something descriptive like "AI Content Generator" or "Customer Feedback Analyzer." This helps you identify it later among your other automations. The naming convention should reflect both the trigger (what starts the automation) and the AI action.

Key Configuration Options:

  • Schedule: Set to run immediately, at intervals, or based on specific triggers
  • Error handling: Configure retries and notifications for failed operations
  • Execution limits: Set usage caps to control costs

Step 2: Adding DeepSeek to Your Workflow

In your Make.com scenario editor, click the plus icon to add your first module. Search for "DeepSeek" in the app selector. You'll see the DeepSeek logo appear among the available applications.

Select "Create a Completion" as your operation - this is the primary way to generate text with DeepSeek's AI. You'll then need to connect your DeepSeek account by entering your API key (available from your DeepSeek account settings).

Pro tip: Store your API key as a variable in Make.com rather than hardcoding it into each scenario. This makes it easier to update if needed and more secure.

Step 3: Configuring DeepSeek Parameters

With DeepSeek added to your workflow, you'll see several configuration options that control how the AI operates. The most important is the "Prompt" field - this is where you tell the AI what to generate.

For best results, craft detailed prompts that include:

  • The exact task you want performed
  • Any context or background information
  • Formatting requirements
  • Tone or style guidelines

You can also adjust technical parameters like "Max Tokens" (response length) and "Temperature" (creativity vs. precision). Start with conservative values and adjust based on output quality.

Step 4: Connecting to Other Apps

The real power comes from connecting DeepSeek's output to other applications. Click the plus icon again and select another app - for example, Google Sheets to store generated content or Gmail to send AI-composed emails.

In the target app's module, map the output from your DeepSeek operation to the appropriate fields. For a Google Sheets integration, you might map the AI-generated text to a "Content" column, along with metadata like generation date and source prompt.

Advanced technique: Use Make.com's router to send different types of AI outputs to different destinations based on content analysis or predefined rules.
