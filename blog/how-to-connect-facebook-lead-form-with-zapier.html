Zapier + Facebook Setup

Zapier acts as the invisible bridge between Facebook and your business tools. Unlike native integrations that often lack customization, Zapier gives you precise control over which fields transfer and where they go.

To begin, log into your Zapier account at zapier.com and click "Create Zap". You'll need:

  • Admin access to the Facebook Page running lead ads
  • A live lead form in Facebook Ads Manager
  • Access to your destination app (CRM, spreadsheet, etc.)

At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how to verify your Facebook account has proper lead access permissions — a common stumbling block for new users.

Trigger Configuration

The trigger is what tells Zapier when to act. For Facebook leads, you'll select "Facebook Lead Ads" as the trigger app and "New Lead" as the trigger event. This creates a real-time connection that monitors your form for submissions.

During setup, you'll:

  1. Connect your Facebook account (ensure it's the one managing the ads)
  2. Select the specific Page containing your lead form
  3. Choose the exact form version currently collecting leads

Pro tip: Always test the trigger with a sample lead to confirm Zapier can access your form data. If no sample appears, submit a test lead through your live Facebook ad first.

Action Setup

The action determines where leads go after submission. Popular choices include:

  • Google Sheets: Creates a new row for each lead
  • CRM systems: Adds leads as contacts (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.)
  • Email platforms: Sends immediate follow-ups (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign)

Select your action app and choose the specific event (like "Create Contact"). You'll then connect that app to Zapier using OAuth — similar to how you connected Facebook earlier.

For CRMs, ensure your account has API access enabled. Some platforms require admin permissions for Zapier integration.

Field Mapping Strategies

Field mapping is where automation becomes powerful. Instead of getting generic CSV files, you can route specific form answers to precise locations in your CRM or spreadsheet.

For example:

  • Map Facebook's "full_name" to your CRM's "First Name" and "Last Name" fields
  • Route "phone_number" to both your CRM and a Twilio SMS automation
  • Pass custom questions like "budget" to specific spreadsheet columns

At 4:50 in the video, you'll see how to handle conditional fields — like only mapping phone numbers when provided, or formatting dates consistently across systems.

Testing & Publishing

Before going live, Zapier lets you test each step with sample data:

  1. Test Trigger: Confirms Zapier can access your Facebook leads
  2. Test Action: Sends a sample lead to your destination app

Open your CRM or spreadsheet to verify the test data arrived correctly. Check for:

  • Proper field formatting (dates, phone numbers)
  • Complete information transfer (no missing fields)
  • Correct destination locations (right tabs, sheets, or lists)

Once confirmed, click "Publish" to activate your Zap. New leads will now flow automatically.

Advanced Tips for Higher Conversions

Basic integration gets leads into your system — these pro techniques help you convert more:

  • Add Filters: Only pass leads who answer "Yes" to qualifying questions
  • Multi-Path Routing: Send mortgage leads to loan officers, auto inquiries to dealers
  • Data Enrichment: Append company info using Clearbit or Hunter.io
  • Instant Alerts: Trigger Slack/SMS notifications for high-value leads

Real-world result: One insurance client increased conversions 217% by adding a 2-minute SMS follow-up to their Zapier workflow.
