Why Make.com for Gmail Automation

Make.com (formerly Integromat) stands out for email automation because of its visual workflow builder and deep integration capabilities. Unlike simple email filters, Make.com can connect your inbox to hundreds of other business tools, creating sophisticated workflows that go beyond basic organization.

The platform uses a trigger-action model perfect for email automation. When a new email arrives (trigger), Make.com can perform multiple actions like copying it to a folder, adding it to a spreadsheet, notifying your team in Slack, and more - all in a single automated sequence.

Step 1: Setting Up Your Make.com Account

Begin by visiting Make.com and clicking "Sign Up" in the top right corner. The free plan provides everything needed for basic Gmail automations, though businesses may want to consider the $16/month Core plan for additional features.

After creating your account, take a moment to explore the dashboard. The left sidebar contains all the tools you'll need, with the "Scenarios" section being where we'll build our email automation. Make.com's interface is designed to guide beginners while offering advanced options when needed.

Step 2: Creating Your First Scenario

Click the "Create a new scenario" button to begin building your automation. Scenarios are Make.com's term for complete workflows that connect apps and automate processes. Name your scenario something descriptive like "Gmail Auto-Organization" to easily identify it later.

The scenario editor shows a blank canvas where you'll build your workflow. The search bar at the top lets you find apps and actions - this is where we'll add Gmail in the next step. Before proceeding, consider what you want to automate (e.g., copying specific emails to folders) as this determines which actions to select.

Step 3: Connecting Gmail to Make.com

In the scenario editor, search for "Gmail" and select it from the app list. You'll see various actions like "Watch emails", "Send an email", and "Copy email to folder". For our example, choose "Copy email to folder" to automate email organization.

When prompted, click "Add" to create a new connection between Make.com and your Gmail account. This opens Google's secure OAuth authentication flow. After approving the connection, you can select which Gmail account to use if you have multiple accounts signed in.

Security note: Make.com only requests the permissions needed for your selected actions. For folder copying, it needs access to view and modify your labels/folders but cannot read email content unless specifically configured to do so.

Step 4: Configuring Email Automation Rules

With Gmail connected, configure the "Copy email to folder" action by selecting your source folder (usually "Inbox") and destination folder. You can create new folders directly from this interface if needed.

Set up filters to determine which emails get copied. For example, you might copy all emails from specific senders, with certain subjects, or containing particular keywords. Make.com's filter builder lets you combine multiple conditions for precise control.

Finally, click "Run once" to test your automation with existing emails. Verify the results in your Gmail account, then activate the scenario to run automatically going forward. The workflow will now process new emails as they arrive.

Advanced Automation Tips

Once comfortable with basic folder copying, explore more powerful automations like:

  • Multi-step workflows: Copy an email to a folder AND add its details to a spreadsheet AND notify your team in Slack
  • Conditional logic: Only process emails that meet multiple criteria (e.g., from a client AND marked important)
  • Scheduled processing: Run your automation at specific times to batch process emails efficiently

Make.com's visual editor makes these advanced workflows accessible without coding. The platform handles all the technical complexity behind the scenes while you focus on designing processes that match your business needs.
