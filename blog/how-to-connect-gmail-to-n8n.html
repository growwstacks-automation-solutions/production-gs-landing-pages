Why Connect Gmail to n8n?

Manual email management is one of the biggest time-wasters in business today. The average professional spends 28% of their workweek reading and answering emails - time that could be automated with the right tools. Connecting Gmail to n8n solves this by enabling:

  • Automatic email responses based on specific triggers
  • Workflows that start when important emails arrive
  • Integration between Gmail and your CRM, task managers, or databases

Key insight: While n8n makes the automation part easy, Google's authentication process is intentionally complex for security reasons. This guide simplifies what Google doesn't.

Step 1: Google Cloud Console Setup

The first hurdle is creating a project in Google Cloud Console - a step many business owners find confusing when they just want to connect their email. Here's exactly what to do:

Step 1.1: Create a New Project

Navigate to Google Cloud Console and click "Create Project" at the top. Name it something recognizable like "n8n Gmail Connection" - this helps if you need to modify settings later.

Step 1.2: Enable Billing

Google requires credit card verification even for free API usage. Don't worry - you won't be charged for basic email automation. This is simply Google's way of preventing abuse of their services.

Pro tip: At 2:15 in the video, you can see exactly where to find the project creation and billing sections in the Cloud Console interface.

Step 2: OAuth Consent Screen Configuration

This is where most people get stuck. Google's OAuth setup has several non-obvious requirements:

Step 2.1: Configure OAuth Consent Screen

In your project dashboard, navigate to "APIs & Services" > "OAuth consent screen". Select "External" user type (even for personal use) and fill in:

  • Application name (visible during login)
  • Your email address
  • Developer contact information

Step 2.2: Add Authorized Redirect URI

The most crucial step: copy the redirect URI from n8n's Gmail node credentials and paste it into the "Authorized Redirect URIs" section. This exact match is required for authentication to work.

Important: Even though your app will show as "unverified" during login, you can safely proceed by clicking "Advanced" and then "Go to [app name]". This is normal for personal/internal use.

Step 3: Finalizing the n8n Connection

With Google Cloud configured, the n8n side is surprisingly simple:

Step 3.1: Enable Gmail API

Back in Google Cloud Console, navigate to "Library", search for "Gmail API", and enable it for your project. This gives n8n permission to interact with your email.

Step 3.2: Create Credentials in n8n

In your n8n workflow, add a Gmail node and create new credentials. You'll need to:

  1. Copy your Client ID and Secret from Google Cloud
  2. Paste them into n8n's credential setup
  3. Complete the OAuth flow when prompted

Success indicator: Once connected, you'll see a green "Connected" status in n8n's Gmail node. Test it by sending yourself an email and triggering a simple workflow.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

After helping dozens of businesses connect Gmail to n8n, we've identified these frequent pitfalls:

Mistake 1: Skipping the Redirect URI

The authorization will fail if the redirect URI in Google Cloud doesn't exactly match what n8n provides. Double-check for any trailing slashes or typos.

Mistake 2: Not Publishing the App

Your app starts in "testing" mode. You must click "Publish App" in the OAuth consent screen settings for the connection to work long-term.

Mistake 3: Using Incorrect Scopes

n8n needs specific permissions to read/send emails. If your workflow isn't working, check that you granted all necessary scopes during the OAuth flow.
