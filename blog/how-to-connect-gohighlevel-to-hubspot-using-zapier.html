Why Connect GoHighLevel and HubSpot?

Most businesses using both GoHighLevel and HubSpot waste countless hours manually transferring data between systems. Contacts added in HubSpot don't automatically appear in GoHighLevel sequences. Deal updates require duplicate entry. Tasks created in one platform remain invisible in the other.

This manual back-and-forth creates data silos, delays follow-ups, and introduces human error. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, we demonstrate how a single Zapier integration can eliminate these pain points completely.

Key benefit: Companies automating this connection report saving 6-12 hours per week on administrative tasks while improving data accuracy between systems by 92%.

Zapier Setup Basics

Before creating your zap, ensure you have admin access to both your HubSpot and GoHighLevel accounts. Zapier will need permissions to read from HubSpot and write to GoHighLevel.

Start by logging into Zapier and clicking "Create Zap." You'll see a two-column interface with "Trigger" on the left and "Action" on the right. This visual workflow builder makes it simple to connect apps without coding.

Pro tip: Name your zap descriptively (like "HubSpot Contact → GoHighLevel Task") so you can easily identify it later among other automations.

Configuring Your HubSpot Trigger

The trigger determines when your zap will run. For our example, we'll use "New Company" in HubSpot as the trigger event. This means whenever a new company record gets added to HubSpot, the zap will activate.

After selecting HubSpot as the trigger app, choose your specific trigger event from the dropdown. Zapier will then prompt you to connect your HubSpot account using OAuth. This secure connection allows Zapier to monitor for new trigger events without storing your login credentials.

  1. Search for and select "HubSpot" as your trigger app
  2. Choose "New Company" as the trigger event
  3. Click "Sign in" to connect your HubSpot account
  4. Follow the OAuth prompts to grant Zapier access
  5. Test the connection to verify it works

Setting Up Your GoHighLevel Action

Now we'll configure what happens in GoHighLevel when our HubSpot trigger activates. A common action is creating a new task for your sales team whenever a high-value company gets added to HubSpot.

Search for "GoHighLevel" in the action app search field. Select "LED Connector" (GoHighLevel's Zapier integration name) and choose "Create Task" as your action event. Connect your GoHighLevel account similarly to how you connected HubSpot.

Field mapping tip: You can pull data from HubSpot into GoHighLevel fields. For example, map the HubSpot "Company Name" to the GoHighLevel "Task Title" field.

Testing Your Zap

Before activating your zap permanently, Zapier will walk you through a test. This creates a real action in GoHighLevel using sample data from HubSpot. Verify the test worked by checking your GoHighLevel account for the new task.

If the test fails, Zapier provides detailed error messages to help troubleshoot. Common issues include incorrect field mappings or insufficient permissions in either connected app.

Important: Always test with non-critical data first. Create a test company in HubSpot rather than using live records during setup.

Advanced Field Mapping Tips

Beyond basic field mapping, Zapier allows you to transform data between systems. You can combine multiple HubSpot fields into a single GoHighLevel field, format dates differently, or even add custom text.

For example, you might create a GoHighLevel task description that includes the HubSpot company name, industry, and a custom note like "New high-value lead from HubSpot - contact within 24 hours."

  1. Click the "+" icon next to any field to add a transform
  2. Use the "Formatter" option to modify text, dates, or numbers
  3. Combine multiple fields using the "Concatenate" transform
  4. Add conditional logic to handle different scenarios
