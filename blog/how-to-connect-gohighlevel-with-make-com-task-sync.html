Why Connect GoHighLevel with Make.com

Businesses using GoHighLevel for CRM and marketing automation often struggle with manual data transfers to other systems. This creates bottlenecks, errors, and wasted time on repetitive tasks. The integration with Make.com solves these problems by automating data flow between platforms.

When connected, these systems can automatically sync contacts, trigger follow-up sequences, update records, and streamline operations. Companies report saving 10-15 hours weekly by eliminating manual data entry between these platforms.

Key benefit: The integration allows you to create sophisticated workflows where actions in GoHighLevel (like form submissions) automatically trigger processes in Make.com, which can then connect to hundreds of other apps.

Prerequisites Before Connecting

Before starting the integration process, ensure you have active accounts with administrative access to both GoHighLevel and Make.com. You'll need:

  • A paid GoHighLevel account (the integration isn't available on free trials)
  • A Make.com account with available scenario credits
  • The login credentials for both accounts handy
  • Clear understanding of which workflows you want to automate

It's also helpful to document your current manual processes between these systems. This makes it easier to identify which tasks to automate first when setting up your integration.

Step 1: Create a New Scenario in Make.com

Begin by logging into your Make.com account and navigating to the main dashboard. Look for the "Create Scenario" button in the top right corner - this opens a blank canvas where you'll build your integration.

Click the plus icon on the canvas to add your first module. In the search bar that appears, type "GoHighLevel" and select it from the list of available apps. This establishes GoHighLevel as part of your workflow.

Step 2: Set Up the GoHighLevel Trigger

The trigger determines what action in GoHighLevel will start your automated workflow. Common triggers include:

  • New contact created
  • Form submission received
  • Appointment booked
  • Task completed

Select the trigger that matches your workflow needs. For example, if you want to sync new leads, choose "New Contact Created." This ensures every new contact in GoHighLevel automatically flows into your Make.com scenario.

Step 3: Configure the Webhook Connection

After setting your trigger, you'll need to configure a webhook - the bridge that connects GoHighLevel events to Make.com. The webhook listens for specific events in GoHighLevel and passes that data to Make.com.

Make.com will generate a unique webhook URL during this step. Copy this URL carefully - you'll need to paste it into your GoHighLevel settings later to complete the connection between platforms.

Step 4: Authenticate Your Accounts

This crucial step links your GoHighLevel account to Make.com. Click the "Connect" button in the GoHighLevel module within Make.com. You'll be redirected to log into your GoHighLevel account and grant permissions.

If this is your first time connecting, you may need to enter additional authentication details. Ensure you use an admin account with sufficient permissions in GoHighLevel to avoid connection issues later.

Step 5: Select Events to Monitor

With authentication complete, specify exactly which GoHighLevel events should trigger your workflow. You can monitor:

  • Specific forms or funnels
  • Particular locations or sub-accounts
  • Certain contact tags or statuses

This customization ensures your automation only runs when relevant events occur, preventing unnecessary triggers. Once configured, click "Save" to activate your integration.

Testing and Troubleshooting

After setup, test your integration by triggering the selected event in GoHighLevel (like creating a test contact). Check Make.com's execution logs to verify the data flowed correctly.

Common issues include authentication errors (solved by reconnecting accounts) and data mapping problems (fixed by adjusting field mappings). The video at 2:15 shows specific troubleshooting techniques for these scenarios.

Pro tip: Create a test workflow with email notifications before building complex automations. This lets you verify the connection works without affecting live data.
