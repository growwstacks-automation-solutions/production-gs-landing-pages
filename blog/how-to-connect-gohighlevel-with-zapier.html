Step 1: Create a New Zap

Log into your Zapier account and click the "+ Create Zap" button in the top navigation. This starts a new automation workflow that will connect GoHighLevel with any other app in Zapier's ecosystem of 5,000+ integrations.

Name your Zap something descriptive like "GoHighLevel to Google Sheets Sync" so you can easily identify it later among your automations. The naming convention should reflect both the trigger (GoHighLevel event) and the action (what happens in the other app).

Step 2: Set Your Trigger

In the trigger section, search for "Lead Connector" - this is GoHighLevel's integration name in Zapier. Select it from the app dropdown menu. You'll then choose which specific event in GoHighLevel should start your automation.

Common trigger events include:

  • New Contact Added
  • Pipeline Stage Changed
  • Opportunity Updated
  • Task Completed

For our example, we'll use "Pipeline Stage Changed" to automate actions whenever a lead moves to a new sales stage in GoHighLevel.

Step 3: Connect GoHighLevel

Click "Sign in to GoHighLevel" and enter your account credentials when prompted. Zapier will request permission to access your GoHighLevel data - approve these permissions to establish the connection.

You'll need to verify your identity through a verification code sent to your email. Once authenticated, select which GoHighLevel account you want to connect if you manage multiple locations or sub-accounts.

Pro tip: Create a dedicated "Zapier" user in GoHighLevel with appropriate permissions rather than using your personal admin account for better security and audit trails.

Step 4: Choose Your Action

Now select what should happen when your trigger event occurs in GoHighLevel. Search for and select your action app - popular choices include Google Sheets, Slack, HubSpot, or Notion.

For our example, we'll choose "Google Sheets - Add Row" to log every pipeline change in a master spreadsheet. Map the GoHighLevel fields (contact name, new stage, timestamp) to the appropriate columns in your Sheet.

You can add multiple actions to a single Zap, like both updating a spreadsheet and sending a Slack notification when a high-value lead enters your closing stage.

Step 5: Test and Publish

Zapier will walk you through testing your integration with sample data from GoHighLevel. Verify the test data appears correctly in your action app before turning on the Zap.

Once published, your automation will run in real-time. Monitor the "History" tab in Zapier for the first few days to ensure everything works as expected. You can always pause or edit the Zap if adjustments are needed.

Remember: Zapier checks for new triggers every 1-15 minutes depending on your plan. For instant updates, consider webhooks or upgrading to a premium Zapier plan.
