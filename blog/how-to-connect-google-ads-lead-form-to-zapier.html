Authorizing Zapier Access

The first step is establishing trust between your Google Ads account and Zapier. This authorization process ensures secure data transfer between the platforms.

From your Google Ads dashboard, navigate to Tools & Settings > Setup > API Center. Here you'll find the option to connect third-party apps. Search for Zapier and click "Authorize." You'll be prompted to log in to your Google account and grant permissions.

Pro tip: Use a Google account with admin-level access to avoid permission issues later. Standard user accounts may lack necessary API permissions.

Configuring Google Ads Lead Form

With authorization complete, we now configure the lead form itself. In Google Ads, go to Assets > Lead Forms. Here you'll either edit an existing form or create a new one specifically for Zapier integration.

The critical step is enabling the "Export leads" option. This is found under the form's Additional Settings. Toggle on "Export leads to external destination" and select Zapier from the dropdown menu.

Important: Save your form after enabling exports. Many users forget this step and wonder why leads aren't transferring.

Setting Up Zapier Integration

Now we switch to Zapier to complete the connection. The Google Ads authorization will trigger a new Zap template automatically - this is your starting point.

Select "Google Ads Lead Form" as your trigger app. Zapier will detect your authorized account. Then choose your destination app (CRM, Google Sheets, etc.). Popular options include:

  • Salesforce - Create New Lead
  • HubSpot - Create Contact
  • Google Sheets - Add Row
  • Slack - Send Channel Message

Mapping Data Fields

Field mapping is where the magic happens - this determines how Google Ads form data populates your destination app. Zapier shows all available fields from both systems side-by-side.

For each Google Ads field (name, email, phone, etc.), select the corresponding field in your destination app. Pay special attention to:

  1. Email fields - Must match exactly for CRM tracking
  2. Phone formats - Some CRMs require specific formatting
  3. Custom fields - Any extra data you collect in your form

Testing Your Integration

Before going live, thorough testing prevents headaches later. Zapier provides a test mode where you can submit a sample lead and verify it appears correctly in your destination app.

Check these critical elements:

  • All mapped fields contain the expected data
  • No errors in the Zapier history log
  • Lead appears in your system within 2 minutes

Testing tip: Use real-looking test data (not "test@test.com") to catch formatting issues that might not appear with placeholder values.

Common Setup Mistakes

Even with careful setup, some pitfalls trip up many users. Being aware of these saves troubleshooting time later.

The top three mistakes we see:

  1. Insufficient Google Ads permissions - The connecting account needs "Standard" or "Admin" access level
  2. Unmapped custom fields - Any extra form fields won't transfer unless explicitly mapped
  3. Zap turned off - It's easy to forget to actually activate the Zap after testing
