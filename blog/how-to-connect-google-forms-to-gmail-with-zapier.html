Zapier Setup Overview

The automation requires just two main components in Zapier: a Google Forms trigger and a Gmail action. When properly configured, every new form submission automatically sends a customized email through your Gmail account.

At 1:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see the simple Zapier interface where we'll build this workflow. The entire setup takes about 15 minutes, but saves countless hours over time. You'll need:

  • A Zapier account (free tier works for basic needs)
  • Access to the Google Form you want to automate
  • A Gmail account for sending the automated messages

Connecting Google Forms

The first step is setting up the trigger - telling Zapier when to send emails. We'll configure Zapier to watch for new Google Form submissions:

Step 1: Create a New Zap

After logging into Zapier, click "Create Zap" in the top left corner. This opens the Zap editor where we'll build our automation.

Step 2: Set Google Forms as Trigger

Search for "Google Forms" in the trigger app selection. Choose the trigger event "New Form Response" which activates whenever someone submits your form.

Step 3: Connect Your Google Account

Authenticate with the Google account that owns or has access to your form. Be sure to select the correct account if you use multiple Google profiles.

Pro tip: At 2:30 in the video, you'll see how to test the trigger connection by submitting a test form response. This confirms Zapier can properly read your form submissions.

Setting Up Gmail Action

With the trigger configured, we'll now set up what happens when Zapier detects a new form submission - in this case, sending an email through Gmail.

Step 1: Add Gmail Action

Click "Add Action" in your Zap and search for Gmail. Select "Send Email" as the action event.

Step 2: Connect Your Gmail Account

Authenticate with the Gmail account you want to send messages from. This should typically be your business email address.

Step 3: Configure Email Details

Zapier provides fields for recipient, subject, and body content. We'll customize these in the next section to personalize each message.

Personalizing Your Emails

The real power comes from inserting form responses into your email template. At 4:45 in the video, you'll see how to:

  • Address respondents by name: Insert their first name from the form into the email greeting
  • Include specific answers: Reference their form responses to create relevant, contextual replies

Zapier shows all available form fields when you click the insert field button. You can mix static text with dynamic fields to create professional, personalized messages automatically.

Example: "Hi [First Name], thanks for your inquiry about [Product Interest]! Our team will follow up within 24 hours."

Testing Your Automation

Before turning on your Zap, thorough testing ensures everything works as expected:

Step 1: Send Test Email

Zapier lets you send a test email using sample form data. Check your inbox to verify the formatting and content.

Step 2: Submit Real Test Response

Fill out your actual form with test data to trigger a real automation cycle. Confirm the email arrives as expected.

Step 3: Turn On Your Zap

Once testing succeeds, activate your Zap to begin processing real form submissions automatically.

At 6:20 in the video, you'll see the testing process in action and learn how to troubleshoot common issues like missing fields or incorrect formatting.
