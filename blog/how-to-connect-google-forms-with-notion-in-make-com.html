The Manual Copy-Paste Problem

Every time someone submits your Google Form, you face the same tedious routine: open the responses sheet, copy the latest entry, switch to Notion, paste the data, and format it correctly. For high-volume forms like job applications or client intake, this can consume 3-5 hours per week of valuable time.

Worse yet, manual transfers introduce errors. Missed fields, incorrect formatting, and delayed updates create unreliable data that undermines decision-making. The solution isn't working harder - it's automating the connection between these two powerful tools.

Time-saving insight: A properly configured Make.com workflow can process 100+ form submissions daily with zero manual intervention, reducing administrative work by 90%.

Step 1: Prepare Your Notion Database

Before connecting anything, structure your Notion database to mirror your form fields. Create properties for each question type:

  • Short text for name, email, and single-line responses
  • Select/multi-select for dropdown and multiple choice questions
  • Date fields for any calendar inputs
  • Checkbox for yes/no questions

Pro tip: Name your Notion properties identically to your Google Forms questions for easier mapping later. At 1:45 in the video tutorial, you'll see how we structured a client intake database with 15 custom fields that match the form exactly.

Step 2: Generate Your Make.com Webhook

The magic bridge between Google Forms and Notion is a Make.com webhook - a unique URL that receives form data and routes it to your destination. Here's how to create it:

  1. Log in to Make.com and create a new scenario
  2. Add the "Webhooks" module as your trigger
  3. Select "Custom webhook" and click "Add"
  4. Copy the generated webhook URL (keep this tab open)

Critical security note: Treat this webhook URL like a password. Anyone with the link could send fake data to your Notion database. For sensitive forms, consider adding basic authentication to your Make.com scenario.

Step 3: Connect the Pieces

With your Notion database ready and webhook generated, the final connection happens in three parts:

Part A: Connect Google Forms to Make.com

In your Google Form, go to Responses → More options (three dots) → Script editor. Paste the webhook URL where shown at 3:10 in the video. This tells Google Forms to send each new submission to your Make.com endpoint.

Part B: Map Fields in Make.com

Back in Make.com, add the Notion module after your webhook. Select "Create database item" and connect your Notion account. Map each form field to its corresponding Notion property using the drag-and-drop interface.

Part C: Test and Activate

Submit a test form and watch the data flow through in real-time. Check that all fields transferred correctly before activating the scenario. Once live, new submissions will appear in Notion within seconds.

Troubleshooting Tips

If data isn't flowing correctly, check these common issues first:

  • Webhook timeout: Make.com free plan processes triggers every 15 minutes. Upgrade for instant transfers.
  • Field mismatches: Recheck that all Google Form questions have corresponding Notion properties with matching types.
  • Permission errors: Ensure your Notion integration has edit access to the target database.
