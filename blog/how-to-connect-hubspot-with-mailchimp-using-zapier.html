Zapier Setup Overview

Zapier acts as the bridge between HubSpot and Mailchimp, transforming what was a manual process into an instant, error-free workflow. The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence:

  1. Trigger: A new contact appears in HubSpot
  2. Action: Zapier adds/updates that contact in Mailchimp

This "when this, then that" logic runs continuously in the background. Unlike human operators, it never gets tired, never makes typos, and never forgets to update lists before a campaign launch.

Configuring HubSpot Trigger

The automation begins in your Zapier dashboard where you'll create a new Zap. Selecting HubSpot as your trigger app tells Zapier to monitor your CRM for specific events.

For this workflow, we'll use "New Contact" as the trigger event. This ensures every fresh lead - whether from a form submission, imported list, or sales manual entry - kicks off the synchronization process.

Mapping Mailchimp Action

After configuring the HubSpot trigger, you'll set Mailchimp as the action app. The "Add/Update Subscriber" action serves two critical functions:

  1. Adds brand new contacts to your selected Mailchimp audience
  2. Updates existing subscribers if their information changes in HubSpot

Field mapping is where the magic happens. You'll connect HubSpot properties (like email, first name, company) to corresponding Mailchimp merge fields. This ensures data flows to the right places automatically.

Testing & Activation

Before going live, Zapier's test mode lets you verify the workflow with real data. This crucial step confirms that:

  • HubSpot contacts appear correctly in the trigger
  • Field mappings transfer data accurately to Mailchimp
  • No errors occur during the sync process

Once testing succeeds, publishing the Zap activates your automation. The switch from manual to automatic happens instantly - your next HubSpot contact will flow to Mailchimp without any intervention.

Advanced Customizations

While the basic integration solves the core sync problem, Zapier offers powerful extensions:

  • Conditional routing: Send contacts to different Mailchimp lists based on HubSpot properties
  • Tag synchronization: Mirror HubSpot contact tags in Mailchimp for segmentation
  • Deal-based triggers: Add contacts to specific sequences when deals reach certain stages

These advanced configurations require no coding - just logical "if this, then that" rules built through Zapier's visual interface.
