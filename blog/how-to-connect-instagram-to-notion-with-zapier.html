Zapier Setup Overview

Zapier acts as the bridge between Instagram and Notion, automatically detecting new posts and sending them to your specified Notion database. The process involves five straightforward steps that take about 10 minutes to complete.

Unlike manual API coding, Zapier handles all the technical complexity behind a simple interface. You'll create what Zapier calls a "Zap" - their term for an automation that connects two apps. This particular Zap watches for new Instagram posts (the trigger) and creates corresponding Notion pages (the action).

Step 1: Create Your Zapier Account

Head to zapier.com and sign up for a free account if you don't already have one. The free plan allows up to 5 Zaps (automations) and 100 tasks per month - perfect for testing this integration.

Once logged in, click the "Create Zap" button at the top of your dashboard. This opens the Zap builder where you'll select Instagram as your trigger app and Notion as your action app. The interface will guide you through connecting both accounts.

Step 2: Connect Instagram

In the Zap builder, search for and select Instagram as your trigger app. Choose "New Media" as the trigger event - this detects when you post new content to your feed.

You'll need to authenticate your Instagram account by clicking "Sign in to Instagram." Zapier uses secure OAuth, so your login credentials are never stored directly. The connection only requests permission to read your posts - it can't publish or edit anything.

Pro Tip: If you manage multiple Instagram accounts, you can set up separate Zaps for each one, routing content to different Notion databases based on the account.

Step 3: Connect Notion

After setting up the Instagram trigger, search for and select Notion as your action app. Choose "Create Page" as the action event - this will generate a new Notion page for each Instagram post.

Authenticate your Notion account by clicking "Connect a Notion Account." You'll need to select which Notion workspace to use and grant permissions. Make sure you're logged into the correct Notion account in your browser during this step.

Step 4: Customize Data Flow

This is where you map Instagram post data to Notion page properties. You can choose to include:

  • Post captions (full text with hashtags)
  • Image URLs (links to the Instagram media)
  • Engagement metrics (like counts)
  • Publication timestamps

Select your target Notion database and configure how you want the information organized. For example, you might create separate properties for caption text, hashtags, and image links to make searching easier later.

Step 5: Test & Publish

Zapier will prompt you to test the connection with a sample Instagram post. This verifies everything works before going live. Check your Notion database to confirm the test page appears as expected.

Once satisfied, name your Zap (e.g., "Instagram to Notion Archive") and toggle it on. The automation will now run in the background, syncing new posts automatically. You can always edit or pause the Zap later from your dashboard.

First-time setup takes under 15 minutes, but saves hours of manual work each month. The Zap requires no maintenance once published - it just works silently in the background.
