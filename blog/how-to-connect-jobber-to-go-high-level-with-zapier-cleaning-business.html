The Cleaning Business Data Dilemma

Cleaning business owners face a constant battle with duplicate data entry. A lead comes in through your website form (Go High Level), your team creates a quote (Jobber), then you need to update both systems when the job is booked, completed, or canceled. This manual back-and-forth wastes 5-10 hours per week while increasing errors.

We recently implemented this integration for a cleaning business using Jobber and Go High Level. They were previously managing everything through Airtable and Google Sheets, leading to missed follow-ups and inconsistent data. The Zapier integration created a seamless flow between both platforms.

Key insight: Go High Level excels at lead nurturing while Jobber handles fulfillment. When connected properly, they create a complete client journey from first contact to recurring service.

Five Key Automations

These five Zapier automations form the foundation of a connected Jobber and Go High Level system:

  1. New Lead Sync: When a lead enters Go High Level, create their profile in Jobber automatically
  2. Quote Sent: When you send a quote from Jobber, update Go High Level and trigger follow-ups
  3. Job Booked: When a job is scheduled in Jobber, categorize the client in Go High Level
  4. Job Completed: When cleaners mark a job complete, trigger review requests and upsells
  5. Job Canceled: When a booking is canceled, initiate win-back campaigns

At 2:45 in the video, you can see how these automations appear in Zapier's interface. Each one follows a similar pattern: Jobber triggers an event, Zapier processes it, then updates Go High Level accordingly.

New Lead Sync

The first automation solves a common pain point: leads coming in through Go High Level forms that then need to be manually entered into Jobber. This creates delays and potential errors.

When a new lead enters your Go High Level pipeline (say, through a website form), the automation:

  • Creates a new client card in Jobber with all contact information
  • Tags them appropriately based on service type
  • Ensures your team can immediately begin working on their quote

Implementation tip: Set up a specific "New Lead" stage in your Go High Level pipeline that triggers this automation. This gives your team visibility into which leads have been synced to Jobber.

Quote Sent Automation

Many cleaning businesses create quotes directly in Jobber. Without automation, this means manually updating Go High Level to reflect the quote status.

The quote sent automation:

  1. Triggers when you send a quote from Jobber
  2. Creates or updates the client card in Go High Level
  3. Moves them to the "Quote Sent" pipeline stage
  4. Starts an automated follow-up sequence

At 5:20 in the video, you can see how this automation connects to Go High Level's follow-up sequences. Each day after the quote is sent, the client receives both email and SMS reminders until they book or the sequence completes.

Job Booked vs Completed

Understanding the difference between these two automations is crucial for proper follow-up timing:

Job Booked: Triggers when a job is scheduled in Jobber's calendar. This helps categorize clients and prepare for upcoming work, but doesn't trigger review requests.

Job Completed: Triggers after cleaners mark the job as finished in Jobber. This initiates:

  • Review request sequences
  • Service quality follow-ups
  • Upsell opportunities to recurring service

The video at 7:45 shows how these automations filter clients into different paths based on whether they're one-time or recurring, ensuring appropriate messaging.

Handling Cancellations

Job cancellations represent both a challenge and opportunity. The cancellation automation:

  1. Detects when a job is canceled in Jobber
  2. Moves the client to a "Booking Canceled" stage in Go High Level
  3. Triggers win-back campaigns
  4. Provides visibility into cancellation reasons

At 9:30 in the video, you can see how this connects to Go High Level's pipeline stages. The system maintains a record of canceled bookings separate from other stages, allowing for targeted remarketing.

One-Time vs Recurring Clients

The most sophisticated part of this integration handles the distinction between one-time and recurring clients. This requires Zapier's premium filters and split paths.

When a job is created or completed:

  • The automation checks whether it's one-time or recurring
  • Clients are routed to different Go High Level pipeline stages
  • This ensures proper remarketing (you don't ask recurring clients to become recurring)

Key benefit: This filtering allows you to automatically upsell one-time clients to recurring service while providing appropriate communication to existing recurring clients.
