Step-by-Step Connection Guide

Connecting Klaviyo to Zapier takes less time than brewing your morning coffee. Follow these simple steps to set up your integration:

Step 1: Access Your Zapier Account

Log into your Zapier dashboard. If you don't have an account yet, you can sign up for free - the basic plan includes everything you need to connect Klaviyo.

Step 2: Navigate to App Connections

In the left sidebar, click on "App Connections." This is where you'll manage all your integrated applications. Look for the "Add Connection" button in the top right corner.

Step 3: Search for Klaviyo

Type "Klaviyo" into the search bar that appears. Select Klaviyo from the dropdown list of available apps.

Step 4: Authenticate Your Klaviyo Account

You'll be prompted to log into your Klaviyo account. Enter your credentials carefully - if you have two-factor authentication enabled, have your phone ready to receive the verification code.

Step 5: Grant Permissions

Review and approve the permissions Zapier requests to access your Klaviyo data. This secure connection allows Zapier to create and update contacts, but won't compromise sensitive information.

Pro Tip: Name your connection something memorable like "Main Klaviyo Account" if you manage multiple integrations. This makes troubleshooting easier later.

1. Automatic List Segmentation

When a new customer completes a purchase on Shopify, Zapier can automatically add them to specific Klaviyo lists based on what they bought, how much they spent, or other purchase attributes. No more manual list management.

2. CRM Sync

Keep your sales team in the loop by having Zapier update your CRM (like HubSpot or Salesforce) whenever a contact engages with a Klaviyo email campaign. This creates a complete customer journey record.

3. Post-Purchase Surveys

Trigger a Typeform survey 3 days after purchase, then feed the responses back into Klaviyo to personalize future communications based on customer feedback.

Troubleshooting Tips

While the Klaviyo-Zapier connection is generally reliable, here's how to handle common issues:

Authentication Errors: If you see connection problems, try re-authenticating in Zapier's App Connections settings. Klaviyo sessions expire periodically for security.

Missing Data: Check your field mappings in each Zap. Klaviyo requires specific field formats that might differ from your trigger app.

Rate Limits: Free Zapier accounts have usage limits. Upgrade your plan if you're moving large contact lists frequently.

Security Considerations

When connecting Klaviyo to any third-party service, security should be top of mind. Here's what you need to know:

Zapier uses OAuth 2.0 for authentication, meaning your actual Klaviyo credentials are never stored by Zapier. The connection token can be revoked at any time from your Klaviyo account settings.

Zapier only accesses the specific Klaviyo data needed for your configured Zaps. Sensitive information like credit card details or passwords are never exposed through the API.

Best Practice: Review connected apps in your Klaviyo settings monthly and remove any unused integrations to minimize potential attack surfaces.
