The Instagram Marketing Pain Most Businesses Ignore

Every Instagram marketer faces the same frustrating reality: you spend hours creating content, but have no organized system to track what you've posted, when it went live, or how it performed. Important posts get buried in your feed, never to be found again when you need to reference them.

Manually copying post details into spreadsheets eats up valuable time that could be spent creating new content. This disorganization leads to duplicate posts, inconsistent posting schedules, and missed opportunities to repurpose top-performing content.

82% of businesses admit they have no system for tracking Instagram posts, relying instead on memory and scrolling through their feed when they need to find old content.

Step 1: Connecting Make.com to Instagram

Connecting your Instagram account to Make.com takes just minutes but unlocks powerful automation capabilities. Start by logging into your Make.com account and creating a new scenario - this is where you'll build your automation workflow.

Search for the Instagram app in Make.com's module library and select it. You'll then be prompted to connect your Instagram account through a secure authentication popup. This uses Instagram's official API, so your login credentials remain protected.

Pro Tip: Use a business Instagram account rather than a personal one for this integration. Business accounts have access to more features through Instagram's API.

Step 2: Setting Up the Instagram Post Trigger

The trigger is what tells your automation to run. For this workflow, we'll use the "Watch Posts" module that activates whenever you publish new content to Instagram.

After adding the Instagram module to your scenario, select "Watch Posts" as your trigger type. You can optionally filter which posts activate the automation - for example, only posts with specific hashtags or from certain accounts. For most users, monitoring all posts from your connected account works best.

Step 3: Configuring the Google Sheets Action

Now we'll set up where the Instagram post data gets saved. Add a Google Sheets module to your scenario and select "Add a Row" as the action type. Connect your Google account and choose the spreadsheet where you want to store your Instagram post archive.

Make.com will guide you through mapping Instagram post fields to spreadsheet columns. At minimum, map these key fields:

  • Post caption/text
  • Image or video URL
  • Publication date/time
  • Post permalink

Time Saver: This automation eliminates 15-30 minutes of manual work per post. For businesses posting daily, that's 5-10 hours saved each month.

Testing and Activating Your Automation

Before going live, test your automation by clicking "Run Once" in Make.com. This executes a manual test run so you can verify everything works correctly. Check your Google Sheet to confirm the test post appears as expected.

Once testing succeeds, toggle the scenario to "Run Continuously." Make.com will now automatically monitor for new Instagram posts and update your spreadsheet in real-time. The platform handles all scheduling and error recovery automatically.
