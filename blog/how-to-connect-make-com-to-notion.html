Make.com to Notion Setup: Step-by-Step

Creating your first integration takes less time than manually syncing data for a single week. Here's the exact process we use with clients:

Step 1: Create a New Scenario

Log into your Make.com dashboard and click "Create a new scenario." This blank canvas will become your automated workflow between Notion and other apps.

Step 2: Add Notion Trigger

Click the first module button and search for "Notion." Select "Watch database items" as your trigger - this tells Make.com to monitor for changes in your specified Notion database.

Step 3: Connect Your Account

Authorize access to your Notion workspace by following the on-screen prompts. Make.com uses secure OAuth connections that never store your login credentials.

Pro tip: Create a dedicated "Automation" page in Notion to organize all your integrated databases. This keeps your automation logic separate from operational content.

Configuring Database Triggers

The real magic happens when you specify which database to watch and what changes should activate your workflow. At the 1:15 mark in the video tutorial, you'll see how to:

  • Select your target Notion database from the dropdown
  • Choose whether to trigger on new items, updates, or both
  • Add filters to only sync specific record types (e.g., high-priority tasks)

This precision targeting prevents your automation from creating noise. One accounting firm we worked with used filters to only sync client invoices marked "approved," reducing unnecessary updates by 73%.

The Power of Field Mapping

Field mapping transforms this from a simple sync to a powerful data transformation tool. When configuring your action module (like adding to Google Sheets), you'll see a mapping interface where you can:

  • Pull specific Notion properties into different columns/cells
  • Combine multiple fields into one destination field
  • Add static text or formulas alongside dynamic data

A real estate team automated their Notion CRM → Mailchimp workflow by mapping property types to specific email templates, creating hyper-personalized campaigns without manual intervention.
