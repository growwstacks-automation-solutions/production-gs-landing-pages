Getting Started with Make.com

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a powerful automation platform that connects Microsoft Teams with hundreds of other business applications. The first step is creating your free account at make.com.

Once logged in, you'll see the dashboard where all your automations (called "scenarios") will live. Don't worry about the interface complexity—we'll walk through each step to connect Microsoft Teams in the simplest way possible.

Creating Your First Scenario

Scenarios are Make.com's term for automated workflows. Think of them as digital assembly lines where information moves automatically between apps based on your rules.

To create one:

  1. Click the "Create a new scenario" button
  2. Name your scenario (e.g., "Teams Daily Updates")
  3. The visual editor will open where you'll build your workflow

Pro tip: Start simple. A basic Teams message automation takes just 3 modules to set up but can save hours each month.

Adding the Microsoft Teams Module

The magic happens when you connect Microsoft Teams to Make.com. At 4:30 in the video, we show the exact steps:

  1. Click the "+" icon in the scenario editor
  2. Search for "Microsoft Teams" in the app library
  3. Select it and authenticate your account

Make.com guides you through Microsoft's secure authentication process. You'll only need to do this once—after authentication, you can create unlimited Teams automations.

Setting Up Triggers and Actions

Automations work by combining triggers (events that start the workflow) with actions (what happens in response). For Microsoft Teams, common triggers include:

  • New channel messages
  • Meeting reminders
  • File uploads

Popular actions include:

  • Sending messages to individuals or channels
  • Posting daily updates
  • Notifying specific team members

At 6:45 in the video, we demonstrate setting up a "new message" trigger that automatically logs important communications to a Google Sheet.

Testing and Scheduling Your Automation

Before going live, always test your scenario using the "Run once" button. Make.com will execute one cycle so you can verify everything works as intended.

Once confirmed, set your preferred schedule:

  • Instant: Triggers immediately when the event occurs
  • Scheduled: Runs at set intervals (hourly, daily, etc.)
  • Custom: Activates at specific times/dates

Triggers initiate your workflow when a specific event occurs (like a new Teams message). Actions are the tasks performed in response (like sending a notification or updating a spreadsheet).

  • Multiple actions can follow a single trigger
