Why Connect Notion to Make.com?

Most businesses using Notion eventually hit the same wall - their data lives in silos. You might track projects in Notion, customer info in your CRM, and tasks in other tools. Without connections between these systems, you waste countless hours manually updating records across platforms.

The Notion-Make.com integration solves this by creating a two-way bridge between your Notion databases and hundreds of other apps. When a record changes in one system, the update automatically flows to the other - no copy-paste required.

Businesses using this integration save 5-10 hours weekly by eliminating manual data entry between Notion and their other tools. The setup takes just minutes but pays dividends in productivity.

Step 1: Create Your Notion Integration

Start by logging into your Notion account through a web browser. Click on your profile photo or initials in the top-left corner to access Settings. Navigate to the "Connections" section and look for "Develop or manage integrations".

Click "New Integration" to create your connector. Give it a descriptive name (like "Make.com Automation") and select "Internal" as the integration type. Choose the workspace where your target databases live, then click Save.

Step 2: Get Your Integration Token

After creating the integration, Notion will generate a unique secret token. This acts as the secure key that allows Make.com to access your Notion data. Copy this token immediately - you won't be able to see it again after leaving this page.

Store the token securely in a password manager or other safe location. If it's compromised, you'll need to generate a new one through the same integration settings.

Pro Tip: Create a separate integration for each distinct automation purpose. This lets you revoke access for specific workflows without disrupting others.

Step 3: Connect Your Notion Database

Open the specific Notion database you want to automate. Click the three dots in the top-right corner, select "Connections", then "Manage Connections". Search for the integration you just created and click "Confirm" to grant it access.

Repeat this step for each database you want to include in your automations. The integration will only have access to databases you explicitly connect.

Step 4: Set Up Make.com Connection

Log into your Make.com account and create a new scenario. Search for "Notion" in the apps list and select it. When prompted for a connection, click "Add" and paste your Notion integration token into the field.

Name the connection something recognizable (like "Notion - Marketing DB") and click Save. Make.com will now be able to interact with your connected Notion databases.

Step 5: Build Your First Automation

With the connection established, you can now build workflows that either:

  1. Trigger actions in other apps when Notion data changes
  2. Update Notion when events occur in other connected apps

A simple starter workflow might watch for new rows in a Notion database and create corresponding tasks in your project management tool. The possibilities are endless once the connection is live.
