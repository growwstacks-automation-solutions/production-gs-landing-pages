Why Automate Paperform Submissions?

Manual data entry from form submissions is one of the biggest time-wasters for small businesses. Every minute spent copying information from Paperform to other systems is time not spent growing your business. Automation solves this by instantly routing data where it needs to go.

By connecting Paperform to Zapier, you create a seamless flow where each submission triggers actions across your tech stack. This could mean adding leads to your CRM, creating tasks in project management tools, or sending notifications to your team - all without lifting a finger after setup.

Businesses automate 15+ hours per month by connecting Paperform to Zapier, according to internal GrowwStacks client data. That's nearly two full workdays regained each month just from eliminating manual form processing.

Prerequisites

Before connecting Paperform to Zapier, you'll need a few things in place. First, an active Paperform account with at least one form created. Second, a Zapier account - the free tier works for basic automations. Finally, access to the apps you want to connect to (like Google Sheets, Slack, or your CRM).

Make sure you have admin access to both Paperform and Zapier, as you'll need permissions to set up integrations. If you're connecting to business apps, have your login credentials ready for those services as well. The entire setup typically takes under 15 minutes for most common workflows.

Step 1: Access Paperform Integrations

Start by logging into your Paperform account and opening the form you want to automate. In the form editor, look for the "After Submission" settings - this controls what happens when someone completes your form. Select "Integrations and Webhooks" to see all available connection options.

Scroll down to find the Zapier section at the bottom of the integrations list. This dedicated area makes connecting to Zapier straightforward compared to some other form builders. Click the "Connect to Zapier" button to begin the authorization process between the two platforms.

Step 2: Connect to Zapier

After clicking "Connect to Zapier," you'll be prompted to authorize the connection between Paperform and your Zapier account. If you're already logged into Zapier in your browser, this may happen automatically. Otherwise, you'll need to enter your Zapier credentials.

Authorization ensures secure communication between the platforms. Paperform will only send data to your specific Zapier account, and Zapier will only trigger actions you've approved. This one-time setup establishes the foundation for all your future Paperform-Zapier automations.

Step 3: Choose Your Automation Path

With the connection established, you'll see two options: using a pre-made Zap template or building a custom Zap from scratch. Templates offer quick setups for common workflows like sending submissions to Google Sheets or Slack. Custom Zaps give you complete control over every detail.

For first-time users, templates provide an excellent starting point. As you become more comfortable with Zapier, you can explore custom builds that match your exact business processes. Either way, the configuration happens right within Paperform's interface - no need to switch between tabs.

Step 4: Configure Your Zap

The configuration popup will guide you through selecting or connecting the apps involved in your automation. For example, if you're sending data to Google Sheets, you'll choose which spreadsheet and worksheet to use. You'll then map Paperform fields to their corresponding locations in the destination app.

Field mapping is where the magic happens - this determines exactly which pieces of form data go where. Take time to align fields correctly, especially for custom form questions. Zapier's interface makes this visual and intuitive, showing you a preview of how data will flow between systems.

Step 5: Test and Activate

Before going live, use Zapier's test mode to verify your automation works as expected. Submit a test form and check that data appears correctly in your destination apps. This catches any mapping errors before real submissions come through.

Once testing succeeds, activate your Zap. From this moment forward, every Paperform submission will automatically trigger your configured actions. The setup is complete, and you've just eliminated hours of future manual work with this 15-minute investment.
