Why Connect Pipedrive with Zapier?

Sales teams using Pipedrive often find themselves stuck in repetitive data entry - copying information from emails, forms, and other apps into their CRM. This manual work not only wastes time but leads to errors and outdated records. The Pipedrive-Zapier integration solves this by creating automated workflows (called zaps) that sync data between platforms instantly.

When connected, these two platforms can work together to eliminate 80% of manual CRM updates. New leads get added automatically when they submit a form. Deal stages trigger notifications in Slack. Closed-won opportunities sync with your accounting software. The possibilities are endless once you establish this foundational connection.

Time savings: Sales teams using Pipedrive-Zapier automations report saving 5-10 hours per week previously spent on manual data entry. That's 250-500 hours annually that can be redirected to selling and building customer relationships.

Step 1: Access Zapier Connections

Begin by logging into your Zapier account at zapier.com. If you don't have an account yet, you can create one for free - the basic plan allows up to 5 zaps which is enough to test the integration. Once logged in, look for the left sidebar navigation (around the 0:45 mark in the video tutorial).

In the sidebar, locate and click on the "Connections" section. This is your central hub for all app integrations in Zapier. Here you'll see any existing connections you've set up with other apps like Gmail, Slack, or QuickBooks. Don't worry if your connections page is empty - we'll add Pipedrive next.

Step 2: Add Pipedrive Connection

In the top right corner of your Connections page, click the "Add Connection" button (shown at 1:10 in the video). This opens a search bar where you can find Pipedrive among Zapier's 5,000+ supported apps. Start typing "Pipedrive" - the search is very responsive and will show matching results as you type.

When Pipedrive appears in the results, click on it to begin the connection process. Zapier will guide you through authenticating your Pipedrive account in the next step. If you don't see Pipedrive, make sure you're spelling it correctly and that your Zapier account has permission to connect with CRM apps.

Step 3: Authenticate Pipedrive

Zapier will now prompt you to log into your Pipedrive account (around 1:35 in the video). Enter the same email and password you use to access Pipedrive directly. Some accounts may require two-factor authentication at this stage - have your phone or authenticator app ready if enabled.

After successful login, you'll see a permissions screen asking what data Zapier can access. For most automations, you'll want to allow all permissions so Zapier can read and write deals, contacts, and activities. Click "Allow" to complete the connection. That's it - your Pipedrive account is now connected to Zapier!

Pro tip: Name your connection something memorable like "Pipedrive Production" or "Sales CRM" if you manage multiple Pipedrive accounts. This helps when building zaps later.

Common Automation Ideas

Now that Pipedrive is connected, here are three powerful zaps sales teams use daily:

1. Form Submissions → Pipedrive Leads

Automatically create new Pipedrive leads whenever someone submits a contact form on your website. This ensures no potential customer falls through the cracks.

2. Email Opens → Pipedrive Activities

When prospects open your sales emails, log this engagement as an activity in Pipedrive to track their interest level.

3. Deal Stage Changes → Slack Alerts

Notify your sales team in Slack when important deals move to new stages like "Contract Sent" or "Closed Won".

Troubleshooting Tips

If your Pipedrive connection stops working, first try re-authenticating in Zapier (remove and re-add the connection). Most issues stem from expired credentials or password changes. For zaps that fail occasionally, check that:

  • Field mappings between apps still match (Pipedrive sometimes updates field names)
  • You haven't exceeded your Zapier task limit for the month
  • Required custom fields in Pipedrive are included in your zap

For complex issues, Zapier's detailed error logs (available on paid plans) show exactly where failures occur. The platform also offers email support on all plans.
