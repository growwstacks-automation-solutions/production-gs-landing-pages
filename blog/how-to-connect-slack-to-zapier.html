Why Connect Slack to Zapier?

Most teams use Slack as their communication hub, but valuable messages often get stuck there. Without automation, you're forced to manually copy information between Slack and other tools like your CRM, project management system, or email. This creates:

  • Delayed responses when you can't immediately transfer messages
  • Missed opportunities as important requests fall through the cracks
  • Frustrating repetition of typing the same information in multiple places

By connecting Slack to Zapier, you create a bridge between conversations and action. Messages automatically trigger the right follow-ups in other systems, keeping work flowing smoothly.

Teams using Slack-Zapier automations save 3-5 hours per week by eliminating manual message handling and reducing context switching between apps.

Step 1: Log Into Zapier

Start by logging into your Zapier account at zapier.com. If you don't have an account yet, you'll need to create one (the free plan works for basic Slack automations).

Once logged in, you'll land on your Zapier dashboard. This is your control center where you'll manage all automations. For security, never share your login credentials or display them on screen during recordings.

Step 2: Access App Connections

In Zapier, click on "My Apps" or "App Connections" in the left sidebar (the exact wording may vary slightly with interface updates). This section stores all your connected apps.

Think of app connections as bridges between services. Each connection you authorize gives Zapier permission to securely interact with that app on your behalf. You can view, edit, or remove connections here at any time.

Step 3: Add New Connection

Click the "Add Connection" button (usually near the top right). This tells Zapier you're ready to link a new app to your account.

You'll see either a search box or a list of popular apps. The search function helps quickly find among Zapier's 5,000+ supported apps. This is where you'll begin the Slack connection process.

Step 4: Search and Select Slack

Type "Slack" into the search bar and select it from the results. Zapier maintains individual connectors for each app, ensuring proper functionality.

When you click Slack, Zapier initiates a secure OAuth flow. This means you'll be redirected to Slack's own authorization page rather than entering credentials directly in Zapier - a safer approach that also allows you to revoke access later if needed.

Step 5: Authorize Slack

The authorization screen will ask you to log into Slack (if you're not already) and select which workspace to connect. Be sure to choose the correct workspace if you're part of multiple teams.

You'll then see the permissions Zapier requests - typically the ability to read channels and post messages. These permissions enable the automations you'll build. After approving, you'll be redirected back to Zapier where Slack will now appear as a connected app.

What to Automate Next
