Zapier Basics: Triggers and Actions

Zapier automations (called Zaps) consist of two core components. The trigger is the event that starts your workflow - in our case, a new Squarespace form submission. The action is what happens next - adding that submission to Google Sheets.

Think of it like dominoes: the trigger knocks over the first piece (your form being submitted), and the action is the last piece falling (data appearing in Sheets). Between them, you can add filters, formatting steps, or conditional logic to customize the flow.

Step 1: Set Up Your Squarespace Trigger

Log into Zapier and click "Create Zap." Search for "Squarespace" as your trigger app and select "New Form Submission" as the event. Zapier will prompt you to connect your Squarespace account - make sure you're logged into the correct site if you manage multiple.

Step 1.1: Account Connection

Click "Sign in to Squarespace" and authorize Zapier to access your form data. This secure connection only allows Zapier to read submissions - it can't edit your site. If you don't see your form listed, try submitting a test entry on your live site first.

Step 1.2: Form Selection

Choose the specific form you want to automate from the dropdown. Pro tip: Name your forms clearly in Squarespace (like "Contact - Main" or "Webinar Signup") to avoid confusion later. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, we show how to identify forms with similar names.

Step 1.3: Test Your Trigger

Zapier will attempt to fetch a recent submission. If none appear, submit your form with test data, then click "Test Trigger" again. Seeing real data here ensures your field mapping will work correctly in the next step.

Troubleshooting tip: If your form uses conditional logic (showing/hiding fields based on answers), make sure your test submission includes all possible field variations to map them properly.

Step 2: Connect Google Sheets Action

Now that Zapier knows when forms are submitted, we'll tell it what to do with that information. Search for "Google Sheets" as your action app and select "Create Spreadsheet Row." Connect your Google account (the same one where your target sheet lives).

Step 2.1: Spreadsheet Setup

Choose your destination spreadsheet and worksheet from the dropdowns. For new sheets, create columns matching your form fields (Name, Email, Message, etc.) beforehand. Zapier can add columns on the fly, but starting with structure prevents messy data.

Step 2.2: Field Mapping

This critical step matches Squarespace fields to Sheet columns. Click each dropdown to pair them correctly. Zapier shows sample data from your trigger test, making verification easy. For custom fields, add descriptive column headers like "Project Budget" instead of generic "Field 3."

Step 2.3: Final Test

Run a test action to confirm data flows correctly to Sheets. Check that all fields appear in the right columns with proper formatting. If dates or numbers look wrong, use Zapier's Formatter tool to adjust them before the data reaches Sheets.

Pro tip: Add a "Submission Date" column mapped to Zapier's timestamp. This creates an automatic audit trail showing exactly when each inquiry arrived.

Field Mapping Tips for Clean Data

Poor field mapping creates spreadsheet chaos. Follow these -tested practices to maintain organized records:

  • Standardize formats: Use Zapier's Formatter to ensure consistent capitalization (Title Case for names), date formats (MM/DD/YYYY), and phone numbers
  • Handle multi-answer fields: For checkboxes selecting multiple options, choose "Separator: Comma" to keep answers in one cell
  • Add source tracking: Include a column with the form name (helpful if routing multiple forms to one sheet)
  • Prepare for empty fields: Map optional form fields anyway - they'll simply leave blank cells when unanswered

At 4:30 in the video, we demonstrate how to handle a complex form with 15+ fields while keeping the spreadsheet usable. The key is logical grouping - cluster related fields together in your Sheet just like they appear on your form.

Testing and Activating Your Zap

Before going live, validate your automation end-to-end:

  1. Submit test forms: Use realistic data (not "asdf") to simulate actual submissions
  2. Check Sheet updates: Verify all fields populate correctly within 10-15 seconds
  3. Test edge cases: Try leaving required fields blank, using long responses, or special characters
  4. Review Zap history: Zapier logs each run - check for errors or timeouts

Once confirmed, toggle your Zap to "On." Monitor it for the first few days, especially during high-traffic periods. Zapier's free plan processes tasks every 15 minutes, while paid plans offer near-instant triggers.

Monitoring tip: Enable Zapier notifications to alert you if the Zap fails. Common issues include expired Google auth or Squarespace form changes that break field mappings.

Advanced Options and Alternatives

Once comfortable with the basics, enhance your automation:

  • Multi-step Zaps: After adding to Sheets, automatically send confirmation emails or create CRM contacts
  • Filters: Only process submissions meeting criteria (like budget over $10k)
  • Paths: Route different form responses to separate Sheets (e.g., by service type)
  • Make.com alternative: For complex workflows, Make's visual builder offers more customization

Remember: Start simple. Get your basic form-to-Sheet flow working perfectly before adding complexity. Most businesses find the core automation handles 80% of their needs without advanced features.
