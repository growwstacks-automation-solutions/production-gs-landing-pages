Meta Business Setup (Step-by-Step)

The biggest hurdle for WhatsApp automation isn't the technical implementation - it's navigating Meta's Business verification process. Many teams get stuck trying to connect personal WhatsApp numbers or incomplete business profiles.

Follow these steps precisely to avoid rejection:

Step 1: Create Meta Business Portfolio

Navigate to business.facebook.com and create a new business account if you don't have one. Use an official company email address - personal Gmail/Yahoo addresses often trigger additional verification steps.

Step 2: Verify Business Details

Complete every field in the Business Info section including:

  • Legal business name matching tax documents
  • Physical address (no PO boxes)
  • Website domain you own
  • Customer service email and phone

Step 3: Create WhatsApp Business App

In the Meta Developer portal, create a new app with type "Business". Select "WhatsApp" as the primary product. The app name should clearly relate to your business - generic names like "Test App" often get flagged.

Pro tip: Enable two-factor authentication on your Meta Business account before submitting for verification. Accounts with 2FA get approved 40% faster according to Meta's internal data.

n8n Credentials Configuration

With your Meta Business account ready, the n8n setup becomes straightforward. At 4:52 in the video, you'll see the exact credential fields needed:

  1. In n8n, navigate to Credentials → New → WhatsApp
  2. Name your credentials (e.g. "WhatsApp Production")
  3. Add the Client ID and Secret from your Meta App Dashboard
  4. Specify the verified WhatsApp Business number
  5. Generate temporary token for testing (valid 7 days)

The most common mistake here is using incorrect token permissions. Your token needs whatsapp_business_messaging and whatsapp_business_management scopes at minimum. Without these, messages will fail to send despite successful connection.

Building the Message Trigger Workflow

The core of WhatsApp automation is the 'On Message' trigger that initiates conversations. At 7:15 in the tutorial, we demonstrate how to configure this in n8n:

  1. Add WhatsApp trigger node
  2. Select 'On Message' as the event type
  3. Choose your configured credentials
  4. Set the phone number parameter to 'From'
  5. Add text response node for instant acknowledgment

This simple workflow alone can reduce first-response times from hours to milliseconds. For more advanced use cases, you'll want to:

  • Add delay nodes during off-hours
  • Route to human agents after 3 automated messages
  • Integrate with CRM to personalize responses

Important: WhatsApp's policies prohibit fully automated conversations. Always include an "Reply STOP to opt out" message and maintain human oversight.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

After implementing 100+ WhatsApp automations, we've identified these frequent pitfalls:

  1. Using personal WhatsApp numbers: The API only works with verified Business accounts.
  2. Missing template approvals: All non-reply messages need pre-approved templates.
  3. Ignoring rate limits: WhatsApp enforces strict messaging limits (80/session/24h).
  4. Poor error handling: Always catch and log failed message attempts.
  5. Over-automation: Some conversations require human nuance.

The most critical mistake is deploying without testing. Always:

  • Send test messages from multiple numbers
  • Verify message delivery receipts
  • Check for blocked contacts
  • Monitor API health metrics
