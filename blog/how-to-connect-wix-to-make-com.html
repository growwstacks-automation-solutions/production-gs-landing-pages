The solution? Webhooks - a simple technology that lets platforms talk to each other automatically. When connected properly, your Wix site can instantly trigger actions in Make.com whenever specific events occur. No more manual data entry, no more missed notifications, and no more frustrated customers waiting for responses.

Businesses using Wix-Make.com automation save an average of 8 hours per week on manual data transfer tasks. That's over 400 hours annually that can be reinvested in growth initiatives instead of administrative work.

Step 1: Create Webhook in Make.com

Webhooks act as digital messengers between platforms. Creating one in Make.com establishes an endpoint where Wix can send data whenever specific events occur.

Log into your Make.com account and create a new scenario. Select the "Webhooks" trigger from the available options, then choose "Custom Webhook." Name your webhook something descriptive like "Wix Form Submissions" and click "Create Webhook." Make.com will generate a unique URL - copy this to your clipboard as you'll need it in the next step.

Pro Tip: Create separate webhooks for different event types (forms, purchases, signups) to keep your workflows organized and easier to troubleshoot.

Step 2: Set Up Wix Automation

In your Wix dashboard, navigate to the Automations section. This powerful but often overlooked feature lets you create rules that trigger actions based on site events.

Click "Create Automation" and select the event that should trigger your workflow. Common options include "Form Submitted," "New Purchase," or "Member Signed Up." Each trigger type will show different configuration options - for forms, select which specific form should activate the automation.

Step 3: Connect the Webhook

With your Wix automation trigger configured, it's time to connect it to Make.com. Under "Then do this," select "Integrations" and choose "Send HTTP Request."

Set the method to POST and paste your Make.com webhook URL into the designated field. Wix will automatically include all relevant event data in the request body. Click "Apply" to save your settings, then activate the automation using the toggle at the top right of your screen.

Important: Always test new automations before going live. Make.com's testing tools let you verify data structure and workflow logic without affecting real customers.

Step 4: Test Your Connection

Before relying on your new automation, verify everything works as expected. Submit a test form or make a test purchase on your Wix site.

In Make.com, check that the webhook received the data correctly. The platform will show you the raw data structure, which you'll use to build your workflow. Common issues to check for include missing fields, incorrect data types, or authentication errors.

Step 5: Build Your Workflow

With data flowing successfully from Wix to Make.com, you can now build powerful automations. Common first steps include:

  1. Data parsing: Extract specific values from the webhook payload
  2. Validation: Check for required fields or format data correctly
  3. Routing: Send different data types to appropriate systems
  4. Notifications: Alert your team about important events
  5. CRM updates: Add new leads or customers to your database

Remember to save and activate your scenario in Make.com after building your workflow. The platform will now automatically process every webhook sent from your Wix site.
