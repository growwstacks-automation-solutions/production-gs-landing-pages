Why Automate Wix with Zapier?

Manual data entry between your Wix website and other business tools wastes valuable time and introduces errors. A 2025 study showed small businesses lose an average of 8 hours per week on repetitive digital tasks that could be automated.

Zapier bridges this gap by connecting Wix with thousands of apps through simple "if this then that" workflows called Zaps. When configured properly, these automations run silently in the background while you focus on growing your business.

Key benefit: Zapier handles the technical integration so you don't need to hire a developer or learn API coding. The visual interface lets anyone create powerful automations in minutes.

Step 1: Create Your Zapier Account

Getting started with Zapier takes less than 2 minutes. The platform offers a free plan that's perfect for testing basic automations before committing to a paid subscription.

How to set up your account:

  1. Visit zapier.com
  2. Click "Sign Up" (you can use Google or email)
  3. Select your role (e.g., "Business Owner")
  4. Choose your company size
  5. Select apps you currently use (helps personalize recommendations)
  6. Click "Continue" to access your dashboard

Pro Tip: Always use the same email for Zapier that you use for your Wix account. This prevents authentication issues later when connecting the two platforms.

Step 2: Enable Wix Automations

Before Zapier can access your Wix data, you need to enable automations in your Wix dashboard. This is a one-time setup that takes about 3 minutes.

Step-by-step instructions:

  1. Log in to your Wix account at manage.wix.com
  2. From the left sidebar, click "Settings"
  3. Scroll down to "Developer Tools" (may be under "Advanced Settings")
  4. Click "Add Custom Code"
  5. Navigate to "Automations"
  6. Toggle automations to "On"
  7. Save your changes

At the 1:45 mark in our video tutorial, you'll see exactly where to find these settings in the Wix interface.

Step 3: Connect Wix to Zapier

With automations enabled in Wix, you're ready to establish the connection in Zapier. This authorization process ensures secure data transfer between the platforms.

Connection process:

  1. In Zapier, click "Create Zap"
  2. Search for "Wix" in the trigger app selection
  3. Choose "Wix Automations" (not manual webhooks)
  4. Click "Sign in to Wix"
  5. Authorize the connection when prompted
  6. Repeat for any additional apps in your workflow

Important: Always test each step of your Zap before turning it on. This catches configuration errors early and prevents data mishaps.

Step 4: Create Your First Zap

Now for the exciting part - building your first automation! Let's create a simple Zap that sends new Wix form submissions to a Google Sheet.

Example workflow setup:

  1. Trigger: "New Form Submission" in Wix
  2. Action: "Create Spreadsheet Row" in Google Sheets
  3. Map Wix form fields to spreadsheet columns
  4. Test with a sample submission
  5. Turn on the Zap when tests succeed

This basic automation eliminates manual data entry while ensuring no leads fall through the cracks. As you become comfortable with Zapier, you can build more complex multi-step Zaps.

Troubleshooting Tips

While Wix-Zapier connections are generally reliable, occasional hiccups occur. These tips will help you diagnose and resolve common issues:

Common Problems and Solutions:

  • Authentication errors: Reconnect Wix in your Zapier account settings
  • Missing data: Check field mappings in your Zap configuration
  • Delayed triggers: Upgrade to a paid Zapier plan for faster processing
  • Failed tasks: Review Zapier's task history for error details
