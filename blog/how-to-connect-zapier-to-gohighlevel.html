Zapier + GoHighLevel Basics

Zapier connects GoHighLevel to thousands of other apps through automated workflows called Zaps. Each Zap consists of a trigger (the event that starts the automation) and one or more actions (what happens after the trigger). For our email automation, the trigger will be an event in GoHighLevel, and the action will be sending a Gmail message.

While GoHighLevel has its own automation features, Zapier provides three key advantages: integration with apps outside GoHighLevel's ecosystem, more advanced logic and filtering options, and the ability to create multi-step workflows that connect several apps together.

Setting Up Your Trigger

Start by creating a new Zap in your Zapier dashboard. In the trigger section, search for "Lead Connector" - this is Zapier's official app for GoHighLevel integration (though it doesn't appear under the GoHighLevel name). Select the trigger event that matches your workflow goal:

Common GoHighLevel Triggers:

  • New Contact: When a new lead enters your CRM
  • Contact Moved Stage: When a lead advances to a new pipeline stage
  • New Appointment: When someone books a meeting
  • Form Submitted: When a contact submits a web form

For our email automation example, we'll use "Contact Moved Stage" to send a customized message whenever a lead reaches your "Qualified" pipeline stage. This ensures timely follow-up exactly when the lead is most engaged.

Connecting GoHighLevel

After selecting your trigger, Zapier will prompt you to connect your GoHighLevel account using an API key. You can generate this key in your GoHighLevel account settings under the API section. Important: Use the API key for the specific location/account you want to automate if you manage multiple businesses.

Once connected, test your trigger to ensure Zapier can access sample data from GoHighLevel. If no data appears, create test activity in your GoHighLevel account that matches your trigger criteria (like moving a test contact to your target pipeline stage), then retest in Zapier.

Security note: Treat your GoHighLevel API key like a password. Never share it in public forums or client communications. If compromised, you can regenerate it in your account settings.

Configuring Gmail Actions

With your trigger working, add an action to your Zap by searching for Gmail. Select "Send Email" as your action event and connect your Gmail account when prompted. You'll then configure the email details using data from your GoHighLevel trigger:

Key Email Fields to Map:

  • To: Map to the contact's email field from GoHighLevel
  • Subject: Include dynamic data like the contact's name or pipeline stage
  • Body: Personalize with relevant details from the trigger event

For our pipeline stage example, you might create a subject like "Next steps for [Contact First Name] - [Pipeline Name]" and reference specific details about their qualification status in the email body. Zapier's dynamic fields let you insert any available data from the trigger into your message.

Testing Your Zap

Before turning your Zap on, always test with real data. Zapier will use your sample trigger data to send a live test email. Check both your sent folder and the recipient's inbox to verify:

  • All dynamic fields populated correctly
  • Email formatting appears as intended
  • Sender information matches your expectations

If anything looks off, adjust your email template in the action step and test again. Once satisfied, name your Zap descriptively (like "GoHighLevel → Gmail: New Qualified Lead") and turn it on. Zapier will now automatically process future matching events.

Advanced Use Cases

While we've focused on a simple one-to-one email automation, Zapier enables much more sophisticated workflows with GoHighLevel. Here are three powerful extensions to consider:

1. Multi-Step Sequences

Add delays between actions to create timed follow-up sequences. For example: send an immediate confirmation when an appointment is booked, then a reminder 24 hours before, and a thank-you message afterward.

2. Conditional Logic

Use Zapier's filters to only send emails when certain conditions are met, like only contacting leads from specific sources or with particular tags.

3. Multi-App Workflows

Combine actions across multiple apps. A single trigger could update a spreadsheet, create a Slack notification, and send both an email and SMS message.

Pro tip: For complex automations involving multiple steps or conditions, consider using Zapier's Paths feature to create branching logic within a single Zap.
