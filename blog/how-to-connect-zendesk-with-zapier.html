Step 1: Access Zapier App Connections

Begin by logging into your Zapier account. If you don't see the left sidebar, click the menu icon in the top-left corner. Navigate to "App Connections" - this is where all your integrated apps are managed.

The App Connections page serves as your integration dashboard. Here you can see which apps are already connected, modify permissions, or add new connections. For Zendesk, we'll be creating a new connection from scratch.

Step 2: Add Zendesk Connection

Click the "Add Connection" button in the top-right corner. In the search bar that appears, type "Zendesk". Select the official Zendesk integration from the dropdown results - be careful not to choose similar-sounding apps.

Zapier will display information about the Zendesk integration capabilities. Review the permissions required - typically this includes reading/writing tickets, users, and organizations. Click "Connect" to proceed to authentication.

Step 3: Authenticate Zendesk

You'll be prompted to enter your Zendesk subdomain (yourcompany.zendesk.com) and admin credentials. For security best practices, we recommend:

  1. Using a dedicated integration user rather than personal admin credentials
  2. Enabling two-factor authentication on your Zendesk account
  3. Restricting API access to Zapier's IP ranges if your plan allows

Once authenticated, test the connection by creating a sample ticket in Zendesk and verifying it appears in Zapier. This confirmation ensures your automations will work as expected.

Common Automation Use Cases

With the connection established, you can now create Zaps (automated workflows) between Zendesk and other apps. The most popular automations include:

  • Ticket to CRM: Create new contacts in HubSpot/Salesforce when tickets are submitted
  • Escalation alerts: Send Slack/Teams messages when high-priority tickets are created
  • Customer feedback: Add NPS survey respondents to a Google Sheets tracker
  • Internal routing: Create Asana tasks when tickets are assigned to specific teams

Pro tip: Start with 2-3 high-impact automations rather than trying to automate everything at once. Measure time savings after 30 days before expanding.
