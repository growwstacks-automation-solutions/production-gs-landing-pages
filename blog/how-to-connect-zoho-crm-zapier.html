Step 1: Access Your Zapier Account

Begin by logging into your Zapier account at zapier.com. If you don't have an account yet, you can sign up for free - the basic plan allows you to create up to 5 Zaps (automated workflows) with limited tasks per month.

Once logged in, you'll land on your Zapier dashboard. This is where you'll manage all your automations. For connecting Zoho CRM, we'll start by creating a new Zap from scratch.

Step 2: Find Zoho CRM in Zapier

From your Zapier dashboard, click on "Explore" in the top navigation, then select "Apps" from the dropdown menu. This brings you to Zapier's app directory where you can search for Zoho CRM.

Type "Zoho CRM" into the search bar at the top of the page. When Zoho CRM appears in the results, click on it to view all available triggers and actions. Zapier maintains direct integrations with thousands of apps, including deep functionality with Zoho CRM's API.

Step 3: Choose Your Trigger Event

After selecting Zoho CRM, you'll need to choose how Zapier should be triggered. This determines what event in Zoho CRM will start your automation. Common trigger options include:

  • New Contact - when a new contact is created in Zoho CRM
  • New Lead - when a new lead enters your pipeline
  • Updated Record - when an existing contact or lead is modified
  • New Module Entry - for custom modules in your Zoho setup

Select the trigger that matches your workflow needs. For most businesses, "New Contact" is the best starting point as it allows you to automate follow-ups whenever someone new enters your CRM.

Step 4: Connect Your Zoho Account

With your trigger selected, Zapier will prompt you to connect your Zoho CRM account. Click the "Sign In" button to begin the authentication process. This will open a new tab where you'll log into your Zoho account and grant Zapier the necessary permissions.

You'll need to select your Zoho domain from the dropdown menu. If you don't see your domain listed, you can search for it manually. This ensures Zapier connects to the correct instance of your Zoho CRM.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated integration user in Zoho rather than your personal account credentials. This maintains security if team members change.

Step 5: Set Permissions

After logging into Zoho, you'll see a permissions screen detailing what access Zapier is requesting. Review these carefully - Zapier typically needs read and write access to your CRM data to function properly.

Click "I Allow" to grant the permissions, then "Accept" to finalize the connection. Zapier will verify the connection and return you to your workflow setup. At this point, your Zoho CRM account is successfully connected to Zapier and ready for automation.
