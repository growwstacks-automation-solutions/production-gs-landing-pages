Getting Started with Verba

Creating your free AI chatbot begins at verba.inc, where you can sign up using your Google or Discord account (1:15 in the video). The registration process takes less than a minute and doesn't require credit card information. Once logged in, you'll see the dashboard where all your bots are managed.

Click "Create Verb" to start building your first chatbot. You'll be prompted to name your bot and provide basic information. Choose a name that reflects your bot's purpose - something like "CommunityHelper" or "FAQBot" works well. The platform guides you through each step with clear instructions.

Creating Your Bot Personality

The magic of Verba lies in how easily you can customize your bot's personality (2:30 in the video). You'll define traits like age, temperament, and response style. Want a professional assistant? A quirky friend? The choice is yours. These settings dramatically affect how your bot interacts with community members.

For the AI model selection, start with the free option unless you need advanced capabilities. The reply style options (normal, crazy, professional) let you fine-tune the tone. The "crazy" setting creates more playful, unpredictable responses - perfect for gaming communities, while "professional" works better for business servers.

Pro Tip: Use another AI like ChatGPT to help generate personality details if you're unsure what to enter. This creates more nuanced and interesting bot behavior from the start.

Connecting to Discord

The most technical step is connecting your Verba bot to Discord (4:50 in the video), but the platform makes it straightforward. You'll need to create a Discord bot application in Discord's developer portal and copy the bot token. Verba provides all necessary links and instructions in their documentation.

When configuring your Discord bot, ensure you enable three critical options: message content intent, server members intent, and presence intent. Missing these will prevent your bot from functioning properly. After pasting the token into Verba and clicking connect, you'll see a success message confirming the integration.

Training Your Bot's Knowledge Base

Verba's training section (7:15 in the video) lets you teach your bot specific information about your community, products, or services. This is where you transform a generic chatbot into a specialized assistant that understands your unique context. The free version allows basic training that significantly improves response quality.

Add common questions and their ideal answers to create a mini-FAQ system. You can also upload documents or provide website links for the bot to reference. The more specific information you provide, the more accurate your bot becomes at handling real user inquiries in your Discord server.

Configuring Memory Settings

Memory management (8:40 in the video) determines how your bot retains and uses conversation context. The free version offers basic memory that allows your bot to maintain context within individual conversations. This creates more natural interactions where the bot "remembers" what was said earlier in the chat.

You can control what information the bot retains and for how long. For privacy-conscious communities, limit memory duration to just the current conversation. More engaged communities might benefit from longer memory spans that create continuity across multiple interactions with the same users.

Testing and Deployment

Before inviting your bot to your main server, test it thoroughly in a private channel (10:20 in the video). Use the invite link generated in Verba to add the bot to your Discord. The first command you'll run activates the bot in your chosen channel - usually something like "/activate [channel-name]".

Start with simple questions to verify basic functionality. Gradually introduce more complex queries to see how the bot responds. Pay attention to both the accuracy of answers and the personality tone you configured. You can return to Verba's dashboard to tweak settings as needed.

Exploring Advanced Features
