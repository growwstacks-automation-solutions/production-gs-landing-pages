Setup Requirements

To build this AI email agent, you'll need just three components:

  1. A Make.com account: Free tier available with limited operations
  2. Google Sheets: Where you'll store your contact list
  3. Gmail or other email service: Configured in Make.com to send messages

At 7:35 in the tutorial, the instructor emphasizes that no prior automation experience is needed. The entire setup uses basic features available in Make.com's free plan, making this an ideal first project for automation beginners.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated Gmail account for sending automated emails to avoid hitting sending limits on your primary account.

Step 1: Google Sheets Trigger Setup

The workflow begins with a trigger that watches your spreadsheet for new contacts. At 9:18 in the video, you'll see how to:

  1. Create a new scenario in Make.com
  2. Add the Google Sheets "Watch new rows" module
  3. Connect your Google account
  4. Select your specific spreadsheet and worksheet

This trigger acts as the starting point for your automation. Whenever a new row appears (like when you add contacts), Make.com will process that data through the rest of your workflow.

The tutorial demonstrates a simple two-column structure with just name and email, but you can expand this to include any relevant contact data for personalization.

Step 2: Email Module Configuration

With the trigger in place, the next step (shown at 14:50) connects the email sending module:

  1. Add the "Send an email" action from Make.com's built-in email app
  2. Configure your sending account (Gmail in the tutorial)
  3. Map spreadsheet fields to the email recipient and content
  4. Create your email template with placeholders for personalization

The beauty of this system is its flexibility. While the tutorial shows a basic text email, you can use HTML templates with images, buttons, and styled formatting. Variables from your spreadsheet (like first name, company, etc.) can be inserted anywhere in the template.

Important: Always test with 2-3 contacts first (as shown at 18:30) before sending to your entire list. Check spam folders to ensure deliverability.

Step 3: Testing Your Workflow

The tutorial demonstrates a crucial testing phase at 19:45 where you:

  1. Run the scenario manually with test data
  2. Verify emails arrive as expected
  3. Check for proper personalization
  4. Review the execution logs in Make.com

This testing process ensures your automation works correctly before scaling up. Make.com provides detailed logs showing exactly what data was processed and any errors encountered - invaluable for troubleshooting.

Once confirmed working, you can set the scenario to run automatically whenever new contacts are added, or schedule batch sends at specific times.

Scaling and Advanced Tips

While the tutorial focuses on basics, several enhancements can take your email agent to the next level:

  • Conditional logic: Send different templates based on contact attributes
  • Delay timers: Space out emails to avoid spam filters
  • CRM integration: Pull data from HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.
  • AI personalization: Connect ChatGPT for dynamic content generation
  • Analytics: Track opens/clicks with services like Mailchimp

The instructor mentions at 20:10 that this basic workflow can be expanded into a complete marketing automation system without writing code. By adding more Make.com modules, you can create sophisticated sequences with follow-ups, surveys, and lead scoring.
