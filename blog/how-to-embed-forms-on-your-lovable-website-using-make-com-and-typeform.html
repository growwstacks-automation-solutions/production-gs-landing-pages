Method 1: Make.com Webhook Forms

Webhooks provide a powerful way to connect your Lovable forms to other applications without complex coding. Here's how to set it up:

Step 1: Create Your Webhook in Make.com

Log into Make.com and create a new scenario. Select "Webhook" as your first module and choose "Custom Webhook". Give it a descriptive name like "Lovable Contact Form" and save it. You'll get a unique webhook URL to use in your Lovable site.

Step 2: Connect to Lovable

In your Lovable project, configure your form to send data to the webhook URL. At the 4:30 timestamp in the video, you can see exactly where to paste this URL in the Lovable form settings.

Pro Tip: Always test your webhook connection before going live. Make.com provides a handy testing feature that lets you verify the data structure coming from your form.

Setting Up Google Sheets Integration

The real power comes when you connect your webhook to other apps. Let's start with Google Sheets for simple data storage:

Step 1: Prepare Your Spreadsheet

Create a Google Sheet with columns matching your form fields (Name, Email, Message, etc.). Make sure to include a Timestamp column to track submission times automatically.

Step 2: Add Google Sheets Module

Back in Make.com, add a Google Sheets module after your webhook. Select "Add a row" as the action, then choose your spreadsheet and worksheet. Map each form field to the corresponding column in your sheet.

At 6:45 in the video, you can see how the test submission appears instantly in Google Sheets, proving the integration works perfectly.

Adding Email Notifications

To ensure you never miss a submission, let's add email notifications:

Step 1: Configure Email Module

Add an "Email" module in Make.com after your Google Sheets step. You can use SMTP (for custom email servers) or connect services like Gmail or Outlook. The video at 9:20 shows the SMTP configuration process in detail.

Step 2: Customize Your Notification

Create a clear subject line like "New Form Submission from Website" and include the key form fields in the email body. You can use HTML formatting for better readability if your email service supports it.

Teams using this method report responding to inquiries 3x faster thanks to instant notifications. You can even route different form types to different team members based on the submission content.

Method 2: Typeform Embeds

For surveys, lead generation forms, or any situation where design matters, Typeform offers beautiful, interactive forms that embed seamlessly into Lovable.

Step 1: Create Your Typeform

Design your form in Typeform's intuitive editor. Add questions, customize the flow with logic jumps, and brand it with your colors and logo. The video at 11:30 demonstrates creating a simple fitness goal survey.

Step 2: Get the Embed Code

Under the Share menu in Typeform, select "Embed in website" and choose the general embed option (not WordPress). Copy the provided iframe code to your clipboard.

At 12:45 in the tutorial, you'll see how to paste this code directly into your Lovable project, placing the form exactly where you want it to appear.

Customizing Your Typeform

Typeform offers several customization options to match your brand:

Design Options

Change colors, fonts, and backgrounds to align with your website. You can even add custom CSS for advanced styling.

Embed Types

Choose between popup, full-screen, or inline embeds depending on your needs. Inline works best for most Lovable implementations.

Response Handling

Set up automatic thank-you messages, redirects after submission, or even connect Typeform to Make.com for advanced automation.

Typeform users see 2-3x higher completion rates compared to standard forms thanks to their conversational design and mobile-friendly interface.
