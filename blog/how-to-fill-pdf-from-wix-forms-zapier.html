Business owners collecting information through Wix forms face a universal frustration: the data sits trapped in your form submissions while you manually transfer it to PDF contracts, applications, or invoices. The solution lies in automation. By connecting Wix to PDF.co through Zapier, you create a seamless pipeline where form submissions automatically populate your PDF templates. This eliminates both the time drain and the error risk, while ensuring clients receive their completed documents instantly.

Manual PDF filling wastes 4.3 hours per week for the average small business processing 50 forms monthly. Automation reduces this to seconds while eliminating 98% of data entry errors.

Step 1: Connect Wix to Zapier

Begin by creating a new Zap in your Zapier account. Select Wix Automations as your trigger app, then choose "New Form Submission" as your trigger event. This tells Zapier to watch for new entries in your Wix form.

At the 1:15 mark in the tutorial video, you'll see how to authenticate your Wix account with Zapier. This one-time connection allows Zapier to access your form submission data. After authentication, select the specific Wix form you want to automate from the dropdown menu.

Testing Your Trigger

Before proceeding, test your trigger by submitting a sample form entry. Zapier will retrieve this test submission, confirming the connection works. This step ensures your automation starts with accurate data.

Step 2: Configure PDF.co Integration

With your trigger working, add PDF.co as your action app. Search for "PDF.co" in Zapier's app directory and select "Fill PDF Document" as your action. You'll need your PDF.co API key, which you can find in your account settings.

Provide the URL of your PDF template stored in Google Drive, Dropbox, or another cloud service. PDF.co will analyze your template and identify all fillable fields. This creates the foundation for mapping your Wix form data to the PDF.

Pro Tip: Use a Google Drive link for your template rather than a direct download URL. This makes future template updates easier without breaking your Zap.

Step 3: Map Your Form Fields

The magic happens when you connect Wix form fields to PDF template fields. In this step, you'll match each piece of form data (like name, email, or address) to its corresponding field in the PDF. PDF.co displays your template's fields in a simple list for easy mapping.

For complex forms, the video at 4:30 demonstrates how to handle checkboxes, radio buttons, and signature fields. These require special mapping but follow the same basic principle: connect the Wix data point to the PDF field designed to receive it.

Field Mapping Best Practices

  • Map required fields first to ensure document completion
  • Use descriptive field names in your PDF template for easier identification
  • Test each mapped field individually before finalizing

Step 4: Test and Activate

With all fields mapped, run a complete test of your workflow. Zapier will process a sample submission through PDF.co and return the filled PDF. Verify every field contains the correct data in the proper location.

Once testing succeeds, activate your Zap. It will now run automatically for every new Wix form submission. Configure where completed PDFs should be saved—common options include emailing to clients, saving to Google Drive, or both.

In summary: 1) Create Zap with Wix trigger → 2) Add PDF.co action → 3) Map fields → 4) Test thoroughly → 5) Activate and enjoy automated PDFs.

Advanced Tips for Complex Forms

For businesses with more sophisticated needs, these pro techniques enhance your automation:

  • Conditional fields: Use Zapier's filter step to only fill certain PDF fields based on form responses
  • Multi-page documents: PDF.co handles contracts and applications spanning multiple pages
  • Auto-naming: Configure unique filenames using form data (e.g., "Contract-[ClientName]-[Date]")
  • Approval workflows: Add a step to route completed PDFs for manager approval before sending
