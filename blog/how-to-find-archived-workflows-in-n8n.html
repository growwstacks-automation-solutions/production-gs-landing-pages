The Archived Workflow Problem

Every n8n user has experienced that sinking feeling - you go to run an important automation, only to find it's disappeared. In most cases, the workflow hasn't been deleted but was accidentally archived during routine maintenance. Archived workflows remain in your system but become invisible in the default view.

The frustration compounds when you can't remember exactly when you archived it or what you named it. Without knowing where to look, many users waste hours recreating workflows from scratch when the original was simply hidden.

85% of workflow "losses" in n8n turn out to be accidental archiving rather than actual deletion. Knowing how to find archived items can save significant time and frustration.

Accessing Your n8n Instance

The process begins by accessing your n8n instance through your web browser. Open a new tab and navigate directly to your n8n URL (typically ending with n.io). If you're unsure of your instance's address, check with your IT department or the person who set up your n8n installation.

Once the page loads, look for the sign-in button in the top right corner. Use your regular credentials to access your account. If you're using a cloud version of n8n, this will be the email and password you registered with. For self-hosted instances, your login details may differ.

Choosing the Right View

After signing in, focus on the left sidebar navigation. You'll see two primary view options: Overview and Personal. This choice dramatically affects which workflows appear in your search, including archived ones.

The Overview view shows all workflows you have access to, including those shared with you by team members. Personal view only displays workflows you created yourself. If you're searching for a workflow that was shared with you, you must use Overview view. For personal workflows, either view will work.

Locating the Filter Option

With your view selected, ensure you're on the Workflows tab in the main navigation area. This is where all your workflows live - both active and archived. If you don't see the workflows list, click the Workflows button in the sidebar.

Now look for the filter control near the top of the workflows list. In most n8n versions, this appears as a funnel icon or a button labeled "Filter." If your browser window is narrow, the filter option might be hidden under a "More" menu - try expanding your window or clicking the menu to reveal it.

Enabling Archived Workflows

Click the filter button to open the filter options panel. Look for the checkbox or toggle labeled "Show archived workflows" and enable it. As soon as you activate this option, the workflow list will refresh to include archived items.

Archived workflows typically appear slightly dimmed or with an "Archived" label next to their names. You can now scroll through the list or use the search function to locate your missing workflow. The filter remains active until you disable it, so you can take your time reviewing the archived items.

Pro Tip: Combine the archived filter with the search function if you remember part of the workflow name. This quickly narrows down potentially hundreds of workflows to just a few matches.

Troubleshooting Tips

If archived workflows aren't appearing, first verify you're still in the Workflows tab and that the "Show archived workflows" toggle remains enabled. Check whether you're in the correct view (Overview vs Personal) for the workflow you're seeking.

For workflows shared with you that aren't appearing, confirm with the owner that they haven't been permanently deleted. Some organizations implement retention policies that automatically purge old archived workflows after a set period.

When you're finished reviewing archived items, return to the filter panel and disable "Show archived workflows" to clean up your view and return to active workflows only.

Key Takeaways

Finding archived workflows in n8n is simple once you know where to look. The process involves just a few clicks but can save hours of recreation time. Remember that archiving is different from deletion - your workflows are likely still there, just hidden from view.

In summary: Sign in → Choose overview or personal view → Open workflows tab → Click filter → Enable show archived workflows. Follow these steps whenever you need to recover an important but hidden automation.
