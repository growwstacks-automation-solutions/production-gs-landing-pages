The PandaDoc ID Problem

Businesses using PandaDoc often struggle with document tracking and management. Every contract, proposal, or agreement gets assigned unique identifier - but finding and tracking these IDs manually creates unnecessary administrative overhead.

Without automation, teams waste time:

  • Manually copying IDs from browser URLs
  • Maintaining spreadsheets of document references
  • Risking errors from incorrect ID transcription

Solution: By extracting the PandaDoc ID directly from the URL and automating its capture with Zapier, you eliminate all manual steps while ensuring 100% accuracy in your document tracking.

Locating the ID

The PandaDoc document ID is cleverly hidden in plain sight. When viewing any document your PandaDoc account, simply look at the browser's address bar:

  1. Open the document you want to track
  2. Look in the URL after "editor/"
  3. The following string of characters is your unique document ID

This ID remains constant throughout the document's lifecycle, making it perfect for automation tracking purposes.

Zapier Trigger Setup

Zapier acts as the bridge between PandaDoc and your destination system (like Google Sheets). The first step is creating the trigger that detects new documents:

  1. Log in to your Zapier account and create new Zap
  2. Search for and select "PandaDoc" as your trigger app
  3. Choose "New Document Created" as your trigger event

This configuration tells Zapier to watch for new documents appearing in your PandaDoc account.

Connecting Your Accounts

For Zapier to access your PandaDoc data, you'll need to establish a secure connection between the two platforms:

  1. Click "Continue" after setting up your trigger setup
  2. Select your PandaDoc account from the dropdown (or add new connection)
  3. Follow the authentication prompts to authenticate Zapier's access

Pro Tip: Zapier will automatically pull a sample document to understand the data structure. This sample crucial for the next mapping step.

Mapping the ID Field

With your accounts connected and sample data available, you can now map the PandaDoc ID to your destination system:

  1. In your Zap's action step, select your destination app (like Google Sheets in our example)
  2. Choose "Create Spreadsheet Row" as the action
  3. Connect your Google account if needed
  4. Locate the PandaDoc ID field in the sample data
  5. Map it to the appropriate column in your Google Sheet

You can repeat this process for other document metadata like title, status, or date.

Google Sheets Integration

Google Sheets serves as perfect log for your automated document tracking purposes:

  1. Select your destination Google Sheet
  2. Choose the worksheet where want to record document IDs
  3. Map the PandaDoc ID to your chosen column
  4. Optionally map other useful fields like creation date, client name, etc.)

Each new PandaDoc document will now automatically appear in your with its complete with its unique identifier.

Testing Your Automation

Before activating your Zap, it's crucial to test the workflow:

  1. Click "Test & Review" in Zapier
  2. Zapier will send sample data through your workflow
  3. Check your Google Sheet for the new row
  4. Verify the PandaDoc ID matches the test document

Tip: Create a test document in PandaDoc first to ensure your workflow captures real-world data accurately.
