Why OAuth Tokens Expire (And Why It Matters)

OAuth token expiration is one of the most common - and most frustrating - reasons Make.com automations fail. These security tokens typically expire every 30-90 days depending on the connected service's policies. Google, Microsoft, Salesforce and other major platforms automatically revoke tokens periodically as a security measure.

When tokens expire, your carefully crafted automations stop working instantly. Critical workflows like customer onboarding sequences, inventory updates, and marketing campaigns grind to a halt. The average business loses 4-6 hours of productivity per expired token while troubleshooting and reconnecting services.

Key insight: OAuth expiration isn't a Make.com bug - it's a security feature designed by the apps you're connecting to (Google, Slack, etc.). The tokens must refresh periodically to maintain secure access.

How to Identify an Expired Token Error

Make.com clearly flags OAuth token issues in your scenario dashboard. Look for these telltale signs at 0:42 in the video tutorial:

  • Red warning icon next to a module in your scenario
  • "Authentication failed" or "OAuth token expired" error messages
  • Failed executions in your scenario history with 401 or 403 error codes

The most reliable indicator is hovering over the red icon in your scenario editor - it will specifically mention OAuth or authentication problems. This differs from other errors like rate limits or invalid data formats.

Step-by-Step Fix for Make.com OAuth Token Errors

Follow this proven 4-step process to restore your automations when OAuth tokens expire:

Step 1: Turn Off the Affected Scenario

Navigate to your Make.com dashboard and locate the failing scenario. Click the toggle switch to turn it off temporarily (shown at 1:15 in the video). This prevents repeated failed executions while you fix the issue.

Step 2: Locate the Expired Connection

Open your scenario editor and look for modules displaying the red warning icon. Click the module to view its settings - the connection field will show an "Update" or "Reconnect" button if the OAuth token has expired.

Step 3: Reauthorize the Connection

Click "Reconnect" and follow the OAuth flow prompts from the connected service (Google, Slack, etc.). You'll need to log in to the original account that authorized the connection and grant permissions again. This process generates a fresh token.

Step 4: Test and Reactivate

After reconnecting, save the module and run a manual test execution (right-click the module and select "Run this module only"). If successful, reactivate your scenario and monitor the next scheduled execution.

How to Prevent Future OAuth Token Issues

While you can't stop tokens from expiring completely, these strategies will minimize disruptions:

  • Weekly connection checks: Set a calendar reminder to review Make.com connections every Monday
  • Notification setup: Configure email alerts for authentication failures in Make.com settings
  • Centralized access: Use a shared business account (not personal) for critical connections
  • Documentation: Maintain a list of all connected services with admin contacts
