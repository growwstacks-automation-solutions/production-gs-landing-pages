Why Make.com Delays Stop Working

Timing is everything in automation. When your Make.com scenarios don't execute on schedule, it can disrupt entire business processes - from missed customer follow-ups to delayed order processing. The frustration is real when you've built what should be a perfectly timed workflow, only to find it running hours or days late.

The good news? Most delay issues stem from just a few common configuration mistakes that are easily fixed once you know where to look. At the 0:45 mark in the video tutorial, we see how simple misconfigurations in time units (minutes vs hours) can completely throw off your automation timing.

85% of timing issues can be resolved by properly configuring just three settings: delay module units, scenario activation status, and scheduling options.

Step 1: Check Your Delay Module Settings

The first place to look when troubleshooting timing issues is your delay module configuration. Many users accidentally set delays in hours when they meant minutes, or vice versa. This simple mistake can make your automation run 60 times faster or slower than intended.

Navigate to your scenario in Make.com and examine each delay module. Verify that:

  • The time unit (minutes/hours/days) matches your intended delay
  • The numerical value is correct
  • The module is properly connected to other steps in your workflow

At 1:15 in the video, we demonstrate how to spot and correct these common configuration errors that silently sabotage your automation timing.

Step 2: Verify Scenario Activation

It sounds obvious, but one of the most frequent causes of timing issues is simply forgetting to activate the scenario after making changes. Make.com requires explicit activation for any configuration changes to take effect.

Check the activation toggle at the top of your scenario page. If it's grayed out, your scenario isn't running. Click the toggle to activate it (it should turn blue). Then save your changes to ensure they're applied.

Pro Tip: Sometimes simply toggling the scenario off and on again can resolve timing issues by forcing Make.com to reinitialize the workflow.

Step 3: Review Scheduling Options

Make.com offers powerful scheduling options that can override or conflict with your delay settings. If your scenario has specific scheduling configured, it might be preventing your delays from executing as expected.

Navigate to your scenario's scheduling settings (shown at 2:30 in the video) and verify:

  • The timezone is correct for your location
  • Any start/end times align with when you want the scenario to run
  • Frequency settings aren't conflicting with your delay intervals

For most timing-sensitive automations, it's best to leave scheduling options at their default "Run scenario immediately" setting and control timing through your delay modules.

Step 4: Test Individual Modules

When troubleshooting complex timing issues, isolate the problem by testing modules individually. Make.com allows you to run specific portions of your scenario to verify they execute as expected.

Right-click on a delay module and select "Run this module only." Observe whether it triggers after the specified delay. This helps identify if the issue is with that specific module or with how it interacts with other parts of your workflow.

At 3:10 in the tutorial, we demonstrate this testing technique that can save hours of frustration when debugging timing problems.

Step 5: Recreate Problem Modules

If a delay module still isn't working correctly after checking settings and testing, try deleting and recreating it. Sometimes modules can become corrupted or stuck in a bad state that prevents proper execution.

To recreate a module:

  1. Delete the problematic delay module
  2. Add a new delay module in its place
  3. Configure it with the same settings
  4. Reconnect it to your workflow
  5. Save and activate the scenario

This "turn it off and on again" approach works surprisingly often for stubborn timing issues in Make.com.

When to Contact Make.com Support

If you've tried all these troubleshooting steps and your scenario still isn't executing on time, it may be time to contact Make.com support. Some timing issues can stem from server-side problems that only their team can diagnose and fix.

When reaching out to support (as shown at 4:20 in the video), be sure to include:

  • Your scenario ID (found in the URL)
  • A detailed description of the timing issue
  • What troubleshooting steps you've already tried
  • Screenshots of relevant module configurations

Make.com's support team can examine server logs to identify if there are any system-side delays affecting your scenario's execution.

Key Takeaways

Properly timed automations are the backbone of efficient business processes. When your Make.com scenarios don't execute on schedule, it can create costly delays and missed opportunities. By methodically checking these key areas, you can identify and fix most timing issues quickly.
