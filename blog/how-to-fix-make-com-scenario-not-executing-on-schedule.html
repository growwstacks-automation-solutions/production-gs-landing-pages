Common Causes of Scheduling Failures

When Make.com scenarios fail to execute on schedule, it often creates a ripple effect of missed deadlines, incomplete tasks, and frustrated teams. The first step to fixing the problem is understanding why it's happening.

Based on hundreds of troubleshooting cases, we've identified the four most frequent culprits behind scheduling failures:

The scheduling switch is turned off: It sounds simple, but this is the #1 reason scenarios don't run. The scheduling toggle might have been accidentally disabled during scenario edits.

Other common issues include timezone mismatches (especially after daylight savings changes), incorrect interval settings, and problems with trigger modules. The good news? All of these are relatively easy to diagnose and fix.

How to Verify Your Scheduling Settings

Start by logging into your Make.com account and navigating to the problematic scenario. At the 0:45 mark in our video tutorial, you'll see exactly where to find the scheduling options.

Follow these steps to verify your settings:

Step 1: Check the scheduling toggle

Look for the scheduling switch in the top corner of your scenario. If it's grayed out or set to "Off," your scenario won't run automatically.

Step 2: Confirm the timezone

Make.com uses your account timezone for scheduling. Verify it matches your actual location - a mismatch of just one hour can cause scenarios to run at the wrong time.

Step 3: Review interval settings

You can specify exact times of day or set regular intervals (like every 30 minutes). Double-check these settings match your intended schedule.

Pro tip: For testing purposes, set a short interval (5-15 minutes) after making changes to quickly verify your fix worked.

Fixing Timezone Mismatches

Timezone issues account for about 40% of scheduling problems we encounter. These often surface after daylight savings time changes or when team members work across different regions.

To fix timezone problems:

  1. Go to your Make.com account settings
  2. Verify your primary timezone under "Profile Settings"
  3. Update if necessary to match your current location
  4. Check each scenario's scheduling settings to ensure they inherit the correct timezone

Remember that some triggers (like webhooks) may have their own timezone settings in connected apps. For mission-critical automations, consider adding a timezone verification step early in your scenario.

Resolving Trigger Module Issues

At the 1:30 timestamp in our video, we demonstrate how to check trigger modules - another common source of scheduling problems. Whether you're using webhooks, RSS feeds, Google Sheets, or email triggers, connection issues can prevent scenarios from running.

Follow this troubleshooting checklist:

  • Reconnect any accounts showing warning icons
  • Verify API keys and permissions haven't expired
  • Check that trigger conditions are still valid (e.g., spreadsheet ranges)
  • Test trigger modules individually using the "Run once" feature

If you're using webhooks, ensure the receiving endpoint is still active and properly configured. A surprising number of scheduling issues trace back to simple connection problems that are easily fixed.

Testing Your Solutions

After making changes, it's crucial to verify your scenario will run as expected. Here's our recommended testing protocol:

Immediate verification

Run the scenario manually using the "Run once" button. This confirms all modules are functioning correctly.

Short-interval testing

Temporarily set your scenario to run every 5-15 minutes. Monitor the execution history to confirm multiple successful runs.

Return to normal schedule

Once verified, restore your original scheduling interval. Consider checking the execution history after the first few automatic runs.

Remember: Make.com may take a few minutes to process scheduling changes. If your scenario doesn't run immediately after updating settings, wait 10-15 minutes before further troubleshooting.

Advanced Scheduling Options

Once you've resolved basic scheduling issues, Make.com offers powerful options to fine-tune your automations:

Day-specific scheduling

Choose specific days of the week for your scenario to run - perfect for weekday-only processes or weekend reporting.

Time windows

Limit executions to certain hours, preventing off-hours notifications or aligning with business hours.

Conditional delays

Add sleep modules to space out actions within a scenario, creating custom intervals between steps.

For complex scheduling needs, consider breaking workflows into multiple scenarios with specialized triggers. This modular approach often proves more reliable than trying to handle everything in one scenario.

Reliable automations require proper scheduling configuration. By following this systematic approach, you can quickly identify and fix the most common Make.com scheduling issues.
