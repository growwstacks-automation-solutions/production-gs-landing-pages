How to Identify Queue Issues

When a Make.com scenario gets stuck, the first sign is typically a yellow queue status indicator on your dashboard. This visual cue means tasks aren't processing as expected. Many users panic when they see this, fearing complex technical issues, but the solution is often simpler than you think.

Start by navigating to your scenario list in Make.com (around 0:45 in the video). Look for any scenarios showing the yellow queue status. If the status isn't immediately visible, check the History or Overview tabs for recent runs - failed operations will appear here with timestamps and error details.

Pro Tip: Make.com maintains queued operations for 7 days before automatically clearing them. However, stuck operations consume resources during this period - it's best to address them immediately.

Checking App Connections

The most common cause of queue issues? Expired app connections. When integrated services like Gmail, Google Sheets, or Airtable update their APIs or require reauthentication, your scenario can grind to a halt.

Click into your stuck scenario and systematically inspect each module (1:10 in the video). Look for warning icons or connection status indicators. Pay special attention to:

  • Google Workspace apps (often require quarterly reauthentication)
  • CRM integrations like HubSpot or Salesforce
  • Database connections (Airtable, MySQL, etc.)

For each connection, verify the app still has valid login credentials and permissions. If you find an expired connection, reauthenticate and test the module individually before proceeding.

Operation Limit Troubleshooting

If connections check out, your next stop should be operation limits (1:50 in the video). Make.com plans include monthly operation quotas - exceeding these will queue new tasks until your billing cycle resets or you upgrade.

Navigate to your account dashboard and check operation usage. If you're nearing or at your limit, you have three options:

  1. Upgrade your plan for higher operation limits
  2. Delete old tasks to free up space (found in History)
  3. Optimize your scenario to use fewer operations

Warning: Free and Pro plans have strict operation limits. If your business relies heavily on automation, consider the Teams or Enterprise plans for more headroom.

Module-by-Module Inspection

With connections and limits verified, conduct a thorough module inspection (2:15 in the video). Click each module in your scenario sequentially, watching for:

  • Red error indicators or warning symbols
  • "Auto-retry" options in the wrench menu
  • Unusual processing times in the History tab

The problematic module often has a wrench icon with retry options available. Common culprits include:

  • Filters with outdated conditions
  • Webhooks awaiting responses
  • Rate-limited API calls
  • Data format mismatches between apps

For each issue found, make the necessary adjustments and test the module in isolation using the "Run once" button before reactivating the entire scenario.

Implementing Error Handling

Prevent future queue issues by building robust error handling (2:40 in the video). Make.com allows you to configure routes that activate when modules fail, keeping your workflow moving despite individual errors.

For critical modules:

  1. Click the wrench icon on the module
  2. Select "Error Handling"
  3. Define alternative actions or notifications

Effective error handling might include:

  • Slack alerts for operations teams
  • Fallback actions when primary modules fail
  • Automatic retries with increasing delays

Best Practice: Implement error handling during initial scenario design - retrofitting is more difficult. Consider it essential infrastructure, not optional polish.

Testing Your Fixes

After addressing potential issues, test your fixes methodically (3:00 in the video). Never assume a scenario will work - verify each change with these steps:

  1. Run the scenario once manually using the "Run once" button
  2. Monitor the execution in real-time via the History tab
  3. Check for new errors in previously working modules
  4. If successful, reactivate scheduling with the toggle switch

For complex scenarios, consider testing in phases:

  1. First half of modules only
  2. Then the full workflow with dummy data
  3. Finally, with live data at reduced volume

This phased approach helps isolate any remaining issues without overwhelming your systems or operation limits.

Clearing Stuck Operations

Even after fixing root causes, you may need to clear stuck operations manually (3:20 in the video). Make.com continues retrying queued tasks unless explicitly cancelled, consuming resources unnecessarily.

To clear the queue:

  1. Navigate to the scenario's History tab
  2. Identify waiting or retrying operations
  3. Select "Cancel" or "Delete" for each stuck task

For bulk operations:

  • Use the batch selection checkbox
  • Apply filters to target only stuck operations
  • Consider exporting logs before deletion for audit purposes

After clearing, monitor new operations to ensure your fixes worked as intended. Persistent queueing suggests undiscovered issues requiring deeper investigation.

Key Takeaways

Make.com scenarios get stuck in queue for predictable, solvable reasons. With systematic troubleshooting, you can identify and resolve these issues quickly, minimizing automation downtime.

Remember: Always check connections first, verify operation limits second, and implement error handling to prevent future queueing. Regular scenario maintenance prevents most issues before they impact your business processes.

For mission-critical automations, consider scheduling monthly checkups to reauthenticate connections, review error logs, and optimize operation usage. This proactive approach keeps your workflows running smoothly.
