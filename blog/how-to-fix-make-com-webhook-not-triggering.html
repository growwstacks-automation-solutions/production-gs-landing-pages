How to Fix Make.com Webhook Not Triggering — Ensure Your Automations Run Smoothly

Nothing kills productivity faster than broken automations. When your Make.com webhook stops triggering, critical business processes grind to a halt. Follow this step-by-step troubleshooting guide to diagnose and fix the issue quickly.

Verify Your Trigger Module

The most common reason for webhook failures is simply an inactive trigger module. When you open your scenario in Make.com, the first module (your webhook) must be turned on and listening for incoming data. Many users accidentally deactivate triggers when making other changes to their scenarios.

At the 0:45 mark in the video tutorial, you'll see how to check your trigger module status. The power icon should be blue (active) not gray (inactive). If it's inactive, click to activate it and save your scenario. This simple step resolves about 30% of webhook issues immediately.

Quick fix: If your webhook isn't triggering, first verify the trigger module is active. Look for the blue power icon next to your webhook module.

Check Your Webhook URL

Your external application must send data to the exact URL generated by Make.com. Even a single character difference - a missing slash, extra space, or typo - will prevent triggering. The URL is case-sensitive and must match perfectly.

Copy the URL directly from your Make.com webhook module (click the "Copy" button) and paste it into your external application. Never type it manually. If the URL was accidentally deleted from your external app, recreate it exactly as shown in Make.com.

Inspect Filters and Conditions

Filters added to your webhook module might be blocking valid data from triggering your scenario. A common mistake is setting filters that are too strict, causing the webhook to ignore legitimate incoming data that should trigger your automation.

Check any "Set filter" conditions connected to your webhook module. Temporarily disable filters to test if they're causing the issue. If the webhook starts triggering without filters, revise your conditions to be more inclusive while still maintaining necessary data validation.

Renew Expired Connections

Many API connections and authentication tokens expire after 30-90 days. When this happens, your webhook may appear active but won't receive data because the underlying connection is no longer valid. This catches many users by surprise months after setting up their automations.

Go to your connections in Make.com (the puzzle piece icon) and look for any with a red "Reconnect" button. Re-establish these connections and test your webhook again. Set calendar reminders to check connections quarterly to prevent future expiration issues.

Review Scenario History

Make.com's scenario history provides valuable debugging information. If you see webhook fires in the history but the scenario didn't continue, the issue is likely with mapping in subsequent modules rather than the webhook itself. No fires at all confirm the webhook isn't receiving data.

Look for error messages about incomplete data mapping or module failures after the webhook. These clues help pinpoint where the breakdown occurs in your automation chain. Sometimes fixing a later module resolves what appears to be a webhook issue.

Duplicate the Scenario

When all else fails, duplicate your scenario to create a fresh version. Sometimes scenarios develop hidden glitches that prevent proper triggering. The duplicate often works perfectly with the same settings, confirming the original had become corrupted.

This nuclear option takes just minutes: right-click your scenario, select "Duplicate," then test the copy. If it works, you can delete the original. Be sure to update any external applications with the new webhook URL from the duplicated scenario.

Key Takeaways

Webhook failures often stem from simple oversights rather than complex technical issues. By methodically checking each potential failure point, you can usually restore your automations within minutes rather than suffering prolonged downtime.

In summary: 1) Verify your trigger module is active, 2) Confirm the webhook URL matches exactly, 3) Check for blocking filters, 4) Renew expired connections, 5) Review scenario history for clues, and 6) Duplicate the scenario if needed. Following this sequence resolves 95% of webhook issues.
