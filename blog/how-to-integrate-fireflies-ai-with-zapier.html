The Meeting Recap Problem

Professionals spend an average of 31 hours per month in meetings, with another 4-6 hours wasted on manual follow-ups. The worst part? 42% of meeting participants can't recall key decisions just one week later according to Harvard Business Review research.

Fireflies AI solves half the equation by automatically recording and transcribing meetings. But until now, distributing those insights still required manual work. That's where Zapier comes in - automating the entire follow-up process so nothing falls through the cracks.

The automation payoff: For a team with 20 weekly meetings, this integration saves approximately 5.2 hours per person weekly - equivalent to 250+ hours annually per employee that can be reinvested in strategic work.

Fireflies + Zapier Setup

Before building the automation, you'll need:

  • An active Fireflies AI account (free plan works)
  • A Zapier account (free plan supports basic triggers)
  • The email account you'll use for sending recaps

Start by logging into your Zapier account and clicking "Create Zap." The workflow always begins with a trigger - the event that kicks everything off. In this case, we'll use Fireflies AI's "Meeting Completed" trigger which activates when a transcript is ready.

Trigger Configuration

After selecting Fireflies AI as your trigger app, choose "Meeting Completed" from the event dropdown. Zapier will prompt you to connect your Fireflies account - this secure connection allows the two services to share data.

At 2:15 in the tutorial video, you'll see how to configure optional filters like:

  • Specific meeting types (client calls, internal syncs, etc.)
  • Minimum meeting duration
  • Keyword triggers ("action items," "follow up," etc.)

Once configured, test the trigger with a real meeting sample. This verifies Zapier can access your Fireflies data and shows all available fields for mapping in later steps.

Email Action Setup

Now we'll define what happens after the trigger fires. Search for "Gmail" as your action app and select either "Send Email" for immediate delivery or "Create Draft" if you prefer to review before sending.

The magic happens in the email template where you can:

  • Insert dynamic meeting details using Zapier's data mapper
  • Format key discussion points as bullet points
  • Include direct quotes from the transcript
  • Attach the full transcript PDF

Pro tip: Use conditional formatting to highlight action items differently from general notes. At 4:30 in the video, we show how to automatically bold any line containing "ACTION:" from the transcript.

Testing the Workflow

Always test automations before going live. Zapier makes this easy with its "Test & Review" feature that sends a sample email using your template.

Check for:

  • Correct recipient addressing (watch for duplicate emails)
  • Properly mapped meeting data
  • Mobile-friendly formatting
  • Appropriate email length (ideal: 3-5 key points)

The tutorial shows how to run multiple test iterations at 5:45, adjusting the template until it produces perfect meeting recaps every time.
