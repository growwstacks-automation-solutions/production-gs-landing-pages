Why Connect Notion With Make.com?

Notion has become the central hub for many businesses - storing project data, customer information, and operational knowledge. But when this data lives in isolation, teams waste hours manually transferring information to other tools like CRMs, accounting software, or marketing platforms.

Make.com solves this by acting as an automation bridge between Notion and hundreds of other applications. Once connected, your Notion databases can automatically:

Trigger actions in other apps: When a new row is added to your Notion project tracker, Make.com can create a corresponding task in Asana, send a Slack notification, or add the client to your CRM.

Step 1: Create Your Make.com Scenario

Scenarios are Make.com's term for individual automation workflows. Think of them as recipes that connect your apps together with specific rules and transformations.

To begin:

  1. Log into your Make.com account (formerly Integromat)
  2. Click the "Create a new scenario" button
  3. Select "Build from scratch" for full control

This blank canvas approach gives you flexibility to design exactly the automation your business needs, rather than being limited by pre-built templates.

Step 2: Add the Notion Module

With your scenario created, you'll see a large plus icon in the center of the screen. Clicking this reveals Make.com's app directory with over 1,000 integrations.

For our integration:

  1. Type "Notion" in the search bar
  2. Select the official Notion connector
  3. Choose "Get a specified page" as your starting action

Pro Tip: The "Get a specified page" module works best for initial integrations because it gives you precise control over which Notion content to access. Later, you can explore triggers like "Watch database items" for real-time updates.

Step 3: Authorize the Connection

Before Make.com can access your Notion data, you need to establish a secure connection between the two platforms.

The authorization process:

  1. Click "Create a connection" in the Notion module
  2. Select "Notion Public" as the connection type
  3. Log into your Notion account when prompted
  4. Review and approve the requested permissions

This OAuth flow ensures your credentials stay secure while granting Make.com limited access to specific pages or databases you choose.

Step 4: Map Your Data Fields

With the connection established, you can now select exactly which Notion content to work with and how it should flow to other applications.

Key mapping steps:

  1. Choose your Notion workspace from the dropdown
  2. Select a specific page or database
  3. Map Notion properties to fields in your target app
  4. Add filters if you only want to sync certain records
