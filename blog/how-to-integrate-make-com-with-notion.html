Why Integrate Make.com with Notion?

Most businesses using Notion eventually hit the same wall — their data lives in silos. You might track projects in Notion, customer info in your CRM, and orders in Shopify. Without integration, this means endless copying and pasting between tabs.

The Make.com integration changes everything. Instead of manually transferring data, you can automate the entire flow. New Notion database entries can trigger actions in other apps, while updates from external systems can automatically reflect in your Notion workspace.

Time savings: Businesses using this integration report saving 5+ hours per week previously spent on manual data entry. That's over 250 hours annually — equivalent to 6+ full work weeks.

What You'll Need Before Starting

Before connecting Make.com to Notion, ensure you have these three essentials ready:

  1. A Notion account with admin access to the workspace containing your databases
  2. A Make.com account (free plan works for basic integrations)
  3. Clear objectives for what data needs to flow between systems

Step-by-Step Integration Guide

Step 1: Create a New Scenario in Make.com

Log into Make.com and click "Create Scenario" from your dashboard. This blank canvas is where you'll build your Notion integration.

Step 2: Add Notion as Your First Module

Search for "Notion" in the app selector and choose it as your trigger app. You'll need to authenticate by clicking "Create a connection" and logging into Notion when prompted.

Step 3: Select Your Data Source

Choose whether you want to trigger actions from a specific Notion database or page. The tutorial shows how to select "Database" and pick from your available workspaces.

Step 4: Map Your Fields

This critical step determines which Notion properties transfer to Make.com. The video demonstrates field mapping at 2:30 — pay special attention to matching data types correctly.

Step 5: Test and Activate

Run a test execution to verify data flows correctly before turning on the scenario. The tutorial shows how to interpret test results and troubleshoot any errors.

Powerful Use Cases for This Integration

While the technical setup is straightforward, the real magic happens when you apply this integration to actual business processes. Here are three transformative applications:

CRM Automation: Sync new leads from your website form to a Notion CRM database, then automatically create follow-up tasks and calendar events.

Content Management: Blog posts drafted in Notion can trigger publication workflows in WordPress or social media scheduling tools.

Project Tracking: Connect your Notion project management system to time tracking apps, invoicing software, and team communication platforms.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Even with careful setup, you might encounter occasional hiccups. Here's how to solve the most frequent problems:

Connection Errors: If Make.com can't connect to Notion, first revoke and recreate the connection. Ensure you're using a Notion account with admin privileges.

Missing Data: Fields not appearing? Double-check that your Notion database properties are set to "Visible" and that you've selected the correct view during mapping.

Rate Limits: Notion's API has usage limits. If you hit them, consider batching operations or upgrading your Make.com plan for higher throughput.
