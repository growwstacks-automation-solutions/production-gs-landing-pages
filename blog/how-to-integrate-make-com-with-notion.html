The Manual Data Problem

Businesses using Notion often hit a frustrating wall - their beautifully organized databases require constant manual updates. Every new lead, project update, or content idea means switching between apps and copying information by hand. This creates:

  • Data entry errors (human mistakes in 1 of every 100 manual entries)
  • Information delays (critical updates waiting hours or days to be recorded)
  • Opportunity costs (teams spending 15-25% of their week on admin work)

Automation solves this: A properly configured Make.com integration can reduce Notion database maintenance by 80% while improving data accuracy. The setup takes less time than most teams spend on manual updates in a single week.

Step 1: Access Make.com Scenarios

Scenarios are Make.com's term for automated workflows that connect apps. To begin:

  1. Log into your Make.com account at make.com
  2. Navigate to the main dashboard (shown at 0:32 in the video tutorial)
  3. Locate the "Scenarios" tab in the left sidebar

This interface serves as mission control for all your automations. The same platform that connects Notion can also automate thousands of other apps - but we'll focus specifically on Notion integration for this workflow.

Step 2: Create New Scenario

With the Scenarios tab open:

  1. Click the blue "Create a new scenario" button
  2. Name your scenario descriptively (e.g. "Notion CRM Sync")
  3. You'll enter the visual workflow builder - a canvas where you'll construct the automation

Pro Tip: Make.com offers templates for common Notion integrations. Before building from scratch, check the template library for pre-made workflows matching your use case.

Step 3: Select Notion Action

In the workflow builder:

  1. Click the "+" icon to add your first module
  2. Search for "Notion" in the app selector
  3. Choose between database actions (most common) or page operations

The most powerful automations use the "Create a database item" action. This allows Make.com to add, update, or modify records in your Notion databases based on triggers from other apps.

Step 4: Establish Connection

The critical technical step - linking your Notion workspace:

  1. Click "Add" next to the Connection field
  2. Select connection type (Public or Internal - see FAQ for differences)
  3. Authenticate with your Notion account when prompted
  4. Choose which Notion pages/databases the connection can access

This secure OAuth process ensures Make.com only accesses the Notion data you specify. The connection remains active until you revoke it, enabling ongoing automation without repeated logins.

Step 5: Save and Automate

Finalize your integration:

  1. Map the data fields between Make.com and Notion
  2. Set any filters or conditions for the automation
  3. Click "Save" to store your scenario
  4. Toggle the scenario to "On" to activate it

Your first automated update should process within minutes. Monitor the scenario history tab to verify successful operations and troubleshoot any errors during initial testing.
