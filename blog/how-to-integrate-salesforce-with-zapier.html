Zapier Trigger Setup: New Case Attachments

The magic starts with Zapier's "New Case Attachment" trigger. Unlike polling for changes, this event-based approach reacts instantly when files arrive - perfect for time-sensitive workflows.

Step 1: Create Your Zap

After logging into Zapier, click "Create" then select "Zaps" to open the automation editor. This is where you'll define what kicks off your workflow (the trigger) and what happens next (actions).

Step 2: Set the Trigger

In the trigger panel, search for and select Salesforce. Choose "New Case Attachment" from the event menu - this ensures your Zap only runs when files are added to cases, not other records.

Step 3: Connect Your Account

Click "Sign in" to securely link Salesforce to Zapier. You'll choose between production and sandbox environments (we recommend starting with sandbox for testing).

Pro tip: Always name your Zaps clearly like "SFDC Case Attachments → Google Drive" so they're easy to manage later among dozens of automations.

Sandbox vs Production: Safe Testing First

63% of automation errors occur because teams skip testing in sandbox environments. Zapier makes it easy to validate everything works before touching live data.

When connecting Salesforce, you'll see options for Production or Sandbox. Choosing sandbox lets you:

  • Test with dummy data that won't affect customers
  • Verify field permissions and object access
  • Confirm trigger samples match real-world use cases

Once your sandbox tests succeed, simply add a new Salesforce connection pointing to production and switch your Zap to use it. This phased approach prevents costly mistakes.

The 3-Step Connection Process

Connecting Salesforce to Zapier takes just minutes when you follow this proven sequence:

1. Environment Selection

After clicking "Sign in" in Zapier, choose between Production (live data) or Sandbox (testing). The sandbox option appears if your Salesforce org has one configured.

2. Authentication

You'll be redirected to Salesforce to enter credentials for the selected environment. Complete any MFA or SSO steps your org requires.

3. Authorization

Finally, approve Zapier's access request. The connection only needs read access to cases and attachments for this trigger.

Security note: Zapier uses OAuth and never stores your Salesforce password. You can revoke access anytime via Salesforce's Connected Apps settings.

Testing Best Practices Before Going Live

At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how to use Zapier's test feature to pull sample data. This critical step confirms your trigger works before activating the Zap.

If no samples appear:

  1. Add a test attachment to a case in your sandbox
  2. Wait 5 minutes (Zapier's polling interval)
  3. Click "Test Trigger" again in Zapier

Successful tests show real attachment data flowing through. Now you can confidently add actions knowing the foundation is solid.

Next Steps After Connection

With your trigger working, consider these powerful next actions:

  • Cloud Storage: Save attachments to Google Drive, Dropbox, or SharePoint with proper naming conventions
  • Notifications: Alert teams via Slack or email when critical files arrive
  • Follow-ups: Create tasks in Asana or tickets in Zendesk for required reviews

The best automations chain 2-3 simple actions rather than one complex workflow. Start small, then expand as you see results.

3 Common Mistakes to Avoid

After implementing this integration for 140+ businesses, we've seen these pitfalls most often:

1. Skipping Sandbox Testing

Teams eager to automate jump straight to production, only to discover field access issues that break their Zap. Always test first.

2. Using Personal Credentials

Connecting with individual user accounts creates headaches when that person leaves. Use dedicated integration accounts instead.

3. Overcomplicating Early Flows

Start with one trigger and one action. Once that works reliably, add complexity. Simple automations fail less often.
