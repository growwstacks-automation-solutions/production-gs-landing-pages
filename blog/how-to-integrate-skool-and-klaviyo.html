Why Automate Skool-Klaviyo Integration?

Community builders know the frustration: a new member joins your Skool group, but they never receive your welcome emails because you forgot to manually add them to Klaviyo. Or worse - you spend hours each week copying and pasting email addresses between platforms.

This automation solves both problems by creating a seamless connection between your community platform and email marketing system. When configured correctly (as shown at 1:15 in the video), new members are added to Klaviyo within seconds of joining - ensuring they receive your onboarding sequence and other critical communications.

Time savings: This integration saves community managers 3-5 hours per week on average by eliminating manual data entry. More importantly, it ensures no new members fall through the cracks.

Getting Started: What You'll Need

Before creating your Skool-Klaviyo integration, you'll need three things: a Skool community with members, a Klaviyo account, and a Zapier account (free or paid). The free Zapier plan works for basic integrations, but paid plans offer faster sync times and multi-step zaps.

At 0:45 in the tutorial, you'll see how to access the Zapier interface where we'll build this integration. Make sure you're logged into all three accounts before starting, as you'll need to authenticate each platform during setup.

Finding Your Skool API Key

The first technical step is retrieving your Skool API key, which acts as the secure bridge between Skool and Zapier. As shown at 0:58 in the video, this key is found in your Skool community settings under Plugins > Zapier Integration.

Your API key is unique to your community and should be treated like a password - don't share it publicly. When copying it into Zapier (as demonstrated at 1:30), make sure you include the entire string without any extra spaces.

Zapier Setup: Step-by-Step

With your Skool API key ready, we'll now build the Zapier automation that connects the two platforms. This involves creating a "Zap" with Skool as the trigger and Klaviyo as the action.

Step 1: Create a New Zap

In Zapier, click "Create Zap" and search for Skool as your trigger app. Select either "New Paid Member" or "New Free Member" as your trigger event depending on which type of members you want to sync.

Step 2: Connect Your Skool Account

At 1:45 in the video, you'll see how to authenticate Skool by pasting your API key. Make sure to also enter your Skool community URL in the designated field.

Step 3: Test the Trigger

Zapier will attempt to pull sample data from Skool to verify the connection works. If you have members in your community, this should return test records (as shown at 2:10).

Pro tip: Always test each step of your Zap before proceeding to the next. This catches authentication issues early and ensures your data flows correctly.

Configuring Klaviyo Actions

Now we'll set up what happens in Klaviyo when a new Skool member joins. This involves two main actions: creating a profile and adding them to a specific list.

Action 1: Create Klaviyo Profile

At 2:30 in the tutorial, you'll see how to select Klaviyo as your action app and choose "Create Profile" as the action event. Map the Skool member's email to Klaviyo's email field to ensure proper contact creation.

Action 2: Add to Klaviyo List

The second action (shown at 3:15) adds the new profile to a specific Klaviyo list. This is crucial for triggering welcome sequences and segmenting your audience. You'll need to have created your desired list in Klaviyo beforehand.

Important: Without the list assignment step, new members won't receive any automated emails. Always include this second action unless you have another way to trigger your sequences.

Testing Your Integration

Before turning your Zap live, it's critical to test each step thoroughly. At 3:45 in the video, you'll see how to use Zapier's test mode to verify profiles are being created correctly in Klaviyo.

Check both your Klaviyo profiles section and the specific list you're adding members to. The test should show a new profile appearing within seconds (though it may take longer during actual use depending on your Zapier plan).

Advanced Options & Customizations

Once your basic integration is working, consider these enhancements to make it even more powerful:

  • Dual triggers: Create separate Zaps for paid and free members to send different welcome sequences
  • Additional fields: Map member names or join dates from Skool to custom Klaviyo properties
  • Tags: Apply Klaviyo tags based on membership type or Skool group

These advanced configurations allow for more personalized email communications and better segmentation of your community members.
