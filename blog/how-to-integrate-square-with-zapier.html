Square Triggers You Can Automate

Zapier recognizes these Square events as automation triggers (timestamp 1:42 in the tutorial):

  • New Appointment: Triggers when a booking is created in Square Appointments
  • New Customer: Fires when a customer profile is added to your Square account
  • New Invoice: Activates when an invoice is created (paid or unpaid)
  • New Order: Detects online or in-person orders through Square POS
  • New Payment: Triggers for completed transactions (including partial payments)

Each trigger provides different data fields. Payments include amount and method, while appointments contain service details and customer contact info. Choose the trigger that matches your workflow goal.

Step-by-Step Integration Guide

Step 1: Connect Square to Zapier

Log into Zapier and click "Make a Zap." Search for Square and authenticate your account (shown at 1:15 in the tutorial). You'll need admin access to your Square account.

Step 2: Choose Your Trigger

Select which Square event should start your automation. For our example, we used "New Payment" (timestamp 1:42). Test the connection to ensure Zapier can see your Square data.

Step 3: Set Up the Action

Choose what happens after the trigger. We demonstrated adding rows to Google Sheets (3:08), but you could also send emails, update CRMs, or post Slack messages.

Step 4: Map Data Fields

Tell Zapier which Square information to send where. In our example, we mapped payment amount, date, and customer email to specific spreadsheet columns.

Step 5: Test & Activate

Run a test with real Square data to confirm everything works, then turn on your Zap. Most workflows process new data within minutes.

Troubleshooting: If your test fails at 2:30 in the video, check that you've granted all necessary permissions in Square and that your trigger event actually exists in your account history.

Pro Tips for Reliable Workflows

Basic Zaps work immediately, but these professional techniques prevent issues as your automation scales:

  • Add Filters: Only process payments over $50 or appointments for specific services
  • Use Paths: Create different actions for in-person vs online orders
  • Schedule Checks: Set important Zaps to retry failed actions automatically
  • Add Delays: Wait 1 hour before sending invoice reminders to allow for payments
  • Backup Data: Send critical information to two destinations (e.g., Sheets and Airtable)

For businesses processing 100+ transactions monthly, we recommend adding error handling and logging to monitor automation health. Our managed clients average 99.7% successful execution rates with these safeguards.
