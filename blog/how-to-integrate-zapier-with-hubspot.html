Why Automate HubSpot With Zapier

Manual data entry between HubSpot and other business apps creates bottlenecks that slow down sales teams and marketing operations. According to recent data, sales reps waste 17% of their workweek on repetitive administrative tasks like transferring contact information between systems.

Zapier solves this by creating automated pipelines between HubSpot and your other critical business tools. When configured properly, these integrations can:

  • Sync new HubSpot contacts to your email marketing platform within seconds

Key benefit: Zapier acts as a universal translator between HubSpot and thousands of other apps, eliminating the need for custom API development or expensive middleware solutions.

Setting Up Your Zapier-HubSpot Connection

The first step in automating HubSpot workflows is establishing the connection between your HubSpot account and Zapier. This authorization process ensures Zapier can securely access your HubSpot data while maintaining all security protocols.

Step 1: Logging Into Zapier

Navigate to Zapier.com and log in using your credentials. If you don't have an account, you can start with their free plan which includes basic HubSpot integration capabilities.

Step 2: Creating a New Zap

Click the "Create Zap" button on your dashboard. This initiates the workflow builder where you'll define your automation rules. Zapier refers to these automated workflows as "Zaps."

Step 3: Selecting HubSpot as Your Trigger App

In the trigger section, search for and select HubSpot from Zapier's app directory. This designates HubSpot as the source system that will initiate your automation workflow.

Pro tip: Always use a dedicated integration user in HubSpot rather than your personal admin account when connecting to Zapier. This improves security and makes permission management easier.

Configuring HubSpot Triggers

Triggers are the specific events in HubSpot that will start your automation workflow. Choosing the right trigger ensures your Zap runs exactly when needed without unnecessary activations.

Common HubSpot Triggers

  • New Contact: Triggers when a new person is added to your CRM
  • Updated Deal: Activates when deal properties change
  • Form Submission: Starts when a HubSpot form is submitted
  • Marketing Email Opened: Triggers when a recipient opens your email

After selecting your trigger event, you'll need to authenticate your HubSpot account with Zapier. This involves:

  1. Clicking "Connect a new account" in the account selection dropdown
  2. Following HubSpot's OAuth authorization flow
  3. Granting the necessary permissions to Zapier

Important: Test your trigger immediately after setup to verify Zapier can successfully retrieve data from HubSpot. This confirms your authentication worked properly.

Mapping HubSpot Fields to Other Apps

Field mapping determines which HubSpot data gets transferred where in your destination app. This critical step ensures information flows accurately between systems without manual intervention.

During the action setup phase, Zapier displays available fields from your HubSpot trigger on the left and empty fields from your destination app on the right. You'll:

  1. Select the destination field you want to populate
  2. Choose the corresponding HubSpot data point from the dropdown
  3. Repeat for all relevant fields in your workflow

Example mapping: When creating Google Sheets rows from new HubSpot contacts, you might map:
HubSpot Contact Email → Google Sheets Email Column
HubSpot Company Name → Google Sheets Company Column
HubSpot Create Date → Google Sheets Date Added Column

Zapier remembers these mappings for future use, allowing you to create similar automations faster. For advanced users, you can also apply formatting functions to transform data during the transfer process.

Testing and Activating Your Workflow

Thorough testing prevents data errors and ensures your automation works as intended before going live. Zapier provides multiple testing opportunities throughout the setup process.

Trigger Testing

The first test pulls sample data from HubSpot to confirm the trigger connection works. This verifies:

  • Zapier can access your HubSpot account
  • The selected trigger event returns expected data
  • All necessary fields are available for mapping

Action Testing

After configuring your action app, running a test sends sample data through the entire workflow. This confirms:

  • Field mappings transfer data correctly
  • The destination app receives and processes the information
  • No errors occur during the automation process

Best practice: Always check your destination app after testing to verify the test data appears exactly as expected. This catches mapping errors before they affect real data.

Once testing completes successfully, name your Zap descriptively (e.g., "HubSpot Contacts → Google Sheets") and toggle it on. Your automation is now live and will run whenever the trigger event occurs in HubSpot.

Advanced Zapier-HubSpot Integration Tips

Once you've mastered basic integrations, these advanced techniques can further optimize your HubSpot automations.

Using Filters

Zapier's Filter step lets you add conditions to your workflow. For example, you might only sync contacts tagged as "Marketing Qualified Lead" or deals valued over $10,000.

Multi-Step Zaps

Premium Zapier plans allow multiple actions in a single workflow. You could create a Zap that:

  1. Triggers when a HubSpot deal reaches "Closed-Won"
  2. Creates an invoice in QuickBooks
  3. Adds the customer to a VIP Slack channel
  4. Schedules a follow-up task in Asana

Custom Field Handling

For HubSpot custom fields, use Zapier's Formatter step to transform data before mapping. This ensures proper formatting in destination apps.

Performance tip: Upgrade to a paid Zapier plan if you need faster polling intervals. Free plans check for triggers every 15 minutes, while premium plans can poll as frequently as every 1 minute.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced users make these integration errors that can disrupt workflows or corrupt data.

1. Insufficient Field Mapping

Failing to map all required fields in your destination app leads to incomplete records. Always verify mandatory fields are populated.

2. Overlooking Authentication Expirations

HubSpot connections periodically expire. Set calendar reminders to reauthenticate before integrations break.

3. Ignoring Rate Limits

Both HubSpot and Zapier impose API call limits. Monitor your usage to avoid temporary suspensions.

4. Skipping Testing

Assuming your Zap works without testing often leads to data issues. Always test with sample data first.

Troubleshooting tip: Zapier's task history shows exactly what data was transferred during each run, making it invaluable for debugging failed workflows.
