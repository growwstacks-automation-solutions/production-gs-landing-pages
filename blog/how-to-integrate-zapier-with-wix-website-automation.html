Step 1: Connect Your Wix Account to Zapier

Before creating any automations, you need to establish the connection between your Wix site and Zapier. This one-time setup grants Zapier permission to access your Wix data securely.

  1. Log in to your Zapier account at zapier.com
  2. Click on your profile icon and select "Connected Accounts"
  3. Click the "+ Add Connection" button
  4. Search for "Wix" and select it from the app directory
  5. A new window will open prompting you to log in to your Wix account
  6. After logging in, select the specific Wix site you want to connect
  7. Click "Authorize" to grant Zapier access to your Wix data

Pro Tip: If you manage multiple Wix sites, you'll need to connect each one separately in Zapier. Give each connection a descriptive name like "Wix - Main Site" or "Wix - Client Portfolio" for easy identification later.

Step 2: Create Your First Zap

With your accounts connected, you're ready to build your first automation (called a "Zap" in Zapier terminology). Think of a Zap as a simple "if this happens, then do that" rule for your website.

  1. From your Zapier dashboard, click "+ Create Zap"
  2. Give your Zap a descriptive name like "Wix Form to Google Sheets"
  3. Search for and select "Wix" as your trigger app
  4. Choose the specific trigger event (like "New Form Submission")
  5. Select the Wix account connection you created earlier

At this point (around 1:45 in the video tutorial), you'll see options to specify which exact form, store, or component on your Wix site should trigger the automation. This ensures your Zap only runs when the specific action you want occurs.

Step 3: Set Up the Wix Trigger

The trigger is the event that starts your automation. For Wix, common triggers include:

  • New Form Submission: When someone submits a contact form, lead gen form, or survey
  • Order Placed: When a customer completes a purchase in your Wix store
  • New Contact: When someone joins your mailing list or member area
  • New Blog Post: When you publish new content on your Wix blog

After selecting your trigger type, you'll need to:

  1. Choose the specific Wix component (like which exact form or store)
  2. Click "Test Trigger" to pull sample data from your Wix site
  3. Review the sample data to ensure Zapier is receiving the correct information
  4. Click "Continue" to proceed to the action setup

Important: Always test your trigger before setting up actions. This verifies Zapier can access your Wix data correctly and shows you exactly what information will be available for mapping in the next steps.

Step 4: Configure the Action App

Now that your trigger is working, it's time to tell Zapier what to do with the Wix data. This is where you'll connect to apps like Google Sheets, Mailchimp, or Slack to automatically receive your website information.

  1. Click "+ Add Action" in your Zap
  2. Search for and select your destination app (like "Google Sheets")
  3. Choose the specific action (like "Create Spreadsheet Row")
  4. Connect your action app account if not already linked
  5. Map the Wix data fields to the appropriate fields in your action app

Field mapping is where the real magic happens. For example, you might map:

  • Wix form "Email" field → Mailchimp "Subscriber Email" field
  • Wix order "Product Name" → Google Sheets "Item Purchased" column
  • Wix contact "Phone Number" → Slack notification message

After mapping your fields, run another test to verify the data flows correctly to your action app. The video tutorial shows this process clearly at the 3:10 mark.

Step 5: Test and Publish Your Zap

Before going live with your automation, it's crucial to test the complete workflow from trigger to action. Zapier makes this easy with its built-in testing tools.

  1. Click "Test & Review" to execute a full test of your Zap
  2. Check your action app (like Google Sheets or Mailchimp) to confirm the test data arrived correctly
  3. If anything looks wrong, go back and adjust your field mappings
  4. Once satisfied, click "Publish" to activate your Zap
  5. Toggle the switch at the top from "Off" to "On"

Your automation is now live! Zapier will monitor your Wix site for the trigger event and automatically execute your configured action each time it occurs. You can always edit, pause, or turn off your Zap later from the Zapier dashboard.

Monitoring Tip: For the first few days, check your action app periodically to ensure data is flowing as expected. Zapier also sends email notifications if any errors occur in your automations.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

While the Wix-Zapier integration is generally reliable, here are solutions to common problems you might encounter:

Trigger Not Firing

If your Zap isn't activating when expected:

  • Verify you selected the correct Wix component (specific form/store)
  • Check that your Wix connection is still active in Zapier
  • Ensure the Zap is turned on (the toggle switch should be green)

Missing Data Fields

If some information isn't transferring:

  • Re-test your trigger to refresh available fields
  • Check if the field exists in your Wix component settings
  • Remap the missing fields in your Zap's action step

Duplicate Entries

If you're seeing repeats in your action app:

  • Add a filter step to check for existing records before creating new ones
  • Use Zapier's deduplication features in premium plans
  • Adjust your trigger settings to run less frequently

For persistent issues, Zapier's help center offers detailed troubleshooting guides and their support team can investigate specific Zap errors.
