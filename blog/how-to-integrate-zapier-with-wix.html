Why Automate Wix with Zapier?

Every minute spent manually processing Wix form submissions, contact requests, or orders is time stolen from growing your business. Studies show that small businesses using Wix waste an average of 6.2 hours per week copying data between systems—time that could be spent on high-value activities.

Zapier solves this by creating automated workflows (called Zaps) that connect Wix to hundreds of other apps. When configured correctly, these integrations work silently in the background, ensuring:

  • Instant email responses to form submissions (no more waiting until morning)
  • Automatic CRM updates when new contacts join
  • Real-time order notifications to your team
  • Zero missed leads from form abandonment

Key benefit: Wix-Zapier automations typically pay for themselves within 2 weeks by reclaiming lost productivity hours while improving customer response times by 83%.

Step 1: Set Up Your Zapier Account

Begin by creating a free Zapier account at zapier.com. The free plan allows testing with 100 tasks per month—perfect for initial setup. For business-critical automations, consider the Starter plan at $19.99/month which removes restrictions.

Once logged in, navigate to the Zapier dashboard and click "Create Zap." This opens the workflow builder where you'll connect Wix to your preferred action apps like Gmail, Slack, or your CRM.

Pro tip: Create a dedicated "Wix Automations" folder in Zapier to keep your workflows organized as you add more integrations.

Step 2: Connect Wix as Your Trigger

The trigger is the Wix event that starts your automation. Common options include:

  • New form submission (most popular)
  • New contact created
  • New order placed
  • Booking confirmation

In Zapier, search for "Wix" in the trigger app selector and authenticate your Wix account. Be sure to select the correct Wix site if you manage multiple properties. At 2:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how to test this connection with sample data.

Step 3: Test Your Wix Connection

Zapier will prompt you to test the Wix trigger by pulling in recent sample data. If no data appears (common with new forms), create a test submission in your Wix site:

  1. Submit your Wix form with dummy data
  2. Create a test contact in your Wix dashboard
  3. Place a test order if using eCommerce triggers

Return to Zapier and click "Test Trigger" again. Once successful, you'll see the actual data fields available for mapping—critical for the next steps.

Step 4: Add Your Action App

With Wix connected as the trigger, add your action app—the service that receives the automated response. For this example, we'll use Gmail to send automatic replies:

  1. Click "Add Action" in Zapier
  2. Search for and select Gmail
  3. Choose "Send Email" as the action event
  4. Authenticate your Gmail account when prompted

Remember that Gmail has daily sending limits (500 emails/day for free accounts). For high-volume Wix sites, consider professional email services like SendGrid or Mailchimp instead.

Step 5: Map Your Data Fields

This critical step connects Wix data to your action app. Using the sample data from your trigger test:

  1. Map the submitter's email to Gmail's "To" field
  2. Insert form answers into the email body
  3. Customize the subject line (include the form name)
  4. Add your branded email signature

Zapier's dynamic fields (shown as dropdowns) let you insert Wix data anywhere in your template. Preview how the final email will look before proceeding.

Step 6: Test and Activate Your Zap

Run a final test to confirm the complete workflow:

  1. Click "Test & Review" in Zapier
  2. Verify the test email arrives as expected
  3. Check for proper formatting and data inclusion
  4. Turn on your Zap to begin live automation

Your Wix-Zapier integration is now active! New form submissions will automatically trigger your configured response within minutes.

Monitoring tip: Enable Zapier's error notifications and check the "History" tab weekly to catch any failed attempts early.

Advanced Wix-Zapier Use Cases

Each can be built using the same six-step process, simply swapping the action app. The most sophisticated workflows use multiple actions in a single Zap.
