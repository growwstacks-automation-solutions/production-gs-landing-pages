LinkedIn Outreach That Gets CEOs to Respond

LinkedIn contains the highest concentration of decision-makers anywhere online, yet most AI agencies approach it all wrong. The key isn't more connections - it's better targeting and strategic follow-up.

At 6:30 in the video, the speaker shows his actual LinkedIn profile that generates consistent meetings. Notice how every element - from the headline to the banner - reinforces one specific message about solving a particular problem for a specific industry.

Connection acceptance rates jump from 10% to 40% when your profile clearly communicates who you help and how. Generic "AI expert" profiles get ignored while specific problem-solvers get meetings.

The LinkedIn Sequence That Works

  1. Connection Request: No note (contrary to popular advice) - let your optimized profile do the talking
  2. Follow-up within 24 hours: Simple, curiosity-based message like "Hey [Name], quick question - are you using AI for [specific use case] yet?"
  3. Value Share (3 days later): Relevant case study or insight about their industry
  4. Soft Close (5 days later): "Last try - would a 15-minute demo be worth your time?"

This sequence respects the prospect's time while systematically demonstrating your expertise. The speaker mentions at 9:45 that sending just 20-30 of these per day consistently books 3-5 meetings weekly.

Optimizing Your Profile for Maximum Impact

Your LinkedIn profile isn't a resume - it's a digital storefront that needs to convert visitors in under 10 seconds. Most AI agency owners make their profiles all about themselves rather than their ideal client's problems.

The video at 7:15 shows the exact headline formula that works best for newcomers: "[Result] for [Industry] using [Solution]." For example: "Recovering $10,000/month in missed calls for dental practices using AI receptionists."

Profile banners should visually reinforce your headline with before/after metrics or client logos. This creates instant credibility before the prospect even reads your about section.

Critical Profile Elements

  • Headline: Lead with client outcomes, not job titles
  • Banner: Visual proof of results (metrics, logos, etc.)
  • About Section: Client transformation story, not your biography
  • Featured: Case studies and demo videos
  • Activity: Regular posts showcasing results (not just tips)

The speaker emphasizes at 8:30 that consistency beats perfection. Even with minimal content, a tightly focused profile outperforms a generic but polished one.

Cold Calling Framework That Converts

While LinkedIn builds long-term relationships, cold calling gets meetings today. Most reps fear it precisely why it works - with fewer competitors calling, your voice stands out.

The speaker shares at 18:30 that cold calling converts 2-5x higher than email because you're having real-time conversations where you can handle objections immediately.

Best calling times are Tuesday-Thursday 10-11:30am or 2-4pm when decision-makers are settled into their day but not yet in end-of-day scramble mode. The video shows actual call logs at 20:15 proving these windows work.

The 4-Part Cold Call Structure

  1. Pattern Interrupt: Say their name twice to break scripted responses
  2. Permission-Based Opener: "Mind if I take 30 seconds to explain why I called?"
  3. Problem Pitch: Focus on one specific revenue leak (not features)
  4. Direct Close: "Would you be opposed to a 15-minute demo chat?"

At 22:45, the speaker emphasizes that sounding slightly unsure actually increases credibility - robotic confidence triggers sales defenses.

The Psychology Behind Effective Call Scripts

Great cold calling isn't about memorizing lines - it's about understanding the psychological triggers that make prospects lower their guard. The most effective scripts sound conversational, not polished.

The video at 24:00 breaks down why the "permission-based opener" works so well. By asking "Do you mind if I take 30 seconds?" you're giving control back to the prospect, which reduces resistance.

Objection handling starts before the objection even comes up by preemptively addressing concerns in your pitch. For example, mentioning "I know I'm catching you out of the blue" disarms the "this is unexpected" objection.

Advanced Scripting Techniques

  • Pacing: Match the prospect's speech rate and energy level
  • Strategic Pauses: Create space for the prospect to engage
  • Verbal Nods: "Right?" "You know?" to build agreement momentum
  • Tonal Variety: Avoid monotone delivery that sounds scripted

The speaker notes at 26:30 that the first 20 calls will feel awkward, but by call 500, you'll develop natural rhythm and confidence.
