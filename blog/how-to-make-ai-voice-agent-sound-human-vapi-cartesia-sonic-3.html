Implementing Emotional Range

The biggest differentiator of Sonic-3 is its ability to convey appropriate emotions throughout a conversation. Where older systems delivered every line with the same flat tone, Sonic-3 can:

Match emotion to content: A joke told with [happy] tags sounds genuinely humorous, while bad news delivered with [sad] tags conveys appropriate sympathy.

To implement this in your voice agent:

  1. Identify emotional moments in your script (greetings, bad news, confirmations)
  2. Wrap relevant phrases in emotion tags: [happy]Why did the scarecrow win an award?[/happy]
  3. Test different emotional deliveries in the Cartesia playground
  4. Add [laugh] tags at natural laugh points in conversations

Perfect Pronunciation Controls

Nothing breaks customer trust faster than an AI agent mispronouncing names or contact information. Sonic-3 solves this with:

Letter-by-letter spelling: Wrap text in <spell> tags (e.g., <spell>T H O M A S</spell>) for perfect pronunciation of names and email addresses.

Additional pronunciation features:

  • Custom pronunciation dictionary for industry terms
  • Automatic handling of dates, times, and numbers
  • Punctuation-aware pacing (commas create natural pauses)
  • SSML tags for emphasis and prosody

Natural Conversation Flow Techniques

Human conversations have rhythm - pauses after important points, faster delivery of routine information, and vocal variety. Implement these Sonic-3 features to achieve natural flow:

Strategic pauses: Insert <break time='500ms'/> tags after key information to give customers time to process.

Additional flow controls:

  1. Use dashes to indicate brief pauses in complex information
  2. Adjust overall speed with <prosody rate='slow'> tags
  3. Vary volume for emphasis on important words
  4. Add slight speed increases for routine confirmations

Vapi Integration Guide

Connecting Sonic-3 to your Vapi implementation takes just minutes:

Simple model ID transfer: Copy your Sonic-3 model ID from Cartesia's playground and paste it into Vapi's voice settings.

Implementation steps:

  1. Create and test your voice model in Cartesia's playground
  2. Click the three dots menu and copy the model ID
  3. In Vapi, navigate to voice configuration settings
  4. Paste the Sonic-3 model ID
  5. Test all emotional tags and pronunciation controls

Common Implementation Mistakes

After deploying dozens of Sonic-3 voice agents, we've identified these frequent pitfalls:

Emotion/content mismatch: Scripts marked [happy] that deliver bad news create confusing customer experiences.

Other mistakes to avoid:

  • Overusing laughter tags in professional contexts
  • Not testing pronunciation of industry-specific terms
  • Setting pauses that are too long (over 1 second feels unnatural)
  • Using the same emotional tone throughout entire conversations
