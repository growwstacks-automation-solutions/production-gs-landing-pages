Entity SEO Explained (Why Keywords Aren't Enough)

Traditional SEO focuses on keywords - the exact phrases people search. But Google's algorithm has evolved to understand entities - the actual concepts, people, places and things behind those words. Where "solar battery" might be your keyword, entities include specific battery types, related equipment, installation scenarios, and technical specifications that demonstrate true expertise.

The solar battery page analyzed in our example had 347 existing entities (like "backup systems" and "residences"), but the workflow identified 1,253 additional entity opportunities from competitors. This gap explains why some pages plateau at position 3-5 - they're not demonstrating the same depth of topic coverage.

Key insight: Google now evaluates content quality based on entity density and relationships, not just keyword frequency. Pages that cluster related entities naturally (like connecting "homesteads" to "off-grid batteries") signal topical authority.

Step 1: Extracting Your Page Entities

The workflow first analyzes your existing content to establish a baseline. For our solar battery example, it identified:

  • 20 consumer goods entities (battery models, specs)

This revealed immediate optimization opportunities - like using "battery management system" instead of just the acronym "BMS" to capture more search variants. The extraction API provides entity types and confidence scores to prioritize which to address first.

Step 2: Competitor Entity Analysis

Rather than guessing what to add, the workflow scientifically identifies gaps by analyzing top-ranking pages. It:

  1. Finds 3-5 competitors ranking for your target terms
  2. Extracts their entities using the same API
  3. Compares against your entity profile

Pro tip: Focus on entities appearing in multiple competitors that you're missing. In our example, "homesteads" appeared in 4/5 competitor pages but was absent from the original content - a clear signal to incorporate this related use case.

Step 3: AI-Powered Entity Clustering

Raw entity lists are overwhelming - the magic happens when AI groups them into semantic clusters. The workflow uses a custom GPT to:

  • Group related entities (like "RV parks" with "mobile installations")
  • Identify the strongest thematic connections
  • Score relevance to your core topic

At 7:45 in the video, you can see how the tool surfaced "Thermal and Protection Systems" as a key cluster with 21 applicable entities - far more comprehensive than the original page's brief safety mention.

Step 4: Content Optimization Recommendations

The final output isn't just data - it's actionable advice. For each content section, the workflow:

  1. Identifies where to add new entity clusters
  2. Provides sample text incorporating missing entities
  3. Flags jargon that should be expanded (like "BMS" → "battery management system")

One powerful example: The tool recommended adding "tier one lithium chemistry cells with 8,000 cycles" instead of just "long-life batteries" - which both adds entities and strengthens the value proposition.

Entities in SEO are distinct concepts, objects, or topics that Google recognizes as having specific attributes and relationships. Unlike keywords which are just search terms, entities represent real-world things with defined characteristics.

Google uses entities to understand content context and relationships between topics. For example, "Tesla" is an entity that Google associates with electric vehicles, Elon Musk, and specific car models.

  • Entities have properties (Tesla → founded in 2003)
  • They relate to other entities (Tesla → electric vehicles)
  • They exist in Google's Knowledge Graph
