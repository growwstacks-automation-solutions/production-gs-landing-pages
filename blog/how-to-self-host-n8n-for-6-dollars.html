Step-by-Step: Create Your n8n Droplet

Digital Ocean calls their virtual servers "droplets." Setting up n8n takes just a few clicks:

Step 1: Find the n8n One-Click App

In your Digital Ocean dashboard, search for "n8n" in the Marketplace section. Select the official n8n application - this automatically installs the latest stable version with all dependencies.

Step 2: Choose Your Server Plan

The $6/month Basic plan (1GB RAM, 1 vCPU) works perfectly for most small business automation needs. At 4:30 in the video, we compare different plans if you need more power.

Step 3: Select Your Server Region

Pick the location closest to you or your customers for fastest performance. All regions support n8n equally well.

Step 4: Set Up Authentication

Create a secure password for your n8n instance and store it somewhere safe. This replaces the email login you use with n8n cloud.

Pro Tip: Enable weekly backups for just $1/month. This protects your automations from accidental deletion or corruption.

Connecting Your Custom Domain (Optional)

While you can access n8n via Digital Ocean's IP address, using a custom domain (like automation.yourbusiness.com) looks more professional and is easier to remember:

  1. Purchase a domain from Namecheap or your preferred registrar (from $1.80/year)
  2. In your domain's DNS settings, create an A record pointing to your droplet's IP
  3. During n8n setup, enter your domain when prompted

The system automatically handles SSL certificate generation, so your connection stays secure. The whole process takes about 5 minutes as shown at 7:45 in the video.

Completing n8n's Initial Setup

Once your droplet is running (usually within 2-3 minutes), you'll complete n8n's first-time setup:

  1. Access your n8n instance by entering the IP or domain in your browser
  2. Choose "Business Owner" as your role when prompted
  3. Set your admin credentials (different from your server password)
  4. Activate your free license key (emailed to you automatically)

That's it! You now have a fully functional n8n instance without monthly execution limits. The interface is identical to n8n cloud, so there's no new learning curve.

Migrating Your Existing Workflows

If you're switching from n8n cloud, transferring your workflows takes just minutes:

  1. In n8n cloud, export each workflow as a JSON file
  2. In your self-hosted n8n, create new workflows and import the JSON
  3. Reconnect any credentials (they don't transfer automatically)

Most workflows work immediately without modification. At 12:20 in the video, we demonstrate migrating a complex multi-step workflow in under 90 seconds.
