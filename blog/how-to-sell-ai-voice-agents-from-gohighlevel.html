Building the Brain: Knowledge Base Setup

An effective AI agent needs a "brain" - a knowledge base containing all information about the business and its services. In GoHighLevel, this starts with crawling the company website (at 4:15 in the video).

The web crawler doesn't just memorize pages - it contextualizes the information, allowing the AI to answer related questions not explicitly stated on the site. For an electrician, this means understanding that "panel upgrade" questions relate to the "electrical services" page, even if those exact words aren't used together.

Supplemental Training with Perplexity AI

Website content alone often lacks technical depth. At 6:30 in the tutorial, we see how to use Perplexity AI to build comprehensive industry knowledge (like Florida electrician certification requirements) then add it via Google Docs.

This two-layer approach - business-specific info from their website plus general expertise from AI-generated docs - creates agents that outperform human receptionists on technical questions. The Google Doc method also allows continuous updates without reconfiguring the entire knowledge base.

Creating the Voice Agent

With the knowledge base ready (11:00 timestamp), we configure the voice agent itself. Critical settings include:

  • Disclosure: "I'm an AI agent" message to comply with regulations
  • Voice selection: Natural-sounding options like "Chill Phil" (British accent)
  • Information collection: Name, email, address, and service needed
  • Calendar integration: Direct booking into the business's Google Calendar

The agent prompt can be customized to match brand voice while ensuring professional, accurate responses to common inquiries.

Phone Number Setup & Testing

Local presence matters - at 16:45 we see how to purchase a phone number with the business's area code ($1.15/month). Testing is critical before going live:

  1. Call from different devices (mobile, VoIP)
  2. Ask edge-case questions not in the knowledge base
  3. Verify calendar booking accuracy
  4. Check notification delivery to staff

The demo at 19:30 shows the agent handling a generator installation inquiry and booking a Wednesday appointment flawlessly - the type of conversion most businesses miss after hours.

Website Integration

At 22:10, we implement the final piece - adding the agent's number to the business website with a "Call Now" button. This creates multiple inbound paths:
