The Hidden Receptionist Crisis

Local businesses face an invisible profit drain: missed calls and inefficient booking systems. Medical practices, auto repair shops, and service businesses lose an average of $2,000 per missed after-hours call - yet can't afford 24/7 staffing.

The traditional solution - hiring receptionists - costs $3,000+/month per employee. This creates the perfect opportunity for AI voice agents that work around the clock at a fraction of the cost.

Key insight: Don't sell "AI technology" - sell the solution to their $3,000/month staffing problem. Businesses care about results, not features.

The $2,500/Month Pricing Strategy

Price your service based on the value you provide, not your costs. When a business spends $3,000/month on staff:

  • $500/month = 83% savings for them
  • $1,000/month = 66% savings
  • $2,500/month = Still saving versus hiring

Start with a simple $500/month package for small businesses. As you add features (appointment booking, CRM integration, after-hours coverage), increase to $1,500-$2,500 for larger clients.

Pro tip: Calculate their current receptionist costs during discovery calls using their Indeed posting salary data. This makes your pricing feel like a bargain.

Objection Handling That Closes Deals

The most common objections and how to overcome them:

"We don't get enough calls to need this"
Respond: "That's perfect - this acts as insurance for when you do miss an important call. What would happen if you missed a $2,000 client call after hours?"

"The AI won't sound professional"
Solution: Play your demo during the call and ask "Does this sound like something your clients would respond well to?"

"We prefer human interaction"
Position it as: "This handles the routine calls so your staff can focus on higher-value interactions."
