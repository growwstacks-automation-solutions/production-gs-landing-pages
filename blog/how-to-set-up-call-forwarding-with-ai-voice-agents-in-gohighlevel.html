Accessing Your Voice Agent Settings

To begin setting up call forwarding, you'll need to navigate to your AI voice agent settings in GoHighLevel. Start by logging into your account and selecting the appropriate sub-account if you manage multiple businesses or locations.

From the left sidebar menu, click on AI Agents then select Voice AI. This brings you to the agent list where you'll see all your configured voice agents. Find the agent you want to set up forwarding for and click the Edit button (shown at 0:42 in the video tutorial).

You'll now see several tabs for configuring your agent: Agent Details, Agent Goals, Phone & Availability, Dashboard, and Logs. The call forwarding settings are located under Agent Goals, but you'll need to enable Advanced Mode first - which we'll cover next.

Enabling Advanced Mode for Call Transfers

By default, GoHighLevel's voice agent settings show basic configuration options. To access call forwarding features, you must first enable Advanced Mode. Here's how:

Step 1: Navigate to Agent Goals

After clicking Edit on your voice agent, select the Agent Goals tab from the top menu. This is where you define how your AI should handle different call scenarios.

Step 2: Switch to Advanced Mode

Look for the toggle switch labeled Advanced Mode near the top of the Agent Goals section (visible at 1:05 in the video). Click this to reveal additional configuration options including call transfer capabilities.

Pro Tip: Advanced Mode unlocks several powerful features beyond call forwarding, including conditional workflows and CRM integrations. Once enabled, you'll see a New Action button that lets you add transfer rules.

Configuring Your Transfer Rules

With Advanced Mode enabled, you can now set up call forwarding rules that determine when and where calls should be transferred. Click the New Action button to begin creating your first transfer rule.

Step 1: Name Your Action

Give your transfer rule a descriptive name like "Forward to Sales" or "Transfer to Manager." This helps identify the rule later when reviewing or editing your configuration.

Step 2: Select Call Transfer Action

From the action type dropdown (shown at 1:32 in the video), choose Call Transfer. This reveals the transfer-specific configuration options.

Step 3: Enter Destination Number

Input the phone number where calls should be forwarded. This can be a team member's mobile, your office line, or any other number where someone can receive the transferred call.

Important: Always test transferred numbers to ensure they can receive calls properly. Some VoIP systems may require special configuration to accept forwarded calls.

Setting Transfer Triggers

The real power of GoHighLevel's call forwarding comes from its intelligent trigger system. You can define exactly when calls should be transferred based on caller requests or conversation patterns.

Common Transfer Triggers:

  • "I want to speak with a manager" - Detects when callers request human assistance
  • Pricing inquiries - Transfers calls asking about costs or quotes
  • Complaints - Routes unhappy customers directly to your support team
  • Specific keywords - Transfers when callers mention competitors or urgent needs

At 2:10 in the video, you can see how to configure these triggers using natural language examples that your AI will learn to recognize. The system becomes more accurate over time as it processes more calls.

Customizing Transfer Messages

GoHighLevel lets you customize what callers hear before being transferred and what your team hears when receiving the call. This creates a seamless handoff experience.

Caller Message:

This is what the caller hears before being transferred (configurable at 2:45 in the video). Example: "Okay great, let me connect you with one of our specialists who can help with that."

Whisper Message:

This brief message plays to your team member before they're connected with the caller. It can include:

  • Caller's name (if collected)
  • Reason for the transfer
  • Any information the AI gathered
  • Suggested response approach

Example whisper: "Transferring call from John regarding pricing for premium package - he's comparing options with competitors."

Testing Your Setup

Before going live with your new call forwarding rules, thorough testing is essential. GoHighLevel provides several ways to validate your configuration.

Test Call Method:

  1. Save your transfer rules and return to the agent list
  2. Click the Test Call button next to your agent
  3. Simulate different scenarios that should trigger transfers
  4. Verify the call routes to the correct number with proper messages

At 3:20 in the video tutorial, you can see this testing process in action. The presenter demonstrates how to trigger a transfer by saying "I'd like to speak to a manager" during the test call.

Monitoring Tip: After going live, regularly check your call logs (under the Dashboard tab) to review transfer performance and identify any needed adjustments to your rules.
