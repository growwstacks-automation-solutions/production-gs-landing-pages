Connecting Knowledge Sources

The chatbot's effectiveness depends entirely on the quality of its knowledge sources. Document360 lets you connect multiple content repositories to create a comprehensive answer engine:

Step 1: Knowledge Base Access

Choose whether to grant access to your entire Document360 knowledge base or limit to specific workspaces, languages, or categories. This is particularly useful for large organizations with segmented content.

Step 2: Website Content

Add crawl links, sitemaps, or specific web pages. The AI will extract relevant information while maintaining source attribution - shown at 1:20 in the video tutorial.

Step 3: Manual Content

For information not in your KB or website, enter text directly or upload documents (Word, PDF, text files). This works well for internal processes or proprietary information.

Step 4: Historical Tickets

Connect your help desk (Zendesk, Freshdesk, etc.) to leverage past ticket resolutions as a knowledge source - turning your support history into instant answers.

Pro tip: Start with your most frequently asked questions and expand over time. The video at 1:45 shows how to prioritize sources for maximum impact.

Testing Responses Before Launch

The playground environment (shown at 3:15 in the video) lets you test the chatbot thoroughly before customers see it:

  1. Ask questions customers typically have
  2. Verify answer accuracy and completeness
  3. Check source citations to ensure proper attribution
  4. Test edge cases and unusual phrasing

This testing phase is critical for identifying knowledge gaps and refining responses. Document360 maintains conversation history so you can review and improve over time.

Ticket Escalation Setup

Even the best chatbot can't answer every question. Configure seamless ticket escalation to:

  • Automatically create tickets when the chatbot can't answer
  • Pre-fill ticket details with the conversation history
  • Route to the appropriate support team based on topic

This creates a safety net that ensures customers always get help while giving your team full context. The video at 3:45 demonstrates the escalation flow.

Security Configurations

Protect your knowledge while making it accessible:

  • Trusted Domains: Restrict where the chatbot can be embedded
  • JWT Authentication: Secure chatbot communications
  • Domain Management: View and control connected sites

These options (shown at 4:10 in the video) ensure your information stays secure while remaining available to legitimate users.

Publishing Your Chatbot

When you're ready to go live:

  1. Click "Publish" in Document360
  2. Choose whether to enable on your Document360 site or external website
  3. Copy the embed code for external sites
  4. Toggle "Launch" to activate on Document360

The video at 4:30 shows the simple one-click publishing process that makes your chatbot immediately available to customers.

Measuring Chatbot Success

Track key metrics to optimize performance:

  • Resolution Rate: Percentage of questions answered without escalation
  • Conversation Length: Average number of messages per session
  • Customer Satisfaction: Post-chat feedback ratings
  • Ticket Reduction: Decrease in simple support requests

Document360 provides analytics to measure these KPIs and identify improvement opportunities - demonstrated at 4:50 in the tutorial.
