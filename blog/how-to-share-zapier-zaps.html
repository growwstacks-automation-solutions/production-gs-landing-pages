Why Share Zapier Templates?

Most teams waste hours rebuilding the same automations for different members. Whether it's onboarding workflows, CRM updates, or notification systems, recreating Zaps from scratch is inefficient and leads to inconsistencies.

Zapier's template feature solves this by letting you package any workflow into a shareable format. Templates preserve the entire Zap structure while keeping your app connections private - recipients connect their own accounts when setting it up.

Teams using shared templates report 72% faster onboarding for new automation users, according to Zapier's internal data. Standardized workflows also reduce errors and maintenance time.

Accessing Your Zaps

Before sharing a Zap, you need to locate it in your account. Start by logging into Zapier.com and confirming you're in the correct workspace - especially important for teams with multiple shared environments.

Navigate to the Assets section in the left sidebar, then select Zaps. This centralized location shows all automations in your current workspace. Use the search bar or filters if you have many Zaps to sort through.

At 0:45 in the video tutorial, you'll see how to quickly identify the right Zap by reviewing its name and description - critical details that will help recipients understand the template's purpose.

Creating a Shareable Template

With your Zap open in the editor, look for the dropdown menu at the top of the page. This menu contains all the management options for your automation.

Select "Create Template" from this menu. Zapier will analyze your workflow and package it into a shareable format, automatically removing any sensitive connection data or credentials.

Pro Tip: Before sharing, double-check your Zap's name and description. These fields become the template's title and first impression for recipients. Clear, action-oriented descriptions get used 3x more often.

Sharing Options

Once your template is created, Zapier provides multiple distribution methods. The simplest is copying the direct link - perfect for pasting into documentation, chat messages, or emails.

For team distribution, use the built-in sharing buttons for platforms like Slack, Microsoft Teams, or email. These options let you add a custom message explaining the template's purpose and ideal use cases.

At 1:30 in the video, you'll see how the sharing panel automatically generates a preview of what recipients will see - including which apps they'll need to connect and any setup instructions you've included.

Security Considerations

While templates are designed to be secure, there are best practices to follow. Never include sensitive data in field labels or descriptions, as these become visible to anyone with the template link.

Templates don't share: Your connected accounts, custom field values, or any data processed by the Zap. Recipients must authenticate each app with their own credentials.

For added security, consider creating a dedicated "Template" folder in your connected apps. This lets you control permissions separately from your main business accounts.
