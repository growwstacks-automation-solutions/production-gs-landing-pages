Crafting the Perfect Spam Filter Prompt

Your AI's effectiveness depends entirely on the quality of your prompt. A generic instruction like "screen spam calls" won't work. You need specific, scenario-based training that covers common spam patterns in your industry.

Effective prompts include:

  • Clear definitions of what constitutes a spam call for your business
  • Examples of common spam phrases to recognize ("free trial", "special offer", etc.)
  • Scripted responses for different spam scenarios
  • Instructions for note-taking and call classification

Pro tip: Include at least 5-10 real examples of spam calls you've received in your prompt. The more specific your examples, the better the AI will perform.

The Manual Call Blocking Process

When your AI identifies a spam call, you'll want to prevent future interruptions from that number. GoHighLevel provides a manual blocking option through the DND (Do Not Disturb) feature.

Here's how it works:

  1. Navigate to the contact record in GoHighLevel
  2. Scroll to the "DND inbound calls and SMS" option
  3. Enable this setting for that contact

Automating DND Workflows for Repeat Offenders

While GoHighLevel doesn't offer direct automatic call blocking, you can create powerful automated workflows that achieve the same result. These workflows trigger when calls meet certain criteria (like being marked as potential spam by your AI).

A basic automated DND workflow includes:

  • Trigger: Call marked as potential spam
  • Action: Apply "Spam Caller" tag to contact
  • Action: Enable DND for inbound calls/SMS
  • Notification: Alert your team about the blocked number

This system creates a self-maintaining defense against repeat spam callers while keeping your team informed. The video at 10:30 shows how to set up these automation rules in GoHighLevel.

Setting Up Your Call Review System

To ensure legitimate calls aren't accidentally blocked, establish a regular review process for calls flagged as spam. GoHighLevel's call logs provide transcripts and AI-generated notes for every interaction.

An effective review system includes:

  • Weekly audits of all flagged calls
  • A simple process to remove DND status for false positives
  • Continuous prompt refinement based on call patterns
  • Team training on reviewing and handling borderline cases

This quality control step ensures your spam filtering remains accurate while adapting to new spam tactics that emerge over time.
