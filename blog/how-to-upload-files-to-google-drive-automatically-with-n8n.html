n8n Google Drive Integration

n8n connects to Google Drive through a secure OAuth 2.0 connection, giving it temporary permission to upload files on your behalf. The platform treats Google Drive like any other node in your workflow - you can trigger uploads from emails, form submissions, database changes, or scheduled intervals.

What makes n8n superior to basic Google Drive apps is its ability to process files before uploading. You can rename documents based on their content, extract text for indexing, convert formats, or even combine multiple files into a single upload. This transforms Google Drive from passive storage into an active document processing system.

Step 1: Setting Up Credentials

Before any automation can occur, n8n needs permission to access your Google Drive. This secure connection ensures only your workflow can upload files, while keeping your account protected.

In your n8n interface, add a Google Drive node and click the credentials section. Select "Create New" then choose Google Drive OAuth2. A popup will appear where you'll log in to your Google account and grant n8n limited access. These permissions can be revoked anytime through your Google security settings.

Pro Tip: Create a dedicated Google service account for automation workflows. This keeps your personal Drive separate and makes permission management easier for teams.

Step 2: Configuring the Upload Node

With credentials established, configure your Google Drive node's settings. Set the operation to "Upload" - this tells n8n you want to send files to Drive rather than download or search for them.

The folder ID field determines where files will be saved. Find this by navigating to your target folder in Google Drive and copying the ID from the URL. Leave blank to use your Drive's root folder. At 3:45 in the video, you'll see how to test different destination folders during setup.

Step 3: Mapping Your Files

File mapping connects your source documents to the Google Drive upload. n8n can process files from emails, webhooks, cloud storage, or even physical scanners. The key is ensuring each file's data structure matches what the Google Drive node expects.

Use n8n's expression editor to transform file names, add metadata, or combine multiple files into a single upload. For example, you could automatically prepend the current date to every uploaded file, or route different file types to specific folders based on their extensions.

Step 4: Testing and Activation

Before going live, test your workflow with the "Execute Node" button. n8n will attempt a trial upload using your configured settings. Check your Google Drive to confirm the file arrived correctly, with the right name and in the proper location.

Once verified, activate the workflow to run automatically. Set it to trigger on a schedule, when new files appear in a monitored folder, or based on actions in other apps. The video at 6:20 demonstrates how to configure different trigger types for varied business needs.

Successful test uploads confirm your automation is ready. From this point forward, files will flow to Google Drive without any manual effort from you or your team.
