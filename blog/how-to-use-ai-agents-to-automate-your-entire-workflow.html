The AI Agent Security Crisis

Most businesses experimenting with AI automation face three deal-breaking problems: exorbitant token costs, complex local setups, and security vulnerabilities that keep enterprise teams awake at night. The shocking statistic? 36% of open-source AI agents ship with at least one critical security flaw - not theoretical risks, but published audit findings.

Traditional agent architectures compound these issues. Most are simply GPT-class models with a flaky function-calling layer bolted on, forcing the system to re-read its own context repeatedly. Every multi-step task burns thousands of tokens as the model struggles to stay on track. Half the open-source stacks require dedicated hardware running 24/7, manual OAuth credential juggling, and crossed fingers that sensitive tokens don't leak to GitHub.

Security isn't a feature - it's the foundation: Pokey Claw's isolated sandbox architecture encrypts all credentials, implements role-based access control, and maintains a full audit log of every tool call. Your authentication tokens never sit in plain text, and any action touching the outside world requires explicit approval.
