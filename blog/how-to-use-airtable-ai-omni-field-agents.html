What Airtable AI Can Build For You

Traditional database setup involves manually creating tables, defining fields, establishing relationships, and configuring views - a time-consuming process that often requires technical expertise. Airtable's AI tools fundamentally change this workflow by understanding natural language descriptions of what you need built.

The platform offers two complementary AI systems: Omni for structural creation and Field Agents for data processing. Together they handle:

Key capability: Omni can generate complete database structures including tables with properly typed fields, linked record relationships, and starter interfaces in under 2 minutes when given clear instructions.

Omni AI: From Prompt to Complete Database

The magic begins when you describe your desired structure to Omni. Rather than manually creating an invoices table with client, date, amount and amount fields, you can write: "Create an invoices table with client name (linked to clients table), invoice date, due date, amount (currency), status (dropdown: draft, sent, paid), and notes field."

Omni interprets this and generates the complete table structure with proper field types and relationships. At 3:22 in the tutorial video, you'll see how this works for building an opportunity tracking interface complete with deal stage visualization.

Step 1: Enable Omni in Workspace Settings

Before using Omni, ensure AI features are activated in your workspace settings. This grants access to the Omni panel where all AI interactions occur.

Step 2: Describe Your Structure

Be specific about table purposes, field names, and field types. Omni understands standard database terminology but benefits from examples.

Step 3: Review and Refine

The AI-generated structure will be about 70-80% complete. Expect to manually adjust some field settings and relationships for optimal performance.

Pro Tip: When planning complex bases, write your complete data model in a document first, then paste sections into Omni for sequential table creation. This maintains consistency across related tables.

Field Agents: Your AI Data Processors

While Omni builds the container, Field Agents handle the content. These AI-powered automations process individual records to perform tasks like document summarization, data categorization, and web research.

At 7:15 in the video, you'll see a Field Agent automatically research company details when a new record is added - pulling in headquarters location, executive team members, and recent news articles without manual input.

Most powerful use cases: Attachment summarization, support ticket categorization of support tickets, extraction of key terms from contracts, and automated company/contact research directly within your records.

AI-Powered Formula Builder

Airtable's formula builder now understands plain English requests, eliminating the need to memorize function syntax. Need to calculate days overdue? Type "Show how many days past due date" and the AI suggests the proper date comparison formula.

The tutorial demonstrates this at 5:48 with a simple quantity x price calculation, but the system handles much more complex logic including nested IF statements, array operations, and conditional formatting.

Accuracy note: Simple math andard business formulas work with about 90% accuracy, while niche use cases may require manual adjustment. Always test with sample data first.

The Field Agent Catalog

Airtable provides over 15 pre-built Field Agents for common use cases, accessible through the browse catalog feature shown at 9:30 in the video. These include:

  • Document summarizers for meeting notes and documents
  • Content generators for marketing copy
  • Company and contact researchers
  • Data extractors for PDFs and attachments

Each can be customized for your specific needs, and connected to other automations for end-to-end processing.

Current Limitations to Know

While powerful, Airtable's AI has boundaries. Complex many-to-many relationships often require manual setup, and some interface types like Gantt charts aren't yet fully supported.

The systems works best when given very clear instructions - vague prompts produce generic results. As shown at 11:05, the technology is improving rapidly with each update.
