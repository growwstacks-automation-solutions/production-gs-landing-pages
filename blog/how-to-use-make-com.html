What Is Make.com?

Make.com (formerly known as Integromat) is a powerful no-code automation platform that connects different applications and services. It solves the common business problem of data being trapped in silos across multiple tools, requiring manual transfers that waste time and introduce errors.

The platform uses a visual builder where you create "scenarios" - automated workflows that move and transform data between apps. Unlike coding solutions that require technical skills, Make.com makes automation accessible to anyone through its intuitive interface.

Key benefit: Businesses using Make.com report saving an average of 10-15 hours per week by automating repetitive tasks between apps like email, spreadsheets, and CRMs.

Getting Started With Make.com

The first step is creating your Make.com account. As shown at the 0:45 mark in the video tutorial, you can sign up with just an email address and password. The free plan provides enough operations to test basic automations before committing to a paid plan.

Once logged in, you'll see the dashboard where all your scenarios will live. The interface might feel overwhelming at first, but focus on these core areas:

  • Scenarios: Your automation workflows
  • Connections: Authorized app integrations
  • Data Stores: Temporary storage for workflow data
  • Settings: Account and billing configuration

Building Your First Scenario

Creating your first automation in Make.com follows a simple process that anyone can master:

Step 1: Create a New Scenario

Click the "Create a new scenario" button on your dashboard. This opens a blank canvas where you'll build your workflow.

Step 2: Add Your First Module

Click the plus button to add your first app module. You'll see hundreds of available apps - start with something simple like Gmail or Google Sheets.

Step 3: Choose a Trigger

Select what event should start your automation, like "New Email" in Gmail or "New Row" in Google Sheets.

Step 4: Connect Your App

Authorize Make.com to access your chosen app by signing in and granting permissions.

Pro tip: At the 2:15 timestamp in the video, you can see exactly how to connect Gmail as a trigger for a scenario.

Connecting Apps and Services

The real power of Make.com comes from connecting multiple apps in a single workflow. After setting up your trigger, you can add additional modules to process and route your data.

For example, you could:

  • Add new Gmail emails to a Google Sheet
  • Send Slack notifications when a CRM record updates
  • Create Trello cards from form submissions

Each connection is secured through OAuth, meaning Make.com never stores your app passwords. You can revoke access at any time from your account settings.

Testing and Activating Workflows

Before running your automation live, Make.com lets you test scenarios with the "Run once" feature. This executes your workflow one time so you can verify everything works as expected.

The platform provides detailed logs showing exactly how data moved through each step of your scenario. If something doesn't work, you'll see where the process failed and can make adjustments.

Once testing is complete, simply click "Turn on" to activate your automation. Make.com will now run your scenario automatically based on the triggers you configured.

Advanced Features to Explore

Once you're comfortable with basic scenarios, Make.com offers powerful advanced features:

  • Filters: Only process data that meets specific conditions
  • Routers: Create branching logic in your workflows
  • Aggregators: Combine data from multiple sources
  • Iterators: Process arrays of data item by item
  • Functions: Transform data with text manipulation, math operations, etc.

These tools allow you to build sophisticated automations that can handle nearly any business process.
