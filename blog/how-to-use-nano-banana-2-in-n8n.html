Text-to-Image Automation Flow

The simplest Nano Banana Pro workflow generates images purely from text descriptions. This is perfect for creating original concept art, marketing visuals, or social media content without any starting images.

The n8n workflow follows this sequence:

  1. API Call to Create Task: Sends your text prompt to Nano Banana Pro with parameters like resolution and aspect ratio
  2. Wait & Poll: Checks task status every 5 seconds until completion
  3. Retrieve Result: Gets the URL of your generated image when ready

Pro Tip: For best results, include specific details in your prompts like "hyperrealistic image of Chicago skyline at sunset with detailed cloud formations and light reflecting off skyscraper windows."

Image-to-Image Transformation

More powerful than text-to-image is the ability to transform existing photos. This lets you place products in different environments, change styles, or enhance details - all automatically through n8n.

The workflow adds one crucial step: uploading your source image to a temporary URL that Nano Banana can access. The n8n HTTP Request node handles this seamlessly before passing the URL to Nano Banana Pro.

Example transformations include:

  • Turning a basic headshot into a professional studio portrait
  • Placing a product on different background scenes
  • Converting sketches into polished renderings

Multiple Images to Single Composition

The most advanced capability combines elements from several images into one cohesive output. This is revolutionary for product marketing where you might want to show multiple items together in a lifestyle setting.

The workflow accepts up to 14 input images and intelligently composites them based on your prompt. For example:

  • Combine separate product photos into a single "in use" scene
  • Merge team headshots into a group photo without actual group photography
  • Create complex infographics by blending charts, text, and illustrations

Important note: These complex compositions take longer (35+ seconds vs 15 for simple images) and may occasionally fail during peak usage times. The workflow automatically retries failed attempts.
