What Is Answerly and Why White Label It?

Answerly is an AI-powered customer support chatbot that businesses can train to handle common questions, reduce support tickets, and improve customer engagement. The platform learns from your knowledge base, documents, and past conversations to provide accurate, instant responses.

White labeling Answerly lets you resell this technology as your own branded product without the development costs. Agencies, SaaS companies, and digital service providers use this approach to:

  • Offer AI chatbots as a value-added service
  • Create recurring revenue streams
  • Differentiate from competitors with proprietary tech
  • Maintain client relationships under your brand

Average time savings: White labeling cuts 3-6 months off developing a comparable chatbot in-house while providing proven AI technology that already integrates with popular platforms.

Step 1: Enable Answerly in Your Products

The white labeling process begins in your product settings dashboard. At the 0:45 mark in the video, you'll see how to:

  1. Navigate to the Products section
  2. Locate Answerly in your available products
  3. Toggle the activation switch to enable it

This makes Answerly available for white labeling while keeping it hidden from clients until your branding is complete. The interface confirms activation with a visual indicator, ensuring you don't proceed with unconfigured settings.

Step 2: Upload Your Custom Branding

Brand consistency builds trust with your clients. Answerly's white label options let you replace all default branding with your visual identity:

  • Main logo: Upload your primary logo (PNG/SVG recommended)
  • Favicon: Add your browser tab icon (minimum 32×32 pixels)
  • App icons: Replace all interface icons with your branded versions

The video demonstrates how these assets appear throughout the interface at 1:15, showing the immediate visual transformation from Answerly's default look to your custom branding.

Pro tip: Use transparent PNGs for logos and maintain consistent dimensions across all icons (the video recommends 64×64 pixels for optimal display).

Step 3: Rename the Product

At 1:45 in the tutorial, you'll see how to completely remove the Answerly name from your product. The name field accepts any custom product title that aligns with your brand voice.

For example:

  • "[YourCompany] AI Assistant"
  • "Smart Support Bot"
  • "[YourProductName] Chat"

This change propagates throughout the interface, including:

  • Login/signup pages
  • Dashboard headers
  • Email communications
  • API documentation

The video shows how the renamed product appears to end users, with no trace of the original Answerly branding.

Step 4: Configure AI Defaults

One of Answerly's most powerful white label features is preset AI configurations. At 2:30 in the video, you'll learn to:

  1. Create a default API key for all new accounts
  2. Select your preferred LLM (GPT-4, Claude, etc.)
  3. Set baseline parameters for response quality

This ensures every client gets started with:

  • Consistent AI performance under your brand
  • No technical setup required on their end
  • Your recommended best practices pre-configured

Enterprise benefit: Large agencies can negotiate volume API pricing with AI providers, then bake those savings into their white-labeled product's margins.

Step 5: Set Up Your Custom Domain

Professional white labeling requires removing all third-party domains. At 3:10, the video shows the hostname configuration where you:

  1. Enter your preferred subdomain (support.yourcompany.com, chat.yourproduct.com, etc.)
  2. Add the CNAME record in your DNS settings
  3. Verify domain ownership

Once propagated, clients will access their chatbots exclusively through your domain, with no visible connection to Answerly. The tutorial includes a troubleshooting tip for common DNS validation issues.

Step 6: Add Your Support Details

Complete the white label illusion by replacing Answerly's support information with your own at 3:45 in the video:

  • Help Center URL: Link to your knowledge base or documentation
  • Support email: Your customer service address
  • "Built by" text: Custom footer attribution

These details appear in:

  • Error messages
  • Widget settings
  • Account management pages
  • System notifications

The video demonstrates how these elements create a seamless brand experience when clients need assistance.

Step 7: Test the Client Experience

Before launching to customers, the tutorial recommends creating a test account at 4:00 to experience your white-labeled product exactly as clients will see it. This reveals:

  • Brand consistency across all pages
  • Proper domain resolution
  • Correct support information
  • AI functionality with your defaults

The video walks through the entire signup and chatbot creation flow, showing how every touchpoint now reflects your brand instead of Answerly's.

Quality assurance: The test account process takes under 2 minutes but prevents embarrassing branding issues before clients see the product.
