From Basic Automation to AI Orchestration

Most IT teams start their automation journey with simple workflows - password resets, ticket routing, or data synchronization between systems. VTS followed this path initially, but quickly recognized the limitations of rule-based automation. "The big move for us was from orchestration to AI orchestration," explains Andrew Turpin, Head of IT at VTS.

Traditional automation excels at repetitive tasks with clear rules, but struggles with ambiguity or decision-making. AI orchestration adds machine learning capabilities that can interpret unstructured data, make judgment calls, and adapt workflows dynamically. This evolution allowed VTS to tackle more complex business problems.

Key insight: While basic automation might handle 30% of employee needs through rigid rules, AI orchestration can address 70%+ by understanding context and intent - just like VTS's genies do with support tickets and meeting summaries.

Building the First IT Genie

VTS's transformation began with what they called the "IT genie" - an AI agent focused on internal IT support. "We took processes and data sources we were familiar with and applied AI functionality to it," says Turpin. This cautious approach allowed them to work with known systems while testing AI capabilities.

The initial use case focused on ticket deflection - automatically answering common IT questions by referencing knowledge bases and past resolutions. When employees submitted tickets, the genie would analyze the request, search documentation, and either provide an immediate solution or route it appropriately. This reduced repetitive work for the IT team while speeding up resolutions.

Success with the IT genie created internal advocates who helped expand the concept to other departments. "It was a great driver to expanding that to other teams," notes Turpin. "We figured out how we could do it for our own needs first, then scaled what worked."

Transforming Support Operations

After proving the model with IT, support operations became the next focus area. "Support has been the next big focus," explains Turpin. "How can we meet support where they are, answer the questions they have using the context already provided in the ticket?"

The support genie took automation to a new level by:

  • Analyzing ticket content and historical resolutions to suggest solutions
  • Automatically categorizing and prioritizing incoming requests
  • Providing agents with relevant knowledge articles before they even opened the ticket

This approach reduced average handle time while improving consistency. Perhaps more importantly, it freed support staff from repetitive research tasks, allowing them to focus on complex issues requiring human judgment.

Capturing Meeting Insights Automatically

One of VTS's most innovative applications of AI orchestration involves meeting summaries. "We're taking those summaries and giving business leaders the ability to ask questions about meetings that maybe they weren't involved in," says Turpin.

This solution addresses a universal pain point - executives missing critical discussions due to scheduling conflicts. The meeting genie:

  1. Joins scheduled meetings (with participant consent)
  2. Records and transcribes discussions
  3. Generates executive summaries with key decisions and action items
  4. Allows leaders to query the content afterward ("What was said about the Q2 budget?")

Transformational impact: Leaders report saving 2-3 hours per week previously spent chasing down meeting notes or asking colleagues "what did I miss?" The genie makes institutional knowledge accessible on demand.

Measurable Time Savings for Employees

The cumulative impact of VTS's genies translates to significant time savings across the organization. "What these agents are doing as a whole is they're giving people time back," emphasizes Turpin. Employees estimate saving 5-10 hours weekly through:

  • Automated ticket resolution (no waiting for IT or support)
  • Instant access to meeting insights (no manual note-taking or follow-ups)
  • Ad-hoc information queries (no emailing colleagues for basic data)

This reclaimed time allows employees to focus on higher-value work. As Turpin puts it: "We're allowing them to offload the work that they were doing so they can better focus on strategic priorities."

The Power of Platform Flexibility

A key factor in VTS's success has been Workato's flexibility. "For me it's flexibility," says Turpin. "Meeting those end users where they are." The platform allows integration with various AI models and interfaces:

  • Employees can interact via chat, email, or dedicated interfaces
  • The system works with multiple LLMs (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.)
  • Genies can access data across VTS's application ecosystem

This adaptability ensures solutions match employee preferences rather than forcing new behaviors. "If they want to use cursor, if they want to use anti-gravity...the platform allows me to make that same information available wherever they need it," explains Turpin.

Future Possibilities with Agentic AI

VTS views their current implementations as just the beginning. "When it comes to the Agentic platform, your imagination is the only thing that's limiting you," says Turpin. Potential future applications include:

  • Automated compliance monitoring across systems
  • Predictive analytics for equipment maintenance
  • Dynamic resource allocation based on real-time demand

The common thread is using AI to not just automate tasks, but to enhance decision-making. As Turpin notes: "If you can think it, in many ways, you can do it...there's a little bit of magic that gets sprinkled from Workato on these processes."
