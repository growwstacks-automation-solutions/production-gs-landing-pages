The $1M Problem Selection Framework

Most AI SaaS products fail because they solve unimportant problems. We nearly made this mistake ourselves before discovering a framework that led to Clarbo's success. The key insight? Not all problems are created equal when building with AI.

Through trial and error, we identified three non-negotiable characteristics for viable AI SaaS problems:

The $1M Problem Checklist: 1) High customer lifetime value (we targeted $250/month per seat), 2) Low churn (once implemented, the solution becomes essential infrastructure), and 3) Regulatory moats (like telecom compliance requirements that prevent easy competition). Our HVAC client example showed 66% revenue increases worth $2M/month - making our $250/seat pricing an easy yes.

We specifically avoided "nice-to-have" tools in favor of mission-critical systems where customers feel the pain daily. At 4:32 in the video, we show how traditional sales teams waste 60% of their time on unanswered calls - a measurable, expensive problem begging for automation.

How Claude Generated 300 Solutions (And We Found 5 Good Ones)

Early attempts at using Claude produced mostly unusable ideas. We learned that quantity leads to quality when AI brainstorming - but only with the right constraints.

Our breakthrough came when we gave Claude this exact prompt (shown at 7:15 in the video):

"Enumerate 10 distinct mechanisms to increase call pickup rates across algorithmic, behavioral, infrastructural, regulatory, psychological, time-based, and identity-based approaches. Don't self-censor for feasibility - diverge wildly."

This generated 300 ideas, 95% of which were impractical. But the 5% that worked became Clarbo's core features:

  • Temporal propensity modeling (optimal call windows)
  • Simultaneous multi-line dialing
  • Dynamic number rotation to improve recognizability
  • Bayesian-optimized call pacing
  • Compliance-aware number pooling

Building the Predictive Pacing Algorithm

The star of our solution was the predictive pacing algorithm that increased calls from 100/hour to 200/hour while improving pickup rates. Here's how Claude helped build it:

Step 1: Data Collection

We fed Claude historical call data from our own businesses - timestamps, durations, pickup rates by time of day. This became the training set.

Step 2: Simulation Design

Claude designed a simulator to test dialing strategies without risking real customer relationships. At 12:40 in the video, we show the simulator interface Claude created.

Step 3: Bayesian Optimization

The AI implemented Bayesian optimization to continuously improve dial timing based on real-world results. This reduced wasted time between calls by 73%.

Key Insight: The algorithm's magic isn't in simultaneous dialing (which risks multiple pickups) but in precisely staggered dialing based on historical pickup latency distributions.

Our Counter-Intuitive Pricing Strategy

Most SaaS founders overcomplicate pricing. We took the opposite approach - starting at $100/month and incrementally increasing until we found resistance at $250/month.

This simple strategy worked because:

  • Enterprise deals (100+ seats) translated to $25,000 MRR per client
  • We targeted 10-15% of the value created (for a $2M/month impact, $250/seat is conservative)
  • Implementation became part of the value (not just software)

At 18:20 in the video, we show how one HVAC company justified the cost: our solution increased their monthly revenue by $2M - making our $25,000 MRR charge an obvious yes.

Real-World Implementation Challenges

Three unexpected challenges emerged when moving from simulation to reality:

1. The "Perfect Storm" Problem

Sometimes multiple calls would connect simultaneously despite staggered timing. Claude helped design a call routing system that automatically redirected extra connections to available agents.

2. Regional Number Regulations

Different states had varying telecom compliance rules. We used Claude to analyze regulations and build location-aware dialing protocols.

3. Human Resistance

Some sales teams feared being replaced. We positioned Clarbo as a "force multiplier" - letting top performers focus on closing while handling the tedious dialing.

Creating Regulatory Moats Against AI Competition

In an era where AI can replicate most software, we intentionally built around regulatory requirements:

  • A2P number registration (legally required for mass dialing)
  • State-by-state telecom compliance
  • Carrier-specific throughput limits

These created natural barriers that pure AI solutions couldn't bypass. As shown at 25:10 in the video, our human team handles compliance paperwork that Claude can't - blending AI efficiency with human oversight.

Why We Made Our Codebase Model-Agnostic

Early on, we made a critical architectural decision: never lock ourselves into one AI model. Here's how we future-proofed Clarbo:

1. Standardized Interfaces

All AI interactions go through adapter layers that can swap Claude for DeepSeek or Codex in minutes.

2. Token Cost Monitoring

Real-time dashboards track compute costs per feature, allowing us to shift workloads to the most cost-effective model.

3. Prompt Portability

We maintain parallel prompt libraries formatted for each major model's quirks.

This approach saved us when Claude's token costs spiked - we seamlessly shifted 40% of workloads to other models without service disruption.

Key Takeaways for AI SaaS Builders

Building Clarbo taught us that AI success requires more than technical implementation - it demands strategic problem selection and business model design.

In summary: 1) Target problems where customers already spend money (we enhanced existing power dialers), 2) Build regulatory or implementation moats, 3) Price based on value created not costs, and 4) Maintain model flexibility. The future belongs to AI-native businesses that combine technical prowess with strategic positioning.
