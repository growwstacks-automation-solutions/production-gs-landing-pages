The 6-Point AI Foundation Most Teams Miss

AI isn't magic - it's only as good as the context you provide. In the webinar, Miles England revealed that 83% of failed AI implementations stem from missing foundational setup. Before activating any agents, six core configurations transform generic outputs into tailored results:

Critical Insight: HubSpot's AI sources tab (Settings > AI > Data Sources) is your control center. Without these six profiles configured, agents lack business context and deliver generic responses that frustrate teams.

1. Brand Kit

Where AI learns your voice, tone, colors and messaging guidelines. Without this, content sounds like generic AI (because it is).

2. Company Profile

Provides industry, size, location and value proposition context. Missing this means AI can't align recommendations with your business model.

3. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

The single most important configuration. Your ICP tells AI who matters, influencing prospecting suggestions, deal prioritization and customer conversations.

4. User Profiles

Teaches AI team roles and responsibilities so it can route questions appropriately (e.g., "I'm the Director of Partnerships").

5. Buyer Profiles

Documents how your buyers think and what matters to them - powering better messaging and engagement suggestions.

6. Selling Profile

New for 2026, this captures your sales motion and style so AI supports (rather than conflicts with) your process.

At 18:32 in the video, Miles demonstrates how these profiles roll up into HubSpot's AI data sources - the foundation every agent builds upon. Teams that skip this step waste months tuning AI that never quite "gets" their business.

Customer Agent: 24/7 Support Without Hiring

Derek Reynolds' demo revealed how the customer agent handles 63% of routine support queries automatically - while logging full transcripts in HubSpot. The key is configuring three knowledge sources:

Pro Tip: For eCommerce businesses, Derek showed how to display order tracking info by adding a simple prompt to CRM fields: "Your UPS tracking number is [number]. Track it here: https://www.ups.com/track?tracknum=[number]"

Knowledge Sources That Matter

1. Website/URLs: The agent can scrape public knowledge bases (like HubSpot's docs) or specific pages you provide. Avoid robot.txt blocked sites.

2. Files: Upload PDFs, Word docs or text files with product manuals, onboarding guides or troubleshooting steps.

3. CRM Fields: Select which contact/company fields to expose (like order status). Enable editing for fields like phone numbers.

Advanced: API Actions

For integrations like shipping carriers, configure API calls that let customers check order status directly in chat. The demo showed a working UPS tracking lookup that took 15 minutes to set up.

At 32:15, Derek emphasizes that world-class agents require iterations: "Start simple, then refine based on chat transcripts and user feedback (thumbs up/down). The first version gets you 80% there - the remaining 20% comes from tuning."

Prospecting Agent: Your Automated BDR

Chloe Papy demonstrated how the prospecting agent researches accounts and drafts hyper-personalized emails - cutting 6 hours/week from outbound workflows. The secret? Connecting it to your Target Accounts and ICP.

How It Works

1. Define Markets: Create filters based on your ICP (industry, tech stack, revenue) to auto-qualify accounts.

2. Enroll Accounts: From Target Accounts, select companies matching your market criteria. The agent researches them automatically.

3. Review Outreach: The agent drafts emails citing specific findings (e.g., "Managing 10-day steel door turnarounds is impressive"). At 47:20, Chloe shows how to edit these before sending.

Key Setting: Under Advanced Instructions, add seniority-based messaging rules like "For C-level executives, keep emails under 100 words with clear ROI."

What It Researches

  • Company summaries (size, growth, challenges)
  • Web presence and values alignment
  • Job openings (indicates expansion)
  • Product/tech analysis
  • Competitive positioning

Chloe's pro tip: "Start with Review Before Sending enabled. Once you're confident in the quality (usually after 20-30 emails), switch to auto-send with your approval rules."

Data Agent: Automatic CRM Enrichment

HubSpot's Maitri Sheath demonstrated how the data agent researches companies and creates "smart properties" in 3 minutes - work that typically takes hours manually.

Real-World Example

For a plant care company post-conference, Maitri created a "Manages Commercial Property?" smart property by:

  1. Clicking "Add Column" in the companies view
  2. Typing the plain-English question: "Does this company manage commercial real estate properties?"
  3. Letting the agent scan websites and create a yes/no column

At 1:18:45: Maitri filters to commercial property managers only, then creates a second smart property showing their primary competitors - all without leaving HubSpot.

Smart Property Ideas

  • Recent funding status
  • Hiring growth signals
  • New market expansion
  • Technology stack changes
  • Executive team updates

Key benefit: Every smart property becomes a filter for segmentation and alerts.
