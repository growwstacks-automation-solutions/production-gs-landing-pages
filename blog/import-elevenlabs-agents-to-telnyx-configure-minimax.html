Why Migrate ElevenLabs to Telnyx?

Many businesses invested time and resources into developing sophisticated voice agents with ElevenLabs, only to discover they need more control over telephony infrastructure or better enterprise features. Telnyx provides carrier-grade voice services with global coverage, advanced call routing, and detailed analytics - but migrating often means losing your carefully tuned brand voice.

This integration solves that problem by letting you bring your existing ElevenLabs agent logic to Telnyx while replacing the voice synthesis with a similar-sounding Minimax voice. The result? All the benefits of Telnyx's infrastructure without sacrificing voice consistency your customers recognize.

Key benefit: Maintain your agent's personality and conversation flow while gaining Telnyx's enterprise telephony features and Minimax's cost-effective voice synthesis.

Step 1: Get Your ElevenLabs API Key

Before beginning the migration, you'll need access to your ElevenLabs account and API credentials. Log into your ElevenLabs dashboard and navigate to the API keys section (typically under account settings).

Create a new API key if you don't have one available, or locate your existing key. This key will allow Telnyx to authenticate with ElevenLabs and retrieve your agent configuration during the import process.

Security tip: Create a dedicated API key for this migration rather than using a master key. You can revoke it after the migration is complete.

Step 2: Configure Telnyx Integration

With your ElevenLabs API key in hand, log into your Telnyx account. Navigate to the "Import Systems" section where you'll find options to connect various AI platforms.

Select ElevenLabs from the available providers. You'll be prompted to enter your API key and give the connection a name (like "ElevenLabs Migration"). This establishes the bridge between Telnyx and your ElevenLabs account.

Pro tip: Name your connection descriptively if you manage multiple agents or environments (e.g., "Production - ElevenLabs Hotel Agent").

Step 3: Import Your Agent

After authenticating, click "Load Available Assistants" to see all agents available in your ElevenLabs account. The system will display agent names and basic details to help you identify the correct one.

Select your agent (in our example, the "5 Seasons" hotel service agent) and initiate the import. Telnyx will copy the agent configuration including its personality, conversation flow, and knowledge base - everything except the actual voice synthesis.

Note: The import process typically takes less than a minute, but complex agents with extensive knowledge bases may take slightly longer.

Step 4: Configure Minimax Voice

Once imported, edit your agent in Telnyx and navigate to the Voices section. Here you'll select English (or your primary language) as the voice language, then choose "Minimax Voices" as the provider.

Listen to the available Minimax voices to find one that closely matches your original ElevenLabs voice. The system provides samples you can compare side-by-side with your existing voice to ensure brand consistency.

Voice matching tip: Focus on similar pitch, tone, and speech patterns rather than trying to find an exact match. Small differences are less noticeable in full conversations than in isolated samples.

Voice Matching Techniques

Finding the perfect Minimax voice to match your ElevenLabs agent requires more than just quick listening. Create a standardized test script that includes various sentence types (questions, statements, numbers) your agent typically uses.

Record this script with your original ElevenLabs voice, then generate samples with potential Minimax matches. Compare them systematically, noting which elements (pitch, pacing, emphasis) differ most. At 1:45 in the video tutorial, we demonstrate this comparison process.

Professional technique: Have team members blind test the voices without knowing which is which. If they can't consistently identify the original, you've found a good match.

Testing Your Migrated Agent

Before going live with your migrated agent, conduct thorough testing. Start with scripted test calls that cover all major conversation flows. Then progress to unscripted testing where team members pretend to be customers with various requests.

Pay special attention to pronunciation of industry terms, proper names, and numbers - these often reveal subtle voice differences. Adjust your Minimax voice settings or consider minor script modifications if needed.

Quality assurance: Document any noticeable differences from your original voice and decide whether they're acceptable or require further tuning. Most minor variations become unnoticeable after a few days of use.
