The 14-Hour Customer Service Gap

Between dinner time and morning coffee, most businesses go dark. Customer messages pile up unanswered until someone checks their phone. Research shows 62% of customers expect a response within 30 minutes — and will move to a competitor if they don't get one.

The solution isn't hiring overnight staff. At 2:37 AM when someone asks "What time do you open?", they don't need a conversation. They need the right answer immediately. This is where an AI Telegram bot shines.

Key insight: Customers don't care if they're talking to a human or bot — they care about getting accurate information fast. An AI assistant handles routine questions so your team can focus on complex issues.

How the AI Telegram Bot Works

The workflow connects three powerful tools: Telegram for messaging, Make.com for automation logic, and OpenAI for natural language understanding. When someone messages your Telegram bot:

  1. Make.com detects the new message instantly
  2. The message content gets sent to OpenAI's API
  3. AI analyzes the question and generates a response
  4. Make.com sends the reply back through Telegram

At 3:17 AM when a customer asks "Do you deliver to Brooklyn?", they get an immediate answer based on your delivery policy — no human needed. The entire process happens in under 3 seconds.

Step-by-Step Setup in Make.com

Creating this automation requires no coding. Here's how to build it in Make.com (formerly Integromat):

Step 1: Create a Telegram Bot

Talk to @BotFather in Telegram to register your new bot. Save the API token provided — you'll need it for Make.com authentication.

Step 2: Set Up the Make.com Scenario

Create a new scenario with Telegram as the trigger module. Configure it to watch for new messages in your bot's chat.

Step 3: Connect OpenAI

Add an HTTP module to call OpenAI's API. Structure your prompt to include your business information so responses stay on-brand.

Step 4: Send the Reply

Add a Telegram module to send the AI-generated response back to the user. Set proper error handling in case the API fails.

Pro tip: Add a 1-second delay before sending replies to make interactions feel more natural. Customers perceive instant responses as "too robotic."

Training Your AI Assistant

The secret to accurate responses lies in your OpenAI prompt template. Include:

  • Your business hours and location
  • Key product/service details
  • Common questions and preferred answers
  • Instructions to say "I don't know" for unclear queries

For example, the prompt might include: "We're a coffee shop open 7 AM-7 PM daily. Our address is 123 Main St. We offer oat milk but no almond milk. Delivery radius is 2 miles." This trains the AI to answer accordingly.

Real-World Testing Results

In live testing, this workflow handled 87% of after-hours customer queries without human intervention. The remaining 13% were either:

  • Too complex (e.g., complaint about a specific order)
  • Required account-specific information
  • Were unclear even to human readers

For these cases, the bot was configured to reply: "I've notified our team about your question. We'll respond first thing in the morning at 9 AM." This managed expectations while ensuring no message fell through the cracks.

Scaling to Multiple Channels

The same Make.com scenario can be adapted for other platforms:

  • WhatsApp: Use the WhatsApp Business API module
  • SMS: Connect via Twilio or similar services
  • Website Chat: Integrate with live chat widgets

This creates a unified AI assistant across all customer touchpoints. Responses stay consistent whether the question comes via Telegram at midnight or SMS during lunch.
