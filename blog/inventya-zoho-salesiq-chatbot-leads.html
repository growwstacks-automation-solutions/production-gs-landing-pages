The Lead Capture Problem Most Businesses Face

Before implementing Zoho SalesIQ, Inventya faced the same challenge as most service businesses: website visitors would browse their offerings, have questions, but often leave without filling out a contact form. "We knew we were missing opportunities," says CEO Aldo Dionus in the video interview. "People want to talk to someone immediately when they're researching strategic services."

The traditional solutions — contact forms and email inquiries — created manual work for their team. Advisors wasted time entering lead details into their CRM and following up with basic information that could have been provided automatically. During evenings and weekends, when no staff were available to respond, potential leads would often move on to competitors.

68% of B2B buyers prefer to start with chat: Research shows most business buyers begin their purchase journey by asking questions via live chat rather than filling out forms. Without chatbot automation, companies miss these critical first interactions.

How Zoho SalesIQ Solves Manual Lead Entry

Zoho SalesIQ provided Inventya with a live chat system that integrates directly with their Zoho CRM. The basic functionality allows website visitors to initiate conversations that get routed to available team members. But the real breakthrough came when they enhanced it with two key features:

  1. Automated lead capture: When a visitor provides contact information during a chat, SalesIQ automatically creates a lead record in Zoho CRM with the full conversation history.
  2. Chatbot availability: The system can handle initial conversations via bot when human agents aren't available, ensuring 24/7 lead qualification.

"What surprised us was how complete the integration is," Dionus notes. "Every chat transcript, visitor location, and even the pages they viewed on our site gets attached to their CRM record. Our advisors have full context before they even make the first call."

Adding ChatGPT for Smarter Conversations

The second phase of Inventya's implementation connected SalesIQ to ChatGPT through Zoho's AI integration capabilities. This transformed their chatbot from a simple FAQ responder to an intelligent assistant that could:

  • Answer complex questions about their services using natural language
  • Qualify leads by asking strategic follow-up questions
  • Route conversations to the most appropriate advisor based on needs
  • Provide personalized recommendations based on the visitor's business type

"We trained the AI with our service descriptions, common client questions, and even our whitepapers," explains Dionus. "Now when someone asks 'How can you help scale my manufacturing business?' the bot responds with specific, relevant information rather than generic marketing copy."

From Chat to CRM: The Automated Workflow

The complete automation workflow Inventya built works like this:

  1. A visitor initiates a chat on Inventya's website
  2. The ChatGPT-powered bot handles initial qualification questions
  3. When ready, the visitor provides contact details (or the bot detects them from returning visitors)
  4. SalesIQ creates a lead record in Zoho CRM with all conversation history
  5. The system tags the lead based on conversation topics and routes it to the appropriate team
  6. Advisors receive notifications with full context before making first contact

Key integration point: The system uses Zoho Flow to connect SalesIQ with their other Zoho apps (CRM, Projects, and Sign) ensuring the lead data flows seamlessly through their entire client onboarding process.

The Business Impact: Hours Saved and Leads Captured

Since implementing this automation, Inventya has seen measurable improvements across their sales process:

  • 37% more qualified leads captured from their website compared to the contact form era
  • 15-20 minutes saved per lead by eliminating manual data entry
  • 28% of new clients now come through after-hours chatbot-initiated conversations
  • Higher quality leads due to the AI's pre-qualification questions

"The biggest benefit isn't even the time savings," says Dionus. "It's that we're capturing conversations with potential clients who would have previously bounced from our site. The bot gets them talking, answers their immediate questions, and gets us their contact details — all before a human ever gets involved."

Implementation Tips from Inventya's Experience

For businesses considering a similar implementation, Inventya's CEO offers these key lessons:

  1. Start with your most common questions: Train your chatbot on the 20% of questions that account for 80% of inquiries first.
  2. Design smooth handoffs: Create clear rules for when the bot should transfer to a human agent.
  3. Map all CRM fields: Ensure every piece of data collected in chat gets mapped to the correct CRM field.
  4. Monitor and refine: Review chat transcripts weekly to identify new questions to add to your knowledge base.

"The system gets smarter over time," notes Dionus. "We add new question responses every month based on actual conversations we're seeing. The ROI has been tremendous."
