What Is the JenesisNow-Zapier Integration?

Insurance agencies using JenesisNow often struggle with disconnected systems. Client information gets entered into JenesisNow, then manually re-entered into CRMs, email systems, and accounting platforms. This duplication creates errors, wastes time, and delays follow-ups.

The new Zapier integration solves this by creating automated pipelines between JenesisNow and other business apps. When a trigger event occurs in JenesisNow (like adding a new client), Zapier automatically sends that data to your other systems without manual intervention.

5,000+ app connections: Zapier acts as a universal translator between JenesisNow and virtually any modern business application including Salesforce, HubSpot, QuickBooks, Gmail, Outlook, Slack, and hundreds of specialized insurance tools.

Current Trigger Events Available

The initial release of the integration supports two key trigger events that cover the most critical automation needs for insurance agencies:

1. New Client Added

Triggers when a new client profile is created in JenesisNow. The workflow receives the complete client record including contact information, policy details, and custom fields. This is ideal for:

  • Automatically adding clients to your CRM
  • Sending welcome emails or onboarding sequences
  • Creating tasks in project management tools

2. New Policy Created

Triggers when a new policy is added to a client's profile. The workflow receives policy details including type, coverage amounts, and effective dates. Common uses include:

  • Policy confirmation emails to clients
  • Internal notifications to account managers
  • Revenue tracking in accounting systems

Setting Up Your First Zap

Creating your first automated workflow between JenesisNow and another app takes about 5 minutes. Here's the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Access Zapier from JenesisNow

Click the Zapier icon in JenesisNow's interface (typically in the top navigation bar). This will prompt you to sign in to your Zapier account or create a free account if you don't have one.

Step 2: Create a New Zap

In Zapier, click "Create Zap" and select JenesisNow as your trigger app. Choose either "New Client Added" or "New Policy Created" as your trigger event.

Step 3: Connect Your JenesisNow Account

Zapier will guide you through authenticating with your JenesisNow credentials. This establishes a secure connection between the systems.

Step 4: Set Up Your Action

Choose the app you want to send data to (like your CRM or email system). Select the specific action (like "Create Contact" or "Send Email") and map the JenesisNow fields to the corresponding fields in your action app.

Pro Tip: Zapier's interface shows sample data from JenesisNow as you set up field mappings, making it easy to verify you're sending the right information to each field in your destination app.

Testing and Verifying Workflows

Before activating any new Zap, it's crucial to test that data is transferring correctly between systems. Zapier provides robust testing tools:

Trigger Testing

After setting up your trigger, Zapier will retrieve sample data from JenesisNow. Review this data to ensure all expected fields are included and formatted correctly.

Action Testing

Zapier lets you perform a test run of your action before turning on the automation. Verify that the test record appears correctly in your destination app with all fields properly mapped.

Live Monitoring

After activation, Zapier provides a history log showing every time your workflow runs. You can review these logs to confirm ongoing successful operation.

Planned Future Updates

The initial integration covers two fundamental trigger events, but JenesisNow plans to expand the capabilities based on user feedback. Expected future updates include:

Additional Triggers

Triggers for policy renewals, payment received events, and status changes are in development. These will allow more comprehensive automation of the client lifecycle.

Two-Way Sync

Future versions may include actions that update JenesisNow based on events in other systems, creating true bidirectional automation.

Custom Field Support

Enhanced support for custom fields in client and policy records will provide even more flexibility in data mapping.
