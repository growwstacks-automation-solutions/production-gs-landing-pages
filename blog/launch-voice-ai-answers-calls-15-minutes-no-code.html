Training With Your Business Knowledge

The quality of your AI agent depends directly on the quality of information you provide. Upload your menu, pricing, policies, FAQs - anything customers typically ask about. At 9:10 in the video, we demonstrate organizing this information into logical categories.

Don't just dump documents and expect good results. Structure the information as you'd explain it to a new employee. Use clear, simple language and anticipate follow-up questions customers might ask.

Critical insight: Well-organized knowledge allows your AI to handle 70% of routine inquiries without human intervention, freeing your staff for higher-value tasks.

Building Conversation Flows

Conversation flows map how calls should progress - from greeting to resolution. The platform provides templates for common business types, which you can customize for your needs.

Avoid the mistake of trying to script every possible conversation. At 11:30 in the tutorial, we show how to define main conversation paths while letting the AI handle natural variations. Modern AI excels at adapting to unexpected inputs while staying on track.

Key flows to include: greeting, main inquiry handling, order processing, appointment booking, complaint resolution, and transfer to human when needed. Test each flow thoroughly before going live.

Testing and Optimizing

Before launching, test your agent extensively. Call it yourself. Have team members try to "break" it with unusual requests. Pay special attention to edge cases - questions outside your knowledge base, angry customers, heavy accents, or fast talkers.

The platform provides analytics showing where conversations succeed or fail. At 14:50 in the video, we demonstrate using these insights to improve your agent. Regular optimization based on real call data dramatically improves performance over time.

Growth hack: Set up alerts for mentions of competitors, complaints, or requests for unavailable products. These insights can reveal business opportunities you might otherwise miss.
