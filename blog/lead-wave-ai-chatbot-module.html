What Makes Lead Wave's AI Chatbot Different?

Lead Wave offers two distinct chatbot solutions - Chatbot Flow and AI Chatbot. While Chatbot Flow uses predefined rules and responses, the AI Chatbot module leverages large language models like ChatGPT and Gemini to generate dynamic, context-aware answers.

The key advantage? The AI Chatbot can understand natural language questions and provide accurate responses about your specific business - something that would require hundreds of manual rules in a traditional chatbot. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how it correctly answers varied questions about office locations and product details after being fed just one block of company information.

80% reduction in support tickets: Businesses using Lead Wave's AI Chatbot report dramatically fewer basic inquiries about store hours, locations and product specs - freeing staff to handle complex issues.

Step 1: Setting Up Your AI Provider

Before creating your chatbot, you need to configure at least one AI provider in Lead Wave's settings. Currently supported options are OpenAI (ChatGPT), Google Gemini, and DeepSeek.

Navigate to Settings → AI Chatbot → Add Provider. You'll need to:

Step 1: Name Your Provider

Give it a recognizable name like "Our ChatGPT" or "Marketing Bot Gemini".

Step 2: Select Provider Type

Choose between OpenAI or Gemini (DeepSeek coming soon).

Step 3: Enter API Key

Paste your valid API key from the respective provider.

Step 4: Configure Model Settings

Select which model to use (GPT-3.5, GPT-4, etc.), set temperature (controls creativity), and max tokens per response.

Pro Tip: If giving access to team members, set token limits to control costs. A 500-token limit prevents extremely long responses while covering most customer queries.

Step 2: Configuring the Chatbot Behavior

With your provider set up, navigate to the AI Chatbot section and create a new chatbot instance. This is where you'll define how it interacts with customers.

At minimum, you need to:

  • Name your chatbot (e.g. "Acme Support Bot")
  • Select the AI provider you configured
  • Choose a personality type (friendly, professional, etc.)
  • Assign a dedicated WhatsApp number (cannot share with other flows)

The video shows this process clearly at 3:40 - notice how the presenter selects their previously configured OpenAI provider and assigns a new number.

The Critical Step Most Businesses Miss

At 4:15 in the tutorial, you'll see the most important configuration - entering your company information. This is what enables the AI to answer specific questions about your business accurately.

You should include:

  • Company name and description
  • All office/store locations with addresses
  • Products/services offered
  • Contact numbers and emails
  • Owner/management names
  • Business hours
  • Common policies (returns, shipping, etc.)

Accuracy matters: The AI will generate responses based on this information. At 5:30, the video demonstrates how incomplete info leads to fictional answers - like making up an office location that doesn't exist.

Setting Up Trigger Words

Unlike traditional chatbots that respond to every message, Lead Wave's AI Chatbot requires specific trigger words to activate. This prevents unnecessary API calls for unrelated messages.

Common triggers include:

  • "start" - Begins the AI conversation
  • "help" - Shows assistance options
  • "agent" - Transfers to human support
  • "end" - Concludes the AI session

At 6:50 in the video, you'll see how typing "start" initiates the chatbot interaction, while subsequent messages are handled intelligently until "end" is received.

Testing and Refining Your Chatbot

Before going live, thoroughly test your chatbot by asking every type of question customers might pose. The video demonstrates this at 7:30 with queries about locations, contacts and products.

Look for:

  • Inaccurate responses (add missing info to your company details)
  • Overly verbose answers (reduce max tokens)
  • Off-brand tone (adjust personality setting)
  • Unnecessary API calls (add more trigger words)

Iterate until the bot provides concise, accurate answers that match your brand voice.

AI Chatbot Best Practices

Based on implementations we've done for clients, here are proven ways to maximize your Lead Wave AI Chatbot's effectiveness:

1. Dedicate a Number

As emphasized in the video at 3:10, always use a separate WhatsApp number for your AI chatbot to avoid conflicts with other flows.

2. Start Simple

Begin with GPT-3.5 before upgrading to GPT-4 - it's cheaper and sufficient for most basic queries.

3. Set Clear Boundaries

Configure your chatbot to transfer to humans for sensitive topics like complaints or refunds.

4. Monitor Regularly

Review conversation logs weekly to identify needed information additions or phrasing improvements.

Real-world results: Our ecommerce clients see 60-80% of pre-sales questions handled automatically by their Lead Wave AI chatbots, with customer satisfaction scores matching human agents for basic inquiries.
