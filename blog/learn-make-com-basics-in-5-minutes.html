What Is Make.com and Why It Matters

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is the secret weapon for entrepreneurs who want to automate their business without coding. Unlike complex platforms that require technical skills, Make.com lets you create powerful workflows by simply connecting apps with a visual interface.

The platform solves a critical problem: time wasted on repetitive tasks. Most business owners spend hours each week manually transferring data between apps, sending follow-up emails, or updating spreadsheets. Make.com eliminates this drudgery by automating these processes behind the scenes.

Key benefit: For every hour you spend setting up a Make.com automation, you save 10+ hours per month in manual work. That's why over 500,000 businesses use it to scale their operations.

Getting Started With Your Free Account

Getting started with Make.com takes less than 2 minutes. Simply visit make.com and sign up for a free account - no credit card required. The free plan gives you 1,000 operations per month, which is enough to test most basic automations.

Once logged in, you'll land on the dashboard that shows your active scenarios (automations), available credits, and team members. As a beginner, focus on three main areas: the Scenario tab (where you build automations), the Organization tab (account settings), and the Connections tab (where you link your apps).

Key Dashboard Features Explained

The Make.com interface contains several important sections that beginners often overlook. In the Organization tab, you'll find your credit usage - this shows how many operations you've used in the current billing period. The subscription area displays your plan details and when your credits reset.

Two particularly useful features are Variables and Scenario Properties. Variables let you store data (like customer emails or order numbers) to use across multiple automations. Scenario Properties contain metadata about your workflows that can help with troubleshooting.

Pro tip: Always name your scenarios based on their function (like "New Lead Email Sequence") rather than generic names. This makes them easier to manage as your library grows.

Understanding Scenarios (Your First Automation)

A scenario in Make.com is simply a complete automation workflow. Think of it as a recipe with multiple steps: when X happens in one app, do Y in another app. For example, "When someone fills out a Typeform, send them a welcome email" is a complete scenario.

Scenarios consist of modules connected in a sequence. The first module is always a trigger - the event that starts the automation (like a new form submission). Subsequent modules are actions - what you want to happen next (like sending an email or updating a spreadsheet).

Make.com Credits Explained

Credits are Make.com's currency for measuring automation usage. Each operation in a scenario consumes credits - sending one email might use 1 credit, while a complex data transformation could use 5. The free plan provides 1,000 credits per month.

Your credit balance resets every 14 days on the free plan. For most beginners, this is more than enough to run several basic automations. Only upgrade to a paid plan when you consistently hit the limit or need more frequent scenario executions.

Important: Testing scenarios in the editor doesn't use credits. Only completed executions on active scenarios consume your balance.

Pro Tips for Beginners

The fastest way to learn Make.com is by copying existing templates. The platform offers hundreds of pre-built scenarios you can duplicate and modify for your needs. Start with simple workflows before attempting complex multi-app integrations.

Always test your scenarios before activating them. Make.com's visual debugger shows exactly how data flows between modules, helping you spot errors. For troubleshooting, the execution history provides detailed logs of every automation run.

Remember that most business automations follow the same basic pattern: trigger → action → action. Master this foundation before exploring advanced features like filters, routers, or data storage.
