How the Automation Works

This system combines three powerful tools: Make.com for workflow automation, ChatGPT for content generation, and Google Sheets as the control panel. The magic happens in the routing logic that connects these platforms.

As shown at 1:30 in the video, the automation has two main paths: one for generating content (using AI) and another for publishing to LinkedIn. The entire process is controlled through dropdown selections in a Google Sheet, making it accessible even for non-technical users.

Google Sheets Control Hub

Your Google Sheet becomes mission control with just four key columns (shown at 2:45 in the demo):

  1. Column A - Idea: Where you input raw content ideas (e.g., "AI works best with humans")
  2. Column B - LinkedIn Post Content: Auto-populated by ChatGPT with the complete post
  3. Column C - Image Link Path: Contains URL to the AI-generated image
  4. Column D - Control: Dropdown with workflow states (Pending → Generate Content → Ready for Review → Approved)

The entire automation triggers based on changes to the Control column. When you select "Generate Content," ChatGPT creates the post and image. When you change to "Approved," it publishes to LinkedIn.

Make.com Workflow Breakdown

The Make.com automation (visible at 3:20) has two parallel routes triggered by the Google Sheet status:

Route 1 - Generate Content: When Control column = "Generate Content", it triggers ChatGPT to create both the post text and matching image, then updates the sheet to "Ready for Review"

Route 2 - Publish Post: When Control column = "Approved", it takes the generated content and image, formats them for LinkedIn, and publishes automatically

This two-path structure ensures you always have human oversight before anything goes live, while still automating 90% of the work.

LinkedIn Publishing

The publishing module (demonstrated at 8:45) handles all the technical details:

  1. Downloads the AI-generated image from its hosted URL
  2. Formats the post content with proper line breaks and spacing
  3. Connects to LinkedIn via Make.com's native integration
  4. Sets visibility preferences (typically "Public")
  5. Posts and confirms success by updating the Google Sheet status

The entire publishing process happens in under 30 seconds once approved, and you can verify the live post at 9:30 in the video.
