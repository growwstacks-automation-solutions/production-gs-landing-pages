The Telephony Pain Points LiveKit Solves

Traditional voice agent deployments face two major challenges: complex infrastructure and call latency. Each call must travel from the user's device through carrier networks, then through a SIP trunk provider before finally reaching your agent. This multi-hop journey introduces points of failure and delays that degrade call quality.

LiveKit currently handles over 2 million daily calls across customer service, sales, and even emergency response systems. Their engineers built a custom SIP stack to support this volume, but recognized the inherent limitations of relying on third-party trunk providers for call routing.

40% latency reduction: By eliminating SIP trunk hops, LiveKit's native telephony service cuts call setup times nearly in half while maintaining carrier-grade reliability.

How Native Phone Numbers Change the Game

LiveKit Phone Numbers represent a fundamental shift in voice agent architecture. Instead of calls routing through multiple networks, agents deployed in LiveKit Cloud now reside in the same data centers as the telephony infrastructure.

This colocation provides three key benefits:

  • Simplified deployment: No SIP trunk configuration or carrier negotiations required
  • Reduced latency: Calls take the most direct path to your agents
  • Unified management: Phone numbers and agents controlled through one interface
