Long Context: Reality Check

The AI world is buzzing about Step Fun AI's Step 3.5 Flash release on Nvidia's NIM platform, with its 256,000 token context window. But behind the hype lies a messy reality - long context models often fail in predictable ways when applied to real marketing workflows.

While the technical achievement is impressive, marketers need to understand that simply stuffing more information into prompts doesn't solve fundamental issues with retrieval, reasoning, and consistency. At 2:15 in the video, the hosts highlight how these models can read an entire brand guide yet still write like a crypto bro - a frustration many marketing teams know all too well.

Key insight: Long context doesn't equal better understanding. Models may have access to more information but still struggle to apply it contextually or reconcile contradictions across documents.

Where Long Context Models Actually Break

Through testing various AI agent workflows, three consistent failure points emerge with long context models:

1. The Expensive Junk Drawer Problem

Just because a model can hold more information doesn't mean it can find the right piece when needed. Without careful structure, important details get lost in the noise, leading to generic or irrelevant outputs.

2. Reconciliation Roadblocks

Models can quote from multiple documents but often fail to notice or resolve contradictions between them. This creates outputs that blend incompatible ideas or mix outdated with current information.

3. Confident Hallucinations

Perhaps most dangerously, these models don't hallucinate randomly but develop consistent incorrect patterns - like deciding your brand always uses M dashes because of one email from 2022.

Practical impact: Social media managers report agents suddenly adopting strange vernacular like calling customers "besties" based on minimal examples in the training data.

The Brand Constitution Approach

The solution isn't to abandon long context but to structure it intelligently. The most effective pattern combines:

Brand Constitution

A short, stable document containing non-negotiable elements:

  • Voice and tone guidelines
  • Forbidden phrases and compliance rules
  • Primary sources of truth
  • Allowed vs prohibited content categories

Job Packet

The changing operational details for each task:

  • Current campaign messaging
  • Audience segment specifics
  • Platform constraints (character limits, etc.)
  • Recent performance learnings

At 4:30 in the video, the hosts demonstrate how this separation creates consistency while allowing flexibility. The constitution ensures brand safety while the job packet keeps content relevant.

The Citation Problem (It's Worse Than You Think)

One of the most dangerous failure modes occurs with research agents and citations. The issue isn't missing sources but:

  • Misaligned citations: Sources that mention the topic but don't support the specific claim
  • Decorative references: Citations added for appearance rather than substance
  • Confidence without validation: Models asserting claims as fact based on tangential mentions

The solution lies in explicit instructions to:

  1. Flag any claims not directly supported by provided sources
  2. Separate verified from unverified information
  3. Request clarification when sources are ambiguous

Critical safeguard: Build workflows where human review is required for any content making claims in regulated industries like health or finance.

Model Council: When Disagreement Helps

Perplexity's Model Council approach - running queries across multiple frontier models and synthesizing agreements/disagreements - offers interesting possibilities:

For Compliance-Critical Content

Model disagreements serve as red flags indicating areas needing human verification. This is particularly valuable for:

  • Legal and medical claims
  • Financial disclosures
  • Regulated industry communications

For Creative Work

Disagreements become brainstorming fuel, providing multiple angles on:

  • Content positioning
  • Messaging approaches
  • Campaign themes

The key is using a fast, inexpensive model for initial passes and only escalating to multiple models for high-stakes outputs where the additional cost justifies the quality improvement.

Agent Governance Never Goes Away

As agents gain more capabilities, governance becomes more critical. Essential safeguards include:

Access Controls

  • Research agents: Read-only access
  • Content publishing agents: Approval workflows
  • Spend management agents: Hard budget caps

Operational Guardrails

  • Kill switches for problematic behavior
  • Comprehensive audit trails
  • Human approval gates for sensitive actions

At 7:45 in the video, the hosts emphasize that the right access questions never disappear - they just become more complex as agents handle more business functions.

Price Per Task: The Adult Metric

Abacus.ai's LiveBench introduces an important shift from price per token to price per task - a metric that actually matters for business operations.

Why it matters: Cheap tokens mean little if an agent requires multiple attempts to produce usable output. Task-based pricing aligns incentives with real-world outcomes.

Effective task definition requires:

  • Clear acceptance criteria (brand compliance, platform requirements, etc.)
  • Quality thresholds (minimal edits needed)
  • Human scoring mechanisms for subjective elements

Implementation tip: Start with small, well-defined tasks like social media posts or email variants before scaling to complex deliverables like full campaigns.
