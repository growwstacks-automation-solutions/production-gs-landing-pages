The Cold Email Personalization Problem

Cold email response rates plummet when messages feel generic, yet personalizing at scale seems impossible for most businesses. The traditional options are equally painful: manually researching each prospect (time-consuming) or paying $2,000+/year for platforms like Clay (expensive).

This workflow solves both problems by leveraging Make.com's automation power with cost-effective tools. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how we process 37 industrial real estate contacts in under an hour - scraping their LinkedIn profiles, generating two personalized sentences with ChatGPT, and preparing everything for email deployment.

Key insight: Personalization doesn't require mentioning obscure details. Focusing on job title and tenure generates 82% of the lift in response rates while being automatable.

Workflow Overview

The complete system uses four connected components: Google Sheets (contact management), Apify (LinkedIn scraping), ChatGPT (content generation), and Make.com (orchestration). Here's how they work together:

  1. Make.com searches your Google Sheet for contacts without a "sent" marker
  2. For each new contact, it scrapes their LinkedIn profile using Apify ($10/1,000 profiles)
  3. The scraped data feeds into two ChatGPT modules that generate personalized opening sentences
  4. Make.com marks the contact as processed and moves to the next one

At 7:30 in the tutorial, you'll see the Apify scraper in action - extracting job titles, company tenure, and professional background information that forms the basis of personalization.

Setting Up Google Sheets

Your Google Sheet serves as both the contact list and progress tracker. The critical columns are:

  • LinkedIn profile URLs
  • Job titles (can be auto-filled from LinkedIn)
  • A "sent" column that Make.com updates after processing
  • Two columns for the generated personalized sentences

The workflow at 4:12 shows the exact Make.com module configuration that:

  1. Searches for rows where "sent" is empty
  2. Processes one contact at a time
  3. Immediately marks them as sent to prevent duplicates

Pro tip: Use data validation on your "sent" column to only allow specific values. This prevents errors if the sheet gets edited manually during automation.

Scraping LinkedIn Profiles

Apify's "Mass LinkedIn Profile Scraper" (10:45 in video) extracts:

  • Current job title and company
  • Tenure duration (both current position and overall at company)
  • Professional summary and experience details

Originally, the workflow used overall company tenure, but at 18:20 you'll see why we switched to current position duration. Some professionals change roles frequently within the same company, making overall tenure less relevant to their current responsibilities.

The scraper costs $10 per 1,000 profiles (1 cent each) and typically takes 5-10 seconds per profile. Make.com waits synchronously for the scrape to complete before proceeding.

ChatGPT Personalization

Two ChatGPT modules generate different personalization elements (22:30 in tutorial):

  1. Tenure-focused: "I see you've been [Job Title] at [Company] for [Duration]..."
  2. Role-focused: "As [Job Title], you understand the importance of [Relevant Challenge]..."

The key to effective AI prompts is providing examples of good outputs (24:50). The workflow uses a system message followed by two user/assistant pairs that demonstrate ideal personalization before making the actual request.

At 26:15, you'll see how we refined the prompts after initial tests produced awkward phrasing. The final version includes rules like "shorten long job titles" and "avoid assumptions about their impact."

Optimizing AI Outputs

The tutorial at 30:40 shows real-time prompt refinement when the first outputs weren't quite right. Common fixes include:

  • Simplifying long job titles ("Senior Director of Finance and Operations" → "Sr Director of Finance & Ops")
  • Removing presumptuous praise ("That's a strong start" → "That experience is valuable")
  • Ensuring the second sentence transitions smoothly to your value proposition

For industrial real estate prospects, the final ChatGPT outputs look like:

"I see you've been VP of Operations at Monomoy Properties REIT for over four years. That consistency shows strong leadership within the organization."

"As VP of Operations, your strategic oversight likely includes identifying cost-saving opportunities. We help reduce waste costs by renegotiating contracts and auditing invoices monthly."

Automating the Process

At 34:50, the workflow shows the complete automation cycle:

  1. Make.com processes one contact per minute
  2. Each contact undergoes LinkedIn scraping → ChatGPT personalization → Sheet update
  3. The "sent" column prevents reprocessing
  4. After 37 minutes, all contacts are personalized and ready for email deployment

This approach scales linearly - 100 contacts would take about 1.5 hours with the same $1 in Apify costs and minimal ChatGPT expenses.

Implementation note: The scenario runs every minute but processes one contact per execution. This pacing prevents API rate limits while maintaining steady progress.

Integrating With Email Platforms

The final step (37:20) exports your Google Sheet as a CSV for upload to email platforms like Smartlead. Each row contains:

  • Contact information
  • Two personalized opening sentences
  • Any other merge fields needed for your template

Your email template uses these sentences as dynamic variables, creating unique openings while maintaining consistent messaging about your offer. The tutorial shows sample emails combining the personalized openings with a standard CTA about waste cost reduction services.

For teams using Salesforce or other CRMs, Make.com can push the data directly rather than using CSV export. This creates a complete closed-loop system from prospecting to outreach.
