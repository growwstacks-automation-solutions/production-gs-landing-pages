How Excel Template Automation Works

The system works by detecting placeholder fields in your Excel templates and replacing them with dynamic values from Make.com scenarios. You create one perfect template with all your formatting, formulas, and structure, then mark the variable fields with simple placeholders like {{customer_name}} or {{invoice_date}}.

When you upload this template to Make.com (shown at 0:45 in the video), the platform automatically detects all placeholder fields and makes them available for mapping in your automation scenarios. This means you can pull data from any connected app - CRMs, databases, eCommerce platforms - and insert it directly into your Excel files.

Setting Up Your First Template

Creating your initial template is the most important step. Start by designing your Excel file exactly how you want the final output to look, complete with all formatting, logos, and static content. Then identify which fields should change with each generation - these become your placeholders.

At 0:30 in the tutorial, you'll see a simple template with three types of placeholders: single fields (filled one, filled two) and a table area (table data). The key is to make your placeholder names descriptive so they're easy to identify when mapping data in Make.com later.

Pro Tip: Use a consistent naming convention for placeholders like {{invoice_number}} or {{product_sku}}. Avoid spaces or special characters to ensure compatibility across all connected apps.

Mapping Data to Placeholders

Once your template is uploaded to Make.com (via the CommandTarot integration shown at 1:00), you'll see all detected placeholders available for mapping in your scenario. The video demonstrates this at 1:30 where simple text values are mapped to test the functionality.

In real-world use, you'll typically map these placeholders to dynamic data from previous steps in your scenario - customer records from your CRM, order details from your eCommerce platform, or financial data from accounting software. Make.com's visual builder makes it simple to connect these data sources without coding.

Dynamic Table Generation

One of the most powerful features shown at 0:35 is dynamic table population. Instead of just filling individual cells, you can define entire table areas in your template that will expand to accommodate varying amounts of data - perfect for invoices with multiple line items or reports with changing data sets.

The tutorial demonstrates a simple 3-column table, but this approach scales to handle complex financial models, inventory lists, or data exports. When your scenario runs, Make.com automatically populates all rows with your mapped data while preserving all formulas and formatting from the template.

Delivery Options for Generated Files

At 2:15 in the video, you'll see two delivery methods in action: email attachment and direct download link. Make.com gives you complete flexibility in how you distribute generated files. Common options include:

  • Emailing files directly to clients or team members
  • Saving to cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, SharePoint)
  • Posting to collaboration tools (Slack, Teams)
  • Making available via secure download links

You can even chain multiple delivery methods - for example, emailing a copy to accounting while also saving to your document management system.
