Why Make.com Gmail Connections Fail

Gmail connections in Make.com fail for predictable reasons. Google's security policies require periodic reauthorization of third-party app access. The OAuth tokens that power your automations expire after 6 months or when Google detects unusual activity patterns.

This isn't a Make.com bug - it's a security feature. Google intentionally limits how long third-party apps can access your account without fresh authorization. The good news? Reconnecting takes less than 2 minutes when you know the right steps.

85% of Gmail connection failures can be fixed by simply reauthorizing the connection through Make.com's interface. No technical skills required.

Step-by-Step Fix for Failed Connections

When your Gmail connection fails, don't panic. Follow this exact sequence to restore functionality:

Step 1: Identify the Failed Connection

Navigate to your Make.com scenario and look for the Gmail module with a warning icon. This indicates the connection needs attention. Hover over the module to see the specific error message.

Step 2: Reauthorize or Create New Connection

Click the connection icon (usually looks like a plug or gear). You'll see two options: "Reauthorize" or "Create new connection." For most cases, reauthorizing works perfectly. If that fails, create a new connection from scratch.

Step 3: Complete Google's Authorization Flow

A new window will open asking you to sign in to Google. Use the same account your automation relies on. Review the permissions Make.com requests (basic profile + email access), then click "Allow."

Step 4: Verify Successful Reconnection

Return to Make.com and check that the warning icon has disappeared. Run a test execution to confirm emails are sending/receiving properly. The connection should now work for all scenarios using that Gmail account.

Preventing Future Connection Issues

While you can't eliminate connection failures entirely, these strategies reduce their frequency and impact:

  • Use a dedicated Google account for automations rather than your personal email
  • Enable 2-factor authentication on the Google account to maintain higher trust levels
  • Set calendar reminders to check connections every 3 months
  • Build error handling into your scenarios to pause workflows when connections fail
  • Monitor execution logs weekly for early warning signs of connection problems

For mission-critical automations, consider having a backup connection ready to swap in when primary connections fail. This redundancy keeps your workflows running while you address the root cause.
