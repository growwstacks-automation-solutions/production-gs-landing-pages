The 3 GHL Automation Builders Explained

Most GoHighLevel users never progress beyond basic automations, leaving hours of potential time savings on the table. The platform actually offers three distinct automation builders, each serving different purposes and skill levels.

At 2:15 in the video, the instructor reveals this critical framework: "There's three different parts to this, and a lot of people don't get this. They think it's just the automation builder, but there's really three different kinds." Understanding this progression is key to unlocking GHL's full potential.

The 3-tier automation mastery path: 1) Basic builder for simple workflows, 2) Advanced builder for complex multi-trigger automations, and 3) AI builder that 10X's your output once you've mastered the first two.

Basic Automation Builder Fundamentals

The foundation of all GHL automations begins with understanding triggers and actions. At 4:30 in the tutorial, the instructor demonstrates: "In every single automation, it starts with a trigger and then has actions... The trigger is the starting point."

Basic automations follow a simple sequence: Trigger → Action → (Optional Wait) → Additional Actions. For example, when a form is submitted (trigger), send an email (action), wait 1 day (optional), then send a text message (additional action). This linear approach works well for 80% of common business workflows.

Pro tip: Set default sender names and numbers in your automation settings (shown at 7:50) to avoid manually entering them for every email or SMS action.

Advanced Builder: Multiple Triggers & Drag-and-Drop

The advanced builder (accessible via Labs) transforms GHL into a powerhouse comparable to n8n or Make.com. As shown at 9:20, "This is more like n8n... where you can get really powerful" with features like:

  • Drag-and-drop interface for visual workflow building
  • Multiple triggers within single automations
  • Keyboard shortcuts for rapid editing
  • Zoomable canvas for complex workflows

The instructor emphasizes at 10:45: "With the original automation builder, you're pretty much having one automation within an automation... But with this, you could have multiple triggers to multiple actions." This enables sophisticated conditional logic that basic automations can't handle.

AI Builder: 10X Your Workflow Creation

GoHighLevel offers three automation builders that serve different purposes:

Triggers and actions form the foundation of all GoHighLevel automations:

Triggers are the starting events that initiate an automation (like form submissions or calendar bookings). Actions are the subsequent steps that execute after the trigger occurs (like sending emails, texts, or creating tasks).

  • Triggers: Form submissions, missed calls, etc.
  • Actions: Emails, SMS, task creation, etc.
  • Every automation begins with a trigger

  • Basic knowledge helps troubleshoot AI outputs
  • Prevents creating ineffective or broken automations
