Trigger Nodes: Workflow Starters

Every automation begins with a trigger - the event that kicks off your workflow. These three trigger types handle 90% of use cases:

1. Manual Trigger

The simplest way to test workflows. Click "Execute Workflow" to run on-demand - perfect for debugging and demonstrations. At 5:42 in the video, we show how to use manual triggers during development.

2. Schedule Trigger

Runs workflows automatically at set intervals - daily reports at 9 AM, weekly data syncs on Mondays, etc. The template includes common scheduling patterns like "weekdays only" and "every 15 minutes."

3. Webhook Trigger

The most powerful trigger type. Receives instant notifications from external services like form submissions, payment processors, or CRMs. At 7:20, we demonstrate configuring webhooks with Typeform and Stripe.

Pro tip: Always use test vs. production webhook URLs during development to prevent live data mishaps. The template includes this safety pattern.

Data Storage Options Compared

Choosing the right data storage method impacts workflow speed and maintainability. These three options cover most needs:

Google Sheets

Ideal when non-technical team members need access. Perfect for lead lists, order tracking, and simple reports. The template shows append/update/clear operations at 10:15.

Airtable

Handles relational data better than Sheets. Use for complex datasets with connections between tables (e.g., customers ↔ orders). At 12:30, we demonstrate linked records and multiple views.

n8n Data Table

The fastest option for workflow-only data. No API calls mean sub-second response times. The template includes examples of temporary data storage during multi-step processes.

Decision guide: Use Sheets for team collaboration, Airtable for complex relationships, and n8n tables for internal workflow data.
