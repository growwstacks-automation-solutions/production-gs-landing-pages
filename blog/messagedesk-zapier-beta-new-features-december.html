Zapier Integration (Beta)

Businesses using multiple tools know the frustration of manual data entry between systems. MessageDesk's new Zapier beta integration solves this by connecting your messaging workflows to over 8,500 other applications.

The integration works both ways - you can trigger actions in MessageDesk from other apps, or use MessageDesk events to trigger actions elsewhere. For example, when a new contact signs up through your website, Zapier can automatically create that contact in MessageDesk and send them a welcome text.

Real-world example: MessageDesk uses this internally to capture new user information in Loops, then automatically creates the contact in their workspace, sends a welcome text, and adds a comment to the thread for tracking.

To access the beta, visit docs.messaged.com for full documentation and a link to request access. The form asks about your intended use cases to help prioritize feature development. As Kyle from MessageDesk mentions in the video (at 1:45), they're particularly interested in learning what workflows customers want to build.

New Collaboration Features

Team messaging often becomes chaotic with multiple conversations happening simultaneously. MessageDesk's October release introduced comments and mentions, and the December update adds threaded comments and reactions to bring order to the chaos.

Threaded comments let you organize discussions by replying to specific comments, creating nested conversations that stay together. This is particularly useful for keeping internal notes separate from customer communications.

The new reactions feature provides lightweight ways for teams to acknowledge messages without typing responses. As shown in the video (at 3:30), you can use eye emoji to indicate you've seen a message, thumbs up for approval, or any other emoji that fits your team's communication style.

Relays & Automation

Manual message handling becomes unsustainable as your business grows. MessageDesk relays solve this by automating common workflows around your inbox.

Relays can automatically assign new conversations to team members, label incoming messages, or send responses to unknown contacts asking for information. As demonstrated in the video (at 4:15), keyword triggers let you automatically respond when customers text words like "sales" or "help."

Holiday-ready feature: The out-of-office relay automatically sends configured messages during specified time frames - perfect for holiday periods when you want to set expectations about response times.

For advanced users, relays can even send webhooks or combine multiple actions into custom workflows. MessageDesk plans to add more triggers and actions to relays in Q1 , expanding what's possible with this powerful automation tool.

Twilio Integration Improvements

Businesses using Twilio-connected numbers often struggle with fragmented conversation histories across different tools. MessageDesk's improved Twilio integration now syncs all messages sent through other Twilio-connected applications into a single MessageDesk view.

This means whether a message originates from your website chat, IVR system, or another Twilio-connected app, it will appear in MessageDesk alongside your direct text conversations. The integration maintains full context, giving your team one place to manage all customer communications.

Documentation at docs.messaged.com details how to connect Twilio numbers and what to expect from the eventstream integration. As Kyle mentions in the video (at 6:10), this provides complete visibility into all your Twilio-connected messaging activity.

Mass Messaging Enhancements

Sending messages to multiple contacts used to require manually adding each recipient. The December update introduces CSV uploads and label-based sending to streamline mass communications.

Now you can upload a CSV file of recipients or select contacts by labels (like "North Region" or "VIP Customers"). As shown in the video (at 7:30), you can also send to all conversations assigned to specific team members.

The new post-send actions take this further by letting you automatically label conversations or assign replies when sending messages. For example, after sending a promotional blast, you could automatically label all responses as "Follow-up Needed" and assign them to your sales team.

What's Coming Soon

MessageDesk's public roadmap at changelog.messaged.com reveals exciting features planned for Q1 . These include additional relay triggers/actions, scheduled messaging improvements, call forwarding for MessageDesk numbers, and enhanced roles/permissions.

The call forwarding feature will let MessageDesk numbers ring through to any phone - mobile, landline, or VoIP. This provides flexibility for businesses that want to maintain professional numbers but route calls to personal devices.

Users can submit and vote on feature ideas through the changelog site. As Kyle explains in the video (at 9:20), MessageDesk publishes detailed change logs for every update, maintaining transparency about product development.
