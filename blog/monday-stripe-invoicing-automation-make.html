The Manual Invoicing Problem

Most sales teams using monday.com face the same frustrating bottleneck. When a deal finally closes and moves to "Won" status, someone has to stop what they're doing, copy all the details into Stripe, manually create an invoice, and send it out. This process typically takes 15-20 minutes per invoice and introduces multiple points where errors can creep in.

Studies show that manual data entry results in invoice errors 18-25% of the time. These mistakes delay payments and create unnecessary back-and-forth with clients. Even when done perfectly, the manual process slows down your cash flow by 3-5 days on average.

The hidden cost: For a salesperson closing 10 deals per week, manual invoicing consumes 2.5-3.5 hours of productive time - equivalent to losing one full day per month just to administrative work.

How the Automation Works

The Make.com workflow solves this by creating a direct connection between monday.com and Stripe. When a deal's status changes to "Won" in monday.com, the automation instantly:

  1. Pulls all relevant deal details (client, services, amounts)
  2. Checks if the customer exists in Stripe
  3. Creates the customer record if needed
  4. Generates a complete Stripe invoice
  5. Attaches the PDF back to the monday.com deal
  6. Updates the invoice status field

This entire process happens in under 60 seconds, with no human intervention required. The client receives their invoice immediately, complete with online payment options.

monday.com Setup

Your monday.com deals board needs these minimum fields for the automation to work effectively:

  • Client Name - Company or individual name
  • Email - Used to match/create Stripe customers
  • Service Description - What you're invoicing for
  • Quantity - How many units
  • Unit Price - Price per unit
  • Total Amount - Calculated field (quantity × unit price)
  • Status - With "Won" as a status option

Additional recommended fields include payment terms, project/delivery date, and any applicable taxes. The more complete your deal records, the more professional your automated invoices will be.

Stripe Configuration

Before connecting to Make.com, ensure your Stripe account has:

  1. Tax rates configured if applicable
  2. Default payment terms set (net 15, net 30, etc.)
  3. Invoice templates customized with your branding
  4. Webhook endpoint available for payment notifications

The automation will use your existing Stripe settings, so take time to configure these properly. For businesses with multiple services, create products in Stripe that match your monday.com service items for cleaner invoicing.

Make.com Workflow Steps

The core automation in Make.com follows this sequence:

Step 1: Watch for Status Changes

The scenario starts with a monday.com "Watch Items" trigger monitoring your deals board for status changes to "Won". This acts as the starting point for the entire workflow.

Step 2: Retrieve Deal Details

Once triggered, the scenario pulls all relevant fields from the monday.com item - client name, email, service details, quantities, rates, and total amount.

Step 3: Customer Check

Make.com queries Stripe to see if a customer already exists with the email address from the deal. This determines which path the workflow takes next.

Step 4: Create Invoice

Using the deal details, the scenario creates a Stripe invoice with line items matching the services in monday.com. The invoice includes all necessary details and is marked as "finalized" so it's sent immediately.

Step 5: Generate PDF

Once created, the workflow generates a PDF version of the invoice and uploads it back to the monday.com deal as an attachment.

Step 6: Update Deal

Finally, the scenario updates the monday.com item with the Stripe invoice number, sent date, and changes the invoice status to "Sent".

Key Insight: The entire workflow typically executes in 45-60 seconds, compared to 15-20 minutes for manual processing. For businesses sending 50+ invoices monthly, this saves 12-16 hours of administrative work.

Handling New vs Existing Customers

The workflow intelligently handles both new and existing Stripe customers through a router module:

For existing customers: The workflow skips straight to invoice creation, pulling the customer ID from Stripe's records. This maintains all historical context and payment history in Stripe.

For new customers: The workflow first creates a customer record in Stripe using the email and company name from monday.com. It then proceeds with invoice creation using the new customer ID.

This dual-path approach ensures seamless operation regardless of whether the client exists in your Stripe account yet. All customer creation follows Stripe's standard fields and validation rules.

Tracking Invoice Status

The basic workflow can be extended to track invoice status back in monday.com by adding these modules:

  1. Stripe Webhook - Listens for invoice payment events
  2. Status Update - Changes monday.com invoice status when paid
  3. Payment Date - Records the actual payment date
  4. Notification - Alerts the salesperson via email or Slack

This creates a complete closed-loop system where your team has real-time visibility into which invoices have been paid, without needing to check Stripe separately. The data stays synchronized across both platforms automatically.

Scaling the Automation

Once the core workflow is implemented, consider these enhancements:

  • Recurring Billing - Automate subscription invoices based on recurring deal items
  • Multi-Currency - Handle international clients with currency conversion
  • Approval Workflows - Add manager approval for large deals before invoicing
  • Accounting Sync - Push paid invoice data to QuickBooks or Xero
  • Late Payment - Automate reminders for overdue invoices

The modular nature of Make.com makes it easy to add these components over time as your needs evolve. Start with the core invoicing automation, then expand based on your specific business requirements.
