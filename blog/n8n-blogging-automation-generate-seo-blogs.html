How the Blogging Automation Works

The workflow follows a meticulous 5-phase process that mirrors how professional content agencies operate:

Phase 1: Topic Refinement

Clients or users start by providing a topic, audience, and word count. The system engages in a brief dialog to clarify goals (like "convert to free quotes" for roofing companies) ensuring the final content aligns with business objectives.

Phase 2: Research

Using Tavily web search (free tier), the system finds 3-5 authoritative sources on the topic. For our roofing example, it pulled guides from industry associations and local business directories.

Phase 3: Section Mapping

The workflow divides the total word count into logical sections with precise word targets. A 1500-word article might become:

  • Introduction (200 words)
  • Material Comparisons (400 words)
  • Color Selection Guide (350 words)
  • Local Climate Considerations (300 words)
  • Conclusion & CTA (200 words)

Phase 4: SEO Optimization

The system identifies primary and secondary keywords through semantic analysis. For our roofing example, it surfaced "roof color Southwest Michigan" as a valuable local term.

Phase 5: Iterative Writing

Using Grock 4.1 writes each section while maintaining the full context of previously written content. A built-in word count verifier ensures each section hits its target within ±30 words.

Key Insight: By writing sections iteratively rather than all at once, the AI maintains coherence across long-form content while precisely hitting word count targets.

Word Count Accuracy System

The workflow includes a novel verification system that:

  1. Calculates the exact word count for each completed section
  2. Compares it to the target (with ±30 word tolerance)
  3. Requests AI to expand or condense if needed

This ensures clients get precisely what they request - whether it's a 800-word quick guide or a comprehensive 3000-word resource. The system handles the math so you don't have to micro-manage the AI.

Precision Matters: In testing, 92% of sections hit their word target on first attempt. The remaining 8% were corrected in one revision cycle.
