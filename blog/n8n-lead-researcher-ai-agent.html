The breakthrough came when we realized AI could automate the entire qualification process - researching companies, scoring them based on our specific criteria, and routing them appropriately - all before our team ever needs to get involved.

68% of salespeople report wasting time on unqualified leads, according to HubSpot research. This system eliminates that problem by qualifying leads before they reach your team.

How the AI Agent Works

The system consists of four specialized AI agents working in sequence. At 2:15 in the video, you can see the complete workflow diagram showing how information flows between each component.

First, an AI interest search agent investigates whether the lead has shown interest in services like yours. Then a general search agent gathers broad company information. These findings feed into a lead scoring agent that rates them 0-100. Finally, a notification agent routes the lead based on its score.

Step 1: Lead Submission

The workflow triggers when a new lead comes in through your booking system, CRM, or web form. In the demo, we use n8n's built-in form for simplicity, but this connects to any lead source.

Step 2: AI Research Phase

Two specialized agents research the lead concurrently - one focused on interest signals specific to your offerings, and one conducting general company research.

Step 3: Scoring & Routing

The system compares research findings against your qualification criteria to generate a score (0-100) that determines how the lead gets routed to your team.

AI Interest Research Agent

This specialized agent (shown at 4:30 in the video) looks for concrete signals that indicate a lead's potential interest in your specific services. For an AI agency, this means searching for evidence the company is adopting AI solutions.

The agent uses targeted queries to Perplexity (a research-focused AI tool) that look for:

  • Job postings for AI/ML roles
  • LinkedIn posts about AI initiatives
  • News articles mentioning AI projects
  • Website content about technology adoption
  • Industry reports showing automation trends

Pro Tip: You can customize the research focus for your industry. A marketing agency might search for "content marketing" or "SEO" signals instead of AI adoption.

General Search Agent

While the interest agent focuses narrowly, the general search agent (shown at 7:45) casts a wider net to understand the company's overall profile. This agent researches:

  • Company size and revenue estimates
  • Years in business and growth trajectory
  • Industry trends and challenges
  • Technology stack information
  • Key personnel details (when available)

The agent uses two separate Perplexity tools - one focused on company research and another specialized for LinkedIn profile searches of the individual lead.

Lead Scoring Agent

All research findings feed into the lead scoring agent (shown at 12:20) which evaluates the lead against your specific qualification criteria. The demo uses these factors:

  • Revenue over $50,000/month
  • Business maturity (years operating)
  • Demonstrated interest in your service area
  • Company size and industry fit
  • Technology adoption signals

The agent outputs a numeric score from 0-100 that automatically routes the lead. Scores are categorized as:

Hot (70-100): High priority - immediate follow-up recommended
Warm (40-69): Medium priority - schedule follow-up
Cold (0-39): Low priority - automated nurture sequence

Notification Routing

The system stores all lead data in Google Sheets (shown at 14:50) and sends customized Slack alerts based on the lead score. At 16:30, you can see the different notification formats:

Hot Lead Alert

Red-highlighted Slack message with all key details and recommended actions. Includes emoji indicators and urgent follow-up timeline.

Warm Lead Alert

Yellow-highlighted message with summary information and suggested follow-up timeframe.

Cold Lead Notification

Basic notification logged in the system but doesn't interrupt the team. Automatically added to nurture sequences.
