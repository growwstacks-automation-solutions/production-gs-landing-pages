The $5,000 Missed Call Problem

Every business owner knows the frustration of missed calls - that sinking feeling when you check your phone to see a missed call notification from an unknown number. Studies show 62% of business calls go unanswered, representing massive lost revenue opportunities across industries.

The hackathon winner, Brett (a self-described "non-coder"), identified this universal pain point as his automation target. His insight? While businesses focus on lead generation, few have systems to capture leads when they're hottest - the moment they call.

Key stat: When leads contact multiple vendors, the first to respond captures 50-78% of the business (Velocify). This system ensures you're always first.

How the Winning System Works

The $5,000 solution follows a beautifully simple premise: When someone calls and gets voicemail, immediately engage them via text where response rates are 3-5x higher than callbacks.

The end-to-end flow:

  1. Missed call detection: Twilio Studio triggers when call goes to voicemail
  2. Instant text engagement: Sends warm, branded welcome message
  3. Appointment booking: Offers two available times via Calendly integration
  4. Lead enrichment: Structured text conversation gathers key details
  5. Value-add followup: Sends property comps (for real estate) or equivalent

At 2:45 in the video, Brett demonstrates how the system handles a demo call - showing the seamless transition from missed call to booked appointment in under 3 minutes.

Twilio Studio Integration

The system's brain is Twilio Studio handling all telephony events. When integrated with n8n:

  • Phase 0: Records new lead phone numbers in Google Sheets
  • Phase 1: Sends ringless voicemail if first-time caller
  • Phase 2: Initiates text conversation when caller presses "1"

Brett emphasizes the importance of checking whether a ringless voicemail was already sent (at 18:20 in the video) to avoid spamming contacts. This conditional logic prevents duplicate messages while ensuring all new callers receive prompt followup.

Automated Appointment Flow

The appointment booking sequence demonstrates elegant simplicity:

  1. Fetch times: Pulls two most recent openings from Calendly
  2. Present options: "Do either of these times work?" (no open-ended questions)
  3. Handle responses: Books selected time or offers more options

Pro tip: The system uses fixed response options (1/2/Other) rather than AI interpretation. This deterministic approach eliminates ambiguity and errors - a key factor in its hackathon success.

At 5:30 in the demo, you'll see how the pre-filled Calendly link creates a frictionless booking experience after the lead selects a time.

Lead Enrichment Process

Post-booking, the system initiates a structured qualification conversation:

  • Buying/Selling: Routes conversation appropriately
  • Urgency level: 1-5 scale gauges seriousness
  • Property details: Address or zip code for research

The pièce de résistance? Automatically sending recently sold comparable properties (for real estate) or equivalent value-add in other industries. At 9:45, Brett shows how the system emails curated listings within minutes of the conversation ending.

Google Sheets CRM Strategy

The entire system uses Google Sheets as its CRM - a brilliant choice for:

  • Accessibility: No specialized software needed
  • Transparency: Entire team can view lead status
  • Flexibility: Easy to export to other systems later

Each interaction updates the lead's row with:

  1. Call timestamp
  2. Appointment details
  3. Enrichment responses
  4. Followup actions taken

At 16:20, Brett explains how the system checks for existing records before creating new ones - preventing duplicates while maintaining complete history.

The No-Code Developer Approach

Most impressive? Brett built this without coding experience. His process:

  • AI collaboration: "I talk to ChatGPT like a lunatic" (his words at 20:15)
  • Iterative debugging: Using error messages to refine function nodes
  • Template adaptation: Modifying existing n8n workflows

Key insight: You don't need to be a developer to build powerful automations - you need persistence in problem-solving and willingness to learn from failures.

His advice for fellow non-coders? "Be very clear on what you want the code to do and give the AI the right context about your incoming JSON structure."

Key Hackathon Lessons

Three factors made this submission stand out:

  1. Solved a measurable problem: 62% missed call conversion is a universal pain point
  2. Practical implementation: Works with tools businesses already use (Twilio, Google Sheets)
  3. Deterministic design: Linear workflow with minimal failure points

As Brett summarizes at 24:50: "Imagine never missing a call again. This system helps convert missed calls into booked appointments so you can stay focused on the important things in your day."
