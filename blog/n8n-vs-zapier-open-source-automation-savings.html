What Exactly Is n8n?

Imagine your team spends hours every week manually transferring data between apps - copying leads from forms to CRMs, updating spreadsheets, sending follow-up emails. n8n eliminates this drudgery through visual workflow automation. Unlike rigid SaaS tools, n8n gives you Lego-like building blocks (nodes) that connect to create custom automation flows.

At the 2:15 mark in the video, we see a perfect example: when a new lead comes in, n8n can automatically add them to your CRM, send a personalized welcome email, and notify your sales team on Slack - all without human intervention. This "if this then that" logic applies to nearly any digital process across your organization.

Key differentiator: n8n is open-source and self-hostable, meaning you install it on your own servers rather than relying on a vendor's cloud. This gives unprecedented control over data, costs, and customization compared to closed platforms like Zapier.

Key Differences: n8n vs Zapier

While both tools automate workflows between apps, their architectures create stark contrasts in capability and cost. Zapier operates as a closed SaaS platform - you pay per task executed on their servers, with data passing through their infrastructure. n8n flips this model entirely.

The self-hosted community edition of n8n is completely free with no usage limits. You only pay for your own server (often $5-$20/month). Compare this to Zapier's $20/month starter plan that allows just 750 tasks. At scale, the savings become dramatic:

  • 10,000 tasks/month: $400+ on Zapier vs ~$10 server cost with n8n
  • 50,000 tasks/month: $1,500+ on Zapier vs same $10 server
  • Enterprise volumes: Zapier costs scale linearly while n8n's remain flat

Beyond cost, n8n's open-source nature means you can inspect the code, modify functionality, and integrate with internal systems that SaaS tools can't access. For regulated industries like healthcare and finance, this data sovereignty isn't just nice-to-have - it's mandatory.

Low-Code vs No-Code: Why It Matters

Zapier follows a strict no-code model - you're limited to the pre-built blocks they provide. n8n's low-code approach means non-technical users can start with simple drag-and-drop workflows, while developers can extend functionality almost infinitely through custom code.

This flexibility future-proofs your automation investment. As needs grow complex, you're not forced to abandon the platform or pay for expensive professional services.
