Why Build a Notion AI Assistant?

Most professionals using Notion still manually update their to-do lists and search through pages when they need information. This creates friction in your workflow - you know the data is in Notion somewhere, but finding it takes time away from actual work. An AI assistant solves this by letting you interact with Notion through natural conversation.

At 3:15 in the tutorial, you'll see how the assistant can add a new task ("walking my cat") and categorize it as "personal" through a simple chat interface. This eliminates the need to open Notion and manually create the item yourself.

Key benefit: Your team can update and query Notion without ever leaving their chat platform (Slack, Discord, etc.), saving an average of 22 minutes per employee daily according to internal tests.

Setting Up Botpress Studio

Botpress provides a visual interface for building conversational AI without coding. After creating a free account, you'll start in the Studio view where you can design your bot's workflow using nodes and cards.

The tutorial begins by creating a "Notion Bot" and setting up a basic greeting message. This establishes the conversational flow - when a user starts chatting, the bot introduces itself and presents menu options (like "Add a new task" or "Ask a question").

Connecting Notion to Botpress

The magic happens when you integrate Notion with Botpress. At 5:40 in the video, you'll see how to install the Notion integration from Botpress's hub. The process uses OAuth authentication, meaning your login credentials stay secure with Notion.

Once connected, you'll have access to Notion action cards in Botpress, including:

  • Create page in Notion
  • Update page properties
  • Get page content
  • Search by title

These cards become the building blocks for your assistant's capabilities.

Automating Task Creation

The tutorial demonstrates how to build a workflow for adding tasks to a Notion to-do list. At 7:15, you'll learn how to:

  1. Ask the user for the task name
  2. Store the input in a variable
  3. Create a new page in your Notion database
  4. Update the page properties (like task type)

Pro tip: Use the "Get page from Notion" card to retrieve your database schema first. This ensures your bot understands the structure of your to-do list (including custom properties like "Type" with options for personal, work, or school tasks).

Building the AI Search Function

Beyond adding tasks, your assistant can answer questions about content in your Notion workspace. At 12:30 in the video, you'll see how to implement this using Botpress's AI Task cards:

1. The user asks a question (e.g., "How many vacation days do I have?")
2. The bot searches your Notion pages for relevant content
3. An AI analyzes the found content and generates a concise answer

This transforms your Notion wiki into a searchable knowledge base that team members can query through natural conversation.
