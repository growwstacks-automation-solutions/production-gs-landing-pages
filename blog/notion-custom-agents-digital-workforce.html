Smilers: Notion's Office Assistant

Every office has those endless Slack threads - "Where are the bagels?" "Has anyone seen my tape?" "When's the next fire drill?" The Notion Environment team found these mundane questions were drowning out actual conversation in their workplace channels. Their solution? Smilers - an always-available custom agent that handles all office logistics questions.

Smilers draws knowledge from two sources: Notion's comprehensive Environment Home database (with everything from workplace ops to event details), and a dedicated knowledge base the team built specifically for the agent. What makes Smilers special is its ability to learn - when employees correct its answers in Slack, it updates its Notion knowledge base automatically.

Trend forecasting: The team is expanding Smilers' capabilities to detect when the same feedback appears 3-5 times, signaling emerging office needs. Next up? Having Smilers draft initial outreach emails to building management - turning passive question-answering into proactive problem-solving.

Tyler's 20 Personal Productivity Agents

Tyler, a communications major with no coding background, has built what might be Notion's most extensive personal agent network - 20 custom assistants each with themed "helmets" representing their functions. His approach was simple: "What do I want to stop doing?" This led to three core agents that automate his entire workflow.

His Task Agent captures action items from meetings and Slack, automatically organizing them in Notion. The Project Agent serves as a command center, tracking progress and upcoming milestones. Finally, his Weekly Agent synthesizes data from the first two, generating a comprehensive weekly update showing completed tasks, project progress, and what's coming next.

No-code creation: "I just talk to Notion AI to help it," Tyler explains. "I give it the right context of what I'm trying to do, and all the integration gets set up for me." This accessibility means anyone can build powerful agents without technical skills.

The Test Triager Engineering Agent

Notion engineers faced a common problem - bug reports would pour into Slack, but the effort to properly file each one meant many got ignored. Their solution? A Test Triager agent that automates the entire process with intelligent enhancements.

When someone reports a bug with a simple Slack reaction, the agent checks if it's already in Notion's task tracker. If so, it responds instantly: "This bug is already assigned to John and being worked on." No duplicate tickets, no wondering if anyone saw your report. Engineers stay focused while every report gets properly tracked.

Engineering efficiency: "There's a lot of these that get reported throughout the day," explains engineer John. "Before, the effort to properly file each one was pretty high. Now the agent makes sure everything's captured while I stay in flow state."

The No-Code Building Process

The most revolutionary aspect of Notion's Custom Agents? You don't need to be an engineer to create powerful automations. As Tyler proved, even non-technical users can build sophisticated agent networks through natural language conversations with Notion AI.

The process starts by identifying pain points - repetitive questions, manual data entry, or context-switching tasks. You then describe what you want the agent to do in plain English. Notion AI helps configure the integrations between your databases, knowledge bases, and communication platforms like Slack.

Continuous improvement: Like Smilers, most agents get smarter over time. They incorporate feedback, detect patterns in incoming requests, and can even propose workflow improvements based on usage data.
