The Problem With Random UGC Creation

Every day, businesses waste thousands on UGC ads that don't convert. The issue isn't the tools - it's the complete lack of system. Most creators start from scratch each time, throwing random prompts at AI tools and hoping something sticks.

The breakthrough comes when we stop treating each ad as a unique creation and start building on proven templates. Ads that have been running for months in your niche already solved the conversion puzzle - your job is to adapt their structure to your product.

85% of failed UGC ads share the same root cause: no systematic approach to template creation and variation. This automation solves that by institutionalizing what works.

Step 1: Finding Winning Templates in Meta Ad Library

Meta's ad library (7:15 in the video) is a goldmine for template discovery. The key is identifying ads that have been running for 4-6 weeks or longer - these are the campaigns actually making money.

For our pre-workout example (like Mr. Hyde's dual tub), we found 2,100 active ads. The system filters for:

  • Active campaigns running longer than 30 days
  • High engagement rates (comments/shares)
  • Clear conversion elements (product close-ups, CTA placement)

At 8:42, we demonstrate how to extract the ad URL using browser inspection tools - a crucial step to feed the winning template into our automation.

Step 2: AI-Powered Template Analysis

Once we have a winning ad, Gemini Pro analyzes its converting elements (10:55 timestamp):

Template analysis includes: Cinematography (camera angles, lighting), editing style (jump cuts, pacing), character actions, audio patterns (voice tone, background music), and emotional triggers.

This creates what we call a "video DNA" - the structural elements that make the ad work, separate from the specific product being sold. The output is a JSON template that can be adapted to any product in the same niche.

Step 3: Automated Prompt Generation

The system then feeds this template into a specialized LLM prompt generator (14:20 in video). This is where most manual processes fail - human-created prompts vary wildly in quality.

Our automated approach:

  1. Preserves all converting elements from the template
  2. Swaps product-specific messaging
  3. Generates multiple prompt variations for A/B testing
  4. Ensures consistent character behavior across ads

The prompt generator also handles logistical details like video duration breakdowns - automatically creating 10s, 15s, and 30s versions from longer templates.

Step 4: Sora 2 Video Generation

With optimized prompts ready, the system pushes to Sora 2 via Kai.ai (18:30 timestamp). We use Kai because:

  • No watermarks on generated videos
  • Cheaper iterations than direct Sora 2 API
  • Built-in upscaling options

The automation generates 3-5 variations per template, focusing on different product angles or CTAs. At $0.15 per second, a 30s video costs $4.50 - compared to $200-$500 for human UGC creators.

Maintaining Character Consistency

One challenge with AI UGC is maintaining consistent spokespeople across ads. Our solution (21:10 in video) uses Sora 2's cameo mode with detailed character references like "chasegpt_1".

By saving:
