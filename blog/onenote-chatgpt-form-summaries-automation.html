The Form Summary Problem

Every business that collects form data faces the same painful process: responses pile up in spreadsheets, someone has to manually read through them all, identify patterns, and create summary reports. For customer feedback forms, employee surveys, or event registrations, this manual processing eats up 2-3 hours per week per form — time that could be spent acting on insights rather than compiling them.

The breakthrough comes when you realize form summaries don't require human judgment — they require pattern recognition and consistent formatting. This is exactly what ChatGPT excels at, especially when given clear instructions. By automating the entire flow from form submission to summarized OneNote entry, businesses can turn days of monthly busywork into a fully hands-off process.

Time savings benchmark: A 10-question form that normally takes 15 minutes to manually summarize can be processed automatically in under 60 seconds with this workflow — a 15x speed improvement that compounds with each additional response.

Configuring ChatGPT Processing

This is where the automation goes from simple data transfer to intelligent analysis. By connecting your OpenAI account (just requires copying an API key from their website), you gain access to ChatGPT's summarization capabilities within the Zapier workflow.

The most important part is crafting your prompt template — the instructions that tell ChatGPT exactly how to process each form response. A well-structured prompt might include:

  • Required elements to include in every summary
  • Formatting preferences (bullets, headings, etc.)
  • Length constraints
  • Any industry-specific terminology to use

At 2:30 in the video, you'll see how to test different prompt variations until the output matches your ideal summary format.

Logging to OneNote Automatically

The final piece connects your Microsoft account and specifies exactly where in OneNote each summary should go. You can organize summaries by date, form type, or any other system — even creating new sections automatically as needed.

One powerful feature shown at 3:45 in the tutorial is the ability to include both the ChatGPT summary and the original form data in the OneNote entry. This creates a perfect audit trail where you can quickly scan summaries for patterns, then drill down to specific responses when needed.

Pro tip: Use OneNote's tagging system to automatically flag summaries containing specific keywords (like "urgent" or "follow-up") for immediate attention.
