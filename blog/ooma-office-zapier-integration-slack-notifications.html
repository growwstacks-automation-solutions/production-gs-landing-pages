Zapier Basics: Triggers and Actions

Zapier automations (called "Zaps") consist of two main components: triggers and actions. A trigger is the event that starts the automation, while an action is what happens as a result.

In this integration, the trigger is "New Call" in Ooma Office - whenever your business line rings. The action is "Send Direct Message" in Slack - creating an instant notification in your preferred channel. Zapier acts as the bridge between these two unrelated apps.

Setting Up the Ooma Office Trigger

To begin creating your Zap, log into Zapier and click the "+ Create Zap" button. You'll first configure the trigger section:

Step 1: Select Ooma Office as the Trigger App

Search for and select "Ooma Office" from Zapier's app directory. If this is your first time connecting Ooma Office to Zapier, you'll need to authenticate your account.

Step 2: Choose "New Call" as the Trigger Event

This tells Zapier to watch for incoming calls to your Ooma Office number. The trigger will activate the moment a call comes in.

Step 3: Test Your Trigger

Zapier will prompt you to test the connection by placing a test call to your Ooma Office number. This generates sample data that shows what information will be available to use in your Slack notification.

Pro Tip: Make sure your Ooma Office account has API access enabled in its settings before attempting to connect with Zapier.

Configuring the Slack Action

After setting up the trigger, you'll configure what happens when a call comes in - in this case, sending a Slack notification:

Step 1: Select Slack as the Action App

Search for and select Slack from Zapier's app directory. Authenticate your Slack account if you haven't already.

Step 2: Choose "Send Direct Message" as the Action Event

This ensures notifications come directly to you rather than a public channel. You can also choose to send to a specific channel if multiple team members need alerts.

Step 3: Customize Your Notification Message

In the message text field, you can include dynamic data from the Ooma Office call like the caller's number, call time, or duration. At 1:45 in the video tutorial, you'll see how to format this for maximum clarity.

Customization Idea: Add conditional formatting to highlight VIP callers or repeat customers by checking their phone number against your CRM.

Testing Your Zap

Before activating your automation, it's crucial to test each component:

Step 1: Test the Trigger

Zapier will show you sample call data from Ooma Office. Verify this includes all the information you want to include in your Slack notification.

Step 2: Test the Action

Zapier will send a test message to your Slack account. Check that it appears as expected and contains all the relevant call details.

Step 3: Make a Live Test Call

Have someone call your Ooma Office number to confirm the entire workflow functions correctly end-to-end before relying on it for business calls.

Advanced Customizations

Once you have the basic integration working, consider these enhancements:

1. Multiple Notification Channels

Add additional actions to notify different team members based on call characteristics like time of day or caller ID.

2. CRM Integration

3. Priority Filtering

Use Zapier's filter step to only notify you for calls matching certain criteria, reducing notification fatigue.
