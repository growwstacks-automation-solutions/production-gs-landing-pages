When OpenAI launched AgentKit, many predicted it would replace n8n for AI automation. After testing both platforms across 5 key business categories, we discovered the truth isn't so simple. While AgentKit excels at conversational agents, n8n remains the undisputed champion for complex business workflows.

Platform Overview

When OpenAI released AgentKit on October 6, 2025, many businesses wondered if their existing n8n automations would become obsolete. Our testing reveals these platforms serve fundamentally different purposes. AgentKit is designed as "Canva for building agents" according to Sam Altman - optimized for quick conversational AI creation. n8n, released in 2019, has evolved into a robust automation platform handling everything from simple triggers to complex enterprise workflows.

Google Trends data shows n8n's steady growth over six years compared to AgentKit's recent spike. While AgentKit makes AI agents accessible to non-technical users, n8n's 500+ integrations and subworkflow capabilities make it indispensable for business automation. At 3:45 in our video, we demonstrate how n8n's modular design handles workflows AgentKit simply can't.

Key insight: AgentKit isn't trying to replace n8n - they target different use cases. AgentKit focuses on conversational AI, while n8n specializes in connecting business systems and complex logic flows.

Ease of Use Comparison

For beginners, AgentKit's interface scores higher (8/10) than n8n's (6/10). Its drag-and-drop flow builder requires no coding knowledge to create basic agents. As shown at 7:30 in our video, you can name your agent, provide instructions, select a model, and define outputs in minutes. The interface includes helpful guardrails and only five logic operations (If, Else, While, Human Approval, Transform).

n8n's power comes with complexity. New users often feel overwhelmed by its numerous nodes and options for variable manipulation. However, this flexibility becomes invaluable when building advanced workflows. Our testing found that while AgentKit gets beginners started faster, n8n's learning curve pays off for serious automation needs.

Pro tip: For teams new to automation, start with AgentKit for simple AI agents while gradually learning n8n for more complex workflows. The platforms can complement each other.

Trigger Capabilities

n8n dominates trigger capabilities (9/10 vs AgentKit's 5/10), offering hundreds of event-based triggers that power real business automation. As demonstrated at 10:15 in our video, n8n connects natively to Gmail (new messages), Slack (mentions, reactions), CRMs, and countless other systems. These triggers enable true autonomous workflows that respond to business events without manual intervention.

AgentKit focuses on chat-based triggers through its SDK (TypeScript/Python) or embedded widgets. While useful for conversational interfaces, this limits its ability to automate backend business processes. For example, a real estate agency could use n8n to automatically respond to new leads from their CRM, while AgentKit might power the chat interface those leads interact with.

Integration Support

Integration support is where n8n shines brightest, scoring 10/10 versus AgentKit's 6/10. n8n's 500+ native integrations cover every major business platform, with the ability to add custom APIs via HTTP requests. At 12:30 in our video, we show how n8n connects HubSpot, Google Sheets, and Twilio in a single workflow - something impossible in AgentKit.

AgentKit offers basic connections to Gmail, Calendar, and Outlook, plus web search functionality. While sufficient for simple agents, businesses needing deep platform integration will find n8n indispensable. The ability to create reusable subworkflows in n8n (shown at 13:45) multiplies its integration power exponentially.

Model Flexibility

AgentKit's biggest limitation is model support (5/10), locking users into OpenAI's ecosystem. n8n scores 9/10 here, supporting 18+ providers including Anthropic, Azure, and local LLMs via OpenRouter. At 15:20 in our video, we demonstrate n8n's model router handling multiple AI providers simultaneously - crucial for businesses needing redundancy or specialized models.

While AgentKit simplifies model selection for beginners, this becomes restrictive for production systems. n8n's flexibility extends to fine-tuning model parameters (temperature, frequency penalty) per workflow step, enabling precise control over AI behavior in complex automations.

UI Components

AgentKit wins the UI component battle (9/10 vs n8n's 6/10) with its polished ChatKit widgets. These embeddable interfaces require no coding and work out-of-the-box, saving weeks of front-end development. At 17:10 in our video, we showcase how ChatKit can deliver interactive, guided experiences beyond static replies.

n8n focuses on backend workflows rather than front-end interfaces. While you can build custom UIs with n8n's HTTP nodes, it lacks AgentKit's turnkey solutions. For businesses needing both robust automation and customer-facing interfaces, combining n8n's workflows with AgentKit's ChatKit may be ideal.

Deployment Control

n8n's deployment flexibility (7/10) surpasses AgentKit's (5/10), with options for cloud, private cloud, and on-premises hosting. This control over data and infrastructure is critical for many businesses. AgentKit currently only offers OpenAI-hosted solutions, though this may change as the platform matures.

The ability to run n8n locally with self-hosted LLMs (shown at 19:45 in our video) provides additional privacy and cost benefits. For enterprises with strict compliance requirements, n8n's deployment options make it the only viable choice between these platforms.

Key Takeaways

After extensive testing, we recommend choosing based on your specific needs:

  • For conversational AI agents: AgentKit's simpler interface and ChatKit widgets accelerate development
  • For business automation: n8n's 500+ integrations and workflow capabilities remain unmatched
  • For enterprises: n8n's deployment flexibility and model support are essential

Final verdict: AgentKit won't replace n8n, but it creates exciting new possibilities when used alongside it. Businesses should evaluate both platforms based on their specific automation requirements.
