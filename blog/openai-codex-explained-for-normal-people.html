What Exactly Is OpenAI Codex?

Most people think AI tools are either for coding or for chatting - but OpenAI Codex breaks that mold. It's an AI assistant that works directly with files on your computer, automating the tedious knowledge work that eats up your day.

Imagine having a digital assistant that could organize your receipts into categorized spreadsheets, create brand guidelines by analyzing your website, or even draft email responses in your voice - all while keeping everything securely on your machine rather than in the cloud.

The key difference: While ChatGPT creates content in the cloud, Codex creates actual files on your computer that you own and control. This local-first approach is why many are calling it the "Claude CoWork killer."

How Codex Differs From ChatGPT

At first glance, Codex looks similar to ChatGPT - there's a chat interface where you type prompts. But under the hood, they work completely differently. ChatGPT is primarily a conversation tool, while Codex is a file creation and automation tool.

The most important distinction is where your work lives. With ChatGPT, everything exists in their cloud. With Codex, everything is saved directly to folders on your computer. This means you maintain full ownership and control of your files.

Another key difference is Codex's ability to work with your existing files. You can point it at a folder full of receipts and have it create an organized spreadsheet, or have it analyze documents to extract key information - tasks that would be cumbersome or impossible with standard ChatGPT.

Getting Started With Codex in 15 Minutes

Setting up Codex is surprisingly simple, especially if you already have a ChatGPT account. The entire process takes about 15 minutes from download to first automation.

First, download the Codex app from chat.openai.com/codex. It's available for both Mac and Windows. After installing, you'll sign in with your ChatGPT credentials - if you have a ChatGPT Plus subscription, Codex is included at no extra cost.

During setup, Codex will ask about your work type. Important: If you're not a coder, avoid selecting technical roles like "engineering." Choose options that match your actual work (marketing, student, etc.) to get the most relevant features.

Once installed, Codex operates through "projects" - which are simply folders on your computer. This structure keeps your automated files organized and easy to find.

Your First Codex Project: Receipt Organization

A perfect first project demonstrates Codex's file creation abilities. In the tutorial video at 4:32, we see how Codex can turn a folder of scattered receipts into an organized expense report.

Simply create a new project pointing to your receipts folder, then give Codex this prompt: "Create an Excel file that categorizes all these receipts with a breakdown of each expense and the total cost for the trip."

Codex will generate a spreadsheet with categorized expenses, subtotals, and even multiple tabs for different views of the data. You can preview the file right in Codex or open it in Excel. The file is saved in your original folder - now neatly organized for accounting or reimbursement.

Pro tip: Adjust the "intelligence level" slider (medium works well for most tasks) to balance speed and accuracy for different projects.

Creating a Brand Kit With Codex

Another powerful use case is creating consistent brand materials. At 7:15 in the video, we see how Codex can analyze a website and generate a complete brand style guide.

The process is simple: create a new project, then use a prompt like "Figure out my brand colors, fonts, and styling by visiting my website [yourURL], then create a markdown file with my brand kit."

Codex will visit your site, identify key branding elements, and create a document you can reference for future projects. This is especially useful for solopreneurs or small teams without dedicated designers.

An interesting feature shown at 8:50 is the "desktop pet" - a small icon that alerts you when background tasks need attention, allowing you to multitask while Codex works.

Skills and Plugins: Codex's Secret Weapons

Two features that set Codex apart are Skills and Plugins. Skills are reusable instruction sets - for example, you could create an "on brand" skill that applies your brand guidelines to any content Codex generates.

Plugins connect Codex to other services. Mention "Gmail" in a prompt, and Codex will suggest installing the Gmail plugin (shown at 13:20). Other available plugins include Google Drive, Canva, and Google Calendar - with more being added regularly.

The combination of Skills and Plugins means Codex gets better the more you use it. Frequently repeated tasks become one-click operations, and deeper integrations allow for more powerful automations.

Time-saver: When Codex does something perfectly, tell it to "turn that into a skill" so you can reuse the exact same approach later.

Automating Email Triage

One of Codex's most powerful features is email automation. At 14:50 in the video, we see how to set up a system that scans your inbox, drafts replies in your voice, and learns from your past responses.

The key is the Gmail plugin. Once installed, you can create automations like "Set up email triage to scan my inbox every morning at 9am, draft replies in my tone, and prioritize based on importance."

Codex will create the necessary files and systems to handle this automatically. You can review drafts before sending, and over time, Codex adapts to your communication style. This alone can save hours per week on email management.

Creating Interactive Dashboards

Codex isn't limited to traditional file types. At 18:30 in the tutorial, we see how it can create interactive dashboards that make your data more engaging and accessible.

Simply point Codex at a project folder and prompt: "Spin up a fun interactive dashboard for me. Make it interesting." Codex will analyze your files and create a visual interface for navigating and understanding your information.

This demonstrates Codex's ability to think beyond standard documents. The files it creates are only limited by your imagination - from dynamic reports to custom tools tailored to your specific needs.
