The Clinical Documentation Bottleneck

Before implementing Overjet Voice, Dr. Choksey's dental practice struggled with inefficient documentation workflows that disrupted patient care. "In the past, my team would have to put the notes together," explains Dr. Choksey. "They would do the medical workup, take blood pressure, have initial conversations with the patient—then I would come into the room and they'd have to summarize everything again."

This redundant process created significant bottlenecks. With the fast pace of clinical conversations, assistants often missed critical details. "It was almost impossible for them to pick up every detail," Dr. Choksey notes. The result was frustrated staff, incomplete records, and valuable clinical time wasted on administrative tasks rather than patient care.

Key insight: Traditional clinical documentation forces healthcare providers to choose between patient interaction and complete records—Overjet Voice eliminates this compromise.

How Overjet Voice Solved Our Problems

"The greatest thing this technology has brought is it can actually just keep up with me," says Dr. Choksey. Overjet Voice's AI-powered documentation captures every clinical conversation in real-time, eliminating the need for staff to manually record notes during exams.

The system's split-screen interface (visible at 1:42 in the video) allows clinicians to review automatically generated notes while maintaining eye contact with patients. "It can actually cue up things you might have missed—almost like a teleprompter telling you how much anesthetic you used or quantifying things," Dr. Choksey explains. This addresses one of the most common documentation gaps in dental practices.

Transforming Perio Charting Workflows

One of the most significant improvements came in periodontal charting—traditionally one of the most time-consuming aspects of hygiene appointments. "Perio charting used to slow everything down," recalls Samantha, a hygienist in Dr. Choksey's practice. "Now I speak, it captures it, and I move on with no interruptions."

Overjet Voice's specialized dental AI accurately captures periodontal measurements and other clinical metrics without requiring an assistant to manually record them. "Incorporating perio charting has really helped our hygienists get the readings they need without having another assistant in the room," notes Dr. Choksey. This optimization has significantly improved clinical operations and staff utilization.

Improving Patient Connections

The hands-free operation of Overjet Voice has transformed patient interactions. "I'm making eye contact, I'm explaining what I'm doing as I do it," describes Samantha. This represents a major shift from traditional documentation methods that required clinicians to divide their attention between patients and computers.

Dr. Choksey emphasizes how this improves care quality: "It's allowing us to be more patient-centric and really focus in on the conversation." By eliminating documentation distractions, clinicians can devote their full attention to patient education and building trust—critical factors in treatment acceptance and satisfaction.

Faster Insurance Approvals

More complete documentation has yielded unexpected benefits in insurance processing. "Because our notes are much more detailed, we find that our pre-authorizations and predeterminations are coming back much quicker," reports Dr. Choksey.

This acceleration reduces patient wait times for treatment approval. "There's less fatigue from patients who have to wait until something comes back to then get them back onto the schedule," Dr. Choksey explains. The practice has seen a measurable reduction in claim denials and requests for additional information—saving both staff time and patient frustration.

Implementation and Adoption

Like many clinicians, Dr. Choksey was initially skeptical about AI documentation. "I was skeptical at first with the automated note-taking and using Overjet," he admits. However, adoption proved easier than expected. "Since I started using it in my day-to-day, it's just become second nature."

The hygienists in the practice reported similar experiences. "The technology became second nature very quickly," says Samantha. Within 2-3 weeks, staff were using the system effortlessly as part of their normal workflow—a testament to Overjet Voice's intuitive design and specialized dental focus.

Measurable Practice Improvements

The cumulative impact of these workflow improvements has been transformative. "At the end of the day, I've now been able to convert that time into more treatment, more interaction time with my patients," summarizes Dr. Choksey. This has led to "better reviews, better overall patient experience."

Key metrics from Dr. Choksey's practice include:

  • 40% reduction in documentation time per patient
  • 30-50% faster insurance approvals
  • 15% increase in hygiene production
  • Significant improvement in patient satisfaction scores

Practice transformation: Overjet Voice didn't just automate documentation—it fundamentally changed how Dr. Choksey's team delivers care, allowing them to focus on what matters most: the patient.
