That spreadsheet of 1,237 leads you haven't contacted in months? It's not just sitting there - it's actively costing you money. Most businesses generate leads constantly through websites, events, and referrals, but 86% never get followed up with according to Harvard Business Review data.

The reason isn't lack of interest - it's the crushing time investment. Researching each contact, drafting personalized emails, and managing follow-ups can take 30+ minutes per lead. At that rate, contacting 100 leads would require 50 hours of work - more than a full week's worth of effort.

The breakthrough: What if you could automate the research and writing while keeping the personalization? That's exactly what this Make.com workflow delivers - turning what was a 30-minute manual process into a 30-cent automated one.

How AI Research Automation Works (Without Being Creepy)

There's a fine line between personalized and invasive. The key is focusing on publicly available professional context rather than personal details. Here's how the system structures its research:

Step 1: Company Context

Using Perplexity AI, the workflow automatically researches:

  • Recent company news (last 3 months)
  • Major projects/initiatives
  • Financial milestones (for public companies)

Step 2: Professional Background

For each contact, it identifies:

  • Current role responsibilities
  • Career trajectory (promotions, role changes)
  • Public speaking/published work

Pro Tip: The system intentionally avoids personal details like family, hobbies, or location - keeping the outreach professional and comfortable.

Email Sequence Psychology That Gets Replies

The 3-email sequence (demoed at 14:20) follows proven psychological principles:

Email 1: The Value Hook

  • Opens with congratulatory note on recent achievement
  • Connects their need to your solution
  • Ends with soft call-to-action (calendar link)

Email 2: The Gentle Nudge

  • References first email without pressure
  • Adds new piece of value (case study, tool, etc.)
  • Shorter than first email

Email 3: The Polite Release

  • Acknowledges they're busy
  • Gives permission to ignore
  • Leaves door open for future

Response Rate Data: This structure achieves 23% reply rates vs. 5% for single emails (based on 1,200 campaigns analyzed). The key is the gradual escalation combined with decreasing pressure.

Why Claude Beats ChatGPT for Outreach Emails

At 18:45, we switch from Perplexity to Claude for email generation because:

28% Higher Response Rates: In A/B tests, Claude-generated emails outperformed ChatGPT consistently by better mimicking human writing patterns.

Key advantages of Claude for outreach:

  • More natural use of "I" vs. corporate "we"
  • Better at maintaining casual tone
  • Fewer unnatural phrases (like overuse of em-dashes)
  • More consistent voice across sequences

The prompt structure includes specific instructions like:

  • "Write as if speaking to a friend"
  • "Use I over we"
  • "Avoid corporate jargon"

The LinkedIn Message Most People Miss (But Works)

At 26:10, we add the LinkedIn component - which increases overall response rates by 37% when combined with email.

The winning formula:

  • Sent 3 days after last email
  • References the email chain ("Following up on my note about...")
  • Even more casual than emails
  • Includes subtle social proof ("We've helped similar companies...")

Key Insight: Many decision makers check LinkedIn more frequently than email. The message serves as a reminder to check their inbox for your original email.

Scaling to 100+ Daily Personalized Emails

The final piece (shown at 31:45) is removing the test limits and adding filters to prevent duplicates:

Step 1: Remove Row Limits

Change from processing 2-5 rows to your entire list (or batches of 100).

Step 2: Add Deduplication Filter

The workflow checks if research already exists before processing, preventing duplicate outreach.

Step 3: Schedule Daily Runs

Set the scenario to run automatically each morning with new leads added overnight.
