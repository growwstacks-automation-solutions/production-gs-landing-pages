Two Connection Methods Compared

Businesses transitioning to voice AI face a critical first decision: how to connect their existing phone system to AI agents. The wrong choice can mean dropped calls, poor audio quality, or complex maintenance. The platform offers two proven methods, each with distinct advantages.

SIP trunking provides direct internet-based routing of calls to your AI agents, offering better call quality and more control. Call redirect is simpler to implement but relies on traditional forwarding. The best choice depends on your telecom provider, technical resources, and call volume needs.

Key difference: SIP trunking routes calls directly over the internet while call redirect uses traditional forwarding. SIP offers better quality at $0.015 per minute versus redirect at $0.02, but requires provider support.

SIP Trunk Setup Step-by-Step

SIP trunking delivers professional-grade call routing for businesses ready to modernize their telephony. This method connects your phone system directly to AI agents using internet protocols rather than traditional phone lines.

Step 1: Access Workspace Configuration

Navigate to your organization profile, select workspaces, and choose the specific workspace for call configuration. Most businesses start with their default Horizon workspace before creating specialized ones.

Step 2: Configure SIP Trunk

Under the call tab, select "Configure" then "Existing SIP Trunk." You'll need your phone label (e.g., "Sales Line"), country code, and full phone number. This creates the connection point between your provider and the AI platform.

Step 3: Set Up Inbound Routing

Select your region (US, Europe, or Asia) and enter the provided gateway addresses. These ensure calls route optimally through the platform's infrastructure. Your telecom provider will need these details to complete their side of the setup.

Pro tip: European businesses save 18% on SIP costs by selecting the Frankfurt gateway rather than London, due to lower regional peering fees.

Call Redirect Setup Step-by-Step

For businesses needing a simpler solution or whose providers don't support SIP, call redirect offers an effective alternative. This method forwards calls from your existing number to an AI-managed number while maintaining your familiar business number.

Step 1: Initiate Call Redirect

In your workspace call settings, choose "Configure" then "Call Redirect." Enter your phone label, country code, and existing business number. This identifies the line being forwarded to the AI system.

Step 2: Select Destination

Choose your destination region (currently US or Europe) and the platform will assign a local number. This becomes the forwarding target that preserves your caller ID and maintains local presence.

Step 3: Complete Forwarding

With your new AI number assigned, log into your phone provider's portal and set up unconditional forwarding to this number. The platform handles all subsequent call routing to your AI agents.

Important: Call redirect adds about 200ms of latency versus SIP's 80ms. For most customer service calls this difference is negligible, but may affect high-frequency trading or emergency lines.
