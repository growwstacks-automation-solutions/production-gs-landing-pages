The Phone-First Patient Challenge

Pine Park Health serves nearly 200 senior living communities across California, Arizona, and Nevada, providing geriatric primary care where patients live. Their older patient population overwhelmingly prefers phone communication - creating an operational paradox. As COO Mike Tadlock explains in the video at 0:45: "The more patients we served, the more our medical assistants became full-time phone operators instead of caregivers."

Before implementing voice AI, medical assistants spent hours daily on:

  • Appointment scheduling calls
  • Visit reminders
  • Medication refill coordination
  • Follow-up communications

The staffing bottleneck: For every 100 new patients, Pine Park needed to hire additional medical assistants just to handle the increased call volume - pulling them away from direct patient care where their skills mattered most.

Why Retell Voice AI Solved Their Bottleneck

After evaluating multiple solutions, Pine Park chose Retell's voice AI platform for three critical capabilities that matched their healthcare needs:

  1. Self-serve workflow editing: Non-technical staff could modify prompts and call flows without developer support (unlike other vendors requiring engineering tickets for every change)
  2. Natural conversation handling: The AI could manage the back-and-forth of scheduling conversations while maintaining HIPAA compliance
  3. Seamless EHR integration: Completed calls automatically updated patient records in their existing systems

As Mike notes at 1:30 in the video: "Retell's UI let our operations team iterate on the patient experience quickly. With other solutions, we'd still be waiting for engineering to make simple prompt changes."

Meet Ava and Jay: The AI Agents Transforming Senior Care

Pine Park deployed two specialized AI agents to handle different aspects of patient communication:

Ava the Appointment Reminder: Calls patients 2-3 days before scheduled visits to confirm attendance and answer basic questions. Listen to a sample Ava call at the 0:10 timestamp in the video.

Jay the Schedule Optimizer: This proactive agent identifies unused appointment slots in provider calendars and calls overdue patients to fill them. Jay's conversational design includes:

  • Natural pauses and verbal affirmations ("mm-hmm", "I understand")
  • Multiple time slot options presented conversationally
  • Follow-up questions based on patient responses

The agents work in tandem - Ava reduces no-shows while Jay maximizes provider utilization, together solving Pine Park's twin operational challenges.

Patient Reception: Surprise and Delight

Perhaps the most striking outcome? How positively patients responded. In post-call surveys (3:10 in video):

  • 33% of respondents didn't realize they'd spoken with AI
  • 82% rated the experience as "excellent" or "very good"
  • Multiple patients commented they appreciated the "personal touch"

Mike shares: "We expected some resistance from older patients, but the opposite happened. They were delighted we were using technology to make their lives easier." This reception validated Pine Park's careful design of the agents' voices, pacing, and conversational style to match their patient demographic.

3 Measurable Business Impacts

The implementation delivered concrete operational and financial results:

1. 38% NPS Increase: Patient satisfaction scores for scheduling interactions jumped from 62 to 86 after deploying Ava and Jay - a statistically significant improvement in a critical service metric.

2. 55.7% Booking Rate: When Jay connects with a patient for outbound scheduling, more than half result in a booked appointment - far exceeding their internal benchmarks for human staff.

3. 2.1 FTE Hours Recaptured Daily: By automating routine calls, Pine Park freed over two full-time equivalent medical assistant hours each day - time now spent on higher-value patient care activities.

These metrics compound over time - the more patients served, the greater the operational leverage from their AI investment.

Key Implementation Lessons

For healthcare providers considering similar automation, Pine Park's experience offers valuable insights:

  1. Start with one workflow: They first automated simple appointment reminders before tackling more complex scheduling calls
  2. Design for your demographic: Slower speech pacing and clear enunciation proved critical for older patients
  3. Measure what matters: They tracked both operational metrics (call volume, time saved) and patient experience (NPS, survey feedback)
  4. Iterate quickly: Retell's self-serve UI allowed 14 prompt revisions in the first month based on real call data

As Mike advises at 4:20: "Be forward-looking but prepared to work through the details. The patient experience comes first, but the operational benefits make it sustainable."
