The Problem With Basic Voice AI

Traditional voice AI systems force businesses into an impossible choice: either handle all calls with AI (frustrating customers who need human help) or escalate all calls (overwhelming support teams). Neither approach scales well as call volumes grow.

The breakthrough shown in this demo is policy-aware routing - where the AI evaluates each call against business rules to determine whether it should handle the inquiry or escalate to a human. This matches how top-performing support teams actually operate.

Key insight: 68% of customer support calls follow predictable patterns that AI can handle, but current systems lack the intelligence to identify which 32% truly need human assistance.

Pipecat + LangGraph Solution

Pipecat provides the real-time voice conversation framework, while LangGraph handles the policy evaluation. Together they create an AI agent that:

  • Conducts natural voice conversations

  • Evaluates against business rules
  • Decides whether to handle or escalate

Policy Evaluation Process

The magic happens in the policy evaluation layer. Here's how it works step-by-step:

Step 1: Caller provides information (like a case number)

Step 2: Context aggregator identifies this as a decision point

Step 3: LangGraph evaluates against business rules

Step 4: System either continues conversation or initiates transfer

This happens seamlessly during the conversation flow - at the 3:40 mark in the video, you can see how quickly the escalation occurs once the VIP case number is recognized.

Implementation Considerations

When planning your own policy-aware voice AI implementation:

  1. Define clear escalation rules - What qualifies a call for human help?
  2. Map common inquiry types - Which can AI handle completely?
  3. Design conversation flows - How will the AI gather needed information?
  4. Test policy evaluation - Does it catch all intended scenarios?

The demo uses a simple VIP rule, but real implementations might evaluate customer value, issue urgency, or other business-specific factors.
