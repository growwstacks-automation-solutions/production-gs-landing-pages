The RAG Blind Spot That's Costing You Accuracy

Most RAG implementations suffer from a critical limitation - they retrieve text fragments without understanding their place in the document's structure. Imagine an insurance agent who can quote policy clauses but doesn't know whether they're from the "Coverage" or "Exclusions" section. That's essentially how most RAG systems operate today.

This structural blindness leads directly to hallucinations and incorrect answers. In testing, we found standard RAG gives misleading answers 60-70% of the time when questions require understanding document hierarchy. The problem isn't the retrieval - it's the lack of context around what's retrieved.

Key insight: A paragraph about "tennis elbow coverage" means something completely different if it appears under "Included Conditions" versus "Specific Exclusions." Standard RAG can't tell the difference.

Context Expansion: The Missing Piece

Context expansion solves RAG's structural blindness by retrieving not just matching chunks, but their surrounding document sections. There are three main approaches:

1. Neighbor Expansion

Fetches adjacent chunks to provide local context. Helpful when answers might span multiple consecutive paragraphs.

2. Parent Expansion

Retrieves the entire section containing a matched chunk. Critical for understanding headings and their relationship to content.

3. Agentic Expansion

The LLM determines which additional sections are needed based on the query. Most flexible but requires careful implementation.

Implementation note: Our n8n solution uses a hybrid approach - starting with parent expansion, then letting a small model decide if additional context is needed.

How We Implemented This in n8n

The system architecture has three key components working together:

1. Hierarchy-Aware Ingestion

During document processing, we extract and preserve structural information - headings, sections, and their relationships. This metadata gets stored in Superbase alongside the vector embeddings.

2. Two-Phase Retrieval

First, standard vector search finds relevant chunks. Then, our n8n workflow consults the hierarchy metadata to determine what additional context to fetch.

3. Dynamic Context Assembly

The system constructs the final context by combining the matched chunk with its relevant structural neighbors before sending to the LLM.

Real-World Example: Appliance Manuals

Consider the manual for an Empava oven. A standard RAG might retrieve this fragment when asked about installation:

"Make electrical connection to junction box using 3-conductor cable"

Without context, the AI might miss that this instruction appears under "Professional Installation Required" with warnings about voiding warranties. Our system retrieves:

  • The matched chunk
  • Its parent section heading
  • Any warning boxes in the section
  • Related "Safety Precautions" from the document

This comprehensive context leads to dramatically more accurate and responsible answers.

Why Document Hierarchy Matters

Document structure provides critical signals for interpretation:

  • Headings frame the meaning of content (e.g., "Requirements" vs "Recommendations")
  • Section nesting shows relationships between concepts
  • Positioning indicates importance (main body vs appendix)
  • Formatting like warning boxes convey urgency

Our testing shows that preserving just heading hierarchy improves answer accuracy by 40%, while full structural awareness delivers 70%+ gains.

Adding context expansion does increase token usage, but we've optimized the n8n workflow to minimize impact:
