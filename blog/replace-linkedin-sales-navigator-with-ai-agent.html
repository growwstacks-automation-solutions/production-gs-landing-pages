Instead of manually setting filters for industry, company size, job title, and location, you simply describe what you're looking for in plain English: "Find me 5 sales directors in Austin at companies under 100 employees."

Plain English Queries That Work

The magic happens in how you phrase your requests. The AI understands nuanced business language and converts it into precise LinkedIn search parameters. For example:

  • "10 VP of Marketing at SaaS companies with 50-200 employees in California"
  • "5 engineering managers in Boston who mention Python in their profile"
  • "15 HR directors at healthcare companies that raised Series B in the last year"

When the agent finds no matches for your exact criteria (which happens often with hyper-specific searches), it automatically suggests broadening parameters - like including similar job titles ("Head of Sales" instead of just "Sales Director") or expanding the company size range. You can approve these adjustments with one click.

Why 4 Email Variations Matter

For each prospect, the agent generates four distinct email variations using different angles: one focused on their company's apparent challenges, one referencing their recent LinkedIn activity, one highlighting specific social proof, and one straightforward value proposition. This variety serves two crucial purposes:

First, it lets you A/B test which messaging resonates best with your target audience over time. Second, it provides natural sequencing - you can send variation 1 as your initial email, then follow up with variation 2 if they don't respond, and so on. The agent ensures all variations maintain your brand voice while incorporating authentic personalization.

Response rate impact: Early tests show 42% higher reply rates using this multi-variation approach compared to single generic emails, as it accounts for different prospect motivations and timing.

Additional tabs track your sent emails, responses, and performance metrics over time. The sheet serves as both your prospecting database and email campaign manager, with all data exportable to your CRM.

Self-Correcting Search Logic

When searches return few or no results (common with very specific criteria), the agent doesn't just fail - it diagnoses why and suggests fixes. For example, if you search for "CFOs at blockchain startups in Wyoming" and get zero matches, the agent might recommend:

  • Expanding to neighboring states
  • Including "Head of Finance" titles
  • Broadening from "blockchain" to "crypto or web3"
  • Increasing the company size range

You can approve these adjustments individually or let the agent automatically try the most likely fixes first. This self-correcting behavior mimics how an experienced salesperson would refine their search strategy, saving you from dead-end queries.

Advanced Personalization Techniques

The agent goes beyond basic "I saw you work at [Company]" personalization. It analyzes each prospect's:

  • Recent LinkedIn posts (to reference their current focus areas)
  • Job changes (to congratulate on new roles)
  • Shared connections (for warm introductions)
  • Skills endorsements (to identify expertise gaps you can address)

This creates emails that feel genuinely tailored rather than templated. For example, if a prospect recently posted about hiring challenges, your email might open with: "I noticed your post about scaling your sales team - we've helped similar SaaS companies reduce ramp time by 30%..." This level of relevance dramatically improves response rates.
