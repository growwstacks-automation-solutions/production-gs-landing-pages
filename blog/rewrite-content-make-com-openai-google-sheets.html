The Content Repurposing Challenge

Every content creator knows the frustration: you spend hours crafting the perfect video or blog post, only to face the daunting task of rewriting it for X, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, and other platforms. The blank page stares back as you struggle to rephrase the same ideas in slightly different ways.

This repetitive work isn't just tedious—it's inefficient. Studies show content teams spend 30-40% of their time simply reformatting existing material rather than creating new value. The solution? Automation that handles the mechanical rewriting while you focus on strategy and quality control.

Key insight: AI excels at content transformation—the exact task most humans find most tedious. By automating this repetitive work, you reclaim hours each week for higher-value activities.

How the Make.com + OpenAI Workflow Works

This automation solves the repurposing problem through a simple but powerful sequence:

  1. Pull video transcripts or blog content from Google Sheets
  2. Break content into manageable chunks using Make.com's Iterator
  3. Send each piece to OpenAI with platform-specific prompts
  4. Receive transformed content tailored for each platform
  5. Write the new versions back to your spreadsheet

The magic happens in the prompts—carefully crafted instructions that tell OpenAI exactly how to rewrite your content for each platform's unique requirements and audience expectations.

Step 1: Setting Up Google Sheets Integration

The workflow begins by connecting Make.com to your content repository in Google Sheets. At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how to:

  • Locate your spreadsheet ID from the URL
  • Configure the "Get Range of Values" module to pull your content
  • Set the correct sheet name and cell range (e.g., A2:C6)
  • Test the connection to ensure proper data flow

Pro tip: Structure your source spreadsheet with clear columns for original content and empty columns where the platform-specific versions will be written. This makes the automation cleaner and easier to maintain.

Step 2: Configuring OpenAI for Content Transformation

With your content flowing into Make.com, the next step is configuring OpenAI to transform it. At 5:40 in the tutorial, you'll learn:

  • How to connect your OpenAI API key to Make.com
  • When to use GPT-3.5 vs. GPT-4 (hint: GPT-3.5 is cost-effective for simple transformations)
  • Setting token limits to control costs
  • Mapping spreadsheet content to the prompt variables

The video demonstrates how each piece of content flows into OpenAI's "Generate Response" module, ready to be transformed according to your platform-specific instructions.

Step 3: Creating Platform-Specific Prompts

The quality of your output depends entirely on the prompts you provide. At 7:20, the tutorial shows how to:

  • Use ChatGPT to help craft effective transformation prompts
  • Tailor instructions for different platforms (e.g., concise hooks for X vs. keyword-rich descriptions for YouTube Shorts)
  • Iterate on prompts based on initial results
  • Add formatting rules (like avoiding em dashes in social posts)

Example X prompt: "Transform this video transcript into a compelling tweet that makes viewers curious to watch the full video. Keep it under 240 characters. Don't summarize the whole video—just tease one interesting aspect. Avoid em dashes."

Step 4: Building the Make.com Workflow

With all components ready, the tutorial at 10:15 walks through assembling the complete workflow:

  1. Adding an Iterator to process content pieces sequentially
  2. Setting up routers for different platform transformations
  3. Configuring the Google Sheets "Update Row" module to write results back
  4. Testing and debugging the sequence

The final workflow handles the entire repurposing process automatically—pulling content, transforming it for multiple platforms, and updating your spreadsheet—while you focus on reviewing and scheduling the results.
