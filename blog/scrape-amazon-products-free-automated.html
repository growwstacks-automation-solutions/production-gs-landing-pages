The Pain of Manual Scraping

Every eCommerce entrepreneur knows the frustration of manually copying product data from Amazon. Whether you're doing market research, competitive analysis, or sourcing products for dropshipping, the process is painfully slow. You spend hours switching between tabs, copying details one field at a time, and pasting into spreadsheets.

The worst part? By the time you've compiled your data, prices and availability may have already changed. Manual scraping simply can't keep up with Amazon's dynamic marketplace.

Time wasted: Our tests show manual scraping takes 3-5 minutes per product. For just 100 products, that's 5-8 hours of tedious work that could be automated.

How the Automation Works

This workflow combines two powerful tools: n8n for automation and Appify for web scraping. Together, they create a seamless pipeline from Amazon to your Google Sheets.

Here's the magic behind the scenes:

  1. You provide an Amazon category URL
  2. Appify scrapes all products in that category
  3. The raw data gets processed in n8n
  4. Selected fields are structured and cleaned
  5. Everything gets saved to your Google Sheet

The entire process runs in about 5 minutes for 20-50 products, compared to hours of manual work.

Setting Up Appify

Appify provides the scraping power through their Amazon Products Scraper actor. The setup takes just 5 minutes:

  1. Create a free Appify account (you get $5 credit)
  2. Find the "Amazon Products Scraper" in their store
  3. Configure basic settings like number of results (start with 20 for testing)
  4. Copy your API key from the settings page

Pro tip: The $5 free credit lets you scrape approximately 500-1,000 products depending on how many fields you capture per product.

Configuring the n8n Workflow

The n8n workflow handles all the automation logic. After importing the pre-built JSON file (available in the video description), you'll need to:

  1. Add your Appify API key to the HTTP Request node
  2. Configure the form trigger with your preferred Amazon category
  3. Test the connection to ensure scraping works

At 2:45 in the video tutorial, you can see exactly where to paste your API key in the HTTP Request node configuration.

Customizing Your Data Fields

The workflow captures over 15 product details by default, but you can easily customize which fields get saved to your spreadsheet. The key component is the Set node which transforms the raw scraped data into structured fields.

Essential fields to include:

  • Product title and ASIN (unique Amazon identifier)
  • Price and discount percentage
  • Rating and number of reviews
  • Bullet points and product description
  • Delivery time information

You can add or remove fields by editing the Set node configuration without changing any code.

Connecting to Google Sheets

The final step connects your scraped data to Google Sheets:

  1. Create a new Google Sheet named "Amazon Products"
  2. Add column headers matching your selected fields
  3. Connect n8n to your Google account
  4. Map each field to its corresponding spreadsheet column

The workflow appends new rows each time it runs, creating a growing database of product information. At 7:30 in the video, you can see how the Google Sheets node is configured to match fields to columns.

Running Your First Scrape

With everything set up, running your first scrape is simple:

  1. Open the workflow in n8n
  2. Enter an Amazon category URL (like Electronics > Home Audio)
  3. Click "Execute Workflow"
  4. Watch as products populate your Google Sheet automatically

Example: Scraping the "Home Audio" subcategory (about 50 products) takes approximately 3 minutes and captures all key product details without any manual work.

Scaling Your Scraping

Once you've tested the workflow, you can scale up your scraping:

  • Increase the number of products per scrape (from 20 to 100+)
  • Add multiple category URLs to scrape simultaneously
  • Schedule daily/weekly runs to track price changes
  • Integrate with other tools like Airtable or your CRM

For larger projects, consider upgrading to paid plans on both n8n and Appify for higher limits and additional features like proxies.
