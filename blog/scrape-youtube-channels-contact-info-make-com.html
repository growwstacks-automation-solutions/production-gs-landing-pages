The Sales Problem YouTube Solves

Sales teams and marketers know YouTube contains a goldmine of potential leads - business owners, influencers, and decision-makers actively promoting their expertise. Yet manually visiting hundreds of channels to hunt for contact information is painfully inefficient. Most give up after a handful of attempts, missing out on qualified leads.

This automation solves three critical challenges: First, it systematically searches YouTube for channels in your target niche. Second, it extracts all available contact information from channel pages and linked websites. Third, it structures the data for immediate use in outreach campaigns - no manual copying required.

Key insight: In testing, 60% of business-focused YouTube channels include at least one contact method in their About section, yet fewer than 5% of sales teams systematically mine this data.

How the Automation Works

The automation combines Make.com's workflow engine with specialized scraping tools to systematically extract and process YouTube channel data. At its core, it performs three key functions: searching for relevant channels, scraping their details, and extracting contact information from any linked websites.

Here's the technical flow: First, a custom HTTP request queries YouTube's search API through Purple Beep Boop's scraping service. This returns channel IDs and basic metadata. Next, another scraper fetches full channel details including About sections. Finally, an email extractor scans any linked websites for contact information before outputting structured data to Google Sheets.

Step 1: Setting Up the YouTube Scrapers

The automation relies on Purple Beep Boop's YouTube scraping endpoints, which provide structured access to channel data that would otherwise require complex parsing. After creating an account, you'll need to:

Step 1.1: Configure API Access

Generate your API key in Purple Beep Boop's dashboard and add it to Make.com's HTTP request headers. The key authenticates your requests and tracks usage.

Step 1.2: Set Up Channel Search Module

Create an HTTP module in Make.com that calls Purple Beep Boop's channel search endpoint. Configure it with your target keywords (like "accounting tips") to find relevant channels.

Pro tip: The search module returns 20 channels at a time with a continuation token for pagination. Set up a repeater to automatically fetch additional pages until all results are processed.

Step 2: Configuring Channel Search

With the scraper connected, you'll customize the search parameters to target your ideal prospects. The demo focuses on accounting channels, but you can modify the keywords for any niche.

Key configuration options include:

  • Search query: Terms like "marketing agency" or "real estate investing"
  • Result sorting: By relevance, subscriber count, or upload date
  • Minimum subscribers: Filter out very small channels
  • Language and region: Target specific markets

At the 2:15 mark in the video, you can see how changing just the search term from "accounting" to "tax preparation" immediately surfaces a different set of high-value channels.

Step 3: Extracting Channel Details

After identifying target channels, the next module fetches their full profiles including About sections where contact information is often listed. This involves:

Step 3.1: Channel Details Request

Using the channel IDs from the search results, make a second API call to Purple Beep Boop's channel details endpoint. This returns richer data including subscriber counts, join dates, and most importantly - the About text and links.

Step 3.2: Link Extraction

Parse the About section to identify any website links. These often contain contact pages with emails, phone numbers, or social media handles not listed directly on YouTube.

Filtering tip: Add a filter module to exclude personal channels by checking for business-related keywords in the description or links to professional websites.

Step 4: Contact Information Extraction

The most valuable step scans extracted website links for contact details. This happens through Purple Beep Boop's bulk email extractor, which:

  • Visits each linked website
  • Scans the HTML for email patterns and phone numbers
  • Returns structured contact data

In testing, this step successfully extracted emails from 40% of scraped channels, with higher success rates for business-focused content. The system found multiple contact methods (email + phone) for about 15% of channels.

Step 5: Output Options and Integrations

The final modules format and export the collected data. The demo uses Google Sheets, but you could integrate with:

  • CRMs: HubSpot, Salesforce, or Pipedrive
  • Databases: Airtable or Notion
  • Email platforms: Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign

Each channel becomes a row with columns for:

  • Channel name and URL
  • Subscriber count and niche
  • Extracted emails and phone numbers
  • Social media handles
  • Date scraped
