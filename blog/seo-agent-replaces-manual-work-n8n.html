The Problem With Expensive SEO Tools

SEO professionals know they need competitor data to succeed, but most rely on expensive tools like Ahrefs and SEMrush that cost $300/month or more. These platforms provide valuable insights, but their subscription models create recurring costs that add up quickly - especially for agencies managing multiple clients.

The breakthrough came when we discovered SEO APIs that offer pay-per-use access to similar data. By combining these APIs with n8n's automation capabilities, we built a workflow that delivers comparable insights at a fraction of the cost. Instead of paying monthly subscriptions, you only pay for the data you actually use.

Cost savings: This workflow reduces SEO tool expenses by 80-90% compared to traditional subscriptions. Where Ahrefs might cost $300/month, this solution averages $30-50/month for most users.

How The SEO Agent Works

The workflow consists of four connected processes that analyze different aspects of competitor SEO performance. Unlike manual tools that require separate reports, this system consolidates all data into a single Google Sheet for easy analysis.

At 2:15 in the video, you can see how the workflow begins with a simple API call to the SEO data provider. The four main components are:

  1. Domain Rank Overview: Shows estimated organic traffic and domain authority metrics
  2. Relevant Pages: Identifies the website's top-performing content
  3. Ranked Keywords Performance: Extracts search volumes and keyword difficulty
  4. Competitor Visibility Overview: Reveals competing domains ranking for similar terms

Each process runs sequentially, with the results automatically populating separate tabs in a Google Sheet. This creates a comprehensive competitor analysis dashboard without manual data entry.

Domain Rankings Analysis

The first module provides a high-level view of a domain's SEO performance. It answers fundamental questions like: How much organic traffic does this site get? What's their domain authority? How does their search visibility trend over time?

At 3:45 in the video, we analyze safehome.org - a home security site getting 1.5 million monthly organic searches. The workflow reveals this key insight immediately, whereas manual tools would require navigating multiple reports.

Key metric: Organic ETV (Estimated Traffic Value) shows how much a site's organic traffic would cost if purchased as ads. For safehome.org, this exceeds $500,000/month - proving the value of their SEO strategy.

Identifying Competitor's Best Pages

The second module extracts a website's top 100 performing pages - the content driving their organic traffic. This is gold for SEO strategists, as it reveals exactly what content works best in your niche.

At 5:20 in the video, we see how safehome.org's "/security-systems/best" page ranks #1 for 486 different searches. This immediately identifies their money page - the content generating the most conversions.

Three ways to use this data:

  • Analyze page structure (headings, internal linking) of top performers
  • Identify content gaps where you could create better versions
  • Discover lucrative sub-niches you may have overlooked

Keyword Performance Data

The third module extracts all keywords a domain ranks for, along with search volumes and difficulty scores. This reveals their keyword strategy - which terms they prioritize and how they perform.

At 8:10 in the video, we see safehome.org focuses on transactional keywords like "alarm security systems" rather than informational content. This makes sense for their business model, as these terms convert better.

Pro tip: Sort keywords by "number one positions" to identify a competitor's most valuable terms - these represent their highest-converting traffic sources.

Competitor Visibility Overview

The final module is perhaps the most powerful - it identifies competing domains you may not have known about. By analyzing who ranks for similar keywords, you discover secondary competitors and new opportunities.

At 10:30 in the video, we see how safehome.org competes with major publishers like New York Times and US News in the home security niche. This reveals the competitive landscape beyond direct competitors.

Practical applications:

  • Find lower-authority sites outperforming on specific terms
  • Discover content partnerships or acquisition targets
  • Identify underserved niches with less competition

Implementation Tips

To get the most from this workflow, we recommend running it monthly for your top competitors. Track changes in their rankings and new pages to stay ahead of their strategy.

Three best practices:

  1. Focus on money pages: Prioritize analyzing pages ranking for commercial keywords
  2. Track trends: Compare monthly reports to spot emerging opportunities
  3. Expand gradually: Start with 3-5 competitors, then add more as needed

The workflow's cost-effectiveness allows this frequent analysis - something prohibitively expensive with traditional SEO tools.
