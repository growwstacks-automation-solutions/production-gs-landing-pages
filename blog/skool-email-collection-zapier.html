The Email Collection Problem

Community leaders using Skool have faced a persistent challenge - while the platform excels at engagement, collecting member contact information has been cumbersome. Without emails, you can't nurture relationships outside Skool or build a true marketing funnel. The workarounds were painful:

For paid groups, you might access emails through Stripe receipts - but this required manual exports and cross-referencing. Free groups had it worse - begging members to voluntarily provide emails through profile fields or membership questions, then manually copying responses.

Two Automated Solutions

Skool's new API features now offer two distinct paths to automate email collection, depending on your group type:

For Paid Groups: The Stripe integration passes purchaser emails automatically through Zapier. When someone joins your paid community, their email flows directly to your CRM without any manual steps.

For Free Groups: The membership questions feature becomes your collection point. While you can't require email submission, most members will provide it when asked through onboarding questions. Zapier then captures these responses automatically.

Paid Group Email Collection

The paid group solution is the most straightforward. When members purchase access through Stripe, their email becomes available through Skool's API. Here's how it works:

  1. Trigger: "New Paid Member" event in Skool
  2. Action: Zapier captures the member's email from the Stripe transaction
  3. Destination: Your CRM adds the email to a list or sequence

Implementation tip: Set up a separate Zap for each payment tier if you offer multiple membership levels. This lets you segment your email marketing based on what members purchased.

Free Group Email Collection

For free communities, the process involves one extra step but is still vastly simpler than manual collection:

  1. Setup: Add "What is your email address?" as a membership question
  2. Trigger: "Answered Membership Question" event in Skool
  3. Filter: Zapier checks if the response contains an email
  4. Action: Valid emails route to your CRM

While not 100% comprehensive (some members may skip the question), this method typically captures 70-90% of member emails automatically. The key is crafting a compelling reason for members to provide their email voluntarily.

Zapier Setup Steps

Configuring the integration requires connecting three systems: Skool, Zapier, and your CRM. Here's the step-by-step:

Step 1: Connect Your Accounts

In Zapier, authenticate connections to both Skool and your CRM. For Skool, you'll need admin access to generate API keys.

Step 2: Choose the Right Trigger

Select either "New Paid Member" (for paid groups) or "Answered Membership Question" (for free groups) as your trigger event.

Step 3: Map the Data Fields

For paid groups: Map the Stripe email field to your CRM's email field. For free groups: Map the specific question answer containing the email.

Step 4: Test and Activate

Zapier will walk you through test mode to verify the connection works before going live.

Pro tip: Add a filter step to validate email formats before they hit your CRM. This prevents malformed entries from causing issues.

CRM Integration Options

While the tutorial demonstrates Kit CRM, several platforms work with this integration:

  • Kit: Directly add members to forms or sequences
  • FloDesk: Create new subscribers in your email lists
  • HubSpot: Add as contacts with appropriate lifecycle stage
  • Webhooks: For unsupported CRMs, use Zapier webhooks to push data

The key is matching Skool member data to your CRM's contact model. Most platforms let you add custom fields for additional information like join date or membership level.
