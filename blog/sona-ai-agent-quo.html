What Sona AI Agent Does

Businesses lose an average of 20% of potential revenue from missed calls - calls that go to voicemail, get dropped, or aren't followed up on promptly. Sona solves this by being your virtual receptionist that never sleeps, handling calls with the consistency and professionalism of your best employee.

Unlike basic IVR systems that frustrates callers with endless menus, Sona uses natural language understanding to have real conversations. It can answer FAQs about your business hours, location, products, or services, collect contact information from leads, and even qualify sales inquiries based on criteria you set.

24/7 availability with human-like interactions: Sona provides instant responses any time of day or night, with voice that sounds natural that most callers can't tell they're talking to an AI. This creates better customer experiences while freeing your team to focus on high-value interactions.

How to Train Sona on Your Business

The key to Sona's effectiveness is how well it understands and represents your specific business. Generic AI responses create frustrating customer experiences. Fortunately, Sona offers three powerful ways to train it on your unique knowledge base:

1. Website import: Sona can crawl your website to automatically extract product information, FAQs, service details, and other structured knowledge. This creates instant baseline understanding without manual data entry.

2. Document upload: Upload PDFs, CSVs, or text files containing your pricing sheets, service manuals, training manuals, or customer support scripts. Sona will process and learn from these documents just like a new employee would.

3. Custom Q&A: Create manual question-and-answer entries for scenarios specific to your business. This is where you teach Sona how to handle nuanced inquiries that might not be documented elsewhere.

Pro tip: Start with website import for broad knowledge, then add custom Q&A for your most common customer inquiries. Test responses in the app before publishing changes to ensure accuracy.

Powerful Jobs Feature

While basic call answering is valuable, Sona's real power comes from its Jobs feature - reusable instruction sets that transform the AI from passive responder into an active participant in your business workflows.

Jobs allow you to define specific actions Sona should take when certain conditions are met. For example:

  • Lead qualification: Ask callers specific questions to assess fit and route to your sales team with pre-qualified leads
  • Support ticket creation: Gather troubleshooting details and automatically open tickets in your help desk system
  • Appointment scheduling: Check calendar availability and book meetings without human intervention

Each job includes natural language instructions that guide Sona's conversation flow, plus backend actions like CRM updates or notifications. The same job can be reused across multiple phone numbers or workflows, and updates propagate automatically wherever the job is used.

Smart Call Transfers

Even the most advanced AI can't handle every customer inquiry. Sona knows when to escalate calls to a human teammate while maintaining seamless customer experience.

The system evaluates multiple factors to determine when transfer is needed:

  • Caller intent: Based on keywords, sentiment, and conversation flow
  • Complexity: When inquiries require human judgment or personal interaction
  • Escalation triggers: When the caller explicitly asks to speak with someone

Transfers can be routed to specific team members based on:

  • Department (sales, support, billing)
  • Availability (next open agent or specific team member)
  • External phone numbers (for field teams or partners)

Zero-drop transfers: Sona provides context to the human agent before transferring, including call summary, caller details collected, so customers never have to repeat themselves.

Implementation Tips

Implementing Sona effectively requires more than just technical setup. These pro tips will help you maximize value from day one:

Start with your top 20%: Identify the 20% of call types that account for 80% of your volume. Train Sona to handle these perfectly first, then expand to edge cases.

Monitor and iterate: Review call logs and transcripts weekly to identify patterns. Look for frequently asked questions you haven't covered or where Sona's responses could be improved.

Human-in-the-loop testing: Before going live, have team members test Sona by calling in with various scenarios. This surfaces gaps in training that might not be obvious in testing.

Iterative improvement: Treat Sona gets smarter over time as you add more training data and refine jobs. Plan to spend 30 minutes weekly reviewing and improving its knowledge base.
