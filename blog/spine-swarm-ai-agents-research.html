Spine Swarm changes this equation fundamentally. How Spine Swarm Coordinates Hundreds of AI Agents

Unlike single-model AI systems, Spine Swarm deploys specialized agents that each handle different aspects of a research task. When you ask Spine a question like "What is the competitive landscape of Lovable?" (as shown at 1:10 in the video), it doesn't just generate an answer—it builds a team.

Here's how the swarm approach works:

  • Research agents identify relevant companies and data sources
  • Analysis agents build detailed profiles on each competitor
  • Tracking agents monitor funding rounds, product launches, and sentiment shifts
  • Synthesis agents combine everything into an executive-ready dashboard

This division of labor mirrors how human research teams work—but without the coordination overhead. As seen at 1:30, every agent's reasoning is visible on Spine's canvas, with sources properly cited.

2. Startup Product Validation

At 1:50 in the video, we see Spine evaluating a new business idea: a platform matching first-time home buyers with off-market properties. Instead of blindly researching, Spine first asks clarifying questions:

  • What markets are you targeting?
  • Are you focused on buyers, sellers, or agents?
  • What's your price point?

Then its agent team divides the research:

  • One sizes the first-time home buyer market
  • Another maps existing players (Zillow, Open Door)
  • Third researches off-market deal mechanics
  • Fourth analyzes state-by-state regulations
  • Fifth scans Reddit for buyer pain points

15 minutes later: structured go/no-go analysis with all sources cited.

From raw notes to executable strategy in one workflow.
