Webhook Setup in Contact Form 7

The automation begins by configuring Contact Form 7 to send submission data to Make.com via webhook. This requires the Contact Form 7 Webhooks extension plugin:

Step 1: Install the Webhooks Extension

From your WordPress dashboard, navigate to Contact → Add-ons and install the Webhooks extension. This adds a new "Webhooks" tab to each form's settings.

Step 2: Create a New Webhook

In your form's Webhooks tab, click "Add Webhook" and select "Custom Webhook". Leave the Key field blank unless you need advanced authentication.

Step 3: Map Form Fields

Assign each form field (name, email, etc.) to a clear variable name that Make.com will recognize. Consistent naming here prevents confusion later.

Pro tip: Name your webhook variables identically to your Google Sheets column headers for automatic matching (e.g. "email" → "Email").

Configuring Make.com Webhook Trigger

With Contact Form 7 ready to send data, we now configure Make.com to receive it:

Step 1: Create New Scenario

In Make.com, start a new scenario and select "Webhooks" as your trigger module. Choose "Custom Webhook" and copy the provided URL.

Step 2: Connect to Contact Form 7

Paste the Make.com webhook URL into Contact Form 7's Webhook settings. Save both configurations.

Step 3: Test the Connection

Submit a test form on your WordPress site. Within seconds, you should see the data appear in Make.com's webhook history, confirming the connection works.

Troubleshooting: If data doesn't appear, check that your WordPress site's IP isn't blocked by Make.com's firewall and that the Webhooks extension is active.

Connecting to Google Sheets

The final step routes form data from Make.com to your spreadsheet:

Step 1: Add Google Sheets Module

In your Make.com scenario, add the Google Sheets "Add a Row" action. Authenticate with your Google account and select the target spreadsheet.

Step 2: Map Data Fields

Match each webhook variable (name, email, etc.) to the corresponding Google Sheets column. Make.com will automatically populate new rows with this structure.

Step 3: Set Up Header Row

Ensure your Google Sheet has a header row with columns named to match your variable mapping. New submissions will append below existing data.

Time-saver: The video at 4:30 shows how to drag-and-drop variables in Make.com for rapid field mapping without manual entry.

Testing the Automation

Before going live, validate the entire workflow:

Step 1: Submit Test Form

Fill out and submit your Contact Form 7 form with sample data. Watch for the success message confirming the webhook fired.

Step 2: Verify Make.com Processing

Check your Make.com scenario history to confirm it received the webhook payload and processed without errors.

Step 3: Check Google Sheets

Open your target spreadsheet - a new row should appear with all submitted form fields populated in the correct columns.

Implementation note: The entire process from form submission to Sheets update typically completes in under 5 seconds - faster than most email notifications.
