The Content Repurposing Challenge

Content creators face a universal problem: you spend hours crafting valuable Substack notes, only to have them disappear into the void after publication. Meanwhile, your Instagram sits dormant because you don't have time to manually reformat content for different platforms.

This workflow solves three critical pain points:

  • Time waste: Manually converting long-form notes to social snippets can take 30-45 minutes per post
  • Inconsistency: Irregular posting schedules hurt algorithm visibility
  • Platform fatigue: Maintaining multiple content formats drains creative energy

Real-world impact: One creator using this automation went from posting 1-2 times weekly to daily Instagram content while actually reducing their workload.

Workflow Overview

The system comprises two main workflows running in Make.com, each handling a different content transformation approach:

Workflow 1: Video Creation

  1. Pulls latest Substack notes via Creative Hub app
  2. Filters to ensure only actual notes (not newsletters) proceed
  3. Generates short videos using Blotato with custom templates
  4. Posts to Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts

Workflow 2: Image Generation

  1. Takes the same Substack notes
  2. Uses Gemini Nano Banana Pro to create branded images
  3. Uploads to Google Drive for storage
  4. Distributes to Instagram feed and Pinterest

Key innovation: The system runs six parallel versions of these workflows to create varied content styles from the same source material, ensuring your social feeds don't look repetitive.

Step 1: Video Creation Process

The video workflow transforms text notes into engaging short-form videos perfect for Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts. Here's how it works:

Template Setup

Using Canva, create a simple background image with your brand colors and logo. This serves as the visual foundation for all generated videos.

Content Processing

The workflow:

  1. Uploads your background template to Blotato
  2. Selects the "minimalist style" video template
  3. Dynamically inserts your latest Substack note as the text content
  4. Adds your branding as a watermark

Pro tip: Add a 45-second delay after video generation to ensure Blotato has enough time to process before attempting to post.

Step 2: AI Image Generation

For static content, the workflow leverages Gemini AI to create visually striking images from your notes:

Prompt Engineering

The system uses detailed prompts to ensure brand-consistent imagery:

  • "Filter minimalist edition" for clean, professional looks
  • "Pink money pearls edition" for feminine/glamorous styles
  • "Stunning aesthetic edition" for artistic interpretations

Error Handling

A smart error-catching module prevents workflow failures when Gemini experiences high load, automatically skipping problematic generations without stopping the entire automation.

Visual variety: By rotating through different prompt styles, your Instagram feed maintains visual interest while keeping content production fully automated.

Cross-Platform Posting Strategy

The automation doesn't stop at content creation - it handles distribution too:

Platform-Specific Optimization

  • Instagram: Square images for feed, vertical videos for Reels
  • Pinterest: Vertical pins with keyword-rich descriptions
  • YouTube Shorts: Ultra-short videos with AI-generated titles

Performance Tracking

The workflow includes optional modules to:

  1. Log which content performs best on each platform
  2. Adjust posting frequency based on engagement
  3. Highlight top-converting posts for potential paid promotion

Real results: One user saw Pinterest views jump from 200 to 3,500 monthly after implementing this system, with new subscribers coming directly from social platforms.
