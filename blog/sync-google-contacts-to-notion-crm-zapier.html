The Contact Management Problem

Most business owners face the same frustrating reality: their contacts are scattered across multiple platforms. Google Contacts holds phone numbers for quick dialing, while Notion contains project notes and meeting details - but the two never talk to each other. This forces manual copying of phone numbers, email addresses, and other details between systems.

The breaking point comes when you need to quickly reach someone but can't remember where their current contact info lives. As shown in the tutorial, this exact pain led to creating the automation - wanting immediate access to contacts within Notion's superior organizational system while maintaining Android's native dialer integration.

Key stat: The average professional wastes 2.3 hours per week manually transferring contact information between systems - time that could be spent on actual relationship building.

Google Contacts Cleanup Strategy

Before setting up any automation, the tutorial reveals a crucial first step: cleaning your Google Contacts. Most people's contact lists contain years of accumulated cruft - old customer service emails, unused app accounts, and outdated professional connections.

The proven method involves systematically working through your contacts alphabetically. At 6:15 in the video, you'll see how tackling just one letter per day can transform a bloated 6,000-contact list into a lean 400-contact powerhouse in under a week. This cleanup ensures you're only syncing contacts you actually need in your Notion CRM.

Zapier Setup Overview

The automation uses Zapier as the bridge between Google Contacts and Notion. While the initial attempt tried using contact labels as triggers, the final solution uses a more reliable approach:

  1. Trigger: New or updated contact in Google Contacts
  2. Filter: Only continue if contact has specific label ("Sync to Notion")
  3. Action: Create or update Notion database item with mapped fields

This structure ensures only your designated contacts sync to Notion, preventing clutter from less important connections.

Trigger Configuration

At 9:42 in the tutorial, you'll see the critical trigger setup that makes this automation work reliably. The key is using "Contact Updated" as the trigger rather than relying solely on label assignments.

This solves a common Zapier limitation where label changes don't always trigger actions. By watching for any contact update (including label changes), then filtering for your specific label, you create a more robust sync system.

Pro tip: Create a dedicated "Sync to Notion" label in Google Contacts and apply it only to contacts you want in your CRM. This gives you precise control over which contacts sync.

Notion Field Mapping

The real power comes in how you map Google Contact fields to Notion's flexible database properties. At 14:30, the tutorial shows how to create these essential mappings:

  • Title: First + Last name (combined in Notion)
  • Phone: Mobile number from Google Contacts
  • Email: Work vs personal distinction
  • URL: Link to messaging conversation (WhatsApp, Messenger, etc.)

These mappings turn Notion into a true CRM where you can see at a glance how to best reach each contact.

Custom Status Fields

One of Notion's biggest advantages is the ability to create custom status fields. The tutorial demonstrates setting up a "Contact Status" property with values like:

  • New
  • Hot Prospect
  • Client
  • Past Client

This allows you to filter and sort your contacts by relationship stage - something impossible in Google Contacts. Combined with Notion's relation properties, you can link contacts directly to projects or meetings.

Handling Messaging Preferences

At 18:15, the tutorial reveals a clever solution for tracking how each contact prefers to communicate. Rather than just storing phone numbers, the automation creates a "Preferred Channel" field that records whether someone uses:

  • WhatsApp
  • Facebook Messenger
  • SMS
  • Other platforms

Even better, the system stores the direct URL to your conversation with that person, letting you jump straight to the right messaging context with one click.
