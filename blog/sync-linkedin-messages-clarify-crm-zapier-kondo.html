The LinkedIn CRM Problem

Most sales teams and founders waste hours each week manually copying LinkedIn messages into their CRM. Important conversations get buried in your inbox, follow-ups slip through the cracks, and you have no centralized record of your outreach history.

The challenge is compounded because LinkedIn doesn't provide email addresses for most connections. Without this critical information, it's impossible to properly track relationships in your CRM or connect conversations to the right company records.

83% of sales professionals say they lose track of important LinkedIn conversations because they don't have a system to automatically log them in their CRM.

How the Integration Works

This Zapier workflow solves the LinkedIn-CRM disconnect by combining Kondo's LinkedIn integration with Clarify's flexible API. Here's the complete flow:

  1. Kondo captures LinkedIn messages and sends them to Zapier via webhook
  2. Zapier looks up contact details using Apollo.io (or another data provider)
  3. The system structures the data using GPT for consistent formatting
  4. Zapier creates a complete contact record in Clarify CRM
  5. The conversation history is added as an activity comment
  6. The contact is added to a designated list for easy filtering

The entire process happens automatically whenever you receive or send a LinkedIn message (or manually when you choose specific conversations to sync).

Setting Up the Kondo Webhook

Kondo acts as the bridge between LinkedIn and your automation workflow. At 1:45 in the video, you can see the exact webhook configuration:

  1. Navigate to Kondo's webhook settings
  2. Create a new webhook for "Clarify Message Sync"
  3. Set the trigger type based on your preference (manual or streaming)
  4. Enter your Zapier webhook URL
  5. Configure the body data to include message content and sender details

Manual vs. Streaming: Manual sync gives you control by requiring a button click to transfer messages. Streaming automatically syncs every new message - great for comprehensive history but may create more CRM records than you need.

Configuring Zapier Steps

The Zapier workflow contains several critical steps to transform raw LinkedIn messages into structured CRM data:

Step 1: Capture the Webhook

Set up a Zapier webhook trigger to receive data from Kondo. This captures the LinkedIn message content, sender information, and conversation history.

Step 2: Look Up Contact Details

Since LinkedIn rarely provides email addresses, the workflow makes an API call to Apollo.io (or another data provider) to enrich the contact record. At 3:12 in the video, you can see the exact Apollo request configuration.

Step 3: Structure Data with GPT

A GPT step extracts key information like company name, domain, and likely email pattern from the available data. This ensures consistent formatting before creating the CRM record.

Enriching Contact Data

The most valuable part of this integration is how it transforms sparse LinkedIn data into complete CRM records. Here's what gets added automatically:

  • Contact Information: Name, LinkedIn profile URL, and (when available) email address
  • Company Details: Company name and domain extracted from the LinkedIn profile
  • Conversation History: Complete message thread with timestamps
  • Context: Notes about where/how you connected (useful for follow-ups)

At 4:30 in the video, you can see how GPT helps structure this information even when some fields are missing from the original LinkedIn data.

Creating Clarify Records

With enriched contact data, the workflow creates complete records in Clarify CRM:

  1. First, it checks if the contact already exists to avoid duplicates
  2. If new, it creates a person record with all available details
  3. It associates the contact with the correct company (creating it if necessary)
  4. Finally, it adds the LinkedIn conversation as an activity comment

This creates a complete timeline of your interactions right within the contact record - no more digging through LinkedIn to remember what you discussed.

Organizing with Lists

The final step adds each synced contact to a designated list in Clarify (like "LinkedIn Outreach - "). This provides several benefits:

  • Easy Filtering: Quickly find all contacts sourced from LinkedIn
  • Campaign Tracking: Group contacts by specific outreach initiatives
  • Follow-up Management: Create targeted sequences for LinkedIn connections

At 5:50 in the video, you can see how lists make it simple to manage hundreds of LinkedIn-sourced contacts without losing track of important relationships.
