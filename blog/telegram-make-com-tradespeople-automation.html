The Tradesperson's Problem

Every tradesperson knows the frustration - you're up on a roof, under a sink, or balancing on scaffolding when you realize you need to send an invoice, create a purchase order, or respond to a customer query. Your laptop is back in the truck, and typing on your phone with dirty hands is slow and error-prone.

This daily productivity killer costs tradespeople hours each week. According to industry surveys, tradespeople waste 3-5 hours weekly on administrative tasks that could be automated. The solution? Voice-powered automation that works where you work - on your phone, hands-free.

The breakthrough: Telegram's voice messaging combined with Make.com's automation platform creates a system where tradespeople can dictate business documents that automatically format into invoices, quotes, purchase orders and more.

How the Automation Works

The system connects Telegram to Make.com through API integrations. When a tradesperson sends a voice message or text through Telegram, Make.com automatically:

  1. Converts voice messages to text using Telegram's built-in transcription
  2. Identifies the sender (employee vs customer) using Telegram IDs
  3. Routes the message to the appropriate workflow branch
  4. Formats the content into the required document type
  5. Sends the completed document to the destination system (Google Sheets, email, CRM, etc.)

The entire process happens in seconds, with no manual typing required. As shown in the demo at 2:15, a simple voice message like "Hey, Joe from maintenance had a pay query..." automatically converts to a properly formatted email.

Setting Up the Workflow

While the complete Make.com workflow shown in the video is complex, the core components are straightforward:

Step 1: Telegram Bot Setup

Create a Telegram bot that will receive messages from employees and customers. This provides the API connection point for Make.com.

Step 2: Make.com Triggers

Configure Make.com to watch for new Telegram messages. The system can trigger on both text messages and voice notes.

Step 3: Message Processing

Add modules to convert voice to text, identify sender type, and extract key information like customer names, product quantities, or service dates.

Step 4: Document Generation

Create templates for each document type (invoice, quote, PO, etc.) that populate with the extracted information.

Step 5: Destination Routing

Connect to your business systems (Google Sheets, email, CRM) to send the completed documents where they're needed.
