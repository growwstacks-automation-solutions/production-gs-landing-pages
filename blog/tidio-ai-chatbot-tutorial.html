Getting Started: Account Setup in 3 Minutes

The barrier to entry is surprisingly low for what Tidio delivers. You don't need technical skills or a developer to get started - just a business email and your website URL.

Here's the exact process shown at 0:45 in the video:

Chat Widget Installation (Shopify/WordPress/Manual)

The chat widget is what customers see on your site - and Tidio makes installation painless regardless of your platform. At 1:30 in the tutorial, you'll see the three installation paths:

For Shopify Stores

From your Tidio dashboard, select "Install" then "Shopify." You'll be redirected to the Shopify App Store where you can install the Tidio Live Chat app with one click. The entire integration happens automatically - no coding or snippet pasting required.

For WordPress Sites

In your WordPress admin, navigate to Plugins → Add New, then search for "Tidio." Install and activate the official Tidio plugin. The widget will appear immediately after activation.

For Other Websites

Choose "Install on a website" in Tidio, then copy the generated code snippet. Paste this just before the closing </body> tag on your site. Basic technical skills are needed, but the widget appears instantly after implementation.

Mobile access: Tidio offers iOS and Android apps so you can respond to chats away from your desk. All conversations sync across devices.

Connecting Your Multi-Channel Inbox

One of Tidio's most powerful features is consolidating all customer communications into a single inbox. At 3:10 in the video, you'll see how to connect additional channels:

  1. Navigate to the "Channels" section in your Tidio dashboard
  2. Click "Connect" for Instagram, Facebook Messenger, or WhatsApp
  3. Follow the authentication steps for each platform
  4. Set routing rules if you have a team (e.g., sales vs support)

Once connected, DMs from these platforms route into the same Tidio inbox as your website chats. Your team can reply from one screen with full context, regardless of where the conversation started. This eliminates the tab-switching chaos of managing multiple communication apps.

For solo operators, enable basic operating hours and an away message so customers know when to expect replies. Teams can set up sophisticated routing rules to ensure billing questions go to finance while product inquiries reach support.

Training Lyro AI With Your Knowledge Base

The magic happens when you train Lyro to answer like your best support agent. At 4:25 in the tutorial, you'll see the knowledge base configuration:

  1. Go to "Lyro" in your Tidio dashboard
  2. Click "Get Started with Lyro"
  3. Import your help docs or FAQ pages (PDF/URL/text)
  4. Manually add common Q&A pairs using real customer phrasing
  5. Set topics Lyro is allowed to answer (e.g., returns but not pricing)
  6. Configure escalation rules for when to hand off to humans

Pro tip: Start with shipping/returns, pricing basics, and account access - these cover 60-80% of repetitive questions. Test with actual customer messages from past support tickets, not just how you wish they'd ask.

If Lyro "hallucinates" (makes up answers), remove vague content and replace with explicit Q&A entries. Keep responses short and specific - long paragraphs confuse the AI. You can also control Lyro's communication style to match your brand voice.

Building Your First Automation Flow

Tidio's visual flow builder lets you automate common interactions without coding. At 5:50 in the video, we create a welcome + lead capture flow:

  1. Open "Flows" and choose a template or start blank
  2. Add a trigger (e.g., "Visitor on site for 10 seconds")
  3. Set conditions (new vs returning visitor)
  4. Add actions: send message, ask for email, apply tags
  5. Create branches (e.g., route "ready to buy" to sales)
  6. Publish and test live

A good day-one flow welcomes visitors, asks if they need help, and offers to email resources if they provide their address. This builds your list while deflecting repetitive questions. The drag-and-drop interface makes it easy to experiment with different sequences.

More advanced flows can:

  • Segment users based on pages visited
  • Trigger abandoned cart messages
  • Schedule follow-ups after purchases
  • Route complex issues to specific departments
