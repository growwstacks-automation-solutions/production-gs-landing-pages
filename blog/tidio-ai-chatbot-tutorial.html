Building Your First Automated Flow

Tidio's visual flow builder transforms complex chatbot programming into a simple connecting-blocks process. Here's how to create an effective flow:

1. Choose a Trigger

Common starting points include "First website visit" or "Specific page viewed." The tutorial at 8:15 shows how page triggers can customize messages for product versus blog visitors.

2. Add Response Blocks

Each block represents a chatbot message or action. Link them to create conversation paths. At 8:45, you'll see how conditional branches based on user responses create dynamic dialogues.

3. Set Delays Between Messages

Natural conversations need pacing. The 5-second delay demonstrated at 9:05 prevents message flooding that feels robotic.

4. Include Human Handoff

Always provide an escape hatch to live support. The video at 10:20 shows the handoff configuration that maintains customer satisfaction.

Conversion tip: Add a lead capture block after providing value, as shown at 10:50. Visitors are 3x more likely to share contact information after receiving help.

Training the AI Chatbot With Your Knowledge Base

Tidio's Lyro AI agent becomes powerful when trained with your specific business information. Follow this process:

1. Upload Supporting Documents

Add PDFs, help articles, or product manuals that contain answers to common questions. The platform will index this content.

2. Provide Sample Q&A Pairs

At 12:30 in the video, you'll see how to enter common customer questions with ideal responses to teach the AI your brand voice.

3. Set Confidence Thresholds

Configure the AI to only answer when certainty exceeds 70% (shown at 13:10). Below this, it should transfer to human support.

4. Monitor and Refine

Review chat logs weekly to identify misunderstood questions requiring additional training.

Warning: Untrained AI chatbots can frustrate customers with irrelevant answers. Budget 1-2 hours weekly for refinement during the first month.
