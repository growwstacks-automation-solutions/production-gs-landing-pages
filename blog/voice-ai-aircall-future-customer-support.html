Why Voice AI is 10x Harder Than Chatbots

While text-based AI like ChatGPT has become commonplace, voice AI presents unique technical challenges that many businesses underestimate. The complexity stems from multiple conversion steps that compound potential errors.

A typical voice AI interaction requires: 1) speech-to-text transcription, 2) text processing by an LLM, and 3) text-to-speech conversion back to human-like voice. Each transition point introduces potential inaccuracies, and telephone networks further degrade audio quality. Emerging voice-to-voice models that skip text conversion may solve these challenges in coming years.

How AI is Transforming Call Center Management

Traditional call center management involved manual call monitoring and subjective performance evaluations. AI automates these processes while adding capabilities that were previously impossible at scale.

Modern systems like Aircall's automatically grade 100% of calls against predefined criteria (friendliness, script adherence, etc.), provide agents with personalized feedback, and even intervene in real-time during sensitive conversations. The Future of Voice-to-Voice AI Technology

Current voice AI systems rely on stitching together multiple technologies (speech recognition, LLMs, voice synthesis). The next frontier is voice-to-voice models that process audio directly without text conversion.

These emerging models can interpret vocal nuances like tone and emotion, enabling more natural conversations. While still in early stages, they promise to reduce error rates and latency while creating more human-like experiences. Aircall is actively testing these technologies for future product integration.
