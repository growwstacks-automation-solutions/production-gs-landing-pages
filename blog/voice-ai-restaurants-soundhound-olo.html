The ROI Numbers That Surprised Even Operators

When voice AI first entered restaurants, operators expected labor savings as the primary benefit. What shocked them was how dramatically it impacted revenue metrics. At the Olo Partner Summit, SoundHound revealed brands using their AI in drive-thrus see:

15% average check size increases from consistent, strategic upselling tailored to each restaurant's highest-margin items. Unlike human staff who vary their approach, the AI perfectly executes upsell strategies without fatigue or inconsistency.

Perhaps more valuable is the AI's dynamic throttling capability. During peak lunch rushes when throughput is critical, the system automatically reduces upsell intensity to keep lines moving. This balanced approach actually reduces drive-offs by 30% compared to human-only lanes where service speed varies wildly.

How AI Handles Complex Orders (And Knows When To Transfer)

One major operator concern was whether voice AI could handle the endless customizations customers request - "no pickles, extra sauce, gluten-free bun but regular fries." SoundHound's system trained on Olo's detailed menu data handles most requests flawlessly.

The AI detects when requests exceed its capabilities, instantly transferring to staff for:

  • Highly complex multi-item modifications
  • Detailed dietary questions beyond basic allergen info
  • Off-menu requests (like bringing back discontinued items)

This hybrid approach maintains order accuracy while freeing staff to focus on food preparation rather than routine order taking.

The Hidden Goldmine: Recovering Lost Phone Orders

While drive-thru gets attention, SoundHound's phone ordering AI quietly delivers massive value. Over 15,000 locations now use the system to capture orders that would have been lost during busy periods.

3-5 additional orders per day per location come from calls that previously would have gone unanswered during lunch rushes. For a 100-location chain, that's $2M+ annually in recovered revenue from a channel many had written off.

The AI answers immediately with perfect consistency, eliminating the variability of human phone operators. Integration with Olo means orders flow directly into the POS without rekeying errors.

Voice Insights: Turning Conversations Into Menu Optimization

SoundHound's Voice Insights product analyzes every customer interaction, revealing patterns impossible to detect manually. One brand discovered customers frequently asked for a discontinued LTO that had underperformed initially.

When they relaunched it based on this data, the item became their best-selling LTO ever - a insight they'd never have uncovered through traditional surveys. The system now tracks:

  • Most requested discontinued items
  • Common modification requests signaling menu gaps
  • Peak times customers ask about dessert (to optimize digital menu board timing)

The Next Frontier: In-Car Ordering Integration

SoundHound already integrates with Hyundai, Stellantis and Lucid vehicles, positioning restaurants for the autonomous driving future. As cars become mobile living spaces, voice ordering allows:

  • Natural language ordering while driving ("I want my usual burger combo")
  • Automatic pickup timing based on GPS ETA
  • Payment via saved profiles without fumbling for wallets

This channel represents the next wave of off-premise dining as drive-thru lanes evolve for autonomous vehicles.

Beyond Ordering: AI for Employee Training

SoundHound's Employee Assist product brings voice AI to back-of-house, solving one of restaurants' biggest challenges: consistent training. Staff can ask:

  • "How do I make the spicy sauce?"
  • "What's in the gluten-free bun?"
  • "Where are the allergen binders?"

Upcoming vision capabilities will let employees photograph items to instantly access preparation guides, reducing reliance on overburdened trainers.
