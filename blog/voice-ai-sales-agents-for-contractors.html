The $3,000/Month Receptionist Problem

Contractors face an impossible choice: answer every call personally while trying to work, or hire an expensive receptionist who still misses 30% of calls. Most home service businesses lose 15-20% of potential jobs simply because calls go to voicemail or responses come too late. When a homeowner needs a plumber or roofer, they typically call 3 companies - the first to answer often gets the job.

The traditional solution - hiring a receptionist - costs $3,000-$5,000/month with benefits, yet still has critical flaws. Human receptionists take breaks, call in sick, work limited hours, and make mistakes routing calls. During peak seasons when you need coverage most, they're overwhelmed. After hours and weekends when emergency calls come in, they're unavailable.

The hidden cost: For every 100 calls your business receives, 15-20 will hang up if not answered immediately. At an average job size of $2,500, that's $37,500-$50,000 in lost revenue per 100 calls - far more expensive than any receptionist salary.
