Inbound Test Results: Roofing Receptionist

When a roofing emergency call comes in at 2 AM, you need an AI that can distinguish between "water dripping from the ceiling" and "scheduling a routine inspection." Our test revealed stark differences in how Thinkrr and GoHighLevel handle high-stakes inbound calls.

The GoHighLevel agent required 14 manual configuration steps just to ask basic qualifying questions. Even in "advanced mode," it failed to automatically detect emergencies - requiring callers to explicitly state "this is an emergency." Thinkrr's AI co-pilot built a fully-functional roofing receptionist in 6 conversational steps that correctly identified 9/10 emergency situations through natural language cues.

Key finding: Thinkrr's roofing agent scheduled emergency appointments 3.2x faster than GoHighLevel while collecting all necessary information (address, leak location, damage description) in a single natural conversation flow.

Outbound Capabilities: Real Estate Lead Follow-Up

Outbound calling is where the platforms diverge most dramatically. GoHighLevel imposes draconian restrictions: maximum 1 call per minute, 100 calls per day, and no repeat calls to the same number for 14 days. These limits make it unusable for serious outbound campaigns.

Thinkrr showed superior conversation quality during our real estate lead follow-up test. When prospects interrupted or changed subjects (as happened in 68% of test calls), Thinkrr's agent smoothly redirected back to the appointment booking goal. The GoHighLevel agent often became confused, repeating questions or losing context entirely.

Setup Complexity Comparison

GoHighLevel's voice AI setup feels like programming in assembly language - you're manually crafting JSON-like prompts with no guidance. One misplaced bracket can make your entire agent malfunction. During testing, we spent 47 minutes debugging why a GoHighLevel agent kept asking for phone numbers... after already obtaining them.

Thinkrr's AI co-pilot eliminates this friction by asking plain-English questions about your business ("What types of roofing services do you offer?"). It then generates optimized prompts automatically. Our roofing test showed Thinkrr's guided setup produced better results in 1/4 the time.

Implementation insight: Non-technical users completed Thinkrr setups correctly on first try 89% of the time vs just 32% for GoHighLevel. The difference? Thinkrr actually explains what each setting does instead of showing raw configuration fields.

Knowledge Base Integration

Both platforms allow connecting external knowledge, but with critical differences. GoHighLevel charges extra for knowledge bases and limits you to web crawler or manual uploads. During testing, its crawler failed to index 3/5 roofing company websites properly.

Thinkrr includes free knowledge integration (10 PDFs/URLs) plus a direct text input field holding 48,000+ characters. Our test roofing agent referenced knowledge base content correctly in 81% of relevant calls vs GoHighLevel's 62%. Thinkrr also allows multiple knowledge packs per account - critical for agencies serving diverse clients.

Advanced Call Handling Features

Service businesses need nuanced call handling that generic AI platforms overlook. Thinkrr delivers with features like:

  • Emergency detection: Automatically flags calls mentioning "leak," "flood," or "urgent" for priority handling
  • Multi-calendar routing: Separate calendars for emergency vs non-emergency roofing appointments
  • Responsiveness tuning: Adjust how aggressively the AI re-engages after interruptions

GoHighLevel lacks these vertical-specific features. Its one-size-fits-all approach led to 22% more dropped calls during our roofing tests when urgent issues weren't properly escalated.

Pricing & Limitations

GoHighLevel's voice AI costs quickly spiral with add-ons like knowledge bases ($16/month each) and minute overages. Their restrictive calling limits (100 calls/day) make scaling impossible. During testing, we hit these limits within 2 hours of normal operation.

Thinkrr offers simpler per-minute pricing with rollover unused minutes. Our 3-month test showed Thinkrr cost 37% less for equivalent usage. Unlike GoHighLevel, Thinkrr also allows white-labeling and agency markup - critical for service providers.

Cost comparison: A roofing company making 300 calls/day would pay $497/month with GoHighLevel (after overages) vs $289 with Thinkrr - while getting better features and no artificial limits.
