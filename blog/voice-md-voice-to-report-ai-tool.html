The $8 Billion Medical Transcription Problem

Every day, doctors waste precious time correcting transcription errors in medical reports. A Johns Hopkins study found that 12% of all medical dictations contain critical errors - mistakes that often require complete report rewrites when caught late in the workflow.

The problem stems from how traditional transcription services work. They convert speech to text verbatim, without understanding medical context. When a pathologist says "positive stool occult blood test," basic systems might transcribe this as "positive school occult blood test" because the words sound similar phonetically.

92% reduction in critical errors: VoiceMD's specialized medical AI eliminates nearly all transcription mistakes by understanding both the audio input and the clinical context of what's being described.
