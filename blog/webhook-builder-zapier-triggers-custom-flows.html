For users who prefer no-code solutions, the platform is adding several new Zapier triggers through its unbranded AI connector app. These include events for new Google reviews, private feedback, video testimonials, contact changes, and review site connections - exactly as outlined at 1:15 in the video.

These triggers solve a common frustration: wanting to connect platform events to other apps but being limited by the available integration points. Now you can create sophisticated multi-app workflows that respond to nearly any activity in the system. The expanded trigger library means fewer compromises when designing your automation strategy.

Custom Flow Examples

What can you actually build with these new capabilities? The video at 1:45 hints at one powerful use case: tracking when trial users connect review sites as an engagement signal. But the possibilities go much further.

Technical vs Non-Technical Implementation

A key point made at 0:55 in the video is that these features serve different audiences. The Webhook Builder caters to technical users who need deep customization, while the Zapier triggers provide a no-code path for common integrations.

This dual approach means you don't have to choose between power and accessibility. Marketing teams can build Zapier flows without coding, while developers can use webhooks for complex proprietary systems. And when needs evolve, you can start with Zapier and later add webhooks for more advanced requirements.
