Two Main Sources for n8n Templates

Businesses often struggle to justify the time investment needed to build automations from scratch. The solution? Leverage the thousands of pre-built templates already available for n8n. There are two primary repositories containing over 10,000 ready-to-use workflows.

The first is the official n8n workflows repository with more than 4,000 production-ready templates. As shown in the video at 0:45, this GitHub repository organizes workflows by category and includes detailed documentation for each template.

Key benefit: These templates have been tested by thousands of users, meaning you're getting battle-tested automation logic without the trial and error.

The second source is n8n's template library, which contains nearly 8,000 additional workflows. This library includes templates for social media automation, lead generation, CRM integrations, and more - all searchable and filterable by popularity and category.

How to Import Templates Into Your n8n Instance

Importing templates is simpler than most businesses realize. The process takes just minutes but can save dozens of development hours. Here's the step-by-step method demonstrated in the video at 1:15:

Step 1: Copy the Template JSON

From either repository, select your desired template and copy the entire JSON workflow. For GitHub templates, you'll need to click "Raw" to access the clean JSON code.

Step 2: Create New Workflow

In your n8n instance, create a new workflow and open the workflow editor. This is where you'll paste the template content.

Step 3: Paste Template Content

Paste the copied JSON into your new workflow. n8n will automatically parse the template and recreate all nodes with their configurations intact.

Pro tip: Some templates may require additional credentials for services like Gmail or LinkedIn. These will be marked with warning icons until configured.

Setting Up Required Credentials

Most templates require some configuration before they'll work in your environment. The most common requirement is adding API credentials for the services the workflow interacts with.

As shown at 3:50 in the video, you'll typically need to:

  1. Create API keys in the respective service (Google, LinkedIn, etc.)
  2. Add these credentials to your n8n instance
  3. Map the credentials to the appropriate nodes in your workflow

The template documentation will specify exactly which credentials are needed. Common ones include:

  • Google Services (Gmail, Calendar, Sheets)
  • Social Media Platforms (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook)
  • CRM Systems (HubSpot, Salesforce)
  • Payment Processors (Stripe, PayPal)

Customizing Templates for Your Needs

The real power of templates comes when you adapt them to your specific business requirements. Every imported template can be modified without breaking the core functionality.

Common modifications include:

  • Adding or removing nodes to extend the workflow's capabilities
  • Adjusting trigger schedules to match your business cadence
  • Changing data fields to match your CRM or database structure
  • Incorporating additional services not in the original template

Implementation tip: Always test modified templates with sample data before deploying to production. The video at 4:20 shows how to use n8n's test feature to validate changes.
