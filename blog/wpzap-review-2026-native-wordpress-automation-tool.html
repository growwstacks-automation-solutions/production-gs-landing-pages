The Problem With External Automation

Most WordPress site owners unknowingly pay a "middleman tax" on their automation. Every time an order processes, a form submits, or a user registers, your data leaves WordPress, travels to an external server (like Zapier or Make), gets processed, and comes back. You're paying monthly fees just to pass messages between plugins that already live on your own server.

The costs compound as you grow. More orders mean higher Zapier bills. More leads trigger more Make.com tasks. Successful sites get penalized with escalating automation fees that have no relation to actual server costs.

Average WordPress site pays $300+/year for automation that could run natively for free. External platforms charge per task while adding latency, privacy risks, and dependency on their uptime.

How WPZap Works

WPZap eliminates the middleman by running automation directly within WordPress. Instead of sending data to external servers, it connects your plugins and services through native WordPress hooks and APIs.

The system uses WordPress's built-in event system to trigger workflows instantly when actions occur on your site. A new WooCommerce order? The workflow starts processing before Zapier would even receive the notification.

60-second setup: Pick a WordPress event as your trigger, select an action (like adding to Mailchimp or Google Sheets), and save. The automation runs entirely on your server with no external dependencies.
