The WordPress Automation Problem

WordPress powers over 43% of websites globally - that's more than 470 million sites. Nearly every one needs automation between plugins, forms, eCommerce, and external services. Yet most rely on expensive middlemen like Zapier that charge $30-$100+ per month just to move data between plugins that already live inside WordPress.

This creates three major problems: recurring costs that never end, data security risks from sending information outside WordPress, and broken workflows when external services change their APIs. Website owners and agencies are effectively paying rent on functionality that should be native to their CMS.

The average WordPress site spends $600+ annually on automation tools like Zapier - money that could be saved with native solutions.

How WPZap Solves It

WPZap is the first native automation engine built specifically for WordPress. Instead of routing data through external servers, WPZap lives entirely within your WordPress dashboard and connects plugins directly. This eliminates monthly fees, removes third-party dependencies, and keeps all data processing within your secure WordPress environment.

The platform works similarly to Zapier but with crucial differences: no task limits, no per-action fees, and no external data transfers. You create automations by selecting triggers (like WooCommerce orders or form submissions) and actions (like sending emails or updating databases) - all processed internally by WordPress.

How WPZap Works

Creating automations in WPZap follows a familiar trigger-action model. You select a trigger event from any WordPress plugin (like a new WooCommerce order) and connect it to one or more actions (like sending a confirmation email or adding the customer to a CRM).

The key difference is everything happens within WordPress - no external API calls, no third-party servers processing your data. This makes automations faster, more reliable, and completely private. Common use cases include:

  • Automatically tagging users based on form submissions
  • Syncing WooCommerce orders to Google Sheets
  • Sending SMS notifications for new content
  • Creating drip email sequences from form entries
