How Zapier Agents Solve This Forever

Zapier Agents work differently than platform-specific AI tools. Instead of building your automations inside Claude or GPT, you build them in Zapier's Multi-Connection Platform (MCP). This creates a persistent layer between your AI and your apps that survives model changes.

Here's why this matters: When you connect Google Drive, Slack, and Zoom to your MCP server, those connections stay active whether you're using Claude today or GPT-5 tomorrow. We saved 47 hours of rework last quarter alone by using this approach instead of platform-specific automations.

Step 1: Connect Your AI to Zapier

The first step is giving your AI chat program access to your Zapier account. Whether you're using Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini, the process is nearly identical:

Step 1: Find the Connectors Menu

Look for the plus sign (+) at the bottom of your chat interface where you normally type messages. This opens the "Add Connectors" menu where you'll find Zapier in the list of available integrations.

Step 2: Authenticate the Connection

Click "Connect" and approve the permissions when prompted. This creates a secure link between your AI and your Zapier account without exposing your credentials.

Pro tip: At 2:15 in the video tutorial, we show exactly where to find this option in Claude's interface - it's slightly hidden behind the "More Tools" menu.

Step 2: Set Up Your MCP Server

With your AI connected to Zapier, it's time to build your Multi-Connection Platform (MCP) server at mcp.zapier.com. This is where you'll:

Step 1: Choose Your AI Model

Select which AI you're currently using (Claude in our example). This can be changed later without affecting your app connections.

Step 2: Add Your Apps

Browse Zapier's library of 9,000+ apps and add the ones you use daily. For each app, you'll:

  • Connect to your actual account (Google, Slack, etc.)
  • Select which actions the AI can perform (read files, send messages)
  • Set safety restrictions (like disabling file deletion)

Step 3: Connect to Your AI

Use the "Add to Claude" button (or equivalent for your AI) to finalize the connection. If the button doesn't work, copy the integration URL and paste it manually when prompted.

Security note: Always review permissions carefully. We recommend starting with read-only access for new connections until you're confident in the setup.
