Why Automate Calendly to Sheets?

Zapier Basics: Triggers and Actions

Zapier automations (called "Zaps") consist of two main components: triggers and actions. A trigger is an event that starts your automation - in this case, a new Calendly booking. An action is what happens next - adding that booking to your Google Sheet.

At 4:30 in the tutorial, you'll see how Zapier's interface makes it simple to select these components. The AI Copilot feature (shown at 5:45) can even suggest the right trigger and action based on what you're trying to accomplish, which is especially helpful for beginners.

Step 1: Set Up Your Calendly Trigger

Start by creating a new Zap and selecting Calendly as your trigger app. You'll need to choose "Invitee Created" as your specific trigger event - this detects when someone books a meeting through your Calendly link.

At 7:10 in the video, notice how important it is to connect the right Calendly account. If you're on the free Calendly plan, you'll need to upgrade to at least the Standard plan ($12/month) for this integration to work properly.

Pro tip: When testing your trigger (shown at 8:30), schedule a real meeting with yourself rather than using sample data. This ensures all fields will map correctly when you connect Google Sheets.

Step 2: Connect Google Sheets

After setting up your trigger, add Google Sheets as your action app. Select "Create Spreadsheet Row" as your action - this will add each new booking as a row in your specified sheet.

At 9:45, you'll see how to connect your Google account and select the exact spreadsheet and worksheet where you want the data to appear. Make sure your sheet already has column headers (like "Name", "Email", etc.) so Zapier knows where to place each piece of information.

Step 3: Map Your Fields

Field mapping (shown at 10:30) is where you connect Calendly data to specific columns in your sheet. For each column header, select the corresponding Calendly field from the dropdown menu. Essential fields to map include:

  • Name → Invitee Name
  • Email → Invitee Email
  • Meeting Type → Event Type Name
  • Scheduled Time → Start Time

The video demonstrates how Zapier's AI can suggest field mappings at 11:20, saving you time guessing which fields correspond to which columns.

Testing Your Zap

Before turning your Zap on, always test it (shown at 12:00). Zapier will create a test row in your sheet using your most recent Calendly booking. Verify that:

  1. All mapped fields appear in the correct columns
  2. Dates and times are formatted properly
  3. No data is missing or misplaced

If something's wrong, you can edit your field mappings and test again until everything works perfectly.

Using Zapier's Copilot AI

At 13:40, the tutorial shows Zapier's new AI Copilot feature in action. When you're unsure which field to map or what action to choose, simply describe what you're trying to accomplish in plain English. The AI will:

  • Suggest the most relevant triggers and actions
  • Recommend field mappings based on your spreadsheet headers
  • Help troubleshoot when something isn't working

This makes Zapier much more accessible for non-technical users who might otherwise get stuck on technical details.
