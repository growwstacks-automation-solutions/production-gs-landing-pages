Zapier's Task Counting Trap

At first glance, Zapier's $20/month plan for 750 tasks seems reasonable entry point into automation. But businesses scaling workflows quickly discover task counting becomes predatory pricing model. Every discrete step in workflow - from data logging to notifications - consumes separate task.

Consider order processing workflow that:

  • Logs incoming data (1 task)
  • Checks inventory levels (2 tasks)
  • Sends customer notification (3 tasks)
  • Updates internal spreadsheet (4 tasks)

200 daily volume: processing 200 orders/day at 4 tasks each burns through 54,000 tasks/month - turning $20 plan into $17,000 annual cost center. That's equivalent hiring full-time employee just manage Zapier usage.

Make.com's Polling Drain

Make.com appears offer respite with $9-$10,000 operation plans. But their consumption model hides equally deceptive through what we've termed "polling drain." Unlike Zapier's explicit per-step counting, Make charges for operations include system simply asking "Is there anything new?"

A workflow checking email inbox every 10 minutes:

  • Makes 6 checks/hour
  • 144 checks/day
  • 4,320 checks/month

Empty queries cost real money: Before processing single actual message, routine polling can burn through 43% of Make's starter 10,000-operation plan. This hidden tax on idle workflows makes cost prediction nearly impossible.

The n8n Free Myth

n8n's open-source model presents as cost-free alternative - until you examine production requirements. While software license carries no fee, enterprise-grade implementation demands:

  • Dedicated server hosting ($40-$80/month)
  • PostgreSQL database management
  • SSL certificates and web application firewall
  • Encrypted regular backups

Labor dwarfs infrastructure: n8n's own documentation warns implementation without DevOps expertise ($80K-$150K/year) risks data loss security vulnerabilities. The true cost self-hosting often exceeds $200-$500/month minimum when accounting for labor.

The Support Hazard

August 30, 2024 marked turning point when Zapier eliminated refunds entirely - locking businesses selecting wrong plan face months locked into expensive contracts. Make.com's support often takes weeks respond, during which operations continue accruing charges.

Common scenarios:

  • Zapier workflows miscounting tasks due complex branching logic
  • Make.com polling loops consuming operations without completing workflows
  • n8n self-hosted instances crashing during critical periods

Vendor lock-in amplifies costs: Once core processes automated through platform, migrating becomes prohibitively expensive - leaving businesses captive to unpredictable billing models.

How To Evaluate Automation Tools

Rather than comparing advertised rates, businesses should:

  1. Model Worst-Case Usage

    Calculate costs at 3X your estimated volume to account for workflow inefficiencies and growth.

  2. Audit Hidden Consumption Patterns

    Identify hidden drains like Zapier's multi-step counting or Make's polling operations.

  3. Factor Labor Costs

    n8n's self-hosted option requires full-time equivalent DevOps - often exceeding $100K/year.

  4. Test Exit Strategies

    Ensure you can migrate workflows without complete rebuild when costs escalate.

Key takeaway: The cheapest upfront option often becomes most expensive long-term due to hidden consumption patterns and labor requirements.
