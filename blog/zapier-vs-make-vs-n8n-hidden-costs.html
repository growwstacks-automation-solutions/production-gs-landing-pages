Zapier's Deceptive Task Counting

Zapier markets itself as an affordable automation solution at $20/month for 750 tasks. But what they don't highlight is that every single step in your workflow counts as a separate task. A simple order processing workflow with five steps (check inventory, format data, send to fulfillment, update spreadsheet, notify team) consumes five tasks per execution.

For a business processing 200 orders daily, this translates to 1,000 tasks daily (5 steps × 200 orders) or 30,000 tasks monthly. At Zapier's Professional plan ($20 for 750 tasks), you'd need 40 task packs ($800/month) just for this one workflow. That's $9,600 annually for a single automation - far from the $240/year advertised price.

Real-world example: One eCommerce business reported their inventory automation alone consumed 54,000 tasks monthly, costing $17,000 annually - more than hiring a part-time employee to handle the process manually.

Make.com's Hidden Operation Costs

Make.com (formerly Integromat) appears cheaper at $9/month for 10,000 operations. But their operation counting includes every polling check - when the platform checks for new data in your connected apps. A workflow checking emails every 10 minutes consumes 144 operations daily (6 checks/hour × 24 hours) just from polling.

With 10 active workflows, you're looking at 1,440 operations daily (43,200 monthly) just from polling - before processing any actual data. That's over 80% of your monthly quota gone before any real work begins. Scaling becomes prohibitively expensive as each additional workflow adds hundreds or thousands of operations just from background checks.

Key insight: Make.com's pricing model disproportionately charges for infrastructure overhead (polling) rather than actual workflow execution, making costs unpredictable as you scale.

n8n's Self-Hosting Reality

n8n promotes its open-source model as a free alternative, but production deployment requires significant infrastructure. The minimum requirements (4GB RAM, 2 CPU cores) cost $40-$80 monthly from cloud providers. Add essential services like:

  • PostgreSQL database setup ($15-$50/month)
  • SSL certificates ($50-$200 annually)
  • Web application firewall ($30-$100/month)
  • Vulnerability scanning ($25-$75/month)
  • Encrypted backups ($20-$50/month)

The total cost typically ranges $200-$500 monthly - plus the salary of a DevOps engineer ($80,000-$150,000 annually) to manage everything. While technically "free" software, the total cost of ownership often exceeds commercial platforms, especially for small teams without existing infrastructure.

Support & Refund Issues

All three platforms present significant challenges when things go wrong. Zapier eliminated refunds entirely in August 2024, leaving users stuck with annual subscriptions up to $2,000 with no recourse. Make.com support takes weeks to respond, often providing vague errors that blame users for platform bugs.

n8n's documentation explicitly warns that self-hosting mistakes can cause data loss and security issues, recommending their cloud version for non-experts. This creates a paradox where the "free" option requires expensive expertise to implement safely, while the managed version carries similar costs to commercial platforms.

User report: "I spent three weeks trying to get a simple Make.com workflow to run reliably. Support kept saying it was my fault, until finally admitting it was a platform bug - after I'd already upgraded my plan trying to fix it."

When Each Platform Makes Sense

Despite the hidden costs, each platform has legitimate use cases:

  • Zapier: Best for non-technical users with simple, low-volume workflows (under 1,000 tasks/month)
  • Make.com: Ideal for complex workflows where you can minimize polling (e.g., triggered by webhooks rather than scheduled checks)
  • n8n: Fits technical teams with existing infrastructure who need complete control over their automation environment

The key is understanding your actual usage patterns before committing to annual plans. Test with real workflows at production scale for at least a month before making long-term decisions.

Alternative Approaches

For businesses frustrated with platform limitations and costs, several alternatives exist:

  1. Hybrid solutions: Combine platforms for specific tasks with custom API integrations for high-volume workflows
  2. Microsoft Power Automate: Included with many Office 365 plans, though with its own limitations
  3. Custom-built solutions: Using tools like Python scripts with scheduling, though requiring development resources
  4. Managed services: Like GrowwStacks, which provide custom automation solutions without platform lock-in

Case study: One client reduced their automation costs by 60% by moving high-volume workflows to custom solutions while keeping low-volume tasks on Zapier.
