The Problem

Finance teams often struggle with manual invoice creation, which is time-consuming and prone to errors. Manually transferring data from platforms like Monday.com to create invoices leads to inefficiencies and delays. This process can be especially challenging when dealing with high volumes of invoices and complex financial data.

The lack of automation results in increased operational costs, slower payment cycles, and potential compliance issues. Finance professionals spend valuable time on repetitive tasks instead of focusing on strategic financial planning and analysis. The need for a streamlined, automated solution is critical to improve productivity and accuracy.

The Solution

The solution is an automated invoice generation workflow built with n8n. This system fetches income data directly from Monday.com boards, generates customized HTML invoices, and creates Google Docs. It then converts these documents to PDF format, uploads them to Google Drive, and updates the corresponding records in Monday.com.

This tech stack was chosen for its flexibility and seamless integration capabilities. n8n's open-source nature allows for extensive customization, while its compatibility with Monday.com and Google services ensures a smooth, automated process. The workflow eliminates manual data entry, reduces errors, and accelerates the invoice delivery cycle.

How It Works — Automated Data Transfer and Invoice Creation

This workflow automates the entire invoice generation process, from data retrieval to final delivery.

  1. Fetch Data from Monday.com: The workflow starts by fetching relevant income data from specified boards in Monday.com using the Monday.com API.
  2. Transform Data: The fetched data is transformed into a format suitable for invoice generation, ensuring all necessary fields are correctly mapped.
  3. Generate HTML Invoice: Using the transformed data, the workflow generates a customized HTML invoice with all the required details, such as client information, line items, and amounts.
  4. Create Google Doc: The HTML invoice is then converted into a Google Doc, providing a shareable and editable version of the invoice.
  5. Convert to PDF: The Google Doc is converted into a PDF file, ensuring the invoice is easily accessible and printable.
  6. Upload to Google Drive: The PDF invoice is uploaded to a designated folder in Google Drive for secure storage and easy access.
  7. Update Monday.com Record: The Monday.com record is updated with a link to the generated invoice in Google Drive, providing a centralized location for all invoice-related information.
  8. Send Notification (Optional): Optionally, a notification can be sent to the finance team or the client, informing them that the invoice has been generated and is available for review.

💡 Centralized Invoice Management: By linking the generated invoice in Google Drive back to the Monday.com record, all invoice-related information is stored in one place, making it easy to track and manage invoices.

The Right Fit — and When It Isn't

This automated invoice generation system is ideal for finance teams that rely on Monday.com for project management and need to streamline their billing processes. It's particularly beneficial for businesses that handle a large volume of invoices and require accurate, timely financial reporting.

However, it may not be the right fit for very small businesses with minimal invoicing needs or those that do not use Monday.com or Google services. In such cases, a simpler invoicing solution may be more appropriate.
