The Problem

Logistics teams and customer service representatives were spending excessive time manually tracking shipments within Monday.com. This involved constantly checking for updates, cross-referencing data, and manually updating the board. The lack of real-time information led to delays in customer communication and increased the risk of errors.

The manual process was not only time-consuming but also prone to inaccuracies. Duplicate entries and inconsistent data made it difficult to maintain an accurate overview of shipment statuses. This resulted in frustrated customers and a significant administrative burden on the team.

The Solution

An automated workflow was developed using N8N to track shipment data from Monday.com, remove duplicate entries, and automate customer notifications. This system monitors Monday.com boards for shipment updates, processes the data, and triggers notifications to customers via Slack or email.

N8N was chosen for its flexibility and ability to integrate seamlessly with Monday.com and other communication platforms. The workflow is designed to handle large volumes of data, ensuring that all shipment updates are tracked and communicated in a timely manner. This eliminates the need for manual tracking and reduces the risk of errors.

How It Works — Streamlining Shipment Updates and Notifications

The automated workflow monitors a Monday.com board for new or updated shipment entries. It then processes the data to remove duplicates and ensure accuracy before triggering customer notifications.

  1. Monitor Monday.com: The workflow continuously monitors the specified Monday.com board for any new or updated shipment entries.
  2. Extract Data: Relevant shipment data, such as tracking number, status, and delivery date, is extracted from the Monday.com board.
  3. Remove Duplicates: The workflow identifies and removes any duplicate entries to ensure data accuracy.
  4. Validate Data: The extracted data is validated to ensure it meets the required format and criteria.
  5. Update Google Sheets: The validated shipment data is updated in a Google Sheets spreadsheet for record-keeping and analysis.
  6. Check Status: The workflow checks the current status of the shipment to determine if a notification is required.
  7. Send Notification: If the shipment status has changed, a notification is sent to the customer via Slack or email.
  8. Log Activity: All activities, including data extraction, validation, and notifications, are logged for auditing and reporting purposes.

💡 Real-Time Updates: Customers receive immediate notifications about shipment status changes, enhancing transparency and trust.
