The Situation

A Growing Brand Drowning in Its Own Operations

A home goods brand selling primarily on Shopify with a secondary Amazon Seller Central presence was generating $6M annually across both channels. At that revenue level, they had the kind of problem most eCommerce founders want: too many orders to keep up with manually. But the symptoms were getting worse.

Their 12-person team — 4 in operations, 5 in customer service, 3 in marketing — was spending an increasing share of each day on tasks that created zero customer value. Order exceptions required manual intervention. Inventory counts drifted between channels, causing oversells. The support inbox was perpetually behind, with the same questions arriving hundreds of times a week. Post-purchase email consisted of a single confirmation email and nothing else.

⚠️ Problems We Were Hired to Fix
Order processing required 2–3 manual touches per exception order — averaging 22 exceptions daily across Shopify and Amazon
Inventory reconciled manually twice a day — still generating 8–12 oversell incidents per week with $4,200/month in forced refunds
Customer service team handling 340+ tickets/week, 70% of which were "where's my order?", return requests, or policy questions
Zero post-purchase automation — no review requests, no cross-sell sequences, no win-back flows for 14,000+ lapsed buyers in their database
Ad reporting compiled manually every Monday morning — 5 hours per week pulling Meta, Google, and TikTok data into a spreadsheet

The CEO's summary during our audit: "We're scaling revenue but not scaling profitably. Every $1M we add feels like it costs $300K more in labor."

The Solution

Four Automation Systems Built in Three Weeks

We scoped a four-phase build prioritized by immediate ROI impact. Phase 1 went live in Week 1 before the full project was complete.

Phase 1 — Week 1
Automated Order Processing & Routing
New Shopify orders flow into a Make.com scenario that evaluates SKU, shipping zone, carrier preference rules, and inventory location — then routes automatically to the correct fulfillment center via ShipStation. Tracking numbers are synced back to Shopify and trigger a branded shipping confirmation email within 90 seconds of fulfillment. Exception orders (address issues, inventory conflicts, oversized items) are flagged in a dedicated Slack channel with one-click resolution options — eliminating the manual inbox triage that consumed 2 hours daily. Amazon orders go through a parallel flow using the SP-API.
⚡ 14 hours/week recovered from ops team in Week 2
Phase 2 — Week 1–2
Real-Time Multi-Channel Inventory Sync
We established Airtable as the central inventory source of truth and built bidirectional sync to Shopify, Amazon Seller Central, and their 3PL warehouse system. Every sale on any channel decrements inventory across all others within 60 seconds. Low-stock thresholds trigger Slack alerts and generate draft purchase orders to their top-5 suppliers in Google Docs — pre-populated with SKU, quantity-to-reorder (based on 30-day velocity), and supplier contact details. The brand had 14 SKUs that were consistently stockedout on Amazon while inventory sat in their Shopify warehouse — the sync eliminated this immediately. Oversell incidents dropped from 8–12 per week to zero in Week 3.
⚡ Oversell incidents: 8–12/week → 0 | $4,200/month in refunds eliminated
Phase 3 — Week 2
AI Customer Support Agent (Gorgias + GPT-4o)
We trained an AI support agent on their product catalog (147 SKUs), return policy, shipping zones and timelines, carrier SLAs, warranty terms, and 6 months of historical support tickets. The agent was integrated into Gorgias as their primary first-responder via Shopify chat widget and email. When a ticket arrives, the agent retrieves live order status from ShipStation (via Shopify API), checks return eligibility against policy rules, and either fully resolves the ticket or prepares a draft response for the human agent with all context pre-loaded. For return requests that meet eligibility criteria, the agent generates a return shipping label and sends it automatically. The human CS team shifted from first-response work to escalation handling and relationship management — a fundamentally better use of their time.
⚡ 67% of tickets resolved without human touch | CS team capacity freed by 60%
Phase 4 — Week 3
Post-Purchase Revenue Flows (Klaviyo)
We built four Klaviyo-connected flows triggered by Shopify purchase events: (1) A review request sequence sent at Day 5 post-delivery, personalized by product category and including product images. (2) A cross-sell flow sent at Day 7 and Day 14, recommending products complementary to the initial purchase using dynamic product blocks. (3) A 30/60/90-day win-back sequence for buyers who hadn't repurchased — segmented by original purchase value with appropriate discount escalation. (4) A post-purchase NPS survey at Day 10, with detractors routed to the CS team automatically and promoters invited to join their loyalty program. For the 14,000+ lapsed buyers in their database, the win-back launch alone generated $22K in revenue in the first 30 days.
⚡ $38K additional annual revenue | 11.2% win-back rate on lapsed buyers

FAQ

Common Questions About eCommerce Automation

How much does eCommerce automation cost for a Shopify store? ▾
For a $3M–$10M Shopify brand, a full operations automation stack — order routing, inventory sync, support AI, and post-purchase flows — typically costs $12,000–$22,000. Single workflow builds (order automation only) start at $4,000–$8,000. This project was $16,000 delivered in 3 weeks. Payback periods typically run 45–90 days based on headcount savings and recovered revenue from post-purchase flows.
How does multi-channel inventory sync work across Shopify and Amazon? ▾
A central inventory source of truth (Airtable, Google Sheets, or your ERP) is connected to all sales channels via Make.com. When a sale occurs on any channel, Make.com fires a webhook, decrements inventory in the central source, and pushes updated quantities to all other platform APIs within 60–90 seconds. Low-stock thresholds trigger Slack alerts and can automatically generate supplier reorder POs at configurable reorder points.
What percentage of customer support tickets can be automated? ▾
Typically 60–70% of eCommerce support tickets can be fully resolved by an AI agent — primarily order status inquiries, return/refund requests, shipping policy questions, and product FAQs. In this build, we achieved 67%. Complex issues (disputes, damaged goods, wrong items received) are routed to human agents with full conversation context already loaded. The result: CS team handles the same volume with 60% less active effort, and response times drop from hours to seconds.
How do post-purchase email flows increase repeat purchase rates? ▾
Three flows drive most of the revenue: Review requests (Day 5 post-delivery) generate 8–15% review rates vs 1–2% for manual outreach — building social proof that improves conversion on product pages. Cross-sell flows (Day 7 and Day 14) with dynamic product recommendations drive 18–24% AOV lift on buyers who engage. Win-back sequences (30/60/90-day for lapsed buyers) with personalized product recommendations and escalating discount offers typically achieve 8–12% reactivation rates. For this brand's 14,000 lapsed buyers, the win-back launch alone generated $22K in 30 days.
Can automation handle Amazon Seller Central integration? ▾
Yes. Amazon's Selling Partner API (SP-API) supports order retrieval, inventory updates, FBA inventory tracking, and fulfillment routing. For Seller-Fulfilled orders, Make.com pulls new Amazon orders and routes them through the same fulfillment logic as Shopify — unified tracking for your ops team. For FBA, SP-API provides real-time inventory levels at Amazon fulfillment centers, which sync back to your central inventory source so Shopify doesn't oversell products that are actually FBA-allocated.
Will automation break if Shopify or Amazon updates their APIs? ▾
Make.com's native Shopify integration is maintained by Make.com — major Shopify API version updates are handled at the connector level automatically. For SP-API, we monitor Amazon's deprecation timeline (typically 12–18 months advance notice) and update affected scenarios before breaking changes take effect. Every automation includes error notifications — if any step fails, you receive an immediate Slack or email alert. All projects include 30 days post-launch support, and our retainer clients are covered for all API maintenance.
