The Problem: A Referral Nobody Sees

Picture a family member just discharged from hospital who needs someone checking in daily. You call a home care agency, explain the situation, fill out the form. They say someone will follow up soon. A week passes. No call. No update. You have no idea whether anyone ever saw the request.

That is the gap this system was built to close.

The agency's entire process ran across half a dozen platforms that did not talk to each other — lead sourcing, cold outreach, a CRM, a task manager, a scheduling tool, and their website. Rather than patching one tool at a time, GrowwStacks built the connective tissue between all of them, so the moment a referral arrives it moves itself through the entire pipeline instead of waiting for someone to notice it.

Lead Sourcing and Enrichment

Leads are pulled in two ways: by region, and by company domain. When the agency supplies a list of companies, domain enrichment identifies the actual decision makers behind those domains and drops them straight into a live Google Sheet.

From there, leads are split by seniority. Senior-level contacts are filtered out and routed into dedicated leadership outreach campaigns rather than being treated like every other contact.

The daily lead sourcing and deduplication workflow — pulling enriched contacts on a schedule, matching them against existing records, and routing senior-level contacts into a separate sheet.

Cold Outreach at Volume

Four to five campaigns run live at once, sending roughly 100 to 150 emails a day across the board. Anyone marked out of office is smart paused automatically, so the system is not emailing someone who is away for two weeks straight.

Every reply is tagged by sentiment automatically — interested, not interested, out of office — sorted without anyone reading each one by hand. Only replies tagged interested move forward, and before anything is created downstream the system checks that the contact is not a duplicate.

From Reply to CRM Opportunity

The second someone replies interested, a new opportunity is created automatically in the CRM and dropped into New Leads, ready for the team to review and progress through communication, negotiation, and proposal.

The outreach pipeline in the CRM — opportunities created automatically from interested replies, moving through New Lead, In Communication, Referral Submitted, and Stalled stages.

The Core: Three Actions on Every Referral

This is the heart of the system. When a case manager submits a referral form, three things fire at once:

  1. Their outreach opportunity flips to Referral Submitted.
  2. A new referral pipeline opportunity is created, sitting in Under Review with every detail from the form already populated on the card.
  3. The same person is logged as an active partner in a separate pipeline.

Because matching the right caregiver to the right family genuinely matters, referrals are fully filterable by service, detail, city, race, ethnicity, and gender. The team is not scrolling through fifty cards to find the one that fits.

💡
One Form, Three Records — A single referral submission simultaneously advances the outreach opportunity, creates the referral record with all form data pre-populated, and registers the referring case manager as an active partner. No duplicate data entry, no manual linking.

Automated Onboarding

Once a service agreement comes in, the system auto-generates every onboarding task into the task manager, assigned to the right person in the right order. When someone finishes their task, the next assignee is notified automatically.

Once every subtask is marked complete and the agreement is confirmed received, the opportunity jumps from Service Agreement Submitted to Ready to Activate on its own. No manual dragging of cards required.

The Same Logic for Caregiver Recruiting

The identical pattern runs through the staff recruiting pipeline. A job application auto-creates a New Applicant opportunity with hours, shift preference, availability, and location already on the card.

Move that candidate to Send Interview Booking Link and the scheduling link goes out automatically. The moment they book, they are marked Interview Scheduled.

The recruiting workflow — an inbound application fires a webhook, checks for an existing contact, and creates or updates the candidate opportunity in the recruiting pipeline.
The staff recruiting pipeline — candidates flow automatically from New Applicant through Send Interview Booking Link, Interview Scheduled, Interview Completed, and Talent Pool.

What used to be five tools and five tabs is now one view.

The Safety Net

Running underneath every pipeline are automatic reminders. If a stage stalls for more than a day or two, the system flags it. Nothing sits untouched long enough to be forgotten.

Why This Matters

This is not really about hours saved, though it saved plenty. It is about a referral never sitting in an inbox long enough for a family member to start wondering whether anybody is even looking at it.

The Right Fit — and When It Isn't

This system suits service businesses running parallel pipelines — client acquisition and staff recruiting at the same time — across multiple disconnected tools, where a delayed response has real human cost. Home care, staffing agencies, and healthcare services fit naturally.

It is less suited to businesses running a single simple pipeline in one tool, where the integration overhead would outweigh the benefit, or to teams whose intake volume is low enough that manual triage remains reliable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our automated home care operations and outreach workflows.

Automated Deduplication. The n8n workflow queries the CRM and existing sheets using the domain and email address to check for pre-existing records before adding a new contact, preventing duplicate outreach.

Smart Pause. The sentiment tagging engine identifies out-of-office responses and automatically pauses the campaign for that specific contact. It schedules a resume action once the contact's return date has passed.

Stall Reminders. If a referral remains in the 'Under Review' stage of the pipeline for more than 48 hours, the safety net script triggers, sending automated alerts to the team via task managers and email.

Multi-Attribute Filtering. Yes. Referrals stored in GoHighLevel can be filtered dynamically by required service type, city/region, caregiver gender preference, and background compatibility matching.

Dynamic Task Handovers. When a team member marks an onboarding subtask complete in ClickUp, a webhook automatically notifies the next person in sequence and updates the CRM opportunity stage.

Enterprise Security & Compliance. Patient referral details are transmitted over encrypted webhook channels, processed securely through self-hosted integrations, and stored in a HIPAA-compliant CRM environment with strict access controls.
