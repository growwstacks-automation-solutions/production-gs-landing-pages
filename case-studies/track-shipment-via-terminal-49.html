The Hidden Cost of Manual Shipment Tracking: 15 Hours a Week Spent Copying Data and Writing Emails

Freight forwarding companies operate at the intersection of multiple carriers, ports, terminals, and customers — and the operational coordination required to keep every stakeholder informed about every shipment is staggering in its repetitiveness. A freight forwarding company came to GrowwStacks with a problem that is endemic to the logistics industry: their entire shipment tracking and customer communication process was entirely manual. Every week, their operations team was logging into multiple tracking platforms, pulling container and vessel statuses, copying that data by hand into their internal Monday.com board, and then personally writing and sending email updates to customers whenever a shipment status changed.

The scale of this manual process was significant. Vessel departed — manual email. Arrived at port — manual email. Out for delivery — manual email. Each status change for each shipment required someone to notice the change, look up the correct customer, draft the appropriate notification, and send it. This was consuming 10 to 15 hours every single week — hours spent entirely on tracking and communication rather than on actual client service, relationship building, or business development. And beyond the time cost, the more insidious problem was the errors: wrong statuses copied from one platform to another, delayed updates that left customers waiting for information that should have been instant, and the inevitable human mistakes that occur when the same repetitive process is performed hundreds of times per week.

Building the Fully Automated Tracking Pipeline: From Shipment Registration to Customer Notification in Seconds

GrowwStacks engineered a complete end-to-end shipment tracking and notification system built on n8n that eliminates every manual step in the tracking workflow. The system connects four platforms into a single automated pipeline: Monday.com serves as the operations board where all shipment records, booking numbers, container numbers, and statuses are maintained. Terminal 49 provides the real-time shipment tracking data — container status, vessel movements, terminal details, and transport events pulled directly via API. n8n orchestrates the entire workflow, handling the trigger logic, data validation, conditional routing, and platform synchronisation. And Missive handles the customer-facing communication, sending formatted status notification emails automatically whenever a shipment status changes.

The system is designed to be intelligent rather than simply automated. Instead of blindly processing every event, the n8n workflow includes decision logic that checks whether a shipment is already registered with Terminal 49, whether it already exists on the tracking board, and what specific action needs to be taken based on the current state. This prevents duplicate records, avoids overwriting existing data, and ensures the automation takes the correct path every time — handling the edge cases that would trip up a simpler system. What used to take 15 minutes per shipment now runs end to end in seconds.

From Shipment Registration to Customer Email: The Complete Five-Step Automation

The system executes across five automated steps that handle the entire shipment tracking lifecycle — from initial registration through real-time monitoring to customer notification — without any manual involvement except reviewing the occasional exception. Here is the complete flow:

  1. Trigger and shipment identification: The workflow begins the moment a new shipment tracking request is created. The n8n automation fires immediately, pulling up the shipment record from the Monday.com operations board. It retrieves the key shipment identifiers — booking number, container number, and bill of lading — and validates that all required data is present before proceeding. This validation step is the foundation of the system's reliability: if a record is missing critical identifiers, it flags the exception rather than processing incomplete data through the pipeline.
  2. Intelligent decision routing: Before taking any action, the workflow hits a series of decision points. Is this shipment already registered with Terminal 49? Does it already exist on the tracking board? Has this specific status already been processed? These checks make the automation intelligent — instead of creating duplicate entries or overwriting existing data, it determines exactly what needs to happen and takes the correct path every time. For new shipments, it registers them with Terminal 49 for tracking. For existing shipments with status changes, it proceeds directly to the data fetch. For shipments with no new updates, it skips processing entirely.
  3. Real-time data retrieval from Terminal 49: Once the workflow knows which path to take, it connects to Terminal 49's API and fetches everything about the shipment in real time — container status, vessel movements, terminal details, port arrivals, transport events, estimated arrival times, and any delays or holds. This is the step that previously required someone to manually log into the Terminal 49 platform, search for the container number, read the tracking page, and copy the relevant data across to the internal board. The API call retrieves all of this data in a single request, structured and ready for automatic processing.
  4. Monday.com board synchronisation: The tracking data retrieved from Terminal 49 is automatically mapped to the corresponding fields on the Monday.com operations board — shipment status, current location, vessel name, estimated arrival, and any event notes. The board update happens in real time, meaning the operations team always sees the current state of every shipment without ever opening Terminal 49 directly. For the operations manager reviewing the board each morning, every row reflects the latest data pulled within the last automation cycle — not the data someone remembered to update yesterday.
  5. Automated customer notification via Missive: Once the tracking data is confirmed and the board is updated, the workflow automatically drafts and sends a customer notification through Missive. The correct email template is selected based on the shipment event type — departure, port arrival, customs clearance, out for delivery — and the shipment details are populated automatically. No one writes a single word. The customer receives a professionally formatted, accurate status update within seconds of the status change being detected, rather than hours or days later when someone on the team remembers to send the update manually.

Implementation: Live in 3 Weeks

  1. Monday.com board audit and schema design: The existing Monday.com board structure is reviewed and optimised for automation compatibility. Fields for booking number, container number, bill of lading, shipment status, vessel name, ETA, and customer contact are standardised and validated. Any existing data inconsistencies — missing container numbers, duplicate records, malformed booking references — are cleaned up before the automation connects. The board becomes the single source of truth for the operations team, with every field structured for both human readability and API-driven updates.
  2. Terminal 49 API integration and tracking registration: The n8n workflow is connected to Terminal 49's API with authentication configured for the company's account. The shipment registration logic is built — automatically registering new shipments for tracking based on container numbers from the Monday.com board. The data mapping is configured to extract and structure the specific fields the operations team needs: container status, vessel movements, terminal details, port events, and transport milestones. The API response handling includes error management for invalid container numbers, unregistered shipments, and API rate limits.
  3. Workflow logic, routing, and synchronisation: The complete n8n workflow is built connecting the trigger, validation, Terminal 49 fetch, Monday.com update, and Missive notification steps. The intelligent routing logic — checking for existing registrations, duplicate prevention, and status change detection — is configured and tested against real shipment data. Edge cases are handled: what happens when a container number returns no data, when a shipment has multiple containers, when Terminal 49 reports an unexpected event type, or when the Monday.com board has a record that doesn't match any Terminal 49 tracking. Error handling and exception flagging ensure the team is alerted to anything the automation cannot resolve on its own.
  4. Missive notification templates and go-live: Customer notification email templates are designed in Missive for each shipment event type — departure, in transit, port arrival, customs clearance, out for delivery, and delivered. Each template is configured with dynamic fields that the n8n workflow populates automatically: customer name, shipment reference, container number, current status, location, and estimated arrival. The templates are reviewed with the operations team for tone and accuracy before activation. The full workflow is run against a batch of real shipments in parallel with the manual process to validate accuracy, and once confirmed, the manual process is retired and the automation takes over completely.

The Right Fit — and When It Isn't

This solution delivers maximum value for freight forwarding companies, customs brokers, third-party logistics providers (3PLs), import/export businesses, shipping agencies, and any logistics operation that manages multiple shipments across carriers and needs to keep customers informed of status changes. The primary use case is any business where the operations team currently spends hours each week manually checking tracking platforms, copying data into internal systems, and sending individual status update emails to customers. If your team recognises the pattern — log in, search, copy, email, repeat — this automation addresses exactly that workflow.

Two practical considerations: the system's real-time tracking capability depends on Terminal 49's carrier coverage. Terminal 49 supports the vast majority of ocean carriers and provides container-level tracking for most international shipping routes, but if your shipment volume is primarily domestic trucking or last-mile delivery without container-level identifiers, the tracking source may need to be adapted to a different API. Additionally, the system is designed for operations teams that already use or are willing to adopt Monday.com as their shipment management board — the Monday.com integration is central to the workflow rather than an optional component. For teams using alternative project management platforms, the board integration can be reconfigured for tools like Airtable, Notion, or Smartsheet during implementation.
