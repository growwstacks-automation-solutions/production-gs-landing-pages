The honest hook

Why most people struggle with this certification

When we sat the Claude Certified Architect exam ourselves, one thing became clear: this isn't something you can pass by reading. It's an architecture certification — and architecture isn't a theory subject. You understand it only once you've built things, broken them, and fixed them with your own hands.

Talking to people preparing for it, we kept seeing the same pattern: smart, capable professionals struggling — not because the material is beyond them, but because the approach is wrong. Watching tutorials, memorising concepts, never actually building. This program is built to fix exactly that.

Architecture cannot be learned from theory alone. You'll be learning by building — writing small tools yourself, running them, breaking them, and fixing them. Watching the live sessions isn't enough; the learning happens in your own hands during the lab. Come in with focus and consistency: two hours a day, six days a week, for eight weeks. Eight weeks, sequenced

The 8-week curriculum

Sequenced by exam weight and hands-on dependency — not the usual "theory first." Every week's assignment is a stripped-down version of a real exam scenario.

Week Focus Exam domain
Week 0 — Pre-start Setup & Onboarding.Install Claude Code, get comfortable in the terminal, run your first script, understand "what is an API call." Environment check so Week 1 runs clean. Foundations
Week 1 Ground floor & first wins.How Claude works, the request/response model, your first real interactions. Everybody ships something small. Foundations
Week 2 Prompt Engineering & Structured Output.Few-shot, prompt chaining, JSON schemas, tool use for structured data — and where schemas help vs. where they don't. ~20%
Weeks 3–4 Agentic Architecture & Orchestration.The agentic loop, stop reasons, agents vs. workflows, orchestrator–subagent design, escalation & human-in-the-loop. The largest, highest-weighted domain — two full weeks of reps. ~27% (largest)
Week 5 Claude Code Configuration & Workflows.CLAUDE.md hierarchy, custom commands, skills, subagents, hooks, the SDK, and CI/CD non-interactive mode. ~20%
Week 6 Tool Design & MCP Integration.Building and consuming MCP servers, effective tool design vs. the anti-patterns the exam tests. Core
Week 7 Context Management & Reliability + Integration.Context windows, summarization, scratchpad, RAG — then tying all five domains together. Core
Week 8 Exam Preparation.Timed mock exams, scenario drills, and weak-area remediation before you sit the real thing. All five

The real exam is six recurring scenarios. Across the program you build a small version of every one — so by Week 8 you've practised the exact situations the exam puts in front of you.
