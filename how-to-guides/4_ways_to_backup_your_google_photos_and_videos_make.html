4 Ways to Backup Your Google Photos and Videos

The are many reasons to back up Google photos and videos, but most of us just want one thing: a safe place for our snapshots.

1. Activate the "back up and sync" feature on your Google Account

Google Photos offers a built-in solution that's perhaps the easiest way to protect your memories. The "back up & sync" feature automatically uploads your photos to Google's cloud servers whenever you're connected to WiFi.

This native solution works identically across both Android and iOS devices. According to Samsung's research, 10% of smartphone users will lose or have their device stolen each year, making automatic backups crucial for preserving your photos.

  • Open the Google Photos app on your device
  • Sign in to your Google Account if you haven't already
  • Tap the menu icon (three lines) in the top left corner
  • Select "Settings" then choose "Back up & sync"
  • Toggle the feature on

The standard Google account includes 15GB of free storage shared across Gmail, Drive, and Photos. At standard quality (3MP photos around 0.5MB each), this holds approximately 30,000 photos.

2. Automatically back up Google Photos to Dropbox

For those who prefer diversifying their backup locations, automating transfers to Dropbox provides an excellent secondary option. This method solves the time-consuming manual process while ensuring your photos exist in multiple secure locations.

The automation requires three accounts: Google, Dropbox, and Make (formerly Integromat). The process uses a pre-built template that handles all the technical details, making setup straightforward even for non-technical users.

Pro tip: Make offers a free tier perfect for testing this integration before committing to a paid plan.

Setup Steps

Begin by logging into your Make account and selecting the "Backup new Google Photos to Dropbox" template. The template guides you through connecting your accounts and configuring the backup parameters.

You'll need to authorize Make to access both your Google Photos and Dropbox accounts. These connections use secure OAuth protocols, meaning you're not sharing passwords directly with the automation platform.

  • Choose which Google Photos folders to back up (or select all)
  • Set a date range for existing photos to include
  • Select your target Dropbox folder

3. Automatically back up Google Photos to Box

Box presents another excellent cloud storage alternative, particularly appealing for users needing more free space than Dropbox provides. While Dropbox offers 2GB free, Box provides 10GB in its basic plan.

The Box integration follows a similar pattern to the Dropbox solution, using Make's automation platform to handle the transfers. The key difference lies in Box's stronger focus on business use cases and compliance features.

Box's interface organizes content differently than Dropbox, with features like watermarking and advanced permission controls that may appeal to professional users. The automation setup mirrors the Dropbox process but uses Box-specific modules.

4. Automatically back up Google Photos to Wasabi

Wasabi offers a unique proposition in cloud storage - low-cost, high-performance storage without the file-sharing capabilities of Dropbox or Box. This makes it ideal for users prioritizing privacy and cost-efficiency over collaboration features.

Wasabi's pricing model differs significantly from competitors, charging only for storage used without additional fees for API requests or data transfers. For large photo libraries, this can result in substantial savings compared to other services.

The Wasabi backup template in Make follows the same basic pattern as the other cloud services but connects to Wasabi's S3-compatible API instead. Setup requires your Wasabi access keys and bucket information.

Bonus: Back up Instagram photos to Google Photos

Instagram's unpredictable content moderation makes backing up your posts essential. The platform frequently removes content for various policy violations, sometimes without warning or clear explanation.

Make's Instagram-to-Google-Photos template solves this by automatically saving new posts to your Google account. The integration captures both individual posts and entire albums, preserving your Instagram content regardless of platform changes.

Setup requires connecting your Instagram and Google accounts through Make. Once configured, the automation runs silently in the background, ensuring you always have copies of your Instagram memories.
