4 Ways to Backup Your Google Photos and Videos

Protect your precious memories with these reliable backup methods, from native Google features to automated cloud solutions.

1. Activate the "Back Up & Sync" Feature

Google's built-in backup solution offers the simplest way to protect your photos. When enabled, this feature automatically uploads your photos and videos to Google's servers whenever your device connects to Wi-Fi.

The process works identically across Android and iOS devices. Open the Google Photos app, sign in to your account, and navigate to Settings > Back up & sync. Toggle the feature on, and your photos will begin uploading to the cloud.

  • 15GB free storage included with every Google account
  • Stores approximately 30,000 standard quality photos
  • Automatic organization by people, places, and things

Pro tip: Regularly check your storage usage in Google One. If you approach the 15GB limit, consider upgrading your storage plan or removing unnecessary files.

2. Automatically Back Up to Dropbox

For those who prefer not to keep all their photos in one service, automating backups to Dropbox provides an excellent alternative. This method requires three accounts: Google, Dropbox, and Make.com (formerly Integromat).

The automation works by creating a workflow in Make.com that monitors your Google Photos for new uploads and copies them to a designated Dropbox folder. You can configure it to back up your entire library or specific albums.

The Make.com template for Google Photos to Dropbox automation

Setup Process

First, locate the "Backup new Google Photos to Dropbox" template in Make.com. After selecting it, you'll need to authorize access to both your Google and Dropbox accounts. The setup wizard guides you through selecting source folders in Google Photos and destination locations in Dropbox.

Advanced options let you filter by date ranges or media types. Once configured, the automation runs in the background, requiring no manual intervention beyond occasional monitoring.

3. Automatically Back Up to Box

Box presents an attractive alternative to Dropbox for photo backups, particularly because it offers 10GB of free storage compared to Dropbox's 2GB. The setup process mirrors the Dropbox method but uses Box's API instead.

The Box integration shines for users who need to store larger photo collections without upgrading to paid plans. The automation template includes options to organize photos by date, album, or other metadata in your Box account.

Pro tip: Box's enterprise features make this solution ideal for professionals who need to maintain photo archives for work purposes while keeping personal and professional media separate.

4. Automatically Back Up to Wasabi

Wasabi offers a different approach to cloud storage, prioritizing affordability and privacy over social features. Unlike Dropbox and Box, Wasabi doesn't focus on file sharing, making it better suited for personal archives.

The Wasabi backup template in Make.com provides robust options for users dealing with sensitive material or those who prefer a more private storage solution. Wasabi's pricing model often proves more cost-effective for large photo libraries.

Bonus: Back Up Instagram Photos

Instagram's content moderation policies sometimes result in deleted posts. An automated backup to Google Photos ensures you never lose important Instagram memories.

The Make.com template for this integration can save both individual posts and entire albums. It triggers whenever you post new content, creating a parallel archive in your Google Photos account.
