Why Typeform? Features, pricing, integrations

Online forms have been instrumental in helping businesses organize data since the early days of the internet. Like email, they're often underestimated despite their powerful capabilities for ecommerce operations including lead generation, client support, and customer onboarding.

Typeform stands out among form builders for three key reasons: it's a market leader with a novel approach to form design, offers robust customization options, and integrates easily with other apps through Make. The platform's conversational interface makes form completion feel more engaging than traditional form builders.

Typeform's clean, conversational interface makes forms more engaging

Among Typeform's most valuable features are Logic Jumps for conditional questions, extensive customization options, and native Stripe payment integration. Pricing starts at $35/month for basic plans up to $70/month for premium features - competitive with other enterprise form builders.

Pro tip: While Typeform doesn't offer as many native integrations as some competitors, its API and Make integration make it easy to connect with hundreds of other business apps.

7 ways to use Typeform for ecommerce optimization

Now let's explore seven practical ways Typeform can help optimize your ecommerce operations and customer experience.

1. Get creative with email marketing campaigns

Typeform transforms traditional email marketing by adding interactive, transactional elements. Unlike one-way promotional emails, Typeform-enabled campaigns create two-way conversations where customers can provide feedback or preferences in exchange for incentives.

Example of a transactional email exchange using Typeform

For example, a clothing brand could offer discounts in exchange for style preferences. This approach combines three powerful elements: interactive exchange ("Tell us your thoughts"), transactional value ("Get 50% off"), and smooth user experience through Typeform's intuitive interface.

2. Make it easy for customers to provide feedback

Effective feedback collection is crucial for ecommerce improvement but often drains resources. Typeform helps streamline this through both passive (website-embedded) and active (triggered) feedback forms.

Pro tip: Automate feedback requests by triggering Typeform emails after purchases. This ensures every customer gets the opportunity to share their experience.

Active feedback loops through Typeform can be triggered by specific actions like purchases or account creation. The forms can be embedded in thank-you pages, confirmation emails, or as popups - all while maintaining a consistent, professional look that matches your brand.

Customer feedback is invaluable for ecommerce improvement

3. Build buyer personas in record time

Accurate buyer personas require real customer data, not guesswork. Typeform's survey templates help gather this information efficiently through website embeds or email campaigns.

Offer incentives to encourage participation, but be mindful of asking overly personal questions that might deter responses. The transactional approach ("Complete this short survey and get 10% off") significantly boosts completion rates.

Developing accurate buyer personas with Typeform surveys

4. Bridge the gap between digital and physical sales

Typeform order forms provide a simple solution for brick-and-mortar stores to begin selling online. The forms can be integrated with payment processors and connected to fulfillment systems.

Key elements include clear calls-to-action, minimal required fields, and seamless payment integration. Orders can be automatically funneled to spreadsheets or inventory systems to streamline fulfillment.

Typeform helps bridge online and offline sales channels

5. Let users customize their orders

Modern consumers expect personalized shopping experiences. Typeform order forms enable extensive customization options including:

  • Quantity selection
  • Color/size preferences
  • Packaging options
  • Shipping methods
  • Warranty selections

The conditional logic (Logic Jumps) ensures customers only see relevant options based on their previous answers, creating a streamlined experience.

Typeform enables detailed product customization

6. Funnel data to where it belongs

Typeform responses can automatically feed into your preferred systems - whether that's Google Sheets, Airtable, your CRM, or task management tools like Trello.

Automated notifications ensure the right team members are alerted to new submissions. For order forms, this might trigger:

  • Inventory updates
  • Accounting entries
  • Warehouse picking
  • Shipping scheduling
Automating data organization prevents confusion

7. Integrate payments into your Typeform forms

Typeform's verified Stripe integration allows you to embed payment processing directly into order forms. This creates a seamless checkout experience without redirecting customers to external payment pages.

The payment block appears as part of your form flow, typically at the end after product selection and customization options. Transactions are processed through Stripe with standard 2.9% + $0.30 fees per transaction.
