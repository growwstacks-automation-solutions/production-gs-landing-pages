Automating customer feedback with AI: Step by step

Here's how the AI survey submission manager processes customer feedback inside Make:

1. Watching for new survey submissions

The workflow triggers immediately when a customer submits feedback through your connected form tool. This instant activation ensures no feedback slips through the cracks.

The workflow activates as soon as a customer submits feedback

2. Analyzing sentiment and language

Make's AI module instantly evaluates whether the feedback is positive, neutral, or negative. Simultaneously, it identifies the response language and can translate it into any target language you specify.

3. Deciding on the next step

Based on sentiment and satisfaction score, the system routes each response appropriately. Positive feedback (scores ≥3) gets logged directly to your database. Negative feedback (scores <3) triggers an alert to your support team before logging.

Pro tip: The system can detect mismatches between ratings and comments - like when a customer leaves positive text but gives a low score - flagging these cases for human review.

4. Sending the message to support

For feedback needing attention, the workflow posts a detailed Slack message containing:

  • The original customer feedback
  • Translated version (if applicable)
  • Sentiment analysis results
  • Recommended follow-up actions
Sample Slack alert showing feedback details and recommended actions

5. Logging data in a database

The final step updates your central database with all feedback entries, including:

  • Customer name/identifier
  • Satisfaction score
  • Sentiment classification
  • Original language
  • Translation (if applicable)
Feedback database showing categorized entries

The database interface provides a user-friendly statistics dashboard visualizing your collected feedback metrics, including:

  • Positive/negative review ratios
  • Language distribution
  • Overall satisfaction trends
  • Response volume over time
