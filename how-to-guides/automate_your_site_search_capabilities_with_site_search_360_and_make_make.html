Connect Make to Site Search 360 to perfect site search

Site Search 360 (SS360) is a powerful search solution designed for any website. It provides a fast, customizable search experience with insightful analytics and document indexing capabilities. When your host or CMS lacks adequate search functionality, SS360 fills that gap with its robust search field.

The integration with Make unlocks new possibilities for managing your search index. Instead of manually adding content, you can automate the entire process. Make serves as the connective tissue between SS360 and your other apps, transforming how you maintain your site's searchability.

Available modules for Site Search 360 in Make platform

What can it help you do?

Make's integration with Site Search 360 opens up numerous automation possibilities. You can connect SS360 to hundreds of other apps through Make's extensive library of integrations. This creates a dynamic search ecosystem that updates automatically as your content changes.

The current modules available include a Trigger for monitoring indexing progress and an Action for adding documents to your index. These building blocks let you create sophisticated workflows tailored to your specific search needs.

1. Send new entries from your RSS feed to SS360

Automatically index new content as soon as it's published. When your RSS feed updates with a new blog post or article, Make can immediately send the URL to SS360 for indexing. This ensures your search results stay current without manual intervention.

Simply configure the RSS module in Make to watch your feed, then map the URL to the SS360 Index Document action. The entire process happens behind the scenes, keeping your search index synchronized with your latest content.

Example workflow for automating RSS feed indexing

2. Watch tweets for links and add them to your domain

Monitor Twitter for specific links and automatically add them to your search index. This is particularly useful for curating content or tracking industry resources. Make can watch designated Twitter accounts and process tweets containing matching URLs.

Configure the Twitter module to follow relevant accounts, then use Make's text parsing to identify links. When matching URLs appear in tweets, Make forwards them to SS360 for indexing. This creates a powerful content aggregation system.

Twitter link monitoring workflow for Site Search 360

How to connect to Make?

Connecting Site Search 360 to Make is a straightforward process that unlocks powerful automation capabilities. Before creating any scenarios, you'll need to establish the connection between the two services.

The connection process involves retrieving your API key from Site Search 360's Control Panel and entering it into Make. This secure handshake allows the two platforms to communicate while maintaining data privacy.

  • Sign up for a free Make account at www.make.com if you don't have one
  • Navigate to your Dashboard and click "Create a new scenario"
  • Select a Site Search 360 module to trigger the connection prompt
  • Click "Add" to initiate the connection process
  • Enter your API Key from Site Search 360's Control Panel under API
  • Paste the key into Make and click "Continue" to complete setup

Pro tip: For complex implementations, consider creating separate scenarios for different content sources (RSS, social media, documents) to maintain better control over your indexing workflows.
