1. Create ActiveTrail subscribers from new Google Forms responses

One of the most common automation needs is adding new contacts to your email lists automatically. With Make, you can set up a scenario where every new Google Forms response automatically creates a subscriber in ActiveTrail. This eliminates manual data entry and ensures no leads fall through the cracks.

The setup process begins with the Google Forms "Watch responses" trigger module. After connecting your Google account, you'll add the ActiveTrail "Create/Update contact" action module. The visual interface lets you map form fields to ActiveTrail contact fields through simple drag-and-drop.

Mapping Google Form fields to ActiveTrail contact properties in Make

2. Create ActiveTrail contacts from a Google Sheets spreadsheet

For marketers managing contact lists in spreadsheets, Make can automatically sync new entries to ActiveTrail. The "Watch a worksheet" trigger monitors your Google Sheet for new rows, while the ActiveTrail module processes each new entry as a contact.

This automation is particularly valuable for teams that collect leads at events or through offline channels before adding them to digital campaigns. The workflow can be enhanced with data validation and formatting steps to ensure clean data imports.

Automating contact creation from spreadsheet rows to ActiveTrail

3. Update contacts in HubSpot CRM based on subscription status in ActiveTrail

Keeping CRM records synchronized with email marketing preferences is crucial for compliance and customer experience. Make can monitor ActiveTrail for subscription changes and automatically update corresponding records in HubSpot CRM.

The workflow starts with ActiveTrail's "Watch for contact unsubscribed" trigger. Make then searches HubSpot for the matching contact and updates their subscription status. This two-way sync ensures your sales team always has accurate email preference data without manual updates.
