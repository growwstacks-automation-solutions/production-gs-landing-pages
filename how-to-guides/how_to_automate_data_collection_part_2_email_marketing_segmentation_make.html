How to Automate Email Marketing Segmentation

Let's illustrate the power of automation with a practical example. Imagine an agency that uses a Typeform lead generation form to gather information from potential clients. On this form, prospects are asked to select the specific service they are interested in, such as "Website Development," "SEO Services," or "Content Marketing."

Once a prospect completes and submits this form, Make.com springs into action. The automation is designed to take the submitted information and automatically add the prospect to the corresponding, relevant email list within the agency's email marketing platform, like ActiveCampaign. This entire process unfolds seamlessly in the background, requiring absolutely no manual intervention from the agency's team.

The beauty of this automation lies in its efficiency. The segmented email list is built dynamically and is ready for use as soon as a new lead comes in. This ensures that prospects immediately begin receiving communications tailored to their expressed interests, significantly improving the chances of engagement and conversion.

A Typeform example where prospects select their service interest.

To construct this type of automation, you'll utilize Make.com's powerful filter feature in conjunction with a router module. The router is essential for creating multiple distinct paths within your scenario, each corresponding to a different service option selected by the prospect in the form.

Since the "Service" field in our example form offers three different choices, the Make.com scenario will be configured with three separate paths. Each of these paths will have its own dedicated filter. These filters are crucial for directing the data down the correct path based on the prospect's selection, ensuring that the automation logic is precisely applied.

For instance, the filter on one of the paths will be set to check if the answer provided in the "Service" field of the form is exactly "Website Development." If this condition is met, the data for that particular prospect will then proceed along that specific path, triggering the subsequent actions defined for website development leads.

An overview of the Make.com scenario connecting Typeform to ActiveCampaign via a router.

Let's delve deeper into the mechanics of this scenario. Once a prospect's data is routed down a specific path based on their service selection, Make.com performs two critical actions. First, it extracts the email address provided by the prospect in the form and uses it to create a new contact entry within ActiveCampaign.

Following the successful creation of the contact, ActiveCampaign generates a unique contact ID. Make.com then utilizes this newly generated contact ID to perform the second action: adding the prospect to a predefined ActiveCampaign list. In our example, this would be a list specifically named "Website Development" for prospects interested in that service.

Detailed view of filter conditions within the Make.com router.

This automated process ensures that the agency can send highly targeted email messages that directly address the prospect's specific needs and interests, as indicated by their form submission. For instance, a prospect on the "Website Development" list will receive content relevant to website design, development processes, and related services, rather than generic marketing emails.

The direct consequence of this personalized communication is a significantly higher probability of nurturing these leads effectively and ultimately converting them into paying customers. By delivering value that resonates with each individual, the agency builds trust and demonstrates a clear understanding of their potential clients' requirements.

Crucially, all these intricate steps occur without any human intervention. The cost savings associated with this automated segmentation are substantial, far outweighing the expense of having an employee manually sift through form responses and painstakingly create corresponding email lists. This efficiency allows the agency to scale its marketing efforts without proportionally increasing its operational overhead.

Pro tip: When setting up your filters in Make.com, ensure that the values you're checking against (e.g., "Website Development") exactly match the options provided in your form. Even a small typo can prevent the automation from functioning correctly, leading to miscategorized leads.

A typical Make.com scenario for email segmentation involves several key components. First, a trigger module initiates the scenario upon a specific event, such as a new form submission. Next, a router module creates multiple pathways based on different conditions.

Filter modules are then used on each path to evaluate the data (e.g., a service preference selected in a form) and ensure it proceeds down the correct route. Finally, action modules for your email marketing platform create new contacts and add them to the relevant segmented lists.
