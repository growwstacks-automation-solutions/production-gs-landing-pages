Facebook Lead Ads

Facebook Lead Ads provide a convenient way for users to submit their information directly within the Facebook platform, streamlining the lead generation process. While similar to traditional web forms, a crucial difference lies in the immediate need to transfer this data to your CRM. If this transfer isn't handled promptly, you risk missing the critical window of opportunity to nurture these leads with relevant content or convert them into paying customers.

Failing to act quickly can result in cold leads and lost potential revenue. The longer a lead waits for a follow-up, the less likely they are to engage. Therefore, automating this step is not just about efficiency; it's about preserving the value of your advertising investment and ensuring that every lead receives the attention it deserves at the right moment.

To illustrate this vital automation, we've designed a Make.com scenario that integrates Facebook Lead Ads with Pipedrive, a popular CRM, and Mailchimp, a widely used email marketing platform. This setup demonstrates how new leads can be automatically added to your sales pipeline and email nurturing sequences, ensuring a cohesive and timely follow-up strategy.

A Make.com scenario illustrating the flow of Facebook Lead Ad data to Pipedrive and Mailchimp.

When the Facebook Lead Ads module within Make.com is triggered, it automatically retrieves all the lead data submitted through a pre-selected Facebook form. This module acts as the initial capture point, ensuring that no lead information is overlooked or delayed.

Following the data retrieval, a router in the Make.com scenario directs the lead information along two distinct paths, executing them in a specific order to ensure comprehensive lead management:

  • Make creates a new contact in Pipedrive using the "Create a Person" module. Immediately after, this new contact is added to a specific deal stage, typically labeled "Lead," using the "Create a Deal" module. This ensures the lead is visible and actionable within the sales pipeline.
  • Concurrently, Make adds the lead's email address to a designated list within Mailchimp. This step is crucial for initiating automated email nurturing sequences, keeping the lead engaged with relevant content from the outset.
Configuration details for the Pipedrive "Create a Deal" module within Make.com.

Pro tip: Customize your Pipedrive deal stages and Mailchimp lists to align perfectly with your sales and marketing funnels. This ensures that each lead is categorized accurately and receives the most appropriate follow-up, maximizing conversion potential.

Moving Website Form Leads to a Facebook Custom Audience

Retargeting is widely recognized as one of the most effective advertising strategies, allowing businesses to re-engage with individuals who have previously shown interest in their offerings. However, the challenge often lies in efficiently collecting and organizing the leads destined for retargeting without resorting to tedious manual processes. Automating this aspect is key to unlocking the full potential of your retargeting campaigns.

The scenario we've outlined below addresses this challenge by utilizing a webhook to automatically capture email addresses submitted through your website forms. This data is then seamlessly transferred to a Facebook Custom Audience, enabling you to build highly targeted segments for your ad campaigns. This eliminates the need for manual list uploads, ensuring your retargeting efforts are always up-to-date and responsive to user behavior.

A Make.com scenario demonstrating how a webhook feeds website form data into a Facebook Custom Audience.

Before you can leverage this powerful automation, a foundational step is to create a Facebook Custom Audience within your Facebook Business Manager account. This requires having both a Business Manager account and an active Facebook Ads account. Once these are in place, you can define the specific audience you wish to build, for example, based on website visitors or customer lists.

After your custom audience is established, the next step involves configuring the Make.com scenario. You'll navigate to the Facebook Custom Audiences module and select the "Add Emails to a Custom Audience" action. Here, you'll choose the specific custom audience you created earlier, linking it to your automation workflow.

The core of this automation lies in mapping the email data element retrieved by the Webhook module to the Facebook Custom Audiences module. This direct mapping ensures that every email address captured by your website form is accurately and automatically added to your chosen Facebook Custom Audience, ready for your retargeting campaigns.

Detailed setup of the Facebook Custom Audience module in Make.com.

Once your webhook has successfully added at least 100 email addresses to your Facebook Custom Audience, these contacts become available for use as a target audience in your Facebook ad campaigns. This threshold is set by Facebook to ensure audience privacy and data quality. At this point, you can launch retargeting campaigns specifically designed for individuals who have already engaged with your lead form, significantly increasing the relevance and effectiveness of your ads.

Similar to the ActiveCampaign scenario discussed in the second part of this series, which focused on automating email marketing segmentation, Make.com offers the flexibility to select the specific custom audience based on the data leads provide in your web form. This means you can create multiple custom audiences, each tailored to different lead segments or interests.

This capability allows you to build highly targeted audiences that are much more likely to convert. By delivering personalized ad content to users based on their expressed preferences or actions, you can expect sky-high conversion rates, making your retargeting efforts incredibly efficient and profitable. Make.com's versatility extends to numerous other applications, enabling you to retrieve data from various sources and forward it to Facebook Custom Audiences.
