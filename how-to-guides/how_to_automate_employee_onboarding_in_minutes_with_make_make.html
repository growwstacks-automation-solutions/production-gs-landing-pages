HR onboarding automation basics

Employee onboarding is a critical process that most companies manage regularly. While exciting for new hires, onboarding presents challenges that can affect both employee performance and overall process effectiveness.

New employees often feel overwhelmed by the volume of information they must process while adapting to a new environment. For companies, poorly organized onboarding can negatively impact first impressions and subsequent employee performance.

Pro tip: Automated onboarding ensures every new hire receives the same high-quality experience while reducing administrative burdens on HR teams.

Standard HR workflows typically include these onboarding tasks:

  • Creating database records for each new hire
  • Sending and processing information collection forms
  • Manually entering form responses into HR systems
  • Preparing and sending employment contracts
  • Distributing company resources and documentation
  • Scheduling introductory meetings and assigning onboarding buddies

The repetitive nature of these tasks makes them ideal candidates for automation. Automated workflows bring consistency and scalability to onboarding processes, allowing HR teams to focus on strategic priorities rather than administrative work.

Automating employee onboarding tasks

Onboarding fundamentally involves data transfer between HR departments and new hires. Automating these processes requires an integration layer with your company's HR database.

For this demonstration, we'll use Airtable as our database platform due to its flexibility and ease of use. The automation workflows will be built using Make (formerly Integromat).

Sample Airtable base showing employee records and onboarding status fields

Setting up your Airtable database

Before building automations, we need to configure an HR database in Airtable. This database will store records for all employees and track their onboarding progress.

The sample database includes an "Onboarding Stage" field with four status options that will trigger different automation workflows. This field acts as the central control point for the entire automated onboarding process.

Onboarding stage field options that trigger different automation workflows

1. Create an employee record and collect information

The first automation creates employee records and collects their information. We'll use Typeform to gather details from new hires, then automatically create corresponding records in Airtable.

This workflow begins with a Typeform trigger that watches for new submissions. When a form is completed, Make creates a record in Airtable containing all the collected information.

Sample Typeform for collecting new hire information

The Make scenario connects Typeform responses to Airtable records through a simple but powerful integration. This eliminates manual data entry while ensuring accuracy and consistency across all employee records.

Typeform module configuration in Make's visual workflow builder

2. Getting contracts signed

The contract signing automation triggers when an employee's onboarding status changes to "Sign Contract." Using Eversign, this workflow generates customized contracts and sends them for electronic signature.

The scenario starts with an Airtable module watching for records with the "Sign Contract" status. When detected, Make retrieves the employee data and passes it to Eversign to generate a contract with all relevant details prefilled.

Pro tip: Contract templates in Eversign can include conditional clauses that activate based on employee type, location, or other factors from your HR data.

Airtable module configuration for watching onboarding status changes

3. Sharing information and documents with new employees

This automation sends welcome packages and company resources when an employee's status changes to "Company Information." The workflow pulls relevant documents and sends them via personalized emails.

The scenario watches Airtable for status changes, then compiles and sends customized emails containing all necessary onboarding materials. This ensures new hires receive consistent information while allowing personalization based on their role, department, or location.
