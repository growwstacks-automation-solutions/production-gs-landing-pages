Employee performance review automation: Leading by example

Employee performance reviews are a critical method for assessing and improving workforce productivity. By conducting regular evaluations, organizations ensure employees meet job requirements, identify development areas, and foster continuous improvement culture.

However, most companies face two major challenges with manual reviews. First, the process consumes excessive manager time that scales with team size. Second, manual processing introduces errors that compromise data quality. Automation with Make solves both problems efficiently.

Pro tip: Start with quarterly reviews before moving to monthly cycles. This gives managers time to adapt to the automated system while still providing regular feedback.

1. Sending the forms to the managers

The first Make scenario triggers automatically at each quarter's end to initiate the review process. It locates performance review forms in a designated Google Drive folder and distributes individualized form links to managers via Slack.

This automated distribution ensures every team member receives their evaluation form promptly. Managers appreciate the direct Slack notification, which includes clear instructions and deadlines. The system eliminates the need for HR to manually track down managers or resend forgotten forms.

Automated Slack message sent to managers with performance review links

2. Automatically generate individual reports for each employee

After form submission, Make creates a Google Sheets spreadsheet for each evaluation. The second scenario activates 1-2 days later to process this data. It first verifies submission completion by checking for current quarter data in the sheets.

If data exists, the automation compiles individual performance reports complete with historical trend graphs. These comprehensive reports automatically deliver to managers via Slack. For missing submissions, the system sends polite but persistent reminders until completion.

Sample individual performance report generated automatically

3. Team reports: From idea to reality

The third scenario executes one week after initiation, allowing time for all reviews to complete. It aggregates individual data into three insightful team visualizations: overall rating distribution, comparative KPI analysis, and individual KPI performance.

These team reports provide executives with instant visibility into organizational performance trends. The automated charts eliminate days of manual spreadsheet work while providing more consistent, error-free analysis than human compilation could achieve.

Pro tip: Use the comparative KPI graphs to identify department-wide training needs or recognize teams exceeding performance benchmarks.

Standardized performance reviews at your fingertips

The complete automation requires just three Make templates that work together seamlessly. The system scales effortlessly across departments and locations, enforcing consistent evaluation standards organization-wide.

By implementing this automation, companies transform performance reviews from a dreaded administrative burden into a strategic advantage. The standardized data enables better talent decisions, while the time savings allow managers to focus on development rather than paperwork.
