How to Automatically Notify Customers About Unpaid Invoices

In this tutorial, we'll show you how you can transform the frustrating tasks of detecting unpaid invoices and reminding your customers about them with the help of automation.

In This Guide

  1. Create a Google Sheets spreadsheet and a new Make scenario
  2. Add the Google Sheets app to your scenario
  3. Add a filter

Step 1: Create a Google Sheets spreadsheet and a new Make scenario

Keeping track of unpaid invoices often becomes a challenge. On top of requiring persistent follow-ups, payments can easily slip through the cracks, leading to cash flow hiccups and lost revenue. Dedicating precious hours to this task can detract from your core business focus, but there's an elegant solution at your fingertips: Automation.

By harnessing the powerful combination of Stripe and Make, we can automate the tasks of detecting unpaid invoices and notifying customers about their missing payments. The first thing we'll do is create a Google Sheets spreadsheet that has the following columns:

  • Name
  • Created date
  • Due date
  • Amount due
  • Customer email
  • Plan
  • PDF link
  • Invoice ID
Initial setup of the Make scenario with Stripe integration

After creating your spreadsheet, log into your Make account and create a new scenario from the dashboard. Within the scenario builder, search for the Stripe app and select the Search Customers module. This is the first step towards collecting unpaid invoices.

Pro tip: If you don't have your Stripe account connected to Make yet, you'll need to add your API key in the module's configuration. Keep this key secure as it provides access to your Stripe data.

Step 2: Add the Google Sheets app to your scenario

Let's add the second app, Google Sheets. Click on the plus sign that appears on the right side of the Stripe module to add another module. Then, search and select the Google Sheets - Search Rows module.

As you did with the Stripe module, you need to connect your Google account to Make to access the module's configuration settings. After connecting your Google account to Make, select the spreadsheet that you want to add the information to, and then select the sheet.

Configuring the Google Sheets module to track invoice data

Next, scroll down to the filter. Here you need to search the sheet to verify if the invoices are already there or not. If they are, you don't want to duplicate them - we will show you how to prevent that in a bit. Now, select the Invoice ID column and map the Invoice ID data item from the Stripe module.

Step 3: Add a filter

In this example, we only want to retrieve invoices that are 10 days past the due date. Another filter is required, but we'll rely on a native Make filter rather than the one available in the Google Sheets module.

Between the two modules in your scenario you'll notice a link: Click it to open the filter menu. The date in Stripe and the date in Make are in different formats, and we need to standardize them so we can compare them in the filter. Let's use the format DD-MM-YYYY.

Setting up date formatting for accurate invoice due date comparison

In the Date and time functions tab, you'll find the formatDate function - please select it. In the top field, simply map the due date data item within the parentheses and enter the date format. Next, select the Datetime operator: Equal to.

Make's now variable gets the current date and time, and we need to subtract 10 days from it so that the filter only lets unpaid invoices that were due 10 days prior to pass through. To do this, use the addDays function, map the now variable, and enter -10. Lastly, enclose the addDays function with the formatDate function to format it as you did with the Stripe due date.
