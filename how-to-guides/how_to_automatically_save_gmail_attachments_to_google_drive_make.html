How to Automatically Save Gmail Attachments to Google Drive

A step-by-step tutorial to learn how to save Gmail attachments to Google Drive without writing a single line of code.

In This Guide

  1. Step 1: Deploy the template
  2. Step 2: Configure the template

Step 1: Deploy the template

Make allows you to build and automate anything in one visual platform, and there are two main ways to do this. You can either create the automation yourself exactly how you want it, or use one of the thousands of available templates.

For automatically saving Gmail attachments to Google Drive, Make offers a pre-built template that you can deploy instantly once you have your Make account. This template handles all the complex parts for you, providing a ready-made solution that just needs configuration.

Pro tip: Before deploying, ensure you're logged into both your Gmail and Google Drive accounts in the same browser session to streamline the connection process later.

Step 2: Configure the template

After deploying the template, you'll need to configure it to work with your specific Gmail and Google Drive accounts. The configuration process is straightforward and takes just a few minutes.

Start with the Gmail - Watch Emails module where you'll connect your Google account to Make. Click "Add" and grant permission to connect both accounts. Then specify the email folder (like your inbox) that Make should monitor for attachments.

Configuring the Gmail module to watch for new emails with attachments

Next, you'll configure the Google Drive module where the attachments will be saved. Connect the same Google account and select the specific Drive folder for storing attachments. Make will then automatically transfer files from Gmail to your chosen Drive location.

Setting up the destination folder in Google Drive

Finally, set the schedule for how often Make should check for new emails by clicking the clock icon on the Gmail module. You can choose intervals from every minute to daily, depending on how frequently you receive important attachments.
