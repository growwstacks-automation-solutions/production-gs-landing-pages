ActiveCampaign stands out among email marketing platforms for its robust automation capabilities. Unlike competitors that treat automation as an add-on, ActiveCampaign makes it a core feature with native app integrations and a visual workflow builder.

While ActiveCampaign's automation tools are powerful, they have limitations. Marketing automation extends beyond email, and the platform lacks native integrations with several important apps. This is where Make (formerly Integromat) becomes invaluable.

Pro tip: Before setting up any integration, ensure you have admin access to both ActiveCampaign and the app you're connecting. This prevents permission issues during configuration.

1. Webinar automation: Create ActiveCampaign contacts from Zoom webinar registrants

While Zoom and ActiveCampaign don't have a native integration, Make bridges this gap perfectly. Webinars serve as excellent lead magnets, but manually transferring attendee data wastes valuable time.

The Make template automatically captures Zoom webinar registrant details, creates corresponding ActiveCampaign contacts, and optionally stores them in Airtable. It intelligently updates existing contacts rather than creating duplicates, maintaining data hygiene in your CRM.

Example workflow for transferring Zoom webinar registrants to ActiveCampaign

2. Automatically send LinkedIn Lead Gen Form data to ActiveCampaign

Surprisingly, ActiveCampaign lacks native integration with LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms, one of the most effective B2B lead generation tools. Manually exporting and importing this data creates friction in your marketing funnel.

The Make solution uses webhooks to instantly capture LinkedIn lead data and create ActiveCampaign contacts. The template requires your LinkedIn form to include first name, last name, email, and phone fields for proper mapping.

Pro tip: Set up a dedicated ActiveCampaign list or tag for LinkedIn leads to track their performance separately in your campaigns.

3. Create ActiveCampaign contacts from new Acuity Scheduling appointments

Acuity Scheduling is a popular booking tool, but moving client data to ActiveCampaign manually is time-consuming. This integration automatically creates contacts from new appointments, enriching your email marketing database.

The template maps key appointment details like name, email, and service booked to corresponding ActiveCampaign fields. You can extend the workflow to trigger specific automation sequences based on the type of appointment booked.

4. Create new ActiveCampaign contacts from Facebook Lead Ads leads

Facebook Lead Ads deliver high-quality leads, but ActiveCampaign's lack of native integration means marketers often resort to manual processes or clunky workarounds.

The Make template uses Facebook's instant webhook notifications to capture lead details the moment they're submitted. The workflow then creates fully formatted contacts in ActiveCampaign, complete with lead source tracking for accurate campaign attribution.

Visualization of the Facebook Lead Ads to ActiveCampaign data flow

5. Create ActiveCampaign contacts from qualified leads using Clearbit and Intercom

This sophisticated workflow combines Intercom conversations with Clearbit enrichment to ensure only marketing-qualified leads enter your ActiveCampaign database.

The template first captures new leads from Intercom, then enriches the data with Clearbit to verify company information and job titles. Only leads meeting your predefined criteria (like specific industries or job levels) are added to ActiveCampaign, preventing list pollution.
