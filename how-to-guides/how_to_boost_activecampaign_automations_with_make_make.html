ActiveCampaign stands out as one of the top email marketing platforms with its strong focus on automation capabilities. Unlike competitors that treat automation as an afterthought, ActiveCampaign provides native app integrations and a drag-and-drop workflow builder to create sophisticated automated sequences.

For users familiar with Make (formerly Integromat), ActiveCampaign's workflow builder will feel pleasantly familiar. Both platforms use a visual interface to connect triggers and actions across different apps. However, while ActiveCampaign excels at email marketing automation, it has some notable limitations that Make can help overcome.

Pro tip: Marketing automation extends far beyond just email. ActiveCampaign's native connectors, while extensive, still miss many popular apps that businesses rely on for lead generation and customer engagement.

By integrating Make with ActiveCampaign, you can:

  • Connect apps that don't have native ActiveCampaign integrations
  • Automate processes beyond just email marketing
  • Build more sophisticated workflows with conditional logic

1. Webinar automation: Create ActiveCampaign contacts from Zoom webinar registrants

While Zoom doesn't natively integrate with ActiveCampaign, webinars remain one of the most effective lead generation tools. Each registrant represents a warm lead that should be nurtured through your email sequences.

This Make template solves the integration gap by automatically capturing Zoom webinar registrants and creating them as contacts in ActiveCampaign. The workflow checks for existing contacts to prevent duplicates, updating information if the contact already exists rather than creating redundant entries.

As an optional step, the template also records registrant data in Airtable for backup purposes. If you don't use Airtable, you can simply remove this module while keeping the core ActiveCampaign integration fully functional.

Visual representation of the Zoom webinar registrant data flowing into ActiveCampaign

2. Automatically send LinkedIn Lead Gen Form data to ActiveCampaign

Surprisingly, ActiveCampaign lacks native integration with LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms - one of the most effective B2B lead generation tools available today. This forces marketers to manually export and import lead data, creating delays and potential errors in their nurturing sequences.

The Make template solves this by instantly capturing LinkedIn lead data through a webhook and creating corresponding ActiveCampaign contacts. The automation preserves all form field data, ensuring your sales team has complete context when following up.

Important requirements for this integration:

  • Your LinkedIn form must collect first name, last name, email and phone number
  • The webhook URL needs to be configured in your LinkedIn Campaign Manager
  • Field mappings between LinkedIn and ActiveCampaign should be verified

3. Create ActiveCampaign contacts from new Acuity Scheduling appointments

Acuity Scheduling is a popular booking tool for service businesses, but its lack of native ActiveCampaign integration means valuable client data often gets stuck in silos. Each new appointment represents an opportunity to nurture the relationship through targeted email sequences.

This Make template automatically creates ActiveCampaign contacts whenever someone books an appointment in Acuity. The workflow captures all submitted information, allowing you to personalize follow-up emails based on the specific service booked.

The integration works particularly well for:

  • Consulting businesses that want to send pre-appointment materials
  • Health practitioners needing to share intake forms
  • Service providers sending post-appointment satisfaction surveys

4. Create new ActiveCampaign contacts from Facebook Lead Ads leads

Facebook Lead Ads generate high-quality leads at lower costs than many other channels, yet ActiveCampaign doesn't offer native integration. This forces marketers to manually download and upload lead data, delaying follow-up and reducing conversion rates.

The Make solution uses Facebook's webhook functionality to instantly pass lead data to ActiveCampaign. Each new lead triggers the creation of a fully populated contact record, complete with all form field responses mapped to corresponding ActiveCampaign fields.

Key benefits of this automation:

  • Leads enter your nurturing sequences within seconds of form submission
  • All lead data is preserved without manual transcription errors
  • You can trigger immediate follow-up actions based on lead responses
Flow of lead data from Facebook Ads directly into ActiveCampaign

5. Create ActiveCampaign contacts from qualified leads using Clearbit and Intercom

Intercom conversations often generate high-quality leads, but manually qualifying and importing them into ActiveCampaign is time-consuming. This advanced workflow combines Intercom, Clearbit, and ActiveCampaign to automate lead qualification and nurturing.

The process begins when a new lead is identified in Intercom. Make then enriches this lead data using Clearbit's powerful firmographic and demographic intelligence. Only leads that meet your predefined qualification criteria (like company size, industry, or job title) are created as ActiveCampaign contacts.

This sophisticated filtering ensures your sales team only spends time on properly qualified leads while marketing nurtures the others until they're sales-ready. The automation handles all the data transfer and decision-making behind the scenes.

Pro tip: You can customize the Clearbit filters to match your ideal customer profile exactly. This ensures only your highest-value leads enter your sales sequences while others continue through educational nurturing.
