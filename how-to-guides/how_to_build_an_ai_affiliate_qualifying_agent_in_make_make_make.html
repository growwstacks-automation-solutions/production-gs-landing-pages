How It Works

The AI Affiliate Qualifying Agent, affectionately named "Scout" in our example, operates through a series of interconnected steps within Make.com to ensure a seamless and efficient lead qualification process.

1. Watch for New Leads

Scout begins by continuously monitoring a central data source, typically a Google Sheet. This sheet is the hub where all new affiliate leads converge, whether they originate from your affiliate registration forms, LinkedIn messages, or a dedicated Gmail folder. This consolidation ensures that no potential partner is overlooked and all leads are captured in one accessible location for processing.

2. Apply Smart Qualification Rules

Once a new lead appears in the Google Sheet, Scout springs into action, applying a set of predefined qualification criteria. These rules can be tailored to your specific business needs, checking for factors such as the validity of the email domain, minimum annual revenue requirements, a verified tax ID, or geographic location. Leads that don't meet these essential criteria are automatically filtered out, preventing wasted time on unqualified contacts.

Agentic automation allows AI to make decisions and adapt in real-time, enhancing workflow efficiency.

3. Enrich CRM Records

For leads that successfully pass the initial qualification, Scout takes the next crucial step: updating your Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system, such as HubSpot. If the company already exists in your CRM, Scout updates the existing record with the latest information. If it's a new contact, Scout creates a fresh record, enriching it with additional data pulled from external sources like ZoomInfo. This ensures your CRM is always up-to-date and provides a comprehensive view of each promising affiliate.

4. Organize and Communicate

Simultaneously, Scout handles the organizational and communication aspects. It creates a new Trello card for each qualified lead, ensuring that all relevant team members have visibility into its status. Concurrently, it dispatches a personalized welcome email to the potential affiliate, initiating the engagement process. The agent can also be configured to handle common questions and initial replies, maintaining consistent and timely communication.

5. Book the Meeting

When a qualified lead indicates readiness for a discussion, Scout seamlessly schedules a meeting directly in the affiliate manager's calendar. It then updates the corresponding Trello card to reflect the "Meeting Organized" status and sends a notification to the manager, allowing them to prepare effectively for the upcoming discussion.

AI agents can automate complex decision-making processes, from data input to actionable output.

6. Manage Delays Gracefully

Not every lead is ready to convert immediately. If a potential affiliate needs more time, Scout intelligently parses notes from interactions (e.g., "reach back out in 6 months") and automatically sets a reminder for future follow-up. This proactive management ensures that no valuable opportunity falls through the cracks, maintaining a robust pipeline without manual tracking.

Quick Setup Guide

Setting up your AI Affiliate Qualifying Agent in Make.com is a straightforward process. Follow these steps to get your automation up and running:

  1. Create Your Make.com Account: If you don't already have one, sign up for a Make.com account.
  2. Set up Google Sheet: Create a new Google Sheet to serve as your central lead repository. Ensure it has columns for all relevant lead information (e.g., Name, Email, Company, Source).
  3. Configure Lead Capture: Connect your lead sources (e.g., website forms, LinkedIn, dedicated email inbox) to automatically feed new leads into your Google Sheet.
  4. Design Your AI Agent: Within Make.com, create a new AI Agent. Define its purpose as "Affiliate Qualifying Agent" and input your specific qualification rules (e.g., minimum revenue, industry, location).
  5. Build the Make Scenario:
    • Start with a "Watch Rows" module for your Google Sheet to trigger on new leads.
    • Add a module to send the lead data to your AI Agent for qualification.
    • Use conditional logic to filter leads based on the AI Agent's output.
    • For qualified leads, add modules to update/create records in your CRM (e.g., HubSpot) and enrich data via ZoomInfo.
    • Include modules to create a Trello card and send a personalized welcome email.
    • For unqualified leads, set up a module to log them or send an automated rejection email.
  6. Test Your Workflow: Run test leads through your system to ensure the AI Agent correctly qualifies them, updates CRM, creates Trello cards, and sends emails as expected.
  7. Activate and Monitor: Once thoroughly tested, activate your Make scenario. Regularly monitor its performance and refine your AI Agent's rules as needed.

Pro tip: Start with a small set of clear qualification rules and gradually add complexity as you gain confidence in your AI Agent's performance. This iterative approach helps ensure accuracy and reduces potential errors.
