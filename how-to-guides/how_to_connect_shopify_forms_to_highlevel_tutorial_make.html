How to Connect Shopify Forms to HighLevel

Step-by-step guide to integrate Shopify Forms with HighLevel using Make for automated lead data collection and customer engagement optimization.

  2. Step 2: Add the HighLevel app

Step 1: Create a new Make scenario and add the Shopify app

Connecting Shopify Forms with HighLevel is essential for any Shopify merchant looking to automate lead data collection and enhance customer interactions. This integration enables you to automatically transfer customer information from your Shopify store to HighLevel, where you can implement sophisticated marketing and sales follow-up sequences.

While API coding might seem daunting, Make provides a no-code solution that makes this integration accessible to everyone. With Make's visual interface, you can connect Shopify and HighLevel in minutes without any technical expertise. The platform handles all the complex API communications behind the scenes.

Creating a new scenario in Make to begin the integration process

Setting up the Shopify trigger

After logging into your Make account, click "Create a new scenario" to begin building your integration. This opens Make's visual workflow builder where you'll construct the automation step by step. The first component you'll add is the Shopify trigger module.

Search for Shopify in the app directory and select the "Watch Customers" module. This module will monitor your Shopify store for new customer sign-ups and form submissions. It serves as the starting point that triggers the entire automation whenever new customer data is detected.

Pro tip: Before proceeding, ensure you have admin access to both your Shopify store and HighLevel account. You'll need these credentials to authenticate the connection between the platforms.

Step 2: Add the HighLevel app

With the Shopify trigger in place, the next step is to add HighLevel as the destination for your customer data. Click the plus icon next to your Shopify module to add another step to your scenario. Search for HighLevel in the app directory and select the "Create a Contact" module.

This module will receive customer information from Shopify and create corresponding contact records in your HighLevel account. You'll need to authenticate your HighLevel account by providing your API key, which can be found in your HighLevel account settings under the API section.

Configuring the HighLevel module to receive Shopify customer data

Mapping data fields

The crucial part of this step is mapping the data fields between Shopify and HighLevel. Make's interface allows you to drag and drop data elements from the Shopify module to corresponding fields in the HighLevel module. For example, map the customer's first name from Shopify to the first name field in HighLevel.

You can map various data points including email addresses, phone numbers, purchase history, and custom form fields. This ensures all relevant customer information captured in Shopify flows seamlessly into your HighLevel CRM for immediate use in marketing campaigns and sales follow-ups.

Step 3: Add a filter

While the basic integration now works, you might want to add filters to control which customers get sent to HighLevel. For instance, you may only want to transfer customers who signed up through specific forms or met certain criteria. Make's filter module allows you to set these conditions easily.

Click on the connection between your Shopify and HighLevel modules to add a filter. You can configure it to check for specific tags, form names, or other customer attributes before allowing the data to proceed to HighLevel. This prevents your HighLevel account from being flooded with irrelevant contacts.

Setting up filters to control which customer records get transferred

Testing your filter

Before finalizing your scenario, it's wise to test your filter with sample data. Make provides a testing mode where you can simulate customer submissions and verify that only the intended records pass through to HighLevel. This step ensures your automation works exactly as intended before going live.

If certain customers aren't being transferred as expected, you can adjust your filter conditions. Make's interface provides various comparison operators (equals, contains, starts with, etc.) to fine-tune your filtering logic until it perfectly matches your business requirements.

Step 4: Schedule and save the scenario

The final step is to determine how often your automation should run. Click the clock icon on your Shopify module to access the scheduling settings. Here you can set the scenario to check for new customers every 15 minutes, hourly, daily, or at any interval that suits your business needs.

Consider your store's typical order volume when setting this frequency. High-volume stores might benefit from more frequent checks (every 15-30 minutes), while smaller stores could opt for daily updates. The goal is to balance timely data transfer with efficient use of your Make operations.

Configuring the schedule for automatic data transfers

Activating your integration

After configuring all settings, save your scenario and toggle it to the "on" position. Make will immediately begin monitoring your Shopify store according to your specified schedule. Each time it detects new customer data that matches your filter criteria, it will automatically create corresponding records in HighLevel.

Monitor your scenario's performance for the first few days to ensure it's working correctly. Make provides execution logs where you can review each transfer and troubleshoot any issues. Once confirmed stable, your Shopify-HighLevel integration will run continuously in the background, saving you hours of manual work.
