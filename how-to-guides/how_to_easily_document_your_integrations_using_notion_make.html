  • Associate documentation with specific clients, projects, or tasks.

Documenting Integrations: First Steps

XRay.Tech's documentation process begins with creating a concise yet thorough summary for each automated workflow. This summary outlines the workflow's purpose and how it operates, providing an immediate understanding for anyone reviewing it.

A valuable tip for maintaining consistency and clarity at this initial stage is to envision that the documentation will be read by someone completely unfamiliar with the integrations. By adopting this perspective, you ensure that sufficient detail is included for the reader to grasp the workflow's objective and the individual tasks that comprise it.

Typically, XRay.Tech dedicates a Notion page to each distinct process. A single process might encompass several individual workflows that collectively contribute to its overall function and successful completion. For more complex processes, Notion's page nesting capabilities allow for the creation of subpages for each specific workflow, maintaining a clear hierarchical structure.

To ensure uniformity across all records, XRay.Tech utilizes a standardized documentation template. This template pre-fills each new Notion page with a series of prompts, guiding the documenter to include all necessary information. This structured approach guarantees that no critical details are overlooked and that all documentation follows a consistent format.

A blank Notion template designed for documenting automated workflows, featuring pre-defined prompts.

As illustrated in the template, specific questions are included to clarify when and why a particular automation is used. These prompts are instrumental in defining the automation's core use case and its strategic value within the business.

Furthermore, the template incorporates questions about the data involved in the workflow. These data-centric prompts help to delineate the technical configuration of the automation and identify all the applications that are integrated into the process. This ensures a comprehensive overview of the workflow's technical architecture.

Example of a Notion page with detailed information about an automation's data handling and connected apps.

In addition to textual descriptions, screenshots of the actual workflows are embedded directly into the documentation. This visual component provides a quick and intuitive way to understand the integration without the need to create new graphics from scratch, saving time and ensuring accuracy.

Next: Adding Attributes to Organize the Documentation

One of Notion's most powerful features, and a primary reason for its suitability as an internal documentation tool, is its ability to incorporate a wide array of custom attributes into each page. These attributes transform static documents into dynamic, filterable records.

For instance, you can establish attributes to track the status of each workflow (e.g., "Active," "In Development," "Archived"), define its type (e.g., "Marketing Automation," "Client Onboarding"), include a direct link to the corresponding scenario in Make.com, list all contributing team members, and even link the page to a specific client profile or designate it as an internal automation. This level of detail provides immediate context and streamlines management.

Custom attributes in Notion for an integration, showing status, type, and a link to the Make.com scenario.

Once these attributes are in place, you can leverage Notion's powerful database views to sort, filter, and group your documentation based on any of these criteria. This functionality allows for highly flexible and insightful analysis of your automation portfolio.

For example, you can quickly generate a view that displays all active marketing automations, or all workflows currently under development for a specific client. This capability provides a comprehensive and accurate overview of workflow status, purpose, and type, all from a single dashboard. This dynamic organization is invaluable for strategic planning and operational oversight.

A Notion database displaying various integrations, categorized and filtered by their custom attributes for easy management.

For XRay.Tech, these adaptable attributes and views are instrumental in helping their employees quickly locate specific integrations and access all relevant information. This significantly reduces search time and ensures that team members always have the most up-to-date details at their fingertips.

It's important to highlight that all these attributes are fully customizable. They can be modified and tailored in minutes to perfectly align with your organization's unique needs and evolving requirements, making Notion an incredibly flexible tool for managing complex automation documentation.
