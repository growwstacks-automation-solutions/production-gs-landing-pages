What This Workflow Does

This powerful automation streamlines the entire process of running an interactive prize giveaway, allowing businesses to capture valuable lead information with minimal manual effort. It connects Google Forms and Google Sheets with barcode generation and email delivery, creating a seamless experience for both participants and administrators. By automating the lead capture and prize notification process, you can focus on engaging with your new prospects rather than getting bogged down in administrative tasks.

The core function of this workflow is to transform a simple form submission into an engaging, real-time prize reveal. When a potential customer enters their details into a Google Form, the system automatically processes their entry, generates a unique barcode (QR code) that determines if they've won, and emails it to them. Upon scanning the barcode, the participant instantly discovers their prize status, and their entry in Google Sheets is updated accordingly. This not only makes the giveaway exciting but also ensures accurate data collection and immediate follow-up potential.

How It Works

Step 1: Participant Submits Google Form

A potential lead fills out a Google Form with their personal details, which is automatically recorded in a designated Google Sheet. This is the initial touchpoint for capturing customer information.

Example Google Form for collecting lead information.

Step 2: Lead Data is Transferred to Google Sheets

Upon submission, the Google Form automatically populates a new row in a connected Google Sheet. This sheet acts as your central database for all giveaway participants.

Connecting Google Form responses to a Google Sheet.
Google Sheet with participant data.

Step 3: Random Number Generation and Barcode Creation

A Make.com scenario is triggered by the new row in Google Sheets. It generates a random number (0-10) to determine the prize outcome (even for win, odd for lose). This random number, along with the participant's row number, is then embedded into a unique QR code (barcode) using a webhook URL.

Configuring the Google Sheets 'Watch Rows' module.
Generating a random number for prize determination.

Step 4: Barcode Email Delivery

The generated QR code is automatically sent to the participant's email address. This email contains instructions on how to scan the barcode to reveal their prize status, creating an interactive and engaging step in the lead nurturing process.

Configuring the Barcodes module with the webhook URL.
The custom webhook URL used for barcode generation.
Sending the QR code via Gmail.

Step 5: Barcode Scan and Instant Result

When the participant scans their unique QR code, a second Make.com scenario is instantly triggered via the embedded webhook. This scenario processes the random number from the QR code, determines if it's a win or lose, and displays a custom HTML message directly on the participant's device.

Step 6: Google Sheet Update with Outcome

Finally, the Google Sheet is updated with the outcome of the prize reveal (e.g., "Win" or "No win"). This provides a comprehensive record of all participants and their results, enabling easy tracking and future marketing efforts.

Quick Setup Guide

Setting up this powerful lead generation system is straightforward. Follow these steps to get your prize giveaway automation running:

  1. Prepare Your Google Form and Sheet: Create a new Google Form to collect participant details (e.g., Name, Email). Link this form to a new Google Sheet to automatically store submissions. Ensure your Google Sheet has columns for "Timestamp", "Name", "Email", "Random ID", and "Confirmation".
  2. Create Scenario 1 (Lead Capture & Barcode Generation):
    • Add a "Google Sheets - Watch Rows" module, selecting your giveaway Google Sheet.
    • Add a "Tools - Set Variable" module to generate a random number between 0-10. Use the formula floor(random(0, 10)). This will determine if the participant wins.
    • Add a "Barcodes - Generate a barcode" module. Select "QR Code" as the type.
    • In the Barcodes module, construct the webhook URL for Scenario 2 (which you'll create next). Embed the generated random ID and the Google Sheet row number into this URL.
    • Add a "Gmail - Send an email" module. Map the participant's email address from the Google Sheet. Attach the generated QR code from the Barcodes module.
    • Add a "Google Sheets - Update a row" module. Map the row number from the "Watch Rows" module and update the "Random ID" column with the generated random number.
  3. Create Scenario 2 (Barcode Scan Processing):
    • Add a "Webhooks - Custom webhook" module. Copy its URL; you'll use this in Scenario 1.
    • Add a "Webhooks - Webhook response" module. Set the status to 200. In the body, use an IF function with the Mod operator to check if the random ID from the webhook URL is even (win) or odd (lose). Display a custom HTML message accordingly.
    • Add a "Google Sheets - Update a row" module. Create a filter before this module to ensure the row number exists. Map the row number from the Custom Webhook.
    • In the "Update a row" module, update the "Confirmation" column in your Google Sheet with "Win" or "No win" based on the same IF/Mod logic used in the Webhook response.
  4. Test and Activate: Run a few test entries through your Google Form and scan the emailed QR codes to ensure both scenarios are working correctly and updating the Google Sheet as expected. Once confirmed, activate both scenarios.

Webhooks act as real-time notifications between applications, enabling instant data transfer and triggering actions in response to specific events.
