How to Retrieve RSS Feed Items and Post to WordPress

Automate content distribution by watching for new RSS feed items and automatically posting them to your WordPress site. Save time and ensure your content is always fresh across your platforms.

What This Workflow Does

This powerful automation streamlines your content distribution process by automatically taking new items from an RSS feed and publishing them directly to your WordPress website. Instead of manually checking various sources and then copying and pasting content, this workflow ensures your WordPress site is always updated with the latest information from your chosen RSS feeds. It's perfect for keeping blogs, news sections, or resource libraries consistently fresh without constant human intervention.

By eliminating the repetitive task of cross-posting, this solution frees up valuable time for content creators, marketers, and business owners. It ensures that your audience receives timely updates, enhances your site's SEO with fresh content, and maintains a consistent content flow across your digital presence. This automation acts as a digital assistant, tirelessly monitoring your preferred information sources and feeding them into your WordPress platform.

How It Works

Step 1: Monitor Your RSS Feed

The workflow begins with an RSS module configured to "Watch RSS feed items." You simply provide the URL of the RSS feed you wish to monitor. This could be a news aggregator, a competitor's blog, industry updates, or even another one of your own content hubs. The module is set to check this feed at regular intervals, looking for any new entries that have been published since its last check. You can specify how many new items it should process per run to manage the volume of content.

Screenshot showing the app selection interface in Make.com.

Step 2: Create a WordPress Post

Once a new RSS feed item is detected, the workflow triggers the WordPress module. This module is set to "Create a Post" on your connected WordPress site. You'll map the relevant data from the RSS feed (like title, description, and publication date) directly to the corresponding fields in your WordPress post. This ensures that the content is accurately transferred and formatted as a new draft or published post on your site.

Screenshot showing the selection of the RSS module in Make.com.

Step 3: Configure Post Details and Publish

Within the WordPress module, you have full control over how the new post appears. You can define the post status (e.g., "Publish" immediately, "Draft" for review), assign categories and tags, and even specify the author. This level of customization allows you to integrate the automated content seamlessly into your existing WordPress content strategy. After configuration, the workflow will automatically publish the new content according to your settings, keeping your site dynamic and engaging.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Create a New Scenario in Make.com: Log in to your Make.com account and click "Create a new scenario."
  2. Add the RSS Module: Search for "RSS" and select the "Watch RSS feed items" trigger.
  3. Configure RSS Feed: Paste the URL of the RSS feed you want to monitor into the "URL" field. Set the "Maximum number of returned items" (e.g., to 2) to control the volume per run.
  4. Add the WordPress Module: Click the plus icon next to the RSS module, search for "WordPress," and select the "Create a Post" action.
  5. Connect WordPress Account: Click "Add" to connect your WordPress site by entering your credentials.
  6. Map RSS Data to WordPress Fields: In the WordPress module, map the "Title" from the RSS feed to the WordPress "Title" field, "Description" to "Content," and "Date created" to "Date." You can also map "Summary" to "Excerpt."
  7. Set Post Status and Categories: Choose "Publish" for the post status (or "Draft" if you prefer to review manually). Select relevant categories and tags for your posts.
  8. Test the Scenario: Save your scenario. Publish a new item to your monitored RSS feed, then click "Run once" in Make.com to test the automation. Verify that the post appears correctly on your WordPress site.
  9. Activate and Schedule: Once tested, set your desired schedule for the scenario (e.g., every hour, daily) and toggle the scenario to "ON."

RSS (Really Simple Syndication) is a web feed format used to publish frequently updated works, such as blog entries, news headlines, audio, and video, in a standardized format. For WordPress, it's incredibly useful because it allows you to subscribe to content updates from other websites and automatically pull that content into your own site. By leveraging RSS feeds, you can keep your WordPress blog or news section fresh and engaging with minimal oversight. It's also crucial to respect copyright and intellectual property rights when aggregating content from external sources. Always ensure you have permission or are adhering to fair use guidelines when automatically publishing content from others' feeds.

The optimal frequency for running your RSS to WordPress automation depends on the nature of the RSS feed and your content strategy. For highly dynamic news feeds that update constantly, running the automation every 15-30 minutes might be appropriate to ensure near real-time updates. For less frequent sources, like weekly blogs or monthly reports, a daily or even weekly schedule would suffice.

Consider the volume of new items expected and your server's capacity. Running the automation too frequently for a feed with many updates could consume excessive operations or strain your WordPress site. Conversely, running it too infrequently might mean your content isn't as fresh as it could be. It's best to start with a moderate frequency (e.g., hourly) and adjust based on observation and your specific needs.
