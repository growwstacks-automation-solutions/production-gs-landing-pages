What This Workflow Does

This powerful automation streamlines your email management by automatically saving incoming emails to a designated folder in Google Drive. Instead of manually archiving each important message, this workflow converts your emails into individual Google Docs files, ensuring they are easily searchable, accessible, and securely stored. This not only frees up valuable time but also provides a robust backup solution for critical communications and data.

For businesses, this means an end to lost emails, disorganized inboxes, and the tedious task of manual archiving. Whether it's client correspondence, project updates, or important receipts, every specified email is systematically cataloged. This enhances operational efficiency, supports compliance efforts, and ensures that your team always has access to the information they need, when they need it, without ever leaving the Google ecosystem.

How It Works

The workflow operates in a few simple, automated steps, ensuring a seamless transfer of your emails to Google Drive.

Step 1: Monitor Incoming Emails

The automation begins by continuously watching your specified email inbox or folder for new messages. You can configure it to monitor all emails, or filter for specific criteria such as sender, subject keywords, or even emails with attachments. This ensures only relevant emails are processed.

Select the Email app and 'Watch Emails' module.

Step 2: Extract Email Content

Once a new email is detected, the workflow automatically extracts its key information, including the subject, sender, date, and the body text. This data is then prepared for conversion into a new document.

Configure the email folder to watch and set criteria.

Step 3: Create Google Doc in Google Drive

The extracted email content is then used to create a new Google Docs file in a specified Google Drive folder. The file is intelligently named using details from the email, such as the subject and sender, making it easy to identify later. The email's body content is directly inserted into the new Google Doc.

Integrate Google Drive to create new files from email content.

Step 4: Schedule and Activate

After a quick test to ensure everything is working correctly, the automation is scheduled to run at regular intervals (e.g., daily, hourly). This ensures continuous, hands-free archiving of your emails, keeping your Google Drive up-to-date without any manual intervention.

Map email subject and content to create organized Google Docs.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your email-to-Google Drive automation up and running:

  1. Create a New Scenario in Make.com: Log in to your Make.com account and click "Create a new scenario."
  2. Add Email Module: Search for the "Email" app and select the "Watch Emails" module. Connect your email account and specify the folder you wish to monitor (e.g., Inbox, Important). Set the maximum number of results to fetch per run.
  3. Define Monitoring Start: A dialog will appear asking when to start monitoring emails. Choose "From now on" or a specific date/time based on your needs.
  4. Add Google Drive Module: Click the "+" icon next to the Email module, search for "Google Drive," and select the "Create a File from Text" module.
  5. Configure Google Drive Module: Connect your Google account. Select the destination Google Drive and the specific folder where you want to save the emails. Map the "Subject" and "Sender Name" fields from the email module to the "File Name" field in Google Drive for easy identification. Map the "Text content" from the email module to the "File Content" field. Ensure "Convert the File to Google Docs document" is set to "Yes."
  6. Test the Scenario: Turn the scenario on and click "Run once." Send a test email to yourself to verify that a Google Doc is created in your specified Google Drive folder.
  7. Schedule the Automation: Click the "Clock" icon on the email module to set a schedule for how often the scenario should run automatically (e.g., daily, every few hours).
  8. Save and Activate: Save your scenario. Your emails will now be automatically archived to Google Drive!
