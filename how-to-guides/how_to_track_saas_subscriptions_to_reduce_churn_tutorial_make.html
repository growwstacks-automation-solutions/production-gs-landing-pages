Understanding SaaS Churn

Customer churn represents the percentage of subscribers who cancel their recurring services during a given time period. For subscription-based businesses, monitoring churn is as critical as tracking new customer acquisition. High churn rates can undermine growth and indicate deeper issues with product-market fit or customer satisfaction.

Churn analysis becomes particularly valuable when you can identify specific cancellation patterns. The automation we'll build helps surface these insights by systematically tracking payment failures and voluntary cancellations across your customer base.

Pro tip: Segment your churn analysis by customer cohorts (signup date, plan type, acquisition channel) to identify high-risk groups and tailor retention strategies accordingly.

Automation Scenario Overview

This workflow connects three key systems: your payment database (PostgreSQL), a centralized tracking spreadsheet (Google Sheets), and your team's task management tool (ClickUp). The automation runs monthly to identify cancellations from the previous billing cycle.

The complete solution performs three core functions: extracts payment status data from your database, filters for cancellations and failed payments, then creates actionable tasks for your customer success team to follow up with affected subscribers.

The automated workflow connects payment data with team task management

Step 1: Create the Make Scenario

Begin by creating a new scenario in Make (formerly Integromat). This serves as the container for your entire automation workflow. Name it descriptively, like "Monthly Churn Detection" to clearly identify its purpose.

Make's visual interface allows you to drag and connect modules that represent different apps and actions. The first module you'll add connects to your PostgreSQL database containing payment records.

Step 2: Add PostgreSQL Module

The PostgreSQL module executes a query against your payment database. Configure it with your database credentials and set up a dynamic query that retrieves transactions from the previous calendar month.

The SQL query should filter for payment records with statuses like "Cancelled" or "Failed." Use Make's date functions to automatically adjust the date range each month, ensuring you always analyze the most recent billing cycle without manual adjustments.

Configuring the PostgreSQL module to query payment data

Step 3: Connect Google Sheets

Add a Google Sheets module to your scenario to log all identified cancellations in a centralized spreadsheet. This creates an audit trail and allows for historical analysis of churn patterns.

Map the fields from your PostgreSQL query to corresponding columns in your Google Sheet. Essential fields to include are customer email, subscription tier, cancellation date, and payment amount. This structured data becomes valuable for analyzing churn drivers over time.

Step 4: Implement ClickUp Tasks

The final component creates actionable tasks in ClickUp for your customer success team. Configure the module to generate specific tasks based on the cancellation reason - for example, "Payment Failed" tasks might go to your billing team while "Voluntary Cancellation" tasks route to account managers.

Include relevant customer details in each task description to provide context for follow-up. Consider adding priority levels based on customer lifetime value or other business rules to help your team prioritize their outreach efforts.

Setting up automated task creation in ClickUp

Testing and Scheduling

Before activating your scenario, run several test executions with different date ranges to verify it correctly identifies cancellations and creates the appropriate tasks. Check that data flows accurately between all connected systems.

Once validated, schedule the scenario to run automatically on the first day of each month. This ensures your team always has fresh cancellation data to work with at the start of each billing cycle, maximizing their chances of successful winback efforts.
