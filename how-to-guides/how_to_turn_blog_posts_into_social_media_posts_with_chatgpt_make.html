Automated Content Conversion Workflow

The process of converting blog posts into social media content involves several automated steps that work together seamlessly. First, the system monitors your WordPress site for newly published articles. When a new post is detected, the content is extracted and sent to ChatGPT for processing.

ChatGPT analyzes the blog content using carefully crafted prompts that identify key points, extract quotable phrases, and generate multiple social media post variations. These include different formats like question prompts, statistic highlights, and summary posts. The generated content is then organized in Airtable for review before being scheduled or published.

Pro tip: Structure your ChatGPT prompts to generate posts in different engagement styles - some should be thought-provoking questions, others should highlight key statistics, and some should summarize core concepts.

Step-by-Step Setup Process

Setting up this automation requires connecting three main components: your content source (WordPress), ChatGPT, and your social media platforms. In Make (formerly Integromat), you'll create a scenario that triggers whenever a new blog post is published. This scenario will handle the entire workflow from content extraction to final posting.

The first module watches your WordPress RSS feed or uses the WordPress API to detect new posts. Once triggered, it extracts the post content, cleans the HTML formatting, and prepares it for ChatGPT processing. The second module sends this content to ChatGPT with your predefined prompts that specify the types of social posts to generate.

Airtable interface used to review and schedule generated social media posts

Optimizing ChatGPT Prompts

The quality of your social media posts depends heavily on how you structure your ChatGPT prompts. Effective prompts should specify the desired tone, length, and format of the output. They should also include examples of your best-performing social posts to guide the AI's style.

For maximum effectiveness, create separate prompts for different post types. One prompt might generate tweet threads, another could create LinkedIn posts, and a third might produce Instagram captions. Each should include platform-specific guidelines about character limits, hashtag usage, and engagement tactics.

Platform-Specific Adaptations

Different social platforms require different content approaches. Twitter posts need to be concise and punchy, while LinkedIn content can be more professional and detailed. The automation can be configured to adapt the same core content for each platform's unique requirements.

For Twitter, the system might generate threaded posts that break down complex ideas. For LinkedIn, it could create longer-form posts with professional insights. Instagram captions would focus on visual storytelling hooks. The automation handles all these variations while maintaining consistent messaging across platforms.

Pro tip: Add platform-specific CTAs to your generated posts. Twitter posts might drive replies, LinkedIn posts could encourage comments, and Instagram captions should prompt saves or shares.

Maintaining Content Quality

While automation handles the heavy lifting, human oversight ensures quality. The Airtable review stage allows your team to approve, edit, or reject generated posts before they go live. This maintains your brand voice while benefiting from AI efficiency.

Establish clear guidelines for what constitutes an approvable post. These might include tone requirements, messaging accuracy, and engagement potential. Over time, you can refine your ChatGPT prompts based on which generated posts perform best and require the least editing.

As your content library grows, the automation can also resurface and repurpose older posts, giving them new life on social media. This creates a sustainable content engine that continuously delivers value to your audience while requiring minimal ongoing maintenance.
