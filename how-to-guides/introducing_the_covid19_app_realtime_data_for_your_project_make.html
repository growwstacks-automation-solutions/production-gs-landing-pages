About the COVID-19 app

The COVID-19 app was specifically developed to provide essential support to individuals and teams grappling with the global pandemic. Its core function is to pull real-time data from the Johns Hopkins Whiting School of Engineering repositories, a globally recognized and authoritative source for pandemic statistics.

This robust data pipeline ensures the delivery of accurate, structured information regarding pandemic numbers across the globe. In an era plagued by information overload and the rapid spread of misinformation, having such reliable data readily available is absolutely critical for effective response and planning.

Use case #1: Dispatch volunteers to critical areas

The problem

As the COVID-19 pandemic continues its global spread, healthcare organizations and non-profits face an urgent need to identify which geographic areas are most affected and to what extent. This information is vital for effectively deploying volunteers and medical personnel to provide necessary assistance and support.

Without precise, up-to-date data, efforts to mobilize aid can be inefficient, leading to delays in critical response and potentially leaving vulnerable communities underserved. The challenge lies in quickly understanding the evolving landscape of the pandemic to make timely and impactful decisions.

The solution

Leveraging the COVID-19 app provides a streamlined approach to this challenge. The initial step involves accurately understanding which areas are experiencing the highest impact, which is fundamental for effective planning and the strategic assignment of resources. By integrating the COVID-19 app with other applications, organizations can begin processing real-time data to pinpoint areas requiring volunteer support and subsequently organize aid efforts accordingly.

This particular use case is designed for simplicity and efficiency: it continuously monitors COVID-19 data and presents it on your preferred project management tool. With all relevant data consolidated on a single screen, organizations can rapidly assess the situation and make informed decisions about where to deploy volunteers and allocate resources most effectively. While Monday.com is an excellent platform for this function, the flexibility of the Make.com ecosystem also allows for seamless integration with other popular tools like Airtable and Google Sheets, providing versatility in how you manage your response.

A visual representation of the COVID-19 stats tracker integrated with a project management tool.

A ready-to-use template simplifies the setup process, allowing you to quickly implement this solution. The "Save stats about COVID-19 in your locations to Monday.com" template automates the transfer of critical data, making it easier to maintain an up-to-date overview of the situation and respond dynamically to changing needs.

Key takeaways

  • The system can be rapidly assembled and deployed using readily available project management apps.
  • Multiple data markers can be picked up to refine the resource assignment process: location, number of available volunteers, assignments per volunteer, and so on.

Use case #2: Deliver essential items to quarantined individuals

The problem

The pandemic has necessitated strict measures such as quarantine and self-isolation for individuals exhibiting symptoms of Coronavirus, requiring them to avoid public spaces like grocery stores and pharmacies. This situation presents a significant challenge, particularly for those living alone or lacking the necessary support networks to acquire essential items.

Many individuals find themselves in a precarious position, unable to access basic necessities, which can exacerbate health risks and lead to increased stress and isolation. Addressing this logistical hurdle requires a compassionate and efficient solution to ensure everyone has access to the supplies they need.

The solution

Businesses and individuals can play a crucial role in supporting isolated community members by implementing a straightforward solution centered around a Google Form. This form, distributed via email or text message to quarantined individuals, allows them to specify their needs, including a list of required supplies, preferred time slots for delivery, and their chosen payment method, along with any other pertinent information.

Once submitted, the system automatically notifies delivery personnel via email, streamlining the coordination process. Concurrently, all collected information is securely sent to and stored within a project management tool, providing a centralized hub for tracking requests and deliveries. To ensure ongoing support, a recurring email can also be set up to check in with served individuals, verifying their continued well-being and identifying any further needs.

An example of a Google Form used to collect essential item requests for quarantined individuals.

Templates like "Use Airtable to handle emergency situations in your country" and "Volunteer Coordination Template" are available to facilitate the rapid deployment of such systems. These pre-built solutions provide a foundation for managing emergency responses, ensuring that communities can quickly adapt to new challenges and provide vital support to those most in need.

  • Time slots for receiving the supplies
  • Payment method

Key takeaways

  • Reduce the exposure risk of entire communities by taking efficient care of neighbors with symptoms.
  • Strengthen the sense of community by ensuring that everyone gets taken care of.
  • Get ahead of potential supply problems, and react accordingly to surging demand.

Use case #3: Unify your messages across online platforms

The problem

The widespread adoption of quarantine and social distancing measures has transformed messaging services and social media platforms into primary communication channels for businesses, organizations, and individuals alike. However, many companies struggle to effectively manage their online presence during these critical times.

A common issue is inconsistent cross-posting, where messages are not synchronized across different platforms or contain varying information. This lack of uniformity can lead to significant confusion among stakeholders and the public, undermining trust and hindering the effective dissemination of vital updates.

The solution

Maintaining immediate and consistent communication with important stakeholders is crucial to keep everyone informed. To achieve this, companies must demonstrate transparency and consistency in their messaging across all social media platforms. The Make COVID-19 app offers an elegant solution by enabling seamless integration with various social media channels.

Organizations can leverage this connectivity to establish scheduled updates that are automatically launched across multiple platforms at predetermined times. These automated posts will contain identical information, ensuring cohesiveness and eliminating the potential for confusion that arises from disparate messages. This structured approach guarantees that everyone receives the same accurate information, fostering clarity and trust during uncertain times.

A template illustrating how to automatically post COVID-19 country stats to social media accounts.

The "Post current COVID-19 country stats to Twitter, Facebook Pages, and LinkedIn" template is an excellent example of how this can be implemented. This template allows for the automated sharing of critical data, ensuring that your audience receives timely and consistent updates across their preferred social media channels.

Key takeaways

  • Updates need to be consistent across the board since not everyone uses the same platforms.
  • In times of uncertainty, having structures in place provides security. Pre-programmed updates offer exactly that: structured information that can be released at the same hour every day.

Use case #4: Get notified about COVID-19 questions

The problem

E-commerce businesses, among others, have experienced a significant surge in customer inquiries related to COVID-19. These questions, arriving through various channels such as calls and emails, demand prompt and accurate responses. However, manually handling such a high volume of inquiries can be incredibly time-consuming and resource-intensive, straining customer service teams and potentially leading to delays and dissatisfaction.

The challenge lies in efficiently processing and responding to these inquiries without overwhelming staff or compromising the quality of service. An automated solution is essential to manage this influx effectively.

The solution

For companies utilizing Freshdesk, an automated channel can be swiftly deployed to manage COVID-related inquiries. When a new ticket is received, it is automatically routed to a designated Slack channel, such as "#covidmail." This centralized approach allows company employees to efficiently evaluate incoming inquiries and determine the appropriate team or individual responsible for providing a response.

This automation significantly streamlines the inquiry management process, reducing manual effort and ensuring that questions are addressed by the right experts. The integration of Freshdesk with Slack creates a collaborative environment where teams can quickly triage and resolve customer concerns, improving response times and overall customer satisfaction.

The "Using FreshDesk Tickets: Save current COVID-19 stats for the location of the requester of a ticket in Freshdesk" template provides a practical example of how to implement this solution. This template helps automate the process of attaching relevant COVID-19 data to customer tickets, providing context for support agents and enabling more informed responses.

  • Using FreshDesk Tickets: Save current COVID-19 stats for the location of the requester of a ticket in Freshdesk

Key takeaways

  • A dedicated channel for COVID-related inquiries improves your workflows and saves your company time and money.
  • The implementation of a shared Slack channel is a great way to keep everyone in the loop about COVID-19 inquiries.
  • The system prevents inquiries from getting siloed in different email accounts.
