Triggering Scenarios with Siri Voice Commands

The Make mobile app brings powerful automation capabilities to your fingertips, and now you can extend that convenience to voice control through Siri integration. This feature allows you to execute Make scenarios simply by speaking a custom phrase, perfect for situations when your hands are occupied or you need quick access to automations.

To set up Siri voice commands, begin by opening the Make mobile app and navigating to the button you want to voice-enable. This can be either an existing button or a new one you create specifically for voice activation. The process works equally well for both simple and complex scenarios.

Pro tip: Choose voice phrases that are natural to say but distinct enough that Siri won't confuse them with other commands. Include the word "Make" or your scenario name for better recognition.

When configuring your Siri command, you'll define both the activation phrase ("When I say") and the specific button action ("Do"). The system provides intuitive prompts to guide you through pairing your voice command with the correct Make button. After setup, test your command to ensure Siri recognizes it clearly and triggers the intended automation.

The Add to Siri button in the Make mobile app interface

Voice Command Best Practices

For optimal performance, keep your voice commands concise yet distinctive. Two to four word phrases tend to work best with Siri's recognition algorithms. Avoid generic phrases like "Run scenario" that might conflict with system commands. Instead, incorporate specific identifiers like "Make start inventory check" or "Trigger client follow-up."

Consider your usage environment when choosing phrases. If you'll be using commands in noisy areas, select words with distinct phonetic patterns. Test your commands in different conditions to ensure reliable recognition. Remember you can always edit or recreate commands if the initial phrase isn't working consistently.

Setting Up Make on Apple Watch

Extending Make's automation capabilities to your Apple Watch provides ultimate convenience for triggering scenarios on the go. The Watch app mirrors your mobile buttons, giving you instant access to automations right from your wrist. This is particularly useful for quick actions, reminders, or situations where pulling out your phone isn't practical.

To install Make on your Apple Watch, open the Watch app on your paired iPhone and navigate to the "Available Apps" section. Locate Make in the list and tap "Install." The process typically takes just a minute, after which the Make icon will appear among your Watch apps. No additional configuration is needed - your buttons automatically sync from the mobile app.

Installing the Make app through the Apple Watch interface

Optimizing for Apple Watch

While all Make buttons work on Apple Watch, some optimizations can improve your experience. Consider creating dedicated buttons for Watch use with simplified names that display clearly on the smaller screen. Group related automations into separate button sets that you can access by force-pressing the Watch face.

The Watch interface supports both tap triggers and (when combined with Siri integration) voice commands. For frequently used automations, add Make complications to your Watch face for one-tap access. Battery life impact is minimal since the Watch delegates actual scenario execution to your paired iPhone.

Pro tip: Combine Siri and Apple Watch by using "Hey Siri" voice commands on your Watch to trigger Make automations without even touching the device - perfect when your hands are full.
