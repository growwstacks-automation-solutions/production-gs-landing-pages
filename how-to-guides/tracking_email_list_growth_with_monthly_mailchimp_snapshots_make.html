The Problem with Mailchimp Reporting

Email marketers need to track subscriber growth over time to measure campaign effectiveness and list health. Unfortunately, Mailchimp provides no way to view historical statistics for your email list on specific past dates. The platform only shows current metrics without any ability to compare month-over-month changes.

This limitation makes it impossible to accurately calculate churn rates or identify growth patterns. Without historical data, marketers can't determine whether recent campaigns actually increased subscribers or if seasonal trends affected list size. The lack of reporting creates blind spots in email marketing analytics.

Pro tip: Many marketers manually record their Mailchimp stats in spreadsheets each month, but this process is time-consuming and prone to human error.

Automated Snapshot Solution

Our solution creates monthly snapshots of your Mailchimp list statistics automatically using Make.com. The automation connects to Mailchimp's API to pull key metrics, then stores this data with a timestamp in Google Sheets. Each month's data becomes a new row in your historical record.

The system captures total subscribers, new subscribers, unsubscribes, and calculates the monthly churn rate. You'll get a complete picture of how your list grows or shrinks over time, with all data accessible in an organized spreadsheet for analysis.

How the Automation Works

The Make.com scenario runs on a monthly schedule, triggering at the same time each month to maintain consistency. It first authenticates with your Mailchimp account using API credentials, then requests the current list statistics from Mailchimp's API.

The spreadsheet automatically calculates the churn rate by comparing the current subscriber count with the previous month's total. All data remains accessible for reporting and analysis.

Calculating List Churn Rate

Churn rate measures how many subscribers join or leave your list in a given period. The formula compares the current month's subscriber count with the previous month's total: (Current - Previous) / Previous × 100. Positive percentages indicate growth while negative numbers show shrinkage.

For example, if your list had 1,000 subscribers last month and 1,200 this month, your churn would be +20% (200/1000×100). If it dropped from 1,100 to 1,000, the churn would be -9.1% (-100/1100×100). These insights help evaluate marketing effectiveness beyond just open/click rates.

Interpreting Churn Rates

Healthy email lists typically show positive churn from month to month, though acceptable rates vary by industry. Negative churn signals problems with content quality, frequency, or list acquisition sources. Comparing churn to industry benchmarks helps assess performance.

Seasonal businesses may see predictable churn patterns. The historical data helps distinguish normal fluctuations from concerning trends. Over time, you can identify what content or campaigns produce the best subscriber retention.
