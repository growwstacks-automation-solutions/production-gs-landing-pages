WebMerge, explained

Contracts, agreements, signup forms, invoices, event tickets - document creation is a necessity in most industries. Yet the manual process of creating these documents can consume valuable time that would be better spent on core business activities.

WebMerge solves this problem as an online platform that allows you to easily collect data, populate documents, and deliver them wherever they need to go. The process breaks down into three simple steps that integrate with your existing tools:

  • Create a template using fillable PDFs, Microsoft Office documents, or WebMerge's built-in editor
  • Choose delivery methods including email, cloud storage, or third-party integrations
  • Merge data from various sources to automatically populate your documents
The WebMerge interface showing document creation options

What makes WebMerge particularly powerful is its integration capabilities. While it offers direct connections to many services, pairing it with Make (formerly Integromat) unlocks even greater potential by connecting WebMerge to virtually any online service.

Pro tip: When setting up your first WebMerge template, include clear merge field names that match your data source fields. This makes mapping data in Make scenarios much simpler later.

WebMerge + Make

The real magic happens when you combine WebMerge with Make's automation capabilities. Make acts as the central hub that connects WebMerge to your other business applications, creating seamless document workflows.

For example, you can automatically generate contracts whenever a new deal is created in your CRM. The process begins in WebMerge where you design your contract template with appropriate merge fields. Then in Make, you create a scenario that:

  1. Watches for new CRM deals
  2. Collects all relevant contact and deal information
  3. Passes this data to WebMerge to populate your template
  4. Delivers the completed document via your preferred method
Configuring a Make scenario to watch for CRM record changes

Advanced implementations can include multiple steps like sending documents for e-signature, storing copies in your document management system, and updating CRM records with status information - all automatically.

For businesses handling high document volumes, this automation can save dozens of hours each week while reducing errors that come with manual data entry. The time saved translates directly into more capacity for client interactions and strategic work.
