WebMerge, explained

WebMerge is an online platform that allows you to easily collect data, populate documents, and send them where they need to go. The creation of contracts, agreements, and other paperwork can be a major time drain, taking focus away from core business activities.

With WebMerge, document generation happens in three simple steps. First, you create a template by uploading a fillable PDF, building one in WebMerge, or using a Microsoft Office document. Next, you choose how to deliver the completed document - through email, third-party integrations, or cloud storage. Finally, you merge data into the template either manually or through automated connections.

The three-step WebMerge document creation process

The platform supports numerous third-party integrations that connect to the sources where your data originates. While WebMerge offers many direct integrations, Make (formerly Integromat) provides a broader solution by linking WebMerge to any online app or service.

Pro tip: For complex document workflows, use Make to connect WebMerge with multiple data sources in a single automation scenario.

WebMerge + Make

When combined with Make, WebMerge becomes part of powerful automation scenarios. For example, you can automatically generate contracts whenever a new deal appears in HubSpot CRM. The integration pulls relevant contact and deal information, populates the document template, and delivers the completed contract.

The setup begins in WebMerge where you create your document template with merge fields. After configuring the template settings and delivery method, you build the automation scenario in Make. The scenario starts with a trigger module (like watching for new CRM records), followed by the WebMerge module that merges data into your template.

Configuring the HubSpot CRM trigger in Make

Advanced scenarios might include additional steps like saving the document to cloud storage or attaching it back to the CRM record. Make's visual interface makes it easy to design these multi-step workflows without coding.

Pro tip: Add a final step to your scenario that uploads completed documents back to your CRM for centralized record keeping.

By automating document creation with WebMerge and Make, businesses typically save 5-10 hours per week on paperwork. This time can be reinvested into customer relationships and strategic initiatives that drive growth.
