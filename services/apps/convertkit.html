Our Approach

3 Ways We Automate ConvertKit

We choose the right integration method based on your existing tech stack, the complexity of your email marketing workflows, and the business conditions that should trigger subscriber actions.

01

ConvertKit Native Automations

ConvertKit's built-in automation builder and native integrations handle common email marketing workflows — form submission triggers, tag-based sequence enrolment, subscriber activity-based branching, and broadcast scheduling. We configure these native automations to manage your email sequences, subscriber journeys, and segmentation rules directly inside ConvertKit for straightforward, platform-native use cases.

Form Triggers Sequence Enrolment Tag-based Branching Subscriber Journeys Native Integrations
02

Make.com & Zapier Integration

Make.com's native ConvertKit module supports all key triggers — new subscriber activity, form submission, list updates, tag changes, and sequence events — plus actions for adding subscribers, managing tags, handling broadcasts, and making direct API calls. We use Make.com and Zapier to connect ConvertKit to Stripe, WooCommerce, WordPress, Zoho CRM, and any other platform in your stack with full business logic applied at every step.

Subscriber Activity Form Submission Tag Management Add Subscriber Broadcast
03

ConvertKit REST API

ConvertKit's well-structured API covers subscribers, forms, sequences, tags, broadcasts, and custom fields. We use it directly — via Python, JavaScript, or Make.com's HTTP module — for custom workflows beyond the native integrations: subscribing contacts programmatically from any data source, bulk tag operations, sequence management, and building complex subscriber enrichment or segmentation pipelines.

Use Cases

What We Build With ConvertKit

Real email marketing automation pipelines we have delivered for e-commerce brands, SaaS businesses, content creators, and digital product sellers using ConvertKit.

🛒

WooCommerce Abandoned Cart → ConvertKit Sequence

When a WooCommerce cart is abandoned, our Make.com scenario identifies the prospect and enrolls them into a targeted ConvertKit nurture sequence automatically. The sequence delivers a series of personalised recovery emails with cart contents, social proof, and a time-limited offer — recovering revenue that would otherwise be lost without any manual intervention from your marketing team.

💳

Stripe Payment → ConvertKit + Zoho CRM

When a Stripe payment is completed, our Make.com scenario retrieves the payment link and customer details, creates or updates the contact in Zoho CRM with the purchase data, and simultaneously adds the customer to ConvertKit with the appropriate tags for their product purchase. This enables cross-sell and upsell email sequences to trigger automatically based on what each customer has bought.

🌐

Stripe Payment → WordPress User + ConvertKit Subscriber

For digital product sellers, we build a complete purchase-to-access pipeline — when a Stripe payment is received, the customer is automatically created as a WordPress user with the correct role and access level, and simultaneously added to ConvertKit as a subscriber tagged with their product. Onboarding sequences start immediately and access credentials are delivered via automated email.

🏷️

Subscriber Tag Management & Segmentation

We build automated tag management workflows that apply, remove, or update ConvertKit tags based on external triggers — a purchase completed, a form submitted, a link clicked, or a CRM status changed. Precise tag-based segmentation enables highly targeted broadcast campaigns and sequence enrolments, ensuring each subscriber receives content relevant to exactly where they are in their journey.

📥

New Lead / Form Submission → ConvertKit + CRM

When a new lead arrives — from a website form, landing page, webinar registration, or lead generation platform — we simultaneously add them to ConvertKit with the correct tags and sequence, and create or update their record in your CRM. This dual-entry ensures every new lead is immediately enrolled in email nurturing while your sales team has a full CRM record to manage the relationship.

📊

ConvertKit Subscriber Activity → External Reporting

We build Make.com scenarios that watch ConvertKit subscriber activity events — opens, clicks, unsubscribes, sequence completions — and log them to Google Sheets or Airtable for external analysis and reporting. This gives marketing teams detailed engagement data in a flexible reporting environment without relying solely on ConvertKit's built-in analytics dashboard.
