Our Approach

3 Ways We Automate Salesforce

We choose the right integration method based on your data volume, the external platforms you need to connect, and whether no-code convenience or enterprise-grade API control is the priority.

01

Make.com & Zapier Integration

Make.com's Salesforce module supports watching records across any Salesforce object — contacts, accounts, leads, opportunities, orders, and custom objects — plus actions for creating, searching, updating, deleting, and bulk-processing records. We use Make.com and Zapier to connect Salesforce to WooCommerce, Google Sheets, external CRMs, email platforms, and any other tool in your stack — with full conditional routing and business rule logic applied at every step.

Watch Records Field Update Triggers Search & Get Bulk Jobs Direct API Calls
02

Salesforce REST API

Salesforce's REST API provides complete programmatic access to every object and record in your org — contacts, accounts, leads, opportunities, orders, custom objects, and more. We use the API inside Make.com for advanced operations, or write Python and JavaScript code for enterprise-grade workflows requiring large-scale data processing, complex business logic, or integration with proprietary internal systems that no-code tools cannot reach.

REST API SOQL Queries Custom Objects Bulk Processing Python / JS
03

Salesforce Native Workflows & Flow

Salesforce's built-in workflow rules, process builder, and Flow automation handle internal CRM processes — field updates on record creation, email alerts on opportunity stage changes, task creation on lead conversion, and approval processes. We configure these native automations to manage routine Salesforce-internal operations, reducing external trigger requirements and keeping straightforward business logic inside the Salesforce platform itself.

Use Cases

What We Build With Salesforce

Real CRM automation workflows we have delivered for enterprise organisations, operations teams, and businesses using Salesforce as their central customer data platform.

🔄

Salesforce Record → Broadly CRM Sync

When a record is created or a vertical field is updated in Salesforce, our Make.com scenario triggers and pushes the relevant data to Broadly CRM — keeping both CRM systems in sync without anyone manually re-entering the same customer data in two places. This is particularly useful for organisations that use Salesforce as the enterprise CRM alongside Broadly for local customer relationship management.

✅

Checkbox Trigger → Account Lookup → Google Sheets → WooCommerce

When a checkbox field is activated on a Salesforce contact, our Make.com scenario searches Salesforce to find the contact, looks up their linked account details, pushes the compiled data into Google Sheets for records, and creates a customer in WooCommerce using the Salesforce contact and account information. The customer record in Salesforce is then updated to reflect the WooCommerce customer ID — creating a bidirectional link.

🛒

WooCommerce Order → Salesforce Contact & Opportunity

We build Zapier workflows where a new WooCommerce order triggers a comprehensive Salesforce update — searching for an existing contact by email, creating a new contact and account if none exists, pushing the order data into Salesforce as a new opportunity with the correct amount and product details, and applying conditional routing to handle different customer types with different Salesforce record structures.

📊

Salesforce Field Updates → Google Sheets Reporting

We build Make.com scenarios that watch for Salesforce record field updates — opportunity stage changes, contact status updates, or account field modifications — and push the updated data to Google Sheets for external reporting and analysis. Finance, operations, and management teams get a live Google Sheet view of Salesforce data without needing Salesforce licenses or logging into the CRM to run reports.

⚡

Multi-Condition Salesforce Routing Workflows

We build complex Zapier and Make.com workflows that receive Salesforce triggers and apply multi-path routing logic — different contact types route to different downstream platforms, different opportunity stages trigger different email sequences, and different account classifications determine different CRM update procedures. These conditional workflows handle the business rule complexity that native Salesforce automation alone cannot manage efficiently.

🔗

Salesforce Bulk Record Processing

We use Make.com's iterator modules combined with Salesforce's bulk API capabilities to process large volumes of Salesforce records in batch operations — updating thousands of contact fields, migrating opportunity data between pipeline stages, syncing account information to external databases, or exporting filtered record sets to Google Sheets or Airtable for analysis — all without manual data manipulation or expensive Salesforce licences for every team member who needs the data.
