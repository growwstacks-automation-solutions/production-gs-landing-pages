What This Workflow Does

This automation bridges two essential productivity tools: Notion for planning and Google Calendar for scheduling. It eliminates the manual work of recreating calendar events by automatically converting new Notion database entries into Google Calendar events with all relevant details.

Teams waste countless hours copying information between apps, risking errors and missed appointments. This workflow ensures your calendar always reflects your latest Notion planning, keeping everyone aligned without extra effort. It's particularly valuable for remote teams managing complex schedules across time zones.

How It Works

1. Notion Database Trigger

The workflow monitors your specified Notion database for new entries containing date/time fields. When a qualifying record appears, it extracts all relevant event details.

2. Data Transformation

The system maps Notion properties to Google Calendar fields, converting your structured notes into proper event formats. This includes handling time zones, durations, and rich text formatting.

3. Calendar Event Creation

Make.com authenticates with your Google account and creates the calendar event with all extracted details. You can configure whether these appear as busy blocks or transparent appointments.

Pro tip: Add a "Calendar Sync" toggle property in Notion to control which entries should sync automatically versus remain private.
