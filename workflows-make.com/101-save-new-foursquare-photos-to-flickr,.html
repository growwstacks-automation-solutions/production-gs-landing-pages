What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of manually transferring photos between Foursquare and Flickr. Every time you or selected users add new photos to Foursquare check-ins, the workflow automatically saves those photos to specified Flickr albums. This eliminates the tedious process of downloading and re-uploading photos while ensuring your location-based photos are properly archived.

The workflow maintains organization by allowing you to specify destination albums in Flickr based on location, date, or other criteria. It preserves all photo metadata during transfer, including geotags and timestamps, creating a seamless bridge between your location check-ins and photo storage platforms.

How It Works

1. Monitoring Foursquare for new photos

The workflow continuously checks your specified Foursquare account or selected users for newly added photos. You can configure it to monitor specific locations or all check-ins.

2. Capturing photo details

When a new photo is detected, the automation extracts all relevant information including the image file, caption, location data, and timestamp from Foursquare.

3. Preparing for Flickr upload

The system organizes the photo data according to your preset rules, determining which Flickr album to use based on location, date, or other criteria you've established.

4. Uploading to Flickr

The workflow then connects to your Flickr account and uploads the photo to the designated album, preserving all metadata and maintaining original image quality.

Pro tip: Set up separate Flickr albums for different cities or trip dates to automatically organize your location photos as they're transferred.
