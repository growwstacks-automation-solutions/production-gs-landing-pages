What This Workflow Does

This automation transforms how your business handles incoming emails by combining the power of ChatGPT with Google Sheets. The system automatically analyzes incoming messages, generates thoughtful draft responses using AI, and organizes them in a spreadsheet for review or direct sending.

For businesses receiving dozens or hundreds of similar inquiries daily, this workflow eliminates the repetitive task of crafting individual responses while maintaining personalization. Customer service teams, sales departments, and executive assistants can all benefit from having AI-powered draft replies ready in seconds rather than spending hours composing emails.

How It Works

1. Email Trigger

The workflow starts when a new email arrives in your designated inbox. Make.com monitors your Gmail account for new messages matching your criteria (specific senders, subjects, or keywords).

2. Content Analysis

The email content is extracted and prepared for ChatGPT. Important details like the sender's name, email subject, and key message points are identified to provide context for the AI.

3. AI Response Generation

ChatGPT processes the email content using your predefined instructions and templates. The AI considers your brand voice, common response patterns, and the specific inquiry to generate a professional draft reply.

4. Spreadsheet Organization

The generated response along with original email details are logged in Google Sheets. This creates a searchable record of all automated interactions and allows for easy review before sending.

5. Optional Approval Flow

Depending on your configuration, responses can be automatically sent or flagged for human review. The spreadsheet interface makes it easy to approve, edit, or reject AI-generated drafts.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Connect your Gmail account to Make.com with appropriate permissions
  2. Set up your OpenAI API connection in Make.com
  3. Create a Google Sheet with columns for: Original Email, Generated Response, Status
  4. Configure your ChatGPT prompts with sample responses and tone guidelines
  5. Test with sample emails and refine your prompts based on results
  6. Set up filters to determine which emails should trigger automation
  7. Decide whether to implement manual review or direct sending

Pro tip: Start by automating responses to your 3-5 most common email types before expanding. This lets you refine the system with focused feedback.
