What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical challenge for nonprofit organizations - efficiently capturing donor communications in their CRM system. Many charities rely on Gmail for daily correspondence but need these interactions properly logged in Access Charity CRM for donor management, reporting, and compliance.

The workflow automatically checks for new Gmail messages at regular intervals (default every 15 minutes), extracts all relevant information including attachments, and creates corresponding records in Access Charity CRM. This eliminates manual data entry while ensuring no important donor communication falls through the cracks.

How It Works

1. Scheduled email checking

The workflow begins by checking your Gmail account for new messages based on your specified schedule. You can adjust the frequency from real-time to daily checks depending on your volume.

2. Email content extraction

For each new email, the system retrieves the sender details, subject line, body content, timestamps, and any attachments. This data is structured for CRM import.

3. CRM record creation

The workflow then creates a new record in Access Charity CRM, mapping all email fields to the appropriate CRM fields. Attachments are uploaded and linked to the contact record.

Pro tip: Add a filtering step to only process emails from known donor domains or with specific subject keywords to avoid cluttering your CRM with irrelevant messages.
