What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common challenge of keeping online communities engaged with fresh content. Many creators and businesses store their content in Airtable but struggle to consistently share updates across platforms. Manual posting is time-consuming and easy to forget, leading to irregular engagement.

The workflow automatically detects new content added to your Airtable base each week, formats it for Discord, and posts it to your designated channel. It preserves all your content structure and metadata while adding Discord-specific formatting like mentions or channel references. Your community gets reliable weekly updates without you lifting a finger.

How It Works

1. Weekly Airtable content check

The workflow runs on your schedule (typically weekly) and queries your Airtable base for records created or modified since the last run. It filters based on your criteria like publish status or content category.

2. Content formatting

Each Airtable record is transformed into a Discord-friendly format. The workflow maps fields like title, description, and author to create an attractive post. You can customize the template to match your brand voice.

3. Discord channel posting

The formatted content gets posted to your specified Discord channel. The workflow handles rate limits and retries if needed. You can configure it to @mention specific roles or include reaction emojis to encourage engagement.

4. Success logging

Each successful post is logged back to Airtable with a timestamp and status. This creates an audit trail and prevents duplicate postings if the workflow runs multiple times.

Pro tip: Add a "Last Shared" date field in Airtable to track exactly when each piece was posted to Discord. This helps with content rotation and prevents accidental reposts.
