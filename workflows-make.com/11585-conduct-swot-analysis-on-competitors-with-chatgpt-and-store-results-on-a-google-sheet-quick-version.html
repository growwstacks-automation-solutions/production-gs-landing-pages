Automate Competitor SWOT Analysis with ChatGPT & Google Sheets

What This Workflow Does

Manual competitor analysis is a time-consuming, inconsistent, and often outdated process. Marketing and strategy teams spend hours scouring websites, social media, and reports to build a basic SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) framework. By the time the analysis is complete, the market may have already shifted, rendering the insights less actionable.

This automated workflow solves that problem. It connects a Google Sheet—your simple list of competitor websites—directly to ChatGPT. Whenever you add a new competitor URL, the automation triggers. ChatGPT visits the website, analyzes the content, and generates a structured SWOT analysis based on publicly available information. The insights are then instantly written back into your Google Sheet, creating a live, searchable database of competitive intelligence.

The business value is immense: you move from sporadic, labor-intensive reports to a continuous, automated intelligence stream. This enables faster strategic pivots, identifies market gaps you can exploit, and ensures your entire team operates from a single source of up-to-date competitor truth.

How It Works

The automation follows a clear, sequential process to turn a website URL into strategic insights.

Step 1: Trigger from Google Sheets

The workflow watches a specific Google Sheet for new rows. You simply paste a competitor's website URL into the designated "Company website" column. This addition acts as the trigger, signaling the automation to begin analysis for that specific entry.

Step 2: Data Retrieval & Processing

The system fetches the content from the provided website URL. It extracts key textual information about the company's offerings, value proposition, and public messaging, preparing a clean data packet for AI analysis.

Pro tip: For more nuanced analysis, you can modify the prompt to include specific industry keywords or focus areas like "pricing strategy," "user experience," or "content marketing."

Step 3: AI-Powered SWOT Generation

The core content is sent to ChatGPT via the OpenAI module with a carefully crafted prompt. The AI acts as a strategic analyst, evaluating the information to identify distinct Strengths (what they do well), Weaknesses (potential gaps), Opportunities (market trends they could leverage), and Threats (external risks or your advantages).

Step 4: Insight Storage & Completion

The finalized SWOT analysis—formatted into four clear text blocks—is automatically inserted back into the same row of your Google Sheet, populating the "Strengths," "Weaknesses," "Opportunities," and "Threats" columns. Your competitor intelligence database is now updated without any manual copy-pasting.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this intelligence system running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the ready-made scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com editor, authorize the connections to your specific Google Sheet and your OpenAI account using your API key.
  3. Configure the Sheet: Ensure your Google Sheet matches the required column structure (A-E as listed above). Point the "Google Sheets" module in the workflow to this exact sheet and worksheet.
  4. Optional: Tune the Prompt: Review the ChatGPT prompt in the "OpenAI" module. You can adjust it to focus on aspects most relevant to your industry (e.g., "highlight supply chain strengths" for manufacturing).
  5. Test & Activate: Add a test competitor website URL to your sheet. Turn on the scenario schedule or run it once manually. Check that the SWOT analysis populates correctly in the row. Once confirmed, your automation is live.
