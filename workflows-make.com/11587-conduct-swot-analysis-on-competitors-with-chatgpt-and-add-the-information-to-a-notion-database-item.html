What This Workflow Does

Staying ahead of your competition requires constant vigilance, but manually researching and analyzing each competitor is a massive time sink. This automation solves that by turning a tedious, periodic task into a seamless, ongoing process. It uses the power of ChatGPT (or another AI model) to perform a structured SWOT analysis on any competitor you specify.

The workflow takes a competitor's name or website as input, prompts AI to research and evaluate their Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats, and then automatically creates or updates a dedicated item in your Notion database. This centralizes all competitive intelligence in a single, searchable, and collaborative hub that your entire team can access.

The business value is immense: you move from reactive, ad-hoc research to a proactive intelligence system. Instead of spending 4-5 hours per competitor every quarter, you get an updated analysis in minutes, allowing you to spot market shifts faster and make strategic decisions with confidence.

How It Works

The automation follows a clear, logical sequence to transform a simple trigger into rich, actionable insights.

Step 1: Trigger the Analysis

The workflow can be started manually by you entering a competitor's name, or it can be scheduled to run automatically (e.g., monthly). It can also be triggered by other events, like a news alert about a competitor receiving funding.

Step 2: AI Conducts the SWOT Research

The competitor name is sent to ChatGPT via the OpenAI module. A carefully crafted prompt instructs the AI to act as a strategic analyst, gathering publicly available information and structuring its findings into the four SWOT categories. The AI provides reasoned, concise points for each quadrant.

Pro tip: Tailor your AI prompt to focus on aspects most relevant to your industry, such as "evaluate their technology stack" for SaaS or "analyze their supply chain transparency" for retail.

Step 3: Format and Update Notion

The structured SWOT output from ChatGPT is then formatted and sent to Notion. The workflow finds or creates a database entry for that specific competitor and updates the relevant properties—like "Last Analyzed Date," "Strengths," "Weaknesses," etc.—with the fresh analysis. This keeps your competitive database always current.
