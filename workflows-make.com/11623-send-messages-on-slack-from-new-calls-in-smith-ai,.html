What This Workflow Does

Every missed call is a missed opportunity—a potential sale, a client in need, or a critical piece of feedback. When you use a service like Smith.ai to professionally handle your calls, the information about those calls can get siloed. Team members might not check the Smith.ai dashboard regularly, leading to delayed responses and frustrated callers.

This automation solves that problem by creating a real-time bridge between Smith.ai and your team's communication hub: Slack. It acts as a vigilant assistant, checking Smith.ai for new calls at a regular interval (every 15 minutes by default) and instantly posting a detailed alert to a designated Slack channel. This transforms a passive log entry into an active team notification, ensuring everyone who needs to know is informed immediately.

How It Works

The workflow is a simple yet powerful sequence that runs autonomously on Make.com.

Step 1: Scheduled Check

A scheduler module triggers the workflow to run automatically at your chosen interval. The template starts with a 15-minute schedule, but you can easily adjust this to be more or less frequent based on your call volume and urgency needs.

Step 2: Fetch New Calls

The workflow connects to your Smith.ai account via its API and retrieves a list of recent calls. It is smart enough to only look for calls that have happened since its last check, preventing duplicate notifications and ensuring every new call is captured.

Step 3: Format the Alert

For each new call found, the automation compiles the key details—such as caller name, phone number, time of call, duration, and any notes from the receptionist—into a clear, readable format.

Step 4: Post to Slack

Finally, the workflow sends the formatted message to your pre-configured Slack channel. The message appears as a clean, actionable alert, allowing your sales, support, or management team to see the call details at a glance and decide on the next steps instantly.

Pro tip: Customize the Slack message to include an emoji (like 📞 or 🔔) and format the caller's number as a clickable link. This small touch improves visibility and makes it one-click easy for a team member to call back.

What You'll Need

To set up this workflow, you will need the following accounts and permissions:

  1. A Make.com account (free tier is sufficient to start).
  2. An active Smith.ai account with administrative access to generate an API key.
  3. A Slack workspace where you have permission to create and post to channels.
  4. (Recommended) A dedicated Slack channel for call alerts, such as #inbound-calls or #sales-leads.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to clone the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Smith.ai: In the first Smith.ai module, click to create a connection. You'll be prompted to log into your Smith.ai account and provide an API key, which you can generate from your Smith.ai dashboard.
  3. Connect Slack: In the Slack module, create a connection by authorizing Make.com to access your Slack workspace. Select the specific channel where you want the alerts to be posted.
  4. Test the Flow: Run the scenario once manually. Place a test call to your Smith.ai number and see the alert appear in your Slack channel.
  5. Activate the Schedule: Turn on the scheduler module. The workflow will now run automatically, checking for new calls and posting alerts without any further action from you.
