What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses, email marketing is a powerful sales channel, but its true financial impact is often a mystery. You know a campaign got clicks, but did it actually drive purchases? How much revenue did that holiday promo email generate? What's the real return on investment (ROI) after accounting for design costs and platform fees? Manually answering these questions involves exporting data from Mailchimp, cross-referencing with orders from your store, and performing complex calculations—a process that's tedious, error-prone, and often skipped.

This automation workflow solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your Mailchimp campaigns and your e-commerce sales data. It automatically pulls campaign performance metrics (sends, opens, clicks) and matches them with customer purchase records, attributing revenue to specific emails. The result is a live Google Sheets dashboard that shows not just engagement stats, but actual dollars earned, cost per acquisition, and ROI percentage for every campaign you run.

By automating this connection, you move from guessing to knowing. You can identify which email themes, product highlights, or discount strategies genuinely boost sales, allowing you to optimize your marketing budget and focus on what truly works.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on Campaign Completion

The workflow is triggered whenever a Mailchimp email campaign ends or when scheduled reporting is needed. Make.com watches your Mailchimp account for these events, ensuring data is captured fresh without manual intervention.

Step 2: Fetch Campaign Performance Data

It retrieves key metrics from Mailchimp: campaign name, send time, open rate, click rate, unique clicks, and total recipients. This data forms the engagement side of the equation.

Step 3: Retrieve Corresponding E-commerce Orders

Next, the automation queries your e-commerce platform (like Shopify, WooCommerce, or a custom database) for orders placed within a defined period after the campaign send. It uses filters like customer email addresses or order timestamps to associate purchases with the campaign.

Step 4: Calculate Revenue Attribution

For each matched order, the workflow sums the revenue. It can apply logic to handle partial attribution (e.g., if a click led to a later purchase) or direct attribution. It also factors in campaign costs if you input them.

Step 5: Update Google Sheets Dashboard

All calculated data—campaign stats, attributed revenue, cost, and final ROI—is written into a structured Google Sheets spreadsheet. The sheet can be formatted to show visual charts, trend lines, and comparative tables, giving you an instant performance overview.

Pro tip: Set up this automation to run daily or weekly for ongoing campaigns. This gives you a rolling view of ROI, allowing you to pause underperforming campaigns early and double down on winners.
