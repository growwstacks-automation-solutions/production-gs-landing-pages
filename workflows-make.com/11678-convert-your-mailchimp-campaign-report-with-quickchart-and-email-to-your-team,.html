What This Workflow Does

Marketing teams spend valuable hours each week compiling campaign reports from Mailchimp, copying numbers into spreadsheets, and trying to present data in a way that stakeholders can quickly understand. This manual process is not only time-consuming but also prone to errors and delays, leaving your team reacting to old information.

This automation solves that problem entirely. It acts as your personal data analyst, automatically fetching the latest campaign performance data from Mailchimp the moment it's available. It then processes this raw data—open rates, clicks, bounces, revenue—and uses QuickChart to generate professional, easy-to-read visual charts. Finally, it compiles these charts into a clean, informative email and sends it directly to your marketing team, managers, or clients. The result is a consistent, timely, and visually engaging report that lands in inboxes without anyone lifting a finger.

The business value is clear: you replace a repetitive, low-value task with a reliable system. This frees up your marketers to analyze the insights and strategize, rather than just gather data. It ensures everyone is making decisions based on the same, up-to-date information, directly driving better campaign performance and ROI.

How It Works

The workflow is a seamless, three-stage pipeline that turns data into delivered insight.

1. Trigger & Data Fetch

The automation is triggered on a schedule (e.g., every Monday morning) or by a specific event in Mailchimp, like a campaign being marked as "sent." It securely connects to your Mailchimp account and retrieves the performance data for the designated campaign. This includes all key metrics stored in Mailchimp's analytics.

2. Data Transformation & Visualization

The raw metric data is sent to QuickChart, a powerful chart generation API. Here, you define the chart type—bar chart for opens per campaign, line chart for click-through trend over time, pie chart for audience device breakdown. The workflow structures the data into the format QuickChart requires, and the service returns a high-quality image URL of the completed chart.

Pro tip: You can create multiple charts in a single run—like one for engagement and another for financial metrics—and combine them into a comprehensive report dashboard in the email.

3. Report Compilation & Delivery

In the final stage, the workflow builds an HTML email. It inserts the chart image(s), adds contextual commentary (e.g., "Open rates increased by 15%"), and formats it with your company branding. It then uses an email module (like Gmail, Outlook, or SendGrid) to send this polished report to a predefined list of recipients. The entire process, from data fetch to sent email, completes in minutes.

What You'll Need

To implement this template, you'll need the following accounts and basic information ready:

  1. A Make.com account (free or paid plan) to host and run the automation.
  2. A Mailchimp account with at least one sent campaign containing performance data.
  3. Access to the QuickChart API (it's free for basic use). No account is needed, just an understanding of the chart parameters you want to use.
  4. An email sending service configured in Make.com, such as Gmail, Outlook, SMTP, or a transactional email service like SendGrid.
  5. A list of recipient email addresses (your team or clients) for the report distribution.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting started with this template is straightforward. Follow these steps to configure it for your business.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your own Make.com account.
  2. Connect Mailchimp: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Mailchimp account. Select the specific audience or campaign you want to report on.
  3. Configure QuickChart: Adjust the "Chart Configuration" module. Define your chart type (bar, line, pie), set the data mapping from Mailchimp, and customize colors or labels to match your branding.
  4. Set Up Email: In the email module, connect your email service (e.g., Gmail). Write the email subject and body template, using placeholders to insert the dynamic chart image and key metrics.
  5. Add Recipients & Schedule: Input the email addresses of your report recipients. Finally, set the schedule trigger (e.g., "Every Monday at 9 AM") or event-based trigger to determine when the report runs.
  6. Test & Activate: Run a single test execution with a past campaign to ensure the data flows correctly, the chart generates, and the email sends. Once verified, activate the scenario.
