What This Workflow Does

Marketing teams spend countless hours each week manually exporting data from Google Analytics, copying it into spreadsheets, and formatting it for reports. This process is not only time-consuming but also prone to errors and delays, leaving decision-makers without timely insights. This automation solves that by creating a direct, scheduled pipeline from Google Analytics to Airtable.

The workflow automatically fetches crucial performance metrics—such as total sessions, bounce rates, user counts, and goal conversions segmented by channel—on a schedule you set (like every Friday). It then structures and inserts this data into your Airtable base. This transforms Airtable from a simple database into a dynamic marketing data hub, where historical trends can be visualized, combined with other data sources, and used to generate actionable reports.

By eliminating the manual grunt work, your team gains back hours each week. More importantly, they gain consistent, reliable data that’s ready for analysis the moment it’s needed, empowering faster, data-driven decisions to optimize campaigns and improve ROI.

How It Works

This Make.com scenario acts as a reliable data bridge, operating on a clear, scheduled cycle to keep your Airtable base updated with the latest marketing intelligence.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

The workflow is initiated by a Schedule module set to run at your chosen interval—for example, every Friday morning. This automation ensures your reporting is consistent and never forgotten, providing a steady stream of fresh data for weekly performance reviews.

Step 2: Fetch Data from Google Analytics

Make.com connects to your Google Analytics account via API. It executes a predefined query to retrieve the specific metrics and dimensions you care about for the chosen date range (e.g., the previous week). This happens securely and without any manual login or export steps on your part.

Step 3: Structure and Transform the Data

The raw data from Google Analytics is processed within the scenario. Modules can filter, sort, or calculate derived values (like conversion rates) to ensure the data is clean, relevant, and formatted correctly for insertion into Airtable’s table structure.

Step 4: Create Records in Airtable

The processed dataset is sent to Airtable. The workflow maps each data point (sessions, channel, bounce rate, etc.) to the corresponding field in your designated table. It creates a new record for each data segment (e.g., a new row for “Organic Search” performance), building a historical log over time.

Pro tip: Use Airtable’s “Linked Records” feature to connect these new analytics records to related campaign briefs or content calendars in other tables. This creates a powerful relational view of what marketing activity drove which results.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this data pipeline running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the pre-built scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, update the connections (called "modules") for Google Analytics and Airtable. Click on each, select "Add connection," and follow the prompts to log in and authorize Make.com to access your accounts.
  3. Configure the Google Analytics Module: Specify the correct GA property, the date range (e.g., "Last 7 days"), and select the metrics and dimensions you want to pull. The template provides a standard set you can modify.
  4. Map to Your Airtable Base: Point the Airtable module to your specific base and table. Map the data fields from the previous step to the corresponding columns in your Airtable. Ensure the data types (text, number, date) match.
  5. Set Your Schedule: Configure the Schedule trigger to run at the frequency that suits your reporting cycle—weekly is recommended to start.
  6. Test & Activate: Run a single test execution to ensure data flows correctly from Google Analytics into Airtable. Check the created records for accuracy. Once satisfied, activate the scenario to let it run automatically on its schedule.
