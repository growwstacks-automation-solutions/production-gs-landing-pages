Sync ActiveCampaign Analytics to Airtable

Automatically import campaign performance data into Airtable for unified reporting, analysis, and data-driven marketing decisions.

What This Workflow Does

Marketers often struggle with data silos. Your email campaign metrics live in ActiveCampaign, while your broader marketing performance and planning happen in Airtable. Manually exporting CSV files, copying numbers, and updating spreadsheets is not only time-consuming but also prone to errors, leading to decisions based on outdated or incorrect data.

This Make.com automation solves that by creating a seamless, scheduled bridge between ActiveCampaign and Airtable. It automatically fetches your latest campaign analytics—opens, clicks, bounces, unsubscribes—and pushes them into a designated Airtable base. This transforms Airtable into a central command center for your marketing performance, where you can correlate email success with other initiatives.

The result is a single source of truth for your marketing efforts. You can track ROI, compare campaign performance over time, and generate reports without ever leaving Airtable. It turns raw data into actionable insights, ensuring your marketing strategy is always informed by the latest results.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a scheduled trigger, acting as a reliable data pipeline from ActiveCampaign to Airtable.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger Initiates the Sync

The automation is set to run on a schedule you define—typically weekly or daily. At the appointed time, Make.com activates the workflow, starting the process of collecting fresh data.

Step 2: Fetch Campaign List from ActiveCampaign

The workflow connects to your ActiveCampaign account via API and retrieves a list of your recent email campaigns. It can be configured to pull all campaigns or filter for specific ones based on date, list, or status.

Step 3: Retrieve Detailed Analytics for Each Campaign

For each campaign identified, the workflow makes a subsequent API call to ActiveCampaign to gather the detailed performance metrics. This includes opens, clicks, unique clicks, forwards, unsubscribes, bounce rates, and more.

Step 4: Transform and Structure the Data

The raw data from ActiveCampaign is then processed. The workflow maps each metric to the corresponding field in your Airtable base, calculates derived values (like click-to-open rate), and formats dates and numbers for consistency.

Step 5: Create or Update Records in Airtable

Finally, the workflow connects to your Airtable base. For each campaign's data packet, it either creates a new record or updates an existing one, ensuring your Airtable table always reflects the most current analytics.

Pro tip: Use a "Last Synced" timestamp field in Airtable. Configure the Make.com scenario to only fetch campaigns created or modified after the last sync timestamp. This incremental approach is more efficient and avoids processing the same data repeatedly.

  2. An Airtable Account: A free or paid Airtable account. You'll need to create a base with a table structured to receive the campaign analytics (e.g., fields for Campaign Name, Send Date, Opens, Clicks, etc.).
  3. A Make.com Account: A free Make.com plan is sufficient to run this scenario. You'll use it to host and execute the automation workflow.
  4. Basic Understanding of APIs (Optional but Helpful): Knowing how to find your API keys in ActiveCampaign and Airtable will make setup faster, but the template guides you through the process.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Duplicate the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com. Use the "Copy" function to duplicate it into your own Make.com workspace.
  2. Connect Your ActiveCampaign: In the first module of the workflow, click to add a connection. Select "ActiveCampaign," enter your account API URL and API key (found in ActiveCampaign Settings > Developer).
  3. Connect Your Airtable: In the Airtable module, add a connection by providing your Airtable API key (from your Airtable account page) and selecting the correct Base ID and Table Name.
  4. Map Your Data Fields: Review the data mapping between the ActiveCampaign modules and the Airtable module. Ensure fields like "Campaign Name," "Sent Date," "Opens," and "Clicks" are correctly aligned with your Airtable table's column names.
  5. Set Your Schedule: Configure the Schedule module to run at your desired frequency (e.g., every Monday at 9 AM). This determines how often the sync occurs.
  6. Test and Activate: Run a single test execution to ensure data flows correctly from a sample campaign into your Airtable base. Once verified, activate the scenario to let it run automatically.
