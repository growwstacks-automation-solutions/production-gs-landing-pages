What This Workflow Does

For businesses running Facebook ad campaigns, a surge in leads is a good problem to have—until it becomes a logistical nightmare. Manually reviewing each lead's location, checking a territory spreadsheet, and emailing the right sales rep is a slow, error-prone process that kills momentum. Leads go cold, reps waste time on mismatched prospects, and sales managers lose visibility.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your Facebook Lead Ads and your sales team. Every time a new lead comes in, the workflow instantly checks the lead's location data (like city or state) against a master Google Sheet that maps territories to sales representatives. It then automatically assigns the lead by notifying the correct rep via email, Slack, or SMS, delivering a warm lead ready for immediate follow-up.

The business value is clear: faster response times, higher conversion rates, and the elimination of administrative drag on your sales team. It turns your lead generation engine into a precision distribution system.

How It Works

The workflow acts as an intelligent dispatcher, operating in the background from the moment a lead submits your Facebook form.

Step 1: Capture the New Lead

The automation is triggered instantly when a new lead form is submitted on Facebook. It captures all the lead's details—name, email, phone number, and crucially, the location information from form fields.

Step 2: Match Location to Sales Rep

The system then queries your designated Google Sheet, which acts as your "routing rulebook." This sheet contains a simple table: one column for locations (e.g., "California," "NYC," "10001") and the corresponding column for the assigned sales rep's contact details. The automation finds the matching row.

Pro tip: Structure your Google Sheet with location keywords (like state abbreviations, city names, and ZIP code ranges) to ensure robust matching even if leads enter their location slightly differently.

Step 3: Assign & Notify

Once the correct rep is identified, the workflow performs the assignment. This can involve multiple actions: sending a personalized email to the rep with the lead's details, posting a message in a dedicated Slack channel, or even logging the lead directly into your CRM with the "Owner" field populated.

Step 4: Log for Accountability

The automation records the action in a separate log sheet, noting the lead's name, location, assigned rep, and timestamp. This creates an audit trail for sales managers to monitor volume, territory performance, and ensure no lead falls through the cracks.

Quick Setup Guide

  2. Connect Facebook: In the Make scenario, authorize the Facebook module to access your Lead Ad forms.
  3. Prepare Your Google Sheet: Create a sheet with clear columns for Location and Sales Rep Email. Share it with view access to your Make.com connection.
