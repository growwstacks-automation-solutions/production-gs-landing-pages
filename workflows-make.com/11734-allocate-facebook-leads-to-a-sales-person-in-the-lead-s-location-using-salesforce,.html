What This Workflow Does

For businesses running Facebook Lead Ads, the excitement of generating new leads is often quickly dampened by the administrative nightmare that follows. Marketing teams download lead lists, sales managers manually sift through spreadsheets to match leads with reps based on complex territory maps, and by the time a lead is assigned, the window for a hot follow-up has often closed. This manual process is slow, error-prone, and wastes valuable selling time.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as an intelligent bridge between your Facebook Lead Ads and Salesforce. Every time a new lead submits a form on Facebook, the workflow instantly captures their data, identifies their location (city, state, or zip code), and uses a pre-defined rule set to assign them to the correct sales representative in Salesforce. The assigned rep receives an immediate notification, allowing them to contact the lead while their interest is highest—often within minutes instead of hours or days.

How It Works

The automation follows a precise, rules-based sequence to ensure accurate and instantaneous lead distribution.

Step 1: Trigger on New Facebook Lead

The workflow is activated the moment a new lead submission is recorded in your Facebook Lead Ads account. Make.com instantly receives the lead's complete data set, including their name, email, phone number, and crucially, their location information from the form fields.

Step 2: Parse and Validate Lead Location

The system extracts the location data (e.g., "San Francisco, CA" or a zip code). It checks this against your internal mapping of sales territories, which defines which cities, regions, or zip codes belong to which Salesforce user or queue.

Pro tip: Maintain a simple, updated spreadsheet or database module within Make.com that maps territories to Salesforce User IDs. This makes updating territories easy when your sales team changes.

Step 3: Match Lead to Salesforce User

Using the territory mapping, the workflow logic identifies the exact Salesforce user responsible for that lead's location. If a lead's location is ambiguous or falls outside mapped areas, the workflow can be configured to assign the lead to a general sales queue or a manager for manual review.

Step 4: Create & Assign Lead in Salesforce

The workflow then creates a new Lead record in Salesforce, populating all fields with the data from Facebook. The most critical action is setting the "Owner" field to the matched Salesforce user. This automatic assignment is what eliminates all manual steps.

Step 5: Notify the Assigned Rep

Finally, the workflow can trigger an immediate notification to the sales rep. This can be done via a Salesforce task, an email alert, or even a message in Slack or Microsoft Teams, ensuring the rep knows a hot lead is waiting in their queue.

What You'll Need

To implement this workflow, ensure you have the following set up and accessible:

  1. A Facebook Business Manager account with active Lead Ads forms.
  2. A Salesforce account with administrative access to create and modify Lead records.
  3. A Make.com account (formerly Integromat).
  4. A clear Territory Map defining which geographic areas (zip codes, cities, states) are assigned to which Salesforce users.
  5. The Facebook Lead Ad form must be configured to capture location data (City, State, or Zip Code is recommended).

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to deploy the template in your Make.com account:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template on Make.com and clone it to your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the cloned scenario, authorize the connections to your Facebook Lead Ads and Salesforce accounts.
  3. Configure the Facebook Module: Select the specific Facebook Page and Lead Form you want to trigger the workflow.
  4. Build Your Territory Map: Set up a "Router" module in Make.com. Create routes for each territory (e.g., Route 1: City contains "Chicago", Route 2: State equals "Florida"). You can use a simple spreadsheet module or a dedicated database to manage this mapping.
  5. Map Salesforce Users: For each route, add a Salesforce "Create a Lead" module. Map the incoming lead data to the Salesforce Lead fields. For the "Owner ID" field, input the Salesforce User ID of the rep for that territory.
  6. Set Up Notifications (Optional): Add an email or notification module after each Salesforce create step to alert the rep.
  7. Test and Activate: Run a test with a dummy lead submission to ensure the lead is created in Salesforce and assigned correctly. Then, activate the scenario.
