What This Workflow Does

For businesses running Facebook ad campaigns, a flood of leads is a good problem to have—until it becomes a logistical nightmare. Manually downloading lead lists, copying data into Salesforce, and figuring out which sales rep covers which region is slow, error-prone, and frustrating. Leads get cold, reps waste time on admin, and opportunities slip through the cracks.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Facebook Lead Ads and Salesforce. The moment someone fills out your lead form on Facebook, their information is instantly captured and transformed into a new Lead or Contact record in Salesforce. But it doesn't stop there. The system intelligently reads the lead's location data (like city or state) and automatically assigns them to the sales representative responsible for that territory.

The result is a fully organized, efficient allocation process. Your sales team receives hot, qualified leads in their Salesforce queue within minutes, complete with all the context they need for a personalized, timely follow-up. This isn't just about saving time; it's about maximizing conversions by ensuring the right person contacts the right lead at the right time.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a trigger-action model, connecting two of the most powerful platforms in marketing and sales.

Step 1: A New Lead is Captured on Facebook

The automation is triggered the instant a new submission comes in through your Facebook Lead Ad. It pulls all the form field data—name, email, phone, location, and any custom questions you've asked.

Step 2: Data is Formatted for Salesforce

The raw data from Facebook is processed and mapped to the corresponding fields in your Salesforce object. This ensures clean, consistent data entry every single time, with no missing information.

Step 3: Location is Analyzed for Territory Assignment

This is the core intelligence of the workflow. The system examines the lead's geographic data (e.g., zip code, state). It then checks against your pre-defined sales territory rules to determine the correct owner.

Pro tip: You can configure the territory logic to be as simple (state-based) or complex (zip code lists, round-robin for overlaps) as your sales structure requires.

Step 4: Lead is Created and Assigned in Salesforce

The workflow creates a new Lead record in Salesforce and assigns the "Owner" field to the identified sales rep. It can also populate additional details like the lead source ("Facebook Ad Campaign X") for accurate tracking.

Step 5: Notification and Follow-Up

Optionally, the system can trigger a notification to the assigned rep via email or Slack, alerting them to the new, hot lead in their territory, prompting immediate action.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Facebook: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Facebook Lead Ad data.
  3. Connect Salesforce: Authorize the connection to your Salesforce org in the subsequent modules.
  4. Map Your Fields: In the "Create Lead" module, ensure the data from Facebook is correctly mapped to your Salesforce Lead fields (e.g., map 'First Name' to 'FirstName').
  5. Configure Assignment Logic: This is the crucial step. Use Make.com's tools (like routers and filters) to set up the rules that assign an Owner ID based on the lead's location. You might use a simple "if State equals CA, set Owner to [Rep ID]".
  6. Test and Activate: Turn on the scenario and test it by submitting a fake lead through your Facebook form. Verify that the correct Lead appears in Salesforce assigned to the right rep.
