What This Workflow Does

For sales teams using Typeform for lead generation and Salesforce as their CRM, a major bottleneck is the manual process of transferring lead data and deciding who should follow up. This delay can cost conversions, and manual assignment often leads to unfair distribution—some reps get flooded while others are underutilized, especially in regions with multiple team members.

This automation solves both problems instantly. It acts as a smart bridge between your Typeform and Salesforce. Every time a new submission comes in, the workflow automatically creates a corresponding lead or contact record in Salesforce. But it doesn't stop there. It then intelligently assigns that lead to a sales representative based on the lead's geographic location (like state or city), and crucially, randomizes the selection if multiple reps are assigned to that same territory.

The result is a fully automated, fair, and immediate lead routing system. Leads are engaged faster by the most locally relevant rep, workload is balanced automatically, and your sales managers are freed from administrative tasks. This turns your lead capture form into a powerful, self-managing distribution engine.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence to ensure accuracy and fairness from the moment a lead submits a form to the moment they appear in a rep's Salesforce queue.

Step 1: Capture the New Typeform Submission

The automation is triggered the instant someone submits your Typeform. It immediately captures all the submitted data, such as name, email, company, and most importantly for this workflow, their location information (e.g., City, State, or Country fields).

Step 2: Route Based on Location

The system reads the lead's location data and matches it against a pre-defined list or database of your sales territories. This determines which pool of sales representatives is eligible to receive this lead.

Pro tip: Maintain a simple, up-to-date Google Sheet or Airtable base that maps locations to sales rep emails or Salesforce IDs. The automation references this as the single source of truth for assignment rules.

Step 3: Randomize Assignment Within the Group

If the matched location has more than one assigned sales rep, the workflow uses a randomizer function to pick one rep from that group. This is the core of ensuring equitable distribution and preventing any single rep from being favored or overloaded.

Step 4: Create the Lead in Salesforce

With the target rep identified, the automation creates a new Lead (or Contact) record in your Salesforce org. It maps all the Typeform field data to the corresponding Salesforce fields and, critically, populates the "Owner" field with the selected sales rep's Salesforce user ID. This automatically places the lead in that rep's list and notifications.

Step 5: Optional Notification & Logging

As a final step, the workflow can send a confirmation Slack message to a sales channel, log the action in a spreadsheet for auditing, or even trigger a welcome email to the lead—all ensuring the process is transparent and immediate follow-up is possible.

What You'll Need

To implement this template, you should have the following set up and ready:

  1. A Typeform account with an active form collecting lead information, including a field for location (e.g., "State", "Country").
  2. A Salesforce account with administrative access to create connected apps (for API access) and modify lead/contact objects.
  3. A Make.com account (formerly Integromat) to build and run the automation.
  4. A Territory Mapping List: A simple spreadsheet or database (like Google Sheets) that defines which sales rep(s) are assigned to each possible location value from your Typeform.
  5. The Salesforce User IDs (or exact email addresses) of your sales representatives for accurate owner assignment.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to configure your automated lead distribution system:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the provided Make.com scenario into your own account.
  2. Connect Typeform: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Typeform account and select the specific form you want to use as the trigger.
  3. Connect Salesforce: Authorize the connection to your Salesforce org. Ensure the connection has permissions to create and update Lead records.
  4. Map Your Territory Data: Update the part of the scenario that holds your location-to-rep mapping. This is typically a Google Sheets module or an array. Input your list of locations and the corresponding sales rep emails or IDs.
  5. Configure Field Mapping: In the "Create Lead" Salesforce module, map the Typeform response fields (like `answer_1`, `answer_2`) to the correct Salesforce Lead fields (like `FirstName`, `LastName`, `Company`, `State`).
  6. Test with a Dummy Submission: Use Typeform's preview mode to submit a test entry. Run the scenario once to ensure it correctly picks a rep based on location and creates the lead in Salesforce.
  7. Activate & Monitor: Turn the scenario on. New Typeform submissions will now flow automatically. Monitor the scenario history for the first few days to ensure smooth operation.
