What This Workflow Does

Businesses that collect lead or customer data in flexible tools like Airtable often face a critical bottleneck: getting that information into their primary Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system, such as Zoho CRM. Manually copying and pasting data is not only time-consuming—taking hours each week—but it's also prone to errors, delays, and inconsistencies. A lead might sit in Airtable for days before a salesperson even knows they exist, costing valuable opportunities.

This automated workflow solves that problem by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between Airtable and Zoho CRM. The moment a new record is added to your designated Airtable base—whether from a web form, an event sign-up, or an internal process—the automation triggers. It securely takes the data, maps it to the correct fields in Zoho CRM, and creates a new contact instantly. Your sales team gains immediate visibility, enabling faster, more informed follow-ups and ensuring no potential customer falls through the cracks.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical, reliable sequence to ensure data integrity and successful contact creation every time.

Step 1: Monitor Airtable for New Records

The workflow starts by watching a specific Airtable table. Using Airtable's "Watch Records" module in Make.com, it detects whenever a new record is created. You can configure it to monitor specific views or filter for certain conditions, ensuring only relevant data triggers the automation.

Step 2: Extract and Structure the Contact Data

Once a new record is detected, the workflow pulls out the necessary information. This includes fields like First Name, Last Name, Email, Company, Phone Number, and any custom notes or tags you've collected. The data is parsed and prepared for the next step.

Pro tip: Use a "Last Modified" field in your Airtable base to ensure the automation only processes truly new or updated records, preventing unnecessary API calls and duplicate entries.

Step 3: Create the Contact in Zoho CRM

The prepared data is then sent to Zoho CRM via its API. The "Create a Contact" module maps each piece of Airtable data to its corresponding Zoho CRM field. You can assign the new contact to a specific owner, add them to a campaign, or tag them with a lead source based on the Airtable record's origin.

Step 4: Confirm Success and Log Results

After Zoho CRM confirms the contact creation, the workflow can log the success, update the original Airtable record with the new Zoho Contact ID for future reference, or even send a notification to your team. This closed-loop process provides full visibility and traceability.

What You'll Need

  1. A Make.com account (formerly Integromat) with an active plan to run the automation.
  2. An Airtable account with a base containing your contact data table.
  3. A Zoho CRM account with administrative access to generate API credentials and modify contact modules.
  4. Your Airtable table should have a dedicated "Last Modified" or "Created Time" field to reliably trigger the automation.
  5. A clear understanding of which fields in Airtable correspond to which fields in Zoho CRM (e.g., "Email" to "Email", "Company Name" to "Account Name").

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automation up and running quickly using the provided template.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the template into your own Make.com scenario.
  2. Connect Your Apps: Authorize the scenario to access your specific Airtable base and your Zoho CRM account via OAuth.
  3. Configure the Airtable Module: Select the correct base, table, and view that contains your new contact records. Ensure the "Watch Records" module is set to detect new entries.
  4. Map the Data Fields: In the Zoho CRM "Create Contact" module, map each piece of data from the previous Airtable step to the correct Zoho CRM field. Pay special attention to required fields in Zoho.
  5. Test and Activate: Run a test with a sample Airtable record to ensure the contact is created correctly in Zoho CRM. Once verified, activate the scenario and set it to run on a schedule (e.g., every 15 minutes) or in real-time.
