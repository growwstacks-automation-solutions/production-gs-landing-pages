What This Workflow Does

For marketing teams, the gap between collecting customer data and using it effectively is often a manual, time-consuming process. You might track webinar sign-ups, lead magnet downloads, or offline purchases in a Google Sheet, but getting that valuable behavioral data into your email marketing platform like Omnisend requires copying, pasting, and manual uploading. This delay means missed opportunities to engage customers when they're most interested.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a live bridge between Google Sheets and Omnisend. Every time a new row is added to your designated spreadsheet—whether it's a new customer, a completed action, or an updated status—Make.com instantly captures that data and creates a corresponding custom event in Omnisend. This transforms static spreadsheet data into dynamic marketing triggers, enabling real-time segmentation and personalized automation without any manual intervention.

The business value is clear: you move from reactive to proactive marketing. Instead of batch-uploading data weekly, your email campaigns can react to customer behavior within minutes. This leads to higher engagement, improved customer experiences, and ultimately, increased sales from more timely and relevant communication.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence, ensuring data flows accurately from your team's spreadsheet to your marketing engine.

Step 1: Monitor Google Sheets for New Rows

The workflow starts by watching a specific Google Sheets spreadsheet for any new rows. You define the sheet and worksheet, and Make.com checks at regular intervals. When a new row appears—containing customer email, event name, timestamp, and other properties—it triggers the entire automation.

Pro tip: Structure your Google Sheet with clear, consistent column headers like "Customer_Email", "Event_Name", "Event_Date", and "Properties". This makes mapping the data in the next steps foolproof.

Step 2: Parse and Format the Data

Make.com takes the raw data from the new row and parses it into the specific format required by Omnisend's API. This involves mapping your spreadsheet columns to Omnisend fields. For example, your "Action" column becomes the Omnisend "eventName", and the "Date" column is formatted into a standard timestamp Omnisend recognizes.

Step 3: Create the Custom Event in Omnisend

Using the formatted data, Make.com sends a secure API call to your Omnisend account. It creates a new custom event for the specified customer contact. This event is now permanently attached to that contact's profile in Omnisend, enriching their record with this new behavioral data point.

Step 4: Trigger Omnisend Automation (The Magic Happens)

This is where the automation delivers real business value. The newly created custom event can now trigger any automation workflow you've set up in Omnisend. For instance, an event named "Downloaded_Ebook" could automatically add the contact to a nurture sequence about related services. The entire process, from data entry to campaign activation, happens automatically.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to configure your live automation in under 30 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Google Sheets: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Google account and select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet you want to monitor.
  3. Connect Omnisend: In the Omnisend module, add your API key from your Omnisend account settings to establish the secure connection.
  4. Map Your Data Fields: This is the crucial step. In the Omnisend "Create a Custom Event" action, map the data from your Google Sheets columns to the correct Omnisend fields. Map the email address to the contact identifier, and your event name column to the Omnisend event name.
  5. Test with a Sample Row: Add a test row to your Google Sheet with dummy data. Run the scenario once to ensure it fetches the row and successfully creates the event in Omnisend. Check the Omnisend contact profile to confirm.
  6. Activate & Schedule: Turn on the scenario in Make.com and set the schedule (e.g., check for new rows every 15 minutes). Your automation is now live.
