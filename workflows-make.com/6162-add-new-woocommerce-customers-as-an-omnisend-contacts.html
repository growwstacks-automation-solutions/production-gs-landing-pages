What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce store owners, a major marketing gap occurs between a customer making a purchase and being added to your email marketing list. Manually exporting new customers from WooCommerce and importing them into Omnisend is slow, error-prone, and means missed opportunities for immediate engagement like welcome emails or post-purchase follow-ups.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between your store and your marketing platform. Every time a new customer account is created in WooCommerce—whether from a purchase, account registration, or checkout—Make.com instantly captures their details and adds them as a contact in your Omnisend audience. This ensures your marketing list is always accurate and growing automatically with your business.

How It Works

The automation acts as a smart connector, listening for events in WooCommerce and taking precise action in Omnisend.

Step 1: Trigger on New Customer

The workflow is triggered the moment a new customer is created in your WooCommerce store. Make.com watches for this event in real-time.

Step 2: Gather Customer Data

Make.com retrieves the new customer's information, such as their name, email address, and any other available details from the WooCommerce order or account.

Pro tip: You can configure the module to only add customers who have opted-in for marketing, ensuring GDPR compliance and a more engaged list.

Step 3: Create Omnisend Contact

The workflow then uses Omnisend's API to create a new contact in your specified audience list. You can map WooCommerce fields directly to Omnisend contact properties.

Step 4: Add Tags for Segmentation (Optional)

As a final step, you can configure the automation to add specific tags in Omnisend based on the customer's data—like "First-Time Buyer" or "Product X Customer"—enabling powerful segmentation from day one.

What You'll Need

  1. A live WooCommerce store with customer accounts enabled.
  2. An active Omnisend account with an audience/list ready to receive contacts.

  4. API connection details for both WooCommerce (Consumer Key & Secret) and Omnisend (API Key).

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect WooCommerce: In the first module, authenticate your WooCommerce store by entering your store URL and API credentials.
  3. Connect Omnisend: In the Omnisend module, authenticate using your Omnisend API key found in your account settings.
  4. Map the Data: Ensure fields like email, first name, and last name are correctly mapped from WooCommerce to the corresponding Omnisend contact fields.
  5. Test & Activate: Create a test customer in WooCommerce to trigger the flow, verify the contact appears in Omnisend, and then turn the scenario on.

Pro tip: Start by adding a filter after the WooCommerce trigger to only process customers from specific countries or who have spent over a certain amount, making your initial list more targeted.
