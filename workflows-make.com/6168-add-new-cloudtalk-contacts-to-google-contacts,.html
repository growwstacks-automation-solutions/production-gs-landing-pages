What This Workflow Does

For sales and support teams using CloudTalk as their calling platform, a critical challenge is keeping their central address book—often Google Contacts—updated with every new prospect or customer. Manually copying contact details from CloudTalk to Google is a repetitive, time-consuming task that is prone to errors and delays. This friction slows down follow-up emails, calendar scheduling, and team collaboration, ultimately costing deals and degrading customer experience.

This automated workflow solves that problem instantly. It acts as a seamless bridge between CloudTalk and Google Contacts. Every time a new contact is created in your CloudTalk account—whether from an inbound call, a manually added lead, or an imported list—this Make.com scenario triggers automatically. It captures the contact's details and creates a corresponding, fully formatted entry in your specified Google Contacts list. The result is a perfectly synchronized, always-up-to-date contact database accessible across Gmail, Google Calendar, and your entire team.

The business value is clear: eliminate a low-value administrative task, reclaim hours per week for your sales team, and ensure no lead falls through the cracks due to outdated or missing contact information. Your team can focus on conversations and closing, not data entry.

How It Works

The automation is built on a simple, reliable "trigger-action" logic within Make.com. Here is a step-by-step breakdown of the process:

Step 1: A New Contact is Added to CloudTalk

The workflow is initiated the moment a new contact record appears in your connected CloudTalk account. Make.com watches for this event continuously, ready to act instantly.

Step 2: Make.com Captures the Contact Data

Make.com receives a webhook or fetches the new contact data from CloudTalk's API. This data includes key fields like the contact's name, phone number, email address, company, and any custom notes or tags assigned in CloudTalk.

Pro tip: You can configure the workflow to only sync contacts that meet certain criteria, such as those tagged as "Hot Lead" or from a specific campaign, preventing your Google Contacts from becoming cluttered.

Step 3: Data is Formatted for Google Contacts

The raw data from CloudTalk is processed and mapped to the correct field structure required by the Google Contacts API. For example, the CloudTalk "Mobile" number field is mapped to the "Mobile phone" field in Google. This step ensures clean, consistent data transfer.

Step 4: A New Contact is Created in Google

Make.com uses your authorized Google account to create a new contact in your specified Google Contacts list or label. The contact is instantly available across Google Workspace. The workflow can also include logic to check for duplicates before creating an entry, updating existing contacts instead.

Step 5: Optional Notifications & Logging

You can add modules to send a confirmation Slack message, log the sync to a Google Sheet, or trigger a follow-up email sequence—all based on the successful creation of the Google contact.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting started with this template is straightforward. Follow these steps to deploy it in your Make.com account:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above. This will open the template in your Make.com account where you can create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, authorize the CloudTalk and Google Contacts modules. This is a secure OAuth process—you'll log into each service to grant Make.com permission.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Point the CloudTalk module to watch for "New Contact" events in your specific CloudTalk account.
  4. Map the Data Fields: Review the data mapping between CloudTalk fields (like name, phone, email) and the corresponding Google Contact fields. Adjust if your CloudTalk uses custom field names.
  5. Set the Destination: Choose the Google account and the specific Contacts list or label where new contacts should be created.
  6. Test and Activate: Run a single test operation by manually adding a dummy contact in CloudTalk. Verify it appears correctly in Google Contacts. Once confirmed, activate the scenario to run automatically.

Pro tip: Start by running the scenario in "Test Mode" or with a limited filter (e.g., only contacts with a specific tag) to ensure it works perfectly before syncing your entire contact history.
