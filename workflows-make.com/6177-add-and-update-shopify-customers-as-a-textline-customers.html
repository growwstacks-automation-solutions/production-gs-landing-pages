What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce brands using Shopify, customer data is your most valuable asset. When you launch SMS marketing campaigns via Textline, manually transferring customer information is a tedious, error-prone task. This workflow solves that by creating a seamless, automated bridge between your store and your communication platform.

Every time a new customer completes a purchase or creates an account in your Shopify store, this Make.com automation instantly captures their details. It then checks if they already exist in your Textline contact list. If they are new, it adds them as a contact. If they already exist, it updates their profile with the latest information, ensuring your SMS lists are always accurate and up-to-date.

The result is a unified customer view. Your marketing team can run targeted SMS campaigns knowing the contact data is fresh, which increases deliverability and engagement while saving countless hours of administrative work.

How It Works

The automation runs on a reliable trigger-and-action logic, ensuring no customer falls through the cracks.

Step 1: Shopify Triggers the Workflow

The scenario is triggered by a specific event in Shopify, typically the creation of a new customer or an update to an existing customer's profile. Make.com watches for this event in real-time.

Step 2: Data is Extracted and Prepared

The workflow pulls key customer data points from the Shopify webhook, such as first name, last name, email, phone number, and any relevant tags or notes. This data is formatted to match Textline's contact field requirements.

Step 3: Check for Existing Contact in Textline

Before creating a duplicate, the automation searches your Textline contacts using a unique identifier (like email or phone number). This intelligent lookup is the core of the "update" functionality.

Step 4: Add or Update Accordingly

If the contact is not found, Textline's API is called to create a new customer entry. If the contact already exists, a separate API call updates their existing record with the new information from Shopify, keeping everything synchronized.

Pro tip: Use Shopify customer tags to automatically segment your Textline lists. For example, tag customers as "VIP" or "First-Time-Buyer" in Shopify, and the workflow can add them to corresponding groups in Textline for targeted messaging.

What You'll Need

To implement this template, you will need the following accounts and access:

  1. A Make.com account (free or paid plan).
  2. An active Shopify store with admin access to create webhooks or use the Shopify app in Make.
  3. A Textline account with API access enabled (typically available in paid plans).
  4. The customer data fields you wish to sync (e.g., phone number, email, name) must be collected in your Shopify checkout.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, authorize the connections to your specific Shopify store and your Textline account.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set the Shopify module to watch for "Customer Created" and "Customer Updated" events.
  4. Map Your Data: In the Textline modules, map the incoming Shopify fields (like customer.first_name) to the corresponding Textline contact fields.
  5. Test and Activate: Use the "Run once" feature to test with a real customer. Once confirmed, activate the scenario to run automatically.
