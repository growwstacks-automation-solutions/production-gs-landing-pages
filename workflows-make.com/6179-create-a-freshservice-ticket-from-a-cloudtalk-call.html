What This Workflow Does

For support teams using CloudTalk for phone communications and Freshservice for ticketing, a critical gap often exists: the manual, error-prone process of creating a support ticket after every customer call. Agents must switch between systems, copy-paste caller details, and summarize the conversation, leading to delays, missed tickets, and inconsistent data.

This automated workflow solves that by acting as a seamless bridge between your phone system and your help desk. Every time a new call is registered in CloudTalk—whether answered, missed, or voicemail—this automation instantly triggers and creates a corresponding, fully detailed ticket in Freshservice. It captures essential metadata like caller ID, call duration, recording link, and timestamps, turning a voice conversation into an actionable, trackable support item without any human intervention.

The result is a closed-loop system where customer voice interactions are never lost. Support managers gain complete visibility into call volume and ticket correlation, while agents can prioritize follow-ups based on accurate, automatically logged information.

How It Works

The automation follows a clean, event-driven sequence to transform a call log into a support ticket.

Step 1: Monitor for New CloudTalk Calls

The workflow starts by watching CloudTalk for new call events. It can be configured to trigger on specific call types, such as all inbound calls, only missed calls, or calls tagged by your team. The moment a call ends and its data is available in CloudTalk, the automation captures the entire call object.

Step 2: Map Call Data to Ticket Fields

Next, the automation parses the call data and maps it to the relevant fields in a Freshservice ticket. The caller's phone number becomes the requester email or a custom field. The call start time becomes the ticket creation date. The call duration and recording URL are added to the ticket description or as private notes, providing full context for the support agent.

Pro tip: Use the call direction (inbound/outbound) to automatically set the ticket priority or type. An inbound customer call could be 'High' priority, while an outbound call might be logged as a 'Follow-up'.

Step 3: Create the Ticket in Freshservice

With all data prepared, the workflow uses the Freshservice API module to create a new ticket. It populates the subject (e.g., "Call from [Phone Number]"), the description with call details, and assigns it to the appropriate group, agent, or service category based on your predefined rules. The ticket is now live in your help desk queue.

Step 4: Optional Notification & Logging

Finally, the automation can send a confirmation to a Slack channel, email the assigned agent, or log the action in a Google Sheet. This provides an audit trail and ensures the team is aware of new, call-generated tickets that may require immediate attention.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 20 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the first module, authorize your CloudTalk account by entering your API key. Then, authorize your Freshservice account in the ticket creation module.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set the CloudTalk module to watch the "Call Finished" or "New Call" event. You can add a filter here if you only want to create tickets for specific call types.
  4. Map the Data: In the Freshservice "Create Ticket" module, map the CloudTalk fields (like caller_number, duration) to the corresponding Freshservice ticket fields (like requester, description).
  5. Test and Activate: Make a test call to your CloudTalk number. Check that a ticket appears in Freshservice with the correct details. Once confirmed, turn the scenario on.
