What This Workflow Does

Missed calls are more than just a ringing phone—they are missed opportunities, frustrated customers, and potential revenue left on the table. In sales and support teams, manually tracking down who missed a call, logging the number, and creating a task for follow-up is a slow, error-prone process that often leads to dropped balls.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a digital bridge between your CloudTalk VoIP phone system and your Asana project management workspace. Whenever a call comes in and isn't answered, the workflow triggers automatically. It captures the call details and creates a new, actionable task in Asana—assigned to the right person, with all the context they need to make a timely callback.

The result is a closed-loop system where communication failures are instantly converted into accountable work items. Your team stops playing detective with call logs and starts focusing on what matters: re-engaging with people who tried to reach you.

How It Works

The workflow runs on Make.com, listening for specific events and moving data between your apps without any manual intervention.

Step 1: CloudTalk Triggers on a Missed Call

The scenario starts when CloudTalk detects a new call event with a status of "missed." This is typically done via a webhook or by polling CloudTalk's API at regular intervals. The workflow instantly receives a data packet containing the caller's number, the time of the call, its duration, and which agent or line it was directed to.

Step 2: Data is Processed and Formatted

Make.com processes the incoming data. It can filter calls based on rules you set (e.g., only calls longer than 20 seconds, or from specific area codes). It then formats this information into a clear title and description for the Asana task, such as "Missed call from +1 (555) 123-4567 at 2:30 PM - 45 secs."

Step 3: A Task is Created in Asana

The formatted data is sent to Asana via its API. The workflow creates a new task in a specified project (e.g., "Call Back Queue" or "Support Missed Calls"). It can assign the task to a specific team member, a rotation, or leave it unassigned. It can also set a due date (e.g., "by end of today") and add relevant tags for prioritization.

Pro tip: Use this workflow to also log voicemails from CloudTalk. You can configure it to create an Asana task with a link to the voicemail recording, ensuring even more context is captured for your follow-up.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" above to open the pre-built template in your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, click the CloudTalk and Asana modules. Connect each one by logging into your accounts and authorizing the integration.
  3. Configure the Trigger: In the CloudTalk module, ensure it's set to watch for "Missed Call" events. You may need to set up a webhook in your CloudTalk settings and paste the URL into Make.com.
  4. Map the Task Details: In the Asana "Create a Task" module, specify the target project. Map the data from CloudTalk (like phone number and timestamp) to the Asana task's title and description fields.
  5. Set Assignment & Due Date (Optional): Configure the task to be assigned to a specific person or a team. You can also add a rule to set the due date to the end of the current business day.
  6. Test and Activate: Use the "Run once" button to test the scenario. Simulate a missed call in CloudTalk or use sample data. Once you confirm a task appears correctly in Asana, turn the scenario "ON."
