Automatically Delete Deputy Schedules from Google Sheets

Keep your workforce spreadsheet perfectly in sync. Whenever a schedule is deleted in Deputy, this automation instantly removes the corresponding row from your Google Sheets, eliminating manual cleanup and errors.

What This Workflow Does

Managing employee schedules across multiple platforms creates a common headache: data drift. When a manager deletes a shift in Deputy because an employee called in sick or an event was canceled, that outdated information often lingers in your Google Sheets reports. This leads to payroll discrepancies, inaccurate labor cost forecasts, and hours wasted on manual reconciliation.

This automation solves that problem by creating a real-time, one-way sync from Deputy to Google Sheets for deletions. It acts as a silent cleanup crew. The moment a schedule is removed in your Deputy account, the workflow is triggered. It searches your designated Google Sheet for the matching entry (using a unique identifier like a Schedule ID) and deletes the entire row. Your spreadsheet instantly reflects the current, accurate state of your workforce, without you lifting a finger.

This template is the essential counterpart to the schedule creation/update template. Together, they form a complete synchronization system, ensuring your Google Sheets is a live, reliable mirror of your Deputy scheduling data for reporting, payroll processing, and strategic planning.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple, reliable sequence to maintain data integrity.

Step 1: Monitor Deputy for Deleted Schedules

The automation starts with a "Watch Schedules" trigger in Deputy. It continuously monitors your Deputy account for any schedule deletion events. This is a passive, real-time watch that doesn't interfere with your normal Deputy operations.

Step 2: Capture the Deleted Schedule's Data

When a deletion is detected, the workflow captures key details about that removed schedule. The most critical piece of data is the unique Schedule ID. This ID is the fingerprint that will be used to find the correct row in Google Sheets. Other details like employee name, date, and time may also be passed along for logging purposes.

Step 3: Find the Matching Row in Google Sheets

The workflow then accesses your specified Google Sheets spreadsheet. It searches through the designated column (typically the first column where you store the Deputy Schedule ID) to find the row that contains the exact same ID as the deleted schedule.

Pro tip: Structure your spreadsheet with the Deputy Schedule ID in column A. This makes the search fast and foolproof, preventing accidental deletion of the wrong row if an employee has multiple shifts.

Step 4: Delete the Row & Confirm

Once the matching row is found, the automation executes a "Delete a Row" action in Google Sheets. The entire row is removed, cleaning your data set. The workflow can then be configured to send a simple confirmation log to another app (like Slack or email) or simply complete silently, knowing the job is done.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Deputy: In the first module, click to add your Deputy connection. Follow the prompts to authorize Make.com to access your Deputy account.
