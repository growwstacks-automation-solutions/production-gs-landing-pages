Automate Employee Onboarding: Typeform to Deputy

Instantly create staff profiles in Deputy from new hire applications submitted through Typeform. Eliminate manual entry and start new employees faster.

What This Workflow Does

Manual employee onboarding is a notorious time-sink for HR teams and hiring managers. Every new hire means copying and pasting information from application forms into workforce management systems—a process prone to errors, delays, and frustration. This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between your recruitment forms and your staff scheduling platform.

When a candidate completes your Typeform application, this workflow automatically triggers and creates a corresponding employee profile in Deputy. All essential details—name, contact information, and address—are transferred instantly and accurately. This means new hires appear in your scheduling system immediately, ready for their first shift assignment, without any administrative intervention.

The business value is clear: reduced time-to-productivity for new staff, elimination of data entry errors, and significant hours saved for your HR department each month. It transforms onboarding from a clerical task into a strategic, automated process.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical, reliable sequence to ensure data integrity from start to finish.

Step 1: Monitor for New Typeform Submissions

The workflow uses Make.com's Typeform module to watch for new responses on your specified form. Each time a candidate submits their application, it captures the entire dataset as a structured package for processing.

Step 2: Parse and Map the Applicant Data

Make.com extracts each field from the Typeform response. The template is pre-configured to look for specific fields: first name, last name, email, phone, and full address components. This mapping ensures the data goes to the correct places in Deputy's employee profile.

Pro tip: Name your Typeform fields exactly as specified in the template (e.g., "First name," "Email") to guarantee a smooth, zero-configuration setup. Consistent field naming is key to reliable automation.

Step 3: Create the Employee in Deputy

The workflow connects to your Deputy account via a secure OAuth connection and uses the "Create Employee" API action. It passes all the collected information to build a complete staff record, including contact details and location data, making the new hire instantly visible to managers.

Step 4: Confirm and Log the Action

After Deputy confirms the employee creation, the workflow can be extended to send a confirmation email to an HR manager, log the event in a Google Sheet, or trigger the next step in your onboarding checklist, like sending a welcome pack.

What You'll Need

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available).
  2. A Typeform account with a form for collecting employee applications.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template to your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, click the Typeform and Deputy modules to authenticate and connect your accounts. This is a one-time, secure OAuth process.
  3. Select Your Typeform: Point the trigger module to the specific Typeform you use for job applications.
  4. Map Your Fields (if needed): Review the data mapping between Typeform answers and Deputy fields. If your form uses the standard field names, this should already be correct.
  5. Test and Activate: Turn on the scenario and submit a test response to your Typeform. Check Deputy to confirm a new employee profile was created successfully.
