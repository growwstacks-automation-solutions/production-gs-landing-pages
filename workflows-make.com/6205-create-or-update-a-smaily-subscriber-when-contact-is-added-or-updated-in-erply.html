What This Workflow Does

For retail and e-commerce businesses using Erply as their point-of-sale system, customer data is constantly being created and updated. However, this valuable information often remains siloed, disconnected from marketing tools like Smaily. Manually exporting CSV files from Erply and importing them into Smaily is a tedious, error-prone process that wastes valuable time and leads to outdated marketing lists.

This automated workflow solves that problem by creating a real-time bridge between Erply and Smaily. Whenever a new customer is added or an existing customer's details change in Erply, the system automatically checks if they exist in Smaily. If they do, it updates their subscriber profile with the latest information. If they don't, it creates a new subscriber. This ensures your email marketing audience is always accurate, complete, and ready for targeted campaigns.

The result is a seamless flow of customer data from your sales system to your marketing platform, enabling timely welcome emails, personalized promotions based on purchase history, and consistent communication that drives repeat business—all without lifting a finger.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to ensure data integrity and efficiency.

Step 1: Monitor Erply for Contact Changes

The workflow starts with a Make.com module that watches your Erply account for specific events. It can be triggered by the creation of a new contact or any update to an existing contact's information, such as email address, phone number, or name changes.

Step 2: Extract and Format Contact Data

Once a trigger is detected, the workflow extracts the relevant contact fields from Erply. This typically includes the customer's first name, last name, email address, phone number, and any custom fields you wish to sync. The data is then formatted to match Smaily's expected input structure.

Pro tip: Use Erply's "Customer Group" or "Type" field to segment contacts in Smaily automatically. For example, sync "Wholesale" customers to a different list than "Retail" customers for tailored messaging.

Step 3: Check for Existing Subscriber in Smaily

Before creating a new entry, the workflow queries your Smaily account using the contact's email address as a unique identifier. This search determines whether the person is already on your list.

Step 4: Create or Update the Subscriber

Based on the search result, the workflow takes one of two actions. If the email is not found, it creates a new subscriber in your designated Smaily list. If the email is found, it updates the existing subscriber's information with the new data from Erply, ensuring their profile is current.

Step 5: Handle Errors and Log Results

The final step involves error handling. If the sync fails (e.g., due to an invalid email format), the workflow can send you a notification or log the issue for review. Successful operations are also recorded, giving you visibility into the automation's activity.
