What This Workflow Does

If you run a WooCommerce store, you're likely using Gravity Forms for lead generation—contact forms, newsletter signups, or quote requests. Each submission is a potential customer, but manually copying their details into WooCommerce is slow, error-prone, and creates a disjointed experience. This automation solves that by instantly turning every form submission into a fully-fledged WooCommerce customer.

The workflow listens for new Gravity Forms entries. When someone fills out your form, it automatically checks if a customer with that email already exists in WooCommerce. If not, it creates a new customer record using the submitted data, such as name, email, and address. This seamless sync ensures your marketing and sales systems are always aligned, giving you a single customer view from the very first interaction.

Beyond saving hours of data entry, this automation unlocks powerful marketing opportunities. New customers can immediately receive welcome emails, special offers, or be added to segmented lists based on how they found you. It bridges the gap between lead capture and customer management, turning prospects into recognizable shoppers instantly.

How It Works

The automation is built on Make.com and acts as a secure bridge between your WordPress site and your WooCommerce store. It follows a logical, error-checking process to ensure data integrity.

Step 1: Trigger on Form Submission

The workflow starts the moment a visitor submits your configured Gravity Form. Make.com receives the submission data in real-time via a webhook or the Gravity Forms API module. All form field values—like email, first name, last name, and phone—are captured and made available for the next steps.

Pro tip: Use hidden fields in your Gravity Form to pass along additional context, such as the page URL or campaign source, which can then be stored as customer metadata in WooCommerce for better tracking.

Step 2: Check for Existing Customer

Before creating anything, the automation queries your WooCommerce store's customer list using the submitted email address as the unique identifier. This duplicate check is crucial for maintaining a clean database and preventing customer frustration from receiving duplicate communications.

Step 3: Create the WooCommerce Customer

If no existing customer is found, the workflow uses the WooCommerce API module to create a new customer record. It maps the Gravity Forms fields to the corresponding WooCommerce customer fields—'Email' to 'email', 'First Name' to 'first_name', etc. The new customer is then fully registered in your store, ready to make a purchase.

Step 4: (Optional) Trigger Follow-up Actions

Once the customer is successfully created, the workflow can trigger a chain of valuable follow-up actions. This could include sending a welcome email via your ESP (like Mailchimp or Klaviyo), notifying your sales team in Slack, or creating a task in your CRM. This step transforms a simple data sync into a powerful customer onboarding sequence.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, add connections for your Gravity Forms site and your WooCommerce store using the respective API modules. You'll need to enter your site URL and API credentials.
  3. Select Your Form: In the Gravity Forms trigger module, select the specific form you want to use as the source for new customers.
  4. Map the Data Fields: In the WooCommerce "Create a Customer" module, map the data from the Gravity Forms submission to the correct WooCommerce customer fields (e.g., map 'Email' to 'email').
  5. Test the Flow: Use Make.com's test feature to run a single execution. Submit a test entry on your live form and verify that a customer appears in your WooCommerce admin panel.
  6. Activate & Monitor: Turn the scenario on. It will now run automatically for every new form submission. Monitor the scenario's history for the first few days to ensure it's running smoothly.

Pro tip: Start by testing with a hidden or low-traffic form to ensure your field mapping is correct before applying the automation to your main contact form.
