What This Workflow Does

Customer feedback is the lifeblood of any growing business, but collecting and organizing it manually is a major bottleneck. Teams waste precious hours exporting CSV files from survey tools like Refiner, reformatting data, and copying it into spreadsheets. This process is error-prone, slow, and creates a lag between receiving feedback and acting on it.

This automated workflow solves that problem entirely. It connects Refiner directly to Google Sheets using Make.com. Every time a customer submits a survey response—whether it's an NPS score or an answer to a critical product-market fit question—the data is instantly captured, structured, and appended as a new row in your designated spreadsheet. This creates a live, centralized log of customer sentiment that your entire team can access and analyze without manual intervention.

The template is specifically configured for key customer health metrics, capturing the "How disappointed would you be?" question and the NPS score. It ensures your most vital feedback is never lost in an inbox or forgotten in an export.

How It Works

The automation acts as a real-time bridge between your survey platform and your data repository. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Trigger from Refiner Webhook

The workflow is initiated the moment a new survey response is submitted in Refiner. You configure a webhook URL generated by Make within Refiner's integration settings. This webhook sends a packet of data containing all the survey answers, timestamps, and user information.

Step 2: Parse and Structure the Data

Make.com receives the webhook payload. The workflow then parses this JSON data to extract the specific fields you care about: the respondent's email (if provided), their NPS score (0-10), their answer to the disappointment question, and the submission time.

Pro tip: You can easily modify the workflow to capture additional survey questions or custom fields by adding more data mapping modules in Make.com. This makes the template adaptable to any survey structure.

Step 3: Add a New Row to Google Sheets

The final module takes the structured data and uses the Google Sheets "Add a Row" action. It connects to your specified spreadsheet and appends the information into pre-defined columns, such as "Timestamp," "Email," "NPS Score," "Disappointment Level," and "Date Collected." Your spreadsheet is updated in seconds.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes. Follow these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy (scenario).
  2. Prepare Your Google Sheet: Create a new sheet or make a copy of the sample. Note the spreadsheet ID and the exact name of the worksheet tab.
  3. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, authenticate the Google Sheets module by connecting your Google account and selecting your prepared spreadsheet.
  4. Get Your Webhook URL: Inside the Make scenario, find the Webhook module and copy its unique, generated URL.
  5. Configure Refiner: Go to your Refiner project settings, navigate to Integrations → Webhooks, and paste the Make.com webhook URL. Save the configuration.
  6. Activate and Test: Turn the Make.com scenario "ON." Submit a test response to your Refiner survey and watch the new row appear in your Google Sheet automatically.
