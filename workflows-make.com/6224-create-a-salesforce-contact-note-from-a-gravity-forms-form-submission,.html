What This Workflow Does

Every time a potential or existing customer fills out a form on your WordPress site—be it a contact request, support ticket, or feedback survey—that valuable interaction often gets trapped in a spreadsheet or an email inbox. Your sales and support teams then waste precious time manually copying this information into Salesforce, a process that is slow, error-prone, and leads to incomplete customer records.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a seamless bridge between your Gravity Forms and Salesforce. The moment a form is submitted, the workflow springs into action. It identifies the correct contact in your Salesforce database (usually by email) and automatically creates a rich, detailed note attached to their record. This note can include the entire form submission—questions asked, products selected, messages sent—providing a perfect historical context for every future interaction.

The result is a unified, real-time view of the customer journey. Your team can now see every touchpoint without switching between apps, enabling personalized follow-ups, faster support resolutions, and more informed sales conversations. It turns passive form data into active intelligence.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical, step-by-step process to ensure data is transferred accurately and attached to the right place in Salesforce.

Step 1: Trigger on Form Submission

The automation is initiated the instant a Gravity Forms form is successfully submitted on your website. The workflow captures all the submitted data, including standard fields like name and email, and any custom fields specific to your form.

Step 2: Find the Salesforce Contact

Using the submitter's email address (or another unique identifier from the form), the workflow queries your Salesforce database to find the matching contact record. This step is crucial for ensuring the note is logged against the correct person.

Pro tip: Configure a fallback action in this step. If the contact doesn't exist, the workflow can be set to first create a new contact record, ensuring no submission is ever lost.

Step 3: Format and Create the Note

The workflow then structures the form data into a clear, readable format for the Salesforce note. It maps specific form fields, adds labels, includes the submission timestamp, and can even apply conditional formatting based on the responses. This creates a note that is immediately useful for your team.

Step 4: Attach Note to Contact Record

Finally, the formatted note is posted to the "Notes" or "Activity" section of the identified Salesforce contact. The process is complete in seconds, providing an up-to-the-minute record of the interaction directly within your primary CRM.

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to clone the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Gravity Forms: In the Make.com scenario, authorize the Gravity Forms module by providing your WordPress site URL and the appropriate API credentials.
