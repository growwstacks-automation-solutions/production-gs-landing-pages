What This Workflow Does

Businesses often use Airtable as a powerful, flexible database for tracking everything from project leads and content ideas to customer support tickets and product feedback. However, turning that data into action requires moving it into a task management system like Asana where teams actually get work done. This manual transfer is a repetitive, time-consuming, and error-prone bottleneck.

This automation solves that problem by creating a live bridge between your Airtable base and your Asana projects. Whenever a new record is added to a specific, filtered view in your Airtable base, this workflow instantly creates a corresponding, detailed task in the designated Asana project. It maps relevant fields like titles, descriptions, due dates, assignees, and custom properties, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks and your team can act immediately.

The result is a seamless operational pipeline. Information captured in Airtable automatically becomes accountable work in Asana, dramatically reducing administrative overhead, speeding up response times, and ensuring data consistency across your tools.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com and operates on a simple trigger-action principle, with powerful filtering capabilities.

Step 1: Monitor a Specific Airtable View

The automation starts by watching a single, pre-defined view within your chosen Airtable base and table. This is crucial—it doesn't watch the entire table. You can create a view filtered by status (e.g., "Ready for Action"), priority, assignee, or project. Only when a new record appears in this specific view does the trigger fire.

Step 2: Map Record Data to Task Fields

Once triggered, the workflow takes the data from the new Airtable record and prepares it for Asana. You configure the mapping: the record's "Title" field can become the Asana task name, a "Notes" field can populate the description, a "Due Date" field sets the task deadline, and a "Assignee Email" field can automatically assign the task to the right team member.

Pro tip: Use Airtable's "Single Select" or "Collaborator" fields to dynamically set Asana tags, custom fields, or assignees, making the automation highly adaptable to different process stages.

Step 3: Create the Task in Asana

The final step is the creation of the task in your specified Asana project and section. The workflow uses the Asana module to create a task with all the mapped data attached. You can even set it to add the original Airtable record URL as an attachment or comment, providing a direct link back to the source data for full context.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automation running in under 15 minutes:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, authorize the Airtable and Asana modules by logging into your accounts.
  3. Configure the Airtable Trigger: Select your base, table, and most importantly, the specific view you want to watch.
  4. Map the Data Fields: In the Asana "Create a Task" module, map the Airtable fields to the corresponding Asana task fields (name, notes, due date, assignee, project, etc.).
  5. Test with a Sample Record: Add a new record to your chosen Airtable view and run the scenario once to see the task appear in Asana.
  6. Activate and Schedule: Turn the scenario on. It will now run automatically every few minutes (based on Make.com's schedule) to check for new records.
