What This Workflow Does

For creative teams, agencies, and e-commerce businesses, Cloudinary is a powerhouse for managing, transforming, and delivering digital media. However, relying on a single platform for storage poses a risk. Accidental deletion, service changes, or the simple need for a searchable, personal archive are real concerns. This automation solves that by creating a seamless, automatic bridge between your professional media management in Cloudinary and the universal accessibility of Google Photos.

The workflow monitors your Cloudinary account for new media assets that match specific tags you define. The moment a new image or video is uploaded and tagged, Make.com detects it, retrieves the file, and automatically uploads it to a designated album in your Google Photos. It turns a manual, error-prone backup task into a silent, reliable background process that ensures every piece of content is securely duplicated.

How It Works

The automation runs on a simple yet powerful trigger-action principle, orchestrated by Make.com.

Step 1: Monitoring Cloudinary

The scenario starts by watching your Cloudinary account. You configure it to check for new assets at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes, hourly). It can filter specifically for assets with certain tags, such as "final_edit," "website_ready," or "client_project_x," ensuring only relevant media is backed up.

Step 2: Retrieving the Media File

When a new, tagged asset is found, Make.com fetches the file URL from Cloudinary. It can handle various formats—JPEG, PNG, GIF, MP4, and more. The module securely accesses the file using your Cloudinary API credentials without storing the media on Make.com's servers.

Step 3: Uploading to Google Photos

The retrieved file is then passed to the Google Photos module. Here, it authenticates with your Google account via secure OAuth and uploads the media directly into your library. You can choose to place it in a specific album (like "Cloudinary Backup - Q3") or upload it to your general photo library, maintaining organization.

Pro tip: Use descriptive tags in Cloudinary (e.g., "backup_essential," "project_alpha") to act as your filter. This way, the automation only syncs the most critical final assets, preventing your Google Photos from being cluttered with every working draft or test upload.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this backup system running in under 10 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Cloudinary: In the first module, create a connection using your Cloudinary API Key and Secret.
  3. Set Your Trigger: Configure the "Watch Assets" module to check your desired resource type (images/videos) and enter the specific tag you want to monitor (e.g., "to_backup").
  4. Connect Google Photos: Authorize the Google Photos module with your Google account when prompted.
  5. Test the Flow: Upload a test image to Cloudinary and apply the tag you configured. The scenario should run and upload the image to your Google Photos library.
  6. Activate: Turn the scenario schedule to "On" and set your preferred polling interval (e.g., every hour).
