What This Workflow Does

For agencies, consultants, and remote teams, manually compiling time entries from Everhour into a weekly report is a tedious, error-prone task that steals valuable hours from billable work. This automation solves that by creating a hands-off reporting system.

The workflow connects your Everhour account to Gmail, pulling time data for a specified period (default is the last 7 days), organizing it into a clear table with columns for date, task, project, client, and cost, and then emailing it to designated recipients on a schedule you set. It turns raw time-tracking data into actionable business intelligence delivered on autopilot.

How It Works

The automation runs on a reliable, scheduled cycle, ensuring your reports are never forgotten.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger & Data Fetch

Every Monday at 8 AM (configurable), the scenario activates. It immediately queries your Everhour account for all time entries logged within the lookback window, securely pulling the raw data into Make.com for processing.

Step 2: Data Structuring & Table Creation

The workflow maps the retrieved data into a structured format. It extracts the key columns—created at, task name, project name, client name, and cost—and prepares them to be rendered as a clean HTML table, ready for email.

Step 3: Email Composition & Dispatch

Using Gmail, the automation composes a new email. It sets the subject (e.g., "Weekly Time Report: [Date Range]"), addresses it to your chosen stakeholders, and inserts the formatted HTML table into the body. The email is then sent automatically, completing the report cycle.

Pro tip: Use the email's BCC field to send a copy to your finance team or a dedicated Slack channel via email integration, keeping everyone informed without cluttering primary inboxes.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your Make.com workspace.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the first module, authorize the connection to your Everhour account using your API token. Then, authorize the Gmail module with your Google account.
  3. Set the Schedule: Configure the scheduler module to run on your preferred day and time (e.g., every Friday afternoon).
  4. Define Recipients: In the Gmail module, enter the "To" and "BCC" email addresses for who should receive the report.
  5. Test & Activate: Run the scenario once manually to ensure it fetches data and sends an email correctly. Then, turn the scenario "ON" to let it run automatically.
