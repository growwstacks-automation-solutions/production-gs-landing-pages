What This Workflow Does

For businesses that manufacture their own products, a critical bottleneck exists between receiving an online order and getting it into the production queue. Manually copying order details from Squarespace into Katana ERP is slow, prone to errors, and delays production starts. This disconnect can lead to missed deadlines, inventory mismatches, and frustrated customers.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between your Squarespace store and Katana. The moment a customer completes a purchase, the workflow springs into action. It captures the entire order—including customer details, items, quantities, and any notes—and automatically creates a corresponding sales order in Katana. If the customer is new or a product isn't yet in your Katana catalog, the system intelligently creates those records first, ensuring the sales order is complete and accurate. This turns a multi-step, manual task into a fully automated background process.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence to ensure data integrity and a smooth handoff from sales to manufacturing.

Step 1: Trigger on New Squarespace Order

The automation is triggered instantly whenever a new order is placed on your Squarespace website. It captures all the order data, such as order ID, customer email, shipping address, and the specific line items purchased.

Step 2: Find or Create the Customer in Katana

The system then searches your Katana contacts using the customer's email address. If a match is found, it links the new sales order to that existing customer record. If not, it automatically creates a new customer contact in Katana using the information from the Squarespace order, ensuring all future orders from this customer are linked correctly.

Step 3: Find or Create Products in Katana

Next, for each product in the order, the workflow checks if it exists in your Katana product list (usually by SKU or product name). If the product is found, it's added to the sales order. If it's a new product, the workflow can create a basic product record in Katana on the fly, preventing the order from failing due to missing items.

Step 4: Create the Katana Sales Order

With the customer and products verified or created, the workflow assembles and creates the final sales order in Katana. This includes all relevant details: quantities, prices, shipping method, and any customer notes from Squarespace. The production team now has a complete, actionable order in their system.

Pro tip: Use this automation as the first step in a larger production pipeline. Once the sales order is in Katana, you can trigger additional automations to notify your production manager, reserve inventory, or even generate a packing slip.
