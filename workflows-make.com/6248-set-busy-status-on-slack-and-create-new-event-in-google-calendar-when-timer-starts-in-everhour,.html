Automate Focus Time: Sync Everhour Timers with Slack & Google Calendar

Start a timer in Everhour, and this workflow automatically sets your Slack status to busy and creates a corresponding event in Google Calendar—zero manual updates required.

What This Workflow Does

For professionals who bill by the hour, work in sprints, or simply want to protect their focus time, the constant switching between a time tracker, a communication app, and a calendar is a silent productivity killer. You start a timer in Everhour, then manually set your Slack status to "in a meeting" or "focusing," and finally, you might block time on your Google Calendar. This fragmented process wastes minutes every day and often gets forgotten, leading to interruptions and inaccurate time records.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your time tracking, team communication, and personal scheduling. The moment you click "Start" on a timer in Everhour, two important things happen automatically: your Slack status updates to "Busy" for a predefined duration (like 60 minutes), and a new event is created on your Google Calendar with the timer's details. This eliminates manual steps, signals your availability to your team, and creates a perfect audit trail of your workday.

The business value is clear: reduced administrative overhead, fewer context switches, improved team transparency, and more accurate records for client billing or productivity analysis. It turns a simple timer start into a powerful productivity signal.

How It Works

The workflow is triggered by a single event and orchestrates actions across two other platforms, all in real-time.

Step 1: Timer Starts in Everhour

The automation is triggered the instant you start a new timer in your Everhour account. Make.com watches for this specific event via the Everhour module. The workflow captures key data from the timer, such as the start time, the associated project or task name, and any notes you've added.

Step 2: Set "Busy" Status on Slack

Using the captured data, the workflow connects to your Slack workspace via the Slack module. It updates your personal Slack status to show a "Busy" indicator (like a red dot) and sets a custom status text—for example, "Focusing on [Project Name]." The duration for this status is controlled by a variable you set in the template, ensuring your team knows not to disturb you for that block of time.

Step 3: Create Event in Google Calendar

Simultaneously, the workflow uses the Google Calendar module to create a new event. This event is titled based on the Everhour task, scheduled for the timer's start time and a calculated end time (start time + your defined duration), and added to your primary calendar. This creates a visual block of time that others can see if they view your calendar, reinforcing your focus period.

Pro tip: Use the notes field in your Everhour timer. The workflow can pull this text into both your Slack status and your Google Calendar event description, providing rich context for yourself and your team at a glance.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under ten minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button on this page. It will open the template in your Make.com account.
  2. Create a Copy: In Make.com, click "Copy" to create your own version of the scenario (workflow).
  3. Authorize Connections: In your new scenario, click on each module (Everhour, Slack, Google Calendar) and authorize Make.com to connect to your accounts. This is a secure, one-time process for each app.
  4. Configure the Duration: Locate the "Set variable" module or the field where the Slack status duration is set. Change the number of minutes to match your typical focus block (e.g., 25 for Pomodoro, 60 for deep work).
  5. Map Calendar Details (Optional): Review the Google Calendar module. You can customize how the event title and description are formed using data from Everhour (like project name and notes).
  6. Test and Activate: Turn on the scenario (set the scheduler to "On") and start a timer in Everhour. Check that your Slack status updates and a calendar event appears. Once confirmed, the automation is live.
