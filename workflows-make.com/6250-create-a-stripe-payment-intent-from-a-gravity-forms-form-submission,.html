What This Workflow Does

Manually processing payments from online forms is a time-consuming and error-prone bottleneck. After a customer submits a Gravity Forms form for a donation, event ticket, or product order, someone on your team must manually create an invoice or payment link in Stripe, send it to the customer, and track its status. This delays payment, creates administrative overhead, and can lead to lost revenue if a step is forgotten.

This automation solves that by creating a direct, real-time bridge between Gravity Forms and Stripe. The moment a form is submitted, the workflow instantly creates a Stripe Payment Intent—a secure object that represents your intent to collect payment. This automatically generates a secure checkout link for the customer, ensuring the payment process begins immediately without any manual intervention from your team.

How It Works

The workflow is a streamlined, three-step process that runs silently in the background, turning form submissions into actionable payment requests.

Step 1: Trigger on Form Submission

The automation is triggered every time a new entry is submitted to your specified Gravity Forms form. Make.com watches for this event and immediately captures all the submitted data, such as the customer's name, email, and the critical payment amount.

Pro tip: Ensure your Gravity Forms form includes a dedicated number or calculation field for the payment amount. This provides a clean, reliable data point for Stripe.

Step 2: Map Data to Stripe

The workflow then routes the captured form data to Stripe via its API. It maps the form fields to the parameters required to create a Payment Intent. This includes the amount (converted to cents), the customer's email for receipt, and a descriptive statement descriptor that will appear on their bank statement.

Step 3: Create the Payment Intent & Notify

Stripe receives the request and creates a secure Payment Intent object. This object contains a unique client secret and a payment link. The workflow can then be extended to email this payment link directly to the customer or store the transaction details in a spreadsheet or CRM for your records, closing the loop automatically.

What You'll Need

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available).
  2. A Stripe account with API keys (found in your Stripe Dashboard under Developers).
  3. A WordPress website with Gravity Forms installed and a form built.
  4. Your Gravity Forms form must include a field for the payment amount and ideally the customer's email.
  5. The Gravity Forms API (via the "Gravity Forms Webhooks" add-on or similar) enabled to send data to Make.com.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Gravity Forms: In the first module, authenticate and select the specific form that will trigger the payment process.
  3. Connect Stripe: Add your Stripe API secret key to the Stripe module to establish a secure connection.
  4. Map the Fields: Map the form field containing the payment amount to the Stripe 'amount' field (remembering to multiply by 100 to convert to cents). Map the customer email field as well.
  5. Test & Activate: Run a test submission with a small amount (e.g., $1.00) to ensure a Payment Intent is created successfully in your Stripe test mode. Then, switch to live mode and activate the scenario.
