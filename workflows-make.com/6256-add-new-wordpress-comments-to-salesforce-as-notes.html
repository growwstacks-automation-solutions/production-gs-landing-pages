What This Workflow Does

For B2B companies, a blog is a powerful lead generation tool. But when a potential customer leaves a thoughtful comment, that signal of interest often gets trapped in your WordPress dashboard, invisible to your sales team. This disconnect means missed opportunities and slower follow-up.

This automation solves that problem by creating a live bridge between your WordPress site and Salesforce. Every time a new comment is posted, it automatically creates a detailed note on the corresponding contact or lead record in Salesforce. This turns anonymous engagement into a rich, actionable sales intelligence, allowing your team to understand a lead's interests before they even make the first call.

The workflow ensures that marketing efforts (blog content) directly fuel sales activities with real-time data, eliminating manual reporting and creating a seamless, informed customer journey from first interaction to closed deal.

How It Works

The process is event-driven and fully automated, requiring no manual intervention once set up.

Step 1: Trigger on New Comment

The workflow is activated the moment a new comment is published on your WordPress site. The Make.com scenario watches for this specific event using the WordPress module.

Step 2: Extract and Format Comment Data

The system captures key details: the commenter's name, email address, the comment text, the post they commented on, and the timestamp. This data is cleaned and formatted for clarity.

Step 3: Find or Create the Salesforce Record

Using the commenter's email address, the automation searches your Salesforce database for a matching Lead or Contact. If found, it proceeds to attach the note. Optionally, it can create a new Lead if no match exists.

Step 4: Create the Note in Salesforce

A new "Note" record is created in Salesforce and linked to the found or newly created Lead/Contact. The note's title and body are populated with the formatted blog comment details, providing immediate context for the sales team.

Pro tip: Add a filter step to only process comments from non-logged-in users or those containing specific keywords. This prevents internal team comments or spam from creating notes, keeping your Salesforce data clean and relevant.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this integration running in under 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a new scenario from it.
  2. Connect WordPress: In the first module, authenticate Make.com with your WordPress site using an application password or OAuth.
  3. Connect Salesforce: Authenticate the Salesforce module with your org, granting access to read/write Notes and search for Leads/Contacts.
  4. Map the Data: Review the data mapping between the WordPress comment fields and the Salesforce Note fields. Ensure the email field is correctly mapped for the search.
  5. Set a Filter (Optional): Add a filter between steps if you only want to sync approved comments or comments on specific posts.
  6. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and post a test comment on your blog. Verify that a note appears on the correct record in Salesforce within moments.
