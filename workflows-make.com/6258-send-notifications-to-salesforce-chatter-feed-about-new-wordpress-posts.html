What This Workflow Does

In today's fast-paced business environment, marketing creates valuable content on WordPress, but sales teams often miss it. This disconnect leads to wasted resources, inconsistent messaging, and lost opportunities. Manual email blasts or Slack messages get buried, and your latest case study or product announcement never reaches the people talking to customers.

This automation solves that by creating a direct, real-time bridge between your WordPress website and your Salesforce Chatter feed. Every time a new post is published, a notification is automatically posted to a designated Chatter feed. This ensures your sales, support, and leadership teams see the content immediately, in the platform where they already work daily.

The result is a unified team that can leverage fresh content to engage prospects, answer questions with authority, and provide timely feedback to marketers. It turns your blog from a static marketing asset into a dynamic sales enablement tool.

How It Works

The workflow is a simple yet powerful sequence that requires no manual intervention once set up.

Step 1: Monitor WordPress for New Posts

The automation starts by watching your WordPress site's RSS feed or using a webhook trigger. The moment a new post is published (or updated, based on your settings), the workflow captures key details like the title, URL, author, excerpt, and featured image.

Pro tip: Use the WordPress "Webhooks" feature for near-instant triggering, as RSS checks have a slight delay. This is crucial for time-sensitive announcements.

Step 2: Format the Chatter Message

The captured post data is then formatted into a compelling Chatter message. You can customize this template to include a catchy intro, the post title as a link, a short summary, relevant hashtags (like #Blog or #NewUpdate), and even @mention specific teams or individuals to draw their attention.

Step 3: Post to Salesforce Chatter

Using a secure connection to your Salesforce instance, the workflow posts the formatted message to a specified Chatter feed. This could be your company's main feed, a dedicated "Marketing Updates" group, or a feed for a specific sales team. The post appears as coming from a designated user or a connected app, driving immediate visibility and engagement.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect WordPress: In the first module, add your WordPress site's RSS feed URL (typically yoursitename.com/feed) or configure a webhook.
  3. Connect Salesforce: In the Chatter module, authenticate with your Salesforce credentials. You'll need to allow Make.com access via a Connected App.
  4. Customize the Message: Edit the text module to craft the perfect Chatter announcement. Use placeholders for the post title, link, and author.
  5. Choose the Feed: Specify the exact Chatter feed ID or group where the notifications should appear.
  6. Test & Activate: Publish a test post in WordPress, run the scenario once manually, and verify the message appears correctly in Chatter. Then, schedule the scenario to run automatically.
