What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Revolut Business, draft payments are pending transactions that require review and approval before funds are sent. Manually logging into the Revolut dashboard to check for new drafts is inefficient and prone to delays, which can stall payments to suppliers, delay payroll, or cause missed financial oversight.

This automated workflow solves that problem by acting as your 24/7 financial sentry. Every time a new draft payment is created in your Revolut Business account, Make.com detects the event instantly and automatically sends a detailed email notification to you or your team. This transforms a manual, reactive process into a proactive, streamlined system, ensuring you're always the first to know about pending financial actions.

The business value is clear: improved cash flow visibility, faster payment approvals, and eliminated manual checking. It provides an audit trail of notifications and ensures that critical financial tasks never fall through the cracks because someone forgot to check the app.

How It Works

The automation is built on a simple but powerful trigger-action sequence in Make.com, connecting your Revolut Business account to your email.

Step 1: Monitoring Revolut Business

The workflow starts with the Revolut Business module in Make.com set to watch for a specific event: "Draft Payment Created." This connection uses Revolut's secure API to monitor your account in real-time without requiring manual login.

Step 2: Capturing Payment Details

When a new draft is detected, Make.com captures all relevant data from the event: the payment amount, currency, recipient details, creation timestamp, and any internal reference or note. This data is structured and prepared for the notification.

Step 3: Sending the Instant Email Alert

The captured data is then passed to an email module (like Gmail or SMTP). Make.com composes a clear, actionable email with the draft payment details and sends it immediately to your predefined email address or distribution list.

Pro tip: Customize the email subject line to include the amount and recipient (e.g., "Action Required: £1,250 Draft Payment to Supplier XYZ"). This allows you to prioritize alerts directly from your inbox.
