What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Revolut Business, managing draft payments—those scheduled or initiated but not yet completed—is a critical part of cash flow forecasting. Manually copying details like amount, recipient, date, and reference from Revolut into an Excel spreadsheet is tedious, error-prone, and creates a lag in your financial records. This disconnect can lead to missed payments, inaccurate cash flow views, and hours wasted on reconciliation.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Revolut Business and Microsoft 365 Excel. Every time a new draft payment is created in your Revolut account, this Make.com workflow instantly captures the details and appends them as a new row in your designated Excel worksheet. The result is a live, centralized ledger of all pending and processed payments, updated automatically without any manual intervention.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple trigger-action principle, ensuring your Excel sheet is always synchronized with your Revolut activity.

Step 1: Monitoring Revolut for New Drafts

The workflow starts with a Make.com module that watches your Revolut Business account. It is configured to trigger instantly whenever a new "draft payment" is created. This could be a scheduled payment to a supplier, a salary batch you've set up, or a one-off transfer you've initiated.

Step 2: Extracting the Payment Data

Once triggered, the workflow pulls all relevant data from the draft payment. This typically includes the payment ID, amount, currency, recipient name/IBAN, scheduled date, status, and any custom reference or note you added.

Pro tip: Ensure your Revolut draft payments include clear references or notes. This data will flow into Excel, making it much easier to categorize expenses and understand the purpose of each transaction later.

Step 3: Structuring and Sending to Excel

Make.com then maps each piece of extracted data to a corresponding column in your Microsoft 365 Excel worksheet. The workflow locates your specific Excel file and sheet, and adds a new row with the fresh payment information. It matches the data to your pre-defined column headers, keeping your spreadsheet organized and consistent.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the template to your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Revolut Business: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Revolut Business account using secure OAuth. You'll be redirected to Revolut to grant permissions.
  3. Connect Microsoft 365 Excel: In the Excel module, sign in to your Microsoft account and select the specific Excel file and worksheet where you want the data to go.
  4. Map Your Data: The template is pre-configured, but review the data mapping. Ensure the fields from Revolut (like 'amount', 'counterparty.name') are correctly linked to your Excel column headers.
  5. Test and Activate: Create a test draft payment in Revolut (e.g., schedule a £1 payment). The workflow should run and add a row to your Excel sheet. Once confirmed, activate the scenario to run automatically.
