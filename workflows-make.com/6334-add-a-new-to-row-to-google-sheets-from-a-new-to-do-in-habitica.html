What This Workflow Does

Habitica is a fantastic tool for gamifying your to-do lists and habits, but its data lives inside the app. For deeper analysis, long-term record-keeping, or sharing progress with others, you need that data in a more flexible environment. Manually copying tasks from Habitica to a spreadsheet is tedious, error-prone, and easy to forget.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between Habitica and Google Sheets. Every time you or your team creates a new to-do in Habitica, this workflow instantly captures the task details—like title, notes, due date, and tags—and adds them as a new, timestamped row in your designated Google Sheet. It turns your dynamic task list into a structured, searchable database without you lifting a finger.

The business value is clear: it transforms subjective task management into objective data. You can now track completion rates, analyze productivity trends over weeks or months, and create custom reports. For teams using Habitica for project sprints or accountability, it provides an automatic audit trail and a central source of truth that’s accessible to everyone.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable automation built on Make.com. It acts as an intelligent middleman, listening for new events in Habitica and writing data to Google Sheets.

Step 1: Trigger on New Habitica To-Do

The scenario starts by monitoring your Habitica account. The moment you create a new to-do item, Habitica sends a notification to Make.com. This trigger is the starting pistol for the entire automation.

Step 2: Capture and Structure the Task Data

Make.com receives the raw data about the new to-do. The workflow then maps this data into a clean, consistent format. It pulls out the key pieces of information you want to log, such as the task name, description, difficulty, due date, and any custom tags you’ve applied.

Pro tip: You can customize which Habitica fields are captured. If you use custom tags for project codes or priorities, make sure to include them in the data mapping for powerful filtering later in Sheets.

Step 3: Add a New Row to Google Sheets

Finally, the formatted data is sent to your pre-configured Google Sheet. The workflow uses the Google Sheets module to append a new row at the bottom of your log. Each column in the sheet corresponds to a specific piece of task data, creating a perfectly organized record.

This process runs in seconds, 24/7. Whether you add a task from your phone at night or during a team meeting, your Google Sheet log is updated in real-time, ensuring your data is always current.

Quick Setup Guide

You can be up and running in under 10 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the pre-built scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Habitica: In the first module, create a connection to Habitica using your API credentials (found in Habitica's Settings > API).
  3. Connect Google Sheets: In the final module, authorize Make.com to access your Google account and select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet where you want the data to go.
  4. Map Your Data: Review the data mapping between the Habitica trigger and the Google Sheets "Add Row" action. Ensure the correct Habitica fields are going into the correct spreadsheet columns.
  5. Test and Activate: Turn on the scenario in Make.com. Create a test to-do in Habitica and watch as a new row instantly appears in your Google Sheet. Once confirmed, your automation is live.
