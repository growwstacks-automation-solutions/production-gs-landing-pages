What This Workflow Does

Many productivity enthusiasts and professionals use Habitica for its gamified approach to managing daily tasks and habits, turning productivity into a rewarding game. Simultaneously, Notion serves as a powerful all-in-one workspace for notes, projects, and databases. The challenge arises when tasks created in the motivational environment of Habitica need to be referenced, planned, or expanded upon within the structured, collaborative space of Notion.

This automation solves that disconnect. It acts as a real-time bridge between the two apps. Whenever you or your team creates a new to-do in Habitica—be it a daily habit, a one-time task, or a reward—this workflow automatically captures that task and creates a corresponding, fully formatted item in your designated Notion database. This eliminates manual copying, ensures no task is forgotten between platforms, and centralizes your entire workload.

The result is a unified productivity system. Your gamified, motivational task list in Habitica seamlessly feeds into your strategic project planning in Notion. You maintain the engagement benefits of Habitica while gaining the organizational power and visibility of Notion, without any extra effort.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger from Habitica

The workflow starts by monitoring your Habitica account. The Make.com scenario uses Habitica's API to watch for the creation of new "to-dos" in your list. This trigger is instant and reliable, catching new tasks as soon as they are added.

Step 2: Extract Task Details

Once a new to-do is detected, the automation pulls all relevant information from Habitica. This typically includes the task title, description, due date (if set), priority, tags, and any custom attributes you've defined. This data is structured and prepared for the next step.

Step 3: Map Data to Notion Properties

The workflow then maps the extracted Habitica data to the specific property fields in your Notion database. For example, the Habitica "Title" becomes the Notion "Task Name" property. The "Due Date" maps to a Notion "Date" property. This step ensures the information fits perfectly into your existing Notion structure.

Pro tip: You can customize this mapping to suit your database. If your Notion database uses "Project" instead of "Task List", simply adjust the property names in the Make.com module.

Step 4: Create the Notion Database Item

Finally, the scenario uses Notion's API to create a new page/item within your specified database. All the mapped data is inserted, creating a fully populated task entry. The new item appears in your Notion workspace exactly as if you had manually created it, but it was done automatically in seconds.

Quick Setup Guide

1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the pre-built scenario on Make.com.

2. Duplicate the Scenario: Inside Make.com, duplicate the template to your own account. This creates a copy you can edit and run.

3. Connect Your Apps: In your duplicated scenario, click the Habitica and Notion modules. Follow the prompts to authenticate and connect your personal Habitica and Notion accounts. Make.com will handle the API connections securely.

4. Configure the Notion Database: In the Notion "Create Database Item" module, specify the exact Database ID of your Notion task list. You can find this ID in the Notion database URL. Ensure the property mappings (like "Title" to "Task Name") match your database's field names.

5. Test and Activate: Create a test to-do in your Habitica account. Run the scenario once to see if it successfully creates an item in your Notion database. Check the data for accuracy. Once confirmed, schedule the scenario to run automatically (e.g., every 10 minutes) or turn it on for instant triggers.

Pro tip: Start with a simple test using a basic task. Once the connection works, you can enhance the workflow by adding more data fields or connecting additional apps like Slack for notifications.
