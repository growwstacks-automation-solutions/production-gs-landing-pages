What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Stripe for payments and QuickBooks for accounting, a major operational headache is keeping customer data synchronized between the two systems. Manually copying new customer details from Stripe into QuickBooks is a repetitive, error-prone task that wastes valuable time and can lead to financial discrepancies.

This automation solves that problem completely. Every time a new customer is created in your Stripe account—whether they sign up for a subscription, make a one-time purchase, or are added manually—this Make.com workflow instantly captures that event. It then checks if the customer already exists in QuickBooks Online and, if not, automatically creates a perfectly matched customer record. This ensures your accounting ledger is always up-to-date with your sales pipeline, enabling accurate invoicing, reporting, and revenue tracking from day one.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable sequence that acts as a bridge between Stripe and QuickBooks.

Step 1: Trigger on New Stripe Customer

The scenario is activated by a Stripe webhook, which sends an instant notification to Make.com whenever a new "customer.created" event occurs. This is the most efficient method, ensuring zero delay between the customer signing up and the sync process beginning.

Step 2: Retrieve Full Customer Details

Make.com takes the customer ID from the webhook and makes an API call back to Stripe to retrieve the complete customer profile. This includes their name, email address, billing details, and any custom metadata you store.

Pro tip: Use the Stripe Customer ID as a unique identifier in QuickBooks. This creates an unbreakable link between the records, making future updates or payment reconciliations much simpler.

Step 3: Check for Duplicates in QuickBooks

Before creating anything, the workflow performs a smart search in QuickBooks. It looks for an existing customer using a key field, typically the email address or a custom field containing the Stripe ID. This critical step prevents duplicate entries that would clutter your accounting system.

Step 4: Create the QuickBooks Customer Record

If no duplicate is found, Make.com uses the QuickBooks Online API to create a new customer. It maps the relevant Stripe data (like "Customer Name" to "Display Name" and email to "Primary Email Address") into the correct QuickBooks fields, creating a clean, usable record for your accountant.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, authorize the connections to your Stripe and QuickBooks Online accounts.
  3. Configure the Webhook: In your Stripe Dashboard, go to Developers > Webhooks. Add a new endpoint using the webhook URL provided by the first Make.com module. Select the "customer.created" event.
  4. Test the Flow: Turn the scenario on. Create a test customer in Stripe. Within seconds, verify that a corresponding customer appears in your QuickBooks Online customer list.
  5. Go Live: Once testing is successful, your automation is live. All future customers will be synced automatically.
