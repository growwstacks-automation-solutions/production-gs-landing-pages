What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Stripe for billing and QuickBooks for accounting, manual invoice reconciliation is a tedious, error-prone, and time-consuming bottleneck. Finance teams waste hours each week copying data from one platform to another, risking typos, missed invoices, and delayed financial reporting. This disconnect creates frustration during month-end closes and can lead to inaccurate books.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a real-time bridge between Stripe and QuickBooks Online. The moment a customer pays an invoice in Stripe, the workflow is triggered. It captures all the critical invoice data—customer details, line items, amounts, taxes, and payment info—and uses it to automatically create a corresponding, fully paid invoice within your QuickBooks account. Your revenue data is synchronized instantly, accurately, and without any human intervention.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical, event-driven sequence to ensure a flawless transfer of financial data.

Step 1: Monitor for Paid Stripe Invoices

The automation continuously watches your Stripe account using a webhook or scheduled check. It listens for a specific event: an invoice transitioning to the "paid" status. This is the trigger that kicks off the entire process.

Step 2: Extract and Structure Invoice Data

Once a paid invoice is detected, the workflow pulls all relevant data from Stripe's API. This includes the invoice number, customer name and email, date, line item descriptions, quantities, unit prices, subtotal, tax amounts, and the final total. It structures this data into a format that QuickBooks can understand.

Pro tip: Ensure your Stripe product names and descriptions are clear, as they will be copied directly to the QuickBooks invoice line items. This maintains clarity for your bookkeeping and customer records.

Step 3: Create the Customer in QuickBooks (If New)

Before creating the invoice, the automation checks if the customer from Stripe already exists in your QuickBooks customer list. If they are a new customer, it will automatically create a new customer record in QuickBooks using the name and email from Stripe, ensuring your CRM stays unified.

Step 4: Generate the Invoice in QuickBooks

With the customer record ready, the workflow uses the QuickBooks Online API to create a new sales invoice. It maps all the extracted Stripe data to the correct fields in QuickBooks: invoice date, due date, line items, taxes, and the total amount. The invoice is marked as paid, and the payment method is recorded, completing the financial record.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make scenario, click on the Stripe module and authenticate your Stripe account. Repeat the process for the QuickBooks Online module.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set the Stripe module to watch for "Invoice Paid" events. You may use instant webhooks for real-time sync or a scheduled poll.
  4. Map the Data: Review the data mapping between modules. Ensure fields like customer email, invoice total, and tax amounts are correctly mapped from Stripe to the corresponding QuickBooks fields.
  5. Test with a Live Invoice: Run the scenario once manually by paying a test invoice in Stripe. Verify that a correct, paid invoice appears in your QuickBooks Sandbox or live account.
  6. Activate: Turn the scenario "ON" in Make.com. Your automation is now live and will process all future paid invoices automatically.
