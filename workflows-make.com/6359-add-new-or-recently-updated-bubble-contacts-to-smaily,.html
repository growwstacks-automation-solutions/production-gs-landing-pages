What This Workflow Does

For businesses built on Bubble, capturing user signups, leads, and customer data is just the first step. The real value comes from engaging those contacts through effective email marketing. Manually exporting lists from Bubble and importing them into an email service provider like Smaily is a tedious, error-prone process that wastes valuable time and leads to outdated lists.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as a real-time bridge between your Bubble application and your Smaily account. Whenever a new "data thing" (like a User, Lead, or Contact) is created or modified in your Bubble database, the workflow is triggered. It instantly takes that contact's information—such as email, name, and any custom fields—and adds or updates them as a subscriber in your designated Smaily list.

The result is a marketing list that is always accurate and current. Your email campaigns reach the right people at the right time, directly based on their interactions with your app, without you ever having to lift a finger for data transfer.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence, ensuring data flows seamlessly from point A to point B.

Step 1: Monitor Your Bubble Database

The workflow starts by watching a specific data type in your Bubble app. You configure it to trigger on the "New thing created" or "Thing updated" event. This sets up a real-time listener for any changes in your chosen Bubble collection.

Step 2: Capture the Contact Data

When the trigger fires, Make.com captures the entire data object from Bubble. This includes all fields you've defined, such as email address, first name, last name, signup date, subscription status, or any custom properties relevant to your business.

Step 3: Format and Prepare the Data for Smaily

The workflow then maps the captured Bubble fields to the corresponding subscriber fields expected by the Smaily API. This step ensures the data structure is correct, handling any necessary formatting like date conversions or merging name fields.

Pro tip: Use this mapping step to enrich your data. You can add static values, like assigning a "Source: Bubble App" tag, or perform simple logic, like setting a default list if one isn't specified in Bubble.

Step 4: Add or Update the Subscriber in Smaily

Finally, the formatted data is sent to Smaily via its "Add Subscriber" or "Update Subscriber" module. The contact is added to your specified list in Smaily, ready to be included in your next broadcast or automation sequence. The workflow can also handle errors, like invalid emails, by logging them for review.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation live in under 30 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Bubble: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Bubble app using your Bubble API Key and app name.
  3. Set the Trigger: Configure the Bubble module to watch the correct data type (e.g., "User") for "Create" or "Update" events.
  4. Connect Smaily: Authorize the Smaily module using your Smaily subdomain and API credentials.
  5. Map Your Data: In the Smaily "Add Subscriber" module, map the fields from Bubble (like `Email`, `Name`) to the required Smaily fields.
  6. Choose a List: Select the specific Smaily email list where new contacts should be added.
  7. Test and Activate: Run a test by creating a dummy contact in Bubble. Check if it appears in your Smaily list. Once confirmed, activate the scenario.
