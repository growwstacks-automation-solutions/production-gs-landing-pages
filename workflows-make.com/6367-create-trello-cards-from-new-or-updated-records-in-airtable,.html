What This Workflow Does

For teams using Airtable as their source of truth for projects, client work, or task backlogs, manually transferring that information to a visual board like Trello is a repetitive, time-consuming chore. This disconnect creates delays, introduces errors, and means your execution platform is always one step behind your planning database.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between Airtable and Trello. Whenever a new record is added or an existing one is updated in your specified Airtable base, this Make.com workflow instantly triggers. It captures all the relevant data—task name, description, assignee, due date, labels—and creates a corresponding, fully-formed card on your chosen Trello board and list. It turns static data into dynamic action items without a single click from your team.

The result is a synchronized ecosystem where planning in Airtable automatically drives execution in Trello. Marketing teams can turn campaign briefs into tasks, software teams can convert product roadmap items into sprint cards, and sales teams can instantly create follow-up cards for new leads—all automatically.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple trigger-action principle, orchestrated by Make.com to ensure reliability and flexibility.

Step 1: Watch for Changes in Airtable

The automation starts by monitoring a specific table in your Airtable base. It can be set to watch for new records, updated records, or both. Make.com polls Airtable at regular intervals, and when it detects a change that matches your criteria, it immediately grabs the entire record with all its field data.

Pro tip: Use Airtable's "Last modified time" field as a trigger filter to catch only the most recent updates, making the workflow faster and more efficient.

Step 2: Structure and Map the Data

Once the record is retrieved, the workflow processes the data. You can use Make.com's modules to format dates, split text, or apply simple logic. The core task here is mapping: you define which Airtable field corresponds to which property of a Trello card. For example, map the "Task Title" field to the card name, the "Notes" field to the description, and the "Deadline" field to the due date.

Step 3: Create or Update the Trello Card

The final step is the action. The workflow connects to your Trello account, selects the correct board and list (you can even use data from Airtable to dynamically choose these), and creates a new card. It populates the card with all the mapped information, potentially adding members, labels, checklists, and attachments. If configured to handle updates, it can also search for an existing card linked to that Airtable record and modify it accordingly.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above. This will copy the ready-made template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, click on the Airtable module and authorize it to access your account. Repeat the process for the Trello module.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Select your Airtable base and the specific table you want to watch. Choose whether to trigger on new records, updates, or both.
  4. Map Your Fields: In the Trello "Create a Card" module, select your target board and list. Then, map the data from the previous Airtable module by connecting Airtable fields to the Trello card properties (name, description, due date, members, etc.).
  5. Test and Activate: Use the "Run once" feature to test the automation with a sample record. Verify the card appears correctly in Trello. Once satisfied, turn the scenario "ON" to let it run automatically.

Pro tip: Start simple. First, automate just the card title and description. Once that works flawlessly, add more complexity like due dates, members, and labels in subsequent iterations.
