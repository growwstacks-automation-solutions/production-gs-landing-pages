Save New Invoiced Invoices to Google Sheets

Automatically log every new invoice from Invoiced into a Google Sheets spreadsheet. Eliminate manual entry, reduce errors, and centralize your billing data.

What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Invoiced for billing, manually copying invoice details into a spreadsheet is a repetitive, error-prone task. This process wastes valuable time that could be spent on growth-focused activities and creates risk of data discrepancies between your billing system and your records.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between Invoiced and Google Sheets. Every time a new invoice is created—whether for a new client, a recurring subscription, or a project milestone—the workflow instantly captures its key details and appends them as a new row in your designated spreadsheet. This ensures your financial log is always complete, accurate, and instantly available for analysis or sharing.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple trigger-action principle, running reliably in the background on Make.com.

Step 1: Monitor Invoiced for New Invoices

The scenario starts by watching your Invoiced account for the "Invoice Created" event. This trigger is instant and does not require polling; it activates as soon as the invoice is finalized in your Invoiced dashboard.

Step 2: Extract Key Invoice Data

Once triggered, the workflow collects essential data from the new invoice. This typically includes the invoice number, client name and email, issue date, due date, total amount, tax, status, and any custom notes or line items you want to track.

Step 3: Format and Add to Google Sheets

The collected data is then structured into a clean row format and sent to your pre-configured Google Sheets spreadsheet. The workflow finds the last empty row and inserts the new invoice record, keeping your log perfectly organized in chronological order.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated "Invoice Log" spreadsheet with separate tabs for different years or statuses (e.g., Paid, Pending). This makes historical analysis and tax preparation much simpler.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, authorize the connections to your Invoiced account and your Google Drive/Sheets account.
  3. Configure the Spreadsheet: Point the "Add Row to Spreadsheet" module to your specific Google Sheets file and worksheet. Ensure the column order in the module matches your sheet's headers.
  4. Map the Data: Review the data being pulled from Invoiced. You can add or remove fields (like line items or custom fields) to match your tracking needs.
  5. Test and Activate: Create a test invoice in Invoiced. Run the scenario once to verify the data appears correctly in your sheet. Then, turn on the schedule to run automatically.
