What This Workflow Does

For businesses using MakePlans for appointments, classes, or services, cancellations are an inevitable part of operations. However, when cancellations are scattered and unlogged, you lose critical data. This workflow solves that by creating a seamless, automated bridge between MakePlans and Google Sheets.

Every time a customer cancels a booking in your MakePlans account, this automation instantly captures that event. It pulls key details—like the booking ID, customer information, service type, cancellation reason, and timestamp—and appends them as a new row in your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. This transforms isolated cancellation events into a structured, searchable database that you can analyze to improve your business.

Pro tip: Use the captured cancellation reason field to identify common pain points. If "Price too high" appears frequently, it might be time to review your value proposition or offer discounts.

How It Works

The automation runs on Make.com, acting as a smart middleman between your MakePlans account and Google Sheets. It's set up to listen for specific events and act on them without any manual intervention.

Step 1: Trigger on New Cancellation

The workflow is triggered the moment a booking status changes to "canceled" in MakePlans. Make.com's MakePlans module watches for this update in real-time or on a scheduled poll, ensuring no cancellation slips through.

Step 2: Gather Booking Data

Once triggered, the workflow fetches all relevant data from that specific booking. This typically includes the customer's name and contact details, the booked service or resource, the original date and time, the cancellation reason (if provided by the customer), and any internal notes.

Step 3: Format and Prepare Data

The raw data from MakePlans is then formatted for clean entry into Google Sheets. This step might involve calculating the lost revenue (original booking price), converting timestamps to your local time zone, or parsing the cancellation reason into a standardized category.

Step 4: Add Row to Google Sheets

The final step is to take the prepared data bundle and add it as a new row at the bottom of your specified Google Sheets spreadsheet. The connection is secure and authenticated, so your data is transferred safely. The sheet now contains a complete, timestamped record of the cancellation.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above. It will open the template in your Make.com account.
  2. Connect MakePlans: In the Make.com editor, click the MakePlans module. You'll be prompted to log into your MakePlans account and authorize the connection.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: Click the Google Sheets module. Authorize Make.com to access your Google account and select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet where you want the data to go.
  4. Test the Flow: Use the "Run once" function in Make.com. You can simulate a cancellation or use an existing one from your history to see a row appear in your sheet.
  5. Activate & Schedule: Turn the scenario "ON." Set the schedule (e.g., check for new cancellations every 15 minutes) and the automation will run continuously in the background.
