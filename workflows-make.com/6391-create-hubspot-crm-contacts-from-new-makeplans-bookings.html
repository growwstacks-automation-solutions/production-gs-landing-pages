What This Workflow Does

For service-based businesses, consultants, clinics, and agencies, every new booking represents a potential customer. But that potential is often lost when booking details are trapped in a scheduling tool, forcing your sales or admin team to manually copy-paste information into your Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system. This process is slow, prone to errors, and delays critical follow-up.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as a real-time bridge between your MakePlans booking calendar and your HubSpot CRM. The moment a client books an appointment, consultation, or service, their details are instantly and accurately transformed into a new contact record in HubSpot. This ensures your sales pipeline is always up-to-date, enabling immediate, personalized follow-up that dramatically increases conversion rates.

The business value is clear: you turn a manual, administrative task into a seamless, zero-effort process. This not only saves significant time and reduces data errors but also ensures no lead falls through the cracks because someone forgot to transfer the information. Your team can focus on selling and serving, not on data entry.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple, reliable sequence to capture and transfer data without any human intervention.

Step 1: Trigger on New MakePlans Booking

The workflow is activated the instant a new booking is created in your MakePlans account. The Make.com scenario watches for this event, capturing the entire booking payload—including customer name, email, phone, service type, date, time, and any custom form fields.

Step 2: Format and Prepare the Data

Before sending data to HubSpot, the workflow often includes a data formatting step. This might involve combining first and last names, parsing the service type into a category, or converting the booking time into your local timezone. This ensures the data lands in HubSpot in a clean, usable format.

Step 3: Create or Update Contact in HubSpot

The core action: the workflow sends the prepared data to HubSpot's API to create a new contact. It maps the MakePlans fields to corresponding HubSpot contact properties (e.g., email, phone, company name). A smart setup can also check if a contact with that email already exists to avoid duplicates, updating their record instead with the new booking information.

Step 4: Log Success and Handle Errors

Finally, the workflow confirms the contact was created successfully and can log the action for your records. If an error occurs (like a network issue), the scenario is designed to retry or send a notification to an admin, ensuring reliability.

Pro tip: Enhance this workflow by adding a step to automatically create a follow-up task for your sales rep in HubSpot or send a personalized welcome email sequence based on the booked service. This creates a full-circle automation from lead capture to first touchpoint.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the pre-built scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, authorize the connections to your specific MakePlans and HubSpot accounts.
  3. Map Your Data Fields: Configure the "Create Contact" module in HubSpot by mapping the incoming MakePlans data (like `customer_email` to HubSpot's `email` property).
  4. Set Up Duplicate Handling: Decide if you want the workflow to search for an existing contact by email before creating a new one to maintain a clean database.
  5. Test and Activate: Use the "Run once" feature in Make.com to test with a recent or test booking. Verify the contact appears correctly in HubSpot, then turn the scenario to "ON" to run automatically.
