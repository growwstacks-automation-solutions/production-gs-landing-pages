What This Workflow Does

Businesses use Google Forms for everything from customer feedback and job applications to event registrations and internal requests. However, the data collected often gets trapped in a Google Sheets spreadsheet, requiring manual work to transfer it into a functional business database like Tadabase. This process is slow, prone to errors, and creates data silos.

This automation solves that problem by creating a live bridge between Google Forms and Tadabase. Every time a respondent clicks "submit," the workflow instantly captures their entry and creates a corresponding record in your designated Tadabase table. This eliminates the lag between data collection and data availability, ensuring your team always works with the most current information in a structured, queryable format.

Pro tip: Use this automation as the foundation for a complete data pipeline. Once the record is in Tadabase, you can build relationships with other tables, trigger internal alerts, or feed the data into dashboards for real-time insights.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com's visual automation platform and operates on a simple trigger-action principle.

Step 1: New Form Submission Trigger

The workflow is activated the moment a new entry is submitted to your connected Google Form. Make.com watches for this event in real-time, capturing the entire response package, including timestamps and all answer data.

Step 2: Data Parsing & Preparation

The raw form data is parsed and prepared for Tadabase. This step can involve formatting text, converting dates, splitting full names into first and last name fields, or applying simple conditional logic to categorize the submission.

Step 3: Create Tadabase Record

The prepared data is then sent to Tadabase via its API. The workflow maps each form field to the correct column in your specified Tadabase table and executes the "Create a Record" action. A successful creation confirms the data is now live in your database.

Quick Setup Guide

You can be up and running in less than 30 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, establish connections to your Google account and your Tadabase account. This is a one-time authentication process.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Select the specific Google Form you want to monitor for new submissions.
  4. Map the Fields: In the Tadabase module, select your target application and table. Then, map each Tadabase column to the corresponding data item from the Google Forms response.
  5. Test & Activate: Run a test by submitting a dummy entry to your form. Verify the record appears correctly in Tadabase. Once confirmed, turn the scenario on. It will now run automatically for every future submission.
