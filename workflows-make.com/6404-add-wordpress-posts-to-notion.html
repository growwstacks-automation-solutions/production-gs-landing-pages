What This Workflow Does

For content teams, marketers, and bloggers, managing content across multiple platforms is a constant chore. You publish a brilliant article on your WordPress site, but your internal team documentation in Notion is still outdated. This creates confusion, slows down collaboration, and means your content assets aren't being fully leveraged.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your public website (WordPress) and your internal workspace (Notion). Every time a post is published or updated in WordPress, this workflow instantly creates or updates a corresponding page in a designated Notion database. The post title, content, author, categories, and publication date are all transferred automatically.

The result is a unified content hub. Your team can plan, edit, and discuss in Notion with the confidence that it always reflects the live site. It eliminates manual copy-pasting, reduces errors, and turns your Notion workspace into a powerful, always-current archive of your published work.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable sequence that runs on Make.com, connecting two of the most popular platforms on the web.

Step 1: WordPress Triggers the Workflow

The automation is activated by a new or updated post in your WordPress site. Using the WordPress module in Make.com, the workflow watches for specific events, such as when a post's status changes to "published." It captures the entire post object, including all its data.

Step 2: Data is Processed and Formatted

Make.com processes the incoming WordPress data. It can extract the HTML content, convert it to a format Notion understands (like markdown), and prepare other fields like the featured image URL, tags, and author name. This step ensures the information is clean and ready for Notion's database structure.

Pro tip: Use this step to filter content. For example, you can set a rule to only sync posts from a specific category (like "Blog") to Notion, keeping other post types like "Pages" on your website only.

Step 3: A Notion Page is Created or Updated

The workflow then connects to your specified Notion database and creates a new page. The WordPress post title becomes the page title, and the content is appended as blocks within the page. Custom properties in the Notion database—like "Status," "Author," or "Publish Date"—are populated with the corresponding WordPress data.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect WordPress: In the first module, add your WordPress site URL and generate an Application Password in your WordPress admin panel (Users > Your Profile > Application Passwords). Enter this as the password in Make.
  3. Connect Notion: In the Notion module, click "Add" to connect your account. You'll be prompted to log into Notion and authorize Make.com. Use the "Internal Integration" secret from your Notion settings.
  4. Configure the Database: Paste the ID of your target Notion database into the designated field. You can find this ID in the database's URL.
  5. Map the Fields (Optional): Review how the WordPress post data (title, content, etc.) is mapped to the Notion page properties and content blocks. Adjust this mapping to fit your specific database structure.
  6. Test and Activate: Run the scenario once manually to test. Publish a draft post in WordPress and check if a new page appears in your Notion database. If successful, turn the scenario "ON."
