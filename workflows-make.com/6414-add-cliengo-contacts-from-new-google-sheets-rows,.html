What This Workflow Does

For businesses using chatbots like Cliengo to capture and qualify website leads, managing the contact list can become a manual, time-consuming chore. Leads often come from various sources—contact forms, webinar sign-ups, downloadable content—and end up in a Google Sheets spreadsheet. Someone then has to manually copy and paste each new lead's details into Cliengo, a process prone to delays and errors.

This free Make.com template solves that problem completely. It creates a seamless, real-time bridge between your Google Sheets and Cliengo. Every time a new row is added to your designated spreadsheet—whether it's one lead or a hundred—the workflow automatically triggers, extracts the contact information, and creates a corresponding new contact in your Cliengo account. This ensures your chatbot can start engaging with leads instantly, without any human intervention.

The business value is clear: faster lead response times, zero manual data entry errors, and the ability to scale your lead capture efforts without adding administrative overhead. Your sales and marketing teams can focus on high-value conversations instead of repetitive copy-paste tasks.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple, reliable sequence. Once set up, it runs silently in the background, keeping your Cliengo contact list perfectly synchronized with your master lead sheet.

Step 1: Monitor Your Google Sheet

The workflow starts by watching a specific Google Sheets spreadsheet for changes. It is configured to trigger whenever a new row is added. You can use an existing lead list or the provided example template. The trigger efficiently identifies the new data without scanning the entire sheet.

Step 2: Extract the New Lead Data

Make.com captures all the data from the new row. This typically includes the lead's name, email address, phone number, source, and any other custom fields you've set up in your sheet columns, such as "Interest" or "Company."

Step 3: Create a Contact in Cliengo

The workflow then takes this bundle of data and uses the Cliengo API module within Make.com to create a new contact. It maps the columns from your Google Sheet (e.g., "Email") to the corresponding fields required by Cliengo. The contact is added to your Cliengo account immediately, ready for segmentation or automated messaging sequences.

Pro tip: Add a "Lead Source" column in your sheet. This allows you to tag contacts in Cliengo based on where they came from (e.g., "Facebook Ad," "Ebook Download"), enabling more personalized follow-up chatbot flows.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting the automation live takes about 10-15 minutes. Follow these steps after clicking "Get This Workflow."

  1. Clone the Template: In Make.com, click the provided link and create a copy of the scenario in your own workspace.
  2. Connect Google Sheets: In the first module, click to add your Google account and select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet that contains your lead list.
  3. Connect Cliengo: In the Cliengo module, click to add a new connection. You will be prompted to log into your Cliengo account and authorize Make.com to access it.
  4. Map Your Data Fields: The template has pre-set mappings. Verify that the column names from your sheet (e.g., "emailAddress") correctly correspond to the Cliengo contact fields. Adjust if your column headers are different.
  5. Test and Activate: Turn on the scenario scheduler or set it to watch for new rows. Add a test row to your Google Sheet and verify that a new contact appears in your Cliengo dashboard within moments.
