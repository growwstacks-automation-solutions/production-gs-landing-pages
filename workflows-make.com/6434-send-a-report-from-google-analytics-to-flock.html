What This Workflow Does

For marketing teams, founders, and agencies, manually checking Google Analytics is a time-consuming chore that often gets deprioritized. This leads to reactive decision-making and missed opportunities. This automation solves that by making key performance data proactive and collaborative.

The workflow automatically fetches a predefined report from Google Analytics for the previous day, formats it into a clear, readable message, and posts it directly into a designated Flock channel. It turns complex data into a simple daily digest that your entire team can see and discuss, fostering a data-driven culture without anyone lifting a finger.

Imagine starting each day with a clear snapshot of your website's performance—sessions, users, top pages, and conversion metrics—already waiting in your team chat. This eliminates the need for manual reporting, ensures consistency, and keeps everyone from the CEO to the content writer aligned on what's working.

How It Works

The automation runs on a reliable, scheduled trigger within Make.com, orchestrating a seamless flow of data from Google Analytics to your team's communication hub.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

Every weekday morning (or on a schedule you set), the Make.com scenario automatically activates. This time-based trigger is the starting pistol, ensuring your report is generated and sent without any manual initiation.

Step 2: Fetch Data from Google Analytics

The scenario connects to your Google Analytics 4 property via API. It requests a specific report for the previous day, pulling metrics like user count, session duration, bounce rate, and goal completions based on your configuration.

Pro tip: Start with a simple report focusing on 5-7 key metrics. You can always expand it later. Overloading the initial report can reduce readability and adoption.

Step 3: Parse and Format the Report

The raw data from Google Analytics is processed and structured into a clean, human-friendly format. Make.com can add emojis, simple charts (using text), and clear labels to make the data instantly understandable at a glance.

Step 4: Send to Flock Channel

The formatted report is sent as a message to a specific channel in your Flock workspace. You can tag relevant team members (like @marketing) to notify them directly, turning the report into a conversation starter for the day's planning.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Google Analytics: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Google Analytics account and select the correct GA4 property and data stream.
  3. Define Your Report: Configure the "Run Report" module. Specify the date range (typically "yesterday"), and choose the metrics (e.g., `activeUsers`, `sessions`) and dimensions (e.g., `pageTitle`) you want to include.
  4. Connect Flock: In the final module, create a connection to Flock using an incoming webhook. You'll get a unique URL from Flock to paste into Make.com. Specify the channel where the report should be posted.
  5. Test and Schedule: Run the scenario once manually to ensure the data flows correctly and the message appears in Flock as expected. Then, set the schedule trigger to run daily at your desired time (e.g., 9 AM).

Pro tip: Use the "Tools" module in Make.com to format numbers (e.g., add commas) and create simple text-based bar graphs for visual impact in the Flock message. A little formatting goes a long way in readability.
