What This Workflow Does

For businesses using ActiveCampaign for email marketing and Endorsal for collecting and managing testimonials, a major bottleneck is manual data transfer. Every new lead or customer who signs up needs to be manually added to Endorsal to request a testimonial. This process is time-consuming, prone to error, and causes delays that can result in missed feedback opportunities when the customer experience is freshest.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between your marketing automation and social proof platforms. It periodically checks ActiveCampaign for new contacts and automatically uploads them as new contacts in Endorsal. The result is a fully automated testimonial collection pipeline that works in the background, saving your team hours each month and ensuring a steady stream of potential reviewers.

Pro tip: Use this automation in tandem with a post-purchase or onboarding email sequence in ActiveCampaign. Time your testimonial request in Endorsal to trigger a few days after a positive customer interaction for the highest response rates.

How It Works

The workflow is a scheduled, event-driven process built on Make.com. It acts as a reliable middleman, ensuring data consistency between the two platforms.

Step 1: Scheduled Check for New Contacts

The scenario is triggered on a schedule you define (e.g., every hour). It connects to your ActiveCampaign account and searches for contacts created since the last check.

Step 2: Data Mapping and Validation

For each new contact found, the workflow maps the relevant fields. It extracts the contact's first name, last name, email, and critically, their phone number. Since a phone number is mandatory in Endorsal, the workflow can be configured to handle cases where it's missing, such as logging an error or using a placeholder.

Step 3: Create Contact in Endorsal

The mapped contact data is then sent via the Endorsal API to create a new contact in your specified Endorsal account. The contact is instantly ready to be added to a testimonial collection campaign.

Step 4: Error Handling and Logging

The workflow includes error-handling routes. If a contact fails to create in Endorsal (e.g., duplicate email, API issue), the error is captured and can be sent to a log or notification channel like Slack or email, allowing for quick troubleshooting without breaking the entire automation.
