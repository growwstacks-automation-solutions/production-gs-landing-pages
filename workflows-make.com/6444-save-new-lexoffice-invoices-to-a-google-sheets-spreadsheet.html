What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Lexoffice for invoicing, manually copying invoice details into a spreadsheet is a repetitive, error-prone, and time-consuming task. This process often leads to outdated records, discrepancies during reconciliation, and lost opportunities for real-time financial insight.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as a digital bridge between Lexoffice and Google Sheets. Every time a new invoice is created or finalized in your Lexoffice account, the workflow instantly triggers. It captures key details like the invoice number, date, client, amounts, and status, then appends them as a perfectly formatted new row in your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. The result is a continuously updated, centralized log of all your invoices without you lifting a finger.

Pro tip: Use the synchronized Google Sheet as a source for live financial dashboards. Connect tools like Google Data Studio or Looker Studio to visualize your revenue, outstanding invoices, and client payment trends automatically.

How It Works

The automation runs on Make.com, monitoring your Lexoffice account and writing data to Google Sheets in a reliable, secure sequence.

Step 1: Monitor for New Invoices

The workflow starts with a Lexoffice module set to watch for new "invoice created" events. This trigger can be scheduled to check at regular intervals (e.g., every hour) or can use webhooks for instant real-time updates whenever an invoice is issued.

Step 2: Extract and Structure Invoice Data

Once a new invoice is detected, Make.com retrieves the full invoice object from Lexoffice. The workflow then maps and extracts the specific fields you need—such as invoiceNumber, totalNetAmount, contactName, invoiceDate, and dueDate—structuring them into a clean data package.

Step 3: Add Row to Google Sheets

The final step takes the structured data and uses the Google Sheets module to "Add a row" to your pre-defined spreadsheet. The template maps each piece of invoice data to the correct column, ensuring your spreadsheet remains organized and ready for analysis.
