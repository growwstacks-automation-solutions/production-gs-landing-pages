What This Workflow Does

For service-based businesses, coaches, and consultants, every booking is a potential client. But the gap between someone booking a call on YouCanBook.me and that person becoming a managed contact in your CRM is often filled with manual, error-prone data entry. This delay can cost you deals.

This automation bridges that gap instantly. Whenever a new booking is confirmed in YouCanBook.me, this Make.com workflow automatically creates a corresponding contact in your Salesflare CRM. It captures the client's name, email, booking details, and any custom form fields, ensuring your sales team has immediate, accurate information to initiate personalized follow-up.

The result is a closed-loop system where your scheduling tool and CRM work as one. You stop losing leads to administrative delays and start building relationships faster, directly from the moment of intent.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable pipeline that moves data from point A to point B without any human intervention.

Step 1: Trigger on New YouCanBook.me Booking

The scenario starts by watching for new events in your YouCanBook.me account. The moment a customer completes a booking for your service, YouCanBook.me sends a webhook with all the booking data to Make.com.

Step 2: Parse and Structure the Booking Data

Make.com receives the webhook and extracts the relevant client information. This includes the attendee's name, email address, phone number, the booked service, date, time, and any answers provided in your custom booking form.

Pro tip: Use Make's data transformation tools to format names properly (e.g., "FirstName LastName") and clean phone numbers before they enter your CRM, ensuring data consistency.

Step 3: Create Contact in Salesflare

Using the structured data, the workflow then makes an API call to Salesflare. It creates a new contact in your specified account, populating fields like name, email, phone, and company. You can also add the booking details as a note or custom field on the contact record for context.

Step 4: (Optional) Trigger Follow-up Actions

Once the contact is successfully created, the workflow can be extended to trigger other actions, such as sending a confirmation email from your ESP, adding the contact to a Salesflare campaign, or posting a notification to a Slack channel for your sales team.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect YouCanBook.me: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your YouCanBook.me account. Select the specific booking page you want to monitor.
  3. Connect Salesflare: In the CRM module, add your Salesflare API key to authorize the connection.
  4. Map the Data Fields: Review the data mapping. Ensure the information from the YouCanBook.me webhook (like `attendee.email`) is correctly mapped to the Salesflare contact fields (like `Email`).
  5. Test the Flow: Make a test booking on your YouCanBook.me page. Run the scenario once to see a contact appear in your Salesflare account.
  6. Activate: Turn the scenario on. It will now run automatically for every new booking.
