What This Workflow Does

For businesses using QuickBooks for accounting and Square for point-of-sale or online payments, managing customer data across two separate systems is a constant headache. Every time a new client is added to QuickBooks for invoicing, that same information needs to be manually entered into Square to track their sales and payment history. This manual process is not only time-consuming but also prone to errors, leading to inconsistent customer records, missed sales insights, and frustrated staff.

This free Make.com workflow template solves that problem by creating a seamless, automated bridge between QuickBooks and Square. The moment a new customer is created in your QuickBooks account, the automation instantly captures that data and creates a corresponding customer profile in your Square directory. This ensures your sales team has immediate access to accurate customer information for transactions, while your accounting team maintains a single, authoritative record.

Pro tip: This automation is perfect for service-based businesses, retailers, and consultants who invoice through QuickBooks but also accept payments or make sales through Square. It ensures a customer's payment method on file in Square can be easily matched to their invoice history in QuickBooks.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple trigger-and-action principle, orchestrated by Make.com. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown:

Step 1: The Trigger in QuickBooks

The automation continuously monitors your QuickBooks Online account for a specific event: the creation of a new customer. Make.com acts as the watchman, using QuickBooks' API to detect this event in real-time.

Step 2: Data Capture and Routing

Once a new customer is detected, Make.com instantly grabs the relevant data points—such as the customer's name, email, phone number, and billing address—and prepares it for the next step. This data is formatted and mapped correctly for Square's system.

Step 3: The Action in Square

Make.com then connects to your Square account via its API and uses the captured data to create a new customer record in your Square Customer Directory. The process happens within seconds, with no human intervention required.

Step 4: Confirmation and Logging

The workflow can be configured to send a confirmation (like an internal notification or a log entry) that the sync was successful, providing peace of mind and an audit trail.

What You'll Need

To use this template successfully, ensure you have the following prerequisites:

  1. An active Make.com account (a free plan may suffice for low volume).
  2. Admin-level access to a QuickBooks Online account.
  3. Admin-level access to a Square account with Customer Directory features enabled.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the template into your own Make.com account.
  2. Connect QuickBooks: In the Make.com scenario editor, click on the QuickBooks module and authenticate your account. Grant the necessary permissions for Make to read new customers.
  3. Connect Square: Similarly, authenticate your Square account in the corresponding module, allowing Make to create customers.
  4. Map the Data Fields (Optional): Review the default data mapping (e.g., QuickBooks "Display Name" to Square "Given Name"). Adjust if your field names differ.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and create a test customer in QuickBooks. Verify that the customer appears in your Square Customer Directory within moments. Once confirmed, your automation is live.
