What This Workflow Does

For sales teams using Salesflare, a critical challenge is getting pipeline data out of the CRM for analysis, reporting, and collaboration. Managers often need to export data manually to spreadsheets for forecasting, while other departments might need visibility into the sales pipeline without CRM access. This manual process is time-consuming, prone to errors, and leads to decisions based on outdated information.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between your active CRM and a flexible spreadsheet. Every time a sales rep creates a new opportunity in Salesflare—whether it's a $5,000 deal or a $500,000 enterprise contract—this workflow instantly captures all the key details and appends them as a new row in your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. It turns your spreadsheet into a living, queryable log of your entire sales pipeline, updated the moment anything changes at the source.

How It Works

The workflow is an elegant, "set-and-forget" automation that runs silently in the background, ensuring your data is always where you need it.

Step 1: Monitor Salesflare for New Opportunities

The workflow starts by watching your Salesflare account for the "Opportunity Created" event. Using a secure connection, it listens continuously. The moment a new deal is logged by any team member, the automation is triggered, capturing the complete opportunity record.

Step 2: Structure and Prepare the Data

Make.com then maps the incoming Salesflare data into a clean, consistent format for your spreadsheet. It extracts key fields like the opportunity name, monetary value, current stage, expected close date, associated company, and owner. This step ensures the data is ready for analysis, not just a raw dump.

Step 3: Add a New Row to Google Sheets

Finally, the workflow connects to your pre-defined Google Sheets spreadsheet and appends the formatted opportunity data as a brand new row. It finds the last used row and adds the information below it, maintaining a perfect chronological history of every deal created in your pipeline.

Pro tip: Use a separate tab in your spreadsheet for "Raw Data" from this automation. Then, create a second tab with pivot tables and charts that reference this data. This keeps your reporting clean and automatically updates as new opportunities sync.

What You'll Need

  1. A Salesflare account with active opportunities and administrator access to create an API connection.
  2. A Google account with access to Google Sheets (part of any standard Google Workspace or personal Gmail account).
  3. A Make.com account (free tier is sufficient to run this specific workflow).
  4. A Google Sheets spreadsheet set up with column headers matching the data you want to capture (e.g., Date Created, Opportunity Name, Value, Stage, Company, Close Date).

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes. Follow these steps after clicking "Get This Workflow".

  1. Clone the Template: Click the link above to open the template on Make.com. Press the "Copy" button to clone it into your own Make.com workspace.
  2. Connect Salesflare: In the first module, click to add your Salesflare connection. You'll be prompted to log in and authorize Make.com to access your Salesflare data. This uses a secure OAuth process.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: In the Google Sheets module, similarly add your Google account and grant permissions. Then, select the exact spreadsheet and worksheet where you want the data to appear.
  4. Map Your Data (Optional): Review the data mapping. The template is pre-configured to pull common fields, but you can easily add or remove columns to match your specific Salesflare setup and reporting needs.
  5. Turn It On: Hit the "Run once" button to test the connection with a live opportunity. If a new row appears in your sheet, you're all set! Finally, schedule the scenario to run "immediately" so it listens for new opportunities continuously.
