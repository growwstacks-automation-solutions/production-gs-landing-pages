What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Airtable as a source of truth for leads, customers, or event attendees, manually transferring this data to your live chat platform like Crisp is a tedious and error-prone task. This disconnect creates delays in customer support, missed opportunities for engagement, and inconsistent data across your tools.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between Airtable and Crisp. Every time a new record is added to your specified Airtable table—whether from a form submission, an import, or manual entry—Make.com instantly captures it and creates a corresponding contact in your Crisp account. The contact is populated with all relevant details, making them immediately visible and actionable for your support or sales team.

The business value is clear: you save hours per week on administrative data entry, eliminate the risk of human error, and empower your team to respond to new contacts with context and speed. This turns your contact list from a static database into a dynamic, actionable asset.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple but powerful trigger-action sequence, managed entirely by Make.com without any manual intervention.

Step 1: Monitor Airtable for New Records

The workflow starts by watching a specific Airtable base and table. Make.com polls the table at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes) or uses a webhook for instant detection. When it finds a new record that matches your criteria, it immediately proceeds to the next step.

Pro tip: Use Airtable views or filters in the trigger module to only watch for records with a specific status (like "Confirmed" or "New Lead"). This prevents syncing incomplete or test data.

Step 2: Map and Format the Contact Data

Make.com extracts the relevant fields from the Airtable record, such as name, email, phone, company, and any custom notes. This data is then mapped to the corresponding fields required by Crisp's "Create a people" API. You can easily transform data here—for example, combining first and last name fields into a single full name for Crisp.

Step 3: Create the Contact in Crisp

The formatted data is sent to Crisp via a secure connection. Crisp creates a new contact in your workspace with all the provided information. The contact is instantly available in your Crisp dashboard, complete with any tags or metadata you've configured to pass along from Airtable.

Step 4: Handle Errors and Log Results

The workflow includes basic error handling. If a contact already exists (duplicate email) or if data is missing, the scenario can be configured to send an alert, update the Airtable record with a status, or simply skip the operation. This ensures reliability and provides an audit trail.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make scenario, click the Airtable module and connect your account. Repeat for the Crisp module.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Select your Airtable base, table, and optionally a view. Set the watch mode (e.g., "Watch records").
  4. Map the Data: In the Crisp "Create a people" module, map the Airtable fields to the Crisp fields. Ensure the email field is correctly mapped, as it's the unique identifier.
  5. Test and Activate: Run the scenario once to test with an existing Airtable record. Verify the contact appears correctly in Crisp. Then, activate the scenario to run automatically.

Pro tip: Start by setting the scenario to run every hour. Once you confirm it's working flawlessly, you can increase the frequency for near real-time sync.
