What This Workflow Does

For project managers, consultants, and remote teams, manually transferring tasks from a project management tool like timeBuzzer into a personal calendar is a repetitive, error-prone chore. This disconnect often leads to missed deadlines, scheduling conflicts, and a fragmented view of your actual workload versus your planned schedule.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between timeBuzzer and Google Calendar. Every new activity you log—whether it's a client call, a development task, or a review deadline—is instantly transformed into a scheduled event on your calendar. The workflow captures key details like the activity title, description, due date, and estimated duration, placing them directly into your daily view. This ensures your calendar is always an accurate, real-time reflection of your committed work, without any manual effort.

The business value is clear: it reclaims administrative time, reduces the risk of human error in scheduling, and provides a unified system for time tracking and calendar management. Teams gain transparency, individuals gain clarity, and projects gain reliability because deadlines are visually tracked and automatically respected.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on New Activity

The workflow is initiated whenever a new activity is created within your timeBuzzer account. The automation platform monitors timeBuzzer for this event, capturing the full data packet associated with the task.

Step 2: Map Task Data to Calendar Fields

The system then maps the relevant activity information to the corresponding fields in a Google Calendar event. The activity title becomes the event title, the description populates the event details, the start date/time is set, and the duration is calculated or applied.

Step 3: Create Event in Selected Calendar

Using your authenticated Google Calendar connection, the workflow creates a new event in a specific calendar you've designated (e.g., your primary work calendar). It sets the visibility, reminders, and any other default settings you've configured.

Step 4: Confirm and Log

Finally, the automation can send a confirmation to another app (like a log in Slack or an email) or simply mark the process as complete. The event now lives in your calendar, synchronized with your project management tool.

Pro tip: Use this automation to create separate calendars for different project types or clients. Filter activities in timeBuzzer by project code or tag, and direct them to specific calendars for ultra-organized scheduling.
