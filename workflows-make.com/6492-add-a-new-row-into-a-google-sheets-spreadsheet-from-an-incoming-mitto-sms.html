What This Workflow Does

For businesses using SMS for customer support, marketing, or notifications, manually tracking incoming messages is a time-consuming and error-prone task. Important details like phone numbers, timestamps, and message content can get lost in the shuffle, leading to missed follow-ups, poor customer experience, and a lack of actionable data. This creates operational blind spots and makes it impossible to analyze communication trends effectively.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your Mitto SMS platform and Google Sheets. Every time a new SMS is received via Mitto, this workflow instantly triggers and appends a new, structured row to a designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. It captures key data points automatically, turning a stream of messages into a organized, searchable database without any manual intervention.

The result is a permanent, centralized log of all SMS communications. This is invaluable for customer service teams needing a conversation history, for marketers tracking campaign responses, and for business owners who require an audit trail for compliance or dispute resolution. It transforms reactive SMS handling into a proactive, data-driven communication strategy.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable automation built on Make.com. It listens for an event from Mitto and performs a single, powerful action in Google Sheets.

Step 1: Trigger on New SMS

The scenario starts with the Mitto module configured as a "Watch SMS" trigger. Whenever an SMS message is received by your Mitto number or shortcode, Mitto instantly sends the message data (like sender, text, and timestamp) to Make.com, kicking off the automation.

Step 2: Structure the Data

Make.com receives the raw SMS data. The workflow maps this data into a clean, consistent format suitable for a spreadsheet row. This typically includes the sender's phone number, the full message body, the date and time of receipt, and any other relevant metadata provided by Mitto.

Step 3: Add Row to Google Sheets

The formatted data is then sent to the Google Sheets module. The module is configured to "Add a Row" to a specific spreadsheet and worksheet you define. Each piece of SMS data is placed into its corresponding column, creating a perfectly organized new entry in your log.

Pro tip: Use a pre-formatted Google Sheet with columns like "Timestamp", "From Number", "Message", and "Status". The automation will populate these columns in order, creating a ready-to-use database from day one.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to clone the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Prepare Your Sheet: Create a new Google Sheet or use the provided sample. Name your columns in the first row (e.g., A1: "Received At", B1: "From", C1: "Message").
  3. Connect Mitto: In the Make.com scenario, click the Mitto module and authorize the connection by logging into your Mitto account and granting permissions to Make.com.
  4. Connect Google Sheets: Similarly, authorize the Google Sheets module to access the specific spreadsheet you prepared.
  5. Map the Data: In the "Add a Row" module, select your spreadsheet and worksheet. Then, map the data from the Mitto module (like `sender` and `text`) to the corresponding column letters in your sheet.
  6. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and send a test SMS to your Mitto number. Within seconds, check your Google Sheet for the new, automatically added row. Once confirmed, your automation is live.
