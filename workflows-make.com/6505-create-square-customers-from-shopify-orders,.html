What This Workflow Does

If you sell both online via Shopify and in-person using Square, you likely have two separate, disconnected customer lists. This fragmentation hurts your business. You can't track a customer's lifetime value across channels, your marketing lacks personalization, and you miss opportunities to build loyalty with your best buyers.

This automation solves that by instantly connecting the dots. Every time a new order is placed on your Shopify store, this workflow automatically creates a corresponding customer profile in your Square Point of Sale system. The customer's name, email, phone, and address are transferred seamlessly, building a unified customer database without you lifting a finger.

The result is a complete view of each customer, whether they shop online or at your brick-and-mortar location. This enables targeted email campaigns, personalized offers, and loyalty programs that work across all sales channels, ultimately driving repeat business and increasing average order value.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable sequence that runs in the background on Make.com. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Monitor for New Shopify Orders

The automation starts by watching your Shopify store for a new "Order created" event. Make.com connects to your Shopify admin and listens in real-time. As soon as a customer completes a purchase, the workflow is triggered, capturing all the order and customer details.

Pro tip: You can filter this trigger to only run for orders from certain locations, with specific tags, or above a certain value, ensuring you only sync your most valuable customers.

Step 2: Extract and Format Customer Data

The workflow then parses the incoming Shopify data. It extracts the essential information needed for a Square customer profile: first and last name, email address, phone number, and billing/shipping addresses. This data is cleaned and formatted to match Square's expected input structure.

Step 3: Check for Existing Customers in Square

Before creating a new entry, a crucial duplicate check occurs. The workflow searches your existing Square customer directory using the customer's email address as a unique identifier. This prevents cluttering your Square account with multiple profiles for the same person.

Step 4: Create or Update the Square Customer Profile

Based on the search result, the workflow takes action. If the customer is new to Square, it creates a brand new customer profile with all the details from Shopify. If the customer already exists, it can update their existing profile with any new information (like a changed address), keeping your records current.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to copy the ready-made template to your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Shopify: In the first module, click to add a connection. You'll be prompted to log into your Shopify admin and authorize Make.com to access your store data.
  3. Connect Square: In the Square module, create a new connection using your Square Developer application credentials (Client ID and Client Secret).
  4. Test the Flow: Use the "Run once" feature in Make.com to test the automation. Place a test order in your Shopify store (or use a recent real order) and watch as it creates a customer in your Square account.
  5. Activate: Once testing is successful, turn the scenario's scheduler to "On." It will now run automatically for every new order.

Pro tip: Start by running the scenario in "test mode" or on a limited set of orders (e.g., only orders with a specific tag) to ensure it works perfectly before syncing your entire customer base.
