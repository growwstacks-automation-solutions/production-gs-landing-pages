What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses, a sale is just the beginning of the customer relationship. The critical next step is capturing that new customer's information in your CRM to enable effective marketing, support, and future sales. Manually copying data from Shopify orders into Agile CRM is a tedious, error-prone task that often gets delayed or forgotten.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. Every time a new order is placed in your Shopify store, this system instantly creates a corresponding contact record in Agile CRM. It captures the customer's name, email, phone, address, and order details, ensuring your sales and marketing teams have immediate, accurate data to work with. This turns a simple transaction into the start of a managed customer journey.

How It Works

The automation acts as a real-time bridge between your e-commerce platform and your CRM, operating silently in the background.

Step 1: Monitor for New Shopify Orders

The workflow is triggered the moment a new order is created in your Shopify store. Make.com watches for this event continuously, ensuring no customer slips through the cracks, even during high-volume sales periods.

Step 2: Extract and Format Customer Data

It then pulls the relevant customer information from the order: billing/shipping details, contact information, and key order data like the purchased items and total value. This data is structured into the format Agile CRM expects for a new contact.

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to check for existing contacts by email before creating a new one. This prevents duplicates and updates returning customer records with their latest order info.

Step 3: Create or Update the Agile CRM Contact

Finally, the workflow sends the formatted data to Agile CRM via its API, creating a new contact or updating an existing one. The contact is instantly available in your CRM, ready to be tagged, added to lists, or targeted with automated campaigns.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Get the template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the ready-made scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Shopify: In the first module, authenticate your Shopify store by following Make.com's secure OAuth process.
  3. Connect Agile CRM: In the CRM module, add your Agile CRM API key and email to establish the connection.
  4. Map the data: Review the default data mapping (like customer email to contact email). Adjust if you want to send additional fields from Shopify.
