What This Workflow Does

In today's remote and hybrid work environments, ensuring everyone has the correct meeting link at the right time is a constant, low-value administrative task. Missed links lead to delayed starts, frustrated participants, and lost productivity. This automation solves that by creating a direct, real-time bridge between your GoToMeeting scheduler and your team's communication hub on Telegram.

Every time a new meeting is created in GoToMeeting, this workflow triggers instantly. It captures the meeting details, including the title, time, and—most importantly—the unique join link. The workflow then processes this link, optionally shortening it for cleaner messages, and posts a formatted notification directly into a designated Telegram chat, group, or channel. This means your team is informed the moment a meeting is set, without you lifting a finger.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple "trigger-and-action" principle, seamlessly connecting two powerful platforms.

Step 1: Monitoring GoToMeeting

The scenario starts by watching your GoToMeeting account for new events. Using Make.com's GoToMeeting module, it polls your account at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes) or uses a webhook if available, to detect any newly scheduled meetings. It filters for relevant meetings, ensuring only the ones meant for your team trigger the notification.

Step 2: Processing the Meeting Data

Once a new meeting is detected, the workflow extracts key information: the meeting topic, start time, duration, and the lengthy join URL. A URL shortener module (like Bitly) can be integrated here to create a tidy, trackable link that looks professional and is easy to click on mobile devices.

Pro tip: Use the data formatting tools in Make to create a clear, consistent message template. Include the meeting title, time in the team's local timezone, and a clear call-to-action like "Click to join."

Step 3: Sending the Telegram Alert

The final step uses the Telegram Bot API via Make.com. The formatted message, complete with the shortened link, is sent automatically to a pre-defined Telegram chat ID. This could be a broadcast channel for the whole company, a specific project group, or a direct message to a team leader for redistribution.

What You'll Need

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available).
  2. A GoToMeeting organizer account with API access or the ability to generate meeting data.
  3. A Telegram account and a Telegram Bot created via @BotFather. You'll need the bot token and the chat ID of the group/channel where notifications should be sent.
  4. (Optional) An account with a URL shortener service like Bitly for cleaner links.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and clone it into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect GoToMeeting: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your GoToMeeting account. Set the watch interval for new meetings.
  3. Configure the Message: In the router or text parser module, customize the notification message. Use placeholders for the meeting title, time, and link.
  4. Connect Telegram: In the Telegram module, input the bot token from @BotFather and the target chat ID. Send a test message to verify the connection.
  5. Activate & Test: Turn the scenario on and schedule a test meeting in GoToMeeting. Within minutes, you should see the alert pop up in your Telegram group.
