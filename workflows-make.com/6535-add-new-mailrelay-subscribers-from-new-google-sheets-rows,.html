What This Workflow Does

Manually managing email lists is a silent productivity killer for marketers and business owners. Every time you collect a new lead from a website form, event, or purchase, you face the tedious task of opening a spreadsheet, copying the email and name, and then pasting it into your email marketing platform. This process is not only slow but also highly error-prone, leading to missed subscribers, typos in email addresses, and delayed campaign outreach.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between Google Sheets and Mailrelay. Whenever a new row is added to your designated Google Sheet—whether by a form submission, a team member, or another app—the workflow automatically triggers. It reads the new contact details and adds them as a subscriber to your specified Mailrelay list. The result is a real-time, accurate, and hands-free way to grow your audience, ensuring no lead is ever forgotten in a spreadsheet again.

How It Works

The workflow runs on Make.com, monitoring your Google Sheet and acting as a smart connector to Mailrelay. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Monitor the Google Sheet

The scenario starts by watching a specific Google Sheet for new rows. You define the spreadsheet and worksheet where your contact data is collected. The trigger is set to activate instantly when a new row is added.

Step 2: Extract the New Contact Data

Once triggered, the workflow captures the data from the new row. This typically includes the subscriber's email address, first name, last name, and any other columns you've configured, such as custom fields or tags for segmentation in Mailrelay.

Pro tip: Structure your Google Sheet with clear column headers (e.g., 'Email', 'First Name', 'Last Name'). This makes mapping the data to Mailrelay fields straightforward and error-free.

Step 3: Add Subscriber to Mailrelay

The core action: the workflow sends an API call to your Mailrelay account. It uses the captured data to create a new subscriber in your chosen list. The process respects Mailrelay's subscription rules and can handle basic field mapping automatically.

Step 4: Log the Result (Optional)

For reliability, you can add a module to log the outcome—success or failure—to another sheet or a notification app. This gives you visibility into the automation's performance and helps troubleshoot any issues, like an invalid email format.
