What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Landingi to capture leads, client inquiries, or project requests, the manual process of transferring that data into a project management tool like Trello is a silent productivity killer. It creates delays, introduces errors, and causes important requests to be forgotten in crowded inboxes or spreadsheets.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your landing pages and your task board. Every time a visitor submits a form on your Landingi page, this Make.com scenario triggers instantly. It captures all the submitted data—name, email, message, custom fields—and uses it to create a new, detailed card on your specified Trello board. The card can be placed in a specific list (like "New Leads" or "To-Do"), include labels, attachments, and assigned team members, turning a form submission into an immediate, trackable action item.

The result is a closed-loop system where marketing capture directly fuels operational execution. Your sales or service team can start working on a request within minutes of it being submitted, dramatically improving conversion rates and customer satisfaction.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple, reliable sequence managed by Make.com.

Step 1: Trigger on New Landingi Submission

The workflow starts by watching your Landingi account for new form submissions. Make.com connects to Landingi via API and monitors your forms in real-time. The moment a submission is recorded, the automation wakes up and grabs the entire payload of data.

Step 2: Parse and Structure the Form Data

Make.com then processes the incoming data. It can filter submissions based on the specific form ID, extract individual field values (like "Full Name," "Email Address," "Project Description"), and even handle file uploads by retrieving the download links. This step ensures clean, organized data is ready for Trello.

Step 3: Create the Trello Card

Finally, the workflow connects to your Trello account via its API. It uses the structured data to create a new card on your chosen board and list. You can map form fields directly to the card's title, description, checklist, or comments. For example, the card title could be "New Lead: [Name]" and the description could contain their contact details and inquiry.

Pro tip: Use Trello's custom fields or labels to categorize cards automatically. For instance, if a form has a "Service Type" dropdown, you can set the automation to add a corresponding label (e.g., "Design," "Consulting") to the new card for instant visual sorting.

What You'll Need

To set up this workflow, ensure you have the following accounts and access ready:

  1. A Make.com account (free tier is sufficient to run this automation).
  2. An active Landingi account with at least one published form collecting submissions.
  3. A Trello account with a board and list where you want the new cards to appear.
  4. API credentials (keys/tokens) for both Landingi and Trello, which you'll provide during the Make.com setup. These are easily obtained from each platform's settings.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to deploy the template in under 15 minutes:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com. Press the "Copy" button to create your own private scenario.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In your new scenario, click on the Landingi module and authorize Make.com to access your account. Repeat the process for the Trello module.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Select the specific Landingi form you want to monitor from the dropdown menu in the trigger module.
  4. Map the Data: In the Trello "Create a Card" module, choose your target board and list. Then, map the form fields from Landingi to the desired fields in Trello (e.g., map "Email" to the card description).
  5. Test & Activate: Use the "Run once" feature to test the workflow. Submit a test form on your Landingi page and verify a card appears in Trello. Once confirmed, turn the scenario ON.
