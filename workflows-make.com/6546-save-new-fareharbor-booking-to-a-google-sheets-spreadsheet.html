What This Workflow Does

Businesses using WhatsApp for customer communication often drown in a flood of unstructured messages. Is a message a sales inquiry, a support complaint, or a booking request? Manually sorting and routing these conversations is slow, error-prone, and doesn't scale. This leads to delayed responses, missed opportunities, and frustrated customers.

This automation solves that by acting as an intelligent receptionist for your WhatsApp channel. Every time a new message arrives, it is instantly sent to Google Dialogflow—Google's conversational AI platform—which analyzes the text and detects the user's intent. The workflow then uses this intent to trigger specific actions, such as creating a support ticket, logging a lead in your CRM, sending an automated acknowledgment, or escalating to a human agent.

By automating intent detection, you transform raw messages into structured, actionable data. This enables proactive customer service, efficient lead qualification, and ensures every message is handled according to its priority and context, 24/7.

How It Works

The workflow connects WhatsApp, Make.com, and Google Dialogflow to create a seamless, intelligent messaging pipeline.

Step 1: Capture New WhatsApp Messages

The workflow is triggered by a new incoming message in your connected WhatsApp Business account (via the official API or a cloud-based service). Make.com captures the message content, sender number, and timestamp instantly.

Step 2: Analyze Intent with Dialogflow

The message text is sent to the Google Dialogflow API. Here, your pre-trained Dialogflow "agent" compares the message against your defined "intents"—like "book_appointment," "report_issue," or "ask_pricing." Dialogflow returns the matched intent name and key extracted parameters (e.g., a date, product name, or order ID).

Pro tip: The accuracy of this step depends on how well you train your Dialogflow agent. Provide plenty of example phrases for each intent, including synonyms and common misspellings your customers use.

Step 3: Route & Take Action Based on Intent

Based on the detected intent, the workflow branches into different paths. For example, a "support" intent could create a ticket in Zendesk, while a "sales" intent adds the contact to a Salesforce campaign and notifies the sales team via Slack.

Step 4: Log Results and Close the Loop

All interactions are logged for tracking and analysis. The workflow can record the message, intent, action taken, and timestamp in a Google Sheet or your database. Optionally, it can send an immediate, context-aware reply back to the customer via WhatsApp to confirm their request is being handled.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Use the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect WhatsApp: In the first module, set up a webhook or use the WhatsApp module to connect your WhatsApp Business account as the trigger.
  3. Configure Dialogflow: In the Google Dialogflow module, paste your GCP service account JSON key and specify your Dialogflow Agent ID and the correct language code.
  4. Define Your Routes: Set up router modules after the intent detection. Add filters for each intent (e.g., "intent = 'support_request'") and connect the appropriate actions for each branch.
  5. Map Your Actions: For each route, connect the apps where actions should happen. Map the data from the WhatsApp message and Dialogflow response to the fields in your CRM, spreadsheet, or notification tool.
  6. Test Thoroughly: Send test WhatsApp messages that match your defined intents. Verify the correct intent is detected and the right actions are triggered in your connected apps.
