What This Workflow Does

Every business collecting leads online faces the same problem: data pollution. Up to 20% of email addresses submitted via forms can be fake, mistyped, or disposable. This wastes sales time, damages email sender reputation, and clutters your precious CRM with useless contacts. Manually checking each submission is slow and unsustainable as you scale.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a seamless, intelligent gatekeeper for your lead funnel. It connects your Google Forms directly to professional email verification via DeBounce and then into HubSpot CRM. The moment someone submits a form, their email is validated in real-time. Only genuine, deliverable addresses become new contacts in HubSpot, ready for your sales team to engage. Invalid submissions are filtered out, keeping your database clean and your team focused on real opportunities.

How It Works

The automation acts as a smart bridge between your form, verification service, and CRM, executing a precise sequence of steps for every submission.

Step 1: Trigger from Google Forms

The workflow is activated instantly whenever a new response is submitted to your connected Google Form. It captures all the response data, with a primary focus on the email address field.

Step 2: Email Verification with DeBounce

The captured email address is sent to the DeBounce API. DeBounce performs a deep check, verifying syntax, domain validity, mailbox existence, and risk score (e.g., if it's a disposable or role-based address). It returns a clear result: "Valid", "Invalid", or "Risky".

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to also check for "catch-all" domains. While technically valid, these domains accept all emails and can lower engagement rates. You might choose to flag them for manual review.

Step 3: Decision & Routing

A router module evaluates DeBounce's result. If the email is classified as "Valid", the workflow proceeds to create a CRM contact. If it's "Invalid" or "Risky", the workflow can branch to a different path—logging the submission to a separate sheet, sending a notification, or simply stopping.

Step 4: Contact Creation in HubSpot

For valid emails, the workflow maps the form data (like name, company, phone number) to the corresponding contact properties in HubSpot. It then uses the HubSpot module to create a new contact record instantly. You can even add tags or set lead scores based on the form answers at this stage.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting this automation live takes about 30 minutes. Follow these steps after cloning the template in Make.com.

  1. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, update each module's connection. Authorize access to your specific Google account, input your DeBounce API key, and connect your HubSpot portal.
  2. Select Your Form: Point the Google Forms module to the exact form you want to use as the trigger.
  3. Map the Data: Ensure the email address field from your form is correctly mapped to the DeBounce verification module. Then, map all other form fields (First Name, Last Name, etc.) to the corresponding HubSpot contact properties.
  4. Set Your Validation Rules: Decide what DeBounce result you consider "Valid" (e.g., only "Valid", not "Risky"). Adjust the router's filter conditions accordingly.
  5. Test and Activate: Run a few test submissions using real and test email addresses. Check that valid ones appear in HubSpot and invalid ones are handled as desired. Once confirmed, turn the scenario on.
