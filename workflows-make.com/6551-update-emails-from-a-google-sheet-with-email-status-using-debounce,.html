What This Workflow Does

Maintaining a clean email list is one of the most overlooked yet critical aspects of digital marketing and sales operations. Businesses collect emails from various sources—website forms, lead magnets, event sign-ups, and purchases—but without validation, these lists quickly accumulate invalid, risky, or disposable addresses. This decay directly hurts deliverability, damages sender reputation, and wastes marketing budget.

This automated workflow solves that problem proactively. Every time a new row containing an email address is added to your designated Google Sheet, the system instantly triggers. It sends the email to DeBounce, a professional email validation service, for real-time verification. The result—whether the email is Valid, Invalid, Risky, or Unknown—is then written back into your spreadsheet automatically. This creates a self-cleaning, always-accurate email database without any manual intervention.

How It Works

The automation follows a precise, reliable sequence to ensure every new email is verified and your records are updated.

Step 1: Monitor Google Sheets for New Rows

The workflow starts by watching your specified Google Sheet for changes. It's configured to trigger whenever a new row is added. The system captures the email address from the designated column, along with any associated data you want to track, like the lead's name or source.

Step 2: Send Email to DeBounce for Validation

The captured email address is immediately sent to the DeBounce API. DeBounce performs multiple checks: syntax validation, domain/MX record verification, and mailbox existence confirmation (without sending an email). It also screens for disposable email addresses, role-based accounts (like info@), and other risk factors.

Pro tip: Use the "Risky" status from DeBounce to create a separate segment for follow-up. These emails might be valid but belong to catch-all domains or temporary inboxes—different messaging or verification steps can improve engagement.

Step 3: Parse the Validation Result

Make.com receives the detailed response from DeBounce. The workflow logic parses this response to extract the core status and often a quality score or reason code. This data is formatted for clear understanding, turning technical API feedback into simple status labels like "Valid - Safe to Send" or "Invalid - Domain Does Not Exist".

Step 4: Update the Original Google Sheet Row

Finally, the workflow updates the very same row in Google Sheets that triggered the process. It writes the validation status, and optionally the score or timestamp, into new columns you specify. This closes the loop, leaving you with an auditable, clean record where the data hygiene process is transparent and complete.

What You'll Need

To implement this workflow successfully, you'll need a few accounts and basic setup:

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available). This is the automation platform that orchestrates the entire process.
  2. A Google Sheets document with your email list. Ensure you have edit access and the sheet is set up with a clear column for email addresses.
  3. A DeBounce account and API key. Sign up for a DeBounce plan that offers API access; they have pay-as-you-go options suitable for most businesses.
  4. Basic understanding of your data flow. Know where new emails are added to your sheet (manually, via form, etc.) and decide which columns should store the validation results.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automated email validation system running in under 30 minutes:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the provided Make.com scenario into your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, establish the connections (called "modules") to your specific Google Sheet and your DeBounce account using your API key.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Point the Google Sheets module to watch the correct spreadsheet and worksheet. Set it to trigger on "New Row".
  4. Map the Data: Tell the workflow which column in your sheet contains the email address to validate. Map this field to the DeBounce module.
  5. Set the Update Action: Configure the final Google Sheets "Update Row" module. Specify which columns should be populated with the validation status (e.g., "Status", "Last Validated").
  6. Test and Activate: Turn on the scenario and test it by manually adding a new email row to your sheet. Check that the status updates correctly within seconds.
