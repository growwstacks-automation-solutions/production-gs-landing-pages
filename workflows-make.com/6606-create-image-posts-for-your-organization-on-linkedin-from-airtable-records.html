What This Workflow Does

Manually creating and posting images on LinkedIn is a repetitive, time-consuming task that often gets deprioritized. Marketing teams have great visual content—product shots, event photos, infographics—sitting in Airtable databases, but turning each record into a social post requires downloading, formatting, writing captions, and publishing. This process can take 15–20 minutes per post, leading to inconsistent posting schedules and missed engagement opportunities.

This automation solves that by creating a direct pipeline from your Airtable content repository to your LinkedIn company page. Whenever a new record with an image is added to your designated Airtable base, the workflow automatically downloads the image, formats it for LinkedIn, composes a post using text from your record fields, and publishes it directly. It transforms your Airtable from a passive database into an active content publishing engine, ensuring your LinkedIn feed stays fresh and engaging without manual intervention.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on New Airtable Records

The workflow is activated whenever a new record appears in your specified Airtable table. It watches for records that contain an image attachment field, filtering out text-only entries to focus on visual content ready for posting.

Step 2: Download and Prepare the Image

The automation retrieves the image file URL from the Airtable attachment field, downloads it to a temporary location, and can optionally resize or format it to meet LinkedIn's optimal image specifications (e.g., aspect ratio, file size).

Step 3: Build the LinkedIn Post Content

Using other fields from the same Airtable record—such as "Post Title," "Description," "Hashtags," or "Call to Action"—the workflow constructs a compelling post caption. It intelligently combines these elements into a well-structured message ready for publishing.

Step 4: Publish to Your LinkedIn Company Page

Via the official LinkedIn API, the automation authenticates with your company page credentials and creates a new post. It attaches the prepared image and publishes the composed text, making the content live on your feed instantly or on a schedule you define.

Pro tip: Add a "Review" step before publishing by routing posts to a Slack channel for quick team approval. This maintains quality control while still automating 90% of the process.
