Automatically Add Zoom Registrants from Google Sheets

What This Workflow Does

Managing event registrations is a repetitive, error-prone task. You collect names and emails in a Google Sheet, then someone has to manually copy each one into Zoom to send invites. This wastes valuable time and leads to mistakes—missed emails, typos, and delayed invites that frustrate potential attendees.

This automation solves that by creating a direct bridge between your registration sheet and your Zoom account. Whenever a new row is added to your designated Google Sheet, the workflow automatically retrieves that registrant's details and adds them as an attendee to your specified Zoom meeting or webinar. It turns a 30-minute manual chore into a 30-second automated process that runs flawlessly 24/7.

The business value is clear: you reclaim hours each week, ensure a 100% accurate transfer of data, and provide a professional, instant registration experience that increases actual attendance rates for your meetings, training sessions, or webinars.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, trigger-based automation built on Make.com. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Trigger on New Sheet Row

The scenario watches your Google Sheet for any changes. The most common trigger is when a new row is added (e.g., from a Google Form submission or manual entry). It immediately captures the data from that row, such as email, first name, last name, and the target meeting ID.

Step 2: Process Registrant Data

Make.com parses the data from the sheet. This step can include simple data formatting or routing logic—for instance, checking if the email is valid or determining which specific Zoom meeting the person should be added to based on a column value.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated "Meeting ID" column in your Sheet. This allows one automation to handle registrations for multiple different Zoom events dynamically.

Step 3: Add Registrant to Zoom

The core action: the workflow uses the Zoom module in Make.com to authenticate with your Zoom account and execute the "Add Meeting Registrant" or "Add Webinar Panelist" API call. It passes the captured email and name seamlessly.

Step 4: Confirm & Log (Optional)

After success, the workflow can update the original Google Sheet row with a status (e.g., "Invite Sent"), send a confirmation email to the registrant via Gmail or another service, or log the action in a separate spreadsheet for audit purposes.

What You'll Need

To use this template, you'll need a few things set up beforehand:

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available).
  2. A Zoom account with admin privileges to generate API credentials (OAuth or JWT).
  3. A Google Sheet with your registrant data. It should have columns for at least Email and First Name. A column for the specific Zoom Meeting/Webinar ID is highly recommended.
  4. Edit access to the Google Sheet so Make.com can watch for changes and optionally update status.
  5. (Optional) A Gmail or other email service account if you wish to send automated confirmation emails.

Quick Setup Guide

Get your automation live in under 15 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Google Sheets: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your specific Google Sheet and select the worksheet to monitor.
  3. Connect Zoom: In the Zoom module, authenticate your Zoom account using OAuth. Ensure you grant the necessary permissions to manage meetings and registrants.
  4. Map Your Data: Map the columns from your Google Sheet (like `Email` and `First Name`) to the corresponding fields in the Zoom "Add Registrant" action. Point the Meeting ID field to your Sheet's Meeting ID column or enter a static ID.
  5. Test with One Row: Run the scenario once with a single test registrant in your Sheet. Verify the person appears in your Zoom meeting's registrant list.
  6. Activate & Schedule: Turn on the scenario's schedule. Set it to run every few minutes or in real-time based on the Webhook trigger for instant processing.
