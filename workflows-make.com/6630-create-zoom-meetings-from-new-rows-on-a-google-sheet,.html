What This Workflow Does

Manually creating Zoom meetings from a list in a spreadsheet is a tedious, error-prone task. You have to copy-paste details like topic, date, time, and attendee emails, then manually distribute the join links back to the team or clients. This process wastes valuable time and often leads to missed meetings or incorrect details.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Google Sheets and Zoom. Whenever a new row is added to your designated spreadsheet—for a client call, team meeting, or webinar—the workflow instantly creates a corresponding Zoom meeting with all the correct settings. It then completes the loop by writing the generated Join URL and Shareable Invite Link directly back into the sheet, creating a perfect, self-updating record.

How It Works

The workflow runs on a schedule or in real-time, acting as your automated meeting coordinator.

Step 1: Watch for New Sheet Entries

The automation starts by monitoring a specific Google Sheet for new rows. It can be triggered on a schedule (e.g., every 15 minutes) or configured to watch for changes instantly using Google Sheets' watch feature.

Step 2: Extract Meeting Details

For each new row, the workflow reads the relevant data from predefined columns. This typically includes the meeting topic, start date and time, duration, agenda, and email addresses of attendees.

Step 3: Create the Zoom Meeting

Using the Zoom API module, the workflow takes the extracted data and creates a scheduled meeting in your Zoom account. It applies all specified settings, such as password protection, waiting room, and automatic recording preferences.

Step 4: Update the Spreadsheet

Once Zoom returns the unique meeting details—most importantly the Join URL and the Share URL—the workflow writes these links back into the original row on the Google Sheet. This instantly provides everyone with access to the correct meeting link.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated "Status" column in your sheet (e.g., "Meeting Created", "Link Sent"). The automation can update this status, giving you a clear visual dashboard of all scheduled meetings and their current state.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to clone the scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make scenario, establish the connections (called "modules") to your specific Google Sheet and your Zoom account. This is a simple OAuth click-through process.
  3. Map Your Sheet: Point the Google Sheets module to your actual spreadsheet URL and specify the worksheet name. Ensure your column headers match what the automation expects.
  4. Configure Zoom Settings: Review the Zoom module settings. You can set default options like enabling a waiting room, requiring a passcode, or allowing automatic recording.
  5. Test & Activate: Run a test by adding a new row to your sheet. Check that a meeting is created in Zoom and that the links are written back correctly. Once verified, activate the scenario to run on your preferred schedule.
