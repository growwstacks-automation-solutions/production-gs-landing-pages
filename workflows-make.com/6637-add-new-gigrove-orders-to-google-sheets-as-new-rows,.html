What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses using Gigrove, managing order information often involves a tedious, manual process: checking the platform, copying customer details, product info, and totals, then pasting them into a spreadsheet for record-keeping, reporting, or accounting. This is not only time-consuming but also prone to human error, leading to inaccurate sales data, delayed reporting, and potential fulfillment mistakes.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a digital bridge between your Gigrove store and Google Sheets. Every time a customer completes a purchase, the workflow is triggered automatically. It captures the new order details and appends them as a fresh row in your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. The result is a continuously updated, centralized, and accurate log of all your sales activity without you or your team lifting a finger.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple, reliable sequence to ensure no order is missed and all data is transferred correctly.

Step 1: Monitor for New Orders

The scenario starts with a "Watch Orders" module from Gigrove. It continuously monitors your Gigrove store for any new order creation events. This is the trigger that kicks off the entire automation process.

Step 2: Extract and Structure Order Data

Once a new order is detected, Make.com retrieves the complete order payload. This includes essential data points like the unique Order ID, customer name, email, shipping address, the items purchased (with names, SKUs, quantities, and prices), the order total, status, and the timestamp.

Pro tip: You can customize which data points are captured. Focus on the fields most critical for your reporting, like product variants or custom order notes, to tailor the log to your business needs.

Step 3: Add a New Row to Google Sheets

The final step is where the magic happens. The workflow connects to your specified Google Sheets file and uses the "Add a Row" module. It maps each piece of extracted Gigrove data (e.g., Order ID, Customer Email, Total) to a corresponding column in your spreadsheet. A new row is instantly created, populating your live sales log.

Quick Setup Guide

You can be up and running in under 10 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Gigrove: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Gigrove account by logging in when prompted.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: In the final module, authorize the connection to Google and select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet where you want orders to appear.
  4. Map Your Data: Review the data mapping. Ensure the information from Gigrove (like 'order.total') is going into the correct Sheets column (like 'Total Amount').
  5. Test & Activate: Create a test order in Gigrove or use an existing one. Turn on the scenario and verify that a new row appears in your Sheets with the correct data.
