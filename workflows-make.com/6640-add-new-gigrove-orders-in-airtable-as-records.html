What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses using Gigrove, managing orders often involves juggling between the sales platform and separate spreadsheets or databases like Airtable. Manually copying customer details, product info, and order totals is not only time-consuming but also prone to human error, leading to data discrepancies, delayed reporting, and operational inefficiencies.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between Gigrove and Airtable. Every time a new order is placed, the workflow triggers automatically. It captures the complete order payload—including items, pricing, customer shipping information, and timestamps—and creates a corresponding, structured record in your designated Airtable base. This transforms Airtable into a live, queryable database of all your sales activity without any manual intervention.

The result is a single source of truth for your e-commerce operations. You gain immediate visibility into new sales, can track trends over time, and have clean, organized data ready for customer service, inventory management, and financial analysis.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple but powerful "trigger-action" sequence, orchestrated by Make.com.

Step 1: Monitoring Gigrove for New Orders

The scenario starts with a Gigrove module set to watch for a specific event: the creation of a new order. You can configure it to check at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes) or use webhooks for instant triggering. When a new order is detected, Make.com immediately pulls all its associated data into the workflow.

Step 2: Structuring and Mapping the Data

Once the order data is captured, the workflow can optionally include routers or data transformation modules. This step ensures the information from Gigrove is correctly formatted to match the field structure in your Airtable table. For example, it might combine first and last name into a single field or convert a timestamp into a more readable date format.

Pro tip: Use this mapping stage to add internal flags or tags. For instance, you could add a "High Value" tag for orders over a certain amount, which can later be used for customer segmentation in Airtable.

Step 3: Creating the Airtable Record

The final and most critical action is performed by the Airtable module. It takes the processed order data and uses it to create a new record (row) in your specified table. Each piece of information—order ID, customer email, product SKUs, total amount, and status—is placed into its corresponding column in Airtable. The process is complete in seconds, making the order data instantly available for your team.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the cloned scenario, click on the Gigrove and Airtable modules. Authenticate and connect your actual Gigrove store and your target Airtable base.
  3. Configure the Trigger: In the Gigrove module, set the trigger event to "New Order" and define the polling interval that suits your sales volume.
  4. Map Your Airtable Fields: In the Airtable "Create a Record" module, select your specific base and table. Then, map each field from the incoming Gigrove data (like `orderTotal`, `customerEmail`) to the correct column in your Airtable table.
  5. Test and Activate: Use the "Run Once" feature in Make.com to test the workflow with a recent real order. Verify the record appears correctly in Airtable, then turn the scenario to "On" to automate all future orders.
