What This Workflow Does

For professionals, freelancers, and teams using RescueTime, a common frustration is data isolation. You get a brilliant daily summary of your productivity, focus time, and distractions, but it lives inside RescueTime. To spot long-term trends, correlate productivity with project outcomes, or create reports for clients, you need to manually copy that data into a spreadsheet—a tedious, forgettable task.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between RescueTime and Google Sheets. Every morning, when RescueTime generates your daily summary report, this Make.com workflow triggers instantly. It captures key metrics—like total time logged, productive time, distracting time, and your productivity pulse—and appends them as a new row in your designated Google Sheet. The result is a continuously growing, timestamped log of your work habits, ready for analysis.

The business value is clear: you transform raw activity data into actionable business intelligence without lifting a finger. This isn't just about saving a few minutes; it's about building a foundational dataset that informs better work habits, accurate project estimations, and evidence-based discussions about productivity with your team.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable automation with a simple trigger and action.

Step 1: Trigger on New Daily Report

The scenario starts by watching your RescueTime account. It uses the "Watch Daily Summaries" module, which polls for a new daily summary report. This report is typically generated shortly after midnight, capturing the previous day's activity.

Step 2: Extract Key Productivity Metrics

Once the new report is detected, Make parses the data. It extracts the most valuable fields, such as the date, total seconds spent on the computer, seconds spent on productive activities, seconds spent on distracting activities, and your overall productivity pulse score. You can customize which exact data points are captured.

Step 3: Add a Row to Google Sheets

The final step takes the structured data and uses the Google Sheets "Add a Row" module. It connects to your specified spreadsheet and appends the new data into the next empty row. Each day's summary becomes a new line in your personal or team productivity ledger.

Pro tip: Use the date from the RescueTime report as the primary row identifier. This allows you to easily sort, filter, and create time-series charts in Sheets to visualize your productivity journey over weeks and months.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes.

  1. Get the template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above. It will clone the scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect RescueTime: In the first module, click to add your RescueTime account. You'll be prompted to log in and authorize Make.com to read your daily summaries.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: In the "Add a Row" module, connect your Google account and select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet where you want the data to go.
  4. Map the data: Ensure the data points from RescueTime (like total_seconds, productive_seconds) are correctly mapped to the corresponding columns in your Google Sheet.
  5. Turn it on: Activate the scenario. It will now run automatically every day after your RescueTime daily summary is ready.

Pro tip: Schedule a weekly reminder to review your new Google Sheets dashboard. Look for patterns: which days are most productive? What times of day are you most distracted? Use these insights to intentionally structure your upcoming week.
