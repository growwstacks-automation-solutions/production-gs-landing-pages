What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Reamaze for customer support, a critical challenge is data silos. Valuable customer information from support chats and tickets lives separately from your main contact directory in Google Contacts. This disconnect forces teams to manually copy-paste contact details, a process that is time-consuming, prone to errors, and often leads to missed opportunities for follow-up.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Reamaze and Google Contacts. Every time a new contact is created in your Reamaze helpdesk—whether from a new chat, email ticket, or social message—the automation instantly springs into action. It intelligently checks your Google Contacts for an existing entry to avoid duplicates, then either creates a fresh contact or updates an existing one with the latest details. The result is a unified, accurate, and automatically updated customer database.

How It Works

The workflow runs on Make.com, acting as the intelligent middleware between Reamaze and Google Contacts. It follows a logical sequence to ensure data integrity and efficiency.

Step 1: Trigger on New Reamaze Contact

The scenario is activated the moment a new contact record appears in your connected Reamaze account. Make.com watches for this event in real-time, capturing key data points like the contact's name, email address, and any associated company or notes.

Step 2: Search Google Contacts for a Match

Before creating anything, the automation performs a smart search within your Google Contacts. It uses the contact's name or email from Reamaze as the search key. This step is crucial for maintaining a clean CRM and preventing multiple entries for the same person.

Step 3: Decide: Update or Create

Based on the search result, a router module makes a decision. If a matching contact is found, the workflow proceeds to update that existing Google Contact with any new information from Reamaze. If no match is found, it takes the path to create a brand new contact in your Google address book.

Step 4: Execute the Sync

Finally, the appropriate Google Contacts module is called. It uses the official Google API to either patch an existing contact's details or insert a completely new one, populating fields like name, email, and organization as mapped from the Reamaze data.

Pro tip: Enhance this workflow by adding a step that logs every sync action (create/update) to a Google Sheet. This creates an audit trail, perfect for troubleshooting or reporting on new customer acquisition from support channels.
