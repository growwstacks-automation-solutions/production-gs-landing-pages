What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Google Contacts alongside a dedicated CRM like CompanyHub, maintaining two separate contact lists is a constant headache. Sales reps add prospects to their personal Google Contacts, but that information never reaches the shared company database. This leads to missed follow-ups, duplicated efforts, and a fragmented view of customer relationships.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless, one-way bridge from Google Contacts to CompanyHub. Every time a new contact is created in any connected Google account, the workflow instantly captures that data and creates a corresponding, fully formatted contact record in your CompanyHub CRM. It transforms a manual, error-prone task into a reliable, hands-off process that ensures your entire team operates from the same, up-to-date information.

The value extends beyond simple data transfer. By automating this sync, you enforce data consistency, accelerate lead response times, and free up valuable hours each week that your team can reinvest in selling and building relationships, rather than copying and pasting information.

How It Works

The workflow is an elegant sequence of events triggered by a new contact entry. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Trigger on New Google Contact

The automation is initiated the moment a new contact is added to a specified Google Contacts list. Make.com watches for this event in real-time, capturing the contact's full details—name, email, phone, company, and any notes.

Step 2: Data Parsing & Formatting

The raw contact data is then processed. The workflow can split full names into first and last name fields, standardize phone number formats, and filter out personal contacts (like "Mom" or "Dentist") based on rules you set, ensuring only relevant business contacts proceed.

Pro tip: Use this step to add a default tag or lead source (e.g., "From Google Sync") to every imported contact in CompanyHub. This makes it easy to report on where these leads originated.

Step 3: Create Contact in CompanyHub

The formatted data is sent to CompanyHub via its API. The workflow maps each piece of Google Contact information to the correct field in CompanyHub, creating a new, complete contact record. It can also assign the contact to a specific team member or add it to a campaign.

Step 4: Confirmation & Error Handling

After the record is created, the workflow can log the action for auditing purposes or send a brief notification to a Slack channel or email. If the API call fails (e.g., due to a duplicate), the workflow can be configured to retry or send an alert, ensuring no contact is ever lost.

What You'll Need

To implement this template, you will need the following accounts and access:

  1. A Make.com account (free or paid plan).
  2. A Google account with Contacts enabled. You'll need to grant Make.com permission to read your contacts.
  3. An active CompanyHub account with administrative access to generate an API key.
  4. Basic information about your CompanyHub setup, such as custom field IDs if you wish to map data beyond standard fields.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Google Contacts: In the first module, click to add a connection. Authenticate with your Google account and grant the necessary permissions to access Contacts.
  3. Connect CompanyHub: In the CompanyHub module, add a new connection using your CompanyHub API key. This is typically found in your CompanyHub settings under API or Integrations.
  4. Test the Flow: Turn on the scenario and create a test contact in your Google Contacts. Watch as Make.com processes it and creates the corresponding entry in CompanyHub.
  5. Refine & Deploy: Adjust any field mappings if needed, set up error notifications, and then activate the scenario for continuous, automated syncing.
