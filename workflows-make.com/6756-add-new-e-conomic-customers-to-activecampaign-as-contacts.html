Automatically Sync E-conomic Customers to ActiveCampaign

Eliminate manual data entry. Every new customer in your E-conomic accounting system is instantly added as a contact in ActiveCampaign for seamless marketing and follow-up.

What This Workflow Does

For businesses using E-conomic for accounting and ActiveCampaign for email marketing and CRM, a significant operational gap often exists. New customers are entered into the financial system, but that valuable information sits isolated from the marketing team. This forces manual, error-prone copying of contact details, delaying welcome sequences, missing sales opportunities, and creating data silos.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as a real-time bridge between your accounting and marketing platforms. The moment a new customer record is saved in E-conomic, the workflow triggers, securely collects the relevant contact information, and creates a corresponding contact in your ActiveCampaign account. This ensures your sales and marketing teams have immediate, accurate access to new leads and customers without lifting a finger.

Pro tip: Use this automation to not just create contacts, but also to tag them based on their customer group in E-conomic (e.g., "Retail," "Wholesale") or add them to a specific list like "New Invoice Clients" for targeted onboarding campaigns.

How It Works

The automation runs on a reliable, event-driven basis using Make.com's powerful integration platform.

Step 1: Monitor for New Customers in E-conomic

The workflow starts by watching your E-conomic account for new customer entries. This can be set to check at regular intervals (e.g., every hour) or can be triggered via a webhook for instant updates, ensuring no new customer is missed.

Step 2: Extract and Format Customer Data

Once a new customer is detected, Make retrieves key details such as the company name, contact person, email address, phone number, and billing address. The workflow can be configured to filter or transform this data, for instance, combining first and last names into a single field for ActiveCampaign.

Step 3: Create or Update Contact in ActiveCampaign

The formatted data is then sent to ActiveCampaign's API. The workflow creates a new contact, populating all the mapped fields. It can also include logic to check if a contact already exists to prevent duplicates, updating information instead of creating a new entry.

Step 4: Confirm and Log the Action

Finally, the workflow confirms the successful creation of the contact. For auditing and error handling, it can log the action, send a notification to a team channel (like Slack), or retry the operation if the first attempt fails, guaranteeing data integrity.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make scenario, add your E-conomic and ActiveCampaign accounts using the respective modules. You'll be prompted to enter your API keys.
  3. Map the Data Fields: Configure the "Create a Contact" module in ActiveCampaign. Map the E-conomic fields (like customer.name, customer.email) to the correct ActiveCampaign contact fields.
  4. Set the Trigger Schedule: Decide how often the workflow should check E-conomic for new customers (e.g., every 30 minutes). For real-time sync, you may need to set up a webhook in E-conomic.
  5. Test and Activate: Run a test by creating a dummy customer in E-conomic. Verify the contact appears correctly in ActiveCampaign. Once confirmed, activate the scenario to run automatically.
