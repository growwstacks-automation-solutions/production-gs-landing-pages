What This Workflow Does

If you use Dotdigital for email marketing and surveys, you know the value of customer feedback. But the process of collecting that data—logging into Dotdigital, exporting CSV files, and manually copying responses into a spreadsheet—is a silent productivity killer. It's repetitive, prone to human error, and delays your ability to act on insights.

This automation solves that by creating a live bridge between Dotdigital and Google Sheets. Every time a new survey response is submitted—whether it's a Net Promoter Score (NPS), customer satisfaction (CSAT), or market research form—the workflow instantly captures it and appends it as a new, organized row in your designated Google Sheet. The data flows automatically on a schedule you control, typically every 15 minutes, ensuring your analysis is always based on the latest information.

The business value is immediate: you reclaim hours spent on manual data entry, eliminate copy-paste mistakes, and centralize all feedback into a single source of truth. This allows marketing, product, and customer success teams to access real-time sentiment data, spot trends faster, and make decisions backed by accurate, up-to-date information.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple but powerful "trigger-action" principle, orchestrated by Make.com.

Step 1: Scheduled Check for New Responses

A "Schedule" module acts as the trigger, waking up the workflow at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes). It doesn't do anything with data itself; it simply signals the workflow to start its process, ensuring continuous, hands-off operation.

Step 2: Fetch Latest Survey Data from Dotdigital

Upon triggering, the workflow connects to your Dotdigital account via a secure API. It queries for any new survey responses collected since the last time it checked. It intelligently fetches only the new data, preventing duplicates and efficiently managing API calls.

Step 3: Map and Structure the Data

Each survey response contains specific fields. The template is pre-configured to map common fields like Email, First Name, and Last Name from the Dotdigital response. This mapping ensures the data lands in the correct columns in your spreadsheet. The workflow can be easily extended to include additional fields like rating scores, comments, or custom answers.

Step 4: Add a New Row to Google Sheets

The final action is performed by the Google Sheets module. It takes the structured data from Dotdigital and uses it to "Add a Row" to your specified spreadsheet and worksheet. Each new response becomes a clean, timestamped row, automatically building your historical feedback database without you lifting a finger.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated "Log" or "Raw Data" sheet to store all incoming responses. Then, create separate "Dashboard" or "Summary" sheets that use QUERY or IMPORTRANGE functions to analyze the data. This keeps your automation simple and your reporting powerful.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above. It will clone the pre-built scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Dotdigital: In the first Dotdigital module, click to add a connection. Enter your Dotdigital API credentials (username and API key) when prompted to authorize the link.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: In the Google Sheets module, authorize Make.com to access your Google account. Then, select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet (tab) where new rows should be added.
  4. Map Your Data Fields: Review the mapping in the Google Sheets module. Ensure the fields from Dotdigital (like "Email") are correctly assigned to the column headers in your sheet (like "Email Address"). Adjust if your survey uses different field names.
  5. Test & Activate: Run a single execution to test. Submit a new test response to your Dotdigital survey, then manually run the scenario in Make.com. Check your Google Sheet to confirm a new row appears. Once verified, turn the scenario "ON" to let it run automatically on its schedule.
