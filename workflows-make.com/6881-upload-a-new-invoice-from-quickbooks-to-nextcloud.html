What This Workflow Does

For businesses using QuickBooks, managing invoice PDFs is a recurring, manual task. After creating an invoice, you must remember to download the PDF and save it somewhere secure—like a shared drive, email, or cloud storage. This process is not only time-consuming but also prone to human error. Invoices get forgotten, saved in the wrong folder, or lost in email threads, creating headaches during tax season, audits, or client inquiries.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a silent, reliable assistant that monitors your QuickBooks account. The moment a new invoice is finalized, the workflow springs into action: it fetches the invoice, generates a PDF, and uploads it directly to a designated folder in your Nextcloud instance. This ensures every single financial document is automatically archived in a secure, centralized, and organized location without you lifting a finger.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com and connects two critical business apps: your accounting software and your private cloud storage.

Step 1: Trigger on New Invoice

The scenario starts by watching your QuickBooks account for new invoice events. Using the QuickBooks module, it polls for any newly created invoices at a regular interval you set (e.g., every hour or every 15 minutes).

Step 2: Download the Invoice PDF

Once a new invoice is detected, the workflow retrieves the specific invoice data and, most importantly, triggers the generation and download of the official PDF document from QuickBooks. This ensures you get the formatted, client-ready version of the invoice.

Step 3: Upload to Nextcloud

The downloaded PDF file is then transferred to the Nextcloud module. The workflow authenticates with your Nextcloud server and uploads the file to a pre-defined folder path. You can customize this path to include the client's name, date, or invoice number for perfect organization.

Pro tip: Enhance this workflow by adding a step to rename the file during upload. Use data from QuickBooks (like "Invoice-#1234-ClientName.pdf") to make files instantly searchable in Nextcloud.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect QuickBooks: In the first module, authenticate your QuickBooks account. Make.com will guide you through the OAuth process to grant secure access.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set how often you want the scenario to check for new invoices (e.g., every hour). You can also filter for specific customers or invoice types if needed.
  4. Connect Nextcloud: In the upload module, enter your Nextcloud server URL and login credentials. Test the connection to ensure it works.
  5. Set the Destination Folder: Define the exact folder path in Nextcloud where invoices should be saved (e.g., `/Accounting/Invoices/2024/`).
  6. Activate and Test: Turn the scenario on and create a test invoice in QuickBooks. Within your chosen interval, check the designated Nextcloud folder to confirm the PDF appears automatically.
