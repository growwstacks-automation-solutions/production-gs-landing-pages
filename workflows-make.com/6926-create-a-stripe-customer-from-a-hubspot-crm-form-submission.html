What This Workflow Does

For businesses using HubSpot for lead generation and Stripe for payments, a frustrating gap often exists. A potential customer fills out a "Contact Sales" or "Start Free Trial" form in HubSpot. Your sales team engages, closes the deal, and then someone has to manually open Stripe, create a new customer record, and copy-paste the contact's details. This manual step is slow, prone to errors, and delays the billing process.

This automation template solves that problem instantly. It acts as a digital bridge between your marketing/sales platform (HubSpot CRM) and your financial platform (Stripe). The moment a form is submitted in HubSpot, this workflow triggers. It captures the new contact's information—like name, email, and company—and uses it to automatically create a corresponding customer profile in Stripe. The result is a seamless, error-free handoff that prepares your new customer for invoicing or subscription setup in seconds, not hours.

The business value is clear: you save administrative time, eliminate data entry mistakes, and accelerate your revenue cycle. Your customer gets a professional, integrated experience from their very first interaction, setting the tone for a smooth business relationship.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical, event-driven sequence. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of the process:

Step 1: Trigger on New HubSpot Form Submission

The automation is initiated whenever a specific form is submitted in your HubSpot CRM. The Make.com scenario watches for this event in real-time, capturing the entire payload of data from the form fields.

Step 2: Parse and Map Contact Data

The workflow extracts key pieces of information from the form submission. This typically includes the contact's first name, last name, email address, and phone number. You can configure it to also pull custom properties like company size, lead source, or billing address if your form collects them.

Pro tip: Use HubSpot's "Contact Property" names to ensure you're mapping the correct data. For example, map firstname to Stripe's name field for accurate customer records.

Step 3: Create Customer in Stripe

Using the parsed data, the automation makes a secure API call to Stripe. It instructs Stripe to create a new customer object with the provided details. The email address is crucial, as it becomes the primary identifier in Stripe. The workflow can also attach metadata from HubSpot (like the original form name) to the Stripe customer for future reference.

Step 4: Confirm and Log the Action

After Stripe confirms the customer creation, the workflow can be set to log the successful transaction, update the original HubSpot contact record with the new Stripe Customer ID, or even trigger a notification to your finance team. This closes the loop, providing full visibility into the automated process.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automation up and running in under 15 minutes:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect HubSpot: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your HubSpot account. Select the specific form that will trigger the scenario.
  3. Connect Stripe: In the Stripe module, add your Stripe Secret Key from the Stripe Dashboard (under Developers > API keys).
  4. Map the Data Fields: Review the data mapping between the HubSpot form fields and the Stripe "Create Customer" fields. Adjust as needed for your specific form (e.g., map "company" to Stripe's description field).
  5. Test with a Dummy Submission: Use Make.com's built-in testing mode. Submit a test entry to your HubSpot form and verify that a test customer appears in your Stripe dashboard.
  6. Activate & Monitor: Turn the scenario on. It will now run automatically for every new form submission. Monitor the scenario history for the first few runs to ensure everything works smoothly.
