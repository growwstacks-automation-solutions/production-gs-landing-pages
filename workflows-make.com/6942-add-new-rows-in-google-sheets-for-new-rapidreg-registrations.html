What This Workflow Does

Managing event, course, or webinar registrations often involves a frustrating back-and-forth between your registration platform and your master spreadsheet. Every time someone signs up via RapidReg, someone on your team must manually copy their details—name, email, ticket type, answers to custom questions—and paste them into a Google Sheet. This process is not only time-consuming but also prone to errors, delays, and data inconsistencies.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a digital bridge between RapidReg and Google Sheets. The moment a new registration is completed, the workflow is triggered. It captures all the submitted data and automatically appends it as a new, perfectly formatted row in your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. Your attendee list updates itself in real-time, without you lifting a finger.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple, reliable sequence to ensure no registration is ever missed.

Step 1: The Trigger – A New Registration

The automation is constantly monitoring your RapidReg account. As soon as a participant completes a registration form, RapidReg sends a notification (a "webhook") to Make.com, which instantly kicks off the workflow.

Step 2: Data Capture & Routing

Make.com receives the payload of data from the registration. This includes all the standard and custom fields you've set up in your RapidReg form. The workflow parses this data, preparing it for transfer.

Step 3: Writing to Your Master Sheet

The final step is the action. Make.com connects to your specified Google Sheets spreadsheet and adds a new row. Each piece of registration data is mapped to its corresponding column—email in column A, first name in column B, and so on—creating a clean, organized, and permanent record.

Pro tip: Use the provided example spreadsheet as your starting point. You can easily add or rename columns to match your specific data collection needs, and the automation will adapt accordingly.
