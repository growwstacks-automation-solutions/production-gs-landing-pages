What This Workflow Does

For businesses and individuals using Virtual Buttons, manually triggering daily actions is a repetitive chore that's easy to forget. Whether it's sending a morning report, posting a social update, or kicking off a data backup, relying on memory leads to missed tasks and inconsistent operations.

This Make.com workflow solves that by fully automating the process. It acts as your digital assistant, configured to activate a specific Virtual Button at a predetermined time every day. Once set up, it runs unattended, ensuring your critical daily action happens precisely when it should, without any manual intervention.

The value is clear: it transforms a daily to-do item into a background process. This not only reclaims valuable minutes from your schedule but also introduces a layer of reliability that manual processes can't match. You gain peace of mind knowing your daily trigger is handled, allowing you to focus on strategic work.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple yet powerful sequence, orchestrated by Make.com.

Step 1: Schedule Trigger

The workflow is initiated by Make.com's Schedule module. You configure this module to run at your desired interval—for example, every weekday at 9 AM, or once a day at midnight. This is the "brain" that decides when the automation should start.

Step 2: Connect to Virtual Buttons

Once triggered, the scenario sends a secure instruction to Virtual Buttons. This is typically done via an HTTP request (a webhook) that mimics the action of physically clicking your designated button on the Virtual Buttons website.

Step 3: Button Activation & Confirmation

Virtual Buttons receives the signal and executes the action associated with that button. The workflow can then be set to log the successful activation or send you a brief notification (like an email or Slack message) for verification, completing the automated cycle.

Pro tip: Use this workflow as a foundation. You can easily branch out from the initial trigger to create multi-step automations. For instance, after activating the button, you could have Make.com wait for a response and then update a Google Sheet or create a task in your project management tool.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your own Make.com account.
  2. Configure the Schedule: In the first module, set your preferred time zone and define the exact schedule (e.g., "Every day at 08:00").
  3. Connect Virtual Buttons: In the next module, you'll be prompted to connect your Virtual Buttons account. Paste your API key or the specific button's webhook URL here.
  4. Test the Connection: Use the "Run once" feature in Make.com to test the workflow. Check your Virtual Buttons activity log to confirm the button was activated.
  5. Activate: Turn the scenario "ON." Your daily automation is now live and will run according to the schedule you set.
