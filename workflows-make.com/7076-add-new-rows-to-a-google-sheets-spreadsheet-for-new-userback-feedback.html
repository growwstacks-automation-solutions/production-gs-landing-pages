What This Workflow Does

For product teams using Userback, valuable user feedback often arrives in a siloed tool, making it difficult to analyze trends, share insights, or track issues over time. Manually copying this data into a spreadsheet is a repetitive, time-consuming task that leads to missed feedback and inconsistent data.

This Make.com automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Userback and Google Sheets. Every time a user submits feedback—be it a bug report, feature request, or general comment—the workflow instantly captures all relevant details and adds them as a new, structured row in your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. This transforms scattered feedback into a centralized, analyzable database.

The result is a live log of user insights that your entire team can access. Product managers can prioritize roadmaps, support teams can identify recurring issues, and executives can gauge user sentiment—all from a single, always-up-to-date source.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple trigger-action principle, ensuring no feedback slips through the cracks.

Step 1: New Feedback Trigger in Userback

The workflow is initiated the moment a new feedback item is created in your Userback project. Make.com monitors your Userback account in real-time, waiting for this specific event.

Step 2: Data Extraction & Mapping

Make.com retrieves the full payload of data from the new feedback. This includes the feedback title, detailed description, URL where it was submitted, user information (like email), browser/device details, attached screenshots, and the precise timestamp.

Step 3: Row Creation in Google Sheets

The workflow then connects to your pre-defined Google Sheets spreadsheet and appends a new row. Each piece of extracted data is mapped to a specific column—creating a clean, organized record that is immediately ready for sorting, filtering, and analysis.

Pro tip: Structure your Google Sheet with columns for 'Priority', 'Status', and 'Assigned To'. You can later extend this automation to automatically assign a priority score based on keywords or route high-priority items to a Slack channel.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Userback: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Userback account and select the specific project you want to monitor.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: In the second module, authorize access to Google Drive/Sheets and select the exact spreadsheet and worksheet where rows should be added.
  4. Map the Data: Review the data mapping. The template is pre-configured, but you can customize which Userback fields go into which spreadsheet columns.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and submit a test piece of feedback in Userback. Verify that a new row appears in your sheet with all the correct data.
