What This Workflow Does

For product teams, user feedback is gold—but only if it's seen and acted upon quickly. Manually checking a feedback tool like Userback throughout the day is inefficient and leads to delayed responses. This automation solves that by creating a direct bridge between Userback and your team's inbox.

Every time a user submits feedback—be it a bug report, feature request, or general comment—this Make.com workflow triggers instantly. It captures all the details from Userback, including screenshots, user comments, browser information, and priority level, then automatically sends a formatted email notification via Gmail to your designated team members. This ensures critical insights never get buried in a dashboard and your team can prioritize and respond in real-time.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple but powerful trigger-action principle, connecting two key platforms seamlessly.

Step 1: Monitor Userback for New Feedback

The scenario starts by watching your Userback project for any new feedback items. Make.com acts as the listener, using Userback's API to check for new submissions at regular intervals or in real-time via webhooks. Whenever a user clicks "Send Feedback" from your app or website, the process begins.

Step 2: Gather Feedback Details

Once triggered, the workflow pulls all relevant data from the new Userback entry. This includes the user's comment, any annotated screenshots they attached, metadata like their browser, OS, and URL, and the feedback category or tag they selected. This rich context is essential for understanding the issue fully without needing to log into Userback.

Step 3: Format and Send the Gmail Notification

The core action: sending the email. Make.com takes the collected data and constructs a clear, actionable email. You can customize the subject line (e.g., "New Bug Report from Userback"), the body (which can include the screenshot as an attachment or inline link), and the recipient list. The email is then sent via your connected Gmail account, landing directly in the inboxes of product managers, developers, or support leads.

Pro tip: Use Gmail's label filtering to automatically categorize these automated emails. Create a label like "Userback Feedback" and have Make apply it, so team members can set up priority inbox rules or easily archive emails after action.

What You'll Need

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes. Follow these steps after clicking "Get This Workflow".

  1. Clone the Template: Click the link above to open the template on Make.com. Press the "Copy" button to create your own private version of the scenario.
  2. Connect Userback: In the first module, click to add a connection. You'll be prompted to log into your Userback account and authorize Make.com to access your projects. Select the specific project you want to monitor.
  3. Connect Gmail: In the email module, add your Gmail connection. Follow the standard OAuth process to grant send-only permissions. Use the account you want the emails to originate from.
  4. Set Your Recipients: In the Gmail module settings, replace the placeholder email address with your actual team's email. You can add multiple addresses separated by commas.
  5. Customize the Email: Edit the subject line and body text to match your team's terminology. You can insert data from Userback (like the user's comment) using the variable picker.
  6. Turn it On: Save your scenario and toggle the scheduler to "On". The workflow will now run automatically based on the schedule you set (e.g., every 15 minutes).
