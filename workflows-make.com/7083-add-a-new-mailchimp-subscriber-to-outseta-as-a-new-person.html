What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Mailchimp for email marketing and Outseta for CRM, a critical gap often exists: new subscribers don't automatically become contacts in your sales pipeline. This forces your team to manually check for new sign-ups and copy-paste data, a process that is slow, prone to errors, and can cause valuable leads to go cold before sales even knows they exist.

This free Make.com workflow solves that problem by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between your marketing and sales tools. Every time a new person subscribes to your Mailchimp list, this automation instantly captures their details—like name, email, and subscription source—and creates a corresponding new "Person" record in your Outseta CRM. It turns a passive email subscriber into an active, trackable lead in seconds, without any human intervention.

How It Works

The automation acts as a smart, reliable connector that listens for new events in Mailchimp and takes precise action in Outseta.

Step 1: Trigger on New Mailchimp Subscriber

The workflow is activated the moment a new subscription is recorded in your designated Mailchimp audience. Make.com watches for this event in real-time, capturing the full set of subscriber data provided in the sign-up form.

Step 2: Format and Prepare the Contact Data

The raw data from Mailchimp is then structured into the format Outseta expects. This step involves mapping fields: the subscriber's email address, first name, last name, and any custom fields (like company name or lead source) are extracted and prepared for the CRM.

Pro tip: Use this step to add valuable context. You can append the text "From Mailchimp: [List Name]" to the contact notes in Outseta, so your sales team immediately knows the lead source.

Step 3: Create a New Person in Outseta

The formatted data is sent to Outseta via its API, instructing it to create a new Person record. The workflow passes the essential details, ensuring the new contact appears in your Outseta dashboard with all the information your sales team needs to start a relevant conversation.

Step 4: (Optional) Log Success or Handle Errors

The final module confirms the record was created successfully. For advanced use, you can add error handling to retry the sync or send an alert if the process fails, guaranteeing data integrity.
