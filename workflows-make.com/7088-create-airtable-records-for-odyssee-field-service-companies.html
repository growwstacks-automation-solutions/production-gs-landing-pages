What This Workflow Does

For field service businesses, capturing new client information efficiently is crucial. Technicians using Odyssee Field Service in the field create company records on-site, but that data often gets stuck in the field service software. Sales, marketing, and management teams then waste time manually transferring this information to shared systems like Airtable for follow-up, reporting, and planning.

This automation solves that disconnect. It acts as a real-time bridge between Odyssee Field Service and Airtable. Whenever a field technician creates a new company profile in Odyssee, this workflow automatically triggers and creates a corresponding, detailed record in your designated Airtable base. The process is instantaneous, accurate, and requires zero human intervention.

The result is a unified operational view. Your office team has immediate visibility into new business opportunities captured in the field. Marketing can launch targeted campaigns, sales can begin outreach, and management can track growth metrics—all using the freshly synced data in Airtable, without asking technicians for spreadsheets or waiting for end-of-day updates.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com and follows a simple "watch-create" logic that runs reliably in the background.

Step 1: Monitor Odyssee for New Companies

The scenario starts with a Make.com module that watches your Odyssee Field Service account. It is configured to check for new "Company" records at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes, hourly, or in real-time depending on the plan). When it detects a newly created company, it captures all relevant data fields like company name, address, primary contact, service location, and any custom notes added by the technician.

Step 2: Format and Prepare the Data

Before sending data to Airtable, the workflow can include a data transformation step. This ensures the information from Odyssee matches the expected format in your Airtable base. For example, it might split a full address into separate "Street," "City," and "ZIP Code" columns, or format phone numbers consistently. This step guarantees clean, usable data arrives in Airtable.

Step 3: Create the Airtable Record

The core action is the "Create Record" module in Airtable. Make.com sends the prepared company data to your specified Airtable base and table. It maps each piece of Odyssee data to the correct field in Airtable. Upon successful creation, Airtable returns the new record ID, confirming the sync is complete. The new company is now fully integrated into your central business database.

Pro tip: Extend this workflow by adding a step to notify your sales team in Slack or via email immediately after the Airtable record is created. This turns automation into actionable intelligence, prompting instant follow-up on hot leads from the field.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the ready-made scenario to your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, click on the Odyssee module and Airtable module to authenticate and connect your respective accounts. This is a secure, one-time process.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set the Odyssee module to watch the "Companies" object. Define how often it should check for new records (polling interval).
  4. Map Your Fields: In the Airtable "Create Record" module, select your base and table. Then, map the data from Odyssee (like "Company Name," "Address") to the corresponding field names in your Airtable table.
  5. Test and Activate: Use the "Run once" feature to test the workflow. Create a test company in Odyssee and verify it appears correctly in Airtable. Once confirmed, turn the scenario "ON." It will now run automatically based on your schedule.
