What This Workflow Does

For businesses that operate both a field service arm and an e-commerce store, managing customer data across Odyssee Field Service and Shopify is a constant, manual chore. Information exists in silos: your service team has one set of contact details, while your online store has another. This leads to duplicate entries, missed marketing opportunities, and a fractured view of your customer.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between the two systems. Whenever a new contact is created in Odyssee Field Service—whether it's from a service inquiry, a new job booking, or a lead form—this workflow automatically triggers and creates a corresponding customer record in your Shopify store. It transfers key details like name, email, phone number, and address, ensuring your customer database is unified from the first interaction.

The result is a single source of truth for customer information. Your marketing team can segment and target customers based on their service history, and your service technicians can understand a client's purchase behavior, leading to more personalized and efficient interactions across your entire business.

How It Works

The workflow is an elegant, "set-and-forget" automation that runs in the background on the Make.com platform.

Step 1: Trigger on New Contact

The workflow is activated the moment a new contact record is added to your Odyssee Field Service account. Make.com watches for this event in real-time using Odyssee's API.

Step 2: Map the Contact Data

The relevant data from the Odyssee contact is extracted. This typically includes the customer's first and last name, email address, phone number, and physical address. You can customize which fields are captured to match your business needs.

Step 3: Create the Shopify Customer

Using Shopify's API, the workflow takes the mapped data and creates a new customer in your designated Shopify store. The customer is added with tags (like "From Field Service") for easy identification and segmentation later.

Pro tip: Use the "Router" module in Make to add conditional logic. For example, you could check if the contact's email already exists in Shopify to prevent duplicates, or only create customers for contacts marked as "Active" in Odyssee.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make scenario, click on the Odyssee module and authorize the connection to your Odyssee account. Repeat the process for the Shopify module.
  3. Test the Trigger: Create a test contact in your Odyssee Field Service to ensure Make.com detects the new event correctly.
  4. Map Your Fields: In the Shopify "Create Customer" module, map the Odyssee contact fields (like `firstName`, `email`) to the corresponding Shopify customer fields.
  5. Activate & Monitor: Turn the scenario on. Create another test contact in Odyssee and verify that a new customer appears in your Shopify admin panel almost instantly.
