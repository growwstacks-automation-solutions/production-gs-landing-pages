What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses, every new customer is a golden opportunity to build a lasting relationship. However, manually copying customer details from WooCommerce into your SMS marketing platform like SMSAPI is tedious, error-prone, and causes delays. This gap means you miss the critical window to send a welcome message or a first-order discount, losing potential sales and engagement.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between your WooCommerce store and SMSAPI. The moment a customer completes a purchase and their profile is created in WooCommerce, this automation instantly captures their name and phone number and adds them as a new contact in your SMSAPI list. It turns a manual administrative task into a silent, reliable background process that fuels your marketing engine.

The business value is clear: you build your SMS marketing list on autopilot. This ensures your most responsive marketing channel is always populated with fresh, qualified leads (your paying customers), allowing you to launch targeted campaigns faster, improve customer retention, and ultimately drive more revenue without lifting a finger.

How It Works

The automation is triggered by a new customer event and moves data securely between platforms in a few logical steps.

Step 1: Detecting a New WooCommerce Customer

The workflow starts by monitoring your WooCommerce store using a "Watch Customers" module. It instantly detects when a new customer account is created, whether from a completed purchase, a newsletter signup, or a manual admin addition. This trigger captures the customer's key data points like first name, last name, email, and most importantly, their phone number.

Pro tip: Ensure your WooCommerce checkout includes an optional or required phone number field. This simple step guarantees you capture the essential data needed for SMS marketing.

Step 2: Formatting and Preparing the Contact Data

Before sending data to SMSAPI, the workflow often includes a data formatting step. It might combine the first and last name into a single "Full Name" field, validate the phone number format to meet SMSAPI's requirements, and filter out any test or admin orders you don't want in your marketing list. This ensures clean, usable data enters your CRM.

Step 3: Creating a New Contact in SMSAPI

The final step is the action. The workflow uses the SMSAPI module "Add a Contact" to create a new entry in your specified contact list or group. It maps the formatted data from WooCommerce—like the customer's name to the "Name" field and their phone number to the "Phone" field—creating a perfectly synced contact ready for your next SMS campaign.

What You'll Need

To set up this workflow, you will need the following accounts and information:

  1. A Make.com account (formerly Integromat) to build and run the automation.
  2. An active WooCommerce store with API access enabled (Consumer Key and Secret).
  3. An SMSAPI account with a valid API token for authentication.
  4. The phone numbers of your customers collected at checkout (ensure this field is enabled in WooCommerce).
  5. (Recommended) A specific Contact List/Group name in SMSAPI where new contacts should be added.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to configure your automated contact sync in under 15 minutes:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and clone the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect WooCommerce: In the first module, authenticate your WooCommerce store by entering your store URL, Consumer Key, and Consumer Secret.
  3. Connect SMSAPI: In the action module, authenticate your SMSAPI account using your API token.
  4. Map the Data Fields: Ensure the phone number and name fields from WooCommerce are correctly mapped to the corresponding fields in the SMSAPI "Add a Contact" module.
  5. Choose a Contact List: Specify the exact name of the SMSAPI contact list where new customers should be added.
  6. Test and Activate: Run a single test cycle by creating a test customer in WooCommerce. Verify the contact appears in SMSAPI, then turn the scenario on "Schedule" to run automatically.
