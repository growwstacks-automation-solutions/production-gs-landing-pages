What This Workflow Does

For sales and marketing teams, the gap between capturing a lead and acting on it is where opportunities are lost. Manually transferring lead information from your CRM (like Nutshell) to your project management tool (like Trello) is a repetitive, time-consuming task that delays response times and introduces errors.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless, real-time connection between Nutshell and Trello. The moment a new lead is entered into your Nutshell CRM—whether from a web form, email, or manual entry—this workflow automatically creates a corresponding card in your designated Trello board. The card is populated with the lead's key details, turning a database entry into an actionable task for your team instantly.

The result is a unified workflow where your sales pipeline directly fuels your task management. Your team spends zero time on data entry, leads are acted upon faster, and everyone has a single, visual source of truth for tracking prospect engagement from initial contact to close.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com and operates on a simple trigger-action principle. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: A New Lead is Captured in Nutshell

The automation is triggered whenever a new person or company record is created in your connected Nutshell account. This could be from an integrated form, an email parsing rule, or a sales rep adding a contact manually.

Step 2: Make.com Watches for the Trigger

The Make.com scenario uses the Nutshell module to monitor your account in real-time. It detects the new lead event and immediately captures the available data, such as the contact's name, email address, company, lead source, and any initial notes.

Step 3: Data is Formatted for Trello

The workflow then processes this data. It can map specific Nutshell fields to corresponding fields in Trello. For example, it can combine the lead's name and company into the Trello card title, and place the email and notes into the card description.

Pro tip: Use Make's text formatting tools to create a clean, readable card description. You can add labels like "High Priority" based on lead score from Nutshell, or include a direct link back to the lead's profile in your CRM.

Step 4: A New Card is Created in Trello

Finally, the workflow uses the Trello module to create a new card in your pre-selected board and list (e.g., "Sales Pipeline" > "New Leads"). The card is created with all the formatted information, and you can even set it to automatically assign the card to a specific team member or add colored labels for categorization.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting this automation live takes about 15 minutes. Follow these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Nutshell: In the first module, click to add a connection. You'll be prompted to log into Nutshell and authorize Make.com to access your data.
  3. Connect Trello: Do the same for the Trello module, authorizing Make to create cards on your behalf.
  4. Configure the Board & List: In the Trello "Create a Card" module, select the specific board and list where new lead cards should appear.
  5. Map Your Data (Optional): Review how the lead data is mapped to the card. You can customize this—for example, you might want to add the lead's phone number to the card description.
  6. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and create a test lead in Nutshell. Within seconds, check your Trello board to confirm a new card appears with the correct details.
