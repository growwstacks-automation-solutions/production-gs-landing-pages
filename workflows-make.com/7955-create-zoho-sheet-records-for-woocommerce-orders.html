What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious manual process of copying WooCommerce order data into spreadsheets for record-keeping. Ecommerce businesses typically spend hours each week transferring order details to accounting systems, inventory trackers, or CRM databases.

The workflow automatically creates a new row in your Zoho Sheets spreadsheet whenever a WooCommerce order is placed. It maps all relevant order details (customer information, products purchased, payment method, etc.) to designated columns, maintaining perfect data consistency without human intervention.

How It Works

1. New order trigger

The workflow monitors your WooCommerce store for new orders through real-time webhooks. As soon as a customer completes checkout, the automation activates.

2. Data extraction

Key order details are pulled from WooCommerce including customer name, email, shipping address, product SKUs, quantities, prices, taxes, and payment status.

3. Data transformation

The raw order data is formatted for Zoho Sheets - converting timestamps, calculating totals, and applying your preferred naming conventions.

4. Zoho Sheets update

The processed data is appended as a new row in your designated Zoho Sheet, with each field mapped to its correct column according to your template.

Pro tip: Add conditional logic to skip test orders or filter by product category before updating your sheets.
