What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common problem for businesses using Amazon SES for transactional emails while managing marketing campaigns in Mailchimp. When customers interact with your transactional emails (like order confirmations or password resets), their contact information stays siloed in Amazon SES unless manually added to Mailchimp.

The workflow automatically detects new contacts in Amazon SES and adds them as subscribers to your Mailchimp list. This ensures your marketing team can nurture these contacts with targeted campaigns, while eliminating hours of manual data entry and reducing the risk of human error.

How It Works

1. Monitors Amazon SES for new contacts

The workflow continuously checks your Amazon SES account for new email addresses that have interacted with your transactional emails. This could include customers who made purchases, signed up for accounts, or contacted support.

2. Processes contact information

Each new contact is processed to extract relevant details like email address, name (if available), and any metadata from the SES record. The workflow can be customized to handle different types of transactional emails differently.

3. Adds to Mailchimp with proper segmentation

The contact is then added to your specified Mailchimp list with appropriate tags based on their origin. For example, purchase confirmations might be tagged as "Customers" while support tickets might be tagged as "Help Request".

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to add custom merge fields in Mailchimp based on data from Amazon SES. This allows for more personalized email marketing campaigns.
