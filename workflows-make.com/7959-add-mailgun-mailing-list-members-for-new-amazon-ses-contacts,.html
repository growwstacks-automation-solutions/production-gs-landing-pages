What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common challenge of manually transferring new contacts from Amazon Simple Email Service (SES) to Mailgun mailing lists. Every time someone interacts with your transactional emails (like order confirmations or password resets), their contact information can automatically join your marketing lists.

The workflow monitors your Amazon SES activity for new contacts at regular intervals. When new contacts are detected, it adds them to specified Mailgun mailing lists with all relevant metadata. This eliminates spreadsheet exports, CSV imports, and the inevitable human errors that come with manual processes.

How It Works

1. Amazon SES Contact Detection

The workflow checks your Amazon SES activity logs for new email interactions. It identifies new contacts based on your configured criteria (first-time senders, specific email types, etc.).

2. Data Processing

Each new contact's information is extracted and formatted for Mailgun's API. The workflow handles field mapping between the different systems and can transform data as needed.

3. Mailgun List Update

The processed contacts are added to your specified Mailgun mailing lists. The workflow includes error handling for duplicates and validation to ensure list hygiene.

What You'll Need

  1. An active Make.com account

Pro tip: Create separate Mailgun lists for different contact sources (e.g., "SES-Purchases" and "SES-Support") to maintain segmentation from the start.
