What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical gap between HR systems and email communication platforms. When new employees are added to Factorial (your HR management system), their contact information often needs to be manually added to email distribution lists. This workflow eliminates that manual step by automatically creating Amazon SES contacts whenever new employee records appear in Factorial.

The system monitors your Factorial account at regular intervals, identifies new hires, extracts their verified email addresses, and adds them as contacts in Amazon Simple Email Service. This ensures your internal newsletters, policy updates, and company announcements reach all employees from day one, without any delays from manual data entry.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Factorial Check

The workflow runs on your chosen schedule (hourly, daily, or weekly) to check Factorial's employee directory for new additions since the last check.

2. New Employee Identification

Make.com compares the current employee list with previously recorded data to identify only genuinely new hires, avoiding duplicate entries.

3. Data Validation

The system verifies each new employee record contains a valid email address before proceeding, preventing errors in Amazon SES.

4. Amazon SES Contact Creation

Validated employee emails are added as new contacts in your Amazon SES account with appropriate tags for segmentation.

5. Confirmation & Logging

The workflow records each successful addition and can notify administrators via email or Slack if configured.

Pro tip: Extend this workflow to include employee department or location data from Factorial as SES contact tags. This enables targeted email campaigns by team or office location.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Connect Factorial: Authorize Make.com to access your Factorial employee data using OAuth
  2. Configure AWS: Set up an IAM user in AWS with permissions to manage SES contacts
  3. Set Schedule: Choose how often to check for new employees (daily recommended)
  4. Test: Add a test employee in Factorial to verify the workflow
  5. Monitor: Check the Make.com logs for successful runs
