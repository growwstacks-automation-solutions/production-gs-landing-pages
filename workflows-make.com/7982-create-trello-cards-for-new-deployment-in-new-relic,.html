What This Workflow Does

This automation bridges the gap between your deployment monitoring in New Relic and project management in Trello. Every time a new deployment is detected in New Relic, the workflow automatically creates a detailed Trello card on your specified board. This eliminates manual tracking and ensures your entire team has visibility into deployment activity.

The workflow solves the common problem of deployment visibility across teams. Developers often know about deployments through New Relic, but product managers, QA teams, and other stakeholders may remain unaware unless manually notified. This automation creates a single source of truth for all deployment activity, accessible to everyone through Trello.

How It Works

1. New Relic detects a deployment

The workflow monitors New Relic for deployment events through webhooks or API polling. When a new deployment is detected, it captures all relevant details including timestamp, version, and deployment notes.

2. Data is processed and formatted

Make.com processes the deployment data, extracting key information and formatting it for Trello. You can customize which fields are included and how they're presented in the card.

3. Trello card is created

The workflow creates a new card on your specified Trello board with all deployment details. You can configure which list the card appears in and optionally assign it to team members.

Pro tip: Add custom labels in Trello to color-code deployments by environment (production, staging, etc.) for quick visual reference.
