What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common pain point for businesses using Tally forms to collect information - the manual transfer of responses to a structured database. Every time someone submits your Tally form, this workflow automatically creates a corresponding record in your Airtable base with all the response data properly mapped to your preferred fields.

The solution eliminates hours of tedious copy-pasting while ensuring your Airtable database remains perfectly synchronized with your latest form submissions. Whether you're collecting leads, processing applications, or gathering customer feedback, this integration keeps your data flowing seamlessly between systems.

How It Works

1. Monitoring for new responses

The workflow regularly checks your Tally form for new submissions at intervals you configure (typically every 15-60 minutes). When new responses arrive, they're immediately processed.

2. Data transformation

Each form response is parsed and prepared for Airtable. The workflow handles data type conversions and can apply basic formatting rules to ensure clean data entry.

3. Field mapping

Your Tally form questions are matched to specific Airtable fields according to your configuration. The mapping preserves all response data while adapting it to your database structure.

4. Record creation

The processed data is used to create new records in your designated Airtable base and table. The workflow confirms successful creation before completing the cycle.

Pro tip: Add a timestamp field in Airtable to track exactly when each response was processed, helping you monitor automation performance.

What You'll Need

  1. A Tally form with submissions you want to track
  2. An Airtable base with a table ready to receive the data
  3. A Make.com account (free or paid)
  4. API access to both Tally and Airtable (included with standard accounts)
  5. Approximately 15 minutes for initial setup

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the template in your Make.com account
  2. Connect your Tally account by authenticating with OAuth
  3. Select your form from the dropdown menu
  4. Connect your Airtable and choose the target base and table
  5. Map form fields to Airtable columns in the configuration panel
  6. Set your polling interval (how often to check for new responses)
  7. Test with a sample submission and verify the Airtable record appears correctly
  8. Activate the scenario to begin automatic processing
