What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious and error-prone process of manually transferring asset information from spreadsheets to your EZOfficeInventory system. Many businesses track equipment purchases and inventory in Google Sheets due to its collaborative nature, but then need to duplicate this data in their formal asset management platform.

The workflow automatically detects new rows added to your designated Google Sheet and creates corresponding fixed asset records in EZOfficeInventory. It maps spreadsheet columns to the appropriate EZOfficeInventory fields like asset name, category, serial number, purchase date, cost, location, and custom attributes. This ensures your asset database stays perfectly synchronized with your procurement records without any manual intervention.

How It Works

1. New row detection

The workflow monitors your Google Sheet for new rows added, either manually or through forms/imports. Make.com checks the sheet at regular intervals you specify (from minutes to days).

2. Data validation

Each new row's data is validated against EZOfficeInventory's requirements. The system checks for required fields, proper formatting of dates/numbers, and valid category selections.

3. Asset creation

Validated data is transformed into EZOfficeInventory's API format and a new fixed asset record is created. The workflow handles all field mappings between your spreadsheet structure and EZOfficeInventory's data model.

4. Confirmation logging

After successful creation, the workflow can optionally mark the spreadsheet row as processed or log the new asset ID back to your sheet for reference.

Pro tip: Add a "Status" column to your Google Sheet that the workflow updates after processing each row. This creates a clear audit trail of which assets have been successfully imported.
