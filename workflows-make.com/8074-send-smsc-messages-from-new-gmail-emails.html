Send SMSC Messages from New Gmail Emails

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the critical business problem of delayed customer communication by instantly converting important emails into SMS messages. When customers send emails to specific folders in your Gmail account (like "Urgent" or "Support"), the workflow automatically forwards their message content via SMS through the SMSC gateway.

The solution eliminates the 2-4 hour response delay typical with email-only support systems. Service businesses, healthcare providers, and e-commerce stores particularly benefit from this immediate notification system, ensuring no customer inquiry goes unanswered during critical time windows.

How It Works

1. New Email Detection

The workflow continuously monitors your specified Gmail folder for new incoming messages. You can configure it to watch multiple folders or apply filters based on sender, subject line, or keywords.

2. Message Processing

When a new email arrives, Make.com extracts the relevant content including the sender information, subject line, and body text. The system can be configured to truncate long messages or reformat content for optimal SMS display.

3. SMS Transmission

The processed content is then sent through your SMSC gateway to the designated recipient numbers. You can set rules to determine whether replies go back to email or continue as SMS conversations.

Pro tip: Add a keyword filter to only forward emails marked "URGENT" in the subject line, reducing unnecessary SMS traffic for routine messages.

What You'll Need

  3. A Make.com account (free tier available)

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Connect your Gmail account to Make.com and select the folder to monitor
  2. Configure your SMSC API credentials in Make.com's HTTP module
  3. Set up parsing rules for email content (what to include in SMS)
  4. Define recipient rules (fixed numbers or extract from emails)
  5. Test with sample emails and verify SMS delivery
  6. Activate the workflow and monitor initial messages
