What This Workflow Does

This automation bridges the gap between idea capture in Evernote and task execution in ProofHub. Many professionals struggle with transferring their notes and ideas into actionable tasks, leading to lost opportunities and inefficient workflows. This template solves that by automatically creating ProofHub tasks whenever you add new notes to specified Evernote notebooks.

The workflow runs in the background, checking for new Evernote notes at regular intervals. When it detects a new note, it extracts the relevant information and creates a corresponding task in your ProofHub project with all the details preserved. This eliminates manual data entry while ensuring no ideas fall through the cracks.

How It Works

1. Monitoring Evernote for New Notes

The workflow starts by connecting to your Evernote account and checking specified notebooks for new notes at your chosen interval (default is every 15 minutes). You can configure which notebooks to monitor and apply filters based on tags or keywords.

2. Extracting Note Content

When a new note is detected, the automation extracts the title, content, creation date, and any attachments. It processes this information to prepare it for ProofHub's task format, preserving formatting and important details.

3. Creating ProofHub Tasks

The system then connects to your ProofHub account and creates a new task in your designated project. The note title becomes the task name, while the content becomes the task description. You can customize additional fields like assignees, due dates, and labels during setup.

Pro tip: Add specific tags in Evernote to automatically set ProofHub task priorities or assignees. For example, tag notes with "#urgent" to create high-priority tasks.
