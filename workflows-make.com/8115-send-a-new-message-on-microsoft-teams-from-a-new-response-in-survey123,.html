What This Workflow Does

Businesses that rely on surveys for customer feedback, support tickets, or field data collection face a critical delay: the time between a submission and a team member seeing it. Manually checking Survey123 for new responses is inefficient and leads to slow response times, frustrated customers, and missed opportunities for quick intervention.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a real-time bridge between Survey123 and Microsoft Teams. Every time a new response is submitted—whether it's a customer describing a problem, an employee reporting a safety issue, or a client providing feedback—a detailed notification is instantly posted to a designated Teams channel. This ensures the right people are aware immediately, turning survey data into actionable team alerts without any manual effort.

Pro tip: Use this for IT support tickets. When an employee submits a problem via Survey123, the IT team gets a Teams message with all details instantly, slashing resolution time.

How It Works

The workflow is triggered by a new entry in your Survey123 form and orchestrates the delivery of that information directly into your team's conversation.

Step 1: Capture the New Survey Response

The automation starts the moment a respondent clicks "Submit" on your Survey123 form. Make.com watches for new submissions via a webhook or scheduled check, capturing the entire response bundle—including answers to questions like name, contact info, and problem description.

Step 2: Parse and Structure the Data

The workflow then parses the raw survey data. It extracts specific answers from the submitted form and structures them into clear, readable variables. For example, it will isolate the "Describe your problem" answer from the "Email" and "Full Name" fields, preparing them for the notification message.

Step 3: Format and Send the Teams Message

Finally, the automation connects to your Microsoft Teams account via a secure connection. It posts a formatted message to your specified channel. This message can include the respondent's details, a summary of their issue, and even actionable elements like @mentions for specific team members or a link back to the full survey response for more context.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the pre-built template in your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Survey123: In the first module, authenticate your Survey123 account and select the specific form you want to monitor for new responses.
  3. Connect Microsoft Teams: In the Teams module, authenticate with your Microsoft account. You'll be asked to select the specific Team and Channel where notifications should be posted.
  4. Map Your Data: This is the crucial step. In the Teams "Create a message" action, map the data from your Survey123 response to the Teams message. Replace the placeholder fields (like "Full Name," "Email") with the actual variables from your survey.
  5. Test and Activate: Run a single test by submitting a dummy response to your Survey123 form. Check that the correct notification appears in your Teams channel. Once confirmed, activate the scenario to run automatically.

Pro tip: Use the "Adaptor" tool in Make.com to easily find and map the correct data fields from your Survey123 response to the Teams message body, avoiding errors.
