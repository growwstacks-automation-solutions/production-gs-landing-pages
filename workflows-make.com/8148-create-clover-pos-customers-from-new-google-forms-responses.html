What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Clover POS, manually entering customer data from online forms, event sign-ups, or loyalty program registrations is a tedious and error-prone task. Every new response in Google Forms means someone must stop what they're doing, open Clover, and type in details—a process that wastes valuable time and often leads to typos or missed entries.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your customer-facing forms and your point-of-sale system. Whenever a new response is submitted to your designated Google Form, the workflow automatically triggers, extracts the customer information, and creates a corresponding customer profile in your Clover POS. This turns a passive data collection tool into an active customer relationship manager.

The business value is immediate: your customer database grows accurately and in real-time, your staff is freed for higher-value work, and new customers can be marketed to or served instantly, improving their first impression and lifetime value.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple trigger-and-action principle, orchestrated by Make.com. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Monitor Google Forms for New Responses

The scenario starts by watching your specified Google Form. It polls for new submissions at regular intervals. The moment someone fills out and submits the form—whether it's for a newsletter, a workshop, or a customer feedback survey—the automation captures the entire response as a data package.

Step 2: Parse and Structure the Customer Data

Make.com reads the form answers and maps them to specific data fields. It intelligently handles the information, ensuring that the customer's first name, last name, email, phone number, and any other collected details (like address or notes) are correctly identified and prepared for the next step.

Pro tip: Structure your Google Form with clear, separate fields (e.g., "First Name" and "Last Name" instead of just "Name") for the cleanest data mapping to Clover's customer fields.

Step 3: Create the Customer in Clover POS

Using the Clover POS module in Make.com, the workflow sends a secure API call to your Clover account. It uses the parsed data to execute the "Create a Customer" function, populating the new customer profile with all the information from the form. The customer is now instantly available in your Clover customer list for transactions, loyalty programs, or marketing.

What You'll Need

To implement this workflow, ensure you have the following set up and ready:

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available).
  2. A Clover POS merchant account with admin access to generate API credentials.
  3. A Google Form that is actively collecting the customer data you wish to sync. You must have edit access to this form.
  4. The customer fields in your Clover POS that you want to populate (standard fields include name, email, phone, and address).
  5. Basic understanding of how to connect apps in Make.com (the template guides you through this).

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automation live in under 15 minutes:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your own Make.com account.
  2. Connect Google Forms: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Google account and select the specific form you want to monitor.
  3. Connect Clover POS: In the Clover module, authenticate with your Clover merchant credentials (you may need your API token from the Clover Developer Dashboard).
  4. Map Your Data Fields: Match the question titles from your Google Form (e.g., "Email Address") to the corresponding customer field in Clover (e.g., "email"). The template provides a common mapping you can adjust.
  5. Run a Test: Activate the scenario and submit a test response to your Google Form. Check your Clover POS customer list to confirm the new profile appears correctly.
  6. Go Live: Once testing is successful, your automation is ready. Every new form response will now flow directly into Clover without any manual intervention.
