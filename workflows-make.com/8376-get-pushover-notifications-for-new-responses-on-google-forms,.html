What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of delayed response to form submissions. Many businesses rely on Google Forms for customer feedback, applications, or surveys, but checking for new responses manually wastes time and risks missing time-sensitive submissions.

The workflow automatically detects new Google Forms responses and sends them as priority notifications through Pushover. Unlike email alerts that might get buried in your inbox, Pushover delivers instant mobile and desktop notifications you can't ignore.

How It Works

1. New response detection

The Make.com scenario continuously monitors your Google Form for new submissions. It checks at intervals you configure (from every minute to hourly).

2. Data processing

When a new response arrives, the workflow extracts key information like submission time, respondent details, and form answers. You can customize which fields to include in notifications.

3. Notification delivery

The system formats the response data into a clear Pushover message and sends it to your specified devices. Notifications include a direct link back to the form response for quick action.

Pro tip: Configure different notification sounds for different form types to instantly recognize priority submissions.
