What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses using Shopify, every new customer represents a potential long-term relationship. However, when customer data sits isolated in your store, your sales and marketing teams miss critical opportunities for follow-up, upselling, and personalized service. Manually exporting CSV files and importing them into your CRM is a tedious, error-prone process that creates delays and data silos.

This automated workflow solves that problem by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between Shopify and NetHunt CRM. The moment a customer completes their first purchase or creates an account, their profile—including contact details, order history, and lifetime value—is automatically created as a new record in NetHunt. This ensures your sales team has immediate, accurate data to engage leads, segment audiences, and drive revenue, all without lifting a finger.

How It Works

The automation is built on Make.com and operates on a simple trigger-action principle, ensuring reliability and speed.

Step 1: Monitor for New Shopify Customers

The workflow starts by watching your Shopify store for new customer creation events. This can be triggered by a new order placement, account sign-up, or both, depending on your configuration.

Step 2: Gather Complete Customer Data

Once triggered, the automation collects all available data for that customer from Shopify: name, email, phone, addresses, total spent, order count, and any tags or notes attached to their profile.

Pro tip: Use Shopify customer tags to automate segmentation in NetHunt. For example, tag high-value customers as "VIP" in Shopify, and the workflow can automatically add a corresponding tag or custom field in the NetHunt record.

Step 3: Create or Update the NetHunt Record

The workflow then sends this structured data to NetHunt CRM via its API, creating a new contact record. It intelligently maps Shopify fields to the corresponding NetHunt fields, ensuring a clean import.

Step 4: Error Handling & Logging

The scenario includes error handling modules. If a sync fails (e.g., due to a duplicate email or API issue), it can send a notification to your team or retry the operation, ensuring no customer falls through the cracks.
