What This Workflow Does

For sales teams, a new lead in the CRM is just the beginning. The real work—the follow-up—often gets lost in the shuffle between meetings, emails, and other tasks. Manually copying contact details from NetHunt into your to-do list is a repetitive, error-prone time sink that costs you deals.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your customer data and your action plan. Every time a new record is created in NetHunt CRM, this Make.com workflow instantly creates a corresponding, detailed task in Todoist. It transforms raw lead information into a clear, prioritized next step for your sales reps, ensuring every new opportunity is captured and acted upon immediately.

The business value is clear: reduced manual data entry, eliminated human forgetfulness, and accelerated sales cycles. By automating this critical handoff, you ensure your team spends time selling, not administrating.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, trigger-action sequence that runs automatically in the background.

Step 1: A New Record Appears in NetHunt

The automation is triggered the moment a new contact, company, or deal is saved in your NetHunt CRM. This could be from a web form submission, an imported list, or manual entry by a team member. Make.com watches for this new record in real-time.

Step 2: Data is Extracted and Prepared

The workflow pulls key information from the NetHunt record. This typically includes the contact's name, email, company, deal stage, notes, and any custom fields you've defined (like "Budget" or "Next Contact Date"). This data is formatted and prepared for the next step.

Pro tip: Use NetHunt's custom fields to capture specific follow-up requirements (e.g., "Send proposal," "Call next Tuesday"). The automation can map these directly into the Todoist task description, creating hyper-contextual actions.

Step 3: A Task is Created in Todoist

Using the prepared data, the workflow creates a new task in your specified Todoist project. The task name can be dynamically generated, such as "Follow up with [Contact Name] at [Company]." The task description can contain all the relevant details from NetHunt, and you can even set a due date based on a "Next Action Date" field.

The entire process from lead creation to task assignment happens within seconds, far faster than any manual process could achieve.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting started is a matter of connecting your accounts and mapping your data.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect NetHunt: In the Make.com scenario, authorize the NetHunt module by logging into your account. Select the specific NetHunt workspace you want to monitor.
  3. Connect Todoist: Similarly, authorize the Todoist module and select the project (e.g., "Sales Follow-ups") where new tasks should be created.
  4. Map Your Data: In the "Create a Task" module, map the fields. Drag the data from your NetHunt record (like `Contact Name`) into the Todoist task `Content` field. Set a due date if desired.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and create a test record in NetHunt. Within moments, check your specified Todoist project for the new, automatically generated task.
