What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common problem faced by support teams and service businesses: manually transferring customer inquiries from web forms into your case management system. When customers fill out your Google Forms for support requests, service inquiries, or project submissions, this workflow automatically detects new responses and creates corresponding cases in Seliom.

The system runs on a scheduled basis (default every 15 minutes) to check for new form submissions. It extracts all the relevant information from the form response – including customer contact details, issue description, priority level, and any custom fields you've set up – and uses this data to create a fully populated case in Seliom. This eliminates the need for support staff to manually copy-paste information between systems, reducing processing time from minutes to seconds.

How It Works

Step 1: Monitor Google Forms for New Responses

The workflow begins by periodically checking your Google Forms for new submissions. Using Make.com's scheduling module, it scans your form responses at regular intervals to detect any new entries that haven't been processed yet.

Step 2: Extract and Process Form Data

When a new response is detected, the automation extracts all the submitted information. This includes standard fields like name, email, and message, plus any custom fields you've added to your form. The system processes this data to ensure it's properly formatted for Seliom.

Step 3: Create Case in Seliom

The processed data is then used to create a new case in your Seliom account. The automation maps each form field to the corresponding case field in Seliom, ensuring all information is accurately transferred and the case is properly categorized and prioritized.

Step 4: Error Handling and Logging

If any issues occur during case creation, the workflow includes error handling to log the problem and can be configured to notify administrators. This ensures that no customer inquiry is lost due to technical issues.

Pro tip: Customize the scheduling frequency based on your support volume. For high-priority inquiries, set the check interval to 5 minutes. For general support, 15-30 minutes is usually sufficient.
