What This Workflow Does

In the crowded world of digital marketing, a physical postcard can be a breath of fresh air for your customers. This automated workflow solves the problem of manual, time-consuming direct mail campaigns by seamlessly connecting your Shopify store to Merci Facteur, a leading online printing and mailing service.

The automation listens for specific customer events in Shopify—like a completed purchase, a birthday, or a period of inactivity—and automatically triggers the creation and sending of a personalized postcard. This transforms a traditionally slow, labor-intensive process into a scalable marketing channel that builds genuine customer relationships and drives repeat business.

Instead of exporting CSV files, manually designing cards, and managing postal logistics, this system handles everything from data transfer to mail delivery with a single, automated trigger. It ensures your thoughtful gestures are never forgotten in the daily hustle, turning customer data into tangible moments of connection.

How It Works

The workflow acts as a smart bridge between your e-commerce data and physical mail delivery. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Trigger from Shopify

The automation is initiated by a specific event in your Shopify store. This could be a new order being placed, a customer's birthday field being updated, or a filter identifying customers who haven't purchased in a set timeframe.

Step 2: Fetch Customer Data

Make.com retrieves the relevant customer details from Shopify, including their full name, shipping address, order value, and any other custom properties you wish to use for personalization.

Step 3: Design Personalization

The customer data is mapped into a pre-designed postcard template within Merci Facteur. Variables like the customer's first name, order number, or a special offer code are dynamically inserted, making each postcard unique.

Step 4: Automate the Send Order

Make.com sends the finalized design and the customer's verified address to Merci Facteur via its API. Merci Facteur then takes over, handling the printing, stamping, and physical mailing of the postcard.

Step 5: Log & Confirm

The workflow can log each sent postcard in a Google Sheet or notify your team via Slack, providing a clear audit trail and confirming that the touchpoint was executed.

Pro tip: Start with a simple "Thank You" campaign for new customers. The ROI is clear, and it sets a positive tone for the relationship. Once that's running smoothly, add more complex triggers like win-back or birthday campaigns.

What You'll Need

To implement this workflow successfully, ensure you have the following set up:

  1. A Shopify Store with admin access to create API credentials or install a Make.com integration app.
  2. An active account with Merci Facteur, including access to their API keys for automation.
  3. A Make.com account (free tier may suffice for low volume).
  4. Clean and validated customer address data in Shopify. International sends require proper formatting.
  5. Pre-designed postcard templates in your Merci Facteur account, with placeholder fields for personalization.
  6. A clear campaign goal (e.g., thank you, birthday, win-back) to define your automation trigger and message.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your postcard automation live in under an hour:

  1. Duplicate the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the ready-made scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, authorize the connections to your Shopify store and your Merci Facteur account using the provided API keys.
  3. Define Your Trigger: Configure the first Shopify module. Choose your trigger event (e.g., "Updated Customer" for birthdays, or "Watch Orders" for post-purchase thank-yous).
  4. Map the Data: In the Merci Facteur module, map the Shopify customer fields (name, address) to the corresponding fields in your chosen postcard template.
  5. Set a Filter (Optional): Add a filter before the send action to control costs. For example, only send to customers in a specific country or with a minimum order value.
  6. Test Thoroughly: Run a test with a controlled trigger (e.g., a test order or a specific customer) and verify a postcard is created in your Merci Facteur dashboard.
  7. Activate & Monitor: Turn the scenario on and monitor the logs for the first few sends. Adjust design or messaging based on initial results.
