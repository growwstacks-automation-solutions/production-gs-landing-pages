What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common, time-consuming problem for businesses that use direct mail: manually transferring customer data from a spreadsheet to a postcard mailing service. Every new row added to your Google Sheets—containing a customer's name, address, and a personalized message—triggers the automatic creation and sending of a physical postcard via Merci Facteur.

The workflow eliminates the need for an employee to copy-paste data, check for errors, and manually initiate each mailing. It turns your Google Sheets into a direct command center for your postcard campaigns. This not only saves hours of administrative work each week but also ensures mailings are sent instantly, reducing delays and improving customer experience. The automation handles the entire process from data capture to physical delivery, making your direct mail efforts scalable and reliable.

Beyond saving time, this integration ensures accuracy. Manual data entry is prone to mistakes—a wrong address, a misspelled name, or a forgotten row. Automation uses the data exactly as it appears in your sheet, guaranteeing that what you intend to send is what gets mailed. This protects your marketing budget from wasted mailings and maintains the professionalism of your customer communications.

How It Works

Step 1: Monitor Your Google Sheet

The workflow watches a specific Google Sheets spreadsheet for new rows. This is typically a sheet used to collect customer information, such as new sign-ups, purchase records, or event attendees.

Step 2: Capture Row Data

When a new row is added, Make.com instantly captures all the relevant columns. This includes the mailing address, recipient name, any custom message fields, and potentially design preferences or campaign codes stored in the sheet.

Step 3: Format Data for Merci Facteur

The captured data is mapped and formatted to match the input requirements of the Merci Facteur API. Address fields are validated, messages are trimmed to fit the postcard layout, and any personalization variables are inserted.

Step 4: Send the Postcard Request

Make.com sends a structured request to Merci Facteur, instructing it to print and mail a postcard using the provided data. This includes specifying the design template, postal service options, and any other parameters you've configured.

Step 5: Confirm and Log

The workflow can then capture the confirmation or tracking information from Merci Facteur and log it back into your Google Sheets or another system. This creates a complete audit trail, showing exactly when each postcard was sent and its status.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated "Status" column in your Google Sheet. The workflow can update this column from "Pending" to "Sent" with a tracking ID after the postcard is dispatched, giving you a real-time view of your campaign progress.

Quick Setup Guide

1. Prepare Your Google Sheet: Create or identify the sheet that will serve as your data source. Ensure columns are named consistently (e.g., "Full_Address", "First_Name").

2. Connect Make.com to Google Sheets: In Make.com, add the Google Sheets module and authenticate with your account. Select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet to monitor.

3. Connect Make.com to Merci Facteur: Add the Merci Facteur module (or use an HTTP module for their API) and authenticate. Configure the postcard template ID, mailing options, and other settings.

4. Map the Data: In the workflow, connect the data from the new Google Sheets row to the corresponding fields in the Merci Facteur request. Test with a sample row.

5. Activate and Monitor: Turn on the workflow. Add a test row to your sheet to trigger the first postcard. Verify the mailing was successful and check your logs.
