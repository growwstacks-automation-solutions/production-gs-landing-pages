What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Quaderno for tax-compliant invoicing and Xero for accounting, manually transferring invoice data is a repetitive, error-prone task. This disconnect creates a high risk of mismatched records, delayed revenue recognition, and hours wasted on reconciliation each month.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Quaderno and Xero. Every time a new invoice is issued in Quaderno, the workflow instantly captures the details—customer information, line items, amounts, taxes—and creates a corresponding, perfectly formatted invoice in your Xero account. It turns a manual, multi-step process into a silent, reliable background operation.

The result is a single source of truth for your finances. Your sales data from Quaderno flows directly into your general ledger in Xero, ensuring accuracy, saving administrative time, and providing real-time visibility into your financial performance without any lag.

How It Works

The automation acts as a intelligent connector, listening for events in Quaderno and taking precise action in Xero.

Step 1: Monitoring Quaderno for New Invoices

The workflow starts by watching your Quaderno account for the "Invoice Created" event. This can be set to trigger instantly or at scheduled intervals, ensuring no invoice slips through.

Step 2: Extracting and Structuring Invoice Data

When a new invoice is detected, the automation pulls all relevant data: the client's name and contact details, invoice date and number, a detailed breakdown of products/services, subtotals, tax calculations, and the final total amount.

Step 3: Preparing and Creating the Xero Invoice

The workflow then maps this extracted data into the exact format Xero requires. It finds or creates the contact in Xero, assigns the correct account codes, applies the proper tax rates, and populates all line item descriptions. Finally, it sends a request to Xero's API to create the draft or approved invoice.

Step 4: Error Handling and Notifications

If anything goes wrong—like a missing client in Xero or an API error—the workflow can be configured to send an immediate alert (via email, Slack, or SMS) to your team. This allows for quick manual intervention while logging the issue for audit purposes.

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to only sync "Paid" or "Sent" invoices from Quaderno to avoid cluttering Xero with drafts or estimates, keeping your accounting records clean and actionable.
