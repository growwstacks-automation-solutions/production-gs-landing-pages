What This Workflow Does

Webinar no-shows represent a significant missed opportunity for businesses. When people register but don't attend, they've already demonstrated interest in your content or services. Manual follow-up processes are time-consuming and often get deprioritized, leading to lost leads and potential revenue.

This automated workflow solves this problem by connecting Livestorm with Mailgun to create a seamless follow-up system. Every time a webinar concludes, the system automatically identifies registrants who didn't attend and sends them a personalized email with the recording, key takeaways, and a clear next step. This ensures no potential customer falls through the cracks while saving your team hours of manual work each week.

How It Works

Step 1: Webinar Completion Trigger

The workflow activates when a Livestorm webinar session ends. Make.com monitors your Livestorm account for completed events and automatically retrieves the attendance data, including all registrants and their participation status.

Step 2: No-Show Identification

The system filters through the attendee list to identify registrants who signed up but didn't actually attend the live session. This segmentation happens automatically based on Livestorm's attendance tracking data.

Step 3: Personalized Email Generation

For each no-show registrant, Make.com composes a customized email using Mailgun's email service. The template can include dynamic fields like the registrant's name, webinar title, and specific call-to-action based on your business needs.

Step 4: Automated Email Delivery

Mailgun sends the follow-up emails immediately or according to your preferred schedule. The system tracks delivery status and can handle bouncebacks or other delivery issues automatically.

Pro tip: Customize your email template to include a link to the webinar recording, key takeaways, and a specific next step like scheduling a one-on-one consultation or downloading related resources.

The optimal time to send no-show follow-ups is within 24 hours after the webinar ends. This timing ensures the event is still fresh in their minds while showing you value their initial interest. Sending too late reduces engagement, while sending immediately might seem pushy.

The sweet spot is 4-6 hours post-webinar for maximum open rates. This gives you time to process the recording and prepare follow-up materials while capitalizing on the registrant's recent intent. Testing different timing with your audience can reveal ideal patterns.

An effective no-show email should include: a recording of the webinar, key takeaways or highlights, a clear call-to-action (like scheduling a demo), and an apology-free tone that focuses on value. Avoid making registrants feel guilty - instead, emphasize how they can still benefit from the content they missed.

Personalization is key - use the registrant's name and reference the specific webinar they signed up for. Including social proof or testimonials from attendees can also increase engagement and conversion rates significantly.
