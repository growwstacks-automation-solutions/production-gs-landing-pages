What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Layerise to guide customers through product experiences, a critical challenge is consolidating that interaction data for analysis. Manually copying completion statuses, feedback scores, and milestone data from Layerise into spreadsheets is tedious, error-prone, and creates reporting delays. This means teams lack real-time visibility into onboarding effectiveness and customer journey health.

This automated workflow solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Layerise and Google Sheets. Whenever a new experience is completed or updated in Layerise, this Make.com scenario instantly captures the data and adds it as a new row in your designated Google Sheets worksheet. It transforms scattered experience data into a structured, queryable database automatically, ensuring your customer success, product, and management teams always have the latest insights at their fingertips.

How It Works

The automation operates on a trigger-and-action principle, requiring no manual intervention once set up.

Step 1: Monitoring Layerise for New Experiences

The workflow begins by watching your Layerise account for specific events. You can configure it to trigger when a user completes an experience, reaches a certain milestone, or when new feedback is submitted. Make.com polls Layerise at regular intervals, acting as the listening ear for your customer journey data.

Step 2: Extracting and Structuring the Data

When a trigger event occurs, the workflow pulls all relevant data points from Layerise. This can include the user's email, experience name, completion percentage, time spent, individual step responses, and any scores or notes. The automation then structures this data into a clear, consistent format suitable for a spreadsheet row.

Step 3: Adding a Row to Google Sheets

The formatted data is then sent to your pre-defined Google Sheets file. The workflow identifies the correct worksheet and appends a new row at the bottom. Each column is mapped to a specific data field from Layerise (e.g., Column A: Timestamp, Column B: Customer Email, Column C: Experience Name, Column D: Completion Status), building a historical log over time.

Pro tip: Use a separate tab in your Google Sheet for "raw" data from this automation. Then, use another tab with QUERY or FILTER functions to create live summary tables and dashboards that automatically update as new rows are added.

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available).

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your own Make.com account.
  2. Connect Layerise: In the first module, click the Layerise icon and authorize Make.com to access your account using your API key.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: In the "Add a Row" module, authorize your Google account and paste the URL of the spreadsheet you want to use.
  4. Map Your Data: Specify which Layerise data fields (like "experience_name" or "user_email") should go into which columns of your Sheet (Column A, B, C, etc.).
  5. Test and Activate: Run a single test operation to ensure a row is added correctly to your Sheet. Once verified, turn the scenario on. It will now run automatically based on your chosen schedule.
