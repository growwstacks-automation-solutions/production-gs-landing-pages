What This Workflow Does

Hosting webinars and online events is a powerful lead generation strategy, but the real value is lost if registrant data sits siloed in your event platform. Manually exporting lists and importing them into your email marketing tool is tedious, error-prone, and creates a critical delay in follow-up. This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between ClickMeeting and ActiveCampaign.

Every time a new person registers for your webinar, workshop, or meeting in ClickMeeting, this Make.com workflow triggers instantly. It captures the attendee's details—like their name, email, and company—and automatically creates a corresponding contact record in your ActiveCampaign account. This means your sales and marketing teams can begin nurturing that lead immediately with targeted emails, without lifting a finger.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable automation that acts as your digital data assistant.

Step 1: New Registration Detection

The scenario starts with a ClickMeeting module set to watch for the "New Registration" event. The moment a registration form is submitted, Make.com receives a webhook with all the submitted attendee data.

Step 2: Data Parsing and Preparation

Make.com processes the incoming data, mapping the relevant fields from ClickMeeting (e.g., email, first name, last name) to the correct field format expected by ActiveCampaign. You can also add logic here, such as applying a specific tag like "Webinar-Registrant-March".

Pro tip: Use this step to enrich the data. You can add the webinar title as a custom field in ActiveCampaign, allowing for hyper-segmented email campaigns later.

Step 3: ActiveCampaign Contact Creation

The final module uses your ActiveCampaign connection to create or update a contact. The new lead is seamlessly added to your chosen list, ready to be entered into an automation sequence, such as a pre-webinar reminder series or a post-event follow-up campaign.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: Authorize Make.com to access your ClickMeeting and ActiveCampaign accounts by entering your API keys or logging in via OAuth.
  3. Configure the Trigger: In the ClickMeeting module, select the specific event or room you want to monitor for new registrations.
  4. Map the Data: In the ActiveCampaign "Create a Contact" module, map the ClickMeeting fields (like registrant.email) to the corresponding ActiveCampaign contact fields.
  5. Test & Activate: Use the "Run once" feature to test with a recent registration. Confirm the contact appears in ActiveCampaign, then turn the scenario to "ON".
