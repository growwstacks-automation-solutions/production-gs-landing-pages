What This Workflow Does

In today's fast-paced remote and hybrid work environments, keeping everyone aligned on meeting schedules is a constant challenge. When a new ClickMeeting conference is created, the host typically has to manually copy the link and message their team via email or Slack. This process is not only time-consuming but also prone to errors—links get sent to the wrong channel, details are forgotten, or team members simply miss the announcement.

This free Make.com template solves that problem by creating a seamless, automated bridge between ClickMeeting and Slack. Every time a new conference is scheduled, this workflow instantly captures the event details and posts a formatted notification directly to your chosen Slack channel or direct message. It turns a multi-step, manual task into a zero-touch process that ensures 100% of your team is informed, 100% of the time.

How It Works

The automation operates on a simple trigger-and-action principle, reliably connecting two critical business tools.

Step 1: Monitoring ClickMeeting

The workflow continuously watches your ClickMeeting account for new events. Using Make.com's reliable scheduler, it checks at regular intervals (e.g., every 5 minutes) for any conferences created since the last check. It captures all relevant data: conference title, start time, host, description, and most importantly, the unique join URL.

Step 2: Formatting the Alert

Once a new conference is detected, the workflow processes the raw data. It structures the information into a clear, actionable message. You can customize this message to include emojis, format the date/time in your local timezone, and highlight key details like whether it's internal or client-facing.

Pro tip: Use Slack's block kit builder within Make.com to create rich, interactive messages. Add a "Join Meeting" button that links directly to the ClickMeeting room, saving your team even more clicks.

Step 3: Posting to Slack

The final step is dispatching the formatted notification to your specified Slack destination. The workflow authenticates with your Slack workspace and posts the message. You can send it to a public channel like #general-meetings, a private project channel, or even a specific person's DM for critical alerts.

Who This Is For

This automation is invaluable for any team or organization that relies on ClickMeeting for virtual gatherings. It's perfect for remote-first companies that need flawless meeting coordination across time zones. Sales teams can use it to instantly notify the entire floor about a newly booked demo. Customer success departments can alert specialists about upcoming client check-ins. Education and training teams can ensure all participants know about new webinar sessions the moment they're scheduled.

What You'll Need

  1. A Make.com account (free tier is sufficient to run this template).
  2. An active ClickMeeting account with administrative or API access to retrieve conference data.
  3. A Slack workspace where you have permission to create an app and post messages.
  4. The Slack channel name or user ID where you want notifications to be sent.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect ClickMeeting: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your ClickMeeting account using your API key.
  3. Connect Slack: In the Slack module, authorize Make.com to access your Slack workspace. You'll be guided through a simple OAuth process.
  4. Configure the Destination: Specify the exact Slack channel (e.g., #meetings) or person you want to receive the alerts.
  5. Customize the Message (Optional): Edit the text module to change the notification's wording, add your company logo, or include specific hashtags.
  6. Turn it On: Activate the scenario. Make.com will immediately begin monitoring ClickMeeting and sending alerts to Slack.
