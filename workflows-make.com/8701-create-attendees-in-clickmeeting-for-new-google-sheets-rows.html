What This Workflow Does

Managing webinar registrations is a common bottleneck for marketers, coaches, and event organizers. You collect names and emails in a Google Sheet (often from a form), but then you face the tedious, error-prone task of manually adding each person as an attendee in your webinar platform like ClickMeeting. This process wastes hours, risks missing registrants, and delays sending confirmations.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a digital bridge between your registration spreadsheet and ClickMeeting. Every time a new row is added to your designated Google Sheet—whether it's from a form submission, a team member's input, or a CSV import—the workflow is triggered. It takes the attendee's details (like name and email) and automatically creates a corresponding attendee record in your specified ClickMeeting webinar. The result is a seamless, hands-off registration pipeline.

Pro tip: Use this automation not just for webinars, but for any event managed in ClickMeeting—workshops, online courses, or live Q&A sessions. It ensures your attendee list is always perfectly synchronized.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple "trigger-and-action" sequence, running reliably in the background on Make.com.

Step 1: Monitor Google Sheets for New Rows

The workflow starts by watching a specific Google Sheets spreadsheet. It's configured to check for new rows at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes) or instantly via a webhook if your form is connected. When it detects a new row, it captures all the data in that row's columns.

Step 2: Map Data Fields

Make.com then maps the relevant columns from the sheet to the required fields in ClickMeeting. For example, it takes the value from the "Email Address" column and maps it to ClickMeeting's "attendee email" field. Similarly, it maps "First Name" and "Last Name" columns. This step is fully customizable to match your specific spreadsheet layout.

Step 3: Create Attendee in ClickMeeting

Using the mapped data, the workflow makes an authenticated API call to your ClickMeeting account. It creates a new attendee for the webinar you've specified in the setup. The attendee is added immediately, and they will typically receive ClickMeeting's automatic confirmation email.

Step 4: (Optional) Log Success or Handle Errors

The workflow can be extended to log each successful addition to another sheet or send you a notification. More importantly, it can be set up to catch errors—like a duplicate email or an invalid format—and notify you so no registration slips through the cracks.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and it will open in your Make.com account. Create a copy to edit.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, click on the Google Sheets module. Authenticate and select your specific spreadsheet and worksheet. Then, authenticate your ClickMeeting account.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set the trigger to watch for new rows in your chosen worksheet. You can test it by adding a dummy row to your sheet.
  4. Map Your Data Fields: In the ClickMeeting "Create Attendee" module, map the spreadsheet columns to the correct attendee fields. Ensure the email field is correctly mapped, as it's usually the unique identifier.
  5. Specify the Webinar: Enter the ClickMeeting webinar ID or select it from a dropdown. This tells the automation which event to add attendees to.
  6. Test & Activate: Run a single test execution with a sample row. Verify the attendee appears in your ClickMeeting control panel. Once confirmed, turn the scenario to "ON" and schedule it to run as often as needed.
