What This Workflow Does

Managing multilingual projects in Layerise requires constant oversight of language lists, translation statuses, and project assignments. Without automation, project managers and localization teams waste valuable time manually exporting data from Layerise and importing it into spreadsheets for reporting, analysis, or sharing with other departments. This process is not only repetitive but also prone to errors and delays, leading to outdated information guiding business decisions.

This Make.com workflow solves that problem by creating a seamless, real-time bridge between Layerise and Google Sheets. It automatically detects new languages added to your Layerise projects or updates to existing ones, and appends them as new rows in a designated Google Sheet. This ensures your entire team has access to a single source of truth for all localization data, updated automatically without any manual intervention.

How It Works

The automation operates on a trigger-action principle, reliably moving data from point A to point B whenever a change occurs.

Step 1: Monitoring Layerise for Changes

The scenario is triggered on a schedule (e.g., every hour) or by a webhook from Layerise. It connects to the Layerise API to retrieve a list of all languages configured within your projects or organization.

Step 2: Processing and Structuring the Data

Make.com processes the raw API response, filtering and mapping the relevant data points for each language. This typically includes the language code, full language name, associated project ID, creation date, and any custom status fields you wish to track.

Pro tip: Use Make's data transformation tools to clean and format the data before sending it to Sheets. For example, you can convert timestamps to your local timezone or combine separate fields into a single, more readable cell.

Step 3: Appending Rows to Google Sheets

The formatted data for each language is then sent to Google Sheets via its API. The workflow uses the "Add a Row" action to insert the new data at the bottom of your specified worksheet. It intelligently maps each piece of data to the correct column header, maintaining a clean and organized spreadsheet.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the provided Make.com scenario into your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make scenario editor, re-establish the connections to your specific Layerise and Google Sheets accounts when prompted.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set how often you want the workflow to run (e.g., every 6 hours) or configure a Layerise webhook if real-time updates are critical.
  4. Map Your Data: Point the Google Sheets module to your specific spreadsheet and worksheet. Ensure the column mapping in Make matches your sheet's headers.
  5. Test & Activate: Run a single test cycle to ensure data flows correctly from Layerise into a new row in your Sheet. Once confirmed, turn the scenario on.
