Send Slack Messages for New Tars Conversations

What This Workflow Does

If you use a Tars chatbot on your website to capture leads, qualify prospects, or answer FAQs, you know the frustration of potentially missing a conversation. Manually checking the Tars dashboard is inefficient and leads can go cold while waiting for a response. This creates a gap between capturing interest and taking action.

This automation workflow bridges that gap instantly. It connects your Tars account to your team's Slack workspace. Every time a visitor starts a new conversation with your Tars bot, this system automatically detects it and posts a detailed alert to a designated Slack channel. Your sales, support, or marketing team gets a real-time ping with the lead's information, enabling them to jump into the conversation or follow up immediately—often while the prospect is still actively browsing your site.

The solution transforms passive lead capture into an active engagement engine. It ensures accountability, dramatically reduces response times, and helps convert more of the traffic you're already paying for, simply by making sure the right person knows about an opportunity the moment it happens.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com and acts as a reliable bridge between Tars and Slack. It follows a simple but powerful sequence.

Step 1: Monitor Tars for New Conversations

The automation is triggered by the "Watch Conversations" module in Tars. It continuously polls your Tars account, checking for any newly initiated chats. As soon as a visitor interacts with your chatbot, the workflow captures the event and all associated data.

Step 2: Structure the Alert Data

Make.com then processes the incoming data from Tars. It extracts the most valuable pieces of information, such as the visitor's name, email address, the answers they provided to the chatbot's questions, and which specific Tars campaign they engaged with. This step formats the raw data into a clear, actionable message.

Pro tip: Customize the data mapping to include only the most critical information for your team. For sales, focus on contact details and budget. For support, include the specific issue described.

Step 3: Send a Formatted Message to Slack

Finally, the workflow uses the "Create a Message" action for Slack. It posts the formatted alert into your chosen Slack channel. The message can include the lead's details, a link to view the full conversation in Tars, and even @mention specific team members to ensure immediate attention.

What You'll Need

To set up this workflow, you will need a few accounts and permissions ready:

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available).
  2. A Tars account with an active chatbot campaign.
  3. Access to a Slack workspace where you have permission to create incoming webhooks or connect an app.
  4. Your Tars API credentials (available in your Tars dashboard settings).
  5. Slack API credentials (a Bot User OAuth Token) for the workspace you want to post to.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Tars: In the first module, click the Tars icon and authenticate your account by entering your API key when prompted.
  3. Connect Slack: In the Slack module, authenticate and select the specific Slack channel where you want notifications to appear.
  4. Map the Data: Review the data fields being sent from Tars. Ensure key information like `contact_name` and `contact_email` are correctly mapped to appear in the Slack message.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario in Make.com. Then, go to your website and start a test conversation with your Tars bot. Within seconds, you should see a notification pop up in your designated Slack channel.
