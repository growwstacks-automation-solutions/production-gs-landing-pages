What This Workflow Does

For creative agencies, marketing teams, and in-house production studios, managing review requests is a constant, manual chore. A client emails a request, a salesperson sends a Slack message, or an internal team fills out a PDF—each requires someone to manually create a project in Filestage, input details, and invite reviewers. This process is slow, prone to error, and creates a bottleneck that delays project kick-off.

This automated workflow solves that by connecting Google Forms, a versatile tool for collecting structured requests, directly to Filestage, a powerful platform for visual feedback and approval. Every form submission—whether for a new video, website design, or marketing brochure—instantly becomes a fully configured Filestage project. The client's name, email, project description, and deadlines are transferred automatically, launching the review process the moment the request is made.

The business value is clear: you eliminate administrative lag, ensure no request falls through the cracks, and free up your team to focus on creative work instead of data entry. It turns a multi-step, human-dependent process into a seamless, instant handoff, improving both client satisfaction and internal team velocity.

How It Works

The automation acts as a digital bridge between your request intake (Google Forms) and your review management system (Filestage). Here’s a step-by-step breakdown:

Step 1: Trigger on New Form Response

The workflow is activated the instant someone submits your Google Form. Make.com watches for new responses and immediately captures all the submitted data, such as the requester's email, project title, description, file links, and desired deadline.

Step 2: Format and Prepare the Data

The data from the form is then structured to match Filestage's requirements. This step might involve combining fields (e.g., first and last name), formatting dates, or parsing text to extract key information. This ensures the project created in the next step is clean and professional.

Step 3: Create a New Project in Filestage

Using the prepared data, the automation logs into your Filestage account via API and creates a new project. It sets the project name, description, and other settings based on the form responses. Crucially, it can also add the submitter (and any other emails provided in the form) as a reviewer to the project.

Step 4: Confirm and Log

Once the project is successfully created, the workflow can perform follow-up actions. It might send a confirmation email to the requester with a direct link to the new Filestage project, post a notification to a Slack channel for your team, or log the event in a Google Sheet for tracking purposes.

Pro tip: Use a "Project Type" dropdown in your Google Form. You can then route different types of requests (e.g., "Social Media Graphic" vs. "Whitepaper") to different Filestage workspaces or apply specific preset settings automatically.

What You'll Need

To implement this workflow, you will need the following accounts and assets set up beforehand:

  1. A Google Workspace account to create and manage the Google Form that will trigger the automation.
  2. A paid Filestage account with API access enabled (typically available on their Business plan or higher).
  3. A Make.com account (free tier is sufficient to build and test the scenario).
  4. A published Google Form designed to capture all necessary information for a Filestage project (e.g., Project Name, Client Email, Description, Deadline, File Uploads or Links).
  5. Optional: A Slack workspace or Google Sheets document if you wish to add notifications or logging as part of the workflow.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automated request-to-review pipeline running:

  1. Duplicate the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the pre-built template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make scenario, authorize the connections to your specific Google Forms and Filestage accounts.
  3. Select Your Form: Point the Google Forms module to the exact form you've created for project requests.
  4. Map the Data: In the Filestage "Create a Project" module, map the form response fields (like "Project Title" and "Client Email") to the corresponding fields required by Filestage.
  5. Test with a Dummy Submission: Submit a test response to your Google Form and run the scenario once to ensure a project is created correctly in your Filestage dashboard.
  6. Activate & Monitor: Turn the scenario on. It will now run automatically for every new form submission. Monitor the first few requests to ensure everything flows smoothly.
