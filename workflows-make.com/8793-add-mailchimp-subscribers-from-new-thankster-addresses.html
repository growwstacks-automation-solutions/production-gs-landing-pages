What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Thankster to manage personalized cards and gifts, every new order represents a valuable customer. However, manually copying email addresses from Thankster into your Mailchimp list is a tedious, error-prone task that often gets forgotten. This leads to missed opportunities for email marketing, follow-up sequences, and building lasting customer relationships.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as a silent bridge between your sales and marketing tools. Every time a new address is created in your Thankster account—whether from a one-time purchase or a recurring order—the workflow instantly and accurately adds that person as a subscriber to your designated Mailchimp audience. It turns a manual, administrative chore into a reliable, hands-off process that fuels your marketing engine.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence, ensuring data flows seamlessly from point of sale to your marketing list.

Step 1: Monitor Thankster for New Addresses

The workflow starts by watching your Thankster account for any new address creation events. Make.com checks at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes) or uses a webhook for real-time triggering. When a new address record appears, the workflow captures key details like the customer's email, first name, last name, and any other relevant order information.

Pro tip: Configure the trigger to filter for specific types of Thankster orders if you only want to add customers from certain products or price points to your main list.

Step 2: Format and Prepare the Subscriber Data

Before sending data to Mailchimp, the workflow can map and format the information correctly. It ensures the email is valid, combines first and last names, and can assign tags (like "Thankster_Customer" or "Product_Greeting_Card") based on the order details. This preparation step is crucial for maintaining a clean, organized Mailchimp audience.

Step 3: Add or Update the Contact in Mailchimp

The processed data is then sent to Mailchimp's API. The workflow adds the new contact to your specified audience. It can be set to "update existing" if the email is already present, ensuring you don't create duplicates. Upon success, you can log the action or even trigger a notification for your team.

  3. Connect Mailchimp: In the Mailchimp module, add your API key and select the default audience where new subscribers should be added.
