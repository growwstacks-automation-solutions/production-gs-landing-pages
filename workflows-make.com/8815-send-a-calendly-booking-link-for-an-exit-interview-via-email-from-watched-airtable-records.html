What This Workflow Does

Employee departures are a critical moment for any organization. Exit interviews provide invaluable insights into workplace culture, management effectiveness, and competitive pressures. However, manually tracking resignations and scheduling these interviews is a time-consuming, often inconsistent process for HR teams. This leads to missed opportunities for feedback and can leave departing employees feeling undervalued.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless, trigger-based workflow. When an employee's resignation is logged in your Airtable base—your central HR database—the system instantly reacts. It composes a personalized email to that employee, containing a direct link to schedule their exit interview via Calendly. The entire process happens automatically, within minutes of the record being created or updated, ensuring prompt, professional follow-up every single time.

The business value is clear: HR saves administrative hours per week, guarantees 100% follow-up rate, and collects more consistent, timely feedback. It transforms a reactive, manual task into a proactive, standardized system that enhances your offboarding experience and protects your organizational learning.

How It Works

The workflow connects three key tools: Airtable as the data source, Make.com as the automation engine, and Calendly & Email as the action outputs. It follows a logical, event-driven sequence.

Step 1: Monitor Airtable for New Resignations

The automation begins by watching a specific table in your Airtable base. It's configured to trigger whenever a new record is added or when a specific field (like "Status" changing to "Resigned") is updated. This ensures the workflow starts at the exact moment the offboarding process should begin.

Step 2: Retrieve Employee Details

Once triggered, the workflow pulls all relevant data from the Airtable record: the employee's full name, email address, department, resignation date, and any other notes. This data is used to personalize the subsequent communication, making the invitation feel tailored and respectful.

Step 3: Generate a Calendly Booking Link

Using the Calendly module in Make.com, the workflow creates a unique scheduling link for the exit interview. This link can point to a dedicated Calendly event type configured for exit interviews, with the appropriate duration, host (e.g., HR manager), and available time slots.

Step 4: Send the Personalized Invitation Email

The final step crafts and sends an email directly to the employee's address. The email is populated with the employee's name, includes the generated Calendly link, and can contain standard messaging about the purpose of the interview, confidentiality, and appreciation for their contributions. It's sent via your preferred email service (like Gmail or Outlook) connected through Make.com.

Pro tip: You can add a step to update the Airtable record after the email is sent, marking the interview as "Invited," or even later when Calendly confirms a booking, marking it as "Scheduled." This creates a full-circle automated tracking system.

To implement this workflow, you'll need access to the following tools and data:

  2. An Airtable base with a table tracking employee data, including a field that indicates resignation status.
