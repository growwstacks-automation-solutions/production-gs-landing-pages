Secure Employee Offboarding Automation

Automatically send detailed access removal emails to your IT team from Airtable when an employee resigns.

Get This Workflow → Make.com · · Free Template

What This Workflow Does

When an employee resigns, HR must ensure all their system access is promptly revoked to protect company data. This process is often manual, slow, and error-prone—IT teams might miss an application or delay action, creating security gaps. This automation solves that by turning Airtable into a secure offboarding command center.

The workflow watches your HR Airtable base for a status change to "Resigned." When triggered, it compiles the employee's access list from linked records and sends a structured, detailed email directly to your IT team or ticketing system. This email includes all necessary information: employee name, department, list of systems, user IDs, and required actions. It ensures a consistent, auditable offboarding request every time, eliminating manual data entry and reducing risk.

For businesses, this means faster, more secure departures. HR saves hours per exit, IT gets actionable alerts immediately, and the company maintains a clear compliance trail. It's part of a larger offboarding sequence that can be extended to handle equipment return, final payroll, and directory updates—all from one Airtable trigger.

How It Works

Step 1: HR Updates Employee Status in Airtable

Your HR team marks the employee's status as "Resigned" in your central Airtable base. This base contains employee records, including their department, manager, and a linked table of all system accesses (e.g., Google Workspace, Salesforce, GitHub, internal apps).

Step 2: Make.com Watches for the Change

The Make.com scenario is configured to monitor that specific Airtable table for new or updated records where the status field changes to "Resigned." It instantly captures the employee record and all associated access records.

Step 3: Data is Compiled and Formatted

The automation pulls the employee's name, email, department, and manager, then fetches the list of systems from the linked access table. It formats this data into a clear, structured message, ready for IT.

Step 4: Secure Email is Sent to IT Team

Using an email module (like Gmail, Outlook, or SendGrid), the scenario sends a detailed email to your designated IT inbox or distribution list. The email can be encrypted, include priority labels, and provide direct links to admin panels for faster action.

Step 5: Audit Log is Updated

Optionally, the workflow can update another Airtable base or log file to record that the offboarding request was sent, timestamp it, and track its completion. This creates a full audit trail for compliance and reporting.

Pro tip: Integrate this with your IT ticketing system (like Jira, ServiceNow, or Zendesk) to automatically create a ticket instead of just an email. This assigns the task, sets due dates, and tracks resolution within your IT workflow.

Quick Setup Guide

1. Prepare Your Airtable Base: Ensure your employee table has a "Status" field and a linked "Systems Access" table listing each application, the employee's user ID, and any special notes for IT.

2. Import the Template: Use the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the Make.com scenario into your account. It will have pre-built modules for Airtable and email.

3. Connect Your Apps: In Make.com, connect your Airtable base and your email service. Set the correct table and watch field for the status change.

4. Configure the Email: Customize the email template with your IT team's address, your company's branding, and the specific information you want included (e.g., links to admin consoles).

5. Test and Activate: Create a test record in Airtable, change its status to "Resigned", and run the scenario to verify the email is sent correctly. Then activate the automation for live use.

Pro tip: Start with a small pilot group (e.g., one department) to refine the process before rolling it out company-wide. This allows you to adjust the data format and email content based on real IT feedback.
