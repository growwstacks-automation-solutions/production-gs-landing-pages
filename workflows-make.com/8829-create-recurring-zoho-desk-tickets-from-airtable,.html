What This Workflow Does

Manual, repetitive tasks are the silent killers of productivity in customer support and operations teams. Remembering to create the same ticket every Monday for a team sync, or every month for a client report, is error-prone and wastes valuable time that could be spent solving actual customer problems.

This automation solves that by connecting Airtable, your flexible database for planning, with Zoho Desk, your powerful customer support hub. It automatically reads a schedule from your Airtable base and creates corresponding tickets in Zoho Desk on the specified frequency—daily, weekly, or monthly. The result is a flawless, hands-off system for recurring customer interactions and internal tasks.

Beyond just saving time, this workflow brings reliability and consistency to your support operations. It ensures no scheduled follow-up is ever missed, helping you build stronger customer relationships through predictable and professional communication.

How It Works

The automation acts as a smart bridge between your planning tool and your action tool. Here’s a step-by-step look at the process:

Step 1: Schedule Monitoring in Airtable

The workflow is triggered on a schedule you set (e.g., every morning at 9 AM). It connects to your designated Airtable base and scans a table—often named "Recurring Tasks" or "Ticket Schedule." It looks for records where the scheduled day matches the current day.

Step 2: Filtering by Frequency

Using a filter module, the scenario checks the "Frequency" field in Airtable (Daily, Weekly, Monthly). For weekly tasks, it also checks the "Day of Week" field. This ensures a task set for "Weekly - Wednesday" only triggers on Wednesdays.

Step 3: Creating the Ticket in Zoho Desk

For each matching task, the workflow uses the Zoho Desk module to create a new ticket. It maps data from Airtable fields into the ticket: the task name becomes the subject, details become the description, and it can even set the priority, department, and assignee automatically.

Step 4: Logging & Error Handling

The scenario includes error handling routes. If a ticket fails to create, it can send an alert to a Slack channel or an email, ensuring you're immediately aware of any issues. Successful creations can also be logged back to Airtable for an audit trail.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in less than 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, update the connections for the Airtable and Zoho Desk modules. You'll need to log into each service and authorize Make.com to access your data.
  3. Configure Airtable: Point the Airtable "Search Records" module to your specific base and table. Ensure your table has the necessary columns (Frequency, Day, Subject, Description).
  4. Map Zoho Desk Fields: In the "Create a Ticket" module, map the data from Airtable to the correct Zoho Desk ticket fields (Subject, Description, Department, Contact).
  5. Set the Schedule: Configure the Schedule module to run as often as you need—typically once per day in the morning to catch all daily and weekly tasks.
  6. Test and Activate: Run a test execution with a sample record in Airtable. Verify a ticket appears in Zoho Desk. Once confirmed, activate the scenario.
