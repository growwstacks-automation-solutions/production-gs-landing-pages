What This Workflow Does

When your support or content team publishes a new article in Zendesk Guide, it's critical that other departments—like sales, success, and engineering—know about it immediately. Without automation, this relies on manual emails, chat messages, or meetings, which are slow, inconsistent, and easy to forget. This creates knowledge silos where teams give outdated information to customers.

This Make.com workflow solves that by creating a direct, real-time bridge between your knowledge base and your team's communication hub, Slack. It automatically detects newly created or updated Zendesk Guide articles and posts a formatted notification to a designated Slack channel. This ensures instant, company-wide awareness of new help content, improving customer interactions and internal collaboration.

The template is pre-configured to check for new articles every 3 hours, a balance between timeliness and respecting API rate limits. You can easily adjust this schedule to match the pace of your content creation, from hourly to daily checks.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence, eliminating all manual steps.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

A scheduler module acts as the workflow's heartbeat, triggering the process at your chosen interval (default: every 3 hours). This is the "when" of the automation, consistently checking Zendesk for new content without you lifting a finger.

Step 2: Fetch New Articles from Zendesk

Upon trigger, the workflow uses the Zendesk Guide API to search for articles created since the last check. It filters and retrieves key details like the article title, URL, author, body summary, and category.

Pro tip: You can modify the search filter to only notify about articles in specific categories (e.g., "Product Updates" or "Billing") or with certain labels, making notifications hyper-relevant to different teams.

Step 3: Format the Slack Message

The workflow then structures the article data into a clean, readable Slack message. You can customize this message to include emojis, format the title as a link, add the author's name for credit, and provide a short excerpt from the content.

Step 4: Post to Slack Channel

Finally, the formatted message is sent to your pre-selected Slack channel. You can configure it to post to a public channel like #help-articles, a private team channel, or even send a direct message to specific key individuals.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this workflow live in under 15 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" above to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Zendesk: In the first Zendesk module, click to add a connection. You'll need your Zendesk subdomain and an API token created from your Zendesk admin settings.
  3. Connect Slack: In the Slack module, add a connection using either a "Send a Message" bot token from a Slack app you create, or a simple Incoming Webhook URL for a specific channel.
  4. Set Your Channel: Enter the exact name of the Slack channel (e.g., #support-updates) where messages should be posted.
  5. Adjust Schedule (Optional): Click the clock icon on the scheduler module to change how often the workflow runs—from every minute to once a day.
  6. Turn It On: Flip the scenario switch to "ON" and publish a test article in Zendesk Guide to see the notification appear in Slack.
