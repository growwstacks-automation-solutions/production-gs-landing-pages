What This Workflow Does

For customer support teams and managers, manually counting daily support tickets is a repetitive, error-prone task that steals time from strategic work. Without automated reporting, your key performance dashboards in tools like Databox are stale, making it difficult to spot trends, manage team workload, or report accurately to leadership.

This Make.com workflow solves that by fully automating the flow of data from Zendesk to Databox. It runs on a daily schedule, queries Zendesk for all tickets created on the previous business day, counts them, and pushes that number as an updated metric to your designated Databox dashboard. The result is a hands-off, reliable pipeline that ensures your support volume KPI is always current and accurate.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple, robust sequence to transfer data between your systems without any manual intervention.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

The scenario is initiated by a Schedule module set to run every day at a specific time (e.g., 10:00 AM). This acts as the consistent starting point, ensuring your reporting happens like clockwork.

Step 2: Fetch Tickets from Zendesk

The workflow then connects to your Zendesk account via its API. It performs a search query, specifically looking for tickets that were created on the *previous* day. It can apply filters to count only relevant tickets, excluding spam or internal tasks if configured.

Step 3: Count and Process Data

Make.com processes the list of tickets returned from Zendesk. A router or iterator counts the number of items in the list, calculating the exact total volume for the day. This number is formatted and prepared for sending to Databox.

Pro tip: You can easily modify the Zendesk search query to count tickets based on other criteria, such as tickets from a specific brand, assigned to a certain group, or tagged with a particular label, giving you segmented metrics.

Step 4: Update Metric in Databox

Finally, the workflow authenticates with your Databox account and uses the "Push Custom Metric" action. It sends the calculated ticket count to a pre-defined metric key in your Databox account. This instantly updates the visualizations on your connected dashboards.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Zendesk: In the first Zendesk module, click to add a connection. Use your Zendesk subdomain and generate an API token in your Zendesk admin settings to authenticate.
  3. Connect Databox: In the Databox module, add a new connection using your Databox API token, which you can find in Databox under "Data Manager" > "Data Sources".
  4. Configure the Metric Key: In the Databox module settings, enter the exact "Custom Metric" key you created in your Databox account. This tells Databox where to place the data.
  5. Set Your Schedule: Adjust the time in the Schedule module to a time that suits your business day, typically after your support team's day has ended.
  6. Test and Activate: Run the scenario once manually to ensure it fetches data and updates Databox correctly. Then toggle the scenario to "ON" to let it run automatically every day.
