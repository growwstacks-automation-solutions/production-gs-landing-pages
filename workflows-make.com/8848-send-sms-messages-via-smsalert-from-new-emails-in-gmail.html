What This Workflow Does

In today's fast-paced business environment, critical emails can get buried in a crowded inbox. A missed urgent client request, a high-priority support ticket, or a time-sensitive sales inquiry can lead to lost revenue and damaged relationships. This automation solves that problem by bridging your email and mobile communication.

This Make.com template creates a direct pipeline from your Gmail account to your phone via SMS. It periodically checks for new emails and uses the retrieved information—like the sender's name, subject line, or a snippet of the body—to compose and send an instant SMS notification through SMSAlert. You receive a text message the moment an important email arrives, ensuring you can respond immediately, no matter where you are.

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to only trigger for emails from specific senders (like key clients) or containing certain keywords (like "URGENT" or "Invoice"). This prevents SMS spam and ensures you're only alerted for what truly matters.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple yet powerful sequence, automating what would otherwise be a manual and error-prone process.

Step 1: Scheduled Email Check

The automation is triggered on a schedule (default: every 15 minutes). It connects to your Gmail account via Make.com's Gmail module and searches for new, unread emails since the last check.

Step 2: Email Data Processing

For each new email found, the workflow extracts key data points: the sender's email address and name, the email subject, and often the first few lines of the message body. This data is formatted and prepared for the next step.

Step 3: SMS Composition & Dispatch

The formatted email data is sent to the SMSAlert module. Here, a concise SMS message is crafted—for example, "New email from [Sender]: [Subject]". This message is then instantly delivered to your pre-defined mobile number via SMSAlert's reliable gateway.

Step 4: Logging & Completion

The workflow marks the email as processed (optional) and logs the action, ensuring you have a record of notifications sent. It then waits for the next scheduled cycle to begin again.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Gmail: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Gmail account. You can restrict access to specific labels or just the inbox.
  3. Configure SMSAlert: In the SMS module, connect your SMSAlert account by entering your API credentials. Set the recipient phone number in the correct international format (e.g., +1XXXXXXXXXX).
  4. Customize the Message: Edit the text template for the SMS. Use data placeholders like the sender's name or email subject to make alerts informative.
  5. Set the Schedule: Adjust the schedule module from the default 15 minutes to an interval that suits your needs (e.g., every 5 minutes for real-time, or hourly for less critical alerts).
  6. Test & Activate: Run the scenario once manually to send a test SMS. If successful, activate the scenario to let it run automatically on its schedule.
