What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses using AmeriCommerce, managing orders often involves a tedious, error-prone process: manually copying data from the store admin into spreadsheets for tracking, reporting, or accounting. This template solves that problem by creating a seamless, automatic bridge between AmeriCommerce and Google Sheets.

Every time a customer completes a purchase on your AmeriCommerce store, this workflow instantly captures the entire order record—including customer details, items purchased, totals, and shipping information—and adds it as a new row in your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. It transforms a manual administrative task into a zero-touch, reliable automation, ensuring your sales data is always centralized, accurate, and up-to-date.

The business value is clear: you save hours per week on data entry, eliminate transcription mistakes that can affect financial records and customer service, and create a live data hub for sales analysis. Whether you're a solo entrepreneur or a growing team, this automation gives you a clean, automated foundation for order management.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on New AmeriCommerce Order

The workflow is activated by the "Watch Orders" module in Make.com, connected to your AmeriCommerce account. It monitors your store in real-time and instantly detects when a new order is created. This trigger ensures no sale is ever missed.

Step 2: Extract and Structure Order Data

Make.com retrieves the full order object from AmeriCommerce. It maps key fields like Order ID, Date, Customer Name, Email, Total Amount, Product SKUs, Quantities, Shipping Address, and Payment Status. The data is formatted and prepared for insertion into a spreadsheet row.

Pro tip: You can customize which fields are captured. Add custom AmeriCommerce fields like discounts, tax details, or notes to tailor the spreadsheet to your exact reporting needs.

Step 3: Add Row to Google Sheets

The formatted data is sent to the "Add a Row" module in Google Sheets. The workflow identifies your specific spreadsheet and worksheet, then inserts the new order as a fresh row. Each column corresponds to a specific data point, creating a clean, structured log.

The process runs silently in the background, 24/7. You can open your Google Sheets spreadsheet at any time to see a complete, chronological list of all orders, ready for analysis, sharing, or integration with other tools.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the Template: Use the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect AmeriCommerce: In the Make.com scenario, add your AmeriCommerce API credentials to the "Watch Orders" module. This establishes the trigger connection.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: Authorize Make.com to access your Google account and select the specific spreadsheet and worksheet where orders should be logged.
  4. Map the Data: Review the "Add a Row" module settings. Ensure the AmeriCommerce data fields (like `orderNumber`, `customerName`, `total`) are correctly mapped to the corresponding columns in your Sheets worksheet.
  5. Test and Activate: Create a test order in your AmeriCommerce store (or use an existing one) and run the scenario once to verify the row appears correctly in Sheets. Then turn the scenario "ON" to automate continuously.

Pro tip: Start with the provided example spreadsheet structure to ensure mapping works smoothly. Then customize your own sheet's columns as needed.
