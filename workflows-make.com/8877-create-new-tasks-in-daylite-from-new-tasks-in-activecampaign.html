What This Workflow Does

For businesses using ActiveCampaign for marketing automation and Daylite for CRM and project management, a critical gap often exists between marketing actions and sales follow-up. When a lead scores highly, completes a form, or triggers a marketing automation, a task is created in ActiveCampaign. However, if that task isn't immediately visible to the sales team in their primary workspace (Daylite), it leads to delayed responses, missed opportunities, and frustrating manual work copying information between systems.

This automation template solves that problem by creating a live, one-way sync from ActiveCampaign to Daylite. It periodically checks for new tasks in ActiveCampaign and uses the retrieved details—like title, description, due date, and associated contact—to automatically create a corresponding task in Daylite. This bridges the marketing-to-sales handoff, ensuring every lead action gets timely attention without anyone having to switch apps or manually transcribe data.

Pro tip: Use this automation not just for lead follow-ups, but also for internal marketing tasks like "Review campaign performance" or "Update email sequence," ensuring cross-departmental accountability within Daylite.

How It Works

The workflow runs on a schedule you control, acting as a reliable bridge between your two essential business platforms.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger & Fetch New Tasks

The automation is initiated by a Schedule module set to run at your chosen interval (e.g., every 15 minutes). It then connects to your ActiveCampaign account and searches for tasks created since the last check. This ensures no new actionable item is missed.

Step 2: Process and Map Task Data

For each new task found, the workflow extracts key information. This includes the task title, detailed notes, priority level, due date, and crucially, the linked contact record. The data is formatted and prepared to match the field requirements in Daylite.

Step 3: Create the Task in Daylite

The workflow then authenticates with your Daylite account and creates a new task. It populates the Daylite task fields with the mapped data from ActiveCampaign, assigning it to the appropriate user or team and linking it to the correct contact or company record in Daylite for full context.

Pro tip: Add a Router module after fetching tasks to filter out low-priority or internal marketing tasks, ensuring only sales-critical actions are pushed to Daylite and keeping your sales team focused.
