Update Contact's List Status in ActiveCampaign from Daylite

What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Daylite for CRM and ActiveCampaign for email marketing, a major operational headache is keeping contact lists synchronized. Sales teams update lead stages, close deals, or mark contacts as inactive within Daylite, but this critical information often doesn't reach the marketing team's ActiveCampaign account. This disconnect leads to prospects receiving the wrong nurture emails, customers being missed in onboarding sequences, and marketing budgets wasted on irrelevant communications.

This Make.com workflow solves that problem by creating an automatic bridge between the two systems. It monitors Daylite for specific contact updates—like a status change from "Lead" to "Customer"—and triggers an immediate, corresponding update to that contact's list membership in ActiveCampaign. The process is fully automated, requires zero manual intervention, and ensures your marketing engine is always working with the most current sales data.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to detect changes and apply updates accurately.

Step 1: Monitor Daylite for Contact Updates

The workflow starts with a Watch Contacts module in Make.com, connected to your Daylite account. It polls Daylite at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes) looking for contacts that have been modified. You can filter this to watch for updates to specific fields, such as a custom "Status" or "List" field that your sales team uses.

Step 2: Route Based on the New Status

Once a changed contact is detected, a Router module evaluates the new status value. For example, if the status is now "Customer," the workflow routes that contact's data down one path. If the status is "Unsubscribed," it routes down another. This allows you to define different actions for different business scenarios.

Pro tip: Use a simple text field in Daylite with clear, standardized values (like Lead, Opportunity, Customer, Inactive) to make routing logic clean and easy to maintain.

Step 3: Update the Contact in ActiveCampaign

Depending on the route, the appropriate Update a Contact module in ActiveCampaign is activated. This module uses the contact's email address (which should be consistent between systems) to find the correct record in ActiveCampaign. It then adds the contact to a specific list (e.g., "Customers") and/or removes them from another (e.g., "Prospects"), effectively syncing their list status.

Step 4: Log the Action for Reliability

A final step, such as adding a note to the Daylite contact or sending a notification to a Slack channel, can be included to create an audit trail. This confirms the sync was successful and provides visibility into the automated process for your team.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to configure the template for your business.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, click the Daylite and ActiveCampaign modules. Re-establish the connections by logging into your accounts and authorizing Make.com to access them.
  3. Configure the Daylite Trigger: In the Watch Contacts module, set the filter to watch for updates to the specific field you use for list status (e.g., "Contact Type" or a custom field).
  4. Map Your Statuses to Lists: Inside the Router, set up routes for each possible status value (e.g., "Customer," "Lead"). For each route, configure the subsequent ActiveCampaign module to add the contact to the correct list ID.
  5. Test with a Single Contact: Turn on the scenario and manually update a test contact's status in Daylite. Verify that the contact is moved to the correct list in ActiveCampaign within a few minutes.
  6. Activate and Monitor: Once testing is successful, let the scenario run. Monitor the scenario's history log for the first few days to ensure it's processing updates smoothly.
