What This Workflow Does

Every time a potential customer fills out your contact form, webinar registration, or quote request on Google Forms, a race begins. The faster that lead reaches your sales team, the higher your chance of closing the deal. Manual processes—where someone must notice the submission, open a spreadsheet, and copy-paste data into Salesforce—create delays of hours or even days. This workflow solves that critical bottleneck.

It acts as an intelligent bridge between your front-end lead capture (Google Forms) and your central customer database (Salesforce). The moment a form is submitted, the automation springs into action. It reads the response, structures the data, and creates a fully-fledged contact record in Salesforce—all within seconds, without any human intervention. How It Works

The automation follows a clean, reliable sequence to transform a form response into a sales-ready contact.

Step 1: Trigger on New Form Submission

The workflow is activated the instant a new response is recorded in your Google Form. Make.com watches for this event continuously, ensuring zero lag in lead capture.

Step 2: Parse and Map Form Data

The automation extracts the submitted information—like name, email address, company, and phone number—from the Google Forms response. It then maps each piece of data to the corresponding field in the Salesforce Contact object (e.g., form field "Email Address" maps to Salesforce field "Email").

Step 3: Create Contact in Salesforce

Using the mapped data, the workflow sends a secure API call to your Salesforce instance to create a new Contact record. It can handle custom fields, ensuring all the information you collect finds its proper home.

Step 4: Confirm and Log (Optional)

After the contact is created, the workflow can retrieve the new Salesforce Contact ID for confirmation. You can add modules to log this activity to a Google Sheet, send a notification to a Slack channel, or trigger a welcome email—making the process fully transparent and actionable.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to copy the template to your Make.com account.
  2. Authorize Connections: In your new scenario, click the Google Forms and Salesforce modules to log in and authorize the connections. This is a secure, one-time OAuth process.
  3. Select Your Form: In the Google Forms trigger module, select the specific form you want to monitor for new submissions.
  4. Map Your Fields: In the Salesforce "Create a Contact" module, map the data from the form response to the correct Salesforce fields (e.g., map `Respondent's Email` to `Email`).
  5. Test and Activate: Use the "Run once" feature to submit a test response to your form. Verify that a contact appears correctly in Salesforce. Then, turn the scenario scheduler to "On" to let it run automatically.

Pro tip: Start by testing with a single, simple form. Once confident, you can clone the scenario to connect multiple forms to Salesforce, or add steps for email notifications and data logging.
