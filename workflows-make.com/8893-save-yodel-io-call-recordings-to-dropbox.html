What This Workflow Does

For sales, support, and legal teams using Yodel.io, call recordings are a goldmine of information—but they become useless if they're lost, disorganized, or hard to access. Manually downloading and filing each recording is a tedious, error-prone task that wastes valuable time every week.

This automation solves that problem completely. The moment a call ends in Yodel.io, this Make.com workflow springs into action. It detects the new recording, securely retrieves the audio file, and uploads it directly to a designated folder in your Dropbox. The process is fully automatic, reliable, and runs 24/7, turning a manual chore into a seamless, behind-the-scenes operation.

The business value is immediate: you gain a secure, centralized archive of all conversations without lifting a finger. This is critical for quality assurance, compliance, training, and having an indisputable record of customer agreements.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, three-step process that connects Yodel.io and Dropbox through Make.com's visual automation platform.

Step 1: Trigger on New Recording

The scenario is triggered automatically whenever a new call recording is available in your Yodel.io account. Make.com polls Yodel.io at regular intervals, so there's no delay. The trigger captures all essential metadata, like the call timestamp, participant information, and the unique URL for the recording file.

Step 2: Download the Recording File

Make.com then uses the provided URL to download the actual audio file (typically in MP3 format) from Yodel.io's servers. This step happens securely within the Make.com platform, ensuring the file is transferred without being exposed or stored unnecessarily.

Pro tip: Ensure "call recording" is enabled in your Yodel.io settings. The automation can only work if Yodel.io is generating the recording files in the first place.

Step 3: Upload to Your Dropbox Folder

Finally, the downloaded audio file is uploaded to your connected Dropbox account. You can configure the exact destination path, such as /Call Recordings/2024/May/ or /Client Communications/[Client Name]/. The file is saved with a clear name, often including the date and caller ID for easy searching later.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the ready-made scenario to your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Yodel.io: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Yodel.io account. You'll likely need your Yodel.io API credentials.
  3. Connect Dropbox: In the upload module, authorize the connection to your Dropbox account. This is a standard OAuth process.
  4. Set Your Folder Path: Configure the Dropbox module to save files to your preferred folder (e.g., /Business/Call Recordings/).
