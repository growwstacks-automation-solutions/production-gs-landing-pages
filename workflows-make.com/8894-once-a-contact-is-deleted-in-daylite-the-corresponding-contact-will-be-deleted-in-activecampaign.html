Automatically Delete ActiveCampaign Contacts When Removed from Daylite

What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Daylite for CRM and ActiveCampaign for email marketing, maintaining a single source of truth for contact data is a constant challenge. When a sales representative decides a lead is no longer relevant and deletes them from Daylite, that contact often remains in ActiveCampaign. This leads to wasted marketing spend, potential compliance issues with data privacy laws, and a degraded sender reputation from emailing uninterested parties.

This automated workflow solves that problem instantly. It acts as a bridge between your two critical systems, ensuring that any contact deletion in Daylite triggers an immediate and corresponding deletion in ActiveCampaign. The process relies on a unique identifier—the Daylite Contact ID—stored as a custom field in ActiveCampaign to accurately match and remove the correct record. This automation enforces data hygiene automatically, saving your team from manual cleanup tasks and protecting your marketing efficiency.

How It Works

The workflow is triggered by a change in your Daylite database and executes a precise search-and-destroy operation in ActiveCampaign.

Step 1: Monitor Daylite for Deletions

The Make.com scenario watches your Daylite account using a dedicated module. The instant a contact record is deleted, the workflow captures the unique Daylite Contact ID of that removed entry. This ID is the critical piece of data needed to find the matching record in your other system.

Step 2: Find the Matching Contact in ActiveCampaign

Using the captured Daylite Contact ID, the workflow searches your ActiveCampaign account. It looks through the custom field where you store this ID (e.g., a field named "Daylite_ID"). This step ensures absolute accuracy, preventing the wrong contact from being deleted even if they have a similar name or email address.

Step 3: Delete the Contact in ActiveCampaign

Once the matching ActiveCampaign contact is found via its custom field, the workflow instructs ActiveCampaign to permanently delete that contact. This removes them from all lists, stops any scheduled automations, and cleans them from your database, completing the sync. The entire process happens in seconds, without any manual intervention.

Pro tip: Before activating this automation, ensure all your existing ActiveCampaign contacts have the Daylite Contact ID populated in a custom field. Run a one-time data sync to backfill this field for historical contacts to guarantee the workflow works for every record.
