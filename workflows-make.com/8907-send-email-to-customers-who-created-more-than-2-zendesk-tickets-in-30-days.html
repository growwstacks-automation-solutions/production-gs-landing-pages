What This Workflow Does

When customers submit multiple support tickets in a short period, it's a clear signal of frustration or a recurring problem. Manually identifying these customers and reaching out is time-consuming and often gets deprioritized. This creates a major risk of churn, as customers feel ignored.

This automated workflow solves that by continuously monitoring your Zendesk account. It scans for customers who have created more than two tickets in the last 30 days, filters out any you've already contacted, and automatically sends them a personalized, caring email. This transforms a reactive support process into a proactive customer success strategy, demonstrating that you value their business and are committed to resolving their issues.

The template runs daily, ensuring no high-need customer slips through the cracks. It’s a foundational automation that builds loyalty, provides valuable insights into product pain points, and frees your support team to focus on solving complex problems rather than administrative tracking.

How It Works

The workflow is a smart sequence that connects Zendesk data with email delivery, all running on a scheduled trigger.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger & Ticket Aggregation

Every day at a time you set (default 10:00 AM), the workflow activates. It connects to your Zendesk account and retrieves all tickets created in the past 30 days. The system then aggregates this data, grouping tickets by the requester's email address to count how many each customer has submitted.

Step 2: Identifying High-Volume Customers

Next, the workflow applies a filter. It checks the count for each customer and only passes through those who have created more than two tickets. This logic is fully customizable—you can easily change the threshold to 3 tickets or adjust the timeframe to 14 days based on your support model.

Step 3: Sending the Proactive Email

For each identified customer, the workflow triggers an email module. You can connect it to your preferred email service (like Gmail, Outlook, or SendGrid). The email template is pre-built but fully editable. A best-practice version would acknowledge their recent contacts, express that you're looking into their issues, and potentially offer a direct line to a senior support agent or a feedback call.

Pro tip: Personalize the email by including the subject lines of the customer's recent tickets. This shows you’ve done your homework and aren’t sending a bulk message, dramatically increasing engagement and trust.

Step 4: Logging & Avoiding Duplicates

To prevent spamming a customer, the workflow can be extended to log which customers have been contacted (e.g., in a Google Sheet or a dedicated app like Airtable). A simple filter at the beginning can check this log and skip anyone who has already received an email in the last 60 days, making the system respectful and intelligent.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this workflow live in under 10 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Zendesk: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Zendesk account. You'll need your subdomain and an API token.
  3. Set Your Email Service: In the email module, connect your Gmail, SendGrid, or other email service and set the "From" address.
  4. Customize the Email: Edit the email subject and body. Use placeholders like the customer's name for personalization. Keep the tone empathetic and helpful.
  5. Test & Activate: Run the scenario once in test mode to ensure it fetches tickets and sends an email correctly. Then, turn on the schedule to run daily.
