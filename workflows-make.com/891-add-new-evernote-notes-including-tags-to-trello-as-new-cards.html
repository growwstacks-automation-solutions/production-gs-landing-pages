What This Workflow Does

For knowledge workers, entrepreneurs, and project managers, the gap between capturing an idea and acting on it is where productivity leaks. You jot down brilliant thoughts, meeting notes, or project ideas in Evernote, but then they languish in digital notebooks, forgotten until it's too late. Manually transferring these notes to your task management system in Trello is tedious, error-prone, and often gets deprioritized.

This automation bridges that gap seamlessly. It creates a direct pipeline from your note-taking app (Evernote) to your action-taking app (Trello). Every time you create a new note in a designated Evernote notebook, this workflow instantly creates a corresponding card in your chosen Trello board. Even better, it preserves your organizational system by converting all Evernote tags into Trello labels, maintaining context and categorization automatically.

The result is a closed-loop system for idea execution. What you capture becomes what you do, without any manual intervention. This eliminates the "swivel-chair" work of copying and pasting, reduces cognitive load, and ensures no valuable insight gets lost in the shuffle between apps.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple but powerful trigger-action principle, orchestrated by Make.com.

Step 1: Watch for New Evernote Notes

The automation begins by monitoring a specific notebook you select in your Evernote account. Using Evernote's API via Make.com, it checks for new notes at regular intervals (or in real-time, depending on your plan). When a new note is detected, the workflow captures all its details: title, content, creation date, and crucially, all assigned tags.

Step 2: Prepare the Data for Trello

Make.com processes the incoming note data. It maps the Evernote note title to the Trello card name, and can place the note content into the card description. It then takes the array of tags from Evernote and prepares them for Trello's label system. This step ensures data compatibility between the two different platforms.

Step 3: Create the Trello Card with Labels

The workflow connects to your Trello account and creates a new card on the specified board and list (e.g., "To Do", "Inbox", or "Backlog"). It uses the prepared data to set the card's name and description. Then, for each Evernote tag, it either finds an existing matching label in Trello or creates a new one, and attaches it to the newly created card. This preserves your tagging hierarchy visually.

Pro tip: Use specific Evernote notebooks for different Trello boards. For example, a "Marketing Ideas" notebook could auto-populate your "Content Calendar" Trello board, while a "Bug Reports" notebook feeds into your "Development Sprint" board.
