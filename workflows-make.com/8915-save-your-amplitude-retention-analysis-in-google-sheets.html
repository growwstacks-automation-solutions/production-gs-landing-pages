What This Workflow Does

Product managers, data analysts, and growth marketers spend hours each week manually exporting retention reports from Amplitude, copying data, and pasting it into spreadsheets for broader team access. This process is not only time-consuming but also prone to human error and delays, leaving teams with outdated information just when they need it most.

This automated workflow solves that problem entirely. It connects your Amplitude account to Google Sheets, scheduling regular exports of your key retention analyses. Whether you track Day 1, Day 7, or Day 30 retention for specific user cohorts, this system ensures your data flows seamlessly into a centralized, shareable, and customizable spreadsheet. The result is a single source of truth for retention metrics that your entire company can access, analyze, and build upon.

Beyond saving time, this automation ensures consistency. Every report follows the same format, arrives on the same schedule, and lands in the same predefined location. This reliability transforms retention analysis from a reactive, manual task into a proactive, strategic asset for your business.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com, a powerful visual automation platform. It acts as a secure bridge between Amplitude's API and your Google Sheets, handling authentication, data formatting, and scheduling automatically.

Step 1: Schedule the Trigger

The automation begins on a schedule you set—daily, weekly, or monthly. Make.com initiates the workflow at your chosen interval, ensuring your data is always fresh without you lifting a finger.

Step 2: Fetch Data from Amplitude

Make.com connects to your Amplitude account via a secure API connection. It requests the specific retention analysis report you've configured, pulling in the latest data for your defined cohorts and time periods.

Step 3: Transform and Structure the Data

The raw data from Amplitude is processed within the workflow. It can be filtered, sorted, or reformatted to match your preferred structure in Google Sheets, such as pivoting rows and columns or adding calculation fields.

Step 4: Update Google Sheets

Finally, the processed data is sent to your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. The workflow can append new rows to a historical log, update a specific dashboard tab, or overwrite a previous report, keeping everything organized and up-to-date.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated "Retention Log" sheet to append all historical data. This creates a time-series dataset perfect for creating trend charts and performing longitudinal analysis directly within Google Sheets.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting started with this template is straightforward. Follow these steps to configure it for your business.

  1. Duplicate the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your own Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, establish connections (called "connections") for both Amplitude and Google Sheets. This is a one-time OAuth process.
  3. Configure the Amplitude Module: Paste your Amplitude API key and select the specific retention analysis report you want to pull data from.
  4. Set the Schedule: Define how often the workflow should run (e.g., "Every Monday at 9 AM").
  5. Map to Google Sheets: Select your target spreadsheet and worksheet, and map the data fields from Amplitude to the correct columns.
  6. Test & Activate: Run a single test cycle to ensure data flows correctly into your sheet. Once verified, turn the scenario "ON" to start automated execution.
