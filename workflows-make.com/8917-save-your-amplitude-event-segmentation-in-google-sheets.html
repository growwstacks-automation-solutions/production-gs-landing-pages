What This Workflow Does

Product and marketing teams rely on Amplitude to understand how users interact with their digital products. However, valuable segmentation data often gets trapped inside Amplitude, making it difficult to share with stakeholders, combine with other business metrics, or use for long-term trend analysis. Manually exporting CSV files is a repetitive, time-consuming task that leads to outdated reports and data silos.

This automated workflow solves that problem by connecting Amplitude directly to Google Sheets. It watches for your defined event segmentation—such as users who performed a specific action within a date range—and automatically sends that data to a specified spreadsheet at regular intervals. This creates a live, centralized data hub that your entire team can access, enabling better collaboration, more sophisticated analysis, and faster, data-driven decisions without any manual intervention.

How It Works

The workflow acts as a reliable bridge between your analytics platform and your collaborative reporting tool. Once set up, it runs silently in the background, ensuring your Sheets are always populated with the latest user insights.

Step 1: Schedule the Data Check

The scenario is triggered on a schedule you define—daily, weekly, or monthly. This timer module in Make.com initiates the process, ensuring your data exports happen consistently without you having to remember.

Step 2: Fetch Segmentation from Amplitude

Make.com then calls the Amplitude API using your credentials. It executes a pre-configured query to retrieve the specific event segmentation data you need, such as "Users who completed onboarding in the last 7 days" or "Users who made a purchase after viewing a feature."

Step 3: Transform and Structure the Data

The raw data from Amplitude is processed and mapped into a clean, tabular format. The workflow handles parsing JSON responses, extracting relevant user properties, and organizing the data into rows and columns suitable for a spreadsheet.

Step 4: Append to Google Sheets

Finally, the structured data is sent to your designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. It can append new rows to an existing sheet, create a new tab for each run, or update a specific range. This keeps a historical record and provides an always-up-to-date dataset for your team.

Pro tip: Use a new sheet tab for each weekly export. This preserves historical snapshots, making it easy to compare week-over-week or month-over-month trends directly within Google Sheets.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes. Follow these steps after cloning the template.

  1. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, authorize the Amplitude and Google Sheets modules using your account credentials.
  2. Configure the Amplitude Query: Input the exact segmentation query from your Amplitude account. Define the events, user properties, and date range you want to capture.
  3. Set the Schedule: Choose how often you want the data to sync. A daily schedule is recommended for most active teams.
  4. Map Your Google Sheet: Specify the exact Google Sheets file ID and worksheet name where the data should be sent. Define the starting cell for appending new rows.
  5. Test and Activate: Run the scenario once manually to verify data flows correctly from Amplitude into your Sheet. Then turn on the scheduler to let it run automatically.
