What This Workflow Does

For businesses that schedule customer meetings through Calendly, a major operational gap exists between the scheduling moment and the support or sales follow-up. Information about the upcoming call often lives in silos—stuck in the scheduler's calendar or email—while the support team remains unaware, unable to prepare or track the customer's journey. This leads to fragmented customer experiences, manual work to create tracking tickets, and missed opportunities for personalized service.

This automation bridges that gap seamlessly. Every time a new event is booked in your Calendly, a corresponding support ticket is automatically created in Zendesk with all the relevant details: who the customer is, what they booked, and when. If the customer cancels, the ticket is automatically closed to keep your queue clean. Furthermore, a notification is sent to a designated Microsoft Teams channel, ensuring your entire team has real-time visibility into new bookings and changes. This creates a unified system where customer interactions are automatically tracked from the first click to the final follow-up.

How It Works

The workflow acts as a smart connector between your scheduling tool and your support hub, triggered by any change in your Calendly calendar.

Step 1: Trigger on Calendly Event Change

The scenario starts by monitoring your Calendly account for new, updated, or canceled events. Using Calendly's webhooks (available on premium plans), it captures the event details the moment a booking is made or changed.

Step 2: Route Based on Event Status

A router module checks the event's status. If the status is "active" (scheduled), the flow proceeds to create a ticket. If the status is "canceled," the flow takes the path to find and close the corresponding existing ticket.

Step 3: Create or Update Zendesk Tickets

For new events, the workflow uses the Zendesk module to create a new ticket. It maps Calendly data—like the invitee's name, email, event name, and scheduled time—into the ticket's subject, description, and requester fields. For canceled events, it searches Zendesk for a ticket matching the event details and updates its status to "closed."

Step 4: Notify the Team on Microsoft Teams

Regardless of the action (create or close), the workflow sends a formatted message to a specified Microsoft Teams channel via an incoming webhook. This alert keeps sales, support, and management aligned on customer activity without anyone needing to check multiple systems.

Pro tip: Use custom Calendly questions to gather crucial info before the meeting (like company size or pain points) and map those answers directly into a private Zendesk comment. This gives your team powerful context before the call even starts.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to configure your own instance of this workflow after cloning the template.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, establish connections (called "modules") for Calendly, Zendesk, and Microsoft Teams using your respective API keys and login credentials.
  3. Configure the Calendly Webhook: Point the first module to your specific Calendly event type(s) you want to monitor (e.g., "15-Minute Consultation," "Product Demo").
  4. Map the Data to Zendesk: In the Zendesk "Create Ticket" module, map the fields from Calendly (like invitee.name, event.name) to the corresponding Zendesk ticket fields (Subject, Description, Requester Email).
  5. Set Up Teams Notification: Create an incoming webhook in your desired Teams channel and paste the URL into the Microsoft Teams module in Make.com. Customize the notification message.
  6. Test and Activate: Run a test by scheduling and then canceling a Calendly event yourself. Verify that a ticket is created/closed in Zendesk and that the notification appears in Teams. Once confirmed, activate the scenario to run automatically.
