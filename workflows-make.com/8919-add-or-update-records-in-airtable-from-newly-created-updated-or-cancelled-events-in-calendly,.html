What This Workflow Does

If you use Calendly for scheduling, you know the frustration of manually transferring appointment details into your CRM, project tracker, or client database. This disjointed process wastes time, introduces errors, and delays your team's response. When a client cancels, that outdated record lingers, causing confusion and missed follow-ups.

This automation solves that by creating a live, two-way bridge between Calendly and Airtable. Every time a new event is scheduled, a corresponding record is instantly created in your Airtable base. If the event is rescheduled or cancelled, the existing Airtable record is updated automatically to reflect the new status. The workflow also sends a Slack notification to your team channel, ensuring everyone is on the same page in real-time.

The result is a perfectly synchronized system where your schedule and your client data are always aligned. You gain a searchable, reportable history of all interactions without lifting a finger, turning your calendar into a powerful business intelligence tool.

How It Works

The workflow runs on Make.com, listening for changes in your Calendly account and orchestrating updates across Airtable and Slack.

Step 1: Trigger on Calendly Event Change

The scenario is triggered by the "Watch Events" module in Calendly. It monitors for newly scheduled, rescheduled, or cancelled events. The moment a change occurs, the event data (like invitee name, email, event URL, and status) is captured and sent into the workflow.

Pro tip: You need a Calendly Premium account to access the "Watch Events" trigger, which is essential for this real-time automation.

Step 2: Route Based on Event Status

The workflow uses a router to determine what type of event occurred. It checks if the event is "new," "updated," or "cancelled." This logic ensures the correct action is taken in Airtable for each scenario.

Step 3: Create or Update the Airtable Record

For a new event, the workflow creates a new record in your specified Airtable base and table. It maps the Calendly data to columns like "Attendee Name," "Attendee Email," "Event URL," and "Status." For an updated or cancelled event, it searches for the existing record (typically using the unique Event URL as a key) and updates the relevant fields, such as changing the status to "Cancelled."

Step 4: Notify the Team on Slack

Finally, a message is sent to a designated Slack channel. The notification includes key details like the client's name, the event type, and its status. This gives your sales, support, or operations team immediate visibility, allowing them to act promptly.

What You'll Need

  1. A Calendly Premium account (required for the "Watch Events" trigger).
  2. An Airtable account with a base and table ready to receive data. Your table should have columns for at least: Event URL, Status, Attendee Name, and Attendee Email.
  3. A Slack workspace and a channel where you want to receive notifications.
  4. A Make.com account to host and run the automation scenario.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation live in minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Calendly: In the first module, connect your Calendly account and ensure the "Watch Events" trigger is active.
  3. Configure Airtable: In the "Create a Record" and "Update a Record" modules, connect your Airtable account and select the correct base and table. Map the incoming Calendly fields to your Airtable columns.
  4. Set Up Slack: Connect your Slack account in the notification module and choose the channel for alerts. Customize the message format if desired.
  5. Test & Activate: Schedule a test meeting in Calendly to trigger the workflow. Verify that a record appears in Airtable and a notification arrives in Slack. Once confirmed, turn the scenario on.
