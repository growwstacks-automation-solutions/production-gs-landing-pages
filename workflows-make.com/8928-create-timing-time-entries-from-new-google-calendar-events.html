What This Workflow Does

For freelancers, consultants, and agency teams, time is literally money. Yet, manually tracking every meeting, client call, and project session is a tedious, error-prone task that often gets forgotten. This leads to lost billable hours, inaccurate project costing, and frustration. This automation solves that by bridging your schedule with your time sheet.

The workflow watches your Google Calendar for new events. Whenever a meeting or blocked work time is added, it instantly creates a corresponding time entry in Timing.app. The entry includes the event title, start and end times, and any notes you’ve added, creating a perfect audit trail of your workday without you lifting a finger.

The business value is clear: accurate, automatic time capture translates directly into precise client invoices, reliable profitability data, and 5-10 hours saved each month on administrative chores. You focus on the work itself, while the system handles the tracking.

How It Works

The automation runs on a simple, reliable trigger-action principle. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Monitoring Your Calendar

The Make.com scenario is set to check your connected Google Calendar at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes). It looks specifically for events that have started since the last check. This ensures no meeting slips through the cracks.

Step 2: Capturing Event Details

When a new event is detected, the workflow extracts key data: the event summary (title), start and end datetime, description, and calendar name. This data forms the basis of your time entry.

Pro tip: Use consistent naming in your calendar events (e.g., "Client: Project X - Strategy Call") to make the auto-generated time entries in Timing immediately understandable and easy to categorize later.

Step 3: Creating the Time Entry

The workflow then uses Timing's API to create a new time entry. It maps the calendar event details directly: the event title becomes the time entry name, the duration is calculated automatically, and the description can be passed as notes. The entry is logged in Timing, ready for review, reporting, or invoicing.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the ready-made scenario into your Make.com workspace.
  2. Connect Your Apps: Authorize Make.com to access your Google Calendar and your Timing account via their respective modules.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set the Google Calendar module to watch the specific calendar you use for work (e.g., "Primary" or "Work").
  4. Map the Data: Review how the event fields (title, start time, end time) map to the Timing time entry fields. Adjust if needed.
  5. Activate & Test: Turn the scenario on and create a test event in your calendar. Check Timing within a few minutes to see the automated entry appear.
