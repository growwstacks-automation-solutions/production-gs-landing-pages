How It Works

The workflow connects three core platforms: Google Sheets for lead intake, Airtable for structured storage and pipeline management, and Slack for instant team communication. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: New Lead Detection

The scenario monitors a specific Google Sheet for new rows. When a form submission, manual entry, or imported CSV adds a row, Make.com captures the lead’s details—name, email, company, source, and any custom fields you’ve set up. This acts as the central hub where all lead sources converge before qualification.

Step 2: Lead Scoring & Categorization

Each lead is evaluated against your predefined scoring rules. Points are assigned based on criteria like budget confirmed (+10), timeline within 30 days (+8), decision‑maker identified (+6), or company size match (+5). The total score determines the lead’s category: “Hot” (ready to buy), “Warm” (needs nurturing), or “Cold” (not a fit).

Step 3: Airtable Record Creation

A new record is created in your Airtable base with all captured fields plus the calculated score and category. The record is automatically placed in the appropriate view—for example, “Hot Leads” board for immediate follow‑up, “Nurturing” for education, or “Archive” for future reference.

Step 4: Slack Notification

A formatted message is posted to your designated Slack channel (or via direct mention). It includes the lead’s name, company, score, category, and a direct link to the Airtable record. High‑priority leads can trigger @here or @channel mentions to ensure rapid response.

Pro tip: Set up different Slack channels for different lead types—like #hot‑leads‑urgent for immediate action and #inbound‑leads for general inquiries. This helps your team instantly see which leads need attention first.
