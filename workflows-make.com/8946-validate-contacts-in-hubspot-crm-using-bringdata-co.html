Validate Contacts in HubSpot CRM Using BringData.co

Automatically verify new contact emails and deactivate invalid records to maintain a pristine, high-performing CRM database.

What This Workflow Does

A dirty CRM is a silent profit killer. Invalid email addresses from typos, fake sign-ups, or defunct accounts pollute your HubSpot database, leading to bounced campaigns, wasted marketing budget, and sales teams chasing ghosts. This automated workflow solves that problem at the source.

Whenever a new contact is created in your HubSpot CRM—whether from a form submission, manual entry, or integration—this Make.com scenario instantly triggers. It sends the contact's email address to BringData.co for real-time validation. If the email is confirmed as invalid (non-existent domain, mailbox not found, etc.), the workflow automatically deactivates that contact in HubSpot. This keeps your active lists clean, protects your sender reputation, and ensures your team only engages with genuine, reachable leads.

How It Works

The automation acts as a smart gatekeeper for your CRM, following a precise sequence to filter out bad data.

Step 1: New Contact Detection

The workflow is triggered the moment a new contact record is created in your connected HubSpot account. Make.com watches for this event in real-time, capturing all the contact's details, with the email address being the key data point for the next step.

Step 2: Email Validation with BringData.co

The captured email address is instantly sent to the BringData.co module. BringData.co performs a comprehensive check, verifying syntax, domain existence, and mailbox availability. It returns a clear result: valid or invalid.

Pro tip: You can configure the validation to check for specific issues like disposable email addresses or role-based accounts (e.g., info@, support@) which often have lower engagement rates.

Step 3: Conditional Action in HubSpot

The workflow now branches based on the validation result. If the email is valid, the contact remains untouched and proceeds into your normal marketing/sales funnel. If the email is invalid, the automation executes a crucial action: it updates the contact's status in HubSpot to "deactivated" or moves it to a specific "Invalid Email" list, effectively quarantining it from your active campaigns.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this protection layer active in under 30 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com editor, click on the HubSpot module and authenticate your account. Repeat the process for the BringData.co module, entering your API key.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Ensure the HubSpot trigger is set to watch for "Contact Creation" events.
  4. Set the Deactivation Action: In the HubSpot update module (the branch for invalid emails), specify how you want to mark the contact. Common options are setting a custom property like "Email Status" to "Invalid" or changing the contact's lifecycle stage.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and create a test contact in HubSpot with a known invalid email (like test@nonexistent12345.com) to see the automation in action. Once confirmed, your CRM is now self-cleaning.
