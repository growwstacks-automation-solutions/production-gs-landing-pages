What This Workflow Does

Every business that generates leads faces the same challenge: how to quickly separate the serious buyers from the casual browsers. Without a system, sales teams waste hours manually reviewing form submissions, trying to guess which leads are ready to talk, and often letting high-potential prospects slip through the cracks due to slow follow-up. This inefficiency directly impacts revenue—leads go cold, opportunities are missed, and sales cycles stretch longer than necessary.

This Automated Lead Qualification & Follow-Up Workflow solves this by creating a seamless, rules-based pipeline. It instantly evaluates new leads from your website, landing pages, or ads, assigns a score based on their actions and profile, and automatically triggers the right follow-up sequence. Hot leads get immediate, personal outreach, while promising leads enter a nurturing journey, all without a single manual step. It transforms lead capture from a reactive chore into a proactive, always-on conversion engine.

How It Works

Step 1: Lead Capture & Initial Data Collection

When a visitor fills out a form on your website, the workflow is triggered. It captures all submitted data—name, email, company, phone, and any custom fields—and immediately sends this information to your CRM. This ensures no lead is ever lost in an email inbox or forgotten spreadsheet.

Step 2: Behavioural Scoring & Segmentation

As the lead interacts with your content (views pricing page, downloads a case study, attends a webinar), the automation adds or subtracts points. A lead who requests a demo gets a high score; someone who only signs up for a newsletter starts lower. This dynamic scoring happens in real-time, automatically updating the lead's profile in your CRM.

Step 3: Intelligent Routing & Personalised Follow-Up

Based on the final score and source, the workflow routes the lead to the appropriate sales rep or marketing sequence. It can send a personalised welcome email, schedule a call in a rep's calendar, or add the lead to a targeted drip campaign—all tailored to their specific interests and engagement level.

Effective B2B lead scoring combines explicit data (job title, company size, industry) with implicit behaviour (website visits, content downloads, email engagement). Score points for actions that indicate buying intent, like viewing pricing pages, attending a demo, or requesting a quote. Deduct points for negative signals, such as unsubscribing or marking emails as spam.

For instance, a Director of Marketing at a 500-employee company who watches a product tour video is far more valuable than a generic contact form submission. Adjust weights based on your ideal customer profile and historical conversion data to ensure your scoring model reflects real-world buying patterns.

  • Explicit: Role, company size, budget authority
  • Implicit: Page views, content downloads, webinar attendance
  • Negative: Unsubscribes, spam complaints, inactivity
