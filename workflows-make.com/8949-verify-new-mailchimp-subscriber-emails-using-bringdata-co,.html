What This Workflow Does

Every marketer knows the frustration of a growing email list that doesn't convert. Often, the problem isn't your content—it's your data. Invalid, mistyped, or disposable email addresses silently poison your marketing efforts, leading to high bounce rates, damaged sender reputations, and wasted ad spend. This automated workflow solves that problem at the source.

When a new subscriber joins your Mailchimp audience, this Make.com scenario instantly triggers. It sends the new email address to BringData.co, a powerful email validation service, for real-time verification. The system checks for syntax errors, disposable domains, and non-existent mailboxes. If the email is deemed invalid, the workflow automatically unsubscribes that address from your Mailchimp list before it can harm your metrics. This ensures only genuine, reachable contacts remain, protecting your investment and maximizing campaign effectiveness.

How It Works

The automation acts as a vigilant gatekeeper for your email list, operating seamlessly in the background. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: A New Subscriber is Added

The workflow is triggered the moment a new contact is added to a specified Mailchimp audience. Make.com captures the subscriber's details, primarily their email address.

Step 2: Real-Time Validation Check

The captured email address is immediately sent to the BringData.co module within the scenario. BringData.co performs a comprehensive check, analyzing the email's format, domain validity, and mailbox existence.

Step 3: Decision & Action

Based on the validation result, the workflow branches. If the email is valid, the process ends gracefully—your list gets a quality subscriber. If the email is invalid, the workflow proceeds to the final, crucial step.

Step 4: Automatic List Cleanup

The scenario uses Mailchimp's API to automatically unsubscribe the invalid email address from the same audience it was added to. This happens within seconds, ensuring your list hygiene is maintained in real-time without any manual intervention.

Pro tip: Configure this workflow to run on a schedule (e.g., daily) to also clean up invalid emails that may have slipped through from other sources, like imported CSV lists or integrated forms.

What You'll Need

To implement this workflow, you'll need the following accounts and access:

  1. A Make.com account (free tier available). This is the automation platform that orchestrates the entire process.
  2. A Mailchimp account with an existing audience/list. You'll need your Mailchimp API key to connect Make.com to your account.
  3. A BringData.co account and API key. BringData.co offers various plans based on the volume of email validations you need.
  4. Basic understanding of where to find API keys in your Mailchimp and BringData.co accounts (usually found in account settings or developer sections).

Quick Setup Guide

Getting this protective system live takes about 15 minutes. Follow these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In your new scenario, click on the Mailchimp module. Create a connection using your Mailchimp API key. Repeat this process for the BringData.co module with its API key.
  3. Configure the Trigger: In the first Mailchimp module, select the specific audience (list) you want to monitor for new subscribers.
  4. Set Up the Router: The scenario uses a router to handle valid vs. invalid outcomes. Ensure the "Invalid Email" branch correctly points to the "Unsubscribe subscriber" Mailchimp module.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario's scheduling (set it to "Immediately" or a short interval). Perform a test by adding a known invalid email (like a typo) to your chosen Mailchimp list and watch the automation clean it up.
