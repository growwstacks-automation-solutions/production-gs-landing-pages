What This Workflow Does

Every business collecting leads online faces a common problem: dirty data. Up to 20% of email addresses collected via forms can be invalid, mistyped, or from disposable services. Adding these to your marketing list hurts your sender reputation, increases bounce rates, and wastes your marketing budget on emails that never reach a real person.

This automated workflow solves that by acting as a quality gatekeeper for your contact list. It connects your Google Forms—used for lead magnets, event sign-ups, or contact requests—directly to MailerCheck for instant email verification. Only emails that pass verification are then created as new, clean contacts in your BigMailer account, ready for nurturing campaigns.

The process eliminates manual checking and data entry, ensuring your marketing team spends time engaging with real prospects, not cleaning spreadsheets. It turns a raw lead capture process into a reliable, scalable system for list growth.

How It Works

The automation runs silently in the background, triggered by each new form submission. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Capture the Lead

A visitor fills out your Google Form, providing their name, email, and other details. The moment they hit "submit," the workflow is triggered. Make.com instantly receives the submission data, including the all-important email address.

Step 2: Verify the Email

The workflow sends the submitted email address to MailerCheck, a dedicated email verification service. MailerCheck performs a series of checks on the email's syntax, domain, and mailbox to determine if it's valid, risky, or invalid. It returns a clear status and risk score.

Step 3: Make a Decision

Based on MailerCheck's response, the workflow makes an automated decision. If the email status is "valid" and the risk is low, the process continues. If the email is marked as "invalid," "disposable," or "high-risk," the workflow can stop, log the entry, or send an alert—keeping your main list clean.

Pro tip: You can extend this workflow to send an automatic reply to users who provide an invalid email, asking them to correct it. This can recover up to 15% of otherwise lost leads.

Step 4: Create the Contact

For valid emails, the workflow takes all the form data (name, company, etc.) and uses it to create a new contact in your BigMailer account. The contact is instantly added to a designated list or tagged appropriately, making them immediately available for your email sequences, newsletters, or sales outreach.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this workflow live in under 30 minutes. Follow these steps after clicking "Get This Workflow":

  1. Clone the Template: In Make.com, use the provided link to create a copy of the template in your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: Authorize the connections to your specific Google account (where the form lives), your MailerCheck account, and your BigMailer account.
  3. Map the Data: Point the Google Forms module to your specific form. Then, map the form fields (like 'Email', 'First Name') to the corresponding fields in the MailerCheck and BigMailer modules.
  4. Set Your Filter: Configure the router or filter module to only proceed to BigMailer if MailerCheck returns a "valid" status. You can adjust the risk score threshold based on your preference.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and submit a test entry to your Google Form. Check that a valid test email becomes a contact in BigMailer and an invalid one is handled appropriately.
