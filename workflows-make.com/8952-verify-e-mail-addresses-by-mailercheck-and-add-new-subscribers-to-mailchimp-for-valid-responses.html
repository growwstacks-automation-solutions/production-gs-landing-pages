What This Workflow Does

Every invalid email address on your Mailchimp list is a direct cost to your marketing efforts and a risk to your sender reputation. Bounce rates climb, engagement drops, and your carefully crafted campaigns land in spam folders. This problem often starts at the point of capture—online forms where typos, fake addresses, and spam signups slip through.

This automation solves that by placing a guardrail between your Google Forms and Mailchimp. It acts as an intelligent filter. When someone submits a form, their email address is instantly sent to MailerCheck for verification. The system checks for validity, mailbox existence, and risk factors. Only emails that pass this check are added as subscribers to your designated Mailchimp list. Invalid entries are filtered out, keeping your list clean from the very first touchpoint.

The result is a powerful, self-cleaning lead capture system. You build an audience of genuinely interested, reachable contacts, which translates to higher open rates, better deliverability, and a stronger return on your email marketing investment—all without any manual intervention from you or your team.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward three-step process that runs silently in the background on Make.com.

Step 1: Capture the Submission

The automation is triggered the moment a new response is submitted to your connected Google Form. Make.com watches for this event and immediately captures all the form data, with a primary focus on extracting the submitted email address field.

Step 2: Verify with MailerCheck

The captured email is sent to the MailerCheck API. MailerCheck performs a real-time validation, checking syntax, domain, and mailbox existence. It returns a detailed result, typically classifying the email as "valid", "invalid", "risky", or "unknown".

Pro tip: You can configure the workflow to only proceed with emails marked "valid". For "risky" addresses (like those from disposable email services), you can route them to a separate review process or a different list.

Step 3: Conditionally Add to Mailchimp

Make.com uses a router to check MailerCheck's result. If the email is valid, the workflow proceeds to add the contact to your specified Mailchimp audience. You can map other form fields (like name, company, etc.) to corresponding Mailchimp merge tags at this stage. If the email is invalid, the workflow stops or can be set to log the attempt elsewhere, ensuring no bad data enters your primary list.

MailerCheck performs real-time checks on syntax, domain validity, and mailbox existence. It looks for common typos, checks if the mail server accepts messages, and identifies disposable or role-based addresses. It returns a risk score and status (e.g., valid, invalid, risky) that the automation uses to make decisions.

This process involves querying DNS records (MX records) and sometimes performing a non-intrusive "ping" to the mail server. It's a sophisticated process that happens in milliseconds, providing a reliable assessment without ever sending an actual email to the address during verification.
