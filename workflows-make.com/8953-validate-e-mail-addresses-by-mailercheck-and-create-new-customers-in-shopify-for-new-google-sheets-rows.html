What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses, a clean customer email list is marketing gold. Yet, most companies waste hours manually checking spreadsheets for typos, fake addresses, and invalid domains before importing contacts into Shopify. This manual process is error-prone, scales poorly, and delays your ability to market to new leads.

This automation solves that problem completely. Every time a new row is added to your Google Sheet—whether from a form, event, or manual entry—the workflow automatically validates the email address using MailerCheck's professional verification service. Only emails that pass validation are created as new customers in your Shopify store. Invalid addresses are flagged for review, keeping your customer database pristine and your marketing deliverability high.

Beyond just saving time, this system ensures data quality from the moment a lead enters your system. You eliminate bounce rates from bad emails, improve your sender reputation with email providers, and create accurate customer segments for targeted campaigns.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical three-step process that mirrors how you'd manually clean data, but does it instantly and without mistakes.

Step 1: Detect New Google Sheets Rows

The automation monitors your specified Google Sheet for new rows. This could be a sign-up sheet from an event, a webinar registration list, or a CSV import. Each new row triggers the workflow, capturing the customer's name, email, and any other relevant data you've collected.

Pro tip: Structure your Google Sheet with clear column headers (Name, Email, etc.) to make data mapping in Make.com straightforward. You can add custom fields like "Phone" or "Lead Source" that will flow through to Shopify customer notes.

Step 2: Validate Email with MailerCheck

The email address from the new row is sent to MailerCheck's API for comprehensive validation. MailerCheck checks syntax, verifies the domain exists and accepts mail, pings the mailbox, and screens for disposable/temporary email services. It returns a clear validation status and a quality score.

Step 3: Create Verified Customer in Shopify

If the email passes validation, the workflow immediately creates a new customer in your Shopify store with all the associated data. The customer is tagged with their source (e.g., "Google Sheets Import") and the validation status. If the email fails validation, the workflow can route that data to a separate sheet for follow-up, send you a notification, or simply skip the Shopify creation—you choose the handling logic.
