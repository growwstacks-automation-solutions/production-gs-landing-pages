Stripe Email Verification & Welcome Automation

Automatically verify new customer emails and send personalized welcome messages—right after a Stripe transaction.

What This Workflow Does

When a new customer completes a payment in Stripe, their email address is often the primary point of contact. However, not all collected emails are valid or deliverable. Invalid emails lead to bounced messages, wasted communication efforts, and a damaged sender reputation. This automation solves that problem by instantly verifying the email's validity using MailerCheck and, if successful, sending a tailored welcome email via MailerSend.

The business value is clear: you ensure that your onboarding or follow-up communication actually reaches the customer. This improves the customer's first impression, provides immediate post-purchase guidance, and protects your email marketing channels from high bounce rates. Instead of manually checking lists and sending emails, this workflow turns a multi-step process into a single, reliable automated sequence.

For businesses that rely on Stripe for payments, this automation bridges the gap between transaction completion and customer engagement. It's particularly valuable for e-commerce, SaaS, and subscription services where timely welcome emails are crucial for retention and satisfaction.

How It Works

Step 1: Stripe Triggers the Workflow

Whenever a new customer record is created in Stripe, the automation receives the customer's details, including their email address. This trigger happens in real-time, ensuring the process starts immediately after the transaction.

Step 2: MailerCheck Validates the Email

The workflow sends the customer's email to MailerCheck, a service that checks its deliverability. MailerCheck evaluates whether the email address is valid, exists, and can receive messages. This step filters out typos, fake addresses, or inactive accounts.

Step 3: Conditional Logic Based on Verification

The automation then decides based on MailerCheck's result. If the email is marked as valid, the workflow proceeds to the next step. If it's invalid, you can configure the automation to log the issue, notify your team, or attempt an alternative action.

Step 4: MailerSend Sends the Welcome Email

For valid emails, the workflow composes and sends a welcome email via MailerSend. You can personalize this email using data from the Stripe customer object, such as their name, purchase details, or account information. The email is dispatched automatically, completing the onboarding loop.

Pro tip: Use the verification result to segment your customer list. Valid emails go into your main onboarding sequence; invalid ones trigger a data cleanup process, perhaps sending a follow-up request for correction via another channel.

What You'll Need

  2. A MailerCheck account or API key for email verification.

  4. A Make.com account to host and run the automation.
  5. A welcome email template designed in MailerSend.

Quick Setup Guide

1. In Make.com, create a new scenario and select the Stripe module as your trigger. Choose "New Customer" as the triggering event.

2. Add a MailerCheck module after the trigger. Configure it to receive the email address from the Stripe customer object and perform a validation check.

3. Set up a router or filter module to handle the verification result. Route valid emails to the next step; handle invalid emails as needed (e.g., send to a different path).

4. Add a MailerSend module for the valid email path. Connect your MailerSend account, design your welcome email using dynamic data from Stripe (customer name, etc.), and set it to send automatically.

5. Test the workflow by creating a test customer in Stripe and observing the entire sequence. Deploy the scenario once testing is successful.
