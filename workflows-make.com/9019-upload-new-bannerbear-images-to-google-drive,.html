What This Workflow Does

For marketing teams, designers, and content creators, Bannerbear is a powerful tool for generating on-brand graphics, social media images, and personalized visuals at scale. However, the process of manually downloading each created image and then uploading it to a storage platform like Google Drive is a repetitive, time-consuming task that interrupts creative workflows and risks asset loss.

This automation solves that problem entirely. It creates a seamless bridge between Bannerbear and Google Drive. Every time a new image is generated in Bannerbear—whether it's a single graphic or part of a batch—this workflow automatically detects it, retrieves the file, and uploads it directly to a specified folder in your Google Drive. This eliminates the download-upload middle step, ensuring your growing library of visuals is always backed up, organized, and instantly available to your team.

Pro tip: Use this automation as the foundation of a centralized "brand asset hub." All auto-saved graphics become searchable in Drive, making it easy for anyone on your team to find and reuse approved marketing materials.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple trigger-action principle, running reliably in the background on Make.com.

Step 1: Trigger on New Bannerbear Image

The scenario starts by watching Bannerbear for any new "image created" events. This could be triggered by an API call from your app, a scheduled template run, or a manual creation within Bannerbear's interface. Make.com captures the event data, including the unique image URL and its metadata.

Step 2: Fetch the Image File

Once triggered, the workflow uses the image URL provided by Bannerbear to download the actual image file (PNG, JPG, etc.) into the automation. This step happens in milliseconds, securing the asset before any link expiration.

Step 3: Upload to Your Google Drive Folder

The downloaded file is then sent to the Google Drive module. You configure this step to point to your specific team or project folder. The workflow can use dynamic naming, so files are saved with clear names like "Social-Post-2025-04-10.png" or based on the Bannerbear template used, keeping everything organized from the start.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above. This will open the template in your Make.com account.
  2. Create a Copy: In Make.com, click "Copy scenario" to add it to your own workspace.
  3. Connect Your Apps: In the scenario editor, click on the Bannerbear module and authenticate with your API key. Then, click on the Google Drive module and sign in with your Google account.
  4. Set Your Destination: In the Google Drive module, select the specific folder where you want all new images to be saved. You can also customize the file naming convention here.
  5. Turn It On: Save your scenario and toggle the scheduler to "ON." The automation is now live and will process every new Bannerbear image from this moment forward.

Pro tip: Test the workflow immediately by generating a simple image in Bannerbear. Watch the logs in Make.com to see it trigger, process, and successfully save the file to Drive. This confirms everything is connected correctly.
