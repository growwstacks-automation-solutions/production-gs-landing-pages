What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses, understanding which marketing efforts actually drive sales is a constant challenge. You might see clicks and leads in WhatConverts, but connecting those leads to shipped orders in ShipStation often involves manual spreadsheets or guesswork. This disconnect makes it impossible to accurately calculate your return on ad spend (ROAS) or identify your most profitable customer acquisition channels.

This Make.com automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between fulfillment and attribution. Every time a new order is created or shipped in your ShipStation account, the workflow automatically captures the customer details and order information, then creates or updates a corresponding lead in your WhatConverts account. This turns every order into a data point that tells you exactly which ad, keyword, or campaign was responsible for the sale.

The result is a closed-loop marketing system. No more manual data entry, no more attribution gaps. You gain a crystal-clear view of how your marketing dollars translate into revenue, enabling smarter budget allocation and higher profitability.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple trigger-action principle, orchestrating data between ShipStation and WhatConverts without any manual intervention.

Step 1: Monitoring New ShipStation Orders

The automation starts by watching your ShipStation account for new events. You can configure it to trigger on "Order Created" or "Order Shipped," depending on when you want the lead to be logged in WhatConverts. The instant ShipStation registers the new order, Make.com captures the entire order payload.

Step 2: Extracting and Structuring Customer Data

Make.com then parses the incoming data. It pulls out crucial information like the customer's name, email address, shipping address, order total, items purchased, and any custom fields or notes. This data is cleaned and formatted to match the expected input for the WhatConverts API.

Pro tip: Use this step to filter orders. For instance, you might only want to create leads for orders above a certain value, or exclude test orders from specific marketplaces.

Step 3: Creating or Updating the Lead in WhatConverts

Finally, the workflow sends the structured customer and order data to WhatConverts via its API. It creates a new lead entry, tagging it with the source campaign and associating the order value. If a lead for that customer already exists (based on email), the workflow can be set to update the existing record with the new purchase data, building a complete customer history.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect ShipStation: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your ShipStation account using your API keys.
  3. Connect WhatConverts: In the final module, authorize the connection to WhatConverts using your API token from the WhatConverts settings.
