What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses, understanding which marketing efforts actually drive sales is the holy grail. Many companies use tools like WhatConverts to track phone calls and form submissions, but they hit a wall when a visitor simply buys a product online. That sale disappears into a spreadsheet, disconnected from the marketing source that initiated the customer journey. This gap in data makes it impossible to accurately calculate your return on ad spend (ROAS) or know which campaigns are truly profitable.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as a seamless bridge between your Magento 2 store and your WhatConverts account. Every time a new order is placed—whether it's a $50 purchase or a $5,000 wholesale order—the system instantly captures that transaction and creates a corresponding "lead" within WhatConverts. This links the final revenue outcome directly back to the original marketing source, keyword, and campaign that brought the customer to your site in the first place.

The result is a closed-loop attribution system. You no longer have to guess which channels work; you have definitive, dollar-based proof. This automation turns your e-commerce platform from a simple sales processor into a powerful analytics engine, empowering you to make data-driven decisions that directly boost your bottom line.

How It Works

The automation is triggered by a new order event and moves data securely between platforms without any manual intervention.

Step 1: Monitoring New Magento 2 Orders

The workflow starts by watching your Magento 2 store for any new order creation. Using Make.com's Magento 2 module, it polls the store's API at regular intervals. The moment an order status changes to "processing" or "complete" (configurable based on your business rules), the automation captures the entire order payload.

Step 2: Extracting and Structuring Lead Data

Make.com then processes the raw order data. It extracts the crucial information needed for a WhatConverts lead: the customer's first and last name, email address, phone number, the total order value, and the items purchased. It can also pull in custom order attributes or notes. This step ensures the data is clean, formatted correctly, and ready to be sent to WhatConverts.

Step 3: Creating the Lead in WhatConverts

Finally, the workflow uses the WhatConverts API module in Make.com to create a new lead. It maps the extracted Magento data to the corresponding fields in WhatConverts. The lead is instantly logged in your WhatConverts dashboard, tagged with the lead source as "Magento Order," and includes all the monetary details. Your sales and marketing teams can now see the complete customer journey, from first click to final sale, all in one place.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the pre-built template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Magento 2: In the first Make.com module, add your Magento 2 Base URL and Access Token. Test the connection to ensure it can fetch order data.
  3. Connect WhatConverts: In the final module, add your WhatConverts API Key and Account ID. Perform a test to verify you can create a lead.
  4. Map the Data: Review the data mapping between the modules. Ensure customer name, email, phone, and order total are correctly passed from Magento to the corresponding WhatConverts lead fields.
  5. Set the Schedule: Configure how often the scenario runs (e.g., every 5 minutes) to process new orders promptly.
  6. Turn it On: Activate the scenario. Place a test order in your Magento store and verify a lead appears in your WhatConverts dashboard within a few minutes.
