What This Workflow Does

Every business that generates leads faces the same challenge: how to quickly separate the hot prospects from the cold ones. Without a system, sales reps spend hours each week manually reviewing contact forms, webinar attendees, and inbound inquiries—only to discover that most leads aren’t ready to buy. This leads to missed follow-up windows, inconsistent prioritization, and wasted time on unqualified leads.

This automated workflow solves that problem by instantly scoring leads based on your criteria, alerting your sales team in Slack, and updating your CRM—all without a single manual step. It turns your Google Sheets into a real-time lead qualification engine that works 24/7, ensuring your team always knows which leads to call first.

How It Works

1. New lead arrives in Google Sheets

Whenever a new row is added to your lead tracking sheet—whether from a Typeform submission, a webinar registration, or a manual entry—the workflow triggers. This could be a contact form fill, a demo request, or a downloaded resource.

2. Lead scoring rules are applied

Based on your predefined criteria (budget, authority, need, timeline, industry, company size, engagement level), the workflow calculates a lead score. For example, a lead from your target industry might get +20 points, while a demo request gets +50 points.

3. Slack notification sent to sales channel

If the lead score meets your qualification threshold, a formatted Slack message is posted to your designated sales channel. It includes the lead’s name, company, score, and a direct link to their CRM record.

4. CRM status updated automatically

The workflow updates the lead’s status in your CRM (like HubSpot or Salesforce) to “Qualified” or “Hot,” adds a note about the scoring, and can even assign the lead to a specific rep based on territory rules.
