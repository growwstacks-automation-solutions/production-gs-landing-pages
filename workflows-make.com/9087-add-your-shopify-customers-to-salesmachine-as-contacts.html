What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses using Shopify, every new customer represents a potential long-term relationship and future sales opportunity. However, this opportunity is often lost when customer data remains siloed in your store's backend, invisible to your sales team in their primary CRM, Salesmachine. Manually exporting lists and importing contacts is a slow, error-prone process that leads to delayed follow-ups and missed deals.

This automated workflow solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between your e-commerce platform and your sales intelligence tool. It monitors your Shopify store at regular intervals, detects newly created customers, and instantly creates corresponding contact records in Salesmachine. This ensures your sales representatives have immediate, actionable visibility into who is buying from you, enabling timely and personalized outreach that can increase repeat purchase rates and customer lifetime value.

How It Works

The automation acts as a diligent data connector, working silently in the background to keep your systems in sync.

Step 1: Monitor for New Shopify Customers

The workflow is triggered on a schedule you define (e.g., every hour or daily). It queries your Shopify store's API to retrieve a list of customers created since the last check.

Step 2: Process and Map Customer Data

For each new customer found, the automation extracts key information such as their full name, email address, phone number, and any available notes or tags from Shopify. This data is formatted to match the contact field structure expected by Salesmachine.

Pro tip: Use this step to enrich the data. You can add a step to calculate the customer's lifetime value from their order history or segment them based on their first purchase category before sending them to Salesmachine.

Step 3: Create Contact in Salesmachine

The formatted customer data is sent to Salesmachine via its API, where a new contact is created. The contact is automatically tagged with its source (e.g., "Shopify") and can be assigned to a specific sales rep or pipeline based on your rules.

Step 4: Log and Handle Errors

The workflow includes error handling. If a contact fails to create (e.g., due to a duplicate email), the error is logged, and the process continues with the next customer, ensuring robustness. You can set up notifications for any failures that require attention.
