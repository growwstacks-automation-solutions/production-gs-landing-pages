What This Workflow Does

For financial advisors, planners, and service-based businesses using Calendly for scheduling and Redtail CRM for client management, a critical gap often exists between booking and data entry. When a new client schedules a meeting, their information sits in Calendly until someone manually copies it into the CRM. This process is time-consuming, prone to errors, and delays follow-up.

This automated workflow bridges that gap seamlessly. It acts as a real-time connector between Calendly and Redtail CRM. Every time a new booking is created in Calendly—whether it's a consultation, discovery call, or follow-up meeting—the workflow automatically triggers. It captures the invitee's details, such as name, email, phone, and meeting notes, and creates a corresponding contact record in Redtail CRM without any human intervention.

The result is a perfectly synchronized system. Your CRM is always current with your latest prospects, your team saves hours on administrative tasks each week, and you can initiate timely, personalized follow-up immediately after a booking, enhancing client experience and conversion rates.

How It Works

Step 1: Calendly Triggers the Workflow

The workflow is initiated by a "New Event" trigger in Calendly. Whenever someone books an appointment through your Calendly scheduling link, Calendly sends the event data—including all the invitee's answers and scheduling details—to Make.com instantly.

Pro tip: Configure your Calendly event types and questions to gather all the information you need for a complete CRM contact, such as company name or service interest.

Step 2: Data is Parsed and Prepared

Make.com receives the raw booking data and maps it to the appropriate fields required by Redtail CRM. The workflow extracts key values like first name, last name, email address, phone number, and any custom answers from the booking form. It can also add contextual data, like tagging the contact based on the type of meeting booked.

Step 3: Contact is Created in Redtail CRM

The prepared data is sent to Redtail CRM via its API. The workflow executes the "Create a Contact" module, populating the new contact record with the mapped information from Calendly. The contact is instantly added to your Redtail database, ready for your team to view and action.

Step 4: Optional Follow-Up Actions

After the contact is created, the workflow can be extended to trigger additional actions. For example, it could add the contact to a specific list, send a welcome email via another integrated system, or create a task for a team member to review the new lead, creating a complete automated onboarding sequence.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to deploy this automation using the provided Make.com template.

  1. Clone the Template: Use the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template on Make.com. Click "Clone" to copy it into your own Make.com scenario.
  2. Connect Your Calendly: In the first module of your cloned scenario, replace the template's Calendly connection with your own. Authorize Make.com to access your Calendly account and select the specific event type you want to trigger the workflow.
  3. Connect Your Redtail CRM: In the "Create a Contact" module, replace the Redtail CRM connection with your own. Enter your Redtail API credentials to establish the link.
  4. Map the Data Fields: Review the data mapping between Calendly outputs and Redtail CRM contact fields. Adjust the mapping to match your Calendly form questions and your preferred Redtail contact field structure.
  5. Test the Workflow: Schedule a test meeting in your Calendly. Watch the workflow run in Make.com and verify that a test contact appears correctly in your Redtail CRM.
  6. Activate and Monitor: Turn on the scenario. It will now run automatically for every new booking. Set up a simple notification to alert you if any errors occur, ensuring long-term reliability.
