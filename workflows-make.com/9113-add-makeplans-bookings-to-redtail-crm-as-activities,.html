What This Workflow Does

For financial advisors, consultants, and service-based businesses, managing client appointments and maintaining an accurate CRM are two critical yet time-consuming tasks. You use MakePlans (or a similar booking system) to let clients schedule appointments easily, but then someone on your team must manually transfer each booking's details—client name, time, service type, notes—into Redtail CRM as a new activity. This process is prone to errors, delays, and creates a disconnect between your scheduling tool and your client history.

This free Make.com template solves that problem by creating a seamless, automatic bridge between MakePlans and Redtail CRM. Every time a new booking is created online, this workflow instantly triggers. It captures all the relevant booking information, formats it correctly, and creates a corresponding activity within the correct client's profile in Redtail. The result is a perfectly synchronized system where your CRM always reflects the latest client engagements without any manual effort.

How It Works

The automation operates on a simple "watch and create" principle, ensuring no booking slips through the cracks.

Step 1: Trigger on New MakePlans Booking

The workflow is initiated the moment a new booking is confirmed in your MakePlans account. Make.com watches for this specific event in real-time.

Step 2: Extract and Map Booking Data

The workflow pulls key details from the booking, such as the client's first and last name, email address, scheduled date and time, duration, the specific service booked, and any notes provided during scheduling.

Pro tip: Use this step to filter out test bookings or appointments from specific staff members by adding a router module. This keeps your CRM clean and only logs genuine client activities.

Step 3: Create Activity in Redtail CRM

Using the mapped data, the workflow connects to your Redtail CRM account via the API. It creates a new "Activity" linked to the client (matched by email or name). The activity subject can be formatted like "Scheduled: [Service Name]" and includes all the booking details in the notes, creating a comprehensive record for your team.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your own Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, authorize the connections to your specific MakePlans and Redtail CRM accounts.
  3. Test the Trigger: Create a test booking in MakePlans to ensure the workflow triggers correctly.
  4. Map the Data: Review the data mapping between MakePlans and the Redtail "Create an Activity" module. Adjust field mappings (like subject line, notes, activity type) to match your preferred format.
  5. Activate & Monitor: Turn the scenario on and monitor the first few executions to ensure activities are created accurately in Redtail. Your automation is now live.
