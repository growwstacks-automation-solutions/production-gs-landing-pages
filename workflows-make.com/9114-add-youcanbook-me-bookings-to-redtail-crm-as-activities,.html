What This Workflow Does

For financial advisors, coaches, consultants, and service professionals, managing client appointments is a core activity. You use tools like YouCanBook.me to let clients self-schedule, but then face the tedious, error-prone task of manually copying each booking into your Redtail CRM as an activity. This disconnect creates operational friction, risks missed follow-ups, and leaves your CRM—the source of truth for client relationships—incomplete.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a silent bridge between your scheduling calendar and your CRM. Whenever a new booking is created in YouCanBook.me, this Make.com workflow instantly captures all the details—client name, email, scheduled time, service type, and any custom form answers—and creates a corresponding activity in the correct contact's record within Redtail CRM. The process is fully automated, running in the background at intervals you set, ensuring your team always has the most up-to-date view of client engagements without lifting a finger.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com's visual automation platform. It follows a logical sequence to watch, transform, and create data.

Step 1: Watch for New Bookings

The scenario starts with a YouCanBook.me module set to "Watch Bookings." It polls your YouCanBook.me account at regular intervals (e.g., every 15 minutes) to check for any new confirmed bookings. When it finds one, it triggers the rest of the workflow and passes along the full booking data.

Step 2: Route and Transform Data

Make.com's router and data transformation tools come into play here. The workflow can check if the booking client already exists in Redtail, map the booking fields (date, time, notes) to the specific activity fields Redtail expects, and format the data correctly. This step ensures clean, accurate data transfer.

Step 3: Create the CRM Activity

The core action: a Redtail CRM module creates a new "Activity" for the contact. It populates the activity type (e.g., "Meeting"), sets the due date/time from the booking, adds description notes, and links it to the correct client record. The activity is logged immediately, ready for your team to see and act upon.

Pro tip: Use this automation to trigger secondary actions. For example, after creating the CRM activity, you can automatically send a calendar invite to the client or an internal notification to your team in Slack.
