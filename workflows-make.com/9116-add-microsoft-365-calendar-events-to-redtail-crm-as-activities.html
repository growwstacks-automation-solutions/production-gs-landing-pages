What This Workflow Does

For financial advisors, wealth managers, and any client-focused professional, the gap between your calendar and your CRM is where critical details get lost. You schedule a client meeting in Microsoft 365, but then you must manually open Redtail CRM, find the client, and log the activity. This double data entry is a repetitive, time-consuming task that often gets postponed or forgotten, leading to incomplete client records.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a silent bridge between your Microsoft 365 Calendar and Redtail CRM. Every time a new event is created in your specified calendar—be it a client review, a prospect call, or a compliance meeting—the workflow automatically triggers. It captures the event details and creates a corresponding activity in the correct client's record within Redtail. This ensures your CRM always reflects your real-world schedule, providing a single source of truth for all client interactions.

Pro tip: Use this automation not just for client meetings, but also for logging internal compliance trainings or important follow-up reminders. By having everything in Redtail, you build a comprehensive audit trail effortlessly.

How It Works

The workflow runs on a simple but powerful trigger-action principle, requiring no manual intervention once set up.

Step 1: Monitor the Calendar

The Make.com scenario starts by watching a specific Microsoft 365 Calendar. You can choose your primary calendar or a dedicated "Client Meetings" calendar. The trigger is set to "Watch Events," which means it instantly detects any new event as soon as it's saved.

Step 2: Process Event Data

When a new event is detected, the workflow captures all relevant data: the event subject, start and end time, location, description/notes, and the attendees. This data is parsed and formatted to fit the structure Redtail CRM expects for an activity.

Step 3: Create the CRM Activity

The formatted data is then sent to Redtail CRM via its API. The workflow searches for the client contact based on the attendee's email address or name and creates a new "Activity" under their record. The calendar event's subject becomes the activity description, the time is logged, and any notes are added for context.
