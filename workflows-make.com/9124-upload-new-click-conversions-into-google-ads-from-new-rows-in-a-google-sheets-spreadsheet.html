What This Workflow Does

If you run Google Ads, you know the frustration of incomplete data. Website conversions are easy to track, but what about the phone calls that turn into sales, the contact form leads your team qualifies, or the invoices that get paid days later? These "offline conversions" are often where your real revenue happens, yet they remain invisible to your ad platform. This means Google's smart bidding is working with half the picture, likely wasting budget on clicks that don't lead to valuable outcomes and missing opportunities to bid higher on the ones that do.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between your offline data in Google Sheets and your Google Ads account. It automatically detects new rows added to a designated spreadsheet—where your sales or support team logs outcomes—and uploads each record as a conversion directly into Google Ads. This closes the attribution loop, giving you a true measure of your return on ad spend (ROAS) and empowering Google's algorithms to optimize your campaigns for what actually matters: real business results.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a scheduled, hands-off basis, ensuring your conversion data is always up-to-date.

Step 1: Monitor Your Google Sheet

The scenario is triggered on a schedule (e.g., every 15 minutes). It connects to your specified Google Sheets spreadsheet and checks for any new rows that have been added since the last run. This is where you store your offline conversion data, such as closed deals, completed calls, or qualified leads.

Step 2: Process and Validate New Data

For each new row found, the workflow extracts the critical information: the Google Click ID (GCLID), the conversion name, value, and timestamp. It performs basic validation to ensure the data is formatted correctly and that required fields like the GCLID are present, preventing errors during the upload.

Step 3: Upload to Google Ads

Once validated, the workflow uses the Google Ads API to upload each conversion. It matches the GCLID from your sheet to the original ad click in Google Ads' system, crediting the conversion to the correct campaign, ad group, and keyword. This action instantly updates your conversion reports and influences automated bidding strategies.

Pro tip: Use a shared spreadsheet that your sales team updates directly from their CRM or call logs. This creates a single source of truth and eliminates the need for manual data exports.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in less than 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the ready-made scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Prepare Google Ads: In your Google Ads account, create a new "Upload" conversion action (e.g., "Phone Sale") and note its name. Generate OAuth credentials for the Make.com Google Ads module.
  3. Set Up Your Sheet: Make a copy of the sample spreadsheet and adapt the column headers to match your data. Share it with your team.
  4. Connect Accounts in Make: In the scenario, connect the Google Sheets and Google Ads modules to your respective accounts using OAuth.
  5. Map Your Data: Point the Sheets module to your new spreadsheet and map the columns (GCLID, conversion name, etc.) to the corresponding fields in the Google Ads upload module.
  6. Test and Activate: Run the scenario once to test with a single row of data. Confirm the conversion appears in Google Ads, then turn on the schedule to run automatically.

Common mistakes include not capturing the Google Click ID (GCLID) on your website forms, using incorrect conversion names or values in Sheets, having mismatched time zones between systems, and not excluding duplicate conversions.

To avoid these pitfalls:

  • Use a hidden form field to capture the `gclid` parameter.
  • Double-check that conversion names in Sheets match exactly those in Google Ads.
  • Ensure timestamps are in a consistent format (like ISO 8601).
  • Use the "deduplicate" option in the Google Ads upload module.
