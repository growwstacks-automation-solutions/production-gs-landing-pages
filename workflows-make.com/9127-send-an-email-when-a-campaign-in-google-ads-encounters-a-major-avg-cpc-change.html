What This Workflow Does

Managing Google Ads campaigns requires constant vigilance, especially when it comes to cost-per-click (CPC) fluctuations. A sudden, unexplained spike in CPC can quickly drain your advertising budget without delivering additional value, while a significant drop might indicate new optimization opportunities you don't want to miss. Manually checking these metrics daily is time-consuming and prone to oversight.

This automated workflow solves that problem by continuously monitoring your Google Ads campaigns for major average CPC changes. It runs daily, analyzes the last 7 days of performance data for each campaign, and compares the most recent two days that meet a minimum click threshold. When it detects a percentage change that exceeds your configured threshold, it automatically sends a detailed email alert with campaign performance details, giving you immediate visibility into budget-impacting changes.

The automation transforms reactive campaign management into proactive optimization. Instead of discovering budget overruns in your weekly report, you receive real-time notifications that enable immediate investigation and action. This approach not only protects your advertising investment but also helps identify positive trends you can capitalize on before competitors do.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a scheduled, automated basis to provide consistent monitoring without manual intervention.

Step 1: Scheduled Data Retrieval

Every day at 9 AM (configurable), the workflow automatically connects to your Google Ads account via the Make.com Google Ads module. It retrieves performance data for all your campaigns over the previous 7 days, focusing specifically on average CPC metrics and click counts for each day.

Step 2: Intelligent Data Filtering

The system filters this data to identify the last two days within the 7-day period that meet your minimum click threshold. This ensures the comparison is based on statistically significant data rather than days with minimal activity that could produce misleading percentage changes.

Pro tip: Set your minimum click threshold based on your typical campaign volume. For smaller campaigns, 10-15 clicks might be sufficient, while larger campaigns might require 50+ clicks for reliable comparison.

Step 3: Percentage Change Calculation

For each campaign meeting the criteria, the workflow calculates the percentage change in average CPC between the two identified days. It then compares this change against your predefined threshold (for example, a 20% increase or decrease).

Step 4: Alert Generation and Delivery

When a campaign exceeds your threshold, the workflow compiles a detailed email containing the campaign name, the specific CPC values, the percentage change, and relevant performance context. This email is sent immediately to your designated recipients, enabling prompt investigation and action.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation for your Google Ads account:

  1. Import the template: Click "Get This Workflow" above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Google Ads: In the first module, authenticate your Google Ads account using OAuth. Ensure you grant access to the appropriate manager account or client accounts.
  3. Configure monitoring parameters: Set your percentage change threshold (e.g., 20% for significant changes) and minimum click requirement (e.g., 15 clicks per day for reliable data).
  4. Set up email notifications: Connect your email service (Gmail, SMTP, etc.) and specify recipient email addresses. Customize the alert message with your preferred formatting and details.
  5. Test and activate: Run the scenario once to ensure connections work and alerts are generated correctly. Then activate the schedule to run daily at your preferred time.

Pro tip: Start with a conservative threshold (like 25-30%) to avoid alert fatigue. Once you understand your campaign's normal fluctuation patterns, you can adjust the threshold to catch smaller but still meaningful changes.
