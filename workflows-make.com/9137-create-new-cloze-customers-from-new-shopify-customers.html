What This Workflow Does

If you sell on Shopify, you know that every new customer represents a potential long-term relationship. But that relationship often starts with a spreadsheet gap: the customer exists in your store, but your sales team can't see them in their primary tool, Cloze CRM. This forces manual copying of emails, names, and order details—a repetitive task that wastes hours each week and leads to missed follow-up opportunities.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as a real-time bridge between your e-commerce platform and your CRM. The moment a customer completes their first purchase on Shopify, the workflow triggers. It collects the customer's profile information and automatically creates a corresponding, fully-fledged contact inside Cloze. Your sales or customer success team now has immediate visibility and can start a personalized engagement sequence without any delay.

The business value is clear: faster response times, zero data entry errors, and a unified view of your customer journey. Instead of reacting days later, your team can proactively welcome, upsell, and retain customers, directly boosting customer lifetime value (LTV) and operational efficiency.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple, reliable sequence to ensure data flows accurately from point A to point B.

Step 1: Monitor for New Shopify Customers

The workflow starts with a "Watch Customer" module in Make.com, connected to your Shopify store. It sits in the background, polling your store at regular intervals (or using webhooks for instant triggers) to detect whenever a new customer record is created.

Step 2: Extract and Format Customer Data

Once a new customer is detected, the workflow pulls the relevant data fields. This typically includes the customer's first and last name, email address, phone number, default shipping address, and any tags or notes attached to their profile in Shopify.

Pro tip: Use this step to enrich the data. You can add a step to calculate their lifetime value from order history or append a lead source tag like "Shopify-Website" before sending it to Cloze.

Step 3: Create or Update Contact in Cloze

The formatted data is then sent to Cloze via its API. The workflow uses the "Create a Person" action to build a new contact. It intelligently maps the Shopify fields to the correct fields in Cloze, populating the contact's name, email, phone, and company details. The workflow can also check if a contact already exists to prevent duplicates.

Step 4: Log Completion and Handle Errors

Finally, the workflow logs the successful sync for your records. If an error occurs—like a missing required field or an API issue—it can be configured to send an alert to your team via email or Slack, ensuring no customer falls through the cracks.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above. This will copy the pre-built scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Shopify: In the first module, click to add your Shopify connection. You'll be prompted to log into your store and authorize Make.com to access customer data.
  3. Connect Cloze: In the Cloze module, similarly add your connection using your Cloze API key (found in your Cloze settings under "Apps & API").
  4. Map Data Fields (Optional): Review the data mapping between the modules. The template has standard mappings, but you can customize them—for example, sending Shopify tags to a custom field in Cloze.
  5. Turn It On: Activate the scenario. It will immediately start monitoring your Shopify store for new customers.
  6. Test It: Create a test customer in Shopify or wait for a real one. Verify that the contact appears correctly in your Cloze account shortly after.
