What This Workflow Does

Staying competitive requires constant awareness of market pricing changes, but manual competitor monitoring consumes valuable time and often misses critical updates. This automated workflow solves that problem by continuously tracking competitor pricing through Airtop and instantly alerting your team via Slack whenever changes occur.

The system eliminates the need for daily manual price checks while providing more accurate, timestamped records of competitor pricing movements. Retailers, ecommerce stores, and SaaS companies using this solution typically save 5-10 hours per week while gaining faster response capabilities to market changes.

Example of competitor pricing data captured by the automation

How It Works

1. Competitor price data collection

The workflow begins by scraping competitor websites or APIs at scheduled intervals to capture current pricing data. Advanced selectors ensure accurate extraction of product prices, sale prices, and any promotional pricing.

2. Change detection analysis

Each new price check gets compared against historical data stored in Airtable. The system calculates percentage changes and flags any adjustments exceeding your configured threshold (typically 2-5%).

Workflow logic for detecting and analyzing price changes

3. Slack notification generation

When significant changes are detected, the workflow formats a detailed Slack message showing the product, old price, new price, percentage change, and direct link to the competitor's product page.

Pro tip: Configure different Slack channels for urgent price drops versus general changes to help prioritize team responses.

4. Historical tracking in Airtable

All price changes get logged in Airtable with timestamps, creating an auditable history of competitor pricing strategies. This data becomes valuable for identifying seasonal trends and predicting future price movements.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure your Airtable connection details and base ID
  3. Add Slack webhook URL for your notification channel
  4. Input competitor product URLs and CSS selectors for price elements
  5. Set your price change sensitivity threshold (recommended 3%)
  6. Test with 2-3 products to verify accurate price detection
  7. Schedule the workflow to run hourly or daily based on needs
