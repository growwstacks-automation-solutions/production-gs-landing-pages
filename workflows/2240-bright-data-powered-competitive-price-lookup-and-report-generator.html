What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the time-consuming challenge of manually tracking competitor pricing across e-commerce platforms. Retailers and brands often spend hours each week checking competitor prices, analyzing trends, and compiling reports - tasks that are perfect for automation.

The workflow automatically collects pricing data using Bright Data's web scraping capabilities, enriches the data with AI-powered analysis, and generates comprehensive reports delivered via HTML email. It transforms raw pricing data into actionable intelligence that helps businesses maintain competitive pricing strategies.

Example of competitive pricing data visualization generated by the workflow

How It Works

1. Competitor Data Collection

The workflow uses Bright Data to scrape competitor websites for product pricing, availability, and promotional information. It handles proxies, CAPTCHAs, and anti-bot measures automatically.

2. Data Enrichment

Collected data is processed through AI models to categorize products, identify pricing patterns, and flag significant changes compared to your own pricing.

Data enrichment process in the workflow

3. Report Generation

The system compiles findings into an attractive HTML report with visualizations showing price comparisons, trend analysis, and recommended actions.

4. Automated Delivery

Completed reports are automatically emailed to stakeholders on a schedule you define, ensuring timely insights without manual intervention.

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for e-commerce managers, pricing analysts, and retail operations teams who need to:

Pro tip: Combine this workflow with your inventory management system to automatically adjust pricing based on competitor movements.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. Bright Data account with web unlocking capabilities
  3. List of competitor URLs and product identifiers
  4. Email service connected to n8n (like SendGrid or SMTP)

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure Bright Data credentials in the webhook node
  3. Set up your competitor URLs and product mapping
  4. Customize the email template with your branding
  5. Test with a small product selection before full deployment
