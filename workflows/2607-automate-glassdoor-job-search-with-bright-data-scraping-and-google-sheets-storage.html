What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming process of manually searching Glassdoor for relevant job postings. Recruitment teams and hiring managers often spend hours each week scanning job boards, copying details into spreadsheets, and organizing the information. The workflow automates this entire process using Bright Data's powerful web scraping capabilities combined with n8n's workflow automation.

The system extracts structured job data including titles, companies, locations, salaries, and requirements from Glassdoor search results. It then cleans and formats this information before storing it in Google Sheets for easy access and analysis. The automation runs on a schedule you define, ensuring your team always has fresh job market data without manual effort.

Scraped job data automatically organized in Google Sheets

How It Works

1. Bright Data scraping configuration

The workflow begins by configuring Bright Data's scraping tools to extract job listings from Glassdoor while avoiding detection. This includes setting up proxy rotation, request throttling, and handling CAPTCHAs when necessary.

2. Keyword-based search execution

The automation executes searches on Glassdoor using your predefined keywords and filters. You can specify multiple search terms, locations, experience levels, and other criteria to target relevant opportunities.

3. Data extraction and cleaning

Bright Data extracts structured data from the search results while n8n processes and cleans the information. This includes standardizing date formats, removing duplicates, and extracting key details from job descriptions.

Data transformation steps in the automation workflow

4. Google Sheets integration

The cleaned job data gets automatically appended to your designated Google Sheet. The workflow maintains data consistency by mapping scraped fields to your spreadsheet columns and preserving formatting rules.
