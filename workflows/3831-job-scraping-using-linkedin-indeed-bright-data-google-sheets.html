What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming challenge of manually monitoring multiple job boards for relevant openings. Recruiters and HR professionals waste hours each week copying job details from LinkedIn, Indeed, and other platforms into spreadsheets. Our workflow automates this entire process while avoiding IP blocks through Bright Data's proxy network.

The system extracts structured job data including titles, companies, locations, salaries, and descriptions. It cleans and organizes this information in Google Sheets, creating a searchable database of opportunities. This enables faster candidate matching and competitive analysis while eliminating tedious data entry work.

Example of scraped job data fields including company, title, and location

How It Works

1. Job Search Configuration

The workflow starts by defining your search criteria - job titles, locations, salary ranges, and keywords. These parameters are formatted for both LinkedIn and Indeed's search systems.

2. Proxy-Protected Scraping

Using Bright Data's residential proxies, the workflow accesses job boards without triggering anti-scraping measures. IP addresses rotate automatically to mimic organic traffic patterns.

3. Data Extraction & Cleaning

Key fields are extracted from each listing and standardized (converting salary formats, normalizing company names, etc.). Duplicate postings are identified and removed.

Data transformation process from raw scrape to cleaned spreadsheet

4. Google Sheets Export

Final data is appended to a designated Google Sheet with consistent column formatting. Timestamps track when each job was added for freshness analysis.

Scraping publicly available job data is generally legal under fair use principles, but must comply with each platform's terms. The safest approach is to only scrape data you could access manually as a regular user, not attempting to circumvent access controls. Most jurisdictions allow collecting publicly visible information for personal use, but restrictions apply for repackaging and reselling scraped data.
