What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the challenge of maintaining consistent, high-quality LinkedIn content by automating the entire creation and publishing process. It combines AI-generated ideas with human oversight and performance-based refinement to ensure your posts resonate with your target audience while saving hours of manual work each week.

The system pulls from your brand guidelines in Notion, generates multiple post variations using OpenAI, collects feedback from your team, schedules optimal posting times, and tracks engagement metrics to continuously improve content quality. This creates a virtuous cycle where each post informs better future content.

The workflow interface showing the multi-step LinkedIn post creation process

How It Works

1. Content Brief Extraction

The workflow starts by pulling your latest content strategy brief from Notion, including target keywords, audience personas, and brand voice guidelines. This ensures all generated content aligns with your marketing objectives.

2. AI Idea Generation

OpenAI analyzes your brief and produces 5-7 post ideas with complete drafts. The system weights suggestions based on past performance data, favoring content types and topics that historically performed well for your audience.

AI generating post variations based on brand guidelines and past performance

3. Human Approval Workflow

Generated posts route to your team for review via email or Slack. Approvers can request revisions or approve posts directly in the workflow interface. Approved content gets scheduled while rejected drafts trigger regeneration with additional guidance.

4. Performance Tracking & Optimization

After publishing, the system monitors engagement metrics and updates your Notion content database. These insights inform future post generation, creating a continuous improvement cycle that increases content effectiveness over time.

Pro tip: Seed your Notion database with 10-15 of your best-performing historical posts to train the AI on your successful content patterns from day one.
