What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious manual process of creating invoices for delivery services. When your team creates a new task in Onfleet (like scheduling a food delivery or package pickup), the system automatically generates a corresponding customer invoice in QuickBooks with all relevant details.

The workflow pulls delivery information including customer details, service fees, distance charges, and any special handling requirements, then formats this data into a properly structured QuickBooks invoice. This eliminates hours of manual data entry while ensuring billing accuracy across all deliveries.

Automated invoice showing mapped delivery details from Onfleet

How It Works

1. New task detection in Onfleet

The workflow monitors Onfleet for new task creation events. When a dispatcher assigns a delivery (whether through the dashboard, API, or mobile app), the system captures all relevant task details including pickup/dropoff locations, estimated distance, and service notes.

2. Data transformation

The raw task data gets processed to extract billable components. The system calculates applicable fees based on your pricing model (flat rate, mileage-based, or time-sensitive charges) and prepares line items for the invoice.

3. QuickBooks invoice creation

Using the processed data, the workflow creates a draft invoice in QuickBooks with the customer's existing account or creates a new customer record. It includes all delivery details as line items with proper tax handling and your business branding.
