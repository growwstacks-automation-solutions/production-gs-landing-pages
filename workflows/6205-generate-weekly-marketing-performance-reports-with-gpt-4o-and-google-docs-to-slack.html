What This Workflow Does

This automation eliminates the tedious manual process of compiling and analyzing weekly marketing reports. It connects your data sources (like Google Ads, Meta, or CRM systems) with GPT-4o's analytical capabilities to generate insightful performance summaries, then formats them in Google Docs and shares them via Slack.

Instead of spending hours each week pulling numbers from different platforms and trying to spot trends, this workflow automatically aggregates data, identifies key patterns, and delivers actionable insights to your team in a polished format. The AI component goes beyond basic number-crunching to provide strategic recommendations based on your historical performance and industry benchmarks.

Sample output showing an AI-generated marketing report delivered to Slack with key metrics and insights

How It Works

1. Data Collection

The workflow begins by pulling raw performance data from your connected marketing platforms. This can include metrics like impressions, clicks, conversions, spend, ROI, and any custom KPIs you track.

2. AI Analysis

GPT-4o processes the collected data, identifying trends, anomalies, and opportunities. It compares current performance to historical benchmarks and generates natural language insights about what's working and what needs attention.

3. Report Generation

The system formats the analyzed data into a structured Google Doc with visual highlights, executive summaries, and detailed breakdowns by channel or campaign. The document maintains your brand styling and preferred report structure.

Automation architecture showing how data moves from source platforms through analysis to final delivery

4. Slack Delivery

The completed report is shared to designated Slack channels with @mentions for key stakeholders. The message includes a link to the full Google Doc along with the most critical insights pulled into the Slack message itself for quick review.

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to run every Friday afternoon so your team has fresh insights waiting when they start their Monday morning.
