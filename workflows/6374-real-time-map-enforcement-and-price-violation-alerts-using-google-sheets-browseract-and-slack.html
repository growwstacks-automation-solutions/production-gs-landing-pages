What This Workflow Does

This automation solution tackles the critical challenge of Minimum Advertised Price (MAP) enforcement, which is essential for brands selling through retail partners. The workflow continuously monitors retailer websites for price violations against your established MAP policies, sending instant alerts to your team when infractions occur.

By automating what was traditionally a manual and time-consuming process, this system ensures consistent price protection across all sales channels. It combines web scraping capabilities with real-time notification systems to create a robust compliance monitoring solution that operates 24/7.

Sample Slack notification showing detected MAP violation with product details

How It Works

1. Price Data Collection

The system pulls your authorized MAP pricing from a Google Sheets database, which serves as the single source of truth for your pricing policies. This spreadsheet can be easily updated by your pricing team as policies change.

2. Retailer Website Monitoring

Using BrowserAct's web automation capabilities, the workflow visits retailer product pages at scheduled intervals to scrape current pricing information. It handles different website structures and can navigate through product listings.

3. Violation Detection

Scraped prices are compared against your MAP thresholds. The system accounts for various pricing formats (e.g., "price with coupon" vs. listed price) and can be configured with different tolerance levels for minor deviations.

Google Sheets interface serving as the centralized MAP pricing database

4. Alert Generation

When violations are detected, the system generates detailed Slack alerts containing product information, the advertised price, your MAP price, and direct links to the violating product pages. Alerts are categorized by severity for prioritized response.

5. Documentation & Follow-up

All violations are logged back to Google Sheets with timestamps, creating an audit trail for compliance purposes. The system can optionally trigger follow-up workflows like automated warning emails to retailers.
