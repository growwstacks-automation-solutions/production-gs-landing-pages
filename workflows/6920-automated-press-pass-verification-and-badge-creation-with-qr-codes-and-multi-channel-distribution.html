What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming and error-prone process of manually verifying press credentials and issuing event badges. It creates a seamless system where journalists can be pre-approved, receive digital passes with scannable QR codes, and get automatic reminders through their preferred communication channels.

The workflow reduces security risks at media events by ensuring only verified journalists gain access, while providing organizers with real-time data on press attendance and engagement. It eliminates the last-minute chaos of badge printing and manual check-ins that often plague high-profile events.

Sample press badge showing dynamic QR code and media credentials

How It Works

1. Press Application Submission

Journalists submit their credentials through a branded online form that captures essential details like media outlet, press ID, and contact information. The system automatically checks these against your media database or approval criteria.

2. Automated Verification

Approved applicants trigger the badge creation process, while rejected ones receive polite decline notices. The verification process can integrate with media databases like Cision or Meltwater for instant credential validation.

3. Dynamic Badge Generation

The system generates personalized digital badges containing encrypted QR codes, journalist photos, and access level indicators. These can include watermarks or holographic effects to prevent duplication.

Automated distribution channels for press badges including email, SMS and messaging apps

4. Multi-Channel Distribution

Badges are delivered via the journalist's preferred channel (email, WhatsApp, SMS) with event details and scanning instructions. The system can send reminder messages as the event approaches.

5. Real-Time Verification

At the event, staff scan QR codes using tablets or smartphones to instantly verify authenticity and check access privileges. The system logs attendance data for post-event reporting.

Pro tip: For high-security events, configure your workflow to generate time-sensitive QR codes that expire after first scan or after the event concludes.
