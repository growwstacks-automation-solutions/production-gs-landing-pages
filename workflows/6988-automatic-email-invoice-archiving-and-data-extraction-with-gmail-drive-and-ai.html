What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious manual process of managing incoming invoices via email. Many businesses receive recurring invoices from vendors, suppliers, and service providers directly to their Gmail inbox. Without automation, accounting teams waste hours each month downloading attachments, renaming files, and manually entering data into accounting systems.

The workflow automatically identifies incoming invoice emails, extracts the attachments, stores them in organized Google Drive folders, and uses AI to pull key data like invoice numbers, dates, amounts, and vendor details. This creates a searchable digital archive while eliminating manual data entry errors.

The workflow automatically processes invoices from email to organized storage

How It Works

1. Email Monitoring

The workflow continuously monitors your Gmail inbox for new messages matching specific criteria (like sender domain or subject line containing "invoice"). When a matching email arrives, it triggers the automation.

2. Attachment Extraction

The system downloads any attachments (typically PDF invoices) and saves them to a designated Google Drive folder structure organized by vendor and date. File names are standardized for easy searching.

Automatically organized invoice storage in Google Drive

3. AI Data Extraction

Using AI-powered OCR (optical character recognition), the workflow scans each invoice to extract key fields like invoice number, date, total amount, tax details, payment terms, and vendor information.

4. Data Validation & Export

The extracted data is validated against business rules (like checking for duplicate invoice numbers) before being formatted for export to your accounting software or spreadsheet.

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to send Slack alerts when high-value invoices arrive or when duplicate invoice numbers are detected.
