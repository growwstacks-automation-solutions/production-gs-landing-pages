What This Workflow Does

This automated workflow solves a critical challenge for recruitment agencies and HR departments: maintaining GDPR compliance while processing candidate CVs. The system intelligently removes personally identifiable information (PII) like names, addresses, and contact details while preserving professional qualifications, work history, and skills.

Using GPT-4's natural language processing capabilities combined with Stirling PDF's document manipulation features, the workflow transforms incoming resumes into anonymized versions that protect candidate privacy while still allowing recruiters to assess professional suitability. This eliminates manual redaction work while ensuring compliance with strict European data protection regulations.

Before and after example of CV anonymization process

How It Works

1. Document Ingestion

The workflow accepts CVs in multiple formats (PDF, DOCX, etc.) from various sources like email attachments, cloud storage, or applicant tracking systems. Stirling PDF converts all documents to a standardized format for processing.

2. Text Extraction

The system extracts all text content from the documents while maintaining structural elements like headings and bullet points. This creates a clean text version of the CV for AI processing.

3. AI-Powered Anonymization

GPT-4 analyzes the text to identify and remove personal information while preserving professional details. The AI understands context to avoid removing relevant professional names (like universities or previous employers).

AI analysis of CV content to identify personal vs professional information

4. Document Reconstruction

The anonymized text is reinserted into the original document format with all formatting preserved. Stirling PDF handles the final document assembly to create a professional-looking anonymized CV.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about CV anonymization and GDPR compliance

GDPR requires removal of direct identifiers like full names, home addresses, personal phone numbers, and email addresses. Birth dates, photos, and national ID numbers should also be removed. The key principle is removing information that could directly identify an individual while preserving professional qualifications.

Our workflow uses contextual AI to distinguish between personal contact details (which get removed) and professional affiliations (which are kept). For example, it will remove "John Smith" but keep "Harvard University" since the latter is relevant professional information.

  • Always remove: Names, addresses, personal contact info
  • Contextual removal: Birth dates if not relevant to role
  • Preserve: Professional qualifications and work history
