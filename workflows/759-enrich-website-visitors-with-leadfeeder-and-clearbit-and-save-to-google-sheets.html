What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of unidentified website visitors who could be valuable leads for your business. Many companies miss out on potential opportunities because they don't know who's visiting their site or how to follow up with them.

The workflow automatically identifies companies visiting your website using Leadfeeder, enriches that data with Clearbit's company intelligence, and saves all the information to a Google Sheet for your sales team. This creates a powerful lead database from your existing web traffic without manual research.

Example output showing enriched lead data in Google Sheets

How It Works

1. Leadfeeder identifies website visitors

The workflow starts by pulling data from Leadfeeder, which tracks companies visiting your website by analyzing their IP addresses. This provides initial company names and visit details.

2. Clearbit enriches the company data

Each identified company is then sent to Clearbit's API, which adds valuable information like industry, employee count, revenue estimates, technologies used, and key decision-makers.

3. Data is organized in Google Sheets

The enriched lead information is automatically formatted and saved to a Google Sheet with columns for all relevant data points, creating a ready-to-use lead database.

Leadfeeder identifies companies with about 85-90% accuracy by matching IP addresses to business networks. While it can't identify individual visitors, it reliably detects when employees from specific companies visit your site. The accuracy improves for larger companies with distinct IP ranges.

For example, when a visitor from Microsoft's corporate network accesses your site, Leadfeeder will recognize it as Microsoft but won't know which employee. This is still valuable for B2B sales teams who want to know which companies are researching their solutions.

  • Works best for corporate networks, not residential IPs
  • Larger companies are easier to identify
  • Updated daily with new IP-to-company mappings
