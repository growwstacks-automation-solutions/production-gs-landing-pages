What This Workflow Does

This automation transforms your BlueSky engagement strategy by instantly delivering resources to followers who show interest in your content. When someone replies to your post with a predefined keyword (like "guide" or "demo"), the system automatically sends them a direct message containing your specified resource link. This creates a seamless experience where followers get immediate value while you save hours of manual messaging.

The workflow solves the challenge of scaling personalized interactions on social media. Instead of monitoring notifications and manually sending resources, this system handles distribution automatically 24/7. It's particularly valuable for creators, educators, and businesses who want to provide instant access to gated content, lead magnets, or support materials without constant manual intervention.

How keyword-triggered DMs work in practice on BlueSky

How It Works

1. Keyword Detection

The workflow continuously monitors replies to your specified BlueSky posts. When it detects a reply containing one of your configured keywords, it captures the user's handle and the specific keyword used.

2. Resource Matching

Based on the detected keyword, the system selects the appropriate resource URL from your predefined list. This allows you to have different responses for different requests (e.g., sending a pricing sheet for "cost" replies versus a case study for "example").

3. DM Composition

The workflow crafts a personalized direct message incorporating the user's handle and your pre-written message template. You can customize the tone and content to match your brand voice while maintaining consistency across automated interactions.

The n8n workflow editor showing how the automation processes replies and sends DMs

4. Delivery & Logging

The system sends the composed DM through your BlueSky account and logs the interaction for tracking purposes. This creates an audit trail showing which resources were sent to whom and when, valuable for analyzing engagement patterns.

Pro tip: Set up a secondary keyword like "stop" that lets users opt out of automated messages. This maintains positive engagement while respecting user preferences.
