What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming challenge of manually tracking box office performance across multiple films and markets. Entertainment professionals, investors, and marketers need real-time insights into which movies are succeeding, but compiling this data manually takes hours each week.

The workflow automatically monitors box office websites via BrowserAct, extracts key performance metrics, logs them in Google Sheets for historical analysis, and sends Telegram alerts when new films meet your criteria. It transforms scattered public data into structured, actionable intelligence without manual effort.

Automated box office data organized in Google Sheets with revenue tracking

How It Works

1. BrowserAct monitors box office sources

The workflow starts by having BrowserAct scrape configured box office tracking websites on a schedule you set. It captures raw HTML containing release dates, revenue figures, theater counts, and other key metrics.

2. Data extraction and formatting

n8n processes the scraped data to extract structured information - converting text like "$12.5M opening weekend" into clean numerical values. The workflow handles different data formats across sources.

3. Google Sheets integration

Formatted data automatically populates your Google Sheet with columns for each metric. The workflow checks for duplicates and only adds new records, maintaining a clean historical database.

Instant Telegram alert showing new box office release details

4. Telegram notifications

For particularly important releases (based on your criteria), the workflow sends formatted Telegram messages with key details. These mobile-friendly alerts ensure you never miss significant openings.
