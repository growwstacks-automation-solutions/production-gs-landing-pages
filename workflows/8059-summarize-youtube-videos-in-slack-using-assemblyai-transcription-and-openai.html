What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common challenge of extracting valuable insights from lengthy YouTube videos without requiring team members to watch the entire content. It automatically processes YouTube links shared in Slack, generates accurate transcriptions using AssemblyAI, and creates concise summaries with OpenAI's powerful language models.

The workflow eliminates manual transcription work and helps teams quickly understand video content for research, learning, or decision-making purposes. It's particularly valuable for content teams, educators, and knowledge workers who regularly consume video content but need text-based summaries for reference.

Sample output showing structured video summary posted directly in Slack

How It Works

1. Trigger: YouTube link detection in Slack

The workflow monitors specified Slack channels for messages containing YouTube URLs. When detected, it extracts the video ID and prepares it for processing.

2. Video processing with AssemblyAI

The system sends the YouTube URL to AssemblyAI's transcription API, which automatically downloads the audio, processes it, and returns a complete text transcript with timestamps.

3. AI summarization with OpenAI

The transcript is then sent to OpenAI's API where it's analyzed and condensed into key points, action items, or executive summaries based on your preferred format.

Pro tip: Configure different summary styles for different Slack channels - bullet points for quick scanning in general channels, detailed paragraphs for research teams.

4. Delivery back to Slack

The final summary is formatted and posted as a threaded reply to the original YouTube link message, keeping all context together in your Slack conversation.
