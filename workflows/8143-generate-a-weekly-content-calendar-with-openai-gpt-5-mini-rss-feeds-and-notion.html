What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common challenge of content creator burnout by automatically generating fresh content ideas every week. It pulls trending topics from your selected RSS feeds, processes them through OpenAI's GPT-5-mini to generate creative content angles, and organizes everything into a structured Notion content calendar.

The workflow eliminates hours of manual research and brainstorming each week while ensuring your content stays relevant to current trends in your industry. It's particularly valuable for marketing teams, bloggers, and social media managers who need to maintain consistent content output.

Sample output showing AI-generated content ideas organized in Notion

How It Works

1. RSS Feed Aggregation

The workflow starts by pulling the latest articles from your configured RSS feeds. You can specify multiple feeds across different industry categories to ensure diverse content sources.

2. AI Content Generation

OpenAI's GPT-5-mini analyzes the aggregated content, identifies key themes, and generates fresh content ideas tailored to your brand voice and audience preferences. The AI considers engagement metrics and trending topics to prioritize the most promising ideas.

3. Notion Calendar Population

The generated content ideas are automatically formatted and added to your Notion content calendar as draft posts, complete with suggested publishing dates, content categories, and initial outlines.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n account (free or paid)
  2. OpenAI API access with GPT-5-mini enabled
  3. A Notion account with a pre-configured content calendar database
  4. RSS feed URLs from your industry sources
  5. Basic familiarity with n8n workflow configuration

Pro tip: Start with 3-5 high-quality RSS feeds from authoritative sources in your niche. Too many feeds can overwhelm the AI with irrelevant content.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the JSON template file
  2. Import it into your n8n account
  3. Configure your RSS feed URLs in the HTTP Request nodes
  4. Add your OpenAI API key in the credentials section
  5. Connect to your Notion account and select your content calendar database
  6. Test the workflow with a manual trigger
  7. Schedule it to run weekly
