What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the most time-consuming aspects of B2B prospecting: finding qualified leads, researching their needs, and crafting personalized outreach. Traditional methods require hours of manual data entry and generic email templates that yield low response rates.

The workflow combines three powerful technologies: BrowserAct for scraping Apollo.io lead data, Google Gemini AI for writing context-aware emails, and Google Sheets for centralized lead management. It transforms raw prospect lists into ready-to-send personalized campaigns with 90% less manual effort.

Gemini AI analyzes scraped lead data to generate hyper-relevant email content

How It Works

1. Scrape Apollo.io lead data

BrowserAct automatically navigates Apollo.io search results, extracting contact details (name, email, company, role) along with firmographic data (company size, tech stack, funding). The scraper handles pagination and follows Apollo's rate limits to ensure compliant data collection.

2. Enrich lead profiles

The workflow supplements scraped data with additional signals from LinkedIn and company websites. This enrichment helps Gemini AI understand each prospect's context - recent promotions, company news, or technology implementations that can inform personalized messaging.

3. Generate AI-powered emails

Google Gemini receives the enriched lead profile and generates a tailored email draft. The AI considers the prospect's industry, role-specific pain points, and any triggering events (like funding rounds) to craft relevant value propositions and call-to-actions.

Automated data flow from Apollo.io scraping to organized Google Sheets records

4. Save to Google Sheets

All lead data and generated email drafts are logged in a structured Google Sheet with timestamps. This creates a searchable prospect database and allows for human review before sending. The sheet includes status tracking for follow-up sequencing.

Pro tip: Add a manual approval step before sending emails to maintain quality control while still saving 80% of drafting time.
