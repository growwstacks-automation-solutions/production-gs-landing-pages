What This Workflow Does

This AI-powered workflow systematically analyzes your website to identify missing pages that could enhance user experience and drive better business results. It combines GPT-4.1-mini's contextual understanding with Perplexity's real-time web data to provide actionable recommendations for content expansion.

The system evaluates your current site structure, compares it against industry standards and competitor offerings, then generates a prioritized list of suggested pages with explanations of how each would benefit your visitors and business goals. Results are automatically compiled into an organized Google Doc for easy sharing with your team.

Sample output showing identified content gaps and recommendations

How It Works

1. Website Analysis

The workflow first crawls your website to map the existing page structure and content hierarchy. It identifies key content areas and notes any obvious structural gaps in your information architecture.

2. Competitive Comparison

Using Perplexity's web search capabilities, the system gathers data on competitor websites you specify. It compares their content offerings against yours to identify pages they have that you're missing.

3. AI Gap Identification

GPT-4.1-mini analyzes the collected data to suggest logical missing pages based on your industry, business model, and existing content. It considers both user needs and business objectives.

AI processing website structure and content relationships

4. Recommendation Prioritization

The workflow scores each suggested page based on potential impact, ease of implementation, and strategic importance. Higher-scoring recommendations appear at the top of your report.

5. Report Generation

All findings are automatically compiled into a formatted Google Doc with clear sections, visual hierarchy, and actionable next steps for your content team.

Pro tip: For best results, provide the AI with your business goals and target audience details before running the analysis. This helps it generate more relevant, strategic recommendations.

Website page gaps are missing content pages that would help visitors find information or complete desired actions. They matter because they directly impact user experience, conversion rates, and search visibility. Common gaps include missing product comparison pages, inadequate FAQ sections, or lacking industry-specific resource pages that competitors offer.

These gaps create friction in the customer journey that can be easily addressed with targeted content.
