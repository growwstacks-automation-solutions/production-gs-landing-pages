What This Workflow Does

This automation solution helps educational institutions maintain academic standards by automatically monitoring for potential integrity violations. It uses GPT-4o's advanced natural language processing to analyze student submissions, flagging potential cases of plagiarism, contract cheating, or unauthorized collaboration with higher accuracy than traditional tools.

The system automatically generates case files, notifies relevant faculty or administrators, and maintains a searchable archive of all integrity cases. This creates consistent documentation for accreditation requirements while reducing the manual workload of monitoring by 60-80% compared to manual processes.

Example academic integrity monitoring dashboard showing detection metrics and case status

How It Works

1. Submission Monitoring

The workflow automatically checks all student submissions through connected learning management systems. It analyzes text similarity, writing style inconsistencies, and other integrity signals using GPT-4o's advanced capabilities.

2. AI-Powered Analysis

GPT-4o evaluates each submission against multiple integrity indicators, providing confidence scores for potential violations. The system only escalates cases that meet your institution's predefined thresholds for review.

Pro tip: Start with conservative detection thresholds and adjust based on your institution's specific needs and false positive tolerance.

3. Case Creation & Notification

For potential violations meeting your criteria, the system automatically creates a case file with all relevant evidence and sends email alerts to designated reviewers. Case files include the original submission, comparison materials, and AI analysis notes.

Case management interface showing violation details and resolution workflow

4. Resolution Tracking

The system tracks each case through resolution, documenting all actions taken and maintaining a complete audit trail. Closed cases are archived with appropriate retention policies while remaining searchable for future reference.
