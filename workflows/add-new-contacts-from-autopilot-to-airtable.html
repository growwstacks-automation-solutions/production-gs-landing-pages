Automatically sync new contacts from your Autopilot CRM to Airtable for better organization and team collaboration

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of manually transferring new contacts between your CRM (Autopilot) and your team's collaboration database (Airtable). When sales teams capture leads in Autopilot, other departments often need access to this information for follow-up, analysis, or project management.

The workflow automatically detects new contacts added to Autopilot and creates corresponding records in your specified Airtable base. This eliminates duplicate data entry, reduces human error, and ensures all teams have immediate access to the latest contact information.

How It Works

1. Trigger: New Autopilot Contact

The workflow monitors your Autopilot account for new contact additions. When a lead fills out a form, signs up through your website, or is manually added to Autopilot, the workflow immediately detects this event.

2. Data Transformation

The contact information is formatted to match your Airtable structure. You can customize which fields get transferred (name, email, company, etc.) and how they're mapped to your Airtable columns.

3. Create Airtable Record

The workflow creates a new row in your specified Airtable base with all the contact details. You can configure additional fields like tags, status, or assignment to team members.

Pro tip: Add a timestamp field in Airtable to track when each contact was synced from Autopilot for better reporting.
