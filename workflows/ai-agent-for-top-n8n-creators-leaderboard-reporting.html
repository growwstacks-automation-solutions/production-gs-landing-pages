What This Workflow Does

Managing a growing community like n8n's requires visibility into who's contributing valuable content and which workflows are resonating with users. Manually tracking these metrics is time-consuming and often inconsistent. This AI-powered automation solves that problem by systematically gathering community statistics, processing the data, and generating insightful reports.

The workflow fetches creator and workflow data from GitHub repositories, processes key metrics like unique weekly inserters and visitors, ranks contributors by impact, and uses AI to generate comprehensive Markdown reports. These reports highlight top performers, identify trending content, and provide actionable insights that help community managers foster engagement and recognize valuable contributions.

How It Works

The automation follows a structured process to transform raw data into valuable community intelligence.

1. Data Collection & Retrieval

The workflow begins by fetching JSON data files containing creator and workflow statistics from predefined GitHub repositories. Using HTTP Request nodes, it accesses these files dynamically based on global variables that can be configured for different time periods or data sources.

2. Data Processing & Normalization

Once retrieved, the data is parsed and separated into distinct streams for creators and workflows. The system processes key metrics including unique weekly and monthly inserters/visitors, contribution frequency, and workflow adoption rates. This normalization ensures consistent comparison across different data points.

3. Ranking & Filtering

The workflow sorts creators by their weekly insert counts and workflows by their popularity metrics. It then selects the top 10 creators and top 50 workflows for detailed analysis, applying business logic to ensure meaningful comparisons and filter out incomplete or irrelevant data.

4. AI-Powered Report Generation

Using AI tools like GPT-4 or Google Gemini, the system generates comprehensive Markdown reports that summarize trends, highlight contributors, and analyze workflow statistics. The reports include formatted tables with detailed metrics and narrative insights explaining why certain workflows or creators are performing well.

5. Distribution & Sharing

Completed reports are saved locally or uploaded to cloud storage like Google Drive. They can also be distributed via email, Telegram, or other communication channels, ensuring community managers and stakeholders receive timely insights without manual intervention.

6. Scheduled Automation

A Schedule Trigger node ensures the workflow runs daily, weekly, or on any custom schedule, keeping reports up-to-date with the latest community activity and freeing team members from repetitive data collection tasks.

Pro tip: Configure the AI model to focus on specific insights like emerging contributor patterns or seasonal workflow trends. This customization makes the reports more actionable for your community strategy.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this community analytics automation running in your environment with these simple steps:

  1. Download and Import: Click the download button above to get the JSON file, then import it into your n8n instance via the "Import from File" option.
  2. Configure Data Sources: Update the HTTP Request nodes with your specific GitHub repository URLs or API endpoints containing community statistics.

Effective community analytics integration involves connecting APIs from platforms like GitHub, community forums, content repositories, and engagement tools. The data is normalized, deduplicated, and enriched with contextual information, then processed through a central workflow that applies business logic before generating unified reports and insights.

A well-designed integration might combine GitHub contribution data, forum engagement metrics, workflow adoption statistics, and support ticket resolution rates to create a comprehensive view of each community member's impact. This holistic approach prevents siloed insights and provides a complete picture of community health.

Key community health metrics include contributor growth rate, content creation frequency, engagement ratios (views vs. contributions), retention of active members, diversity of contributions, and quality indicators like adoption or reuse of shared workflows. The most valuable reports combine quantitative metrics with qualitative insights about community dynamics.

Beyond basic numbers, look at metrics like "time to first contribution" (how long before new members become active), "contribution depth" (are members creating simple or complex content), and "network effects" (how contributions spark further community activity). These deeper indicators often reveal more about community sustainability than surface-level counts.

Community analytics should run weekly for operational insights (top contributors, popular content), monthly for trend analysis and recognition programs, and quarterly for strategic health assessments. Automated workflows can generate these reports on schedule while allowing for on-demand reporting when specific insights are needed.
