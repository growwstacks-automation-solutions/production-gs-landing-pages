What This Workflow Does

Tax season panic over missing receipts is a universal small business headache. This workflow solves it by creating a fully automated, intelligent receipt management system. It monitors your Gmail for receipts, uses AI to understand them, logs the key details in a Google Sheet for reporting, and safely archives the original PDF in Google Drive.

The system turns chaotic email attachments into structured, searchable financial data. No more manual downloading, data entry, or frantic searches for proof of purchase. Everything is captured, categorized, and stored the moment it arrives in your inbox, giving you a real-time view of expenses and a bulletproof audit trail.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical, multi-step process to ensure accuracy and completeness.

1. Trigger & Collection

The workflow runs on a schedule (e.g., daily at 8 AM). It searches your Gmail for unread emails with a specific label like "Receipt" or matching certain senders. It identifies emails with attachments, typically PDFs or images containing receipt information.

2. Data Extraction & AI Processing

The attached receipt file is downloaded. An AI model (like OpenAI) reads the text content and extracts structured data: date, merchant name, total amount, tax, subtotal, category, and a brief description. This transforms an unstructured document into clean, usable data fields.

3. Storage & Logging

The original PDF is uploaded to a designated folder in your Google Drive, organized by date or merchant. Simultaneously, the extracted data fields are appended as a new row in your master Google Sheet. Each row includes a clickable link back to the stored PDF in Drive.

4. Cleanup & Completion

Finally, the processed email is marked as read in Gmail to prevent duplicate processing. The workflow logs its activity and awaits the next trigger, creating a continuous, hands-off system.

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this template in under 15 minutes.

  1. Download & Import: Click the download button above to get the JSON file. In your n8n instance, go to Workflows > Import from File and select the downloaded template.
  2. Configure Credentials: In the n8n credentials panel, set up OAuth2 connections for Gmail, Google Sheets, and Google Drive. Add your API key for OpenAI or another AI service.
  3. Set Your Targets: In the "Upload File" node, select your target Google Drive folder ID. In the "Append to Google Sheet" node, select your spreadsheet and worksheet.
  4. Adjust the Trigger: In the "Schedule Trigger" node, set your preferred run frequency (e.g., daily, hourly). In the "Gmail" node, confirm the search query matches your labeling system (e.g., `label:receipt is:unread`).
  5. Test & Activate: Use n8n's "Test Workflow" feature on a single email. Check that data appears in your sheet and the PDF is in Drive. Once confirmed, activate the workflow.
