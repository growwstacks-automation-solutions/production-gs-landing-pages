AI-Curated Indian Marketing Newsletter with RSS & Gmail

What This Workflow Does

Staying updated with Indian marketing and advertising news is time‑consuming. Manually scanning multiple publications, filtering irrelevant stories, and compiling a digest can take hours each week. This workflow automates the entire process, delivering a curated, AI‑powered newsletter directly to your inbox (or your team’s) every day.

It connects to RSS feeds from leading Indian marketing sources like Campaign India and Economic Times BrandEquity, uses an AI model to evaluate each article’s relevance, stores the selected stories, and then builds and sends a professional HTML email via Gmail—all on a schedule you set. The result is a consistent, high‑quality newsletter that keeps you informed without any manual effort.

Beyond saving time, this automation ensures you never miss important updates about brand launches, campaign announcements, digital media shifts, or industry trends. It’s perfect for marketing agencies, brand managers, and business leaders who need to stay ahead in India’s fast‑moving advertising landscape.

How It Works

The workflow runs on a daily schedule, but you can adjust the timing to match your preferred delivery window (e.g., 7:30 PM IST for an end‑of‑day recap).

Step 1: Fetch & Merge RSS Feeds

The workflow begins by pulling the latest articles from your configured RSS feeds. It uses the RSS Read node to ingest content from Campaign India, Economic Times BrandEquity, and any other marketing publications you add. These articles are merged into a single stream for processing.

Step 2: AI Relevance Filtering

Each article is passed to an AI node (like OpenAI’s GPT) with a custom prompt that evaluates whether the story is relevant to Indian marketing and advertising. The AI scores articles based on criteria you define—such as brand launches, campaign announcements, or digital media changes—and only the most meaningful updates proceed.

Step 3: Store in Data Table

Approved articles are stored in an n8n Data Table. This acts as a temporary holding area, ensuring no duplicate stories are sent and allowing you to review the selection before sending. The table automatically clears old entries after sending to keep the next day’s digest fresh.

Step 4: Build HTML Newsletter

The workflow constructs a clean, mobile‑friendly HTML email using the stored articles. It formats headlines, summaries, and links into a professional layout. You can customize the template to match your branding, add your logo, or include additional sections like a personal note or upcoming events.

Step 5: Send via Gmail (with SMTP Fallback)

Finally, the email is delivered via Gmail’s API. The template includes a built‑in fallback: if Gmail encounters an issue (like rate limits), the workflow automatically switches to a configured SMTP server (e.g., SendGrid, Mailgun, or your company’s email server) to guarantee delivery.

What You’ll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self‑hosted).
  2. Gmail account with API access enabled (or an SMTP service like SendGrid/Mailgun for the fallback).
  3. OpenAI API key (or another AI model provider) for the relevance filtering.
  4. RSS feed URLs for your preferred Indian marketing publications (the template includes examples).
  5. Recipient email address(es)—you can send to yourself, a team distribution list, or a subscriber list.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated “sender” email address (e.g., newsletter@yourdomain.com) to improve deliverability and branding. Set up SPF/DKIM records if using your own domain with SMTP.

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this workflow in under 10 minutes.

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure the RSS nodes by replacing the example feed URLs with your preferred sources (Campaign India, BrandEquity, etc.).
  3. Set up your AI credentials in the OpenAI (or equivalent) node. Adjust the filtering prompt if you want different criteria.
  4. Connect your Gmail account in the Gmail node and enter the recipient email address. Optionally, add SMTP credentials as a fallback.
  5. Adjust the schedule trigger to your desired time (default is daily at 7:30 PM IST).
  6. Test the workflow with a manual trigger to verify the email format and delivery, then activate the schedule.
