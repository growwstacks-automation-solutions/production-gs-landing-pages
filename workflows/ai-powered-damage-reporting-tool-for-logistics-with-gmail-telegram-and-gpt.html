What This Workflow Does

Manual damage reporting in warehouses is slow, error-prone, and creates paperwork bottlenecks. This automation transforms that process into a seamless digital flow. Warehouse operators simply take photos of damaged pallets using Telegram on their mobile devices. The workflow automatically analyzes the images with AI, extracts critical information, generates professional reports, and emails them to relevant teams—all without manual data entry.

The system creates a complete digital audit trail for quality control, supplier claims, and operational improvement. It standardizes reporting across shifts and locations, ensures nothing gets lost or delayed, and gives management real-time visibility into damage incidents. This isn't just about replacing paper; it's about creating intelligent, data-driven logistics operations.

How It Works

1. Photo Submission via Telegram

A warehouse operator encounters a damaged pallet. They open the Telegram bot on their phone, take a clear photo of the damage, and send it. The workflow instantly detects this new message and downloads the image for processing.

2. AI Damage Analysis with GPT-4o

The downloaded image is sent to OpenAI's GPT-4o Vision model. The AI analyzes the photo, identifying damage type (crush, tear, water), severity (minor, major, critical), affected areas, and potential causes. It generates a structured damage assessment.

3. Pallet Identification

The bot then asks the operator for a photo of the pallet's barcode or label. A second AI model (GPT-4o Mini) extracts the pallet number from this image, linking the damage report to the specific inventory item.

4. Report Generation & Email Dispatch

The workflow combines the damage analysis and pallet ID into a professional HTML email report. This includes timestamps, location data (from Telegram metadata), severity ratings, recommended actions, and clear photos. The report is automatically sent via Gmail to predefined recipients like quality managers, logistics supervisors, and procurement teams.

5. Operator Confirmation

The Telegram bot sends a confirmation message to the operator, acknowledging successful report submission and providing a reference number. The entire process completes in under two minutes.

What You'll Need

  1. Telegram Bot Token: Create a bot via @BotFather on Telegram and obtain the API token.
  2. OpenAI API Key: An active account with GPT-4o Vision access (costs apply per image analysis).
  3. Gmail Account: A Google account with SMTP access enabled (or use Gmail OAuth).
  4. n8n Instance: A running n8n installation (cloud or self-hosted).
  5. Recipient List: Email addresses for quality, logistics, and procurement teams who should receive reports.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n instance via the workflow import function.
  2. Configure Telegram: In the "Telegram Trigger" node, paste your bot token. Start the workflow and send /start to your bot to activate.
  3. Set Up OpenAI: Add your OpenAI API key to both AI nodes. Adjust the system prompts if you have specific damage classification needs.
  4. Connect Gmail: Authenticate the "Send Email" node with your Gmail account. Update the "To" field with your recipient email addresses.
  5. Test Thoroughly: Send a test photo through Telegram. Verify the AI analysis is accurate and the email arrives correctly formatted.
  6. Train Your Team: Brief warehouse staff on the new process—how to take clear photos, what the bot expects, and confirmation messages.
