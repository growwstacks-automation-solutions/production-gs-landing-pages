What This Workflow Does

This automation creates an intelligent knowledge assistant that answers questions by searching through your Google Sheets data using AI. Instead of manually digging through spreadsheets or documents, employees or customers can ask natural language questions and get instant, accurate answers pulled directly from your curated knowledge base.

The workflow connects three powerful technologies: Google Sheets as your editable knowledge repository, OpenAI for understanding questions and generating responses, and Supabase Vector Search for finding the most relevant information. It's like having a 24/7 expert who knows everything in your company documentation and can explain it in plain language.

Businesses use this to reduce support ticket volume, onboard new employees faster, provide instant answers to common customer questions, and ensure consistent information sharing across teams. The system learns from your data and improves as your knowledge base grows.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical pipeline that transforms raw spreadsheet data into intelligent responses.

Step 1: Data Preparation & Vector Storage

Your Google Sheets content is processed into smaller, searchable chunks. Each chunk is converted into a numerical vector (embedding) using OpenAI's embedding model. These vectors capture the semantic meaning of your content and are stored in Supabase's vector database for lightning-fast similarity searches.

Step 2: Question Processing

When a question arrives (via webhook, form submission, or scheduled trigger), it's also converted into a vector. The system then searches Supabase for the most semantically similar content chunks from your knowledge base, returning the top matches ranked by relevance.

Step 3: AI Response Generation

The retrieved knowledge chunks are fed to OpenAI's language model along with the original question. The AI synthesizes a coherent, helpful answer based strictly on the provided information, ensuring accuracy and preventing hallucinations. The response can be formatted, enriched, and delivered through your preferred channel.

Step 4: Delivery & Logging

The final answer is sent to the requester via email, Slack, or integrated directly into your application. The system logs all queries and responses for continuous improvement, allowing you to identify knowledge gaps and frequently asked questions.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google Sheets document containing your knowledge base (FAQs, documentation, policies)
  2. An OpenAI API key (for embeddings and chat completions)
  3. A Supabase account with the Vector extension enabled
  4. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with workflow execution capabilities
  5. Basic understanding of how to set up API credentials in n8n

Pro tip: Start with a focused Google Sheet containing 50-100 of your most frequently asked questions or critical procedures. This gives the AI enough context to provide valuable answers while keeping initial setup simple.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure credentials: Set up connections for Google Sheets, OpenAI, and Supabase in n8n's credentials management.
  3. Connect your data: Update the Google Sheets node with your actual spreadsheet URL and range.
  4. Set up Supabase: Create a table with a vector column in your Supabase project and note the connection details.
  5. Test the flow: Trigger the workflow manually with a sample question to verify all connections work.
  6. Deploy: Set the workflow to active and connect it to your preferred trigger (webhook, schedule, or form submission).
