What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious problem of manually tracking news relevant to your business. Instead of spending hours each week scanning websites, blogs, and news outlets, this workflow uses AI to search the web, curate articles, store them in a database, and alert your team.

It’s designed for professionals who need to stay informed about industry trends, competitor movements, market shifts, or specific topics of interest. The system runs on a schedule you set (daily, weekly, etc.), ensuring you receive a consistent feed of structured information without any manual intervention.

The value is clear: reclaim time, reduce human error, centralize knowledge, and act on insights faster. Whether you’re in marketing, research, finance, or leadership, this automation turns news monitoring from a chore into a strategic asset.

How It Works

Step 1: Schedule Trigger

The workflow begins with a schedule trigger. You configure it to run at intervals that match your needs—perhaps every morning at 9 AM or once a week on Monday. This automation kicks off the entire process without any manual initiation.

Step 2: Define Your Search Topics

A “Set” node allows you to specify the topics you want to monitor. You can list keywords, phrases, or even complex queries. You also set the freshness parameter (e.g., “last 7 days”) to ensure you get recent news.

Step 3: AI-Powered Web Search via Linkup

The workflow sends your topics to Linkup’s API. Linkup uses AI to understand context and intent, searching across the web for relevant articles. It returns a list with each article’s title, URL, summary, and publication date—already structured for easy handling.

Step 4: Store Articles in Airtable

The workflow loops through each article found and creates a new record in your Airtable base. Each record includes the extracted details, organizing everything in a clean, filterable database that your team can access and analyze.

Step 5: Notify Your Team via Slack

Once all articles are stored, a final notification is sent to a Slack channel you choose. The message confirms the process is complete and reports how many articles were found, closing the loop and ensuring immediate awareness.

What You'll Need

  2. A Linkup API key (free plan includes €5 monthly credits, sufficient for ~1,000 runs).
  3. An Airtable account with a base and table ready to receive news records.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Adjust the Schedule: In the Schedule Trigger node, set your preferred frequency and time.
  3. Set Your Topics: Replace the example topics in the “Set news parameters” node with your own keywords.
  4. Connect Accounts: Add your Linkup API key, select your Airtable base/table, and choose your Slack channel in the respective nodes.
  5. Activate: Toggle the workflow to “Active” and let it run. Monitor the first execution to ensure everything works as expected.

Pro tip: Start with broad topics and refine based on the results you receive. Use Airtable’s filtering to categorize articles by relevance, and consider adding an AI sentiment analysis node later to gauge positive/negative coverage.
