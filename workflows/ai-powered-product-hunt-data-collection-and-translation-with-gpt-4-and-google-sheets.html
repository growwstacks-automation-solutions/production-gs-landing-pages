What This Workflow Does

Manual market research is time-consuming and inconsistent. Teams spend hours each week browsing Product Hunt, copying data, translating foreign language posts, and trying to organize everything in spreadsheets. This process is prone to human error, misses important launches during busy periods, and creates data silos that are hard to analyze.

This automation solves that by creating a systematic, always-on intelligence gathering system. It connects directly to Product Hunt's API, fetches new product launches daily, uses AI to translate and categorize each entry, and stores everything in a structured Google Sheet. What used to be a fragmented manual process becomes a reliable, searchable database that grows automatically.

The result is consistent market intelligence without the manual labor. You get a living directory of competitors, emerging technologies, and market trends that updates itself while your team focuses on strategic analysis rather than data collection.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Trigger & Data Fetching

A schedule trigger runs the workflow daily (configurable to any frequency). It connects to Product Hunt's GraphQL API using secure authentication and retrieves the latest product posts with their details—name, description, votes, comments, maker information, and launch date.

2. Data Processing & Deduplication

Each product entry is processed through a code node that standardizes the data structure. The workflow then checks your existing Google Sheet to see if this product has already been recorded, preventing duplicates and ensuring your database remains clean and unique.

3. AI-Powered Enrichment

For new products, the workflow sends the content to an AI agent (GPT-4 or compatible). The AI performs multiple enrichment tasks simultaneously: translating non-English text to your preferred language, extracting key technologies mentioned, generating a concise summary, and classifying the product into functional categories like "Developer Tools" or "Marketing Automation."

4. Category Management & Storage

Enriched categories are matched against a master dictionary in a separate Google Sheet tab to maintain consistency. New categories are added to this dictionary automatically. Finally, the complete enriched product entry—with original data plus AI-generated insights—is appended to your main "Tools" sheet, ready for analysis, filtering, or integration with other systems.

What You'll Need

  1. Product Hunt OAuth2 credentials – Required for API access to fetch product data.
  2. Google Sheets account – Where the collected data will be stored and organized.
  3. OpenAI API access (or compatible LLM service) – Powers the translation and enrichment steps.
  4. n8n instance – Either self-hosted or n8n.cloud account to run the workflow.
  5. Basic spreadsheet setup – A Google Sheet with "Tools" and "Categories" tabs (the workflow can create these).

Pro tip: Start with a weekly schedule to test the workflow, then increase to daily once you confirm the data quality matches your needs. This prevents overwhelming your sheet during initial setup.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure credentials for Product Hunt, Google Sheets, and OpenAI in n8n's credentials management.
  3. Update the Google Sheet ID in the Google Sheets nodes to point to your destination spreadsheet.
  4. Adjust the schedule trigger to your preferred frequency (daily recommended).
  5. Test with manual execution to verify data flows correctly before enabling the schedule.
  6. Review the initial data in your Google Sheet and customize the AI prompts if needed for your specific use case.
  7. Activate the workflow and let it run automatically according to your schedule.
