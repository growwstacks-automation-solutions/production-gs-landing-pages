What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the critical challenge of staying current with vendor compliance changes across hundreds of suppliers. It combines AI analysis of vendor policy documents with intelligent scanning of compliance RSS feeds to provide a comprehensive risk monitoring system.

The automation continuously checks vendor websites for policy updates, extracts key clauses using natural language processing, and compares them against your compliance requirements. Simultaneously, it monitors regulatory RSS feeds for changes impacting your vendor relationships, applying the same risk scoring framework to both data streams.

How It Works

1. Policy Document Monitoring

The workflow checks each vendor's policy webpage at configurable intervals (daily/weekly). When changes are detected, it extracts the full text and sends it to the AI analysis engine.

2. RSS Feed Processing

Configured compliance feeds are scanned for new entries. Relevant articles are filtered based on your industry keywords and processed through the same risk assessment pipeline as policy documents.

3. AI Risk Analysis

An AI model analyzes the text for risk factors like weakened security terms, reduced SLAs, or non-compliant data handling clauses. It outputs structured risk scores across multiple dimensions.

4. Integrated Scoring

Policy changes and regulatory updates are evaluated against your custom risk matrix. The system generates weighted scores that reflect your organization's specific risk tolerance priorities.

5. Alerting & Reporting

High-risk findings trigger immediate notifications while all results log to your vendor management system. The workflow includes pre-built integrations for common platforms.

Pro tip: Configure different risk thresholds for critical vendors versus general suppliers to focus review efforts where they matter most.

Effective risk scoring combines quantitative factors (policy change frequency, compliance violations) with qualitative AI analysis (sentiment, ambiguity). Scores should weight factors based on your risk appetite - a healthcare provider might prioritize data security changes while a manufacturer focuses on supply chain continuity risks.

The template includes a baseline scoring model that evaluates impact (how much risk the change introduces) and velocity (how quickly the risk is evolving). You can adjust weights for different vendor tiers - perhaps applying stricter scoring to cloud providers handling customer data than office supply vendors.

  • Combine likelihood and impact assessments
  • Weight scores by vendor criticality
  • Include both objective and subjective factors
