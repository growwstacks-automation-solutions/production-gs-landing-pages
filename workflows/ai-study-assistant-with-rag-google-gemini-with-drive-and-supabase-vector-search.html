AI Study Assistant with RAG: Chat with Your Google Drive Documents

Free n8n template that builds a conversational AI assistant powered by Google Gemini, searching your documents with Supabase vector search.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the frustrating problem of manually searching through dozens of documents, research papers, or training materials to find specific information. Instead of scrolling through files, you get a conversational AI assistant that understands natural language questions and provides accurate answers drawn directly from your Google Drive content.

The system uses Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture, which combines the power of Google Gemini's AI with semantic search capabilities. When you ask a question, it doesn't just guess—it actually searches your documents for relevant information, then synthesizes that information into a clear, helpful response. This eliminates AI hallucinations and ensures every answer is grounded in your actual materials.

Whether you're a student preparing for exams, a researcher managing papers, or a business professional building a knowledge base, this workflow transforms static documents into an interactive knowledge partner available 24/7.

How It Works

The system consists of two interconnected workflows that work together seamlessly:

1. Document Indexing Pipeline

This sub-workflow automatically processes your Google Drive folders. When you provide a Drive link, it fetches all files, converts them to plain text, generates 768-dimensional embeddings using Google Gemini's embedding model, and stores everything in a Supabase vector database. This creates a searchable knowledge base of your documents.

2. Study Chat Agent

The main workflow provides a conversational chat interface. When you ask a question, it converts your query to an embedding, searches the Supabase vector database for semantically similar document chunks, retrieves the most relevant content, and uses Google Gemini 2.5 Pro to generate a natural, context-aware answer. It maintains conversation history and can even handle mathematical calculations.

3. Automated Link Detection

The system intelligently detects Google Drive links shared in chat. When you paste a Drive folder URL, it automatically triggers the indexing pipeline, processes the documents, and adds them to the knowledge base—all without manual intervention.

What You'll Need

  1. Google Drive Account with documents you want to query (free Google account works)
  2. Google Gemini API Key (available through Google AI Studio, includes free tier)
  3. Supabase Account with Postgres database (free plan sufficient for most use cases)
  4. n8n Instance (self-hosted or n8n.cloud account)
  5. 15-20 minutes for initial setup and configuration

Pro tip: Start with a small test folder of 5-10 documents to verify everything works before indexing large collections. The free tiers of Gemini and Supabase are more than enough for personal or small team use.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Configure Google Drive OAuth in the Drive nodes to connect to your account
  3. Add your Google Gemini API key to both the embedding and chat model nodes
  4. Set up Supabase credentials in the Vector Store and Postgres nodes
  5. Test the webhook by accessing the provided URL to open the chat interface
  6. Share a Google Drive folder link in chat to trigger automatic indexing
  7. Start asking questions about your documents and receive AI-powered answers

RAG combines a search system with a generative AI model. Instead of asking an AI to recall information from its training, RAG first searches your specific documents (like those in Google Drive) for relevant content, then uses that content to generate an accurate, up-to-date answer.

This prevents AI hallucinations and ensures answers are based on your actual materials, making it perfect for studying, research, and internal knowledge bases. Traditional chatbots might make up plausible-sounding but incorrect information, while RAG systems always reference your source documents.

  • Eliminates factual errors from AI training data limitations
  • Works with your private, proprietary documents
  • Provides citations back to source material

Vector search converts text into numerical representations (embeddings) that capture semantic meaning. When you ask a question, it's also converted to a vector. The system then finds document chunks with the most similar vectors, not just keyword matches.

This allows the AI to understand questions like "explain the main arguments" and find relevant passages even if those exact words aren't in the text, enabling much more natural conversation with your documents. It understands concepts and relationships, not just vocabulary.

  • Finds conceptually similar content, not just keyword matches
  • Handles synonyms and related concepts automatically
  • Returns ranked results by relevance score
