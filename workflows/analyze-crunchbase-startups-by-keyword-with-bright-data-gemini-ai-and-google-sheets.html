What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the challenge of manually researching startups in specific markets or technologies. Venture capitalists, corporate strategists, and business development teams spend countless hours identifying relevant companies, analyzing their funding history, and assessing competitive positioning.

The workflow automatically extracts startup data from Crunchbase using Bright Data's web scraping capabilities, enriches the information with Gemini AI analysis, and organizes everything in Google Sheets for easy review. It transforms weeks of manual research into minutes of automated processing.

How It Works

1. Keyword-based startup discovery

The workflow begins by searching Crunchbase for startups matching your specified keywords, industries, or locations. Bright Data handles the web scraping at scale while avoiding detection.

2. Data enrichment with Gemini AI

Each startup profile gets analyzed by Gemini AI to assess business viability, competitive advantages, and market positioning. The AI adds insights that aren't explicitly stated in the raw data.

3. Competitive benchmarking

The system compares funding patterns, hiring trends, and technology adoption across similar startups to highlight market leaders and emerging threats.

4. Google Sheets integration

All analyzed data flows into a structured Google Sheet with separate tabs for different analysis categories. The sheet includes dynamic filters and summary dashboards.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n account (self-hosted or cloud)

  4. Google Sheets with edit permissions
  5. List of target keywords or industries to analyze

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Connect your Bright Data credentials in the web scraping node
  3. Configure your Gemini API key in the AI analysis nodes
  4. Paste your target Google Sheet URL in the output node
  5. Set your search parameters (keywords, locations, industries)
  6. Run the workflow and monitor the execution

Pro tip: Schedule this workflow to run weekly to maintain an always-current startup database. Add conditional alerts for when competitors raise funding or expand to new markets.
