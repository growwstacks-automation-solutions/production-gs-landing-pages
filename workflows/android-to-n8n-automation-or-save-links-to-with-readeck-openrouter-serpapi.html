What This Workflow Does

If you're drowning in browser tabs, constantly copying links from your phone to your computer, or struggling to find that article you saved months ago, this automation solves it. This workflow creates a seamless bridge between your Android device and a self-hosted bookmarking platform like Readeck.

Instead of manual copy-pasting, you simply share a link from any app on your phone. The automation captures the URL, uses AI to analyze the content and generate smart tags (like "Tech Tutorials" or "Productivity Tools"), and saves it with clean metadata to your personal knowledge base. It turns chaotic bookmarking into a streamlined, searchable system that works silently in the background.

How It Works

1. Trigger from Android

Using the "HTTP Shortcuts" app (or similar), you create a one-tap shortcut in your share menu. When you find an article, video, or social post worth saving, you tap "Share" and select this shortcut. It sends the URL and page title directly to your n8n webhook.

2. AI Processing & Cleaning

The n8n workflow receives the data, which can often be messy—especially from social media apps. It extracts the clean URL and title, then sends the content to an AI model (like ChatGPT-4 via OpenRouter) to analyze the context and generate relevant tags automatically.

3. Save to Readeck

Finally, the workflow takes the processed data—clean URL, title, and AI-generated tags—and saves it as a structured bookmark in your self-hosted Readeck instance. The entry is immediately searchable and organized within your personal knowledge base.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for researchers, content creators, students, professionals, and anyone who consumes information across devices. If you regularly save articles for later reading, collect references for projects, or want to build a personal library of valuable resources, this workflow eliminates the friction. It's particularly valuable for teams who want a centralized, tagged repository of research links without relying on cloud-based services that compromise privacy.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow.
  2. The "HTTP Shortcuts" app installed on your Android device (free).
  3. A self-hosted Readeck instance or similar bookmarking platform that offers an API.
  4. API keys for OpenRouter (or another AI provider) and SerpAPI (for enhanced metadata if needed).
  5. Basic comfort with n8n to import the JSON template and configure connection credentials.

Pro tip: If you don't want to self-host Readeck, you can modify this workflow to save bookmarks to other services like Notion, Airtable, or even a simple Google Sheet. The core value is the AI tagging and one-tap saving from your phone.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automated bookmarking system running in under 30 minutes:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure the nodes by adding your API credentials for OpenRouter, SerpAPI (optional), and your Readeck instance.
  3. Set up the webhook trigger in n8n and note the unique webhook URL it generates.
  4. Install and configure "HTTP Shortcuts" on your Android phone. Create a new shortcut that sends a POST request to your n8n webhook URL with the shared URL and title as parameters.
  5. Add the shortcut to your share menu and test it by sharing a link from your browser or any app.
