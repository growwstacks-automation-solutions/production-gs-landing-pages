Automate Arxiv Paper Summarization with ChatGPT

Free n8n workflow to automatically fetch, process, and summarize Arxiv research papers. Save hours on academic research and literature reviews.

What This Workflow Does

Academic research is time-consuming. Researchers, students, and professionals spend hours each week reading lengthy papers to extract key insights. This workflow automates that entire process.

When you provide an Arxiv paper ID, this n8n automation fetches the paper, extracts its content, processes the abstract and main sections, and generates a structured summary using ChatGPT. The output includes clear sections: Abstract Overview, Introduction, Key Results, and Conclusion—giving you the essential information in minutes instead of hours.

The system handles the entire pipeline: from webhook trigger to cleaned text extraction, AI summarization, and structured output delivery. It's like having a research assistant who works 24/7.

How It Works

1. Paper Retrieval

The workflow starts with a webhook that accepts an Arxiv paper ID. It then sends an HTTP request to fetch the paper's HTML content directly from the Arxiv repository.

2. Content Extraction

Specialized nodes extract the abstract and main sections from the HTML, then split the content into manageable paragraphs for processing. The system cleans the text by removing unnecessary links and formatting artifacts.

3. AI Summarization

ChatGPT processes the extracted content through a summarization chain that identifies key points, methodologies, findings, and conclusions. The AI is prompted to maintain academic rigor while being concise.

4. Structured Output

The summarized content is aggregated and reorganized into a standardized format. A final information extraction node classifies everything into the four key sections researchers need most.

5. Delivery

The workflow responds to the original webhook with the complete structured summary, ready for integration with your research database, note-taking app, or team collaboration tools.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. OpenAI API key for ChatGPT access
  3. Webhook endpoint to trigger the workflow (can be from other apps like Slack, email, or a research dashboard)
  4. Arxiv paper IDs for the research you want summarized
  5. Basic n8n knowledge for importing and configuring workflows

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and save the JSON file.
  2. Import into n8n by going to your n8n dashboard, clicking "Workflows," then "Import from file."
  3. Configure credentials by adding your OpenAI API key in the ChatGPT node settings.
  4. Test the webhook by copying the webhook URL from the first node and sending a test request with a sample Arxiv paper ID.
  5. Customize output by adjusting the prompt templates in the summarization chain if you need different summary formats.
  6. Connect to your tools by adding nodes to send summaries to Notion, Google Docs, Slack, or your research database.
  7. Schedule automation by adding a schedule trigger if you want regular summaries of new papers in your field.
