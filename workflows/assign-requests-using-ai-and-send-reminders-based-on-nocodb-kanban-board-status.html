What This Workflow Does

This automation transforms chaotic support ticket management into a streamlined, AI-assisted process. It's designed for support project managers who need to keep developers informed while automatically updating clients—especially crucial when managing SLA agreements.

The workflow creates a simple yet powerful incident management system using NocoDB's free Kanban board, enhanced with n8n's automation capabilities. It handles everything from initial ticket submission through AI-powered categorization to deadline tracking and automated notifications.

Instead of manually sorting emails and chasing updates, this system automatically categorizes incoming requests, assigns appropriate priority levels, tracks response times against SLAs, and sends reminders to both internal teams and external clients when deadlines approach.

How It Works

1. Incident Submission & AI Categorization

When a client submits an incident through the form, the workflow retrieves your incident definitions from NocoDB and sends both the request and definitions to an AI model. The AI compares the new request against your predefined categories and determines the appropriate priority level, then creates a structured ticket in your NocoDB tasks table.

2. Automated Ticket Creation & Assignment

The categorized ticket appears in your NocoDB Kanban board with all relevant details: expected category, assigned category, response and resolution deadlines, and assigned team member. The system automatically calculates deadlines based on your SLA definitions for each incident type.

3. Scheduled Status Monitoring

A schedule trigger runs regularly to check ticket statuses. If a ticket hasn't been picked up within the expected response time, or if resolution is approaching its deadline, the system automatically sends notifications via Slack or email to both the assigned developer and the client.

4. Multi-Channel Notifications

The workflow sends tailored messages: internal alerts to developers about overdue tasks, and professional status updates to clients to maintain transparency. All communication is logged back to the ticket for complete audit trails.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. NocoDB account with two tables created: Incident Definitions and Tasks
  3. OpenAI API key or compatible AI model access for categorization
  4. Slack workspace with appropriate channel/webhook permissions
  5. Email service (SMTP or transactional email provider like SendGrid)
  6. Basic understanding of your incident categories and SLA requirements

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in under an hour:

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the download button above
  2. Create your NocoDB tables with the exact column structure shown in the template documentation
  3. Connect your credentials for NocoDB, OpenAI, Slack, and your email service in n8n
  4. Customize incident definitions in your NocoDB table to match your support categories
  5. Test the workflow by submitting a sample incident through the form trigger
  6. Adjust notification timing to match your actual SLA requirements
  7. Deploy and monitor the first few days to ensure proper categorization and notifications

Pro tip: Start with broader incident categories and refine them as the AI learns from your corrections. The system becomes more accurate over time as it processes more tickets.
