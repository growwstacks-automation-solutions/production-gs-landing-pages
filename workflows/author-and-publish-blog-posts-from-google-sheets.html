What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious, time-consuming process of manually moving blog content from planning to publication. Most businesses spend hours each week copying content between spreadsheets, WordPress, and editing tools, dealing with formatting issues and missed deadlines.

The workflow creates a seamless pipeline where your Google Sheets content calendar becomes the single source of truth. It automatically processes each blog topic through planning, AI-assisted drafting, human review stages, and final publishing to WordPress—all while giving you control at every critical decision point.

Think of it as having a dedicated content assistant that never forgets a deadline, always follows your formatting guidelines, and ensures your publishing schedule runs like clockwork—while you focus on strategy and creativity.

How It Works

1. Content Planning in Google Sheets

You maintain a simple spreadsheet with blog topics, scheduled dates, status columns, and next actions. The workflow reads this sheet hourly to identify what needs processing.

2. AI-Assisted Draft Generation

For topics marked "plan" or "draft," the automation uses configured AI models (via OpenRouter) to generate content based on your prompts and guidelines stored in the same spreadsheet.

3. Human Review Checkpoints

After each automated stage, the workflow updates the spreadsheet and pauses, waiting for you to review the output and set the next action. This ensures quality control and brand alignment.

4. Automated WordPress Publishing

When content reaches "final" status and you set the action to "publish," the workflow uses WordPress REST API to create the post with proper categories, tags, and scheduled publication times.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with workflow execution capabilities
  2. Google Sheets access with editing permissions for your content calendar
  3. WordPress website with REST API enabled and application password configured
  4. OpenRouter account or similar AI service for content generation (optional for manual drafting)
  5. Basic understanding of your content workflow and review process

Pro tip: Start with a simple spreadsheet structure and expand as you get comfortable. The workflow is designed to be flexible—you can begin with just topic and date columns, then add complexity like keyword targets, tone guidelines, and custom prompts later.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the download button above
  2. Duplicate the sample Google Sheet and update the "Settings" node with your spreadsheet URL
  3. Configure credentials for Google Sheets, WordPress (app password), and OpenRouter if using AI
  4. Test with one blog topic marked as "idea" with "plan" action to see the full cycle
  5. Schedule the workflow to run hourly or adjust frequency based on your publishing volume
  6. Customize prompts and models in your spreadsheet's Config tab to match your brand voice
