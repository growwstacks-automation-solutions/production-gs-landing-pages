Auto-clip Long Videos into Viral Short Clips with Vizard AI

Automatically turn long YouTube videos into viral short clips using Vizard AI, then publish to social media and log results in Google Sheets. Save hours of manual editing.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a major pain point for content creators, marketers, and social media managers: manually editing long videos into short, engaging clips for platforms like TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. The process is time-consuming, inconsistent, and often misses the most viral-worthy moments.

The workflow automatically takes any long-form video URL (like a YouTube video, webinar recording, or podcast episode), sends it to Vizard AI for intelligent clipping, filters the generated clips based on a "viral score," publishes the best clips to your social channels, and logs all results in Google Sheets for performance tracking. It transforms hours of manual work into a fully automated, scalable content pipeline.

You can trigger it via a form submission, a scheduled timer, or whenever a new video is added to a playlist. It ensures your social feeds are consistently populated with high-potential short clips without any daily manual intervention.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger & Input Collection

The workflow starts by collecting a video URL and a target "viral score" threshold. This can come from a manual form (for ad-hoc processing), a schedule (for regular content batches), or an automated trigger when a new video is added to a designated source.

Step 2: Vizard AI Clipping Project Creation

The video URL is sent to Vizard AI, which analyzes the entire video to identify high-engagement segments. It creates a clipping project, using AI to detect key moments based on audio peaks, visual action, text overlays, and viewer retention patterns.

Step 3: Status Polling & Clip Retrieval

The workflow periodically checks the Vizard AI project status until processing is complete. Once ready, it retrieves all generated short clips along with their AI-assigned viral scores and metadata.

Step 4: Filtering & Selection

Using a "Filter" node, clips are evaluated against your configured viral score threshold. You can also set a maximum number of clips to publish per video to avoid flooding your channels. Only the highest-scoring, most promising clips proceed.

Step 5: Social Media Publishing

The selected clips are automatically published to your configured social platforms. The workflow can add platform-specific captions, hashtags, and scheduling. It handles the upload process to Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, or Twitter.

Step 6: Results Logging in Google Sheets

Every action—from project creation to clip publication—is logged in a Google Sheets spreadsheet. This creates a clear audit trail, tracks performance metrics over time, and allows for easy analysis of which source videos yield the best short clips.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure Credentials: Set up your Vizard AI API key, social media platform credentials, and Google Sheets connection in the respective n8n nodes.
  3. Adjust Thresholds: In the "Set Configuration" node, define your minimum viral score and maximum number of clips to publish per video.
  4. Set the Trigger: Choose your starting trigger—a form, schedule, or webhook—and connect it to your video source.
  5. Test & Activate: Run the workflow with a sample video URL to verify clipping, filtering, and publishing work correctly. Then activate it for automated execution.

Pro tip: Start with a higher viral score threshold (e.g., 85) to publish only the best clips initially. As you gather performance data, you can adjust the threshold to optimize output volume versus quality.
