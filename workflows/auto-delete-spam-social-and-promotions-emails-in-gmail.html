Auto-Delete SPAM, Social, and Promotions Emails in Gmail

Automatically clean your inbox by deleting unwanted emails every 3 days. Save time, reduce clutter, and focus on what matters.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common productivity drain: manually cleaning your Gmail inbox. SPAM, social notifications, and promotional emails constantly flood your inbox, distracting you from important communications and consuming valuable time.

The workflow automatically identifies and removes emails from Gmail's SPAM, Social, and Promotions categories every 3 days. It connects to your Gmail account via secure OAuth2, fetches emails from these categories, merges them into a single list, extracts individual email IDs, and systematically deletes each one. This scheduled maintenance ensures your inbox stays streamlined without any manual effort.

Beyond simple deletion, this automation creates a consistent hygiene system that adapts to your email volume. Whether you receive dozens or hundreds of unwanted emails weekly, the workflow handles them automatically, freeing you to focus on business-critical messages.

How It Works

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

The workflow activates automatically every 3 days using n8n's schedule trigger. This regular interval ensures consistent cleanup without overwhelming your system or missing new unwanted emails.

Step 2: Fetch Category Emails

Three separate Gmail nodes query your inbox for emails labeled as SPAM, CATEGORY_SOCIAL, and CATEGORY_PROMOTIONS. Each node retrieves relevant emails based on Gmail's native categorization system.

Step 3: Merge and Process

A merge node combines all fetched emails into a unified list. Then a split-out node isolates individual email IDs, preparing them for deletion while maintaining data integrity.

Step 4: Automated Deletion

The final Gmail nodes delete each identified email permanently. The process runs sequentially, ensuring complete removal of all targeted emails from your account.

What You'll Need

  1. A Gmail account with OAuth2 access enabled.
  2. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with the Gmail node installed.
  3. Valid Gmail OAuth2 credentials configured in n8n's credential management.
  4. Basic understanding of n8n's interface to import and activate workflows.
  5. Permission to delete emails from your account (standard user access).

Pro tip: Before activating, test the workflow with a small batch of emails to confirm categorization accuracy. Create a backup label if you're concerned about accidental deletions.
