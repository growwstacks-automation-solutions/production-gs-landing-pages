What This Workflow Does

Manual invoice processing is a time-consuming, error-prone bottleneck for finance teams. This intelligent automation transforms your accounts payable department by creating a seamless pipeline from invoice submission to payment. It monitors your Jotform for new invoice submissions, uses advanced AI to extract every data point from PDFs or images, analyzes the content for potential fraud, and then intelligently routes each invoice for approval, automatic payment, or investigation.

The system doesn't just read text—it understands context. It identifies vendor details, line items, totals, taxes, and dates with over 99% accuracy. More importantly, it applies a multi-layered fraud detection engine that checks for duplicates, suspicious amounts, new vendors, document anomalies, and timing patterns that often indicate fraudulent activity. This proactive approach prevents financial losses before they happen.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical, secure process that mirrors best-practice financial controls while adding AI-powered intelligence.

1. Invoice Capture & Submission

Vendors or staff submit invoices through a dedicated Jotform. The form captures the invoice document (PDF, image, scan) along with any supplementary details. The workflow automatically detects new submissions, triggering the processing pipeline instantly, eliminating manual email sorting and data entry queues.

2. AI-Powered Data Extraction

Using OpenAI's vision capabilities, the system performs intelligent OCR (Optical Character Recognition). It doesn't just find text—it comprehends the invoice structure, accurately extracting vendor name, address, invoice number, date, line item descriptions, quantities, unit prices, subtotals, tax calculations, and final amount. This data is structured into a clean, consistent format for validation.

3. Multi-Dimensional Fraud Analysis

This is the core intelligence layer. The AI evaluates 15+ risk factors: checking for duplicate invoice numbers or amounts, flagging round-number invoices, verifying vendor information against trusted databases, assessing document quality, identifying future-dated invoices, and detecting patterns like frequent small-amount submissions. Each invoice receives a risk score (0-100) with detailed reasoning.

4. Intelligent Routing & Approval

Based on the risk score and invoice amount, the system makes a decision: Low-risk invoices under a set threshold (e.g., $5,000) are auto-approved and sent directly to your accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero, etc.). Medium-risk or larger invoices are routed to the appropriate manager via email or Slack with all analysis attached. Critical high-risk invoices trigger immediate alerts to the CFO or finance lead for investigation, blocking any payment.

5. Audit Trail & Reconciliation

Every action, from submission to final disposition, is logged in a Google Sheet or database with timestamps, the AI's reasoning, the decision taken, and the responsible person. This creates a complete, searchable audit trail for compliance, month-end closing, and spend analysis.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this system running in your environment in under an hour.

  1. Import the Template: In your n8n instance, navigate to Workflows → Import from File. Paste the downloaded JSON. All nodes and configuration notes will appear.
  2. Configure Jotform Trigger: Connect your Jotform account and select the invoice submission form. Test the connection by submitting a sample invoice.
  3. Add OpenAI Credentials: Insert your OpenAI API key into the two AI nodes (OCR Extraction and Fraud Detection). Set your cost limits in the node settings.
  4. Connect Your Accounting System: In the HTTP Request or dedicated node (QuickBooks, Xero), add your accounting software API credentials. Map the extracted invoice fields to the correct ledger accounts and payment terms.
  5. Set Approval Rules: Update the email addresses in the "Send Email" nodes to your finance manager and CFO. Configure the amount thresholds in the "IF" node (default is $5,000). Add your Slack webhook URL if using alerts.
  6. Create the Audit Log: Create a new Google Sheet with columns for Timestamp, Invoice ID, Vendor, Amount, Risk Score, AI Reasoning, Status, and Approver. Connect the "Google Sheets" node to this sheet.
  7. Test Thoroughly: Run the workflow with 5-10 sample invoices (including a known duplicate) to verify extraction accuracy, fraud flagging, and routing logic before full deployment.
