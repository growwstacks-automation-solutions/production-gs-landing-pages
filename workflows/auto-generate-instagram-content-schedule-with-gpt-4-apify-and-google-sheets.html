Auto-generate Instagram Content Schedule with GPT-4 & Google Sheets

Transform blog posts into a complete Instagram content calendar with AI-generated captions, hashtags, and posting dates—fully automated.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

Creating consistent, engaging Instagram content is a time-consuming challenge for marketers, content creators, and small businesses. Between brainstorming ideas, writing captions, selecting hashtags, and maintaining a posting schedule, social media management can consume 10+ hours per week.

This automation solves that problem by connecting your blog content directly to Instagram planning. It scrapes your latest blog posts, analyzes them with AI, and generates a complete content calendar with optimized captions, visual descriptions, and strategic posting dates—all automatically exported to Google Sheets for easy review and editing.

The workflow ensures your Instagram content aligns with your brand pillars, maintains consistent messaging, and leverages your existing content investments, turning hours of manual work into a fully automated process that runs on schedule.

How It Works

1. Content Gathering & Strategy Input

The workflow begins by reading your content strategy from Google Sheets, including your brand pillars, posting objectives, frequency targets, and content formats. Simultaneously, it retrieves your latest blog posts (either from a pre-scraped list or via live web scraping with Apify) containing titles, URLs, and descriptions.

2. Data Merging & AI Processing

Blog content is matched with appropriate content pillars based on themes and keywords. The merged data is then sent to GPT-4 (or your preferred AI model), which generates Instagram-optimized captions, suggests visual concepts, creates relevant hashtag sets, and recommends posting dates based on your frequency requirements.

3. Holiday & Seasonal Adjustments

The AI checks if scheduled posts align with holidays or seasonal events and adjusts the tone and messaging accordingly. A reference library of successful post examples guides the AI to maintain your brand voice and content structure preferences.

4. Schedule Export & Review

All generated content—including dates, days of week, pillars, formats, captions, and hashtags—is written back to a dedicated "Output" tab in Google Sheets. This creates a ready-to-use content calendar that your team can review, tweak, and implement directly or connect to social media publishing tools.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for content creators who repurpose blog content across platforms, marketing teams managing multiple social media accounts, small business owners wearing multiple hats, photographers showcasing their work, nonprofits with limited resources, and agencies serving clients who need consistent content output. If you spend more than 5 hours per week on Instagram planning, this workflow will save you significant time.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. OpenAI API key (for GPT-4 access) or alternative AI model credentials
  3. Google Sheets account with a spreadsheet containing your content strategy columns
  4. Apify account (optional, for live blog scraping) or pre-prepared blog data
  5. Basic understanding of your Instagram content pillars and posting goals

Pro tip: Start with a small batch of blog posts (5-10) to test the AI's caption style and adjust your content strategy inputs before scaling to your full content library.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure Google Sheets nodes with your spreadsheet ID and authenticate with your Google account.
  3. Add your OpenAI API key to the AI agent node and test with a sample prompt.
  4. Set up your content strategy sheet with columns for Pillar, Objective, Frequency, Format, Structure, and Examples.
  5. Prepare your blog data sheet with URL, Title, Description, and Preferred Month columns.
  6. Run the workflow manually once to verify all connections and review the output in Google Sheets.
  7. Schedule the workflow to run weekly or monthly using n8n's scheduler trigger.
