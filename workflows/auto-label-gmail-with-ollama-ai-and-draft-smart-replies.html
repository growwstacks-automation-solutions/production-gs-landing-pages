What This Workflow Does

This workflow solves the universal problem of email overload. It automatically monitors your Gmail inbox, uses a local Ollama AI model to intelligently categorize each incoming email, applies the appropriate Gmail label, and even drafts context-aware replies for urgent messages—all while keeping your data completely private on your own infrastructure.

Instead of manually sorting through dozens or hundreds of daily emails, this system does the triage for you. It identifies what needs immediate attention, what can be scheduled for later, and what's just informational. The result is a clean, organized inbox where important communications never get buried in newsletters or promotional content.

The workflow also creates a searchable log in Google Sheets, giving you analytics on your email patterns and AI classification accuracy over time. This isn't just automation—it's intelligent email management that learns and adapts to how you communicate.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence that mimics how an efficient assistant would process your emails, but at scale and with perfect consistency.

1. Email Monitoring & Extraction

The workflow starts with a Gmail trigger that checks for new unread emails at regular intervals. When a new email arrives, it extracts all relevant data: sender, subject, body content, attachments, and metadata. This clean extraction ensures the AI has complete context for accurate classification.

2. AI-Powered Classification

Using your locally-running Ollama instance (with models like Mistral or Llama 3), the email content is analyzed and classified into one of six categories: Urgent, Action Required, Follow-up, Newsletter, Automated, or Spam/Promotional. The AI also assesses priority, sentiment, and generates a brief summary.

3. Smart Routing & Labeling

Based on the AI's classification, the email is routed through a switch node to the appropriate processing path. The workflow then applies the corresponding Gmail label (🔴 Urgent, 📋 Action Required, etc.), instantly organizing your inbox according to the email's importance and category.

4. Draft Generation & Logging

For high-priority emails (Urgent and Action Required), the AI generates a thoughtful reply draft that you can review and send from your Gmail Drafts folder. Meanwhile, every processed email is logged to Google Sheets with its classification details, creating an audit trail and performance analytics.

Pro tip: Start with a 30-minute polling interval, then adjust based on your email volume. For high-traffic inboxes, consider 15-minute intervals; for lighter use, hourly checks may suffice.

What You'll Need

  1. Gmail account with OAuth credentials (read, modify, and compose permissions)
  2. Ollama installed locally with at least one model pulled (Mistral, Llama 3, or Gemma 2 recommended)
  3. n8n instance (self-hosted recommended for local Ollama connectivity)
  4. Google Sheets access (optional, for logging and analytics)
  5. Pre-created Gmail labels: 🔴 Urgent, 📋 Action Required, 💬 Follow-up, 📰 Newsletter, 🤖 Automated, 🚫 Spam-Promo

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this email automation system in under an hour:

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file
  2. Configure Gmail OAuth by connecting your Google account with read, modify, and compose permissions
  3. Create the six Gmail labels manually in your inbox, then use n8n's "Get Many Labels" operation to find and update each label ID in the workflow
  4. Set up Ollama locally (ensure it's running at http://localhost:11434 with your preferred model pulled)
  5. Connect Google Sheets (optional) for logging by setting up OAuth and entering your spreadsheet ID
  6. Test with sample emails by sending yourself test messages of different types and verifying the classification accuracy
  7. Adjust the polling interval based on your email volume and activate the workflow

Pro tip: Before going live, run the workflow manually with 10-20 real emails from your inbox. Review the classifications and adjust the AI system prompt if needed to better match your communication patterns.
