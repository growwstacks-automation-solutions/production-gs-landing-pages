What This Workflow Does

This automation solves two common problems teams face with Zoom meetings: lost recordings and disorganized meeting records. Every time a Zoom meeting ends with recording enabled, this workflow automatically saves the video file to your specified Google Drive folder and creates a detailed log entry in Airtable.

The Airtable record includes key meeting details like date, duration, participants, and a direct link to the recording. This creates a centralized, searchable database of all your meetings that can be filtered by project, client, or topic.

How It Works

1. Trigger: Zoom meeting ends

The workflow activates when a Zoom meeting recording is ready. n8n checks Zoom's API for new completed recordings at regular intervals.

2. Download recording file

The system retrieves the recording file from Zoom's servers. For longer meetings with multiple files, it combines them into a single recording.

3. Upload to Google Drive

The recording gets uploaded to your specified Google Drive folder with a consistent naming convention that includes the meeting date and topic.

4. Create Airtable record

Key meeting details are extracted and logged in Airtable, including the Google Drive link, meeting participants, duration, and any custom fields you configure.

Pro tip: Add custom fields in Airtable for project names, client names, or meeting types to enable powerful filtering and reporting.
