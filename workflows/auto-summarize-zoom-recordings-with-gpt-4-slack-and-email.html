Auto-Summarize Zoom Recordings with GPT-4

Transform meeting recordings into actionable insights. Automatically send AI-generated summaries to Slack and email—no manual note-taking required.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

Meetings are essential for collaboration, but manually taking notes and distributing summaries consumes valuable time that could be spent on execution. This automation solves that problem by automatically processing Zoom recordings as soon as they're available.

The workflow uses OpenAI's GPT-4 to analyze meeting transcripts, extract key decisions, action items, and discussion points, then formats this information into professional summaries. These summaries are automatically delivered to designated Slack channels for immediate team visibility and sent via email for formal documentation and archival.

Beyond simple transcription, the AI identifies who committed to what actions, highlights unresolved questions, and categorizes discussion topics—transforming raw recordings into structured, actionable intelligence that keeps everyone aligned and accountable.

How It Works

1. Zoom Recording Completion Trigger

When a Zoom meeting recording is processed and ready, Zoom sends a webhook notification to the workflow. This trigger contains metadata about the meeting including participants, duration, and access links to both video and transcript files.

2. Data Extraction & Normalization

The workflow retrieves the meeting transcript from Zoom's API and structures the raw data. It extracts participant information, timestamps, and conversation segments, preparing a clean input for AI processing while preserving context about who said what.

3. AI-Powered Analysis with GPT-4

GPT-4 analyzes the complete transcript with specialized prompts designed for meeting summarization. It identifies key decisions, action items with assignees, discussion topics, unresolved questions, and follow-up requirements—creating a structured summary far beyond simple transcription.

4. Multi-Channel Distribution

The generated summary is formatted appropriately for each channel: a concise version for Slack with emoji indicators and @mentions for action items, and a more detailed version for email with proper formatting and attachments if needed.

5. Team Notification & Archival

The summary posts to your designated Slack channel, notifying relevant team members. Simultaneously, it sends an email to specified recipients (meeting participants, project managers, or entire teams) with the complete summary for record-keeping and reference.

What You'll Need

  1. Zoom Business/Enterprise account with recording enabled and webhook permissions
  2. OpenAI API key with GPT-4 access (or GPT-3.5-turbo as alternative)
  3. Slack workspace with appropriate channel access and bot permissions
  4. Email service (Gmail/Office 365 SMTP or n8n's built-in email node)
  5. n8n instance (self-hosted or cloud) with webhook exposure for Zoom triggers
  6. Basic meeting structure where participants are clearly identified for accurate action item assignment

Pro tip: Configure different AI prompts for different meeting types. Sales calls might focus on objections and next steps, while team syncs should emphasize action items and blockers. Create separate workflows or conditional logic to handle various meeting categories.

Quick Setup Guide

1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance using the import workflow feature.

2. Configure Zoom webhook in your Zoom App Marketplace dashboard, pointing the "recording.completed" event to your n8n webhook URL.

3. Add credentials for OpenAI (API key), Slack (OAuth or bot token), and your email service in n8n's credentials management.

4. Customize channels and recipients by updating the Slack channel ID and email recipient fields in the workflow.

5. Test with a sample recording by manually triggering the workflow or scheduling a test meeting with recording enabled.

6. Refine AI prompts based on your initial outputs to better match your team's terminology and summary preferences.

7. Activate the workflow and begin automating summaries for all future recorded meetings.
