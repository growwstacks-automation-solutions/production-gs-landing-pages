What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the challenge of making English video content accessible to Japanese-speaking audiences and team members. When new videos are uploaded to YouTube, the workflow automatically extracts the captions, sends them to DeepL for high-quality Japanese translation, then posts the translated text to designated Slack channels.

Global teams often struggle with language barriers in video content. Manual translation is time-consuming and delays information sharing. This workflow maintains content velocity while ensuring accurate Japanese translations reach the right people instantly through Slack, where collaboration happens.

How It Works

1. YouTube Trigger

The workflow activates whenever a new video is uploaded to your YouTube channel or when you add specific hashtags. It extracts the video metadata and available captions.

2. DeepL Translation

The English captions are sent to DeepL's API which returns professional-quality Japanese translations. The workflow handles text segmentation and formatting for optimal translation results.

3. Slack Notification

The translated text is formatted with the original video link and posted to your specified Slack channels. You can customize the message format and include additional context.

Pro tip: Add a custom emoji reaction requirement in Slack to trigger human review when translations need verification.
