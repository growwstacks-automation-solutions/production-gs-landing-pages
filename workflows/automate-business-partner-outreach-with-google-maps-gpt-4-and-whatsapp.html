What This Workflow Does

Manual partner outreach is time-consuming and inefficient. Sales teams spend hours searching Google Maps for potential partners, copying contact details into spreadsheets, and sending generic messages that get ignored. This process is repetitive, error-prone, and doesn't scale.

This n8n workflow automates the entire partner outreach funnel. It scrapes business listings from Google Maps, enriches missing data using AI, generates personalized outreach messages, and sends them via WhatsApp. The system also handles replies intelligently, qualifying leads and storing conversation history. What used to take days of manual work now happens automatically, allowing your team to focus on closing deals instead of finding them.

How It Works

1. Location-Based Scraping

The workflow starts by searching Google Maps for businesses in specific categories (restaurants, malls, bars) within your target locations. Using the Apify Google Maps Scraper, it extracts business names, addresses, phone numbers, ratings, and social media links.

2. AI Data Enrichment

Scraped data often has missing contact details. The workflow sends incomplete records to Perplexity AI to find missing phone numbers, email addresses, and key decision-makers. This enrichment step ensures you only reach out to contactable leads.

3. Personalized Message Generation

GPT-4 analyzes each business profile and creates customized outreach messages. The AI considers the business type, location, and available information to craft relevant opening lines that feel personal rather than robotic.

4. WhatsApp Delivery & Reply Handling

Messages are sent via WhatsApp using GOWA nodes, complete with typing indicators and read receipts. When recipients reply, an AI agent analyzes the response, determines intent, and follows up appropriately—whether that's scheduling a meeting or requesting more information.

5. Centralized Data Storage

All data flows into Google Sheets for easy tracking. Qualified leads are separated from general contacts, and conversation history is stored in PostgreSQL for future reference. A Pinecone knowledge base helps maintain context across conversations.
