What This Workflow Does

Manual cross-border tax compliance is a major pain point for finance teams. Juggling spreadsheets, databases, and constantly changing regulations leads to errors, missed deadlines, and potential penalties. This automation solves that by creating a seamless, end-to-end system for processing daily revenue transactions.

The workflow automatically pulls transaction data from sources like Google Sheets or a PostgreSQL database, validates and standardizes the information, applies the correct tax rules based on jurisdiction, calculates obligations, identifies anomalies, and generates ready-to-submit compliance reports. It transforms a chaotic, manual process into a reliable, automated pipeline that runs on schedule.

By connecting your financial data sources with calculation logic and alerting systems, you ensure accuracy, save dozens of hours each month, and gain peace of mind that your tax filings are timely and correct.

How It Works

The workflow is built as a logical sequence of steps that mirror a finance team's manual process, but executed automatically and flawlessly.

Step 1: Data Ingestion & Validation

The workflow is triggered on a daily schedule. It first retrieves new revenue transactions from your configured sources—typically a Google Sheet used by sales teams or a PostgreSQL database from your CRM. Each transaction is checked for required fields (date, amount, customer location, product type) and standardized into a common format.

Step 2: Tax Rule Application & Calculation

Each validated transaction is then assessed against your pre-configured tax rules. The workflow determines the applicable jurisdiction based on customer location, applies the correct tax rate (which can be stored and versioned within the workflow), and calculates the tax amount. Complex scenarios, like tax-exempt customers or specific product categories, are handled through conditional logic.

Step 3: Anomaly Detection & Alerting

Before finalizing, the workflow analyzes the batch for anomalies—unusually high transactions, missing customer tax IDs, or amounts that deviate from historical patterns. If any issues are detected, an immediate alert is sent to a designated Slack channel, allowing your team to review and intervene quickly.

Step 4: Report Generation & Archiving

All processed transactions and their calculated tax liabilities are compiled into a structured report. This report can be formatted as a CSV, PDF, or directly uploaded to a Google Drive folder for easy access. A summary of the day's processing, including total revenue and tax calculated, is also sent via Slack for visibility.

Step 5: Database Logging & Audit Trail

Every transaction and the result of its processing is written back to your PostgreSQL database. This creates a complete, timestamped audit trail that is invaluable for financial audits, quarterly reviews, or simply understanding your tax position over time.

What You'll Need

  1. A source of transaction data: A Google Sheet with a consistent format or access to a PostgreSQL database containing sales/order information.
  2. Tax rule configuration: Knowledge of the tax rates, thresholds, and rules for the jurisdictions you operate in.
  3. API access: Credentials (OAuth or service accounts) for Google Sheets, Google Drive, and Slack.
  4. Database credentials: Connection details for your PostgreSQL instance if using it as a source or destination.
  5. An n8n instance: You can use n8n.cloud or self-hosted n8n to import and run this workflow.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in your environment in under 30 minutes.

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure your credentials for Google Sheets, PostgreSQL, Slack, and Google Drive in the n8n credentials manager.
  3. Map your data sources: Update the workflow nodes to point to your specific Google Sheet URL or PostgreSQL table and column names.
  4. Define your tax logic: Adjust the "Set" and "IF" nodes to encode your specific tax rates and rules for different customer locations.
  5. Set your alert channels: Update the Slack node with your preferred channel ID for receiving alerts and daily summaries.
  6. Test and activate: Run the workflow once manually to verify data flows correctly, then activate the Schedule Trigger node for daily automatic execution.
