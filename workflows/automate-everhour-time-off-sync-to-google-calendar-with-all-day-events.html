What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common challenge of tracking team availability across multiple tools. When team members request time off in Everhour, managers and colleagues often need to check multiple places to see who's out when. This workflow automatically creates all-day events in Google Calendar for each time-off request, giving everyone a single, visual reference point for team availability.

The solution eliminates manual calendar updates, reduces scheduling conflicts, and provides better visibility into team capacity. It's particularly valuable for distributed teams where quick visual reference to time-off schedules improves coordination and prevents double-booking of critical resources.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on new Everhour time-off requests

The workflow monitors Everhour for new time-off submissions. When an employee submits a vacation, sick day, or other absence request, the automation captures all relevant details including dates, duration, and reason.

Step 2: Format the calendar event

The system converts the time-off data into Google Calendar's required format, creating all-day events that span the entire duration of the absence. The event title includes the employee's name and type of leave for quick identification.

Step 3: Create calendar event

The workflow adds the formatted event to your designated Google Calendar. It handles recurring time-off patterns and ensures the calendar displays the absence as a blocked-out period, making it instantly visible to anyone checking team availability.
