What This Workflow Does

Manual expense reporting is a time-consuming, error-prone process that frustrates employees and burdens finance teams. This automation solves that by creating a seamless pipeline from expense submission in Airtable to recorded transactions in QuickBooks.

The workflow automatically detects new expense submissions, validates approval status, creates corresponding expense records in QuickBooks, attaches digital receipts, and updates the submission status—all without human intervention. This eliminates duplicate data entry, ensures compliance, and accelerates reimbursement cycles.

By connecting your collaborative expense tracking in Airtable with your official accounting in QuickBooks, you maintain flexibility for team submissions while ensuring financial integrity and real-time bookkeeping accuracy.

How It Works

1. Trigger on New Expense Submission

The workflow monitors your Airtable base for new expense records. When an employee submits an expense with details like amount, date, vendor, and receipt URL, the automation immediately begins processing.

2. Filter for Approved Expenses

Before creating accounting entries, the system checks if the expense has been marked "Approved" by a manager. Unapproved or pending expenses are held until approval occurs, preventing unauthorized spending from entering your books.

3. Create QuickBooks Expense Record

For approved expenses, the workflow maps Airtable fields to corresponding QuickBooks fields—amount, date, vendor ID, expense account, and payment account. This creates a properly categorized expense in your general ledger.

4. Download and Attach Receipt

The system retrieves the receipt from the URL provided in Airtable, downloads the file, and uploads it as an attachment to the newly created QuickBooks expense. This creates a complete audit trail automatically.

5. Update Airtable Status

Once the expense is successfully recorded in QuickBooks with its receipt attached, the workflow updates the original Airtable record to "Done," providing visibility to the submitter and closing the loop.

What You'll Need

  1. Airtable base with expense tracking table containing: Status, Amount, Date, Vendor, Receipt URL, and accounting code fields
  2. QuickBooks Online account with API access enabled
  3. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with Airtable and QuickBooks nodes installed
  4. Approval workflow in Airtable (can be simple status column or more complex with assigned approvers)
  5. Receipt storage accessible via URL (Google Drive, Dropbox, or direct upload to Airtable)

Pro tip: Before going live, run a test with a small dollar expense to verify field mappings. Create a "Sandbox" vendor and expense account in QuickBooks specifically for testing automation workflows.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in under an hour:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Connect your Airtable account in the trigger, search, and update nodes. Enter your Base ID and Table ID.
  3. Authenticate with QuickBooks Online using OAuth2 in the create expense and upload file nodes.
  4. Replace the placeholder Company ID in the QuickBooks node URLs with your actual QuickBooks Company ID.
  5. Map your Airtable field names to match the template's expected fields (Status, Receipt URL, etc.).
  6. Test with a single approved expense to verify the complete flow from submission to QuickBooks entry.
  7. Activate the workflow and begin automating your expense reporting immediately.
