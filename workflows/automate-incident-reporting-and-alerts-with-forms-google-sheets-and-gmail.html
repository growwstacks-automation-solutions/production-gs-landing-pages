What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow automates the entire incident reporting process from initial submission through documentation and alerting. When team members report an incident through a form, the system automatically logs all details in Google Sheets for record-keeping and sends immediate email alerts through Gmail to the appropriate responders.

The automation eliminates manual data entry, reduces response times, and ensures consistent incident documentation. Operations teams can track issues from report to resolution with full audit trails, while managers gain visibility into incident frequency and patterns through the automatically updated spreadsheet.

How It Works

1. Form Submission Trigger

The workflow starts when someone submits an incident report form. The form captures essential details like incident type, severity, location, description, and any immediate actions taken.

2. Data Processing

The workflow processes the form data, adding timestamps, assigning unique incident IDs, and categorizing the report based on predefined rules. Critical fields are validated to ensure complete information.

3. Google Sheets Logging

All incident details are automatically recorded in a Google Sheets log with columns for status, assignee, resolution notes, and other tracking fields. The sheet serves as the central incident database.

4. Gmail Notifications

Based on incident type and severity, the system sends targeted email alerts to response teams through Gmail. Notifications include all relevant details and direct links to the spreadsheet record.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for operations teams, facilities managers, IT departments, and safety officers across industries. Companies with distributed teams, multiple locations, or regulatory reporting requirements benefit most from streamlined incident documentation.
