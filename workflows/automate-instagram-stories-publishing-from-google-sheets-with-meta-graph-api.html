Automate Instagram Stories Publishing from Google Sheets

This workflow automatically posts Instagram Stories from video URLs in your spreadsheet. Connect your content calendar to Instagram with zero manual work using the Meta Graph API.

What This Workflow Does

This automation eliminates the tedious manual process of uploading Instagram Stories one by one. Marketing teams often waste hours each week scheduling and posting Stories, especially when managing multiple accounts or running coordinated campaigns. The workflow pulls video URLs, captions, and scheduling details from your Google Sheets content calendar and publishes them directly to Instagram through the Meta Graph API.

By connecting your spreadsheet to Instagram's publishing API, you maintain complete control over your content while removing the repetitive posting tasks. The system can handle everything from single Story posts to complex multi-slide sequences with automatic timing between slides. You'll never miss optimal posting times or forget to publish scheduled content again.

How It Works

1. Spreadsheet Setup

Create a Google Sheet with columns for video URLs, captions, publish dates/times, and any other metadata. The workflow reads this as your content queue, processing rows in order or based on scheduled times.

2. API Authentication

The workflow authenticates with Meta's Graph API using your Instagram Business account credentials. This establishes a secure connection between your automation and Instagram's publishing system.

3. Content Processing

For each row in your spreadsheet, the system downloads the video file, formats it for Instagram Stories specifications, and prepares all metadata. It validates each piece of content before sending to Instagram.

4. Automated Publishing

The workflow publishes Stories at their scheduled times through the API. You can monitor successful posts through status updates written back to your spreadsheet or integrated notifications.

Pro tip: Add a "status" column to your spreadsheet to track which Stories have been published and view any error messages for failed attempts.
