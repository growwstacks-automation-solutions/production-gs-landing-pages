What This Workflow Does

Manual inventory management is a time-consuming, error-prone process that can lead to stockouts, excess inventory, and missed sales opportunities. This workflow automates the entire inventory replenishment cycle by connecting Google Sheets (where you track stock levels) with Gmail (for supplier communication).

The system continuously monitors your inventory, identifies items falling below minimum thresholds, calculates required order quantities, generates professional purchase orders, and emails them directly to suppliers—all without manual intervention. It creates a complete audit trail in Google Sheets, giving you real-time visibility into stock levels, order history, and supplier communications.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Inventory Check

The workflow runs automatically on a schedule you define (daily, hourly, or weekly). It connects to your Google Sheets inventory master file to read current stock levels, minimum thresholds, and supplier contact information.

2. Low-Stock Identification

For each inventory item, the system compares current stock against your predefined minimum threshold. Items requiring replenishment are flagged and queued for processing, with calculations for optimal order quantities based on your business rules.

3. Purchase Order Generation

For each low-stock item, the workflow generates a complete purchase order including SKU, item description, quantity needed, unit price, total cost, and delivery requirements. This data is pulled from your Google Sheets and formatted into a professional PO template.

4. Automated Supplier Communication

The system sends the purchase order directly to the supplier's email address via Gmail, with your company branding and all necessary details. It can CC internal team members for visibility and includes tracking information in the email body.

5. Audit Trail & Reporting

Every action—stock check, PO generation, email sent—is logged back to Google Sheets with timestamps. This creates a complete audit trail for accounting, provides data for inventory performance analysis, and ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for retail stores, e-commerce businesses, warehouses, manufacturing operations, and any business managing physical inventory. It's particularly valuable for small to medium businesses where inventory management is handled manually or with basic spreadsheets. Procurement managers, operations directors, and business owners will benefit from the time savings and error reduction.

Pro tip: Start with your top 20% highest-value or fastest-moving items. Automating these first delivers 80% of the benefits while you refine your process before scaling to your entire inventory.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google Sheets document with your inventory data (columns for SKU, description, current stock, minimum threshold, supplier email)
  2. Google Workspace account with Sheets and Gmail access
  3. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with Google Sheets and Gmail nodes configured
  4. Supplier email addresses and basic purchase order template
  5. Defined reorder points and quantities for your products

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Connect your Google account to authorize access to Sheets and Gmail within n8n.
  3. Update the Google Sheets URL in the workflow to point to your inventory spreadsheet.
  4. Configure the schedule trigger to match your inventory check frequency (daily is recommended).
  5. Customize the email template with your company details, logo, and standard PO terms.
  6. Test with one product by temporarily lowering its threshold to trigger the workflow.
  7. Monitor the audit log in your Google Sheets to verify everything works correctly.
