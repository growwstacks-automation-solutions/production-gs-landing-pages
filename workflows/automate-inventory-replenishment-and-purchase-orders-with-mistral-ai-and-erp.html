What This Workflow Does

This AI-powered automation solves one of the most time-consuming challenges in inventory management: knowing when to reorder products and how much to purchase. Traditional methods often lead to either stockouts (losing sales) or overstocking (tying up capital). The workflow continuously monitors your warehouse stock levels and sales velocity, then uses Mistral AI's machine learning capabilities to forecast demand with precision.

When inventory for any SKU drops below the calculated reorder point, the system automatically generates purchase orders in your ERP system with optimal quantities. It factors in lead times from suppliers, seasonal demand fluctuations, and current sales trends to make data-driven procurement decisions 24/7 without human intervention.

How It Works

1. Real-time inventory monitoring

The workflow connects to your inventory management system or ERP to track current stock levels across all warehouses and locations. It checks for inventory changes at configurable intervals (daily, hourly, or real-time depending on your needs).

2. Sales velocity analysis

Historical sales data is analyzed to determine how quickly each product sells. The system identifies patterns like weekly peaks, seasonal trends, and growth trajectories to predict future demand more accurately than static reorder points.

3. AI demand forecasting

Mistral AI processes the sales data with machine learning algorithms to generate probabilistic demand forecasts. It considers multiple variables simultaneously that humans can't process manually, including promotional impacts, competitor activity, and economic indicators.

4. Automated purchase order generation

When stock levels for any item fall below the AI-calculated safety threshold, the workflow automatically creates purchase orders in your ERP system with optimized quantities. It accounts for supplier lead times to ensure replenishment arrives exactly when needed.

Pro tip: Start with conservative thresholds for new products, then let the AI adjust recommendations as it learns your actual sales patterns. This prevents overordering during the initial learning phase.
