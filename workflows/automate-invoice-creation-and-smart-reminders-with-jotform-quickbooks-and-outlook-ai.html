What This Workflow Does

This automation eliminates the tedious manual process of creating invoices and following up on payments. When a customer submits information through your Jotform (like a service request or order form), the workflow automatically generates a professional invoice in QuickBooks with all the correct details.

The system then sends the invoice to your customer via email and sets up smart payment reminders in Outlook. If payment isn't received by the due date, polite follow-up emails are automatically sent at optimal intervals to maintain cash flow without straining customer relationships.

How It Works

1. Form Submission Trigger

The workflow starts when a customer submits your Jotform with service details, products ordered, or project information. All form fields are captured including contact information, items/services, quantities, and pricing.

2. QuickBooks Invoice Creation

The system takes the form data and creates a complete invoice in QuickBooks with your branding, terms, and payment options. Line items, taxes, and discounts are automatically calculated and applied based on your QuickBooks settings.

3. Email Delivery

The completed invoice is sent to the customer via Outlook with a professional template. The email includes payment terms, due date, and clickable payment options for easy settlement.

4. Smart Reminder System

Outlook AI monitors payment status and sends automated reminders at strategic intervals (3 days before due, on due date, and follow-ups if late). The tone becomes progressively more urgent while maintaining professionalism.

Pro tip: Customize reminder intervals in the workflow to match your business's typical payment cycles - service businesses often use 7/14/30 day sequences.
