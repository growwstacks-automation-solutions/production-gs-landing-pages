What This Workflow Does

Manual invoice processing is a time-consuming, error-prone task that costs businesses thousands of hours annually. This automation eliminates the manual work by creating a seamless pipeline from invoice receipt to accounting entry.

The workflow watches your Gmail for incoming invoices, extracts key data using OCR technology, validates the information against your business rules, logs everything to Google Sheets for record-keeping, notifies your finance team via Slack for approval, and finally creates draft bills in Xero—all automatically. It transforms a multi-day process into a matter of minutes.

How It Works

1. Trigger: Gmail Monitoring

The workflow continuously monitors a designated Gmail label or inbox for new emails containing PDF or image attachments. When an invoice arrives, it captures the email metadata and downloads the attachment for processing.

2. Data Extraction: OCR Processing

The invoice attachment is sent to the OCR.space API, which converts the document text into machine-readable data. The system extracts vendor details, invoice numbers, dates, amounts, line items, and payment terms with high accuracy.

3. Validation & Business Logic

Extracted data passes through multiple validation checks: verifying numerical amounts, checking for required fields, comparing against vendor databases, and flagging high-value invoices that require special attention. Invalid invoices are routed for manual review.

4. Notification & Approval

Valid invoices trigger Slack notifications to your finance channel with key details. Approvers can review and approve directly from Slack, creating a fast, collaborative approval process without switching between applications.

5. Accounting Integration

Once approved, the workflow creates a draft bill in Xero with all extracted details populated correctly. It also logs the complete transaction to Google Sheets for audit trails and reporting, then labels the original email as "Processed" in Gmail.

Pro tip: Configure different Slack channels for different invoice types (e.g., #invoices-urgent for amounts over $10,000, #invoices-routine for regular suppliers) to prioritize your team's attention effectively.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your business:

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure credentials for each service (Gmail, OCR.space, Slack, Xero, Google Sheets) in n8n's credentials management.
  3. Update node settings with your specific Slack channel IDs, Gmail label names, and Google Sheet ID.
  4. Customize validation rules in the "If" nodes to match your business requirements (currency, amount thresholds, required fields).
  5. Test with sample invoices by sending PDFs to your monitored Gmail address and verifying the complete flow.
  6. Activate the workflow and monitor initial executions to ensure everything processes correctly.

Pro tip: Start with a small subset of invoices (e.g., from one reliable vendor) during testing. Once confident, expand to all incoming invoices. Keep manual review active for the first week to catch any edge cases.
