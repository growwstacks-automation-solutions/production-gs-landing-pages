What This Workflow Does

Job searching is a full-time job in itself. You spend hours scrolling through LinkedIn, reading descriptions, and trying to guess which roles are a good fit for your skills. This process is repetitive, inefficient, and often leads to missed opportunities.

This automation solves that by turning your job search into a smart, hands-off pipeline. It systematically scans LinkedIn for new postings based on your criteria (job title, location, remote work, etc.), downloads your resume from Google Drive, and uses Google's Gemini AI to analyze each job description against your experience. It calculates a match score, suggests resume improvements, and can even draft a tailored cover letter. All results are neatly organized in a Google Sheet, and you get an email summary.

Instead of guessing, you get a data-driven shortlist of the best opportunities, saving 10-15 hours per week and letting you focus on preparing for interviews, not finding them.

How It Works

The workflow runs on a schedule you set (e.g., daily or weekly) and follows a clear, logical sequence.

1. Trigger & Configuration

A Schedule Trigger node kicks off the process. A Config node holds your preferences: job titles to search for, locations, remote work preference, and the maximum number of jobs to fetch per run.

2. Fetch Your Resume & Scrape LinkedIn

The workflow first retrieves your latest resume (PDF) from a specified Google Drive folder. It then uses the HTTP Request node to scrape LinkedIn job search results pages based on your configured keywords. It extracts key details: job title, company, link, and the full description.

3. AI-Powered Resume Matching

This is the core intelligence. For each job found, the workflow sends both the job description and your resume text to the Google Gemini AI node (or your chosen LLM). It uses a prompt to: a) Analyze the match between your skills/experience and the job requirements. b) Generate a match score (e.g., 85%). c) Provide specific feedback on why it's a good fit or where gaps exist. d) Optionally, draft a personalized opening paragraph for a cover letter.

4. Data Organization & Notification

All this structured data—job details, match score, AI insights, and cover letter draft—is appended as a new row in a Google Sheet. This becomes your master tracking dashboard. Finally, a Gmail or email node sends you a concise notification with the top matches and a link to the sheet.

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this template in under 15 minutes.

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above. In your n8n instance, go to Workflows > Import from File and select the downloaded JSON.
  2. Set Up Credentials: In the workflow, configure the credential nodes: Google Drive, Google Sheets, Gmail, and Google Gemini AI (or your chosen LLM).
  3. Configure the Search: Open the "Config" node. Input your target job titles (e.g., "Senior Product Manager"), locations ("Remote"), and set a job limit (start with 20).
  4. Link Your Documents: In the Google Drive node, point to the file ID of your resume PDF. In the Google Sheets node, input the ID of your destination spreadsheet (create a blank one first).
  5. Test & Activate: Click "Execute Workflow" to test a single run. Check the Google Sheet for results. If successful, activate the Schedule Trigger to run automatically (e.g., every Monday at 9 AM).
