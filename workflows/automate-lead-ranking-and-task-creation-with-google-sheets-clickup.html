What This Workflow Does

Sales teams waste countless hours manually sifting through spreadsheets to identify which leads to contact next. Warm opportunities get buried under new inquiries, and follow-up timing becomes inconsistent. This automation solves that by creating a systematic, data-driven approach to lead prioritization.

The workflow pulls lead data from your Google Sheets, calculates a priority score based on engagement activity and days since last contact, then automatically creates follow-up tasks in ClickUp for your top 10 leads. It even suggests optimal contact times based on each lead's timezone, ensuring your sales team always knows exactly who to contact and when.

How It Works

1. Data Extraction from Google Sheets

The automation connects to your Google Sheet containing lead information—name, engagement score, last contact date, timezone, and email. It reads this data in real-time, ensuring your prioritization is always based on the most current information.

2. Priority Score Calculation

Each lead receives a weighted score combining engagement (70%) and recency (30%). The system calculates days since last contact and normalizes this with engagement metrics to identify genuinely warm leads versus cold ones.

3. Sorting and Filtering

Leads are sorted by their calculated priority score, and the top 10 are selected for immediate follow-up. You can easily adjust this threshold based on your team's capacity.

4. ClickUp Task Creation

For each prioritized lead, a task is created in your designated ClickUp list with all relevant details: lead name, contact information, priority score, and suggested follow-up time based on their timezone.

5. Sheet Status Update

The Google Sheet is updated to mark which leads have been queued for follow-up, preventing duplicate work and providing visibility into the automation's actions.

Pro tip: Schedule this workflow to run daily at 8 AM local time. This gives your sales team a fresh, prioritized task list every morning, ensuring they focus on the highest-value activities first.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your lead automation running in under 15 minutes:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Connect your credentials for Google Sheets and ClickUp in the n8n nodes.
  3. Update the spreadsheet ID in the Google Sheets node to point to your actual lead tracking sheet.
  4. Configure the ClickUp node with your specific workspace, space, and list IDs.
  5. Test the workflow using the manual trigger to ensure tasks are created correctly.
  6. Schedule it to run daily for automatic, hands-free lead prioritization.

Important: Before going live, review the weightage formula (70% engagement, 30% recency) and adjust it to match your sales process. Some businesses value recent engagement more heavily, while others prioritize overall engagement history.

Lead scoring evaluates and prioritizes leads based on their likelihood to convert. Lead routing determines which sales rep or team should handle the lead based on territory, expertise, or workload.

While scoring answers 'how hot is this lead?', routing answers 'who should handle it?'. Many automation workflows combine both: score the lead first, then route it to the appropriate rep with context about why it was prioritized.
