What This Workflow Does

This automation transforms the chaotic client onboarding process into a streamlined, efficient system for marketing agencies. When a new client submits your Typeform onboarding questionnaire, the workflow automatically organizes their responses in Google Drive while simultaneously conducting AI-powered research about their business and industry.

The system eliminates manual data entry, reduces errors, and ensures every client file contains consistent, well-organized information. By the time your team meets the client, you'll already have their completed questionnaire, preliminary research, and properly structured folders - giving you more time to focus on strategy rather than administrative setup.

How It Works

1. Typeform submission triggers the workflow

When a potential client completes your agency's onboarding Typeform, the workflow automatically captures all their responses. This includes contact information, business details, project requirements, and any uploaded files or documents.

2. Data validation and processing

The system checks for complete responses, formats information consistently, and flags any missing critical details that might require follow-up. This ensures your team never receives incomplete client information.

3. Google Drive folder creation

A dedicated client folder is created in your agency's Google Drive with a standardized structure. Subfolders are generated for contracts, assets, research, and deliverables based on your agency's preferred organization system.

4. AI-powered research initiation

Using the client's business information from the Typeform, OpenAI analyzes their industry, competitors, and market positioning. The system generates a preliminary research report that's saved directly to the client's folder.

5. Team notification and next steps

The workflow notifies your account team via email or Slack and creates any necessary tasks in your project management system. The entire onboarding package is ready before your first strategy call.

Secure automation requires encrypted data transfer between Typeform, Google Drive, and AI tools. Client information should be stored in restricted-access folders with proper permission controls. The workflow should exclude sensitive data from AI processing unless explicitly authorized. Regular audits ensure compliance with data protection regulations like GDPR for international clients.

Implement two-factor authentication for all connected accounts and consider using a dedicated service account for automation rather than personal credentials. For highly sensitive clients, you might maintain certain manual steps while automating less critical portions of the workflow.
