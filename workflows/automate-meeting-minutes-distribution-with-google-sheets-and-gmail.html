Automate Meeting Minutes Distribution with Google Sheets & Gmail

Stop manually emailing notes. This free n8n workflow automatically sends polished meeting summaries from your Google Sheet directly to stakeholders' inboxes via Gmail, saving hours per week.

What This Workflow Does

Manually compiling and emailing meeting notes is a repetitive, time-consuming task that often gets delayed or forgotten. This automation solves that by instantly transforming the notes in your Google Sheet into a professional email summary and sending it via Gmail as soon as the meeting concludes.

The workflow triggers whenever a new row is added to your designated "Meeting Minutes" sheet. It extracts key details like discussion topics, decisions made, action items with owners, and next steps, then formats them into a clear, actionable email. This ensures every attendee and stakeholder receives identical information simultaneously, creating immediate alignment and accountability.

Beyond simple distribution, this system creates a reliable, searchable paper trail. It turns your meeting notes from a static document into an active communication tool that drives projects forward and ensures nothing falls through the cracks between meetings.

How It Works

1. Trigger: New Row in Google Sheets

The workflow activates automatically when you add a new meeting record to your Google Sheet. This could be triggered manually after a meeting or connected to your calendar to create a placeholder row when an event ends.

2. Data Extraction & Formatting

n8n reads the structured data from your sheet—typically including columns for Agenda Items, Decisions, Action Items (with Owners), Deadlines, and Key Takeaways. The workflow organizes this information into a logical, readable format suitable for email communication.

3. Email Composition & Delivery

Using your connected Gmail account, the workflow generates a professional email with a clear subject line (e.g., "Meeting Minutes: [Project Name] - [Date]"), a formatted body containing the extracted notes, and sends it to your predefined distribution list. You can customize the template to match your company's communication style.

Pro tip: Add a "Confidential" column to your sheet. Configure the workflow to check this flag and automatically redact sensitive information or send to a restricted distribution list when marked.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google account with access to Google Sheets and Gmail.
  2. A Google Sheet structured with columns for your meeting data (e.g., Date, Topic, Decisions, Action Items, Owner, Next Step).
  3. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with the Google Sheets and Gmail nodes installed and configured.
  4. A list of recipient email addresses (can be stored in the sheet or configured in the workflow).

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in under 15 minutes:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Connect your Google account to both the Google Sheets and Gmail nodes in the workflow canvas.
  3. Configure the Google Sheets trigger by selecting your specific "Meeting Minutes" spreadsheet and worksheet.
  4. Set up the email template in the Gmail node. Customize the subject line and body to pull data from the correct sheet columns.
  5. Add your recipient list—you can hardcode emails, pull them from a sheet column, or connect to a Google Contacts list.
  6. Test the workflow by adding a sample row to your sheet. Check that the email sends correctly and contains all necessary information.
  7. Activate the workflow and start adding your real meeting notes to the sheet.

Pro tip: Create a standardized note-taking template in your Google Sheet with required columns (Topic, Decision, Action, Owner, Deadline). Share this template with your team to ensure consistent data that the automation can process perfectly every time.
