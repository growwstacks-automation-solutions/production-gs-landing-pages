What This Workflow Does

Meetings are essential for collaboration but create massive administrative overhead. Teams waste hours after each meeting: someone has to listen to recordings, type notes, extract action items, and chase people for follow-ups. This manual process is error-prone, inconsistent, and delays project momentum.

This intelligent automation solves that by creating a complete AI meeting assistant. It automatically detects when Google Meet sessions end, retrieves the recording from Google Drive, transcribes the conversation with speaker identification, analyzes the discussion using Claude AI to understand context and decisions, then distributes a polished summary to Slack and creates actionable tasks in Notion—all without human intervention.

The system transforms unstructured meeting conversations into structured, actionable intelligence. It captures not just what was said, but what needs to happen next, who's responsible, and by when. This ensures decisions translate into execution, not just conversation.

How It Works

The workflow orchestrates six different services into a seamless pipeline that mimics how an ideal executive assistant would handle meeting follow-up.

1. Meeting Detection & Recording Retrieval

The automation starts by checking Google Calendar for completed meetings in the last 24 hours that included Google Meet links. It filters for confirmed events, then searches Google Drive for the corresponding recording file based on meeting timestamps and titles. If no recording is found, it stops gracefully with a clear error message.

2. Intelligent Transcription & Speaker Diarization

The recording is sent to AssemblyAI, which performs advanced speech-to-text with speaker identification (who said what), sentiment analysis, and highlights key phrases. This creates a rich transcript that preserves conversation flow and emotional tone, not just raw words.

3. AI-Powered Analysis & Insight Extraction

Claude AI processes the transcript with specific instructions to extract: a concise 2-3 paragraph summary capturing main discussion points and decisions; all action items with clear responsible parties; any mentioned dates or deadlines; and overall meeting sentiment (positive, neutral, or concerning).

4. Distribution & Task Creation

The polished summary is posted to a designated Slack channel for team visibility. Simultaneously, each identified action item becomes a task in Notion with assignee, due date (if mentioned), and context from the meeting discussion. This creates immediate accountability without manual data entry.

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this workflow in under 30 minutes to start automating your meeting follow-ups.

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance using the import functionality
  2. Configure credentials for each service in n8n's credentials management: Google OAuth2, AssemblyAI API, Claude API, Slack webhook, and Notion integration
  3. Adjust trigger settings in the Google Calendar node to match your meeting patterns (time range, calendar selection)
  4. Customize output channels by setting the target Slack channel and Notion database ID in the respective nodes
  5. Test with a sample meeting by manually triggering the workflow with a recent recording to verify transcription quality and task creation
  6. Activate and schedule the workflow to run automatically (e.g., hourly or daily) to process completed meetings
