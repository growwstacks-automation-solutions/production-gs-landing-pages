Automate News Intelligence with Gemini AI for RSS Feeds to Notion and Slack

Free n8n workflow to automatically monitor RSS feeds, summarize with AI, tag, and send prioritized briefings to Notion and Slack. Save hours on market research.

What This Workflow Does

Staying on top of industry news, competitor moves, and market trends is essential for business growth, but manually scanning dozens of sources consumes valuable time and leads to missed information. This automation solves that problem by creating a fully automated intelligence pipeline.

The workflow reads your configured RSS feeds each morning, uses Google's Gemini AI to summarize articles and assign relevant tags based on your business priorities, saves structured records to a Notion database for permanent reference, and delivers a concise Slack digest highlighting the top items your team needs to know. It transforms overwhelming information streams into actionable business intelligence.

How It Works

1. Daily Trigger & Configuration

A schedule trigger runs the workflow each morning (configurable to weekdays only). The workflow begins by loading your configuration: RSS feed URLs, Notion database ID, Slack channel, and tag dictionary.

2. RSS Feed Processing

The workflow fetches all new articles from your configured RSS feeds. It extracts the title, content, publication date, and source URL for each item, preparing them for AI analysis.

3. AI Summarization & Tagging

Each article is sent to Google's Gemini AI with custom instructions to generate a concise summary and assign relevant tags from your dictionary. The AI identifies key points, extracts mentions of competitors or products, and categorizes the content by relevance to your business.

4. Data Structuring & Prioritization

The AI output is parsed into structured data. Articles receive priority scores based on relevance, recency, and source importance. The workflow sorts items to highlight the most important information for your team's attention.

5. Notion Database Update

Each processed article creates a new entry in your Notion database with the summary, tags, priority score, original URL, and publication date. This builds a searchable knowledge base of industry intelligence that your entire team can reference.

6. Slack Digest Delivery

The top 3-5 prioritized items are formatted into a clean Slack message with summaries, tags, and direct links. The message is posted to your designated channel, giving your team a quick morning briefing without needing to leave their communication platform.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with execution capabilities
  2. Google Gemini API access for AI summarization and tagging
  3. Notion account with a database created using the template structure
  4. Slack workspace with app installation permissions for your channel
  5. RSS feed URLs for the news sources you want to monitor
  6. Optional: Google Sheet for maintaining your custom tag dictionary

Pro tip: Start with 3-5 high-quality RSS feeds rather than dozens of sources. Better coverage of key publications delivers more value than superficial scanning of many low-relevance sources.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure the "Workflow Configuration" node with your RSS feed URLs, Notion database ID, and Slack channel
  3. Set up credentials in n8n for Notion, Slack, and Google Gemini AI
  4. Adjust the schedule trigger to run at your preferred morning time (e.g., 9 AM weekdays)
  5. Test with a manual execution to verify all connections work properly
  6. Activate the workflow and let it run automatically each day
