Automate LinkedIn News Publishing with Gemini AI

Transform RSS feeds into engaging LinkedIn posts with AI-powered summarization

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming challenge of maintaining an active, valuable LinkedIn presence. It automatically fetches relevant industry news from RSS feeds, processes the content through Gemini AI to create professional-quality summaries, and publishes them to your LinkedIn profile at optimal times.

For marketing teams and business professionals, this workflow eliminates hours of manual content curation while ensuring your network receives timely, insightful updates that position you as an industry thought leader.

How It Works

Step 1: RSS Feed Collection

The workflow begins by pulling the latest articles from your configured RSS feeds (TechCrunch, MIT Technology Review, etc.). It filters for relevance based on your specified keywords and quality thresholds.

Step 2: AI Content Processing

Selected articles are sent to Gemini AI, which generates concise, professional summaries while preserving key insights. The AI adapts its output to match LinkedIn's professional tone and your brand voice guidelines.

Step 3: LinkedIn Publishing

The processed content is automatically formatted for LinkedIn and published through the LinkedIn API. The workflow can be scheduled to post during your audience's peak engagement hours.

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to add personalized commentary to AI summaries for a human touch that boosts engagement.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. Google Gemini API credentials
  3. LinkedIn Developer Account with publishing permissions
  4. RSS feeds from industry-relevant publications
  5. 10-15 minutes for initial configuration

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure your RSS feed sources in the HTTP Request nodes
  3. Add your Gemini API key in the AI processing section
  4. Authenticate with LinkedIn in the LinkedIn node
  5. Set your preferred posting schedule in the Interval Trigger
  6. Test with a small RSS feed before full deployment
