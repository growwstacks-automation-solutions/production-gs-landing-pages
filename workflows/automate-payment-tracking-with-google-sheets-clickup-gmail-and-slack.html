Automate Payment Tracking with Google Sheets, ClickUp, Gmail & Slack

Free n8n workflow to eliminate manual payment follow-ups, reduce errors, and ensure timely collections.

What This Workflow Does

Manual payment tracking is a time-consuming, error-prone process that directly impacts your cash flow. This automation solves that by creating a seamless system that monitors payment statuses, creates follow-up tasks, and keeps your team informed—all without manual intervention.

The workflow pulls lead and payment data from Google Sheets, checks whether payments are marked as "Open," and then routes actions accordingly. For open payments, it creates dedicated ClickUp tasks for follow-up and sends email confirmations to stakeholders. For closed or non-actionable cases, it sends Slack notifications to maintain team visibility. This ensures no payment slips through the cracks while keeping all updates transparent and centralized.

How It Works

1. Fetch Payment Data from Google Sheets

The workflow starts by retrieving lead records from your Google Sheet, including lead name, company, payment verification status, and whether the status is "Open" or "Closed." This serves as your single source of truth for all payment tracking.

2. Route Based on Payment Status

An "If" node evaluates each record. If the status equals "Open," the workflow proceeds to create follow-up tasks. If not, it sends a notification to Slack to inform the team that no action is required.

3. Create ClickUp Tasks for Open Payments

For open payments, the automation creates a dedicated task in your specified ClickUp list. The task includes all relevant details—lead name, company, amount, due date—creating a clear action item for your finance or sales team.

4. Send Email Confirmations via Gmail

Once the ClickUp task is created, the workflow sends an email confirmation through Gmail to assigned watchers or stakeholders. The email includes task details, a direct link to the task, and any relevant payment information.

5. Notify Team via Slack

For non-open payment cases, the workflow posts a notification to your designated Slack channel. This maintains team awareness of payment statuses without creating unnecessary tasks, ensuring everyone stays informed.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for finance teams handling multiple payment verifications, companies wanting transparent payment tracking processes, teams already using ClickUp for task management, and managers who need visibility into pending versus closed payment statuses. It's particularly valuable for B2B service providers, agencies, consultants, and any business with recurring invoices or project-based billing.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google Sheet with lead/payment data (columns for lead name, company, payment status, amount)
  2. ClickUp account with API access and a designated list for payment tasks
  3. Gmail API credentials or SMTP access for sending emails
  4. Slack workspace with appropriate channel permissions
  5. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the automation

Pro tip: Before implementing, clean your Google Sheet data to ensure consistent formatting in the "Status" column. Use exact values like "Open," "Closed," "Pending" to avoid routing errors.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Google Sheets: Connect to your payment tracking sheet and map the relevant columns.
  3. Set up ClickUp: Connect to your workspace, select the target list, and configure task fields.
  4. Connect Gmail: Authenticate with your Google account and set the recipient email addresses.
  5. Configure Slack: Connect to your workspace and select the notification channel.
  6. Test the workflow: Run a test with sample data to ensure all connections work correctly.
  7. Activate and schedule: Set the workflow to run on a schedule (e.g., daily at 9 AM) for continuous monitoring.
