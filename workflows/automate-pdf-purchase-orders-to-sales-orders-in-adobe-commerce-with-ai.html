Automate PDF Purchase Orders to Adobe Commerce Sales Orders

Convert emailed PDF purchase orders into sales orders automatically using AI validation and Company Credit payment in Magento 2.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical bottleneck for B2B companies: manually processing PDF purchase orders received via email. Sales teams typically spend hours each week opening emails, extracting data from PDF attachments, validating customer information, and manually creating orders in Adobe Commerce (Magento 2). This workflow eliminates that entire manual process.

The system automatically monitors your designated email inbox for incoming purchase orders, extracts product data from PDF attachments using AI-powered parsing, validates the customer against your Adobe Commerce database, checks Company Credit authorization, and creates the sales order automatically. It handles exceptions gracefully with detailed logging and notification systems, ensuring no order falls through the cracks while freeing your team from repetitive data entry tasks.

How It Works

1. Email Monitoring & PDF Extraction

The workflow begins by monitoring your business email inbox (IMAP, Gmail, or Microsoft Outlook) for incoming messages with PDF attachments. When a purchase order arrives, it downloads the PDF attachment and uses AI-powered text extraction to identify customer information, product SKUs, quantities, pricing, and shipping details regardless of the PDF's specific format.

2. Customer Validation & Credit Check

Extracted customer data is validated against your Adobe Commerce customer database. The system checks if the customer exists, has an active account, and is authorized to use Company Credit as the payment method. If validation fails at any step, the workflow triggers exception handling with notifications to your sales team.

3. Order Creation in Adobe Commerce

Once validated, the system uses Adobe Commerce's REST API to create a complete sales order with all extracted line items, customer details, shipping address, and Company Credit as the payment method. The order is processed with the same accuracy as manual entry but in seconds rather than hours.

4. Error Handling & Notifications

Comprehensive error handling manages common issues: invalid PDF formats, missing customer data, insufficient credit limits, out-of-stock items, or API connectivity problems. Each exception triggers appropriate notifications (Slack, email, or dashboard alerts) with detailed context for quick resolution.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (self-hosted or n8n.cloud)
  2. Adobe Commerce (Magento 2) with API access enabled
  3. Company Credit payment method configured and active
  4. Business email account (IMAP, Gmail, or Outlook) for receiving purchase orders
  5. Structured PDF templates used consistently by your customers (though AI can handle variations)
  6. Optional: Slack, Microsoft Teams, or email for error notifications

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n instance using the "Import from File" option.
  2. Configure email trigger: Replace the manual trigger node with your preferred email trigger (IMAP, Gmail, or Outlook) and connect your business email credentials.
  3. Set up Adobe Commerce API: Configure the HTTP Request nodes with your Adobe Commerce API credentials, base URL, and authentication tokens (use environment variables for security).
  4. Customize PDF parsing: Adjust the text extraction nodes to match your specific PDF format and data fields. Test with sample purchase orders from your customers.
  5. Configure notifications: Set up error notification channels (Slack, email, etc.) and test the exception handling flow with invalid data.
  6. Run in production: Activate the workflow and monitor the first few automated orders. Review logs and adjust validation rules as needed.

Pro tip: Start with a test email folder and a small subset of customers before full deployment. This allows you to refine validation rules and exception handling without affecting live orders.
