What This Workflow Does

Accounting teams waste countless hours manually entering purchase bills into accounting systems—copying vendor details, creating items, checking for duplicates, and reconciling totals. This repetitive, error-prone process slows down accounts payable, creates data inconsistencies, and ties up valuable staff time.

This n8n workflow eliminates that manual effort entirely. It combines AI-powered Optical Character Recognition (OCR) with QuickBooks integration to automatically convert uploaded purchase bills into fully reconciled QuickBooks bills. The system extracts structured data from PDFs and images, validates the information, syncs vendors and items, and creates clean accounting records—all without human intervention.

The automation handles multiple bills in a single upload, prevents duplicate entries, maintains data accuracy, and ensures your QuickBooks records are always up-to-date. What used to take hours now happens in minutes, with greater consistency and reliability.

How It Works

1. Bill Upload & Preparation

The workflow starts with an n8n form trigger where users upload one or multiple purchase bills (PDFs, scanned images, or digital documents). The system automatically splits multi-page documents and prepares each bill for processing. This flexible input method allows batch processing of weekly or monthly bills in one operation.

2. AI OCR Data Extraction

Each document passes through an AI-powered OCR engine that extracts structured invoice data. Unlike basic text recognition, this AI understands invoice layouts and identifies key fields: invoice numbers, dates, vendor names and addresses, line items (including descriptions, quantities, unit prices, and amounts), subtotals, taxes, and grand totals. The system handles various formats and can even interpret handwritten notes on scanned documents.

3. QuickBooks Vendor & Item Reconciliation

The extracted vendor information is checked against your existing QuickBooks vendor list. If the vendor doesn't exist, the workflow automatically creates a new vendor record with all necessary details. Similarly, line items are matched with existing QuickBooks items, and missing items are created on the fly. This prevents duplicate entries and maintains a clean, organized chart of accounts.

4. Data Validation & Bill Assembly

Before creating the bill, the workflow validates all extracted data. It checks mathematical accuracy (line item totals match subtotals, taxes are calculated correctly), flags any discrepancies for review, and ensures all required fields are present. The system then assembles a complete QuickBooks-compatible bill payload with proper categorization, tax handling, and memo fields.

5. QuickBooks Bill Creation & Notification

The validated bill is created in QuickBooks with the correct vendor, items, amounts, and due dates. Once complete, the system can send notifications to accounting staff via email or Slack, providing a summary of processed bills and any issues that require attention. The entire process from upload to created bill typically takes under 60 seconds per document.
