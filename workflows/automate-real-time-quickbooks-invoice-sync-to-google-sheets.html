What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious problem of manually copying invoice data from QuickBooks to spreadsheets for reporting and backup. When a new invoice is created or updated in QuickBooks, this workflow instantly captures it and logs the essential details—invoice ID, customer name, due date, and domain—into a designated Google Sheet.

It ensures your financial records are always accurate and up-to-date without any manual intervention. The automation acts as a real-time bridge between your accounting system and your reporting tools, enabling custom dashboards, secure data backups, and easy sharing with team members who don't have direct QuickBooks access.

The workflow uses a webhook to trigger instantly when invoice events occur, fetches the complete invoice details, formats the data cleanly, and then either adds a new row or updates an existing row in your Google Sheet. This prevents duplicates and maintains a perfect mirror of your QuickBooks invoice data.

How It Works

Step 1: Instant Webhook Trigger

The workflow activates the moment an invoice is created or updated in QuickBooks. The QuickBooks webhook sends a notification containing the invoice ID to n8n, kicking off the process in real time without any scheduled polling or manual checks.

Step 2: Fetch Complete Invoice Details

The initial webhook notification only contains the invoice ID. This step uses that ID to make a call back to the QuickBooks API and retrieve the complete invoice data, including customer details, amounts, due dates, and status information.

Step 3: Format Key Data for Reporting

A simple code node cleans up the data fetched from QuickBooks. It extracts only the fields you need for reporting—ID, domain, customer name, and due date—and structures them perfectly for spreadsheet insertion, removing unnecessary metadata.

Step 4: Append or Update in Google Sheets

The final node connects to your Google Sheet and uses the "Append or Update" operation. If the invoice ID doesn't exist in the sheet, it adds a new row. If the ID already exists (for updated invoices), it updates the existing row with the latest information, ensuring your sheet is always current.

Pro tip: Set up your Google Sheet with the exact column headers (ID, Domain, Customer Name, Due Date) before activating the workflow. This ensures automatic column mapping and prevents configuration errors.

What You'll Need

  1. QuickBooks Online account with webhook capabilities enabled in the Intuit Developer Portal.
  2. Google account with Google Sheets and Google Drive APIs enabled for OAuth2 authentication.
  3. A Google Sheet created with the specific headers: ID, Domain, Customer Name, Due Date in the first row.
  4. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) where you can import and run the workflow.
  5. Basic understanding of how to configure webhooks in QuickBooks and connect Google services via OAuth.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure QuickBooks credentials in n8n and copy the webhook production URL from the Webhook node.
  3. In your Intuit Developer Portal, paste the webhook URL and subscribe to Invoice Create and Update events.
  4. Configure Google OAuth2 credentials in n8n, ensuring Sheets and Drive APIs are enabled.
  5. Create your Google Sheet with the required headers, then select it in the "Append or update row in sheet" node.
  6. Activate the workflow and test by creating a sample invoice in QuickBooks to verify automatic sync.

Pro tip: Start with a test Google Sheet and a few sample invoices before connecting to your main financial sheet. This allows you to verify data formatting and mapping without risking your actual records.
