What This Workflow Does

Manual sales tracking is a hidden cost for many online businesses. When a customer completes a Stripe Checkout payment, that valuable data often sits isolated in your payment platform, disconnected from your marketing automation in RD Station Marketing. This creates delays in follow-up campaigns, inaccurate lead scoring, and missed opportunities for personalized engagement.

This workflow solves that by automatically capturing every successful Stripe payment and sending it as an Order & Payment Event directly to RD Station Marketing. The moment a transaction is confirmed, your marketing team receives real-time data—product purchased, amount, currency, and customer details—ready to trigger automated sequences, update contact scores, and measure campaign ROI accurately.

Beyond basic tracking, this automation ensures your sales and marketing data are unified. You can segment buyers by purchase value, launch post-purchase nurture flows instantly, and attribute revenue directly to marketing efforts without manual spreadsheet exports or delayed reporting.

How It Works

Step 1: Stripe triggers the workflow

The workflow listens for Stripe Checkout Session completion events via a webhook. Whenever a customer successfully pays, Stripe sends a payload containing the transaction details, including items purchased, amount, and customer email if collected.

Step 2: Data extraction and validation

The workflow extracts the relevant payment information—like line items, total amount, and currency—and validates the payment status. It filters out failed or pending payments, ensuring only confirmed sales proceed.

Step 3: Formatting for RD Station Marketing

Using a Set node, the workflow structures the data into the specific format RD Station Marketing expects for an Order & Payment Event. This includes mapping Stripe fields to RD Station’s custom event parameters.

Step 4: Send to RD Station Marketing

The formatted payload is sent to RD Station Marketing via its API using a community node. The event is recorded against the customer’s profile, updating their lead score and triggering any configured automation rules based on the purchase.

Step 5: Optional enrichment and logging

You can extend the workflow to enrich customer data from a CRM before sending, log the event to a database for audit purposes, or trigger secondary actions like sending a thank-you email or updating internal dashboards.

What You'll Need

  1. A Stripe account with Checkout enabled and API keys (Secret Key & Webhook Secret).
  2. An RD Station Marketing account with API access (public token and private token).
  3. A self‑hosted n8n instance (community node required) or n8n Cloud with custom node support.
  4. The RD Station Marketing community node installed in your n8n environment.
  5. Basic understanding of webhook configuration in Stripe.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to import and configure the workflow in your n8n environment:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure the Stripe Trigger node with your Stripe Secret Key and Webhook Secret. Set the event to “checkout.session.completed”.
  3. Add your RD Station Marketing credentials in the “send2RDMKT” node (public and private tokens).
  4. Test with a real or test payment to verify the data flows correctly and creates an event in RD Station.
  5. Deploy the webhook by copying the workflow’s webhook URL into your Stripe dashboard under Developers → Webhooks.
  6. Monitor and extend—add error handling, customer enrichment, or secondary actions based on your business logic.

Pro tip: Use Stripe’s test mode to simulate payments without charging real money. This lets you verify the entire flow before going live.
