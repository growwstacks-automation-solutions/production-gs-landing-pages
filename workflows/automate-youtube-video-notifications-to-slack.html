What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of manually sharing new YouTube videos with your team. When content creators publish videos, they typically have to copy the link, switch to Slack, find the right channel, and post a message—a process that takes 5-10 minutes per video and is often forgotten.

The workflow automatically monitors your YouTube channel's RSS feed every 30 minutes. When a new video is detected, it extracts the title, description, publication date, and direct link, then formats a rich Slack message with a "Watch Now" button. The entire process happens without any manual intervention, ensuring your marketing, sales, and support teams are immediately aware of new content.

Beyond basic notifications, this automation creates consistency in your internal communications. Every video gets the same professional announcement format, complete with relevant metadata. This eliminates the "Did you see our new video?" follow-up questions and ensures maximum internal engagement with your content.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Monitoring

A Cron node triggers the workflow every 30 minutes (configurable). This regular checking ensures new videos are detected promptly without overwhelming APIs or creating unnecessary load.

2. YouTube RSS Feed Fetching

The HTTP Request node retrieves your channel's public RSS feed using YouTube's standardized format. This approach is more reliable than screen scraping and respects YouTube's terms of service while providing structured video data.

3. Video Data Processing

A Code node parses the RSS XML, extracting key information: video title, URL, publication timestamp, and description snippet. It filters for videos published within the last 2 hours to avoid duplicate notifications for older content.

4. Message Formatting

The workflow constructs a professional Slack message using blocks format. This includes a clear header, formatted video information, publication date, and an interactive button that links directly to the YouTube video.

5. Slack Delivery

The formatted message posts to your designated Slack channel (default #general). You can customize the bot name and icon to match your brand, making notifications instantly recognizable to your team.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with workflow execution capabilities
  2. YouTube channel ID (found in your channel URL or page source)
  3. Slack workspace with permission to create apps and post to channels
  4. Slack app credentials (OAuth token with chat:write and channels:read scopes)
  5. Basic understanding of how to import JSON workflows into n8n

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Find your YouTube channel ID by viewing your channel page source and searching for "channel/"
  3. Update the HTTP Request node with your channel ID in the RSS URL
  4. Create a Slack app at api.slack.com with appropriate OAuth scopes
  5. Connect Slack credentials in n8n using OAuth2 authentication
  6. Specify your target Slack channel in the Slack node configuration
  7. Test with a recent video by triggering the workflow manually first
  8. Activate the schedule and let it run automatically every 30 minutes

Pro tip: For teams with multiple YouTube channels, duplicate the workflow and change only the channel ID. You can route different channel notifications to different Slack channels based on content type or team responsibility.
