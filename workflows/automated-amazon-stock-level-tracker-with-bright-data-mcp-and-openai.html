What This Workflow Does

Running an e-commerce business means constantly worrying about inventory. Stockouts lead to lost sales and frustrated customers, while overstocking ties up capital and increases storage costs. Manual tracking across Amazon, supplier websites, and internal spreadsheets is slow, error-prone, and reactive.

This automation solves that by continuously monitoring stock levels across all your sales channels and supplier portals. It uses Bright Data to scrape real-time availability data, then employs OpenAI to analyze trends, predict shortages, and generate intelligent alerts. The result is a proactive inventory management system that tells you exactly what’s running low, when to reorder, and which suppliers have stock—before your customers ever notice a problem.

Beyond simple threshold alerts, this workflow provides actionable insights: it can prioritize products based on sales velocity, forecast demand using historical patterns, and even suggest alternative suppliers if your primary source is out of stock. It transforms inventory management from a daily chore into a strategic, data-driven advantage.

How It Works

Step 1: Data Collection via Bright Data

The workflow starts by scheduling regular checks of your configured product URLs on Amazon and supplier websites. Bright Data’s MCP client handles the web scraping, bypassing blocks and extracting clean stock availability, pricing, and estimated delivery dates. This raw data is structured and passed to the next stage.

Step 2: AI Analysis & Trend Detection

OpenAI processes the collected data, comparing current levels against historical trends and sales velocity. It identifies products trending toward stockouts, flags items with unusually long lead times, and highlights seasonal demand patterns. The AI can also summarize findings into concise reports for management.

Step 3: Alert Generation & Action Triggers

When a stock level falls below a configured threshold or a shortage is predicted, the system triggers automated alerts. These can be sent via email, Slack, SMS, or directly into your procurement software. For critical items, the workflow can automatically draft purchase orders or contact suppliers via email templates.

Step 4: Centralized Inventory Dashboard

All processed data is sent to Google Sheets (or your preferred database), creating a live inventory dashboard. This gives your team a single, real-time view of stock health across all products and suppliers, replacing fragmented manual spreadsheets.

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for e-commerce retailers, Amazon sellers, distributors, and any business that manages physical inventory across multiple channels. If you sell products online, source from various suppliers, or need to ensure stock availability for customer orders, this automation eliminates the manual oversight burden.

Procurement teams, warehouse managers, and operations directors benefit most. It’s also valuable for businesses scaling rapidly, where manual tracking becomes impossible, or for those selling seasonal products where demand forecasting is critical.

What You'll Need

  1. A running n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted).
  2. Bright Data account with MCP access for web scraping.
  3. OpenAI API key for AI analysis capabilities.
  4. Google Sheets (or similar) for the inventory dashboard.
  5. List of product URLs/supplier pages you want to monitor.
  6. Alert channels configured (email, Slack, etc.).

Pro tip: Start by monitoring just your top 10–20 highest‑value or fastest‑selling products. This gives immediate ROI and lets you refine the workflow before scaling to hundreds of SKUs.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure Bright Data: Add your Bright Data credentials to the MCP Client node and set the target product URLs.
  3. Set up OpenAI: Enter your OpenAI API key in the AI node and adjust the analysis prompts to match your inventory logic.
  4. Connect Google Sheets: Link your Sheets account and specify the spreadsheet where inventory data should be logged.
  5. Define alert thresholds: Set your minimum stock levels and choose notification channels (email, Slack, etc.).
  6. Schedule execution: Set the Schedule Trigger node to run daily, hourly, or as needed for your business.
  7. Test & refine: Run the workflow manually to verify data extraction and alert accuracy, then adjust parameters.
