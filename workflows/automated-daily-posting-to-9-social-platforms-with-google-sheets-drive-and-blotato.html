Automated Daily Social Media Posting to 9 Platforms

Publish content daily across 9 social platforms from a simple Google Sheet. Pull images/videos from Drive, post via Blotato, and update status automatically.

What This Workflow Does

Managing social media across multiple platforms is a time-consuming task that often requires logging into each platform separately, uploading media, crafting captions, and scheduling posts. This process can take hours each week and is prone to human error and inconsistency.

This automation solves that problem by creating a centralized content management system using Google Sheets. When you mark a post as "Ready to Post" in your spreadsheet, the workflow automatically fetches the corresponding image or video from Google Drive, publishes it to 9 social platforms simultaneously via Blotato, and then updates the spreadsheet status to "Posted." This eliminates manual posting while ensuring consistent, scheduled content delivery across all your social channels.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Trigger & Content Check

The workflow runs every 3 hours (configurable) to check your Google Sheet for posts marked "Ready to Post." It retrieves one post at a time, including all content details like captions, hashtags, and media file names.

2. Media Retrieval from Google Drive

Using the file name from the spreadsheet, the automation locates and downloads the corresponding image or video from your Google Drive. The Drive folder must be set to public (anyone with link) for this to work seamlessly.

3. Upload to Blotato

The media file is uploaded to Blotato's platform, which prepares it for distribution across social networks. Blotato handles platform-specific formatting requirements and optimization.

4. Multi-Platform Publishing

Blotato publishes the content to all connected social accounts (up to 9 platforms). You can choose to post immediately or schedule for later. The workflow supports images, videos, slideshows, carousels, and threaded posts.

5. Status Update & Completion

Once publishing is confirmed, the workflow updates the Google Sheet status from "Ready to Post" to "Posted," creating an audit trail of published content and preventing duplicate posts.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with "Verified Community Nodes" enabled
  2. Blotato account with API key (paid feature for API access)
  3. Google Sheets with the template structure (don't change column names)
  4. Google Drive with a public folder for media storage
  5. Social media accounts connected to your Blotato dashboard
  6. Blotato community node installed in your n8n instance

Pro tip: Keep media files under 60MB for Google Drive compatibility. For larger files, consider using Amazon S3 instead of Google Drive.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Create credentials for Google Sheets and Google Drive in n8n
  3. Install the Blotato community node and create Blotato credentials with your API key
  4. Copy the sample Google Sheet template and update with your content
  5. Make your Google Drive folder containing images/videos public (Anyone with the link)
  6. Connect your social media accounts in the Blotato dashboard
  7. Test with one post marked "Ready to Post" in your sheet
  8. Adjust the schedule trigger from 3 hours to your preferred frequency
