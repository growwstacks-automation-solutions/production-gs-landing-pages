What This Workflow Does

Manual compliance review is time-consuming, error-prone, and difficult to scale. Legal teams, compliance officers, and quality assurance professionals spend countless hours comparing documents against policies, often missing subtle requirements or applying standards inconsistently. This creates regulatory risks, delays approvals, and ties up valuable human resources on repetitive tasks.

This automated workflow solves these problems by leveraging AI to validate document compliance instantly. When a document is uploaded, the system processes it through a vector database, compares it against your established policies and procedures using an AI agent, and returns a detailed compliance analysis. The result is consistent, auditable compliance checking that works at scale, freeing your team for higher-value work while reducing regulatory risks.

The system doesn't just check for keywords—it understands context, identifies requirements, and provides specific citations for both compliance and non-compliance findings. This transforms compliance from a bottleneck into a competitive advantage, enabling faster document processing while maintaining or improving accuracy.

How It Works

Step 1: Document Upload and Processing

The workflow begins when a document (PDF, DOCX, etc.) is uploaded via a webhook. The system extracts text content, converts it into vector embeddings using an embedding model, and stores these embeddings in a Qdrant vector database. This creates a searchable representation of the document's content that the AI can understand and compare against policies.

Step 2: Policy Submission and Context Setting

Next, the relevant policy or procedure text is submitted along with descriptive context. This establishes the compliance standard against which the document will be evaluated. The system links this policy to the previously uploaded document using a unique document identifier, ensuring the right standards are applied to each validation.

Step 3: AI-Powered Compliance Analysis

The AI agent receives both the document embeddings and the policy text. It performs a semantic comparison, identifying where the document aligns with requirements and where gaps exist. The analysis goes beyond simple keyword matching to understand intent, requirements, and contextual compliance.

Step 4: Structured Compliance Reporting

The workflow outputs a comprehensive compliance report including a compliance summary, non-compliance findings, specific text citations with page references, and a confidence score. This structured output provides actionable insights for reviewers and creates an audit trail for regulatory purposes.

What You'll Need

  1. A running n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. Access to a vector database like Qdrant (cloud or local)
  3. An embedding model (OpenAI, HuggingFace, Ollama, or similar)
  4. Basic understanding of your compliance requirements and policies
  5. Documents in processable formats (PDF, DOCX, TXT, etc.)
