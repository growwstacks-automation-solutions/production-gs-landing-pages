What This Workflow Does

If you manage learning programs, customer communities, or support groups on WhatsApp, you know the pain: hundreds of messages daily, repeating questions, and no organized way to capture knowledge. This workflow solves that by automatically transforming WhatsApp conversations into structured FAQ documents.

It captures group messages (already saved in Google Sheets), analyzes them weekly using GPT-4 to identify recurring questions and concerns, then generates a clean, organized FAQ document in Google Docs with suggested answers. Instead of spending hours manually reviewing chats, you get a professional knowledge base delivered automatically every Monday.

The system works particularly well for course administrators, community managers, and customer support teams who need to scale their knowledge management without increasing manual labor.

How It Works

Step 1: Data Capture & Weekly Organization

The workflow starts by retrieving all WhatsApp conversations from your Google Sheets database (you need to have messages exported there first). It organizes messages by ISO week number, creating clean blocks of conversation for AI analysis. This preprocessing ensures the AI receives structured data instead of raw chat logs.

Step 2: AI-Powered FAQ Detection

Each weekly message block is sent to an AI Agent (GPT-4) that analyzes the content to identify frequently asked questions, common concerns, and recurring themes. The AI doesn't just find questions—it also suggests appropriate answers based on context and previous discussions in the group.

Step 3: Automated Document Creation

Using the AI's output, the workflow creates a new Google Docs FAQ document based on your template. It structures questions by category, includes suggested answers, and maintains a consistent format week after week. The document is automatically saved to your Google Drive with proper naming and organization.

Step 4: Scheduled Execution

The entire process runs automatically every Monday at 6 AM, ensuring you start each week with updated knowledge documentation. No manual triggering required—it's a true set-and-forget automation.
