Automated funding intelligence: CrunchBase to Google Sheets tracking workflow

Never miss important funding events again with automated tracking of investment data from CrunchBase to your Google Sheets dashboard

What This Workflow Does

This automated workflow solves the time-consuming challenge of manually tracking funding rounds, investments, and financial movements in your industry. Instead of spending hours each week scouring CrunchBase for updates, this system automatically pulls the latest funding data into an organized Google Sheets dashboard.

The workflow connects directly to CrunchBase's API to extract structured data about funding rounds, investor details, company valuations, and acquisition activities. It transforms this raw data into actionable intelligence by organizing it in Google Sheets with consistent formatting, automatic date tracking, and customizable filters.

How It Works

1. CrunchBase API connection

The workflow starts by authenticating with CrunchBase's API using your credentials. It can be configured to search for specific companies, industries, funding ranges, or geographic locations based on your intelligence needs.

2. Data extraction and transformation

Key funding details are extracted from the API response, including round amounts, dates, lead investors, and valuation data. The workflow cleans and standardizes this information, converting dates to consistent formats and normalizing currency values.

3. Google Sheets integration

The processed data is then written to your designated Google Sheet in a structured format. The workflow intelligently appends new records while avoiding duplicates, maintaining a clean historical record of funding activity.

4. Scheduled execution

You can set the workflow to run automatically on a schedule (daily, weekly, etc.), ensuring your funding intelligence stays current without manual intervention. Email or Slack notifications can be added to alert you to significant funding events.

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for venture capital analysts, corporate development teams, investment researchers, and competitive intelligence professionals. Startup founders can use it to track competitor funding, while sales teams can identify newly-funded companies as potential customers.

Business development professionals monitoring for acquisition targets and financial journalists tracking industry trends will also benefit from this automated solution. Essentially, anyone who needs to stay informed about capital flows in their sector can leverage this workflow.
