What This Workflow Does

Manual interview management creates chaos in hiring pipelines. Recruiters waste hours checking calendars, sending reminder emails, chasing feedback, and updating spreadsheets. Candidates miss interviews, hiring managers lack visibility, and important details slip through the cracks.

This automated interview tracker solves these problems by creating a seamless system that connects your Google Calendar, Sheets, and Gmail. It runs every 5 minutes to scan for upcoming interviews, automatically sends preparation reminders to candidates and internal teams, collects results via simple webhook submissions, updates your central tracking sheet, and sends appropriate pass/fail notifications—all without manual intervention.

The result is a professional, consistent hiring process that reduces administrative work by 80%, decreases no-shows by 40%, and gives your recruitment team real-time visibility into every candidate's status.

How It Works

Step 1: Scheduled Calendar Scanning

A schedule trigger runs every 5 minutes to check your Google Calendar for upcoming events. It looks for interviews specifically—filtering by keywords like "Interview," "Screening," or candidate names—to ensure only relevant events are processed.

Step 2: Smart Event Filtering

The workflow intelligently identifies which calendar events represent interviews versus other meetings. It extracts candidate details, interview links, times, and panel information, preparing this data for the next steps.

Step 3: Automated Reminder System

For interviews happening within the next 24-48 hours, the system sends personalized reminder emails to candidates (with interview details and preparation tips) and internal notification emails to interview panels (with candidate information and discussion points).

Step 4: Result Collection via Webhook

After interviews conclude, hiring managers submit simple pass/fail results through a secure webhook form. This can be integrated with your existing tools or used as a standalone feedback collection method.

Step 5: Centralized Tracking in Google Sheets

All interview data—scheduled times, candidate information, results, and feedback—automatically populates a master Google Sheet. This becomes your single source of truth for the entire hiring pipeline, with real-time updates.

Step 6: Conditional Notification Branching

Based on the submitted result, the workflow branches: for passes, it sends congratulatory emails to candidates with next steps; for fails, it sends polite rejection notifications while maintaining positive candidate experience.

What You'll Need

  1. Google Workspace account with access to Calendar, Sheets, and Gmail
  2. Google Sheet prepared with columns for Candidate Email, Interview Link, Status, Result, Meet Link, Feedback, and Updated At
  3. OAuth2 credentials configured for Google Calendar and Gmail API access
  4. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow
  5. Webhook endpoint for result submissions (can be generated within n8n)

Pro tip: Create a dedicated "Hiring" calendar in Google Calendar to separate interview events from other meetings. This makes filtering more accurate and keeps your personal calendar clean.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Google connections by setting up OAuth2 for Calendar and Gmail in n8n's credentials.
  3. Update the Google Sheet ID in the workflow nodes to point to your tracking sheet.
  4. Customize email templates for reminders and notifications to match your company's tone and branding.
  5. Adjust the schedule trigger if needed (default is every 5 minutes).
  6. Test with a dummy interview by creating a test event in your calendar and verifying the full flow.
  7. Activate the workflow and let it run automatically for your real interviews.
