What This Workflow Does

For freelancers, finding the right projects is a constant, time-consuming battle. Manually refreshing job boards, reading lengthy descriptions, and tracking applications across spreadsheets eats into billable hours and causes you to miss prime opportunities.

This automation solves that by acting as your 24/7 job hunting assistant. It systematically scans Upwork for new postings that match your skills, uses artificial intelligence to extract the key details, organizes everything into a master spreadsheet, and pushes a clean summary directly to your inbox. You move from reactive searching to proactive opportunity management.

The result is a significant competitive edge. You see relevant jobs faster, understand client needs instantly, and can submit well-crafted proposals while others are still scrolling. It transforms job hunting from a chore into a streamlined, data-driven component of your business.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Discovery & Scraping

A schedule trigger kicks off the workflow at intervals you set (e.g., every 6 hours). It connects to a web scraping service (like Apify) configured to search Upwork for specific keywords, categories, and budget ranges, pulling in fresh job data automatically.

2. Data Structuring & Filtering

The raw job data is processed. Key fields like job title, description, budget, client history, and posted date are extracted and organized into a consistent format. Initial filters can remove spam, irrelevant postings, or opportunities outside your desired criteria.

3. AI-Powered Analysis & Summarization

Each job description is sent to an AI model (like OpenAI). The AI generates a concise summary, highlighting core requirements, must-have skills, project scope, and potential red flags. This turns pages of text into a few bullet points you can assess in seconds.

4. Centralized Logging to Google Sheets

All processed job data—including the original link, AI summary, and metadata—is appended as a new row in a designated Google Sheet. This creates a permanent, searchable database of all opportunities you've seen, which is invaluable for tracking and analysis.

5. Intelligent Notification Delivery

Finally, a formatted email digest is compiled and sent to you via Gmail or your email service. The digest presents the AI summaries with direct links to apply, allowing you to review and act on multiple opportunities from a single, organized message.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow.
  2. Access to a web scraping tool like Apify, with credentials and a configured "actor" for Upwork.
  3. An OpenAI API key (or alternative AI service) for the summarization step.
  4. A Google account with Sheets and Drive access to create the logging spreadsheet.
  5. Email sending credentials (like Gmail/ SMTP) to receive the notification digests.

Pro tip: Start by scraping a narrow set of keywords for your niche. This prevents overload and ensures high-quality matches. You can always broaden the search later.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button and import the JSON file into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure Credentials: In n8n, set up credentials for your scraping service, OpenAI, Google Sheets, and your email provider.
  3. Customize Search Parameters: In the scraping node, update the keywords, job categories, and any filters to match your expertise.
  4. Set Up Your Spreadsheet: Create a new Google Sheet and note its ID. Update the Google Sheets node in the workflow with this ID and your desired sheet name.
  5. Test & Activate: Run the workflow once manually to ensure all connections work and data flows correctly. Then activate the schedule trigger for automatic runs.
