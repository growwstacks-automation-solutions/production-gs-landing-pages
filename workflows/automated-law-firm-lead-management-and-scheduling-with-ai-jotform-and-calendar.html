What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the critical challenge of converting legal inquiries into booked consultations without manual intervention. When potential clients submit information through your Jotform, the system automatically processes their details, uses AI to qualify the lead based on your criteria, and schedules appointments directly in your calendar.

The automation handles the entire intake funnel: from initial form submission to sending confirmation emails with consultation details. It eliminates the administrative burden of back-and-forth emails, phone tag, and manual calendar coordination that plagues many law practices. By working 24/7, it captures leads even when your office is closed, ensuring no potential client falls through the cracks.

How It Works

1. Client submits Jotform intake questionnaire

The workflow triggers when a new submission arrives in your customized Jotform. The form collects all necessary preliminary information about the potential case, client contact details, and preferred consultation times.

2. AI analyzes and scores the lead

An AI module evaluates the submission against your firm's criteria for case viability, urgency, practice area fit, and potential value. It assigns a qualification score and flags any red flags or special considerations.

3. System checks calendar availability

The workflow interfaces with your calendar system to identify available slots that match both the client's preferences and your firm's scheduling rules (e.g., certain days/times reserved for specific case types).

4. Automated booking confirmation

Once a suitable time is identified, the system sends the client a confirmation email with consultation details, any preparatory materials, and payment instructions if applicable. It simultaneously blocks the time in your calendar.

5. CRM integration and follow-up

The lead data automatically populates your CRM with all relevant details, and the system schedules follow-up reminders based on your firm's workflow (e.g., sending intake documents 24 hours before the consultation).
