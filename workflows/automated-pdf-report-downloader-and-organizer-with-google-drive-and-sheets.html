What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of manually downloading and organizing PDF reports from various sources. Many businesses waste hours each week downloading invoices, statements, reports, and other documents, then struggling to find them later in disorganized folders.

The workflow automatically processes PDF documents by downloading them from specified URLs, applying consistent naming conventions, storing them in properly organized Google Drive folders, extracting key metadata, and maintaining a searchable index in Google Sheets. This creates a complete document management system without manual intervention.

How It Works

1. PDF Download Trigger

The workflow starts when new PDF URLs are added to your trigger source. This could be from an email parser, webhook, or manual list input. The system checks each URL to verify it points to a valid PDF document.

2. Smart File Naming

Each downloaded PDF gets renamed according to your predefined naming convention. This typically includes elements like document type, date, source identifier, and any relevant metadata extracted from the file.

3. Google Drive Organization

The system creates dated subfolders in your designated Google Drive location based on your folder structure preferences. Documents are automatically filed in the correct folder based on their type and metadata.

4. Metadata Extraction

Key information is extracted from each PDF, including text content for searchable PDFs. Common extracted data includes invoice numbers, dates, amounts, and other document-specific fields.

5. Google Sheets Tracking

All documents are logged in a Google Sheet with their metadata, download timestamp, storage location, and direct links. This creates a searchable database of all your documents.
