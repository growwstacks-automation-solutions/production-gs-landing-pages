Automated Website Audit & Personalized Outreach with Lighthouse & GPT-4

Transform cold outreach into data-driven conversations with automated technical audits and AI-generated personalized emails.

What This Workflow Does

Traditional cold outreach for digital marketing services suffers from two critical problems: it feels generic and lacks substance. Prospects receive dozens of "we can improve your website" emails daily, and yours gets lost in the noise. This workflow solves that by automating deep technical analysis and creating genuinely personalized outreach.

The system pulls business details from a Google Sheet (which you can populate using various lead sources), finds company emails through AI-powered scraping, captures website screenshots, runs comprehensive Lighthouse audits covering Performance, SEO, Accessibility, and Best Practices, performs UI/UX analysis using GPT-4, and generates outreach emails that reference specific, data-driven insights about the prospect's actual website issues.

Instead of saying "we can help with SEO," you're saying "your homepage scores 42/100 on performance with a 4.2-second load time, and I noticed three accessibility violations that might be affecting conversions." This transforms you from another salesperson into a consultant who's already done valuable work.

How It Works

Step 1: Lead Ingestion & Data Enrichment

The workflow begins by reading prospect information from a Google Sheet containing business names, websites, and basic details. It then uses AI-powered scraping tools to find relevant contact emails and additional company information, creating a complete prospect profile before any analysis begins.

Step 2: Technical Audit & Screenshot Capture

For each website, the system captures a screenshot of the homepage and runs a comprehensive Lighthouse audit. This generates scores across four critical categories: Performance (loading speed, interactivity), SEO (meta tags, mobile-friendliness), Accessibility (screen reader compatibility), and Best Practices (security, modern web standards).

Step 3: AI-Powered Qualitative Analysis

GPT-4 analyzes the screenshot and audit results to provide qualitative insights that raw metrics can't capture. It identifies design inconsistencies, evaluates content clarity, spots conversion funnel obstacles, and assesses overall user experience, adding the human-like analysis that makes outreach feel genuinely thoughtful.

Step 4: Personalized Email Generation

Using all collected data—technical scores, qualitative insights, company information, and industry context—the AI crafts a personalized outreach email. It highlights the most pressing issues, suggests specific improvements, and positions your services as the logical solution, all while maintaining a consultative, helpful tone.

Step 5: Output & Integration

The completed audit report and generated email are saved back to Google Sheets or your preferred CRM. You can review, add personal touches, and send through your email platform, or connect directly to email automation tools for fully hands-off outreach at scale.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with basic workflow editing permissions
  2. Google Sheets credentials configured in n8n for reading prospect lists and writing results
  3. OpenAI API key with GPT-4 access for qualitative analysis and email generation
  4. Lighthouse node installed in your n8n environment (community node)
  5. Basic understanding of your target audience and service offerings to customize prompts

Pro tip: Start with a small batch of 10-20 prospects to refine your AI prompts and email templates before scaling. Track which audit insights generate the best responses and adjust your workflow accordingly.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file
  2. Configure credentials for Google Sheets and OpenAI in n8n's credential manager
  3. Prepare your prospect sheet with columns for business name, website URL, and any initial notes
  4. Customize the "Set" nodes with your agency details, service offerings, and email signature
  5. Test with 2-3 websites to ensure Lighthouse runs correctly and emails generate properly
  6. Schedule or trigger the workflow based on your prospecting rhythm—daily, weekly, or on-demand
