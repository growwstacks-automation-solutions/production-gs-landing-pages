What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming task of manually creating and sending weekly product promotion emails for e-commerce businesses. By connecting Algolia's powerful product search with Gmail's email delivery, it creates professional, data-driven newsletters automatically.

The workflow pulls your latest product recommendations from Algolia, formats them into an attractive HTML template, and sends them to your subscriber list on a scheduled basis. This ensures your customers regularly see your best products without requiring manual effort from your marketing team.

How It Works

1. Algolia Product Selection

The workflow queries your Algolia index to select products based on your configured criteria - whether that's trending items, seasonal products, or inventory that needs promotion.

2. Email Template Assembly

Selected products are automatically inserted into a pre-designed HTML email template. The template includes product images, prices, and direct links to your store.

3. Subscriber List Management

The system pulls your current email subscriber list from your CRM or database, ensuring you're always sending to up-to-date contacts.

4. Scheduled Delivery

Emails are sent via Gmail at your specified time, typically mid-week when e-commerce engagement peaks. The schedule can be adjusted as needed.

What You'll Need

  1. An active Algolia account with product data indexed
  2. A Gmail account or Google Workspace email
  3. An HTML email template designed for your brand
  4. A subscriber list (can be integrated from your CRM)
  5. n8n instance to run the workflow

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Connect your Algolia credentials in the workflow settings
  3. Configure your product selection criteria (trending, inventory, etc.)
  4. Upload your HTML email template or customize the provided one
  5. Connect your Gmail account and test the email sending
  6. Set your preferred schedule (weekly recommended)
  7. Activate the workflow and monitor performance
