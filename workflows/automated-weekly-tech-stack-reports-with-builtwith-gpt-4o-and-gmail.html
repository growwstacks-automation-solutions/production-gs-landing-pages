What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming challenge of manually tracking competitors' and clients' technology stack changes. Instead of spending hours each week researching website technologies, the workflow automatically compiles a comprehensive report highlighting key changes, trends, and insights.

The system pulls data from BuiltWith to detect new technologies being adopted, then uses GPT-4o to analyze the findings and generate executive summaries. Finally, it delivers polished reports directly to your Gmail inbox on a scheduled basis, keeping your team informed without manual effort.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Data Collection

The workflow runs weekly to scan your predefined list of company websites using BuiltWith's API. It detects all technologies in use and compares them against previous scans to identify changes.

2. AI-Powered Analysis

Raw technology data gets sent to GPT-4o, which identifies meaningful patterns, categorizes changes by importance, and writes concise summaries explaining the business implications of each technology shift.

3. Report Generation

The system compiles findings into a professional report format with visual indicators for significant changes, executive summary, and detailed breakdowns of new technologies detected.

4. Automated Delivery

Completed reports are sent as formatted emails through Gmail, with options for PDF attachments and team distribution lists. The system maintains historical records for trend analysis.

  5. List of target company domains to monitor

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Connect your BuiltWith API credentials in the first node
  3. Add your OpenAI API key in the GPT-4o analysis step
  4. Configure your Gmail account in the email delivery node
  5. Add the domains you want to track in the company list
  6. Set your preferred schedule (weekly recommended)
  7. Test the workflow with a single domain first

While highly reliable for common technologies, it may occasionally miss custom-built solutions or obscure tools. For most competitive intelligence purposes, its accuracy is more than sufficient when combined with human review of key findings.

The most strategic changes to monitor include analytics platforms, marketing tools, ecommerce systems, and security implementations. These often indicate shifts in business strategy or operations.

For example, a competitor switching to Shopify Plus suggests ecommerce expansion, while adopting Hotjar may signal improved user experience focus. The AI analysis in this workflow helps prioritize changes by potential business impact.

GPT-4o transforms raw technology data into actionable insights by explaining what each change means in business context. It can identify patterns across multiple companies and suggest strategic implications.
