What This Workflow Does

Are you tired of constantly opening YouTube to check if your favorite creators have posted new content? This habit creates digital distraction, breaks your focus, and wastes precious time. This automation solves that problem by bringing the notifications to you.

The workflow automatically monitors your YouTube subscriptions, detects new video publications, and sends you a clean, informative email notification for each one. No email means no new videos—eliminating the need to check your feed manually. It transforms your YouTube consumption from a reactive habit to an intentional, scheduled activity.

Beyond personal use, businesses can adapt this system to monitor competitor channels, track industry trends, or stay updated on relevant educational content without manual oversight.

How It Works

The automation runs on a scheduled basis (default: hourly) to provide timely notifications without overwhelming your inbox.

1. Fetch Your Subscriptions

The workflow connects to the YouTube Data API using OAuth2 authentication to retrieve your complete list of subscribed channels. This ensures it monitors exactly the creators you care about.

2. Monitor Channel RSS Feeds

Instead of using YouTube's API for video checks (which has strict rate limits), the system accesses each channel's public RSS feed. This provides reliable, real-time updates on new publications without hitting API quotas.

3. Filter and Process New Videos

The workflow compares newly detected videos against previous runs, filtering out duplicates. By default, it also excludes YouTube Shorts, though this can be customized based on your preferences.

4. Generate and Send Email Notifications

For each new video, the system creates a visually clean email containing the video thumbnail, title, channel name, publication time, and a direct clickable link. These are delivered via your configured SMTP email service.

Pro tip: Configure the schedule trigger to run during your preferred notification times. If you only want to check videos once per day, set it to run in the morning so you can batch your YouTube viewing.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automated YouTube notifications running in minutes:

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure YouTube credentials in the "YouTube OAuth2 API" node by following the authentication prompts.
  3. Set up SMTP credentials in the email node with your email provider's server details.
  4. Update email addresses in the "Send Email" node with your sender and recipient addresses.
  5. Adjust the schedule if desired—the default checks hourly, but you can change this to daily or any interval.
  6. Activate the workflow and test with a manual execution to ensure emails deliver correctly.

Pro tip: To exclude specific channels, add their channel IDs to the "Filter out channels" step. Find a channel's ID by visiting their YouTube page, clicking "Share channel," then "Copy channel ID."
