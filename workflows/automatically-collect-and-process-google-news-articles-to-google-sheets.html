What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming problem of manually tracking news articles by creating a systematic pipeline from Google News to organized spreadsheet data. Instead of wasting hours browsing multiple sources, the workflow automatically collects, cleans, and structures relevant articles based on your predefined search terms.

For businesses, this means always having up-to-date market intelligence without manual effort. The workflow filters out duplicates, extracts key metadata, and formats everything for easy analysis in Google Sheets - perfect for creating dashboards, reports, or alerts when important news breaks.

How It Works

1. RSS Feed Collection

The workflow starts by pulling articles from Google News RSS feeds based on your configured search queries. You can monitor specific keywords, companies, or industry terms across all news sources or select publications.

2. Data Extraction & Cleaning

Each article's metadata is extracted, including title, source, publication date, summary, and direct link. The workflow standardizes date formats, removes HTML tags from summaries, and handles character encoding issues.

3. Deduplication Process

Before adding new entries, the system checks for existing articles with matching titles or URLs in your Google Sheet. This prevents duplicate entries from different news sources covering the same story.

4. Google Sheets Integration

Cleaned and deduplicated articles are appended to your specified Google Sheet with proper column formatting. The workflow maintains data integrity while adding new rows, preserving formulas and data validation rules.
