What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common productivity challenge of important emails getting buried in your inbox. When you label an email as "To-Do" in Gmail, the workflow automatically creates a corresponding task in Google Tasks with all relevant details. The subject becomes the task title, the body becomes notes, and any mentioned dates are captured as due dates.

Professionals waste an average of 5 minutes per flagged email manually copying information between systems. This workflow eliminates that friction while ensuring no action item falls through the cracks. It's particularly valuable for client-facing roles, project managers, and anyone juggling multiple ongoing conversations that require follow-up.

How It Works

1. Email Detection

The workflow monitors your Gmail account for new emails with the "To-Do" label. This trigger ensures only emails you've manually flagged as actionable get processed.

2. Content Extraction

Key information is extracted from the email including subject line, sender, body text, and any attachments. The system intelligently parses dates mentioned in the email to suggest due dates.

3. Task Creation

A new task is created in your Google Tasks list with the email subject as the title. The full email content is included as notes, preserving important context. If dates were detected, they're set as the task due date.
