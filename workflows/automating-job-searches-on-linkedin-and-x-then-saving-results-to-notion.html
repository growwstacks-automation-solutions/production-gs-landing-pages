What This Workflow Does

Manual job hunting is time-consuming and inefficient. You spend hours scrolling through LinkedIn and X (formerly Twitter), copying links, taking notes, and trying to organize everything in spreadsheets. Opportunities slip through the cracks, and you waste precious time on repetitive searching instead of preparing applications.

This automation solves that by creating a systematic, hands-free job discovery pipeline. It automatically searches for relevant positions across LinkedIn and X based on your customized criteria—job titles, locations, experience levels, and keywords. The workflow then filters out irrelevant listings (like contract roles if you want full-time) and saves the polished results directly into your Notion database.

Think of it as your personal recruitment assistant that works 24/7. It ensures you see every relevant opportunity as soon as it's posted, gives you a clean, organized list to review, and eliminates the manual grunt work. You get more time for what actually matters: tailoring applications and networking.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence to find, process, and store job opportunities.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

A scheduler triggers the workflow daily at your chosen time (default is 5 AM). This ensures fresh results without manual intervention. You can adjust frequency based on your search urgency—daily for competitive roles, weekly for broader searches.

Step 2: LinkedIn Search Execution

The workflow sends structured search queries to LinkedIn's job listings. It uses parameters you define: keywords like "senior product designer" or "AI designer," location filters (remote, specific cities), experience levels, and date posted ranges. The system mimics a human search but does it consistently and thoroughly.

Step 3: X (Twitter) Opportunity Scan

Simultaneously, the automation scans X for job posts from recruiters, companies, and industry leaders. It searches hashtags, keywords, and specific accounts to find opportunities that might not appear on traditional job boards. This taps into the "hidden" job market.

Step 4: Data Cleaning & Filtering

Raw results from both platforms pass through filtering nodes. These remove duplicates, exclude jobs containing unwanted terms (like "contract" or "freelance" if you prefer full-time), and verify that critical information—job title, company, application link—is present. Only clean, complete listings move forward.

Step 5: Notion Database Population

Each qualified job is formatted into a standardized record and added to your Notion database. Fields typically include job title, company, location, salary range (if available), application URL, date found, and status. This creates a single, searchable hub for all your opportunities.

Pro tip: Add a "Priority" column in Notion to rank jobs by fit. Use the automation to tag listings based on keywords like "remote," "senior," or specific technologies you excel in.

Quick Setup Guide

Get your automated job search running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Download the Template: Click the download button above to get the JSON file.
  2. Import to Zapier: In your Zapier dashboard, create a new Zap and use the import function to load the template.
  3. Connect Your Accounts: Authorize Zapier to access your LinkedIn (via webhooks or RSS if available), X, and Notion accounts.
  4. Customize Search Parameters: In the "Set Search Criteria" step, update keywords, locations, and filters to match your target roles.
  5. Configure Your Notion Database: Ensure your Notion database has columns matching the output fields: Title, Company, Location, URL, Date Posted, Status.
  6. Set the Schedule: Choose how often the search runs—daily for fresh results, weekly for broader monitoring.
  7. Test & Activate: Run a test to see sample jobs populate your Notion database, then activate the automation.

Pro tip: Start with broad searches, then refine based on results. It's easier to filter out noise than to miss opportunities because your criteria were too narrow initially.
