What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical challenge for legal teams and compliance officers: determining which Large Language Model (LLM) performs best for specific legal document tasks. Instead of manually testing different AI models—a process that can take days—this workflow automatically runs parallel evaluations across multiple LLMs using test cases stored in Google Sheets.

The system pulls legal document samples from Google Drive, sends them to various AI models via OpenRouter (including GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, and others), and systematically records accuracy, response time, and cost metrics. This enables data-driven decisions about which AI delivers the most reliable results for contract analysis, compliance checking, clause extraction, or legal research—all while controlling costs and ensuring consistent evaluation standards.

By automating what would otherwise be a tedious manual process, legal professionals can focus on interpreting results rather than administering tests, ultimately leading to better AI adoption decisions and more efficient document processing workflows.

How It Works

1. Test Case Retrieval

The workflow begins by reading your evaluation spreadsheet from Google Sheets. Each row contains a test case with document references, expected outputs, and scoring criteria. This structured approach ensures consistent testing across all LLM models.

2. Parallel Processing Engine

Unlike sequential testing that waits for each response, this workflow fires multiple requests simultaneously (2 per second by default). This parallel processing dramatically reduces total testing time from hours to minutes, especially when evaluating dozens of documents across multiple AI models.

3. Document Processing & LLM Querying

For each test case, the system retrieves the corresponding legal document from Google Drive, converts it to text, and sends it to OpenRouter with your specific prompt. OpenRouter acts as a unified gateway to 50+ different LLMs, allowing apples-to-apples comparisons without managing multiple API integrations.

4. Results Logging & Analysis

All LLM responses are automatically recorded back to Google Sheets alongside the original test cases. The workflow can be extended to calculate accuracy scores, compare against expected outputs, and generate performance dashboards—giving you clear visibility into which models work best for your specific legal use cases.
