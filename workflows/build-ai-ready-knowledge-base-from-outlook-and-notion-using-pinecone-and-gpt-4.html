What This Workflow Does

Businesses accumulate valuable knowledge in two key places: email conversations (Outlook) and internal documentation (Notion). But this information sits siloed, making it difficult for AI assistants and support teams to access the right context when needed.

This automation solves that by automatically building and maintaining a unified, AI-ready knowledge base. It captures relevant Outlook emails and Notion pages, processes them into searchable vectors, and stores everything in Pinecone. Your AI agents can then reference this knowledge to generate accurate, context-aware responses, draft emails in your company's tone, and answer questions based on actual documentation.

The system creates a "living" knowledge base that updates automatically as new information arrives, eliminating manual copying, formatting, and categorization work.

How It Works

1. Outlook Email Capture

When you move an email thread to a designated folder (like "Knowledge Base"), the workflow triggers. It captures the entire conversation, removes duplicate messages, and prepares the content for storage. This gives your AI agent access to real customer interactions, common questions, and your team's communication style.

2. Notion Documentation Sync

When a new page is added to your selected Notion database, the workflow extracts the content, headings, and key information. This includes policies, product details, procedures, and reference materials that form the factual backbone of your knowledge base.

3. Vector Processing & Storage

The content from both sources is converted into numerical vectors using Cohere's embedding model (which supports multiple languages). These vectors capture semantic meaning, not just keywords. They're then stored in Pinecone under separate namespaces—"emails" for communication context and "knowledgebase" for documentation.

4. AI Agent Empowerment

Your AI agent (using OpenAI's GPT-4) is equipped with two Pinecone tools. When queried, it searches both namespaces to retrieve the most relevant emails and documentation. This allows it to answer with specific examples from past conversations and accurate information from your documentation.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. Microsoft Outlook account with API access
  3. Notion account and a database to monitor
  4. Pinecone account for vector storage
  5. Cohere API key for embeddings
  6. OpenAI API key for the AI model

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template into your n8n workspace.
  2. Connect your Outlook account and specify which folder to watch (e.g., "Knowledgebase").
  3. Connect your Notion account and select the target database.
  4. Add your Pinecone, Cohere, and OpenAI credentials in the respective nodes.
  5. Test with a sample email and Notion page to verify storage.
  6. Activate the workflow—it will now automatically update your knowledge base.

Pro tip: Start with a specific Outlook folder and one Notion database to test the system. Once working, you can expand to multiple sources by duplicating the capture nodes.
