What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the constant challenge of producing fresh, engaging content for your WordPress blog. Instead of spending hours researching topics, writing articles, optimizing for SEO, and manually publishing, this workflow handles everything automatically.

The system fetches trending news and topics from multiple RSS feeds, uses AI to analyze relevance to your niche, generates complete SEO-optimized articles with proper structure and keywords, creates matching images, and publishes everything to your WordPress site. It maintains a consistent publishing schedule that search engines love while freeing your team for higher-value work.

How It Works

1. Topic Discovery & Research

The workflow begins by scanning multiple RSS feeds from sources like TechCrunch, BBC, and industry-specific publications. It filters for recent articles (last 24 hours) and uses AI classification to identify topics relevant to your predefined interests.

2. Content Generation & Optimization

For each relevant topic, the AI generates multiple title options, selects the best based on SEO potential, creates a detailed outline with H1/H2 headers, then writes a complete 1000-1500 word article. The content includes analogies, examples, and proper keyword density for optimal search performance.

3. Image Creation & Enhancement

Based on the article theme, the system generates a custom featured image using AI image generation. It optimizes the image for emotional impact and visual consistency with your brand, then prepares it for web publication.

4. WordPress Publishing & SEO Setup

The complete article, metadata, and image are sent to your WordPress site via REST API. The workflow sets up proper Yoast SEO metadata, categories, tags, and publishes the post. Everything is logged in a database for performance tracking and future optimization.

What You'll Need

  1. A WordPress website with REST API access enabled
  2. OpenAI API key for content generation (GPT-4o-mini recommended)
  3. Leonardo AI account for image generation (or alternative image AI service)
  4. MongoDB Atlas account for logging and performance tracking (free tier works)
  5. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the automation

Pro tip: Start with a reduced frequency (2-3 posts per week) to test quality before scaling to daily publishing. Monitor engagement metrics and adjust your topic filters based on what performs best with your audience.
