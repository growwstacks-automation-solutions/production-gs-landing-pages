What This Workflow Does

Managing email campaigns manually is time-consuming and error-prone. This automation solves that by connecting your lead sources directly to Emelia, automating the entire campaign lifecycle. Instead of manually creating campaigns, copying email lists, and checking performance, this workflow handles it all in the background.

The template performs three key actions sequentially: first, it creates a new campaign in your Emelia account with predefined settings. Next, it adds a contact to that campaign—perfect for when new leads come in from forms, CRMs, or spreadsheets. Finally, it retrieves the campaign details, giving you instant visibility into its status and setup.

This end-to-end automation is ideal for businesses running consistent email sequences for sales outreach, customer onboarding, or newsletter campaigns. It ensures no lead falls through the cracks and that your campaign data is always synchronized with your other tools.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical three-step process that mirrors how you'd manually manage campaigns, but entirely automated.

Step 1: Campaign Creation

The first Emelia node creates a new campaign in your account. You can configure campaign parameters like name, subject lines, email content, and scheduling directly in the node settings. This step can be triggered by various events—a new product launch, a scheduled time, or when a certain number of leads accumulate in your database.

Step 2: Contact Addition

Once the campaign exists, the workflow adds a contact to it. This is where you'd typically connect a data source like Google Sheets, Airtable, or a CRM. The node takes contact details (email, name, custom fields) and subscribes them to the newly created campaign, ensuring they receive the appropriate email sequence.

Step 3: Campaign Verification

The final Emelia node retrieves the campaign information, confirming it was created correctly and providing details like ID, status, and settings. This data can be sent to notifications (Slack, email) or logged in a database for auditing purposes, giving you confidence that your automation executed successfully.

What You'll Need

  1. An Emelia account with API access enabled
  2. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow
  3. Your Emelia API credentials to authenticate the connection
  4. A data source (optional but recommended) like Google Sheets, Airtable, or a CRM where your contacts are stored
  5. Basic understanding of your email campaign structure and goals

Pro tip: Before automating at scale, test the workflow with a small segment of contacts and a simple campaign. This helps you verify everything works correctly and allows you to refine your email content based on initial engagement metrics.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure the Emelia nodes by adding your API credentials in each node's settings.
  3. Set up your campaign details in the first Emelia node—name, emails, timing, etc.
  4. Connect your contact source (if using) by adding a node before the "Add Contact" step—Google Sheets, Airtable, or HTTP request nodes work well.
  5. Test the workflow manually using the "Execute Workflow" button to ensure all three steps complete successfully.
  6. Add a trigger to automate execution—schedule, webhook, or database trigger based on your needs.
