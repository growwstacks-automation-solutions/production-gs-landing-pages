What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common challenge of maintaining a personal journal when most of your meaningful interactions happen in digital conversations. It automatically transforms your Discord chats into a structured, visual journal in Notion using AI analysis and image generation.

Each night, the workflow processes your day's conversations, using GPT-4 to identify key themes, emotional tones, and important moments. DALL-E then creates unique images representing these insights, resulting in a rich multimedia journal that captures your day more vividly than text alone could.

How It Works

1. Discord Message Collection

The workflow begins by gathering your messages from specified Discord channels at a scheduled time (typically end-of-day). It filters for meaningful conversations while ignoring casual chats or commands.

2. AI Analysis with GPT-4

Your collected messages are sent to GPT-4, which performs sentiment analysis, identifies key topics, and summarizes the day's most significant interactions. The AI structures this into a coherent journal entry format.

3. Visual Generation with DALL-E

Based on the analysis, the system prompts DALL-E to generate 1-3 images representing the day's emotional tone or main themes. These could be abstract representations or concrete illustrations of key moments.

4. Notion Journal Creation

The final step creates a new page in your Notion journal database, combining the AI-generated text summary with the DALL-E images in an aesthetically pleasing layout. Entries are automatically dated and tagged for easy reference.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)

  3. OpenAI API access for GPT-4 and DALL-E
  4. A Notion account with a pre-configured journal database
  5. Basic familiarity with n8n workflow configuration

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the JSON template file
  2. Import it into your n8n instance
  3. Configure the Discord node with your server/channel IDs
  4. Add your OpenAI API key to the GPT-4 and DALL-E nodes
  5. Connect the Notion node to your journal database
  6. Set your preferred schedule (we recommend 10pm daily)
  7. Test with a small sample of messages before full activation
