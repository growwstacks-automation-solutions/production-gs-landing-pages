What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of information overload by intelligently curating content from multiple RSS feeds and transforming them into a concise, personalized daily newsletter. Instead of manually scanning dozens of news sources or receiving generic newsletters, this workflow uses Google Gemini AI to analyze, summarize, and personalize content based on your preferences.

The system automatically fetches the latest articles from your selected RSS feeds, processes them through Gemini AI to extract key insights, removes duplicate content, and formats everything into an easy-to-read email digest. You'll receive only the most relevant information tailored to your interests, saving hours each week while staying better informed.

How It Works

1. RSS Feed Collection

The workflow begins by pulling the latest articles from your configured RSS feeds. You can add any number of sources - industry blogs, news sites, or even Google Alerts converted to RSS.

2. Content Filtering

Articles are filtered based on your predefined criteria (keywords, categories, or sentiment analysis) to ensure only relevant content proceeds to the next stage.

3. AI Processing with Google Gemini

Selected articles are sent to Google Gemini AI which performs several functions: summarizes long articles, identifies key takeaways, removes redundant information across sources, and adapts the writing style to your preferences.

4. Newsletter Assembly

The processed content is organized into a clean HTML email template with categorized sections, prioritized headlines, and optional personal commentary from the AI.

5. Scheduled Delivery

The completed newsletter is sent at your preferred time each day via your email service of choice (Gmail, Outlook, Mailchimp, etc.).

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for professionals, researchers, and knowledge workers who need to stay updated but don't have time to manually review multiple news sources. Executives, marketers, investors, and consultants will particularly benefit from having AI-curated insights delivered daily.

Content creators and community managers can also adapt this system to automatically generate newsletter content for their audiences, saving significant time on content curation while providing more value.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. Google Gemini API access
  3. RSS feed URLs for your preferred news sources
  4. Email service credentials (SMTP or API)
  5. Basic understanding of n8n workflows (or willingness to learn)

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure your RSS feed URLs in the HTTP Request nodes
  3. Add your Google Gemini API key in the AI node
  4. Set up your email service credentials in the Email node
  5. Adjust the schedule trigger to your preferred delivery time
  6. Test with a small number of feeds before scaling up

AI-powered newsletters provide several advantages over basic RSS readers. Google Gemini analyzes content at a deeper level, identifying key themes, summarizing complex information, and connecting related stories across sources. Unlike simple RSS aggregators that just republish full articles, AI can tailor content length, highlight most relevant sections, and even adapt writing style to your preferences.

For example, a marketing professional might receive summaries that emphasize campaign strategies and industry trends, while an investor gets versions focused on financial implications. This contextual understanding creates more valuable digests than chronological article lists.

  • Identifies connections between disparate sources
  • Adapts summaries to reader's knowledge level
  • Filters out redundant information across feeds

The system works best with RSS feeds that provide full article content or substantial excerpts. News sites, industry blogs, and research publications typically offer high-quality feeds. Avoid feeds that only provide headlines or very short snippets, as these don't contain enough content for meaningful summarization.

Specialized industry sources often yield better results than general news aggregators. For instance, a technology executive would benefit more from curated feeds like Hacker News for tech, Stratechery for strategy, and niche industry newsletters than from generic business news.

  • Prioritize feeds with complete article text
  • Mix broad coverage with niche sources
  • Include 3-5 high-quality feeds per interest area

Personalization occurs at multiple levels in this automation. First, you configure initial preferences like keywords, topics of interest, and preferred summary length. Over time, Gemini adapts the newsletter's content selection, prioritization, and even writing style. For example, if you consistently engage with financial analysis but skip cultural commentary, the system will emphasize financial insights while reducing less relevant content. This dynamic adjustment creates a truly personalized experience.

  • Initial setup captures explicit preferences
  • AI learns from reading behavior patterns
  • Content weighting adjusts automatically

While powerful, AI newsletter automation has some limitations. Complex topics requiring deep expertise may be oversimplified in summaries. The system also relies on source feed quality - poorly written or intentionally misleading content may produce subpar summaries. Visual content like charts and infographics often don't translate well to text summaries.

Timeliness can be another challenge for rapidly evolving stories, as the workflow runs on a schedule rather than real-time. For breaking news, you might still need to check primary sources. These limitations mean AI newsletters work best as complements to, rather than complete replacements for, all information sources.

  • May oversimplify complex subjects
  • Depends on source content quality
  • Works best on scheduled rather than real-time basis
