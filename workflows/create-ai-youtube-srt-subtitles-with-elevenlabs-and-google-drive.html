How It Works

The automation follows a streamlined process that mimics what a human editor would do, but at machine speed and consistency.

Step 1: Video Input & Audio Extraction

The workflow starts when you provide a YouTube video URL. It fetches the video content and extracts the audio track, preparing it for speech recognition. This step handles various video formats and qualities, ensuring clean audio input for accurate transcription.

Step 2: AI Speech Recognition with ElevenLabs

The extracted audio is sent to ElevenLabs' speech recognition API, which converts spoken words into text with timestamp information. The AI identifies speaker changes, handles different accents, and recognizes technical vocabulary with impressive accuracy.

Step 3: Intelligent Text Segmentation

Raw transcription is processed into readable subtitle segments. The system splits text based on natural pauses, sentence boundaries, and optimal reading speed (typically 2-3 lines of 35-40 characters each). This ensures subtitles are comfortable to read while matching the video's pacing.

Step 4: SRT Format Generation

The segmented text with precise timestamps is formatted into standard SRT (SubRip Text) format. Each entry includes sequential numbering, start/end times in hours:minutes:seconds,milliseconds format, and the subtitle text. This format is universally compatible with video platforms and editing software.

Step 5: Google Drive Storage & Organization

The final SRT file is automatically uploaded to your specified Google Drive folder, organized by date, project, or video title. The workflow can also trigger notifications or update project management tools when subtitles are ready for review or publication.

Pro tip: For best results, ensure your source video has clear audio with minimal background noise. Consider adding a step to normalize audio levels before transcription if working with varied source materials.

SRT (SubRip Text) is the most widely supported subtitle format with simple timecoding. Unlike VTT or SSA, SRT files are compatible with virtually all video platforms, editing software, and media players without conversion. They contain sequential numbered entries with start/end times and text.

SRT's simplicity makes it ideal for automation—easy to generate, edit, and process. While other formats support advanced features like styling or positioning, SRT's universal compatibility makes it the practical choice for most distribution scenarios, especially when automating at scale.

  • Universal compatibility across platforms
  • Simple format ideal for automation
  • Easy to convert to other formats if needed
