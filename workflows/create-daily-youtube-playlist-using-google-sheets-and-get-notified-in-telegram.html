What This Workflow Does

If you follow multiple YouTube channels for news, education, or entertainment, you know the struggle: endless browsing through subscriptions, missing important uploads, and wasting time trying to organize what to watch. This automation solves that by acting as your personal video curator.

The workflow reads a list of your preferred YouTube channels from a Google Sheet, fetches the latest videos uploaded in the last 24 hours, creates a fresh daily playlist on your YouTube account, and sends you an instant notification on Telegram when it's ready. It even cleans up by deleting yesterday's playlist, keeping everything organized automatically.

How It Works

The automation runs on a daily schedule, executing a precise sequence that connects three powerful platforms.

Step 1: Read Your Channel List from Google Sheets

The workflow starts by connecting to your Google Sheet, which acts as a simple database. You maintain a list with columns for Channel Username, Channel Name, Channel Link, and Channel ID. This centralized control allows anyone on your team to update the channel list without touching the automation code.

Step 2: Fetch Latest Videos from YouTube API

For each channel in your sheet, the automation queries YouTube's API to retrieve the most recent videos (typically the last 8) uploaded within the past 24 hours. It can pull from any public channel, whether you're subscribed or not—perfect for monitoring competitors or industry content without subscription clutter.

Step 3: Create a Date-Stamped Playlist

Using the fetched videos, the workflow creates a new YouTube playlist on your account with a clear naming convention (like "Daily Watch - YYMMDD"). This creates a clean, chronological archive of daily content that's easy to reference later.

Step 4: Send Telegram Notification

Once the playlist is created, the automation sends a formatted message to your Telegram account with the playlist title, link, and count of videos added. This instant alert means you never have to manually check if the automation ran successfully.

Step 5: Clean Up Previous Playlist

Finally, the workflow identifies and deletes the playlist created on the previous day, preventing playlist bloat on your YouTube account and maintaining a clean, rolling window of content.

What You'll Need

  1. A YouTube account with API access enabled (free through Google Cloud Console)
  2. A Google Sheets document with the proper column structure for channel management
  3. A Telegram account and bot token (free to create via BotFather)
  4. n8n instance (self-hosted or cloud) to run the automation
  5. Basic understanding of connecting APIs via OAuth (we provide step-by-step instructions)

Pro tip: Start with just 3-5 channels in your Google Sheet to test the workflow. Once confirmed, you can scale to dozens of channels. The automation handles rate limits automatically.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance.
  2. Create your Google Sheet with columns: Channel User Name, Channel Name, Channel Link, Channel ID.
  3. Set up credentials in n8n for YouTube API, Google Sheets, and Telegram Bot.
  4. Update the workflow nodes to point to your specific Sheet ID and Telegram chat ID.
  5. Test with a manual trigger before enabling the daily schedule.
  6. Disable the "Delete Yesterday's Playlist" branch on the first run (as there's no yesterday's playlist yet).
  7. Enable the schedule trigger for daily execution at your preferred time.
