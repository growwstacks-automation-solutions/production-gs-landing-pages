Create Salesforce Accounts From Google Sheets — Automatically

Stop manually copying contacts from spreadsheets into Salesforce. This n8n workflow reads your Google Sheet, checks for existing accounts, creates missing ones, and syncs all contacts — no code, no duplicates, no wasted hours.

What This Workflow Does

If your sales or operations team maintains prospect and client data in Google Sheets but uses Salesforce as the CRM of record, you know the pain: someone has to manually create accounts, check for duplicates, and then add each contact one by one. For a sheet with 50 contacts, that's easily 2-3 hours of repetitive data entry — and one typo can create duplicate records that haunt your reporting for months.

This n8n workflow eliminates that entire process. It reads your Google Sheet, searches Salesforce for each account by name, creates accounts that don't exist yet, normalises the data across both branches, and then creates or updates the contacts in Salesforce. The whole thing runs in seconds, handles edge cases cleanly, and never creates duplicates.

How It Works

Step 1: Read Data From Google Sheets

The workflow starts by pulling every row from your designated Google Sheet. Each row should contain contact information (name, email, phone) along with the associated account/company name. You simply paste your Sheet ID into the Google Sheets node — no API configuration beyond standard OAuth.

Copy your Sheet ID from the Google Sheets URL — this is all the workflow needs to connect to your data.

Step 2: Search Salesforce for Existing Accounts

For each contact's account name, the workflow queries Salesforce to check whether an account with that name already exists. This is the critical deduplication step — it prevents the workflow from creating duplicate accounts that would clutter your CRM and throw off reporting.

Step 3: Branch — Create or Skip

The workflow splits into two paths based on the search result. If an account doesn't exist in Salesforce, the workflow creates it with the relevant details from your spreadsheet. If the account already exists, it skips creation and moves directly to contact handling. Both branches then converge so every contact gets processed regardless of whether their account was new or existing.

Paste the Sheet ID into the Google Sheets node — the workflow handles everything else from here.

Step 4: Create or Update Contacts

With all accounts resolved (existing ones found, new ones created), the workflow normalises the data from both branches into a consistent format. It then creates new contacts or updates existing ones in Salesforce, linking each contact to the correct account. The end result is a fully synced Salesforce CRM with zero manual entry.

Pro tip: Schedule this workflow to run daily or weekly using n8n's Cron trigger instead of the manual trigger. That way, your team can keep adding rows to the Google Sheet and Salesforce stays automatically in sync without anyone clicking a button.

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for sales teams managing prospect lists in spreadsheets before they're ready for CRM entry, operations teams onboarding batches of new clients from partner referrals or events, agencies importing client rosters from shared documents, and any business where data lives in Google Sheets but needs to end up in Salesforce without the copy-paste marathon.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance — self-hosted or n8n Cloud
  2. Google account — with access to the source Google Sheet
  3. Salesforce account — with API access enabled
  4. Google Sheet — with columns for contact name, email, phone, and account/company name

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the JSON template using the button above
  2. In n8n, go to Workflows → Import from File and select the downloaded JSON
  3. Open the Google Sheets node and connect your Google account credentials
  4. Paste your Sheet ID into the Google Sheets node configuration
  5. Open the Salesforce nodes and connect your Salesforce credentials
  6. Verify the field mapping matches your Google Sheet column names
  7. Click Execute Workflow to run the sync
