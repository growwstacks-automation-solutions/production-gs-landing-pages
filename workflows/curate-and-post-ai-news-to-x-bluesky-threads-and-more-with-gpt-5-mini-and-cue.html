What This Workflow Does

Manually sharing AI and tech news across multiple social platforms is a massive time sink. This automation solves that by creating a complete, hands-off content pipeline. It scans trusted RSS feeds daily, uses AI to craft platform-specific posts, and prepares everything for review—freeing up hours each week for strategic work.

The workflow pulls from sources like TechCrunch AI, Ars Technica AI, The Verge AI, and MIT Tech Review. It filters for recent articles, checks against a Google Sheets log to avoid duplicates, selects one story, and uses GPT-5 mini to generate five unique posts tailored for X, Bluesky, Threads, Mastodon, and Facebook. All posts are saved as drafts in Cue for your final review before publishing.

This isn't just about saving time—it's about building consistent thought leadership. Regular, valuable updates keep your audience engaged and position you as a reliable source in the fast-moving AI space, all without daily manual effort.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Trigger & Feed Collection

A schedule trigger (default: daily at 9 AM) initiates the workflow. It simultaneously fetches articles from your configured RSS feeds, combining them into a single stream of potential content.

2. Deduplication & Selection

The workflow queries a Google Sheet that logs all previously posted articles. It compares new items against this history, filters out anything older than seven days, and randomly selects one fresh, unposted article to ensure variety and avoid repetition.

3. AI-Powered Content Generation

The selected article's URL and metadata are sent to the OpenAI GPT-5 mini node. A custom prompt instructs the AI to generate five distinct social posts—each optimized for the tone, style, and character limits of X, Bluesky, Threads, Mastodon, and Facebook.

4. Draft Creation & Logging

The five generated posts are sent to the Cue node, which creates a single draft containing all platform variations within your connected Cue account. Simultaneously, the article's details are written back to the Google Sheet, creating a permanent record to prevent future duplicates.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automated news curator running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Install the Community Node: In your n8n settings, go to "Community Nodes" and install @cuehq/n8n-nodes-cue.
  2. Create Your Tracking Sheet: Create a new Google Sheet named "AI News Tracker" with columns: article_url, title, source, processed_at.
  3. Configure Credentials: In the workflow, set up credentials for Google Sheets (OAuth2), OpenAI (API Key), and Cue (API Key from your Cue settings).
  4. Connect Your Social Accounts: In the "Create Draft in Cue" node, select your Cue profile and map each platform slot (1-5) to your connected social accounts.
  5. Customize & Activate: Adjust the schedule trigger if needed, review the AI prompt for tone, then click "Activate" to publish the workflow.

Pro tip: Start by running the workflow manually once to test the connection flow and see a sample draft appear in your Cue account. This verifies all credentials and permissions are working before enabling the schedule.
