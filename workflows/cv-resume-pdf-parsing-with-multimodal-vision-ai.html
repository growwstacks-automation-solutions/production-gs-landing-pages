What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical problem in modern recruitment: efficiently processing hundreds of resumes while maintaining accuracy and preventing manipulation. Traditional applicant tracking systems rely on text extraction, which can be fooled by hidden formatting or miss visual context. This workflow uses multimodal AI vision to convert PDF resumes to images, then analyzes them with advanced language models like Google Gemini or GPT-4.

The system automatically downloads candidate resumes from sources like Google Drive, converts them to image format to neutralize hidden text tricks, and uses AI to extract structured candidate data. It can filter applications based on qualifications, years of experience, skills, and education—processing in minutes what would take recruiters hours to review manually.

How It Works

1. Resume Collection & Preparation

The workflow starts by fetching PDF resumes from your designated source—this could be Google Drive, email attachments, an ATS export, or a webhook from your career page. Each PDF is prepared for AI analysis by converting it to a high-quality image format.

2. PDF to Image Conversion

Using a service like Stirling PDF (self-hosted for privacy), the workflow converts PDF pages to PNG images. This step is crucial because it removes any hidden text, white-on-white formatting, or other visual tricks candidates might use to game automated systems. The image preserves all visual elements including layout, fonts, and formatting.

3. Multimodal AI Analysis

The image is sent to a multimodal AI model (Google Gemini 1.5 Pro or GPT-4 Vision) configured with specific instructions. The AI analyzes the visual content, extracts structured data, and evaluates candidate qualifications against your criteria. It can understand complex resume layouts, tables, and even handwritten notes.

4. Data Extraction & Filtering

The AI extracts key information: candidate name, contact details, work history, education, skills, certifications, and more. Conditional logic then filters candidates based on your requirements—for example, "5+ years of experience in Python" or "Master's degree in Computer Science."

5. Output & Integration

Structured candidate data is output to your preferred destination: your ATS, CRM, Google Sheets, or internal database. The workflow can trigger notifications, create candidate profiles, or even schedule initial interviews for qualified applicants.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n instance via the workflow import function.
  2. Configure credentials: Set up your Google Gemini API key in n8n's credentials manager. Add access to your resume source (Google Drive, etc.).
  3. Set up PDF conversion: Deploy a private instance of Stirling PDF or configure your preferred PDF-to-image service.

  5. Test with sample resumes: Run the workflow with a few test resumes to verify extraction accuracy and adjust prompts as needed.
  6. Connect outputs: Configure the final nodes to send processed data to your ATS, CRM, or notification system.
