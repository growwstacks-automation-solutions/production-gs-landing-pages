Cybersecurity Intelligence: Create Daily Digest & Viral Topics with Gemini AI

Automate the analysis of security reports. Summarize, deduplicate, and identify trending threats—delivered daily to your inbox.

What This Workflow Does

Security teams are drowning in information. Hundreds of cybersecurity reports, advisories, and news articles flood in daily, making it impossible to manually track everything. Critical threats get missed, analysts suffer burnout, and response times slow down.

This automation solves that by creating an intelligent filtering and summarization system. It collects reports from RSS feeds, uses AI to understand and categorize them, removes duplicates, and delivers two clear outputs: a daily digest of organized threats and a viral topics report highlighting emerging patterns over the past week.

Think of it as your automated security analyst—working 24/7 to read, summarize, and alert you to what truly matters, so your human experts can focus on strategic response.

How It Works

Step 1: Collect & Merge Intelligence Feeds

The workflow triggers every morning, pulling the latest articles from configured cybersecurity RSS feeds (like CISA, KrebsOnSecurity, or vendor blogs). It merges all sources into a single stream of raw intelligence.

Step 2: AI-Powered Processing & Deduplication

Google Gemini AI reads each article, extracts key entities (threat actors, CVEs, techniques), writes concise summaries, and identifies duplicate reports covering the same incident. This reduces noise by 60–80%.

Step 3: Generate & Send Daily Digest

The AI organizes remaining unique reports into logical categories (e.g., "Ransomware," "Zero-Days," "Phishing Campaigns") and formats a professional email digest. This is sent automatically to your security team's inbox.

Step 4: Track Viral Topics Over Time

The system stores each day's digest. Every week, it reviews the last seven days, using Gemini Pro to identify topics that appear repeatedly or are gaining momentum—signaling emerging threats that need extra attention.

Step 5: Deliver Weekly Trend Report

A second email is generated highlighting these "viral" topics with context on why they're trending, potential impact, and recommended actions. This provides strategic foresight beyond daily alerts.

Who This Is For

This template is ideal for small to mid-sized security teams, IT managers responsible for threat monitoring, MSSPs (Managed Security Service Providers) servicing multiple clients, and compliance officers needing documented continuous monitoring. If you're manually scanning feeds or relying on fragmented alerts, this automation consolidates your intelligence workflow.

Pro tip: Start with 3–5 high-quality RSS sources. Too many feeds create noise; too few miss coverage. Focus on sources that match your industry's threat landscape.

What You'll Need

  1. A running n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted).
  2. Google Gemini API key (free tier available).
  3. SMTP email credentials (Gmail, SendGrid, etc.) to send digests.
  4. List of cybersecurity RSS feed URLs you want to monitor.
  5. Optional: Baserow account for storing historical reports (can use n8n's internal storage instead).

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this workflow in under 15 minutes:

  1. Download & Import: Click the download button above to get the JSON file. In your n8n instance, go to Workflows → Import from file and select it.
  2. Configure RSS Feeds: Open the "RSS Read" nodes. Replace the example URLs with your actual cybersecurity feed links.
  3. Set Up AI: In the "Google Gemini Chat Model" nodes, add your API key from Google AI Studio.
  4. Connect Email: Update the "Send Email" node with your SMTP server details and recipient addresses.
  5. Adjust Schedule: The workflow triggers daily at 7 AM. Modify the Schedule Trigger node if you prefer a different time.
  6. Test & Activate: Click "Execute Workflow" once to verify everything works. Then toggle the workflow to active.
