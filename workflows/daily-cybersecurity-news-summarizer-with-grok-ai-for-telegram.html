What This Workflow Does

For security teams, IT managers, and tech enthusiasts, staying updated with the latest cybersecurity threats is a time-consuming daily chore. Manually scanning multiple news sources, blogs, and advisories can take hours, and critical information can still be missed.

This automation workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as your personal cybersecurity news aggregator and analyst. Every morning, it automatically fetches the latest security bulletins, uses AI to extract the essential insights, filters out noise and sponsored content, and delivers clean, summarized updates directly to your team's Telegram channel.

You get a concise, actionable briefing of the day's most important vulnerabilities, breaches, and threat intelligence—without ever opening a browser. This transforms a reactive, manual process into a proactive, automated intelligence stream.

How It Works

The workflow is built in n8n and operates on a scheduled trigger, executing a series of intelligent steps to transform raw data into valuable intelligence.

1. Scheduled Trigger & News Fetching

The workflow activates daily at a time you set (e.g., 9 AM). It connects to configured RSS feeds from trusted cybersecurity sources like BleepingComputer. It pulls in the latest articles, capturing titles, descriptions, publication dates, and links.

2. Content Cleaning & Enrichment

Each article URL is fetched, and the HTML is cleaned to extract the core text, removing ads, navigation, and other webpage clutter. The system also identifies and extracts any relevant images from the article to include in the final post.

3. AI-Powered Summarization

The cleaned article text is sent to an AI model (like OpenRouter's Grok). The AI is instructed to produce a concise, two-sentence summary that highlights the core threat, affected systems, and key implications. A memory buffer node maintains context across articles for coherent output.

4. Data Merging & Sponsored Content Filter

The original metadata (title, author, date, link), the cleaned content, the AI summary, and any images are merged into a single data package. A filter node then checks for markers of sponsored or promotional content (like "Sponsored" in the author field) and removes them from the queue.

5. Automated Telegram Posting

The final, filtered items are looped through. For each news item, the workflow sends a formatted photo message to your specified Telegram channel. The post includes the headline, publication date, AI summary, relevant categories, an image (if available), and a direct "Read more" link. A short delay between posts prevents rate-limiting and creates a natural, digestible feed.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes.

  1. Download the Template: Click the "Download Template" button above to get the JSON file.
  2. Import into n8n: In your n8n dashboard, go to Workflows > Import from File and select the downloaded JSON.
  3. Configure Credentials: Set up the required credentials for the Telegram Bot and your chosen AI provider (OpenRouter) in n8n's credentials management.
  4. Set Your Channel: In the "Send Photo Message" Telegram node, enter your specific Telegram Channel ID (e.g., @YourChannelName).
  5. Adjust the Schedule: Modify the Schedule Trigger node to run at your desired time (e.g., every weekday at 9 AM).
  6. Test & Activate: Click "Execute Workflow" once to test. If successful, activate the workflow to run automatically.
