What This Workflow Does

Manual Google Analytics reporting consumes valuable marketing hours every week. Teams waste time collecting data, comparing metrics, writing summaries, and distributing insights—often missing critical trends until it's too late. This automation solves that by transforming raw analytics into actionable intelligence delivered automatically.

The workflow runs daily, fetching today's and yesterday's performance data, calculating percentage changes, identifying trends (like traffic drops), generating human-readable AI summaries, and distributing insights via WhatsApp alerts, email reports, and ClickUp tasks. It turns complex data into clear business actions without manual intervention.

Beyond basic reporting, it creates a closed-loop system: when problems are detected, tasks are automatically assigned; when opportunities appear, alerts notify decision-makers instantly. This ensures your team spends time acting on insights rather than finding them.

How It Works

1. Daily Data Collection

A schedule trigger runs the workflow each morning. It connects to Google Analytics API and retrieves key metrics—users, sessions, page views, bounce rates—for both the current and previous day. This dual dataset enables immediate day-over-day comparison.

2. Data Standardization & Comparison

The workflow cleans and structures both datasets identically, then calculates percentage changes for each metric. It applies business logic to flag significant changes (like traffic drops over 20%) and tags them with trends like "lowTraffic" for further processing.

3. AI-Powered Insight Generation

GPT-4 analyzes the compared data, identifying not just what changed but why it matters. It generates concise, professional summaries that highlight key trends, provide context ("traffic dropped likely due to weekend patterns"), and suggest potential actions—transforming numbers into narratives.

4. Multi-Channel Distribution

Based on urgency and content type, insights are routed appropriately: critical alerts go to WhatsApp for immediate attention, comprehensive reports email to management, and actionable items create ClickUp tasks assigned to relevant team members with due dates and context.

5. Historical Logging & Tracking

All processed data, summaries, and alerts are logged to Google Sheets, creating a searchable historical record. This enables trend analysis over time and provides audit trails for decision-making processes.

Quick Setup Guide

Step 1: Download the template and import it into your n8n instance. The workflow structure will load with placeholder nodes.

Step 2: Configure credentials for each service—Google Analytics, OpenAI, your communication channel (WhatsApp/Email), ClickUp, and Google Sheets. Test each connection individually.

Step 3: Update the Google Analytics node with your property ID and desired metrics. Adjust the comparison thresholds in the Function node based on what constitutes a "significant" change for your business.

Step 4: Customize the AI prompt in the OpenAI node to match your reporting style. Add industry-specific context or key questions you want answered about the data.

Step 5: Set recipient details—WhatsApp numbers, email addresses, ClickUp list IDs—and configure the schedule trigger (daily at 9 AM local time is recommended).

Step 6: Run a test execution with manual trigger to verify all connections work and outputs are formatted correctly. Adjust any templates or mappings as needed.
