What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of manually checking NASA's Astronomy Picture of the Day (APOD) website each day. Instead, it automatically delivers the stunning daily cosmic image along with its professional astronomical explanation directly to your email inbox.

The workflow connects to NASA's official APOD RSS feed, extracts the latest image and description, then formats and sends it via your preferred email service. You'll receive a beautifully formatted email each morning with that day's celestial wonder, perfect for starting your day with cosmic inspiration.

How It Works

1. Fetching NASA's daily image

The workflow begins by accessing NASA's public APOD RSS feed, which updates daily with a new astronomical image and detailed description. The automation checks this feed at your specified time (typically early morning).

2. Processing the image data

The system extracts the high-resolution image URL, title, copyright information (if applicable), and the astronomer's explanation from the RSS feed. It prepares this content for email formatting while preserving all attribution.

3. Composing the email

The workflow then constructs an HTML email with the image prominently displayed, followed by NASA's official description. You can customize this template to match your preferred layout and styling.

4. Sending via your email service

Finally, the automation connects to your configured SMTP email service (Gmail, Outlook, SendGrid, etc.) to deliver the formatted APOD email to your specified inbox address.

Pro tip: Set the workflow to run around 6am local time so the NASA image arrives in your inbox by the time you start your day.
