What This Workflow Does

Staying on top of industry news, competitor moves, and technology trends is a massive time sink for teams. Manually scouring news sites, blogs, and feeds is inefficient and inconsistent. This automation solves that by creating a hands-free, intelligent news monitoring system.

It fetches articles based on your chosen keywords (like "AI," "Cybersecurity," or your competitors' names), uses an AI agent to filter out low-quality or irrelevant clickbait, summarizes the top stories, and delivers them directly to your team's Slack channel. Simultaneously, it archives every article's key data—title, author, summary, and URL—into a Google Sheet, building a searchable database for historical analysis.

Every Monday, the workflow reviews the past week's data and uses AI to generate a high-level trend report, highlighting emerging patterns and strategic insights you might have missed.

How It Works

Step 1: Scheduled News Fetching

A Cron trigger runs the workflow daily at your chosen time (e.g., 8 AM). It calls the NewsAPI with your configured keywords to pull in the latest articles from thousands of sources.

Step 2: AI-Powered Quality Filtering

Each article is sent to an AI model (via OpenRouter or OpenAI). The AI acts as a smart editor, scoring articles for relevance and quality, and filtering out clickbait, off-topic, or low-information pieces.

Step 3: Summarization & Translation

The AI then generates concise, actionable summaries of the top 3-5 filtered articles. Optionally, it can translate these summaries into another language (like Japanese via DeepL) for global teams.

Step 4: Delivery to Slack

The curated, translated summaries are formatted into a clean, readable message and posted to a designated Slack channel, keeping your team informed without them ever leaving their communication tool.

Step 5: Data Archiving to Google Sheets

All article metadata—title, author, source, summary, and URL—is appended as a new row in a Google Sheet. This creates a permanent, queryable record of all coverage.

Step 6: Weekly Trend Analysis

A separate weekly Cron trigger reads the past week's data from the Google Sheet. An AI agent analyzes this data to identify overarching trends, sentiment shifts, and key themes, generating a strategic report.

Pro tip: Use specific, long-tail keywords (e.g., "generative AI regulation" instead of just "AI") to dramatically improve the relevance of your filtered results and reduce noise.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this intelligence system running in under 30 minutes:

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above and import the JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Credentials: In n8n, set up credentials for NewsAPI, your chosen AI model (e.g., OpenRouter), DeepL (optional), Google Sheets, and Slack.
  3. Set Your Keywords: Open the "Set Keyword" node and change the default "technology" to your topics (e.g., "SaaS pricing," "climate tech").
  4. Map Your Google Sheet: In the "Append row in sheet" and "Read sheet (weekly)" nodes, select your Google Sheet file and map the title, author, summary, and URL columns.
  5. Select Your Slack Channel: Choose the destination channel in the Slack nodes.
  6. Activate & Test: Turn on the workflow and run a manual test to see the first digest appear in Slack and your Sheet.

Pro tip: Start with a broad keyword to see the volume of results, then refine to 2-3 specific terms. The AI filter handles the rest, ensuring quality over quantity.
