What This Workflow Does

Finding and qualifying grant opportunities is a time-consuming process that often involves daily manual checks across multiple websites, followed by hours of reading eligibility criteria. This automation solves that problem by creating a systematic pipeline that discovers, evaluates, and prioritizes funding opportunities for your business.

The workflow automatically scrapes AI grant listings from grants.gov (or any tender website), removes duplicates to prevent redundant work, uses AI to assess eligibility and business interest, stores qualified opportunities in a database, and sends summarized alerts to your team via email. It transforms what was typically a 15-20 hour weekly research task into a fully automated process that runs in the background.

Pro tip: While this template focuses on AI grants, the same framework works for any funding category—simply adjust the search parameters and qualification criteria.

How It Works

1. Automated Grant Discovery

A scheduled trigger runs daily to fetch new grant listings from grants.gov. The system filters results based on your specified categories (default is "AI") and timeframes (typically last 24 hours).

2. Intelligent Deduplication

The Remove Duplicates node tracks Grant IDs to filter out opportunities already processed by your workflow. This prevents your team from reviewing the same grant multiple times and ensures every new listing gets proper attention.

3. AI-Powered Qualification

New grants are analyzed by AI nodes that evaluate eligibility based on your business profile and determine interest level. The AI considers factors like funding amount, geographic restrictions, industry focus, and application requirements.

4. Centralized Storage

Qualified opportunities are saved to an Airtable database (or your preferred system) with all relevant details: deadline, funding amount, eligibility score, interest level, and direct links.

5. Team Notification

A second scheduled trigger collects newly qualified grants, compiles them into a professional email newsletter format using HTML nodes, and sends alerts to your designated team members via Gmail or your email service.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow
  2. Airtable account for storing qualified grant opportunities (free tier works)
  3. OpenAI API key or alternative LLM service for AI qualification
  4. Gmail account or email service credentials for sending alerts
  5. Team email list of recipients who should receive grant alerts

Note: These are not hard requirements. You can substitute Airtable with Google Sheets, Notion, or any database. OpenAI can be replaced with Claude, Gemini, or open-source models available through n8n.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Duplicate the sample Airtable base from the provided link in the workflow description.
  3. Configure your API credentials for OpenAI and your email service in the respective nodes.
  4. Adjust grant search parameters in the HTTP Request node if you want different categories or filters.
  5. Customize the AI prompts to match your specific eligibility criteria and business interests.
  6. Set your team email list in the email node and test the workflow with a manual trigger.
  7. Schedule the triggers to run at optimal times for your workflow (daily morning scans work well).
