Automatically identify internal keyword competition across your website using AI-powered analysis and real-time GSC data.

What This Workflow Does

Keyword cannibalization is a silent killer of SEO performance. When multiple pages on your site compete for the same search term, search engines get confused, traffic gets split, and no single page can achieve a top ranking. Manual detection involves hours of spreadsheet analysis across hundreds of keywords and pages—a tedious, error-prone process.

This automation solves that problem entirely. It continuously monitors Google Search Console data, uses GPT‑4o to intelligently assess competition patterns, and delivers clear, actionable reports. You get real-time alerts on high-risk keywords, detailed reasoning behind each assessment, and specific remediation steps to consolidate authority and boost rankings.

The system is built for scale, handling up to four client websites simultaneously with dedicated processing paths. It transforms reactive SEO troubleshooting into proactive, automated site hygiene.

How It Works

Step 1: Real-Time Monitoring Trigger

A Google Sheets trigger monitors your keyword list every minute for changes. When new keywords are added or modified, the workflow instantly activates, ensuring your analysis is always based on the latest target terms.

Step 2: Google Search Console Data Fetch

The workflow connects to the GSC API and pulls 30 days of performance data—including clicks, impressions, CTR, positions, and page URLs—for each keyword. This raw data is grouped and structured for analysis.

Step 3: AI-Powered Risk Assessment

GPT‑4o analyzes the grouped data, identifying how many pages rank for each keyword, their positions, and performance metrics. It categorizes risk as High (5+ competing pages), Moderate (3+ in top 10), Low (2 pages with one dominant), or No Risk (single page). The AI provides a written explanation for each classification.

Step 4: Comprehensive Reporting

All results—risk levels, AI reasoning, observations, and recommended actions—are automatically saved back to Google Sheets. The report also includes full keyword performance metrics and flags which keywords are missing from search results entirely.

Keyword cannibalization occurs when multiple pages on your website compete for the same search keyword, confusing search engines and diluting ranking potential. It splits traffic, reduces click-through rates, and prevents any single page from achieving top rankings, ultimately hurting your overall organic visibility and conversions.
