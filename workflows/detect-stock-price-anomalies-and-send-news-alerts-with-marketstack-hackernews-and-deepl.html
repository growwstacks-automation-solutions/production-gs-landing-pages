What This Workflow Does

This automated workflow solves the challenge of manually monitoring stock prices and correlating them with relevant news. It continuously tracks specified stocks using Marketstack's API, applies statistical analysis to detect unusual price movements, then searches HackerNews for related discussions. When an anomaly is detected, it translates relevant news articles using DeepL and sends actionable alerts to Slack.

The system eliminates hours of manual monitoring while providing more comprehensive coverage than human analysts could achieve. It combines quantitative analysis with qualitative context, giving traders and investors a complete picture of market movements and potential catalysts.

How It Works

1. Stock Price Monitoring

The workflow pulls daily closing prices from Marketstack's API for your configured stocks. It maintains a running history to establish normal price patterns and volatility baselines.

2. Anomaly Detection

Using statistical methods, the system compares current price movements against historical patterns. It flags deviations that exceed configured thresholds for percentage change or volatility.

3. News Gathering

When an anomaly is detected, the workflow searches HackerNews for recent discussions mentioning the stock ticker or company name. It prioritizes high-engagement threads and authoritative sources.

4. News Translation

Relevant foreign-language articles are automatically translated using DeepL's API, which specializes in financial terminology accuracy. The system preserves key numerical data and proper nouns during translation.

5. Alert Delivery

The compiled alert package - containing the price anomaly details and translated news context - is formatted and sent to your Slack channel. Alerts include actionable links and summary statistics.

Automated stock anomaly detection systems can be highly accurate when properly configured. They use statistical models to identify deviations from normal price patterns. Accuracy improves with quality historical data and proper parameter tuning. However, no system is perfect - anomalies should be verified before trading decisions.

Modern systems achieve 85-95% accuracy in backtesting for significant price movements. They're particularly effective at flagging large, sudden changes while filtering out normal volatility. Regular calibration against actual market events helps maintain and improve detection accuracy over time.

Volatile stocks and emerging market securities benefit most from anomaly detection. Stocks with predictable patterns provide fewer trading opportunities from anomalies. Small-cap stocks often show more detectable anomalies than large, liquid stocks. Sector-specific stocks may show patterns tied to industry news cycles.

Technology and biotech stocks frequently exhibit meaningful anomalies due to product announcements and regulatory decisions. Commodity stocks respond to supply chain disruptions. The system works best for stocks where news events drive price movements rather than steady, gradual changes.

Anomaly detection systems should run at least daily for most trading strategies. Intraday traders may benefit from hourly or minute-by-minute monitoring. The frequency depends on trading style and holding periods. More frequent checks increase responsiveness but also computational costs and potential false positives.

For long-term investors, daily checks at market close are typically sufficient. Active traders might configure 15-minute intervals during market hours. The system can automatically adjust frequency based on market conditions, checking more often during earnings seasons or market turmoil.
