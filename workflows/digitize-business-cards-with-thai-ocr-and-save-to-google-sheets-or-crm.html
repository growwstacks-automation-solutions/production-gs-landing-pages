Digitize Business Cards with Thai OCR & AI Enrichment

Automatically extract text from Thai/English business cards, enrich contact details with AI, and save directly to Google Sheets or your CRM.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious, error-prone process of manually typing contact information from business cards into your database. Sales teams, event organizers, and networkers waste countless hours each month on data entry, often introducing typos in critical email addresses or phone numbers.

The workflow uses advanced AI OCR (Optical Character Recognition) combined with Large Language Models to extract text from business card images—including challenging Thai language cards—parse the information into structured fields (name, title, company, email, phone), enrich the data with job classification and industry context, and save perfectly formatted contact records directly to Google Sheets or any CRM.

Beyond simple extraction, the system can optionally draft personalized greeting emails and trigger immediate follow-up sequences, turning passive card collection into active lead generation.

How It Works

1. Image Submission & Trigger

The workflow starts when a business card image is submitted via form, webhook, or cloud storage folder. The included n8n Form Trigger provides a simple upload interface, but you can replace this with any image source including email attachments, mobile app uploads, or scanned document folders.

2. AI-Powered OCR Extraction

Typhoon OCR processes the image, extracting all text with special attention to multi-language support. Unlike basic OCR that just returns text blocks, this AI-enhanced service understands business card layouts, distinguishes between languages (Thai and English mixed on same card), and handles varied fonts and backgrounds with high accuracy.

3. LLM Structuring & Parsing

The extracted text passes to a Large Language Model that identifies and categorizes each piece of information. The LLM doesn't just match patterns—it understands context, so "John Smith, CEO at TechCorp" correctly identifies "John Smith" as name, "CEO" as title, and "TechCorp" as company, even if the formatting is unconventional.

4. Contact Enrichment

Two enrichment paths are available: a cost-free LLM-based enrichment that classifies job type, seniority level, and industry sector, or an enhanced version using Search API (SerpAPI) to add company descriptions, LinkedIn profiles, and professional background information for richer lead profiles.

5. Data Storage & Integration

The cleaned, enriched contact record saves to Google Sheets with proper column formatting. This step is easily modified to connect with HubSpot, Salesforce, Notion, Airtable, or any database via n8n's 300+ application integrations.

6. Optional Automated Follow-up

An optional branch uses the LLM to draft a context-aware greeting email based on the contact's role and company, then sends it via Gmail or your email service. This creates immediate engagement while the interaction is fresh.
