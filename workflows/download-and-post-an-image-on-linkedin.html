Download and Post an Image on LinkedIn

Automate your social media content pipeline—download images from any source and post them directly to LinkedIn with consistent scheduling

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

Manual LinkedIn content creation consumes valuable hours each week that could be spent on strategic business activities. This workflow automates the entire process of sourcing images, preparing them for posting, and publishing to LinkedIn with proper scheduling.

The automation connects to image sources (like design tools, stock photo libraries, or internal databases), downloads the appropriate images, formats them for LinkedIn's specifications, adds your caption and hashtags, and posts them at predetermined times. It eliminates the repetitive tasks of downloading, resizing, writing captions, and manually posting while ensuring your LinkedIn presence remains active and professional.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger and Image Source

The workflow begins with either a schedule trigger (post every Tuesday at 9 AM) or an event trigger (when a new image is added to your design folder). It connects to your image source—this could be Google Drive, Dropbox, Canva, Unsplash API, or any other service where you store marketing visuals.

Step 2: Image Processing and Preparation

Once the image is retrieved, the workflow processes it for LinkedIn compatibility. This includes resizing to optimal dimensions (1200x627 pixels for LinkedIn articles), compression for faster loading, and adding any watermarks or branding elements if configured. The system can also convert file formats if necessary.

Step 3: Content Assembly

The workflow assembles the complete post by combining the processed image with your caption template. It can pull dynamic data like current dates, product names, or campaign tags from other systems. Hashtags are added based on your predefined categories, and the post is checked for character limits and LinkedIn guidelines.

Step 4: LinkedIn Publishing

The final step connects to LinkedIn's API using your authenticated credentials. The workflow uploads the image, sets the caption, selects the appropriate audience settings (public, connections only, etc.), and publishes the post. It can also schedule for future publication if you're building a content queue.

Pro tip: Use this workflow to create a "content recycling" system—automatically repost your top-performing images with updated captions every 90 days to maximize reach without creating new visuals.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with basic configuration
  2. LinkedIn Company Page access or personal account with publishing permissions
  3. Image source credentials (API keys or access to services where images are stored)
  4. Content calendar outline (know what you want to post and when)
  5. Basic understanding of LinkedIn's content guidelines (image specs, character limits, etc.)

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your n8n environment:

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Configure LinkedIn credentials in the LinkedIn node—use OAuth2 for secure authentication
  3. Set up your image source connection whether it's a cloud storage service or design platform
  4. Customize the caption template with your brand voice, hashtags, and any dynamic fields
  5. Test with a single image before enabling the full schedule to ensure everything works
  6. Activate the workflow and monitor the first few posts for any adjustments needed

Pro tip: Start with a conservative posting schedule (2-3 times per week) and gradually increase frequency as you refine your content and observe audience engagement patterns.
