What This Workflow Does

Academic researchers, faculty, and graduate students face a constant challenge: manually switching between AI models for different research tasks while losing conversation context across sessions. This leads to repetitive work, inconsistent analysis, and hours wasted on administrative tasks rather than actual research.

This n8n workflow solves this by creating an intelligent research automation system. It accepts research queries via webhook, stores them in Pinecone vector databases for semantic search, and intelligently routes requests to the most appropriate AI model—OpenAI's GPT-4 for creative synthesis, Anthropic Claude for logical reasoning, or NVIDIA NIM for specialized academic domains. The system maintains complete conversation history, enabling follow-up queries that reference previous interactions, cited papers, and contextual insights.

Results are automatically consolidated, formatted with proper academic citations, and delivered via email with full tracking. This eliminates manual model switching, context loss, and repetitive credential management—streamlining research workflows from literature review to hypothesis generation and paper drafting.

How It Works

1. Query Reception & Vector Storage

The workflow begins when a researcher submits a query through a webhook interface. This could be a literature review question, data analysis request, or hypothesis exploration. The query is immediately converted into embeddings using OpenAI's embedding model and stored in Pinecone vector database, creating a searchable memory of all research interactions.

2. Intelligent AI Model Routing

Based on the query type and complexity, the workflow automatically selects the optimal AI model. Simple fact-checking might use GPT-4, complex logical reasoning routes to Claude, and specialized technical queries go to NVIDIA NIM models. This multi-model approach ensures each task gets the most capable AI assistant.

3. Context-Aware Processing

Before processing, the workflow retrieves relevant context from Pinecone—previous queries on this topic, cited papers, methodology preferences, and project history. This context is injected into the AI prompt, creating a continuous conversation rather than isolated interactions.

4. Result Synthesis & Delivery

AI-generated responses are automatically formatted with academic citations, structured according to research paper conventions, and validated against source materials. The final output is delivered via email with options for Google Sheets logging, reference manager integration, or direct document creation.
