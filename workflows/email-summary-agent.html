Email Summary Agent

Automatically summarize important emails to reduce inbox overload and improve meeting preparation

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

Email overload is a major productivity drain for modern teams. Professionals spend an average of 2.5 hours per day managing emails, often missing critical action items buried in lengthy threads. This problem becomes particularly acute before important meetings, when team members scramble to review relevant correspondence.

This n8n workflow automatically processes incoming emails, extracts key information, and generates concise summaries. It identifies action items, decisions, and important details while filtering out noise. The summaries can be delivered via Slack, saved to Notion, or added directly to your calendar events - ensuring you're always prepared without wasting time manually reviewing every message.

How It Works

1. Email Trigger

The workflow monitors your email account (Gmail, Outlook, or IMAP) for new messages matching your criteria. You can filter by sender, subject keywords, or labels to ensure only relevant emails are processed.

2. Content Analysis

Using natural language processing, the workflow analyzes the email content to identify the core message, key points, and any action items. It intelligently handles long threads by focusing on the most recent and relevant parts.

3. Summary Generation

The system generates a structured summary that includes: the main purpose of the email, any decisions made, action items (with owners and deadlines if specified), and important details. This happens in seconds without human intervention.

4. Delivery

Summaries are delivered to your preferred destination. Common options include: Slack channels for team visibility, Notion pages for documentation, or calendar events for meeting preparation. You can configure multiple outputs.

Pro tip: Set up separate workflows for different email types (client communications, internal updates, meeting follow-ups) to get tailored summaries for each context.
