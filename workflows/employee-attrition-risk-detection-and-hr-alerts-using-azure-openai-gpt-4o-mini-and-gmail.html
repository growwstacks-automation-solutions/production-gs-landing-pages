What This Workflow Does

Employee turnover is expensive, disruptive, and often preventable. Traditional HR approaches rely on annual surveys or exit interviews—reactive measures that come too late. This automation transforms retention from reactive to proactive.

The workflow continuously monitors employee data sources, extracts subtle behavioral and sentiment signals, applies AI analysis to calculate attrition risk scores, and automatically notifies HR or managers with specific insights and recommended actions. It catches early warning signs that human review might miss, enabling timely intervention that can save key talent.

By automating the detection process, you move from guessing who might leave to knowing with data-backed confidence, allowing your HR team to focus on meaningful retention strategies rather than manual data analysis.

How It Works

1. Data Ingestion & Trigger

The workflow activates when new employee data becomes available—whether from performance reviews uploaded to Google Drive, survey responses, email communications, or HR system updates. It supports multiple file formats including PDFs, text documents, and structured data.

2. Content Extraction & Preparation

Files are downloaded and processed to extract analyzable text. PDFs are converted, irrelevant formatting is stripped, and content is prepared for AI analysis while maintaining context about the employee and data source.

3. AI-Powered Signal Analysis

Azure OpenAI GPT-4o-mini analyzes the extracted text for key attrition indicators: sentiment tone, burnout language, engagement levels, performance changes, career progression mentions, workload stress, and managerial relationship cues.

4. Structured Risk Scoring

The AI maps findings to a consistent schema: numerical risk score (0-1), risk level (low/medium/high), primary drivers, recommended interventions, and escalation flags. This structured output ensures consistent decision-making.

5. Conditional Routing & Escalation

Based on configurable thresholds (e.g., risk_score ≥ 0.7), the workflow routes cases appropriately. High-risk cases trigger immediate alerts, while medium-risk cases might schedule follow-ups. Context is maintained across multiple data points for the same employee.

6. HR Alert Generation & Delivery

Personalized email alerts are composed with employee details, risk factors, specific quotes or evidence, and actionable recommendations. These are sent directly to HR business partners or managers via Gmail for immediate attention.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about employee attrition detection and AI automation

Employee attrition risk detection is the process of identifying employees who are likely to leave a company before they resign. It's crucial because replacing an employee costs 50-200% of their annual salary in recruitment, training, and lost productivity.

Proactive detection allows HR and managers to intervene early, address underlying concerns, and implement retention strategies. This preserves institutional knowledge, maintains team morale, and saves significant financial resources that would otherwise be spent on hiring and onboarding replacements.

AI transforms retention from reactive to proactive by analyzing patterns humans might miss. Traditional methods rely on visible signs or exit interviews—both occurring after departure decisions are made.

AI processes unstructured data like performance review comments, survey responses, email communication patterns, and activity metrics to identify subtle early warning signs. It provides objective risk scoring based on multiple data points, enabling interventions before employees actively job search.

The most predictive signals combine behavioral, sentiment, and contextual data. Negative sentiment in feedback, decreased meeting participation, changes in work patterns, and missed performance goals are strong indicators.

Other key signals include lack of career progression mentions, increased LinkedIn activity, changes in manager relationship quality, compensation dissatisfaction expressions, and workload stress indicators. The combination of multiple signals across different data sources provides the most accurate risk assessment.

Automation delivers timely, structured alerts with specific risk factors and recommended actions. This eliminates hours of manual data review and ensures no at-risk employee slips through the cracks due to workload or oversight.

Managers receive actionable insights rather than raw data, enabling meaningful retention conversations. HR teams can focus on strategic interventions instead of administrative monitoring, transforming their role from reactive problem-solving to proactive talent preservation.

Transparency and purpose limitation are essential. Employees should understand what data is analyzed, how it's used, and that the goal is retention support—not surveillance or punishment. Implement clear data governance policies.

Focus on aggregated behavioral patterns rather than individual surveillance. Ensure compliance with data protection regulations like GDPR or CCPA. Use anonymized or pseudonymized data where possible, and establish strict access controls. Regular audits and employee communication maintain trust in the system.

Small businesses often benefit more because each departure has a larger proportional impact. Losing one key employee in a 20-person company can cripple operations and client relationships for months.

Automated detection helps small teams be proactive despite limited HR resources. The implementation cost is typically far lower than the cost of even one turnover event. Small businesses can start with basic data sources and scale as they grow, making it accessible and valuable at any size.

Calculate ROI by tracking both direct cost savings and productivity preservation. Direct savings include reduced recruitment costs (agency fees, advertising, interviewing time), decreased onboarding expenses, and lower training costs for replacements.

Also measure improved employee satisfaction scores, increased retention rates, and manager effectiveness in retention conversations. Most organizations see positive ROI within 6-12 months through reduced turnover alone, with additional benefits in employee engagement and operational continuity.
