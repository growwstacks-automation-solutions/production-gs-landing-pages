What This Workflow Does

Creating comprehensive FAQ sections for multiple services, products, or website pages is time-consuming and repetitive. Marketing teams spend hours crafting questions and answers that cover setup, permissions, integrations, use cases, and pricing benefits—only to repeat the process for each new offering.

This automation solves that problem by connecting Google Sheets (where you maintain service information) with AI (OpenAI's GPT) to generate polished, consistent FAQ documents at scale. Simply update your spreadsheet, and the workflow automatically creates detailed Q&A content, formats it properly, and saves organized files to Google Drive.

The system handles everything from reading your service data to generating AI-enhanced answers and updating completion status—turning what was a multi-hour manual task into a fully automated process that runs in minutes.

How It Works

Step 1: Read Service Data from Google Sheets

The workflow starts by reading your Google Sheets document containing information about different services, categories, or items needing FAQ content. This serves as your central database that the automation references.

Step 2: Generate Standard Questions and Answers

For each service entry, the system creates a set of standard questions covering key areas customers care about: setup requirements, permission needs, integration possibilities, common use cases, and pricing benefits.

Step 3: Enhance Answers with AI

An OpenAI GPT model reviews and enhances the answers, making them more comprehensive, natural-sounding, and tailored to your specific service descriptions. The AI combines predefined templates with generated content for optimal results.

Step 4: Format and Organize Q&A Pairs

The workflow structures the questions and answers into properly formatted documents, creating a text file (JSON) for each service or category with clear section headings and logical flow.

Step 5: Save to Google Drive Folders

Generated files are automatically saved to specific Google Drive folders, organized by integration type (native, credential-only, non-native) or category, maintaining a clean file structure for easy access.

Step 6: Update Completion Status

After processing each service, the workflow updates the status in your original Google Sheets document to mark it as completed, providing clear tracking of what's been processed and what remains.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance by downloading the JSON file and importing it through the n8n interface.
  2. Set up credentials for Google Sheets, Google Drive, and OpenAI when you first open the workflow.
  3. Prepare your Google Sheets document with service information. Use the example template as a starting point for column structure.
  4. Configure the "Define Sheets" node with your specific spreadsheet IDs and sheet names.
  5. Adjust folder IDs in the "Prepare Job" node to match your Google Drive folder structure.
  6. Test with a small dataset before running the full automation to ensure output quality meets your standards.
  7. Activate the workflow for scheduled or manual execution based on your content update needs.
