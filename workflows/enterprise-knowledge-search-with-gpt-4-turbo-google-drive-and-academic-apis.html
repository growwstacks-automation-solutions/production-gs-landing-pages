What This Workflow Does

This enterprise-grade automation solves the critical business problem of information overload and inefficient research. Teams spend countless hours manually searching through Google Drive folders, academic databases, news sources, and internal documents to find relevant information for projects, decisions, and client work.

The workflow implements a sophisticated Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system that intelligently searches multiple knowledge sources simultaneously, processes the information with quality scoring, and generates comprehensive, source-grounded responses using GPT-4 Turbo. It transforms scattered information into actionable intelligence.

Instead of requiring employees to be expert searchers across multiple platforms, this automation delivers consolidated, accurate answers in minutes—complete with citations and source references. It's like having a dedicated research assistant who never sleeps and knows exactly where to look in your entire knowledge ecosystem.

How It Works

1. Intelligent Query Processing

The workflow begins by analyzing the user's search query to determine intent, scope, and appropriate sources. It considers factors like technical depth required, preferred response style, and language preferences before routing the search to relevant data sources.

2. Multi-Source Search Execution

Simultaneously searches across configured sources: web search engines for current information, academic databases for peer-reviewed content, news APIs for recent developments, and internal Google Drive documents for proprietary knowledge. Each source returns ranked results based on relevance.

3. Data Aggregation & Quality Scoring

Information from all sources is unified, deduplicated, and scored for quality based on source credibility, recency, and relevance to the original query. The system prioritizes academic papers for technical accuracy and internal documents for business context.

4. AI-Powered Synthesis with GPT-4 Turbo

GPT-4 Turbo processes the aggregated information, generating coherent responses that cite specific sources. The AI is prompted to maintain academic rigor for technical queries while adapting tone for business or creative requests as specified by the user.

5. Response Formatting & Delivery

Final answers are formatted according to user preferences—comprehensive reports with executive summaries, concise bullet points, technical documentation, or presentation-ready content. Results are delivered via webhook, email, Slack, or optional PDF generation.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with workflow execution capabilities
  2. OpenAI API key with GPT-4 Turbo access for AI processing
  3. Google Service Account credentials with access to relevant Drive folders
  4. Academic API keys for services like Crossref, arXiv, or PubMed
  5. News API key (optional) for current events integration

Pro tip: Start with a limited scope—configure the workflow to search only 2-3 critical knowledge sources initially. Once you verify accuracy and relevance, gradually expand to additional sources. This phased approach minimizes configuration complexity and helps identify optimal search parameters for your specific use case.

Follow these steps to implement this enterprise knowledge search system in your organization:
