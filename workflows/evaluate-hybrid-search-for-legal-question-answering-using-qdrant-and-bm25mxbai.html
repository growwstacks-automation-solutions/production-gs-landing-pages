What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow template solves a critical problem for legal technology teams: evaluating the accuracy of AI-powered document retrieval systems. Legal professionals spend countless hours searching through case law, statutes, and legal commentary. Traditional keyword search often misses conceptually relevant documents, while pure semantic search might overlook exact statutory citations.

The template implements and evaluates hybrid search—combining BM25 keyword matching with mxbai-embed-large-v1 semantic search—using Qdrant vector database. It measures retrieval performance on the LegalQAEval dataset, calculating hits@1 accuracy to determine how often the top retrieved document contains the correct answer to legal questions.

By providing quantitative metrics on search performance, this workflow helps legal tech teams make data-driven decisions about their AI research tools before deployment, saving development time and ensuring higher quality results for end users.

How It Works

The workflow follows a systematic evaluation pipeline designed to test hybrid search capabilities on legal documents.

Step 1: Dataset Preparation

The workflow loads a subset of the LegalQAEval dataset containing legal questions and corresponding text chunks with answers. It processes these documents to ensure proper formatting and prepares them for both keyword and semantic indexing.

Step 2: Hybrid Search Configuration

Two parallel search methods are configured: BM25 for exact keyword matching (crucial for legal citations and specific terminology) and semantic search using mxbai-embed-large-v1 embeddings (for conceptual understanding of legal principles).

Step 3: Reciprocal Rank Fusion

The workflow implements Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF), a zero-shot fusion technique that combines results from both search methods. RRF gives higher weight to documents that appear in both result sets, balancing precision and recall.

Step 4: Evaluation & Metrics

For each legal question in the evaluation set, the workflow runs the hybrid search and checks whether the correct answer appears in the top retrieved documents. It calculates hits@1 (top result accuracy) and other relevant metrics to quantify search performance.

Step 5: Results Analysis

The workflow outputs comprehensive evaluation results, showing where hybrid search outperforms individual methods and identifying areas for improvement in legal document retrieval systems.
