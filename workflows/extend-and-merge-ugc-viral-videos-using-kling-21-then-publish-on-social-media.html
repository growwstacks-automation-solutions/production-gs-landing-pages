Extend and Merge UGC Viral Videos Using Kling 2.1

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a major pain point for content creators, marketers, and social media managers: the time-consuming process of extending short viral videos and publishing them across multiple platforms. Manually editing videos, waiting for AI generation, and uploading to each social network can take hours per video.

The workflow automates the entire pipeline from start to finish. It takes your short UGC-style videos, uses Kling 2.1 AI to realistically extend them based on your prompts, merges the original and extended clips seamlessly, then publishes the final video to TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, X, and YouTube simultaneously. All controlled through a simple Google Sheet interface.

What used to be a 3-4 hour manual process becomes a fully automated system that runs while you focus on strategy and creativity. The workflow handles API communications, status monitoring, error recovery, and platform-specific formatting automatically.

How It Works

1. Input & Frame Extraction

The workflow starts by reading your video queue from a Google Sheet. For each video, it extracts the last frame using Fal.ai's FFmpeg API. This frame becomes the starting point for AI generation, ensuring visual continuity between your original content and the AI-extended portion.

2. AI Video Generation

The extracted frame and your custom prompt are sent to RunPod's Kling 2.1 AI model. The system monitors the generation job, waiting for completion and handling any API delays or errors automatically. You specify the desired extension duration in your Google Sheet control panel.

3. Video Merging & Processing

Once the AI-generated clip is ready, it's merged with your original video using Fal.ai's merging functionality. The workflow ensures smooth transitions and proper formatting, creating a seamless extended video that maintains your brand's visual style and quality standards.

4. Storage & Multi-Platform Publishing

The final video is uploaded to Google Drive for archival and simultaneously distributed to all your social platforms. For YouTube, it uses Upload-Post API; for other platforms (TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, X, LinkedIn), it uses Postiz API. Each platform receives properly formatted content with your specified titles and descriptions.

5. Progress Tracking & Completion

Throughout the process, your Google Sheet is updated with real-time status, video URLs, and completion markers. You get a complete audit trail showing exactly what was processed, when, and where it was published—all without ever leaving your spreadsheet.

Pro tip: Create template prompts in your Google Sheet for different content types (educational, entertaining, promotional) to maintain consistent brand voice across all AI-extended videos.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your n8n environment:

  1. Download and import the workflow JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Duplicate the Google Sheet template and add your video URLs, prompts, and durations
  3. Configure API credentials in each corresponding n8n node (Fal.ai, RunPod, Upload-Post, Postiz)
  4. Set up Google OAuth for Sheets and Drive nodes in n8n
  5. Test with one video to verify all connections work correctly
  6. Schedule execution or trigger manually via your Google Sheet updates
  7. Monitor initial runs and adjust prompts based on AI output quality

Pro tip: Start with a small batch of 3-5 videos to test the system before scaling up. This lets you refine prompts and duration settings without wasting API credits on unsatisfactory results.
