What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical challenge for financial analysts, traders, and policy observers: manually monitoring influential social media accounts for potentially market-moving content is time-consuming and inconsistent. High-profile communications can trigger immediate market reactions, but without systematic monitoring, teams miss early signals or waste hours on irrelevant posts.

The workflow automatically extracts posts from Donald Trump's Truth Social account, structures the information, and estimates potential stock market impact. It transforms unstructured social data into actionable intelligence with directional assessments (positive/negative/neutral) and magnitude estimates (None/Small/Medium/Large), delivering filtered insights directly to Slack channels where teams can act on them.

How It Works

1. Browser Session Creation

The automation initiates a browser session using an Airtop profile that's pre-authenticated with Truth Social. This approach handles platform authentication and mimics human browsing behavior to avoid detection as automated scraping.

2. Content Extraction

The system navigates to the target profile URL and uses natural language processing with a defined JSON schema to extract up to three recent posts. It captures author information, post text, image URLs, and direct links to each publication.

3. Impact Assessment

Each post undergoes automated analysis to estimate potential market impact. The system evaluates content against historical patterns, language intensity, topic relevance, and the influencer's track record of moving markets.

4. Data Filtering & Delivery

The workflow filters out posts with no estimated impact and structures remaining content for clear presentation. Selected posts with impact summaries are formatted and sent to designated Slack channels, ensuring teams receive only relevant intelligence.

5. Session Cleanup

After completing the monitoring cycle, the browser session terminates to free resources and maintain system efficiency for subsequent runs.
