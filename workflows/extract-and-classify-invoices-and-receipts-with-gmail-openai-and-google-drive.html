Automatically Extract & Classify Invoices & Receipts with AI

What This Workflow Does

For freelancers, consultants, and small business owners, managing incoming invoices and receipts is a constant administrative headache. They arrive scattered across emails in various formats, requiring manual downloading, categorization, and filing—a process that wastes hours each month and increases the risk of missing payments or losing important documents.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as your intelligent financial assistant, continuously monitoring your Gmail for attachments, using AI to determine whether each document is an invoice or receipt, and systematically organizing them into a dedicated Google Drive folder. The workflow can also automatically forward processed documents to your accountant or bookkeeper, creating a seamless financial record-keeping system.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical, multi-step process that mimics how a meticulous human would handle the task—but with perfect consistency and at digital speed.

1. Trigger & Setup

The process begins when you trigger the workflow (via webhook or schedule). It first creates a timestamped folder in your Google Drive specifically for this batch of documents, ensuring organized storage from the start.

2. Email Retrieval & Filtering

It connects to your Gmail account and retrieves all emails with attachments within your specified date range. Optional filters can exclude emails from specific senders or to certain addresses, giving you control over what gets processed.

3. PDF Extraction & AI Classification

The workflow isolates PDF attachments (the most common format for invoices and receipts) and sends each document's content to OpenAI for analysis. The AI determines whether each PDF is an invoice, a receipt, or neither, applying consistent classification logic that doesn't fatigue or make mistakes.

4. Organized Storage & Notification

Classified documents are uploaded to their appropriate subfolders within the main Google Drive folder. Finally, the workflow can compile all processed documents and email them to your designated recipient (like your accountant), completing the entire process without any manual intervention.

What You'll Need

  1. Gmail Account: The source of your invoices and receipts. The workflow needs API access to search and retrieve emails with attachments.
  2. Google Drive Account: Destination for organized storage. A folder will be created automatically to house all processed documents.
  3. Google Cloud Project: With Gmail and Drive APIs enabled, and either OAuth 2.0 credentials or a service account set up for secure API access.
  4. OpenAI API Account & Key: Provides the AI intelligence for accurately classifying documents as invoices or receipts based on their content.
  5. n8n Instance: Either self-hosted or using n8n.cloud to run the workflow automation.

Pro tip: Start with a small date range (like the past week) for your first test run to verify classification accuracy and folder structure before processing your entire backlog.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these straightforward steps:

  1. Download & Import: Download the template file and import it into your n8n instance via the workflow import function.
  2. Configure Credentials: Set up the credential connections for Gmail, Google Drive, and OpenAI in n8n's credential management section.
  3. Adjust Parameters: In the "Configure" node, set the sendInvoicesTo email address where processed documents should be forwarded (optional).
  4. Test Trigger: Use the webhook trigger or manually execute the workflow with a sample date range to verify all connections work.
  5. Schedule or Integrate: Set the workflow to run on a schedule (daily/weekly) or connect it to an AI chat tool that supports tool use for on-demand processing.
