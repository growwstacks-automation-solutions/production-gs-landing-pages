What This Workflow Does

For Colombian businesses and professionals, managing electronic invoices (facturas electrónicas) is a time-consuming administrative burden. Each day brings ZIP files with PDF and XML attachments to your Gmail—requiring manual extraction, data entry, validation, and organization for accounting and DIAN compliance.

This automation eliminates that entire process. It monitors your Gmail every 30 minutes for invoice attachments, uses AI to extract critical information (NIT numbers, CUFE codes, totals, dates), validates calculations, stores the original documents in Google Drive with proper naming, and updates a master Google Sheets database—all without human intervention.

The system is specifically designed for Colombian tax requirements, handling DIAN-standard formats, recognizing Spanish financial terminology, and ensuring the extracted data meets local compliance needs while providing immediate access to organized financial records.

How It Works

1. Email Monitoring & Attachment Extraction

The workflow starts by checking your Gmail inbox for new messages containing .ZIP attachments—the standard format for Colombian electronic invoices. When found, it automatically downloads and extracts all files within the archive.

2. File Filtering & Preparation

Only PDF and XML files (the actual invoice documents) are selected for processing. Other file types are ignored. The system prepares these documents for AI analysis while maintaining their original integrity for compliance purposes.

3. AI-Powered Data Extraction

Using GPT-4o via a LangChain agent, the workflow reads and interprets each invoice to extract: document type (factura/nota crédito), invoice number, emission date, emitter and receiver NIT numbers (without verification digit), company name, subtotal, IVA, total amount, CUFE code, and a concise purchase summary in Spanish.

4. Validation & Error Checking

A calculator node verifies that Total = Subtotal + IVA, ensuring mathematical accuracy. Invoices with discrepancies can be flagged for review or processed with warnings, maintaining data integrity throughout the system.

5. Organized Storage & Database Update

The original PDF is uploaded to Google Drive with a standardized filename (YYYY-MM-DD-INVOICE_NUMBER.pdf). All extracted data is then inserted or updated in Google Sheets using a unique key (NIT_Emitter + Invoice_Number) to prevent duplicates and maintain a clean, searchable database.
