What This Workflow Does

Manual lead generation from Google Maps is time-consuming and inefficient. Sales teams spend hours searching for businesses, visiting websites, and collecting email addresses—a process that's repetitive and prone to errors. This workflow automates that entire process, turning what used to take days into minutes.

The automation systematically searches Google Maps for businesses based on your criteria (location, industry, keywords), extracts their website URLs, visits those websites to find email addresses, validates the emails, and exports everything to Google Sheets. It's a complete lead generation system that runs automatically, giving you a constantly updated list of qualified prospects without manual effort.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence to ensure accurate lead collection while minimizing errors and duplicates.

Step 1: Search Google Maps for Businesses

The workflow starts by querying Google Maps based on your search parameters. You can specify location, business type, keywords, and other filters to target exactly the prospects you want. The system extracts business names, addresses, phone numbers, and website URLs from the search results.

Step 2: Extract Website URLs

Using regex filtering and pattern matching, the workflow identifies legitimate business website URLs from the Google Maps data. It filters out social media profiles, directory listings, and invalid links to ensure you're getting actual business websites where email addresses are likely to be found.

Step 3: Scrape Email Addresses from Websites

For each valid website, the workflow sends HTTP requests to scan the site's pages for email addresses. It looks for common email patterns, contact forms, "mailto:" links, and other indicators of business contact information. The system handles different website structures and can navigate multiple pages if necessary.

Step 4: Clean and Validate Data

All collected emails go through validation and cleaning processes. The workflow removes duplicates, filters out generic addresses (like info@ or support@ if desired), and validates email format. This ensures your final list contains only high-quality, unique business contacts ready for outreach.

Step 5: Export to Google Sheets

The final step organizes all collected data—business name, address, phone, website, and email—into a structured format and appends it to your Google Sheets document. The workflow can create new sheets, add headers, and format the data for easy use in your sales or marketing processes.

Pro tip: Start with small batches (10-20 businesses) to test your search parameters before scaling up. This helps you refine targeting and avoid hitting rate limits.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your lead generation system running quickly:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance
  2. Create a Google Sheet with two tabs: name one "searches" and the other "emails"
  3. Add your search queries to the "searches" tab (one query per row)
  4. Connect Google Sheets credentials in n8n by creating an OAuth connection
  5. Update the document ID in the Google Sheets nodes to point to your spreadsheet
  6. Test with a small batch by adding 2-3 search queries and running the workflow manually
  7. Schedule automation once testing is successful—set it to run daily or weekly

Pro tip: Use specific, targeted search queries rather than broad terms. "New York corporate law firms" will yield better results than just "lawyers."
