What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow automates the collection and visualization of market capitalization data from Yahoo Finance, delivering actionable financial insights directly to your Telegram channel. It solves the problem of manually tracking stock market trends by providing automated, real-time updates on company valuations.

The workflow leverages Bright Data's web scraping capabilities to extract accurate financial data, then processes and formats this information into clear visualizations that can be instantly shared with your team or investment group via Telegram. This eliminates hours of manual data collection while ensuring you never miss important market movements.

How It Works

1. Data Extraction from Yahoo Finance

The workflow begins by connecting to Bright Data's scraping infrastructure, which reliably extracts market cap data from Yahoo Finance pages without triggering anti-bot protections. It can be configured to monitor specific tickers or pull comprehensive market data.

2. Data Processing and Analysis

Raw financial data is cleaned and structured, with calculations performed to identify percentage changes, market cap rankings, and other key metrics. The workflow can be customized to focus on specific sectors or market segments.

3. Visualization Creation

The processed data is transformed into visual formats like charts and tables using n8n's built-in visualization tools. These graphics highlight trends and make complex financial data easily digestible at a glance.

4. Telegram Delivery

Final visualizations are automatically sent to a designated Telegram channel or group, where stakeholders can immediately access the latest market insights without needing to log into specialized platforms.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)

  3. Telegram bot token and channel/group ID

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure Bright Data credentials in the webhook node
  3. Set up your Telegram bot and insert its token
  4. Specify target companies by ticker symbol
  5. Test with a single company before scaling to your full watchlist
