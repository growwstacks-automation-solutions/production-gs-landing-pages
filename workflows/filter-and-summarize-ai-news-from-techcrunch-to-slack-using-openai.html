What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the challenge of staying updated on AI developments without spending hours manually scanning news sites. TechCrunch publishes dozens of articles daily, but only a fraction relate specifically to artificial intelligence. The workflow automatically identifies relevant content, extracts key insights, and delivers concise summaries directly to your team's Slack channel.

By combining web scraping with OpenAI's natural language processing, the system filters out irrelevant articles while preserving important AI-related news. Each summary includes the article's core points, saving your team from reading full pieces that may not be pertinent. The result is a streamlined information pipeline that keeps everyone informed without information overload.

How It Works

1. Fetching TechCrunch articles

The workflow begins by scraping TechCrunch's RSS feed or website to collect recent articles. It extracts key metadata including titles, URLs, publication dates, and full text content. This creates a pool of potential articles for AI analysis.

2. AI content filtering

Each article gets processed through OpenAI's API to determine its relevance to artificial intelligence. The system analyzes text patterns, keywords, and contextual clues to score each piece's AI focus. Only articles passing a customizable threshold proceed to summarization.

3. Generating summaries

For qualifying articles, OpenAI generates concise summaries highlighting key points while preserving accuracy. The AI extracts core concepts, technical developments, and business implications in 3-5 bullet points or a short paragraph format.

4. Delivering to Slack

Final summaries get formatted with source links and published to your designated Slack channel. The workflow includes error handling to manage API limits, connectivity issues, and content formatting requirements.

Pro tip: Adjust the AI sensitivity threshold based on how narrowly you want to define "AI-related" content. Higher values catch only pure AI topics while lower values include adjacent tech areas.
