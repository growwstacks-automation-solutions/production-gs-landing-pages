Automatically monitor Twitter for brand mentions and share them with your team in Slack

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the challenge of manually monitoring Twitter for brand mentions and sharing them with your team. Many companies miss valuable conversations about their brand because they don't have an efficient way to track and distribute these mentions across their organization.

The workflow automatically searches Twitter for mentions of your company name, product names, or relevant keywords, then posts those mentions to a designated Slack channel. This ensures your entire team stays informed about public conversations happening about your brand in real-time.

How It Works

1. Twitter search setup

The workflow connects to Twitter's API and performs regular searches based on your configured keywords. You can specify exact phrases, hashtags, or account mentions to monitor.

2. Filtering results

New tweets matching your search criteria are filtered to exclude retweets or replies if desired. You can also set up rules to prioritize certain types of mentions.

3. Slack notification

Each qualifying tweet is formatted into a clean Slack message with the tweet content, author information, and direct link. The message is posted to your chosen Slack channel where team members can engage with it.

Businesses should track direct mentions (@yourbrand), indirect mentions ("your brand name" without @), relevant hashtags, product names, executive names, and common misspellings. Each type provides different insights into how people discuss your brand.

Twitter API v2 offers more advanced filtering capabilities and better rate limits compared to v1.1. Version 2 provides more precise search operators, conversation threading, and enhanced metadata about tweets and authors.

For brand monitoring, v2 allows filtering by engagement metrics, language, or media type. A retail brand might use v2 to prioritize tweets with product photos, while a news outlet might filter for high-engagement threads. The newer API provides more relevant data with fewer API calls.

  • v2 has more sophisticated search operators
  • Better rate limits for continuous monitoring
  • Enhanced tweet metadata available

For most businesses, checking Twitter mentions every 15-30 minutes provides optimal coverage without overwhelming teams. High-volume brands or those in crisis-prone industries may need near real-time monitoring (1-5 minute intervals).

The ideal frequency balances responsiveness with practical workflow. A consumer electronics company might monitor continuously during product launches, while a B2B software firm might check hourly. Automation makes frequent checks feasible without manual effort.

  • Adjust frequency based on tweet volume
  • Increase monitoring during high-risk periods
  • Automation enables frequent checks without fatigue
