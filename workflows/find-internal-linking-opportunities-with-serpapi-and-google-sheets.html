What This Workflow Does

Internal linking is one of the most powerful yet time-consuming SEO tasks. Manually finding where to add links between your pages requires checking search results for each keyword and URL combination—a process that can take hours for even a modest website.

This automation solves that problem by systematically analyzing your target pages and keywords, then searching your entire site for relevant linking opportunities. It identifies pages that should reference each other but currently don't, creating a prioritized action plan in Google Sheets that your content team can implement immediately.

The workflow transforms what was once a tedious, manual audit into an automated system that continuously improves your site's internal link structure—a critical factor that search engines use to understand your content hierarchy and authority distribution.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to identify and organize internal linking opportunities efficiently.

1. Input Your Target Data

You provide a Google Sheet containing your target URLs and the primary keywords you want each page to rank for. This becomes your master list of pages needing stronger internal linking.

2. Automated Site Search

The workflow uses SerpAPI to search your website for each keyword, filtering out the target URL itself to find other relevant pages on your domain that mention or relate to that topic.

3. Intelligent Filtering & Matching

The system evaluates search results based on relevance scores, page authority, and content similarity to identify the best potential linking partners for each target page.

4. Structured Output Generation

All discovered opportunities are written back to Google Sheets with clear recommendations, including source page, target page, suggested anchor text, and relevance score. When no strong matches are found, it marks "N/A" to prevent wasted effort.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this internal linking automation running in under 30 minutes:

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file
  2. Create a Google Sheet with columns for: Domain, Target URL, Primary Keyword
  3. Enable Google Sheets API in Google Cloud Console and create service account credentials
  4. Get your SerpAPI key from their dashboard (or set up Google Programmable Search Engine)
  5. Configure credentials in n8n for both Google Sheets and SerpAPI nodes
  6. Update spreadsheet IDs in the workflow to point to your source and destination sheets
  7. Test with 2-3 URLs first to verify the search results and matching logic work correctly
  8. Schedule the workflow to run weekly or monthly for continuous optimization

Pro tip: Start with your highest-priority pages (commercial landing pages, pillar content) before running the automation across your entire site. This ensures you get the biggest SEO impact fastest.
