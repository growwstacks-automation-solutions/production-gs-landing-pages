What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common problem for shared Netflix accounts: ensuring everyone who needs access receives important emails like temporary login codes, billing notifications, and new device alerts. When you share a Netflix account with family members, roommates, or team members, manually forwarding each email is time-consuming and prone to errors.

The workflow automatically monitors your Gmail inbox for emails from Netflix, then forwards them to all specified recipients via Mailjet. This ensures that login codes arrive instantly when someone needs to access the account, billing alerts reach the right people, and everyone stays informed about account activity without any manual intervention.

Beyond convenience, this automation provides reliability. It runs 24/7, never forgets to forward an email, and handles the process consistently. Whether you're managing a family entertainment account or a business subscription shared among team members, this workflow eliminates the communication gaps that often occur with shared digital services.

How It Works

Step 1: Monitor Gmail for Netflix Emails

The workflow starts by checking your Gmail inbox every minute for new emails from Netflix. It uses Gmail's search capabilities to filter for emails from Netflix's official domains, ensuring only legitimate Netflix communications are processed.

Step 2: Extract Email Content

Once a Netflix email is detected, the workflow extracts the full email content including subject, body, sender information, and any attachments. This preserves the complete context of the original message so recipients receive exactly what Netflix sent.

Step 3: Prepare Recipient List

The workflow accesses your predefined list of recipient email addresses. This list can include family members, roommates, or team members who need access to Netflix notifications. You can easily update this list as your sharing needs change.

Step 4: Forward via Mailjet

Using Mailjet's email delivery service, the workflow sends the Netflix email to all recipients. Mailjet ensures reliable delivery, handles bounce management, and provides delivery tracking that Gmail's native forwarding doesn't offer.

Step 5: Log and Monitor

Each forwarding action is logged with details about what was sent, when, and to whom. This creates an audit trail and helps troubleshoot any delivery issues. The workflow can also send you notifications if errors occur during the forwarding process.

What You'll Need

  1. A Gmail account with Netflix emails arriving (this is the source inbox being monitored)
  2. A Mailjet account (free tier available) for sending the forwarded emails
  3. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow
  4. List of recipient email addresses for who should receive Netflix emails
  5. Custom domain email address for sending from Mailjet (Gmail addresses aren't supported as Mailjet senders)

Pro tip: Use a custom domain email (like yourname@yourdomain.com) as the Mailjet sender address rather than trying to use a Gmail address. Mailjet doesn't support Gmail addresses as senders, but custom domain emails work perfectly and look more professional to recipients.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Connect your Gmail account following n8n's OAuth authentication process
  3. Configure the recipient list in the "Set all recipients" node with the email addresses of everyone who should receive Netflix emails
  4. Connect your Mailjet account using your API credentials from the Mailjet dashboard
  5. Set your custom domain email as the sender address in the Mailjet node configuration
  6. Activate the workflow and test by having Netflix send a test email (like a login request)
