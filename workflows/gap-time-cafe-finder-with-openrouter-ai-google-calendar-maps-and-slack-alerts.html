Gap-time Cafe Finder with OpenRouter AI, Google Calendar, Maps & Slack Alerts

Automatically find the best cafes during your schedule gaps. This intelligent workflow checks your calendar, calculates travel time, searches nearby venues, uses AI to pick the perfect spot, and sends Slack alerts.

What This Workflow Does

Busy professionals, freelancers, and students often have unpredictable gaps between meetings, classes, or appointments. These short windows of free time—15 to 45 minutes—are perfect for grabbing coffee, getting focused work done, or taking a productive break. But manually checking your calendar, searching for nearby cafes, evaluating options, and deciding where to go eats up that precious time.

This automation solves that problem entirely. It acts as your personal schedule assistant, continuously monitoring your Google Calendar, calculating realistic travel time using Google Maps, fetching your personal cafe preferences from a Google Sheet, searching for nearby venues via Google Places, using OpenRouter AI to intelligently select the best match, and delivering a curated recommendation—or an urgent alert—directly to your Slack channel.

The workflow transforms reactive time management into proactive opportunity discovery. Instead of wasting your gap time deciding, you receive an actionable suggestion with ratings, distance, and a direct Maps link, allowing you to immediately head to a quality spot that fits your preferences and schedule.

How It Works

Step 1: Schedule Trigger & Calendar Check

The workflow runs on a scheduled trigger (e.g., every 30 minutes). It connects to your Google Calendar and retrieves your next upcoming event, including its location, start time, and title.

Step 2: Travel Time Calculation

Using Google Maps Distance Matrix API, it calculates the travel time from your current location (configurable) to the event venue. This determines your actual usable "gap time"—the time available before you need to start traveling.

Step 3: Conditional Logic & Gap Evaluation

If the gap time is sufficient (e.g., ≥30 minutes), the workflow proceeds to cafe search. If it's too short, it branches to send an urgent Slack alert reminding you to prepare for your next appointment, providing event details and travel time.

Step 4: Preference Retrieval & Venue Search

The workflow reads your cafe preferences from a Google Sheet (e.g., "needs Wi-Fi", "quiet atmosphere", "good espresso"). It then uses Google Places API to search for cafes near your current location within a defined radius.

Step 5: AI-Powered Recommendation

OpenRouter AI analyzes the list of nearby cafes, your preferences, and the available gap time. It selects the best-matched cafe, considering ratings, distance, amenities, and your constraints, and formats a personalized recommendation.

Step 6: Slack Notification Delivery

The final recommendation—with cafe name, rating, distance, travel time, and a Google Maps link—is sent to your configured Slack channel. You get an actionable message right where you communicate.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n Instance: Self-hosted or cloud version.
  2. Google Account: Access to Google Calendar and Google Sheets.
  3. Google Cloud Project: With Google Maps Platform APIs enabled (Distance Matrix API & Places API) and corresponding API keys.
  4. OpenRouter API Key: For AI analysis and recommendation generation.
  5. Slack Workspace: With an API token or webhook URL for sending notifications.
  6. A Google Sheet: To store your personal cafe preferences (quiet, Wi-Fi, coffee type, etc.).

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the Workflow: Download the JSON template and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure API Keys: In the "Workflow Configuration" node, replace placeholder keys with your actual Google Maps and Google Places API keys. Set your default current location.
  3. Authenticate Google Services: Connect the "Get Next Calendar Event" node to your Google Calendar account and select the calendar to monitor. Authenticate the "Get User Preferences" node with Google Sheets and point it to your preference sheet.
  4. Set Up AI & Slack: Authenticate the OpenRouter Chat Model node with your OpenRouter API key. Connect both Slack nodes to your workspace and specify the channel ID for notifications.
  5. Adjust Scheduling & Thresholds: Modify the Schedule Trigger interval to match your routine. Update the minimum gap minutes variable to your preferred threshold.
  6. Activate & Test: Turn on the workflow and test with a mock calendar event to ensure alerts and recommendations flow correctly.

Pro tip: Start with a longer gap threshold (45 minutes) to ensure the workflow has enough time to find and recommend venues. Once confident, lower it to 30 minutes for more frequent suggestions.
