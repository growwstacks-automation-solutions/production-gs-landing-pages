What This Workflow Does

Manual incident reporting is slow, error-prone, and diverts critical IT resources from actual problem-solving. This automation transforms raw incident data into professional, AI-analyzed reports within minutes instead of hours.

The workflow accepts incident details via webhook, uses GPT-4 to generate root cause analysis and recommended actions, checks severity levels, sends immediate Slack alerts for critical issues, formats everything into a clean report, converts it to PDF, emails it to your operations team, and archives a copy to Google Drive—all automatically.

How It Works

1. Incident Data Collection

The workflow starts with a webhook trigger that accepts incident details including ID, severity level, affected systems, and description from your monitoring tools or manual entry forms.

2. AI-Powered Analysis

GPT-4 analyzes the incident data to generate professional root cause analysis, impact assessment, and recommended corrective actions based on the context provided.

3. Severity-Based Alerting

The workflow automatically detects high-severity incidents and sends immediate alerts to designated Slack channels with key details and urgency indicators.

4. Professional Report Generation

All incident data and AI analysis are formatted into a structured, readable report with proper sections, headers, and consistent styling.

5. PDF Creation & Distribution

The formatted report is converted to a professional PDF document and automatically emailed to your operations team and stakeholders.

6. Secure Archiving

A copy of the PDF report is saved to Google Drive for compliance, audit trails, and historical reference.

Pro tip: Configure different Slack channels for different severity levels—critical incidents to #ops-emergency, medium to #ops-alerts, and low to #ops-notifications for optimal team response.

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for IT operations teams, DevOps engineers, security teams, compliance officers, and any organization that needs consistent, audit-ready incident documentation. It's particularly valuable for companies subject to regulatory requirements (SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA), technology startups with limited IT staff, and enterprises managing complex system architectures.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. OpenAI API key for GPT-4 analysis
  3. Slack workspace with appropriate channel access
  4. Gmail account or SMTP credentials for email delivery
  5. HTML/CSS to PDF service (like PDFShift, DocRaptor, or similar)
  6. Google Drive account (optional, for archiving)

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure the Webhook node to receive incident data from your monitoring tools
  3. Connect your OpenAI credentials to the GPT-4 node
  4. Set up Slack credentials and specify your operations channel
  5. Configure Gmail or your SMTP service for report delivery
  6. Set up your PDF generation service credentials
  7. Test with sample incident data and activate the workflow

Pro tip: Start with a test Slack channel and a small group of email recipients during initial setup. Once confirmed working, expand to production channels and full distribution lists.
