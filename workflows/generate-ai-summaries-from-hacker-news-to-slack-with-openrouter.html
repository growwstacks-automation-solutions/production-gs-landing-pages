Generate AI summaries from Hacker News to Slack with OpenRouter

Automatically distill tech discussions into actionable insights delivered to your team

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of information overload from Hacker News by delivering curated, AI-generated summaries directly to your Slack channel. Instead of manually browsing through hundreds of comments and posts, your team gets distilled insights on the tech topics that matter most to your business.

The workflow monitors Hacker News for relevant discussions, uses OpenRouter's AI models to generate concise summaries, and posts them to your designated Slack channel. You can customize it to focus on specific keywords, technologies, or industry trends relevant to your team.

How It Works

1. Monitoring Hacker News

The workflow regularly checks Hacker News for new posts matching your criteria. It can monitor specific keywords, trending topics, or high-engagement threads based on upvotes and comment counts.

2. Content Analysis

For each qualifying post, the system analyzes the discussion thread, identifying key comments and themes. It filters out low-quality or off-topic contributions to focus on substantive content.

3. AI Summarization

Using OpenRouter's API, the workflow sends the curated content to an AI model (like GPT-4 or Claude) which generates a concise summary highlighting main points, differing opinions, and key takeaways.

4. Slack Delivery

The final summary is formatted and posted to your designated Slack channel, complete with source links. Team members can immediately engage with the insights through reactions, comments, or saved messages.

Pro tip: Configure separate Slack channels for different topics (e.g., #tech-news, #startup-trends) to keep summaries organized by theme.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure your Hacker News search parameters (keywords, point thresholds)
  3. Connect your OpenRouter API credentials
  4. Set up a Slack incoming webhook and paste the URL into the workflow
  5. Test with sample data and adjust summarization prompts as needed
  6. Schedule the workflow to run at your preferred frequency
