Generate content from Google Sheets to X with GPT-4

AI-powered workflow to automatically create and post social media content from spreadsheet data

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

This automation transforms raw data from Google Sheets into polished social media posts using GPT-4's advanced AI capabilities, then publishes them directly to X (formerly Twitter). It solves the time-consuming process of manually creating content from spreadsheets - a common challenge for marketers managing product launches, event promotions, or data-driven content.

The workflow automatically extracts key information from your spreadsheet, structures it into coherent prompts for GPT-4, generates multiple post variations with proper formatting and hashtags, then schedules or immediately publishes the best-performing version to your X account. This creates a seamless pipeline from data to published content without manual intervention.

How It Works

Step 1: Spreadsheet Data Extraction

The workflow connects to your Google Sheet and identifies new or updated rows containing content parameters like product details, event information, or key messages. It extracts structured data fields that will inform the AI content generation.

Step 2: AI Content Generation

Using GPT-4, the system transforms spreadsheet data into engaging social media posts. The AI considers your specified tone, formatting preferences, and any examples of previous successful posts to maintain brand consistency.

Step 3: Quality Review & Selection

The workflow generates 2-3 variations of each post, applies any required compliance checks, and either automatically selects the optimal version based on your criteria or holds them for manual review.

Step 4: X (Twitter) Publishing

Approved posts are automatically published to your X account according to your preferred schedule. The system can include images, hashtags, and mentions as configured in your original spreadsheet data.

Pro tip: Include example posts in your Google Sheet to train the AI on your preferred writing style. The more examples you provide, the more accurate the output.
