What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common problem for content creators, researchers, and marketers: how to generate genuinely novel, relevant ideas from existing documents. Instead of relying on generic AI prompts, it uses InfraNodus's GraphRAG (Graph-based Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to analyze PDFs as a network of concepts.

The workflow uploads PDF files (research papers, competitor reports, market analyses), extracts their text, builds a knowledge graph, identifies structural gaps between topic clusters, and then uses AI to generate ideas, questions, or prompts that bridge those gaps. This produces content that is both context-aware and innovative.

Businesses use this to uncover blind spots in competitor discourse, accelerate research ideation, and create marketing content that addresses unmet audience needs—all without manually reading hundreds of pages.

InfraNodus knowledge graph showing concept clusters and the gaps between them—the source of novel ideas.

How It Works

Step 1: Upload PDFs via Web Form

A user-friendly web form (built with n8n's Form Trigger) allows you or your team to upload multiple PDF documents. This can be made public for client submissions or kept internal for research teams.

Step 2–4: Extract & Clean Text

The workflow uses Code and PDF to Text nodes to convert uploaded PDFs into plain, analyzable text. It handles formatting variations and prepares the content for graph construction.

Step 5: GraphRAG Analysis & Idea Generation

The extracted text is sent to the InfraNodus GraphRAG API node. It builds a knowledge graph, calculates network metrics to find the most important concepts and disconnected clusters, then uses built-in AI to generate ideas that connect those gaps.

Step 6: Output Delivery

The generated ideas are displayed back in the web form, sent via email/Slack, or saved to a database. You can route them directly into your content calendar or research pipeline.

Pro tip: You can modify Step 4 to always target the largest structural gap, ensuring the most significant unexplored connections are addressed first.
