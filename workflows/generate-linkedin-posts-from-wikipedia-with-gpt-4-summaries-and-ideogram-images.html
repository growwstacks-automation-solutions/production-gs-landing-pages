What This Workflow Does

Creating consistent, valuable LinkedIn content is time-consuming. Researching topics, writing engaging summaries, designing visuals, and scheduling posts can take hours each week. This automation solves that problem by turning Wikipedia's vast knowledge base into ready-to-publish LinkedIn content.

The workflow automatically scrapes Wikipedia articles on topics you specify, uses AI to generate professional summaries optimized for LinkedIn's audience, creates relevant images using Ideogram AI, and publishes everything directly to your LinkedIn profile. What used to be a multi-hour process becomes a fully automated system that runs in minutes.

Perfect for thought leaders, marketers, and businesses wanting to establish authority without the constant content creation grind. You maintain an active, educational presence while focusing on higher-value activities like engagement and relationship building.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical four-step process that transforms raw Wikipedia content into polished LinkedIn posts.

Step 1: Article Selection & Data Extraction

You provide a Wikipedia article title through a simple web form. The workflow uses Bright Data to scrape the article's full content, including headings, paragraphs, and key information. This ensures you're working with accurate, well-structured source material.

Step 2: AI-Powered Summarization

The scraped content passes through OpenAI's GPT-4 or Anthropic's Claude to create LinkedIn-optimized summaries. The AI is instructed to maintain professional tone, highlight key insights, and keep the content under 2000 characters—perfect for LinkedIn's format.

Step 3: Visual Content Creation

Based on the generated summary, Ideogram AI creates a relevant, professional image. The workflow automatically formats the image to LinkedIn's preferred dimensions (1280x704 pixels) and style guidelines, ensuring visual appeal and platform compatibility.

Step 4: LinkedIn Publishing & URL Generation

The final step posts the complete content package—text summary and generated image—to your LinkedIn profile via the LinkedIn API. Once published, the workflow captures and returns the post URL for easy sharing and tracking.

Pro tip: Start with well-known Wikipedia articles in your industry to ensure high-quality source material. The AI works best with comprehensive, factual content that it can transform into engaging business insights.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this automation running in under 15 minutes with these straightforward steps:

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n instance via "Workflows → Add workflow → Import from JSON"
  2. Configure Credentials: Set up API credentials for Bright Data, OpenAI, Ideogram AI, and LinkedIn in n8n's credential management
  3. Test the Trigger: Use the webhook form trigger to submit a test Wikipedia article title and verify the scraping works
  4. Adjust AI Prompts: Customize the system message in the AI Agent node to match your brand voice and content style
  5. Set Up Scheduling: Add a Schedule Trigger node to run the workflow daily or weekly for consistent content
  6. Monitor First Run: Execute the workflow once manually to ensure all connections work and the LinkedIn post publishes correctly

Important: Always test with a non-critical LinkedIn account first. Verify the content quality and formatting before connecting to your main business profile.
