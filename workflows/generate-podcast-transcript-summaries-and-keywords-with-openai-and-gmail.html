What This Workflow Does

This automation workflow solves the time-consuming task of manually processing podcast transcripts by automatically generating AI-powered summaries and SEO-optimized keywords. Content creators, podcasters, and marketing teams often spend hours analyzing episode content to create show notes, social media snippets, and SEO metadata. This workflow eliminates that manual effort by leveraging OpenAI's natural language processing capabilities to extract key insights and generate ready-to-use content assets.

The system processes podcast transcripts through multiple AI analysis steps, creating comprehensive summaries that capture the essence of each episode while identifying the most relevant keywords for search optimization. This enables podcasters to quickly repurpose their content across multiple platforms, improve discoverability through better SEO, and maintain consistent episode documentation without additional manual work.

How It Works

Transcript Input Processing

The workflow begins by receiving podcast transcripts through Gmail attachments or direct text input. The system automatically processes these transcripts, cleaning and formatting the text for optimal AI analysis. This initial step ensures that the content is properly structured for the summarization and keyword extraction processes.

AI-Powered Analysis

OpenAI's language model processes the transcript to identify key themes, main points, and important concepts. The AI analyzes the entire conversation contextually, understanding nuances and extracting the most valuable information. This analysis forms the foundation for creating comprehensive summaries and relevant keyword lists.

Summary Generation

The system generates multiple types of summaries including executive summaries for quick overviews, detailed breakdowns for show notes, and social-media-friendly snippets. Each summary type is tailored for specific use cases, ensuring the content is immediately usable without additional editing.

Keyword Extraction

OpenAI identifies the most relevant keywords and phrases from the transcript, prioritizing terms based on frequency, context, and search volume potential. The workflow generates both primary keywords for SEO optimization and secondary keywords for content tagging and categorization.
