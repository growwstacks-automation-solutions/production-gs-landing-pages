What This Workflow Does

Manual invoice creation is a tedious, error-prone bottleneck for freelancers, consultants, and small businesses. Every time a client submits an order form, you must copy their details, find product codes, calculate totals, generate the invoice, and finally email it. This workflow eliminates that entire process.

It automatically triggers when a customer submits a Jotform order form. The system checks if the customer exists in Xero, creates or updates their contact record, generates a professional invoice with the correct line items, and emails it directly to the client—all within minutes, without you lifting a finger.

This isn't just about saving time; it's about improving accuracy, speeding up payment cycles, and delivering a seamless, professional client experience from the moment they hit "submit."

How It Works

The automation follows a logical, four-step sequence that mirrors your ideal manual process but executes it flawlessly every time.

1. Trigger: Form Submission

The workflow starts when a customer completes your Jotform. This could be a service inquiry, product order, or project request. The form captures all necessary details: client name, email, address, service/product selection, quantities, and any special notes.

2. Customer Sync with Xero

The system takes the submitted email address and checks your Xero contacts. If the customer exists, it updates their record with any new information from the form. If they're new, it creates a fully detailed contact in Xero. This ensures your customer database is always current and accurate.

3. Invoice Creation

Using the product or service codes defined in your Xero account, the workflow constructs a proper invoice. It maps the form selections to the correct Xero items, applies pricing, calculates totals (including tax if configured), and generates a ready-to-send invoice document within your Xero account.

4. Automated Delivery

Finally, the system uses your connected email service (like Gmail) to send the invoice directly to the client. The email can be customized with a personal message, payment instructions, and a link to the PDF invoice hosted in Xero. The entire process—from form submit to invoice sent—happens automatically.

What You'll Need

  1. A Jotform account with a form configured to capture order/client details.
  2. A Xero account with your products/services already set up as items with unique Codes.
  3. An email service (like Gmail or an SMTP server) configured in n8n to send messages.
  4. Basic understanding of how to set up a Jotform webhook to trigger the workflow.
  5. Your form's product/service selections must exactly match the Item Codes in your Xero account.

Pro tip: Before you start, log into your Xero account and note down the exact "Code" for each billable item you sell. This mapping is crucial for the automation to work correctly.

Quick Setup Guide

Importing and configuring this workflow is straightforward. Follow these steps to get it running in your n8n instance.

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure your credentials: Set up the Xero, Jotform (Webhook), and Gmail (or your email app) nodes with your account details.
  3. Map your data: In the workflow, check the "Create Invoice" node. Ensure the field mapping (like `itemCode` from Jotform) matches your Xero item Codes.
  4. Set up the Jotform webhook: In your Jotform form settings, add a new Webhook integration and point it to the n8n webhook URL provided when you activate the workflow.
  5. Test it: Submit a test form entry and watch the workflow create a contact and invoice in Xero, then send the email.
