What This Workflow Does

Content creators face a massive challenge: producing 1-2 long-form YouTube videos per week is demanding enough, but extracting 5-10 engaging short-form clips from each video adds another 10-15 hours of manual work. This repetitive process involves reviewing footage, identifying key moments, writing captions, creating thumbnails, and optimizing titles—work that steals time from strategy and creativity.

This n8n automation solves that problem completely. When you upload an edited video to Google Drive, the system automatically generates transcriptions, creates A/B testable titles, produces AI thumbnails with brand consistency, identifies optimal clip timestamps with action-oriented captions, and builds YouTube descriptions with chapter markers. What used to take half a day now happens automatically while you focus on creating your next video.

How It Works

1. Video Upload & Transcription

The workflow monitors a designated Google Drive folder. When a new video appears, it creates an Airtable record and sends the video URL to AssemblyAI (via Apify) for transcription with precise H:MM:SS.mmm timestamps.

2. Title Generation & Thumbnail Creation

GPT-4o-mini analyzes the transcript and generates 3 title variations optimized for A/B testing. When you add your thumbnail prompt and reference images to Airtable and click "Generate thumbnail," your prompt gets optimized and sent to Kie.ai's Nano Banana Pro model for consistent branding.

3. YouTube Integration & Caption Polling

After you upload the video to YouTube (as unlisted) and add the video ID to Airtable, the workflow polls YouTube's API every 5 minutes to check when auto-generated captions are ready—saving you from manual checking.

4. Clip Identification & Description Building

Once captions are available, Grok 4.1 Fast analyzes the transcript to identify 3-8 elite clips (45+ seconds each) with proper start/end boundaries and engaging captions. GPT-4o-mini then generates a complete YouTube description with chapter timestamps.

5. Centralized Content Storage

All outputs—titles, thumbnail URL, clip timestamps with captions, and description—are saved to Airtable, creating a centralized content repository ready for publishing across YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and other platforms.

What You'll Need

  1. Airtable Pro account (for automations and button formulas)
  2. Google Drive for video upload monitoring
  3. Apify account for video transcription via AssemblyAI actor
  4. OpenAI API key for title and description generation (GPT-4o-mini)
  5. OpenRouter API key for clip identification (Grok 4.1 Fast)
  6. Kie.ai account for AI thumbnail generation (Nano Banana Pro model)
  7. YouTube Data API credentials for caption polling
  8. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)

Pro tip: Start with one video to test the workflow end-to-end before automating your entire content pipeline. This helps you understand timing (10-15 minutes processing per video) and customize prompts for your niche.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Duplicate the Airtable base template and connect it to your n8n instance
  2. Create a Google Drive folder specifically for uploading edited videos
  3. Activate the workflow and copy webhook URLs into Airtable button formulas
  4. Upload your first edited video to the Google Drive folder to trigger the system
  5. Add thumbnail prompt and reference images to Airtable, then click "Generate thumbnail"
  6. Upload to YouTube as unlisted, paste the video ID into Airtable, and check the clip generation box
  7. Use provided timestamps to manually clip videos in your editor (the only remaining manual step)
  8. Copy all generated content from Airtable to publish across your platforms
