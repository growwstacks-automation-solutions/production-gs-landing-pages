What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of unsecured calendar sharing and lack of tracking in meeting scheduling. Traditional Calendly links can be shared indefinitely, leading to unauthorized bookings and lost tracking of invite sources. Our template generates unique, single-use booking links for each recipient while automatically logging all activity.

The system creates personalized booking pages that expire after first use, records each generated link in Google Sheets with timestamps and metadata, and sends real-time Slack notifications when appointments are scheduled. This maintains the convenience of self-scheduling while adding enterprise-grade tracking and security.

Example of a personalized booking page generated by the workflow

How It Works

1. Trigger Event

The workflow activates when new contact data is received, typically from a CRM, form submission, or manual entry. This contains the recipient's name, email, and meeting type.

2. Link Generation

The system calls Calendly's API to create a unique, single-use booking link with personalized parameters. You can customize the event type, duration, and pre-filled fields.

3. Tracking Setup

Each generated link is immediately logged in Google Sheets with the recipient's details, generation timestamp, and unique identifier. This creates an audit trail for all booking activity.

Automated tracking log in Google Sheets showing link status

4. Notification System

When a recipient books a meeting, the workflow sends a detailed Slack notification to your team channel. This includes the attendee's information, meeting details, and a link to the Google Sheets record.

Pro tip: Add conditional logic to route different meeting types to specific team members or channels based on your business rules.
