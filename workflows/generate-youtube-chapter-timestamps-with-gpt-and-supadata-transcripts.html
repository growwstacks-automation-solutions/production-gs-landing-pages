What This Workflow Does

This n8n automation template solves a common pain point for YouTube creators: manually creating timestamp chapters for videos. Instead of spending 15-30 minutes per video analyzing content and formatting timestamps, this workflow automatically detects when you publish a new video, extracts the transcript using SupaData, uses AI to identify natural topic breaks, and appends professionally formatted chapters to your video description.

The system works by monitoring your YouTube channel's RSS feed for new uploads. When a video is detected, it fetches the transcript, analyzes the content structure, generates logical chapter points with descriptive titles, and updates your YouTube description—all without manual intervention. This not only saves time but also improves viewer experience and retention rates.

Beyond basic automation, this template includes smart deduplication to prevent reprocessing the same video, error handling for failed transcript extraction, and customizable AI prompts to match your channel's specific tone and formatting preferences.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence that mimics how a human editor would create chapters, but with AI precision and consistency.

1. New Video Detection

The workflow starts by monitoring your YouTube channel's RSS feed. When a new video is published, it captures the video URL, title, and metadata. This trigger can be scheduled to run hourly or daily depending on your publishing frequency.

2. Transcript Extraction

Using the SupaData API, the system fetches the complete transcript of your YouTube video. This includes not just the spoken words but also timing information for each segment, which forms the foundation for accurate chapter placement.

3. AI Chapter Generation

The transcript is sent to an AI model (like OpenAI's GPT) with carefully crafted prompts that instruct it to identify natural topic transitions, key points, and logical breakpoints. The AI returns structured chapters with timestamps and descriptive titles.

4. Description Update

The workflow retrieves your video's current description from YouTube, appends the generated chapters in the standard YouTube timestamp format (00:00 Introduction, 02:15 Main Topic, etc.), and pushes the updated description back through the YouTube API.

Pro tip: Configure the AI prompt to match your brand voice—add emojis, call out sponsor segments, or create consistent formatting across all your videos automatically.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation for your YouTube channel:

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure credentials: Set up your YouTube OAuth, SupaData API, and AI service credentials in n8n.
  3. Set your channel RSS: Replace the RSS feed URL in the trigger node with your YouTube channel's feed.
  4. Customize AI prompts: Adjust the prompt in the AI node to match your preferred chapter style and formatting.
  5. Test with manual trigger: Use the manual trigger node to test the workflow on an existing video before enabling automation.
  6. Activate the schedule: Switch to the RSS trigger and set your preferred polling frequency (hourly recommended).

Important: Always test with the manual trigger first on a single video to ensure chapters are generated correctly before enabling full automation for your entire channel.
