Automatically Save MP4 Attachments from Gmail to Google Drive

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common business problem: video files trapped in email inboxes. When clients, team members, or partners send MP4 attachments via Gmail, they remain buried in threads—difficult to organize, share, or archive. Manual downloading and re-uploading to Google Drive consumes valuable time and creates version control issues.

The workflow automatically monitors your Gmail for incoming emails containing MP4 attachments, extracts the video files, and uploads them directly to specified Google Drive folders. It generates shareable webview links, making videos instantly accessible to your team without digging through emails. This creates a centralized, organized video library while eliminating repetitive manual work.

How It Works

Step 1: Monitor Gmail for New Emails

The workflow watches your Gmail inbox (or specific labels) for new messages. You can configure filters to only process emails from certain senders, with specific subjects, or containing particular keywords. This ensures only relevant video attachments trigger the automation.

Step 2: Extract MP4 Attachments

When a matching email arrives, the automation identifies all MP4 attachments. It can handle multiple videos per email and processes each file individually. The system captures metadata like original filename, sender information, and email subject for later organization.

Step 3: Upload to Google Drive

Each MP4 file uploads to a designated Google Drive folder. You can organize videos by date, sender, project, or any other criteria. The automation can create folder structures dynamically based on email metadata, ensuring consistent organization without manual intervention.

Step 4: Generate Shareable Links

After upload, the workflow creates webview links for each file. These links can be shared with team members, added to project management tools, or stored in your CRM. Optional notifications can alert relevant parties that new video content is available.

Pro tip: Combine this workflow with other automations to automatically transcribe videos, create thumbnails, or notify your editing team when new raw footage arrives.
