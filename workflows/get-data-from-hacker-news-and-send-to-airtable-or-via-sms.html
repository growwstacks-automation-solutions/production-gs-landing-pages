What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common problem for tech teams, founders, and content creators: staying updated with Hacker News trends requires constant manual checking, which is time-consuming and easy to miss. The workflow automatically monitors Hacker News for new posts, filters for relevant content, translates technical terms if needed, and then either sends SMS alerts to your team or saves structured data to Airtable for later analysis.

Instead of spending 30-60 minutes daily scanning through hundreds of posts, you receive only the most relevant updates in your preferred format. The system can be configured to track specific keywords, monitor competitors, capture startup launches from "Show HN" posts, or build a knowledge base of emerging technologies.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence to transform raw Hacker News data into actionable business intelligence.

Step 1: Fetch & Filter Hacker News Data

The workflow connects to the Hacker News API to retrieve the latest or top stories. You can configure it to pull from specific categories (new, top, best, ask) and apply filters based on score thresholds, keyword matching, or time ranges. This ensures you only process relevant content.

Step 2: Process & Translate Content

Each post is analyzed and processed. If translation is enabled (for non-English teams), the workflow sends key terms or full content to a translation service like LingvaNex. The system can extract metadata like author, points, comments, and URLs for comprehensive tracking.

Step 3: Route to Destination

Based on your configuration, the processed data is routed to one or multiple destinations. For immediate alerts, it sends formatted SMS messages via Vonage. For long-term storage and analysis, it creates structured records in Airtable with all relevant fields populated.

Step 4: Schedule & Monitor

The workflow runs on a schedule you define—every hour, daily, or weekly. Failed runs trigger notifications, and you can monitor performance through n8n's execution history to ensure reliable delivery of tech intelligence.

Pro tip: Combine this with other workflows to automatically create tasks in your project management tool when specific technologies are mentioned, or to generate social media content from trending discussions.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in under 15 minutes:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance
  2. Configure the Hacker News node to fetch from your preferred category (top, new, best, ask)
  3. Set up your Airtable connection with base ID, table name, and field mappings
  4. Add your Vonage credentials and test phone numbers for SMS delivery
  5. Adjust filtering logic to match your keyword interests and score thresholds
  6. Test with manual trigger to verify data flows correctly to both destinations
  7. Activate the schedule to run automatically at your preferred interval

Pro tip: Start with a conservative schedule (once daily) and broad filters, then refine based on what content you find most valuable. You can create separate workflows for different alert types—immediate SMS for high-priority items, daily digest for moderate interest, and Airtable-only for everything else.
