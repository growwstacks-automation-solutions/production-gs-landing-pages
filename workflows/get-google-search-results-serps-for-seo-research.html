What This Workflow Does

Manual SEO research is time-consuming and inconsistent. This automation solves that by programmatically fetching Google search results for your target keywords, capturing ranking positions, competitor URLs, and page titles. It transforms hours of manual checking into minutes of automated data collection.

The workflow connects to scraping APIs to retrieve SERP (Search Engine Results Page) data, processes up to 5000 searches with a free account, and organizes results for immediate analysis. You get structured data showing exactly where your website ranks, who's ranking above you, and what content is dominating search results for your industry.

How It Works

1. Keyword Input

The workflow accepts keywords from your database or a manual list. You can connect it to your existing keyword research tools or simply input terms directly.

2. SERP Data Fetching

Using the ScrapingRobot API (or similar services), the system queries Google search for each keyword, capturing the first few pages of results with metadata like URLs, titles, and descriptions.

3. Data Processing

Results are cleaned and structured, identifying your website's position, tracking competitor domains, and extracting key metrics about the search landscape.

4. Output & Storage

Processed data is formatted for your preferred database, spreadsheet, or reporting tool, ready for analysis, visualization, or integration with other marketing systems.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. ScrapingRobot account (free tier offers 5000 searches)
  3. API key from your chosen scraping service
  4. Keyword list in database, spreadsheet, or text format
  5. Output destination (Google Sheets, Airtable, database, etc.)

Pro tip: Start with your 20-30 most important keywords to validate the setup. Once confirmed, expand to track hundreds or thousands of terms. Schedule the workflow to run weekly for consistent tracking without manual intervention.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Create a ScrapingRobot account and obtain your API key.
  3. Add your API key to the HTTP Request node's authentication settings.
  4. Connect your keyword source – either link to your database or use the included "Set Keywords" node.
  5. Configure your output to send data to your preferred storage (Google Sheets, database, etc.).
  6. Test with 2-3 keywords to verify everything works correctly.
  7. Schedule the workflow to run automatically at your desired frequency.

Tracking SERP rankings is crucial because it shows how visible your website is for target keywords. It helps you measure SEO campaign effectiveness, identify ranking opportunities, monitor competitor movements, and understand how Google's algorithm changes affect your positions.

Without tracking, you're optimizing blindly. For example, you might create great content but not realize you're stuck on page two because of technical issues or stronger competitors. Regular tracking provides the data needed for informed optimization decisions.

For most businesses, checking rankings weekly is sufficient for ongoing campaigns. During major website changes or SEO pushes, daily tracking might be needed. Monthly checks work for established sites with stable rankings.

  • Monthly: For established sites with stable rankings

SERP tracking shows your position in search results before users click, while Google Analytics shows what happens after they visit your site. SERP data reveals visibility and opportunity; Analytics reveals user behavior and conversion.

  • SERP: Visibility, opportunity, competitor analysis
