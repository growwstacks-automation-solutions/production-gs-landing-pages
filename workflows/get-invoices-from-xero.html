What This Workflow Does

Manually checking Xero for new invoices, downloading them, and distributing them to relevant teams is a repetitive, time-consuming task that's prone to human error. This automation workflow solves that by automatically retrieving invoices from your Xero account and routing them to your chosen destinations—whether that's your accounting team's inbox, a shared cloud folder, or your CRM system.

The workflow connects to Xero's API on a schedule you define, fetches new or updated invoices based on your criteria, and processes them according to your business rules. It can filter by date, status, client, or amount, then transform and deliver the data exactly where you need it. This ensures your financial records are always up-to-date without manual intervention.

Beyond simple retrieval, this template serves as a foundation for building sophisticated financial automation. You can extend it to trigger payment reminders, update client records, generate reports, or sync with other accounting platforms—turning Xero from a static record-keeping tool into an active component of your automated business operations.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a trigger-action principle, executing a series of connected steps whenever scheduled or triggered.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

A schedule node initiates the workflow at your preferred interval—daily, weekly, or hourly. This acts as the automation's heartbeat, ensuring regular, consistent invoice checks without requiring manual initiation.

Step 2: Xero Invoice Retrieval

The workflow authenticates with your Xero account using secure OAuth tokens, then queries the Xero API for invoices. You can configure filters to retrieve only specific invoices (e.g., "Sent" status, last 7 days, specific contacts) to keep data relevant and manageable.

Step 3: Data Processing & Transformation

Each retrieved invoice is processed through function or set nodes to extract key information: invoice number, date, total amount, client details, due date, and status. This structured data is then prepared for the next steps.

Step 4: Conditional Routing & Actions

Based on your rules, invoices are routed to different destinations. Overdue invoices might trigger email alerts to your accounts team, paid invoices could be archived to cloud storage, and high-value invoices might be logged in your CRM for sales follow-up.

Step 5: Delivery & Notification

The final step delivers the processed invoices and data to your chosen endpoints—email, Slack, Google Sheets, database, or other business applications—completing the automated cycle from retrieval to actionable delivery.

What You'll Need

  1. A Xero account with administrative or standard user permissions to access the API.
  2. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with the Xero node installed and configured.
  3. OAuth credentials from Xero's developer portal to authenticate the connection.
  4. Clear understanding of which invoice data you need (fields, filters, date ranges).
  5. Destination systems ready to receive the data (email addresses, folder paths, API endpoints).

Pro tip: Before setting up the automation, map out your ideal invoice workflow. Decide which invoice statuses trigger which actions (e.g., "Overdue" → alert team, "Paid" → archive, "Draft" → ignore). This planning ensures your automation aligns with actual business processes.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your n8n environment:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure the Xero node by adding your OAuth credentials from the Xero Developer Portal.
  3. Set your invoice filters in the Xero node—choose date ranges, statuses, and contact filters relevant to your needs.
  4. Map the output fields to ensure the data structure matches your destination systems.
  5. Connect your destination apps (email, Slack, Google Sheets, etc.) and test with a small batch of invoices.
  6. Activate the workflow and monitor the first few runs to verify data accuracy and delivery.
