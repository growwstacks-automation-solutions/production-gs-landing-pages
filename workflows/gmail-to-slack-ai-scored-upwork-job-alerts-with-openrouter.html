Gmail to Slack: AI-scored Upwork Job Alerts

Automate your freelance job search. Get AI-scored Upwork opportunities delivered to Slack, so you apply first to the best matches.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

For freelancers on Upwork, speed and relevance are everything. The first quality application often wins the job, but manually sifting through dozens of email alerts is time-consuming and inefficient. This automation solves that problem by creating a smart filtering system between your Gmail and Slack.

The workflow automatically scans your Gmail for new Upwork job alerts, extracts key job details, uses AI to score each opportunity against your profile and preferences, and delivers only the high-potential matches directly to your Slack channel. It transforms a chaotic inbox into a prioritized pipeline of qualified opportunities.

By automating the initial screening process, you save 5-10 hours per week on manual job searching while increasing your application quality. The AI scoring considers factors like required skills, budget alignment, project duration, and your historical success patterns to identify the best fits.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical four-step process that mimics how a skilled freelancer would evaluate opportunities, but at machine speed and consistency.

1. Gmail Monitoring & Extraction

The workflow polls your Gmail inbox every 10 minutes (configurable) specifically looking for emails from Upwork's job alert system. When found, it extracts the job title, description snippet, client details, budget range, and required skills from the email body using text parsing techniques.

2. AI-Powered Opportunity Scoring

Each extracted job is sent to an AI model (OpenRouter or your preferred provider) along with your freelancer profile. The AI analyzes the job requirements against your skills, experience, rate preferences, and project history to generate a match score from 0-100. This score determines priority.

3. Intelligent Filtering

Jobs are filtered based on your minimum score threshold. You can set different thresholds for different job types—for example, higher scores for fixed-price projects, lower for hourly. Only jobs meeting your criteria proceed to notification.

4. Slack Notification & Team Coordination

Qualified jobs are formatted into rich Slack messages with key details: score, budget, timeline, required skills, and a direct link to apply. These appear in your designated Slack channel, allowing for quick team discussion and immediate action.

What You'll Need

  1. An Upwork Freelancer Plus subscription ($19.99/month) to enable email job alerts (required for the workflow to have data to process)
  2. Gmail account with API access enabled (OAuth credentials)
  3. Slack workspace with app creation permissions to build a custom app with chat:write and channels:read scopes
  4. OpenRouter API key or alternative AI provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) for the scoring functionality
  5. Self-hosted n8n instance (this workflow uses community nodes not available in n8n Cloud)
  6. Your detailed freelancer profile including skills, rate ranges, preferred project types, and experience highlights for the AI to reference

Pro tip: When setting up your freelancer profile for the AI scorer, include specific technologies, frameworks, and soft skills you possess. The more detailed your profile, the more accurate the matching becomes. Include phrases like "10+ years React experience" or "specialize in SaaS onboarding flows" rather than generic statements.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting this automation running requires about 30 minutes of configuration. Follow these steps carefully:

  1. Import the template into your self-hosted n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file.
  2. Configure Gmail credentials in the first node, ensuring you've enabled Gmail API access in your Google Cloud Console.
  3. Set up your Slack app at api.slack.com, install it to your workspace, and add the OAuth token to n8n's Slack credential.
  4. Add your OpenRouter API key (or alternative) in the LLM Model node settings.
  5. Customize the Opportunity Scorer node by replacing the placeholder text between <my_profile> tags with your actual freelancer bio and preferences.
  6. Adjust the filter threshold in the Filter node—start with 70/100 and adjust based on results.
  7. Test with historical emails by temporarily changing the Gmail node to fetch older messages before enabling the schedule.
