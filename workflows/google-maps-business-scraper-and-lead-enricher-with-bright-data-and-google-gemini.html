What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming challenge of manual lead research by automatically extracting business information from Google Maps, enhancing it with additional data points from Bright Data, and using Google Gemini AI to analyze and qualify leads. It transforms hours of manual research into an automated process that delivers ready-to-use lead information directly to your CRM or sales team.

The workflow is particularly valuable for B2B companies, sales teams, and marketing agencies that need to build targeted prospect lists in specific geographic areas or industries. By combining scraping, data enrichment, and AI analysis, it creates comprehensive lead profiles that help sales teams prioritize outreach and personalize their approach.

The workflow interface showing the sequence of scraping, enrichment, and AI analysis steps

How It Works

1. Google Maps Business Scraping

The workflow begins by extracting business listings from Google Maps based on your specified search criteria (location, industry keywords, etc.). It collects essential details like business names, addresses, phone numbers, websites, and customer reviews.

2. Bright Data Enrichment

Each scraped business profile is then sent to Bright Data for enhancement. This service adds valuable information like company size, estimated revenue, social media profiles, and executive contact details when available, creating a more complete picture of each potential lead.

3. Google Gemini AI Analysis

The enriched data is processed by Google Gemini, which evaluates lead quality, identifies potential customer fit based on your criteria, and can even generate personalized outreach suggestions. The AI helps prioritize which leads to contact first based on predicted conversion likelihood.

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to automatically flag high-priority leads based on specific criteria like business size, location, or review sentiment to help your sales team focus their efforts.

4. CRM Integration

The final step delivers the processed lead information to your preferred CRM or sales platform, complete with all the enriched data and AI insights. This creates ready-to-use lead records that sales teams can immediately act upon.

Scraping publicly available business information from Google Maps is generally legal, but compliance depends on how the data is used. Avoid scraping personal data, respect robots.txt files, and use the information only for legitimate business purposes. Bright Data helps ensure compliance with data protection regulations.

Legal precedents like the hiQ vs LinkedIn case have upheld the right to scrape public data, but it's wise to consult legal counsel about specific use cases. Ethical scraping focuses on business contact information rather than personal details, and always provides opt-out options in communications.

  • Focus on business, not personal data
  • Respect rate limits and terms of service
  • Provide clear opt-out in all communications
