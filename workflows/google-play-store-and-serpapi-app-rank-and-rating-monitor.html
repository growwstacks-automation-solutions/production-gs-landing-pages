Google Play Store and SerpApi App Rank & Rating Monitor

Automatically track your mobile app's search rankings and user ratings in the Google Play Store

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

This automation solution continuously monitors your app's position in Google Play Store search results for your target keywords, while simultaneously tracking rating changes and review trends. For mobile app developers and marketers, maintaining strong search visibility is crucial for organic growth, but manual tracking is time-consuming and inconsistent.

The workflow solves this by automatically querying the Play Store via SerpApi (which handles the complex scraping), then processing the results to extract your current rankings. It can alert you when positions drop below thresholds or when ratings change significantly, allowing you to respond quickly to negative trends.

How It Works

1. Configure Target Keywords

The workflow begins with your list of important keywords that potential users might search for in the Play Store. These should include both branded terms and generic phrases relevant to your app's functionality.

2. Query Play Store via SerpApi

Using SerpApi's Google Play Search API, the workflow programmatically searches the Play Store for each keyword and extracts structured data about your app's position in the results.

3. Process Ranking Data

The raw API results are parsed to determine your exact ranking position for each keyword, along with competitor apps appearing in the same results.

4. Check Rating Updates

Simultaneously, the workflow checks your app's current average rating and total review count, tracking changes over time.

5. Send Alerts & Log Data

If rankings drop below configured thresholds or ratings change significantly, the workflow can trigger alerts via email, Slack, or other channels. All data is logged for historical analysis.

Pro tip: Combine this with a review monitoring workflow to get complete visibility into your app's Play Store performance. Tracking review sentiment alongside rankings helps identify the causes of position changes.
