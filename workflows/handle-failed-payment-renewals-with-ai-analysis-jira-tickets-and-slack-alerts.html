What This Workflow Does

Failed subscription renewals are a silent revenue killer for SaaS businesses and subscription-based companies. When a customer's payment method declines, it often goes unnoticed until the customer churns or complains. This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between your payment provider, AI analysis, and your finance team.

The workflow automatically processes failed payment webhooks, validates critical data, uses AI to assess urgency and churn risk, creates prioritized Jira tickets for your finance team, and sends immediate Slack alerts. For incomplete data, it routes to error handling with detailed diagnostics—ensuring nothing slips through the cracks. This transforms what was traditionally a manual, reactive process into a proactive revenue recovery system.

How It Works

Step 1: Webhook Reception & Validation

Your payment provider (Stripe, Chargebee, PayPal, etc.) sends a webhook when a subscription renewal fails. The workflow immediately validates four critical fields: customer ID, email, subscription ID, and amount. Missing data triggers an error alert instead of creating incomplete tickets.

Step 2: AI Analysis & Risk Assessment

OpenAI analyzes the failure reason from the webhook data, determines urgency level (low/medium/high), calculates a churn risk score based on customer history and payment patterns, and drafts a personalized recovery email template for the finance team to use.

Step 3: Priority Routing & Jira Creation

A switch node routes high-value failures (>$500) to 'High' priority tickets. The system then creates a comprehensive Jira Finance Task with all relevant data, AI insights, and the drafted recovery email—giving your team everything they need in one place.

Step 4: Team Notification & Follow-up

Simultaneously, a detailed Slack message alerts the finance channel with customer details, amount, churn risk score, and a direct link to the Jira ticket. This ensures immediate team awareness and enables quick collaboration on recovery strategies.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. OpenAI API key or compatible LLM credentials
  3. Jira Software account with permissions to create tasks in your Finance project
  4. Slack bot token with posting rights to your finance/alert channel
  5. Payment provider that supports POST JSON webhooks (Stripe, Chargebee, Recurly, etc.)
  6. Webhook endpoint configured in your payment provider pointing to your n8n instance

Pro tip: Start with a test webhook payload before connecting to live payment data. Use the sample JSON structure provided in the workflow comments to validate everything works correctly.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in under 30 minutes:

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure credentials: Add your Jira, Slack, and OpenAI credentials to the respective nodes.
  3. Set up webhook: In your payment provider, create a webhook pointing to https://your-n8n-url/webhook/payment-failed-renewal with POST method.
  4. Test with sample data: Use the test button in n8n with the provided sample payload to verify all connections work.
  5. Activate workflow: Turn the workflow ON and monitor the first few executions to ensure proper routing.
