What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming process of creating consistent, high-quality LinkedIn content. Instead of manually writing posts, designing images, and scheduling publications, this workflow combines AI generation with human oversight to produce professional content at scale.

The system starts with an AI agent that drafts LinkedIn posts based on your topics or prompts. It then generates instructions for image customization, which ImageKit processes to add text overlays or design elements. After human approval (or revision requests), the final content publishes directly to your LinkedIn profile.

This approach maintains brand voice and quality control while automating 80% of the content creation process. It's ideal for businesses, marketers, and thought leaders who need regular LinkedIn presence but lack the time for daily content creation.

How It Works

1. AI Content Generation

The workflow begins with a Mistral AI agent (or alternative AI model) that receives a content prompt or topic. The AI generates a complete LinkedIn post draft, including hashtags, tone adjustments, and image customization instructions.

2. Image Processing & Storage

ImageKit API processes the base image according to AI-generated instructions—adding text overlays, adjusting colors, or applying branding elements. The finished image uploads to AWS S3 for reliable, publicly accessible storage.

3. Human Approval Loop

An email notification sends the draft post and image to designated reviewers. They can approve immediately, request revisions, or reject entirely. If revisions are needed, the workflow loops back to the AI for adjustments.

4. LinkedIn Publishing

Once approved, the workflow publishes the complete post (text + image) directly to your LinkedIn profile via LinkedIn API. It handles scheduling, formatting, and any platform-specific requirements automatically.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. Mistral AI API key (or alternative like OpenAI)
  3. ImageKit.io account with API access
  4. AWS S3 bucket configured for public access
  5. LinkedIn Developer App with posting permissions
  6. Email service configured in n8n for approval notifications

Pro tip: Start with a small set of base images and let the AI suggest variations. This creates visual consistency while allowing creative flexibility.
