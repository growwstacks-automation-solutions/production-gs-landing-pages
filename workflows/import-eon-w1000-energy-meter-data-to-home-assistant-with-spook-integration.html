Import E.ON W1000 Energy Meter Data to Home Assistant

Automatically process energy consumption data from E.ON smart meters and integrate it with Home Assistant using n8n and Spook integration for comprehensive smart home energy management.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common challenge of manually tracking energy consumption from E.ON W1000 smart meters. Homeowners and businesses with solar panels or energy monitoring needs often receive daily email exports from their energy provider but lack an automated way to integrate this data into their smart home systems.

The workflow automatically processes emails from the E.ON portal containing 15-minute import/export data and daily meter readings. It extracts the relevant information from attached Excel files, transforms the data into the proper format, and updates your Home Assistant installation via the Spook integration. This creates a seamless bridge between your energy provider's data and your smart home dashboard.

By automating this process, you eliminate manual data entry, ensure accurate real-time energy tracking, and gain valuable insights into your consumption patterns without any daily effort.

How It Works

Step 1: Email Trigger & Attachment Processing

The workflow monitors your email inbox for messages from E.ON with specific subject patterns. When a new energy report email arrives, it automatically downloads the attached Excel file containing your energy data.

Step 2: Data Extraction & Transformation

The Excel file is parsed to extract key columns including 15-minute +A (import) and -A (export) data, along with daily 1.8.0 and 2.8.0 meter readings. The data is cleaned, formatted, and grouped by hour for proper integration.

Step 3: Home Assistant Integration via Spook

Using the Spook integration, the processed energy data is sent to Home Assistant where it updates sensor entities for grid energy import and export. This creates accurate, timestamped energy statistics in your Home Assistant recorder.

Step 4: Meter Reading Updates

The workflow also updates input_number entities in Home Assistant with the current meter readings, ensuring your energy dashboard always displays the most recent consumption data from your E.ON meter.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with access to email and webhook capabilities
  2. Home Assistant installation with Spook integration configured
  3. E.ON W1000 smart meter with scheduled email exports enabled
  4. Email account credentials for receiving E.ON energy reports
  5. Home Assistant Long-Lived Access Token for API authentication
  6. Basic understanding of Home Assistant entity configuration

Pro tip: Set your E.ON scheduled export to include 7 days of historical data when first configuring this workflow. This allows the system to backfill any missed data and ensures your Home Assistant energy history is complete from day one.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure the email trigger node with your E.ON report email credentials
  3. Set up the Home Assistant node with your Long-Lived Access Token
  4. Create the required input_number and template_sensor entities in Home Assistant
  5. Test the workflow with a recent E.ON energy report email
  6. Activate the workflow and verify data appears in Home Assistant
