What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the critical challenge of influencer vetting in modern digital marketing. Brands risk reputation damage and wasted budgets when partnering with influencers who have fake followers, controversial content, or poor engagement. Manual vetting is time-consuming and inconsistent across team members.

The workflow automatically analyzes Instagram influencers across multiple brand safety and performance dimensions. It checks for red flags in content history, calculates authentic engagement rates, assesses audience quality, and compiles everything into standardized Slack reports for your team to review. This transforms a typically subjective process into a data-driven decision framework.

Example of automated influencer audit report delivered to Slack

How It Works

1. Influencer Profile Analysis

The workflow begins by scraping publicly available Instagram profile data, including post history, follower count, and engagement metrics. Advanced algorithms analyze content for potential brand safety issues like controversial topics or inappropriate imagery.

2. Engagement Quality Assessment

Beyond basic metrics, the system evaluates engagement authenticity by analyzing comment patterns, follower growth history, and engagement rate consistency. This helps identify purchased followers or inauthentic interactions that inflate metrics.

3. Scoring & Risk Flagging

Each influencer receives a composite safety score based on customizable weightings for different risk factors. The workflow flags high-risk profiles and highlights top candidates based on your predefined criteria.

4. Slack Report Generation

Comprehensive reports are automatically formatted and delivered to designated Slack channels. Reports include key metrics, risk assessments, and direct links to influencer profiles for quick team review.

Pro tip: Customize the scoring thresholds to match your brand's specific risk tolerance. Luxury brands might weight content quality higher, while youth-focused brands may prioritize engagement rates.
