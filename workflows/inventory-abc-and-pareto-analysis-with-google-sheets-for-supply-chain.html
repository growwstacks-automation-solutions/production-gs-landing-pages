What This Workflow Does

Manual inventory classification is a time-consuming, error-prone task that leaves businesses with suboptimal stock levels. This automation solves that by connecting directly to your Google Sheets sales data to perform professional ABC and Pareto analysis automatically.

The workflow aggregates daily sales transactions, calculates each product's contribution to total revenue, and classifies items into A, B, and C categories based on the Pareto principle. It also computes demand variability metrics (mean, standard deviation, coefficient of variation) to support XYZ analysis, giving you a complete picture of both value and predictability.

Instead of spending hours each month manually sorting spreadsheets, you get automatically updated classifications that help you prioritize purchasing decisions, optimize warehouse space, and reduce capital tied up in slow-moving inventory.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to transform raw sales data into actionable inventory insights.

Step 1: Data Extraction

The workflow connects to your Google Sheets document where daily sales transactions are stored. It reads records containing date, item SKU, quantity sold, and turnover value, ensuring all relevant historical data is captured for analysis.

Step 2: Data Cleansing & Aggregation

Zero-sale items are filtered out, then sales are aggregated by store, item, and day. This consolidation creates a clean dataset showing total sales and turnover per product across your selected time period.

Step 3: Pareto Analysis

Items are sorted by descending turnover value, and cumulative contribution percentages are calculated. The system identifies which products constitute the top 20% generating approximately 80% of revenue—your critical A-items.

Step 4: ABC Classification

Based on configurable thresholds (typically A=top 80% cumulative value, B=next 15%, C=remaining 5%), each product receives its ABC classification. This categorization directly informs stocking policies and reorder frequency.

Step 5: Demand Variability Calculation

For each item, the workflow calculates mean sales, standard deviation, and coefficient of variation (CV). These metrics help identify products with stable versus unpredictable demand patterns, supporting XYZ classification.

Step 6: Results Export

Final classifications and metrics are appended to dedicated output sheets—ABC XYZ for combined analysis, Pareto for revenue contribution insights, and Store Sales for location-specific performance—creating ready-to-use reports for your team.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about inventory automation and analysis

ABC analysis is an inventory categorization method that classifies items into three groups based on their consumption value or sales contribution. A-items are the most valuable (typically the top 20% generating approximately 80% of revenue), B-items are moderately valuable, and C-items are the least valuable but often highest in quantity.

This classification helps businesses prioritize stock management, reorder policies, and storage strategies. High-value A-items receive frequent monitoring and higher safety stock, while low-value C-items can be managed with simpler, less frequent reviews.

Pareto analysis applies the 80/20 principle to inventory, identifying that roughly 80% of your sales come from 20% of your products. By automating this analysis, you can continuously monitor which items drive revenue and adjust your inventory strategy accordingly.

This helps reduce overstock on low-performing items, allocate warehouse space more efficiently to high-turnover products, and ensure purchasing budgets focus on revenue-generating inventory. The analysis also reveals changing patterns over time as product popularity shifts.

Manual inventory analysis in spreadsheets is time-consuming, error-prone, and quickly becomes outdated. Every month or quarter, someone must extract data, apply formulas, sort results, and update classifications—a process that can take hours and introduces calculation errors.

Automation ensures real-time classification as new sales data arrives, eliminates human calculation errors, and frees up supply chain analysts to focus on strategic decisions rather than data crunching. It also enables consistent reporting across multiple locations or product categories.

Connecting Google Sheets provides a familiar, collaborative interface for data entry while leveraging automation for complex analysis. Teams can continue using their existing sales tracking sheets while the workflow automatically processes, classifies, and generates insights.

This bridges the gap between operational data collection and analytical decision-making without requiring expensive ERP systems. It's particularly valuable for businesses transitioning from manual processes to automated systems, as it doesn't disrupt existing workflows.

While ABC classifies by value, XYZ analysis categorizes items by demand predictability. X-items have stable, predictable demand; Y-items have moderate variability; Z-items have highly irregular demand. Combining both analyses gives a 9-box matrix that informs both inventory value AND replenishment strategy.

For example, an AX item (high value, predictable) might have minimal safety stock, while a CZ item (low value, unpredictable) might be ordered only when needed. This combined approach helps optimize safety stock levels and reorder points more precisely than either analysis alone.
