What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common but time-consuming business problem: combining data from multiple sources into a single, unified dataset. Whether you're merging customer lists from different CRMs, consolidating sales data from multiple platforms, or combining inventory records from various warehouses, manual merging is error-prone and inefficient.

The workflow automatically joins two datasets based on matching rules you define—similar to SQL JOIN operations but without writing code. It can append new records, keep only existing matches, or create comprehensive merged datasets that give you a complete picture of your business information.

By automating this process, you eliminate hours of weekly spreadsheet work, reduce human error by 90%+, and ensure everyone in your organization works from the same accurate, up-to-date information.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence to fetch, process, and merge data from different sources:

Step 1: Data Collection

The automation connects to your data sources—this could be Google Sheets, Airtable, databases (MySQL, PostgreSQL), CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot), or any other app with an API. It retrieves the datasets you want to merge on a schedule or trigger.

Step 2: Data Normalization

Before merging, the workflow cleans and standardizes your data. This includes formatting dates consistently, standardizing text cases, removing extra spaces, and converting data types so fields match properly across datasets.

Step 3: Rule-Based Matching

You define matching rules: exact matches on unique identifiers (like email or customer ID), fuzzy matching on names or addresses, or complex conditional logic. The workflow compares records across datasets and identifies which ones should be merged based on your criteria.

Step 4: Merge Execution

The actual merge happens here. You can choose to: append all records, keep only matching records, keep only non-matching records, or create a comprehensive merge that combines matching fields while preserving all data.

Step 5: Output & Delivery

The merged dataset is sent to your destination of choice—another Google Sheet, database table, CRM, or business intelligence tool. You can also set up notifications to alert your team when merges complete or when conflicts need manual resolution.
