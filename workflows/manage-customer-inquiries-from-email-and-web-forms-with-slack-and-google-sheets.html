What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the common challenge of customer inquiries getting lost across different channels. When businesses receive questions via email, contact forms, and other sources, it's easy for messages to slip through the cracks or get delayed in siloed inboxes. The workflow automatically captures inquiries from any source, creates a centralized record in Google Sheets, and notifies the appropriate team members via Slack.

By connecting these systems, you eliminate manual data entry while ensuring every inquiry gets logged and tracked to completion. The workflow maintains a complete audit trail of when inquiries were received, who handled them, and how long resolution took - valuable data for improving customer service metrics.

How It Works

1. Capture inquiries from multiple sources

The workflow monitors your designated email inboxes and web form submissions for new customer inquiries. It can handle various formats including contact form submissions, support emails, and even social media messages if configured.

2. Standardize and enrich inquiry data

Incoming inquiries are processed to extract key information like customer name, contact details, inquiry type, and urgency level. The workflow can automatically categorize inquiries based on keywords or other criteria you define.

3. Log details in Google Sheets

Each inquiry creates a new row in your Google Sheet tracker with all relevant details plus timestamps. The sheet serves as your master record for all customer inquiries, with columns for status, assigned team member, and resolution notes.

4. Notify team via Slack

The workflow posts each new inquiry to a designated Slack channel with @mentions for the appropriate team members. The Slack message includes key details and quick action buttons to update status without leaving Slack.

5. Track through resolution

As team members update the inquiry status in either Slack or Google Sheets, the workflow keeps both systems in sync. This creates a closed-loop process where nothing gets lost and customers receive timely responses.

Pro tip: Add a "priority" field to your web form and set up different Slack notification rules for high-priority inquiries to ensure urgent requests get immediate attention.
