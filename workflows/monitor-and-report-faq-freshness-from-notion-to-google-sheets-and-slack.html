What This Workflow Does

Stale FAQs create customer confusion and increase support tickets. This automation solves that by monitoring your Notion knowledge base for outdated content, creating an audit trail in Google Sheets, and alerting your team via Slack when updates are needed.

The workflow automatically tracks last-edited dates, identifies FAQs approaching their review deadlines, and generates reports showing which content needs attention. This replaces manual checking processes that are time-consuming and often inconsistent.

How It Works

1. Checks Notion FAQ database

The workflow regularly scans your Notion FAQ database, checking the 'Last Edited Time' property for each entry. It compares these dates against your content review policies you define.

2. Logs changes in Google Sheets

All detected updates get logged in a Google Sheet with timestamps, editor information, and change details. This creates a searchable history of all FAQ updates.

3. Sends Slack notifications

When content exceeds your freshness threshold (e.g., 90 days without updates), the workflow pings designated Slack channels with links to stale FAQs needing review.
