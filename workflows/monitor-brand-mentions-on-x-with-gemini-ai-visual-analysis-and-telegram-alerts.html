What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the challenge of comprehensive brand monitoring on X (formerly Twitter) by combining text analysis with cutting-edge visual recognition. Traditional brand monitoring tools often miss crucial context when users mention your brand alongside images or videos without direct text references.

The automation continuously scans X for brand mentions, analyzes both the text content and any attached images using Gemini AI's multimodal capabilities, scores the relevance of each mention, and sends filtered alerts to your Telegram channel. This gives you a complete picture of how your brand is being discussed across social media.

How It Works

1. X Mention Monitoring

The workflow starts by querying X's API for recent posts containing your brand keywords or hashtags. It captures the full post content, including any media attachments and engagement metrics.

2. Multimodal AI Analysis

Each post undergoes dual analysis: text content is processed for sentiment and context, while attached images are scanned by Gemini AI for brand logos, products, or relevant visual context that might not be mentioned in the text.

3. Relevance Scoring

The system combines text and visual analysis to generate a composite relevance score, filtering out casual mentions from substantive brand discussions that require your attention.

4. Telegram Alert Delivery

High-value mentions trigger instant alerts in your Telegram channel, including the original post content, analysis summary, and direct links for quick response.

Pro tip: Configure different Telegram channels for different alert levels - critical mentions can go to your executive team while general brand discussions go to marketing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about brand monitoring and AI-powered social listening

Visual brand monitoring is crucial because up to 40% of brand mentions on platforms like X happen without direct text references. Users often post images of products, logos, or storefronts without tagging or mentioning the brand name. Traditional text-based monitoring misses these valuable interactions.

For example, a customer might tweet a photo of your product with a complaint or compliment but not use your brand name. AI-powered visual analysis catches these posts, giving you opportunities to engage that you'd otherwise miss. This is especially important for brands with strong visual identities or physical products.

  • Identifies unofficial product usage
  • Catches brand appearances in memes
  • Tracks visual sentiment (positive/negative imagery)

AI transforms brand monitoring by understanding context rather than just matching keywords. Basic tracking alerts you every time your brand name appears, regardless of relevance. AI analyzes whether the mention is substantive, the sentiment, and whether it requires action.

A retail brand might get thousands of casual mentions daily ("Just walked past [brand] store"). AI identifies the 5-10% that matter - customer complaints, influencer endorsements, or competitor comparisons. This contextual understanding reduces alert fatigue while ensuring you don't miss important conversations.

  • Distinguishes between casual and substantive mentions
  • Identifies sentiment and urgency
  • Recognizes indirect references and synonyms

Gemini AI can analyze various visual content types including product photos, logos, packaging, storefronts, branded merchandise, and even screenshots of your website or app. The AI recognizes these elements even when partially obscured or from unusual angles.

For instance, a food brand would want to know when customers post photos of their meals at restaurants - even if the post just says "Dinner out." Gemini can identify the branded packaging or menu items. The AI also detects text within images, catching mentions that only appear in image captions or memes.

  • Works with photos, screenshots, and memes
  • Recognizes logos from different angles
  • Reads text embedded in images

Telegram is ideal for brand monitoring alerts because it supports rich media, group chats, and instant notifications. You can create dedicated channels for different alert types (urgent issues, positive mentions, competitor sightings) and customize who receives them.

A common setup includes a main brand monitoring channel for the marketing team, with critical alerts forwarded to a leadership group. Telegram's formatting preserves post links, images, and context. Many teams find it more actionable than email alerts that get buried in inboxes.

  • Supports images, links, and formatted text
  • Allows for team discussion threads
  • Provides instant mobile notifications

Beyond just counting mentions, brands should track sentiment trends, engagement rates, influencer impact, and issue frequency. The most valuable metrics show how mentions affect business outcomes rather than just volume.

A software company might track how feature complaints correlate with churn, while a retailer monitors how store location mentions drive foot traffic. Combine social data with CRM and sales metrics to measure real impact. Over time, you'll identify which types of mentions warrant immediate response.

  • Sentiment trends over time
  • Mention-to-engagement ratios
  • Conversion impact of responses

For most brands, monitoring should run continuously with checks every 15-30 minutes. High-profile brands or crisis situations may need real-time monitoring. The frequency depends on your response capabilities - there's no point in minute-by-minute alerts if your team can only respond hourly.

During product launches or PR crises, increase monitoring frequency. For general brand maintenance, 4-6 checks per day often suffices. Balance comprehensiveness with API rate limits and processing costs. This workflow lets you easily adjust frequency as needs change.

  • Standard: Every 15-30 minutes
  • High-priority: 1-5 minute intervals
  • Adjust based on team capacity
