What This Workflow Does

Manual competitor price tracking is time-consuming, error-prone, and reactive. This automation solves that by providing continuous, intelligent monitoring of competitor pricing with AI-powered analysis and smart alerting.

The workflow automatically collects competitor price data on a scheduled basis, compares it against historical pricing, detects meaningful changes, and uses AI to assess market impact. It then routes alerts intelligently—urgent changes to Slack for immediate attention, routine updates via email for review—while maintaining complete audit logs for trend analysis.

This transforms what was traditionally a manual spreadsheet exercise into a proactive competitive intelligence system that delivers actionable insights, not just raw data.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Data Collection

The workflow runs on a configurable schedule (default every 6 hours) to fetch current competitor pricing from your data sources while retrieving previous price snapshots from Google Sheets for comparison.

2. Data Normalization & Comparison

Current and historical data are normalized into a unified format. The Compare Datasets node identifies new products, removed products, price increases, decreases, and unchanged items—creating a complete change picture.

3. Change Evaluation & Classification

Percentage changes are calculated and classified by severity: urgent (≥10% change), routine (5–10% change), or no alert (<5%). This tiered approach ensures your team focuses on what matters most.

4. AI-Powered Strategic Analysis

An AI agent evaluates each significant change for competitive impact, generating concise recommendations: match the price, hold your position, or differentiate with value. It justifies urgency based on market implications.

5. Intelligent Alert Routing

Urgent alerts are sent to Slack channels for immediate team visibility. Routine updates are delivered via Gmail for scheduled review. All alerts and monitoring sessions are logged to Google Sheets for auditing and trend analysis.

6. Historical Data Maintenance

The workflow updates price history records, ensuring each monitoring cycle builds upon the last. This creates a valuable historical dataset for identifying long-term pricing trends and competitor strategies.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Configure credentials for Google Sheets, OpenAI, Slack, and Gmail in n8n
  3. Set up your Google Sheets with three tabs: Price History, Alert Logs, and Monitoring Logs
  4. Replace the sample API endpoint with your actual competitor price data source
  5. Adjust alert thresholds in the Filter nodes to match your business rules
  6. Test with a manual trigger to verify data flow and alert delivery
  7. Activate the schedule trigger for automated monitoring at your preferred frequency
