What This Workflow Does

Running out of critical construction materials can bring projects to a halt, costing thousands in delays and emergency orders. This automation solves that by turning your Google Sheet into a smart inventory manager. It automatically monitors stock levels for items like cement, lumber, fixtures, or office supplies, and sends instant email alerts when supplies dip below your set thresholds.

The workflow eliminates manual spreadsheet checking and guesswork. It runs on a schedule you define (daily, hourly, etc.), fetches current stock data from your Google Sheet, processes any additions or deductions, identifies items needing reorder, and notifies your team via email before you run out. This ensures materials are always available when needed, without overstocking capital.

Beyond basic alerts, the system maintains a clean audit trail with timestamps, helping with procurement planning and budget forecasting. It's particularly valuable for construction firms, property managers, facility maintenance teams, and any business managing physical inventory across multiple sites or projects.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical pipeline that mimics how a diligent warehouse manager would work, but automatically and without human error.

1. Scheduled Trigger & Data Fetch

The workflow starts on a schedule (like every morning at 8 AM). It connects to your designated Google Sheet—your single source of truth for inventory—and retrieves all current stock data, including item names, quantities on hand, and your predefined minimum thresholds.

2. Stock Level Analysis

Each item's current quantity is compared against its minimum threshold. The system calculates which items are below the "reorder point." This logic can be customized; for example, you might want alerts at 20% stock remaining, or only for specific high-priority materials.

3. Alert Generation & Delivery

For items identified as low, the workflow composes a clear, actionable email. This email includes the item name, current quantity, threshold, and often a direct link to your procurement sheet or ordering system. It's sent to your designated team members (project managers, procurement officers, site supervisors).

4. Data Logging & Update

After checking, the workflow can optionally update the "last checked" timestamp in your sheet. This creates an audit trail, proving the system is running and providing data for weekly or monthly inventory health reports.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google Sheet with your inventory data. Columns should include at least: Item Name, Current Quantity, Minimum Threshold (reorder point), and optionally Unit and Last Updated date.
  2. Google Sheets API access configured in n8n (uses OAuth2—follow our setup guide).
  3. An email service (SMTP) account for sending alerts. This can be Gmail, Outlook, SendGrid, or your company's email server.
  4. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) where you'll import this JSON workflow template.
  5. Basic familiarity with navigating n8n's interface to paste credentials and adjust schedule settings.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Download the template using the button above. This gives you a JSON file.
  2. Import into n8n: In your n8n dashboard, go to Workflows > Import from File and select the downloaded JSON.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: Click the Google Sheets node, add a new credential (using OAuth2), and select your inventory spreadsheet. Map the sheet name and range.
  4. Configure the Email Node: Enter your SMTP details (host, port, user, password) and set the "To" email address for alerts.
  5. Set the Schedule: In the Schedule Trigger node, define how often to check stock (e.g., "Every day at 9 AM").
  6. Activate and Test: Toggle the workflow ON. Manually reduce a quantity in your sheet below its threshold and verify you receive the alert email.
