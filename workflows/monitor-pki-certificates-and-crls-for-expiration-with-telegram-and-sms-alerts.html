What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow automates the monitoring of Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) certificates and Certificate Revocation Lists (CRLs) for impending expiration. It eliminates the risk of service disruptions caused by expired certificates by sending proactive alerts via Telegram and SMS before critical deadlines.

The system checks certificate validity periods at configurable intervals, then triggers multi-stage notifications starting 30 days before expiration. This gives IT teams ample time to renew certificates while maintaining continuous security and compliance. The workflow centralizes monitoring for all certificates across your infrastructure.

How It Works

1. Certificate Inventory Setup

The workflow begins with a configured list of certificates to monitor, including their locations and domains. You can specify different alert thresholds for various certificate types based on their criticality.

2. Scheduled Expiration Checks

On a daily or weekly schedule (configurable), the workflow checks each certificate's expiration date against the current date. It calculates the remaining validity period and flags certificates approaching their expiration thresholds.

3. Multi-Stage Alert System

When a certificate enters a warning period (typically 30, 15, and 7 days before expiration), the workflow sends progressively urgent alerts. Each notification includes certificate details, expiration date, and renewal instructions.

4. Redundant Notification Channels

Alerts are sent simultaneously via Telegram (for rich formatting and documentation) and SMS (for guaranteed delivery). The workflow can be extended to include email, Slack, or other channels as needed.

5. Audit Logging

All monitoring activities and alerts are logged with timestamps, creating an audit trail for compliance purposes. The logs record which certificates were checked, their status, and when alerts were sent.
