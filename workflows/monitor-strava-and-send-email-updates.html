What This Workflow Does

Staying consistent with fitness goals is challenging, especially when you're only accountable to yourself. This automation solves that problem by creating an external accountability system that works automatically. It connects your Strava account to your email, monitoring your daily or weekly activity against predefined goals.

When you don't meet your targets—whether it's running distance, cycling time, or overall active minutes—the system immediately notifies your chosen accountability partners. This creates a powerful psychological incentive: you know that skipping a workout means explaining it to people who care about your progress. The automation handles the awkwardness of asking for check-ins and ensures consistent, data-driven feedback without manual effort from you or your supporters.

Beyond personal fitness, this pattern is valuable for coaches managing client progress, wellness programs in organizations, or anyone building habit-tracking systems. It transforms passive data collection into active intervention, turning fitness metrics into meaningful conversations and support.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence that checks, evaluates, and communicates—all without manual intervention.

1. Strava Activity Monitoring

The workflow connects to your Strava account via API and retrieves your latest activities. It can check for specific types of exercise (running, cycling, swimming), duration, distance, or frequency based on your configured parameters. The system runs on a schedule you define—daily, weekly, or at specific times that make sense for your routine.

2. Goal Evaluation & Conditional Logic

Your predefined fitness targets are compared against actual Strava data. The "If" node determines whether you've met your minimum requirements. You can set multiple conditions: "If running distance < 5km" or "If total weekly activity < 150 minutes." The logic is completely customizable to match your specific fitness program and goals.

3. Automated Notification System

When conditions aren't met, the workflow triggers email notifications to your designated accountability partners. The emails can include specific details about what was missed, your historical performance for context, and even encouraging messages or suggestions. The system maintains privacy by only sharing what you configure—never more than you're comfortable with.

4. Feedback Loop & Adjustment

The automation includes logging so you can review when notifications were sent and why. This creates a feedback loop where you can adjust goals as you progress or modify notification rules based on what's most effective for your motivation style.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance – either self-hosted or n8n.cloud account
  2. Strava account with API access (free developer credentials)
  3. Email service credentials (SMTP settings for Gmail, Outlook, or your email provider)
  4. Clear fitness goals – specific, measurable targets for activity type, duration, or frequency
  5. Accountability partners' email addresses – people who have agreed to support your fitness journey

Pro tip: Start with conservative goals that are easy to achieve, then gradually increase them. This builds success momentum and makes the accountability system encouraging rather than punishing.

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this workflow in under 10 minutes with these steps:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance
  2. Connect your Strava account by creating an API application at Strava.com and adding your client ID/secret to n8n
  3. Configure your email credentials in the Send Email node (SMTP settings for your provider)
  4. Set your fitness thresholds in the "If" node conditions (distance, time, frequency)
  5. Add accountability partner emails in the email node recipient fields
  6. Test the workflow with manual trigger to ensure emails send correctly
  7. Activate the schedule to run daily or weekly based on your preference
