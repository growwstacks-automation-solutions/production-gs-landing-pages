What This Workflow Does

Nathan is a conceptual framework for creating a programmable personal assistant that handles day-to-day administrative and communication tasks. Instead of manually managing emails, scheduling, and information retrieval, this n8n workflow provides the foundation for an AI-powered helper that works in the background.

The template demonstrates how to connect email triggers with decision logic, AI processing, and action nodes to create an automated response system. It solves the problem of administrative overload by providing a scalable way to filter important communications, generate responses, and execute routine tasks without constant human intervention.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence that mimics how a human assistant would process requests and take appropriate actions.

Step 1: Email Monitoring & Trigger

The workflow begins by monitoring your email inbox (via IMAP) for new messages. It filters incoming emails based on sender, subject keywords, or priority markers to identify which communications require assistant intervention versus those that need your direct attention.

Step 2: Content Analysis & Classification

Email content is analyzed using natural language processing or pattern matching to determine intent. The system classifies messages into categories like "meeting request," "information inquiry," "task assignment," or "urgent matter" to route them appropriately.

Step 3: Decision Logic & Response Generation

Based on the classification, the workflow applies predefined decision rules. For simple inquiries, it can generate automated responses using templates. For meeting requests, it can check your calendar availability. Complex matters are flagged for human review with summarized context.

Step 4: Action Execution & Follow-up

The assistant executes appropriate actions: sending responses, creating calendar events, logging tasks in project management tools, or forwarding information to relevant team members. It can also schedule follow-up reminders and track conversation threads.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template file using the button above
  2. Import it into your n8n instance via the "Workflows" → "Import from file" option
  3. Configure the Email Trigger (IMAP) node with your email server credentials
  4. Adjust the filter conditions in the Switch node to match your priority senders or keywords
  5. Set up the Send Email node with your SMTP details for automated responses
  6. Test with a controlled email to yourself before enabling full automation
  7. Monitor the execution logs for the first few days to fine-tune the logic
