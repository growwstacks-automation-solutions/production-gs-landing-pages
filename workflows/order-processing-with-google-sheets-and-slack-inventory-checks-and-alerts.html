What This Workflow Does

Manual order processing creates costly bottlenecks, inventory errors, and customer dissatisfaction. This automation transforms your order management system into a seamless, error-proof operation that automatically validates incoming orders, checks real-time inventory levels, and alerts your team instantly about stock issues.

The workflow acts as a central nervous system for your fulfillment operations. When an order arrives via webhook or API, it automatically validates customer details, checks product availability against your Google Sheets inventory database, processes successful orders, and sends immediate Slack notifications for any stock shortages—all without human intervention.

This eliminates the financial damage of overselling, reduces order processing time by 70%, and ensures your team always has accurate, real-time visibility into inventory status. It's particularly valuable for growing businesses experiencing scaling pains with manual processes.

How It Works

1. Order Receipt & Validation

The workflow begins when an order arrives from your e-commerce platform, website form, or custom application via webhook. It automatically validates the order structure, checks for complete customer information, and ensures all required product data is present before proceeding to inventory checks.

2. Real-Time Inventory Verification

The system queries your Google Sheets inventory database in real-time, comparing ordered quantities against available stock levels. It applies business rules you define (like minimum stock thresholds) and determines whether items are available, low stock, or out of stock.

3. Conditional Processing & Notifications

Based on inventory availability, the workflow branches automatically. For in-stock items, it proceeds with order confirmation and logging. For low-stock or out-of-stock situations, it immediately sends detailed Slack alerts to your warehouse or purchasing team with specific product SKUs and quantities needed.

4. Automated Logging & Tracking

Every order attempt—whether successful or flagged for inventory issues—gets logged to a dedicated Google Sheet with timestamps, customer details, inventory status, and resolution notes. This creates an audit trail for performance analysis and helps identify recurring stock issues.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with available workflow execution capacity
  2. Google Sheets with inventory data including SKUs, quantities, and reorder thresholds
  3. Slack workspace with appropriate channel permissions for sending alerts
  4. Order source capable of sending webhook/API calls (e-commerce platform, form tool, or custom application)
  5. Basic understanding of your current order and inventory processes to configure thresholds

Pro tip: Before implementing, conduct a one-week audit of your current order processing to identify the most common failure points (wrong inventory counts, delayed alerts, etc.). This helps you configure the automation to solve your specific pain points.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your business:

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file
  2. Configure Google Sheets connection by authorizing n8n access to your inventory spreadsheet
  3. Set up Slack integration by creating a Slack app and connecting it to your alert channel
  4. Customize inventory thresholds in the "If" nodes to match your business rules
  5. Test with sample orders to ensure alerts trigger correctly and logs populate accurately
  6. Connect your order source by configuring the webhook URL in your e-commerce platform or application
  7. Run parallel testing for one week alongside your existing process before full switchover
