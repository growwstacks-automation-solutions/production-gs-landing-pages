What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of email attachments cluttering your inbox and becoming difficult to find later. It automatically monitors your Gmail account for incoming emails with attachments, extracts those files, and organizes them into Google Drive folders named after each sender.

The system creates a logical folder structure that makes it easy to locate any document you've received. Instead of searching through emails or a disorganized Downloads folder, you'll have all attachments neatly filed by sender in Google Drive, where they're accessible to your team and protected by Google's security.

How It Works

1. Trigger on new Gmail emails

The workflow starts when a new email arrives in your Gmail inbox. You can configure it to watch specific labels or apply filters to only process emails that meet certain criteria.

2. Check for attachments

The automation examines each incoming email to determine if it contains attachments. If no attachments are found, the email is ignored to save processing time.

3. Extract sender information

The system identifies the sender's email address and name, which will be used to create or identify the appropriate destination folder in Google Drive.

4. Create or locate sender folder

In your Google Drive, the workflow checks if a folder exists for this sender. If not, it creates one using the sender's name or email address as the folder name.

5. Save attachments to Drive

Each attachment is downloaded from Gmail and uploaded to the sender's folder in Google Drive. The original filename is preserved by default, but you can customize naming conventions.
