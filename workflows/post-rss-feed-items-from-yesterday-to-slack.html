What This Workflow Does

Manually checking multiple websites, blogs, or news sources for updates is a repetitive and time-consuming task that often leads to missed information. This automation solves that by creating a hands-free daily digest system.

The workflow connects to any RSS or Atom feed, fetches all items published "yesterday" (based on publication dates), and posts them directly to a designated Slack channel. It acts as your automated news curator, ensuring your team stays updated on industry news, competitor moves, software updates, or any content published via RSS—without anyone having to leave Slack or set calendar reminders to check websites.

By centralizing updates in a communication tool your team already uses, you reduce context switching, improve information retention, and create a searchable archive of relevant content right within your team's workflow.

How It Works

The automation runs on a schedule you set (e.g., every morning at 9 AM) and follows a clear, logical process.

Step 1: Schedule Trigger & RSS Fetch

A Schedule Trigger node initiates the workflow daily. The RSS Feed Read node then connects to your specified feed URL and retrieves all available items.

Step 2: Filter for Yesterday's Items

A Function node processes the feed items, comparing their publication dates to "yesterday" (in your timezone). It filters out any items not published within that 24-hour window, ensuring you only get fresh, relevant updates.

Step 3: Format & Post to Slack

Each filtered item is formatted into a clean, readable Slack message block. The Slack node authenticates with your workspace and posts the formatted digest to your chosen channel, tagging relevant team members if configured.

What You'll Need

  1. An active n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted).
  2. A Slack workspace where you have permission to create an app and post to channels.
  3. The URL of the RSS feed you want to monitor.
  4. (Optional) Basic familiarity with n8n's interface to paste credentials.

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this workflow in under 10 minutes.

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above and import the JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Slack: Create a Slack app in your workspace, grant it chat:write permissions, and add the Bot User OAuth Token to the Slack node in the workflow.
  3. Set Your Feed: In the RSS Feed Read node, paste the URL of the feed you want to monitor.
  4. Choose Channel: In the Slack node, select the channel where updates should be posted.
  5. Activate & Test: Turn the workflow ON and run it once manually to see a test post in your Slack channel.

Pro tip: Use the Function node to customize the message format. You can add emojis, change the layout, or even pull in extra data before posting to Slack.
