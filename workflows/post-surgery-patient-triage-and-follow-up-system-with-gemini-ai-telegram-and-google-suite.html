What This Workflow Does

Manual post-surgery follow-up is time-consuming, inconsistent, and risks missing critical patient complications. Clinics often struggle to maintain daily check-ins with every patient while prioritizing urgent cases.

This automation solves that by creating a structured, AI-powered triage system. It automatically sends scheduled check-in messages via Telegram to all patients within their recovery window. When patients reply about their symptoms, the workflow captures their response, cleans the data, uses Gemini AI to analyze the content, and classifies it into low, moderate, or high intensity. Based on this classification, it triggers appropriate actions: supportive messages for low concern, scheduled follow-up appointments for moderate issues, or immediate email alerts to doctors for high-priority cases.

How It Works

Step 1: Scheduled Patient Check-ins

The workflow reads from a Google Sheet containing patient details (name, surgery type, follow-up duration, doctor email). A schedule trigger sends personalized Telegram messages to each patient during their recovery period, asking about their symptoms and recovery progress.

Step 2: Patient Response Capture & Processing

When a patient replies via Telegram, the message is captured by a Telegram Trigger node. The workflow then cleans and structures the response, removing irrelevant information and preparing it for AI analysis.

Step 3: AI-Powered Symptom Triage

The structured patient response is sent to Google's Gemini AI model. Custom prompts analyze the content against medical guidelines to determine severity. The AI classifies each case as low intensity (routine recovery), moderate intensity (needs follow-up), or high intensity (requires immediate medical attention).

Step 4: Automated Action Based on Severity

A Switch node routes cases based on the AI classification. Low-intensity cases receive automated supportive responses. Moderate cases trigger Google Calendar event creation for follow-up appointments. High-intensity cases immediately send alert emails to the assigned doctor via Gmail with all relevant patient details.

Pro tip: Customize the AI prompts to match your specific surgical specialties. The classification thresholds for "moderate" vs. "high" intensity should reflect your clinic's specific protocols and risk factors.

Who This Is For

This template is ideal for surgical clinics, hospitals with outpatient procedures, telemedicine providers specializing in post-operative care, and healthcare organizations managing chronic condition follow-ups. It's particularly valuable for practices performing high volumes of procedures (like cataract surgeries, orthopedic procedures, or cosmetic surgeries) where consistent follow-up is crucial but manually intensive.

What You'll Need

  1. Google Workspace Account with OAuth2 credentials for Sheets, Calendar, and Gmail
  2. Telegram Bot Token created via @BotFather on Telegram
  3. Google Gemini API Key from Google AI Studio
  4. n8n Instance (cloud or self-hosted) with credential access
  5. Google Sheet with patient columns: Name, Surgery Type, Follow-up Duration (days), Doctor Email, Telegram Chat ID

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file.
  2. Configure credentials for Google Sheets, Gmail, Google Calendar, Telegram Bot, and Gemini AI in n8n's credential management.
  3. Update the Google Sheet ID in the Google Sheets node to point to your patient database.
  4. Test the Telegram integration by sending a message to your bot and verifying the workflow triggers correctly.
  5. Activate the Schedule Trigger to begin automated daily check-ins for patients in their follow-up window.
  6. Monitor initial runs and adjust AI prompt thresholds based on your clinical judgment of the classifications.
