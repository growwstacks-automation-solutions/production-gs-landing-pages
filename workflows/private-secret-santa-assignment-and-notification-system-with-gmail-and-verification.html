Automated Secret Santa Assignment & Notification with Gmail

Fully automate your gift exchange with private pairings, automatic emails, and mathematical fairness verification—all while keeping the organizer completely blind.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

Organizing a Secret Santa gift exchange manually is time-consuming, prone to errors, and often compromises privacy. This automation solves all three problems by completely handling the entire process from participant list to verified completion.

The workflow takes a simple spreadsheet of names and email addresses, then uses a derangement algorithm to create perfect random pairings where no one gets themselves. It then sends beautifully formatted, personalized emails to each participant revealing only their assigned person. Crucially, after sending each email, it automatically deletes the message from the "Sent" folder, ensuring the organizer cannot see or trace who was assigned to whom.

Finally, the system generates a numerical verification summary (like "1 sent to 2, 2 sent to 3") that proves the exchange was mathematically fair without revealing any identities. This combination of automation, privacy, and verification transforms what's typically an administrative headache into a seamless, magical experience for everyone involved.

How It Works

1. Participant Data Input

The workflow begins by accepting a list of participants with their names and email addresses. This can come from a Google Sheet, Airtable base, or simple manual input. The system validates the data to ensure no duplicates and proper email formatting.

2. Derangement Algorithm Assignment

Using a mathematical derangement algorithm, the workflow creates random pairings with a critical guarantee: no participant is ever assigned to themselves. This is the mathematical foundation that ensures true "Secret" Santa where everyone gives to someone else.

3. Private Email Generation & Dispatch

For each pairing, the workflow generates a personalized email using Gmail. The email includes the recipient's name, gift-giving instructions, budget details, and deadline—all formatted professionally. Each email is sent directly to the participant.

4. Sent Message Deletion for Privacy

Immediately after sending each email, the workflow deletes the message from the organizer's Gmail "Sent" folder. This is the privacy mechanism that keeps the organizer completely blind to the final pairings while maintaining a verifiable audit trail in the workflow itself.

5. Anonymous Verification Output

The system outputs a numerical verification map showing the assignment pattern without revealing names. This allows the organizer to confirm that everyone was assigned, no one has themselves, and the exchange is mathematically sound—all without spoiling the surprise.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this Secret Santa automation in your organization:

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance using the import function.
  2. Connect your Gmail account in the Gmail node by authenticating with OAuth2—this takes about 60 seconds.
  3. Prepare your participant data in a Google Sheet or CSV with columns for Name and Email at minimum.
  4. Configure the email template in the HTML node to match your organization's tone, including budget, deadline, and any theme details.
  5. Run a test with 2-3 participants first to verify everything works correctly before executing with your full list.
  6. Execute the workflow with your complete participant list and watch as all assignments and notifications happen automatically.
  7. Check the verification output to confirm mathematical fairness, then relax knowing the entire exchange is handled.
