What This Workflow Does

Manufacturing and engineering firms drown in critical emails—invoices from suppliers, purchase order confirmations, equipment alerts, and shipping notifications. This workflow automates the processing of these messages using AI to extract key information and deliver instant Telegram alerts to the appropriate teams.

The system monitors your Gmail inbox for specific message types, uses AI to "read" and categorize the content, then triggers actions based on the email type. Invoices get parsed for amounts and due dates, engineering alerts get prioritized by urgency, and purchase orders get logged in your systems—all without manual intervention.

How It Works

1. Email filtering and categorization

The workflow scans incoming Gmail messages using predefined filters to identify invoices, purchase orders, and engineering alerts. It checks subject lines, sender addresses, and attachment types to categorize each message correctly.

2. AI-powered data extraction

For invoices, the AI reads PDF attachments to extract vendor details, invoice numbers, line items, totals, and due dates. For engineering emails, it identifies equipment IDs, error codes, and urgency indicators from the message content.

3. Automated alert routing

Based on the email type and extracted data, the system sends formatted Telegram alerts to different teams. Accounting receives invoice summaries, procurement gets PO updates, and maintenance teams get equipment alerts with priority indicators.

What You'll Need

  1. A Gmail or Google Workspace account with admin access
  2. A Telegram account with permissions to create bots
  3. An n8n instance or account (self-hosted or cloud)
  4. AI service access (OpenAI, Google Vertex AI, etc.)
  5. Designated Telegram channels/groups for each alert type

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Connect your Gmail account in the email trigger node
  3. Configure your AI service credentials in the processing nodes
  4. Set up Telegram bot tokens and channel IDs for alerts
  5. Test with sample emails and refine filters as needed
