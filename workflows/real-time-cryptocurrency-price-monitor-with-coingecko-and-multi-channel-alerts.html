Real-time Cryptocurrency Price Monitor with CoinGecko & Multi-Channel Alerts

What This Workflow Does

Manually tracking cryptocurrency prices is time-consuming and emotionally draining. Traders and investors often miss crucial price movements because they can't monitor markets 24/7, leading to missed opportunities or unexpected losses. This automation solves that problem by providing constant, unbiased monitoring of your cryptocurrency watchlist.

This n8n workflow connects to the CoinGecko API to fetch real-time prices for Bitcoin, Ethereum, and any other cryptocurrencies you specify. It compares current prices against your predefined upper and lower limits, evaluates complex conditions (above, below, or both thresholds), and sends instant notifications through your preferred channels—Email, Telegram, or Discord—only when your specific conditions are met.

The system includes intelligent cooldown logic to prevent alert fatigue during volatile periods, automatically updates alert history in Google Sheets for record-keeping, and provides both success confirmations and error notifications to ensure reliability. It transforms reactive trading into proactive strategy execution.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Price Checking

The workflow triggers at configurable intervals (default: every minute) using a scheduler. This creates a 24/7 monitoring cycle that never sleeps, ensuring you catch price movements regardless of time zone or trading hours.

2. Watchlist Data Retrieval

It reads your cryptocurrency watchlist from a Google Sheets document, including symbols, upper/lower price limits, alert directions, and cooldown settings. This centralized configuration makes it easy to manage multiple coins and adjust strategies without modifying the workflow itself.

3. Real-time Price Fetching

For each cryptocurrency in your watchlist, the workflow queries the CoinGecko API to get the latest market price. CoinGecko provides reliable, real-time data for thousands of cryptocurrencies with a generous free tier that supports most monitoring needs.

4. Smart Condition Evaluation

The system compares current prices against your thresholds using your specified direction logic ("above," "below," or "both"). It also checks cooldown periods to avoid duplicate alerts and ensures you only receive notifications when conditions genuinely warrant attention.

5. Multi-channel Notification Delivery

When conditions are met, alerts are sent simultaneously through all configured channels: Email for formal records, Telegram for instant mobile notifications, and Discord for team collaboration. Each message includes relevant details like symbol, current price, threshold, and timestamp.

6. History Tracking & Confirmation

The workflow updates your Google Sheets with the last alert price and timestamp, creating an audit trail. It then sends a confirmation that the monitoring cycle completed successfully, or an error notification if any step failed, ensuring system reliability.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with execution permissions
  2. Google Sheets document with your cryptocurrency watchlist (columns: symbol, upper_limit, lower_limit, direction, cooldown_minutes, last_alert_price, last_alert_time)
  3. Google Sheets API credentials configured in n8n for spreadsheet access
  4. CoinGecko API endpoint (free tier sufficient for most monitoring needs)
  5. Notification channel credentials: SMTP settings for Email, Bot Token for Telegram, and Webhook URL for Discord
  6. Basic understanding of your trading strategy and price thresholds

Pro tip: Start with longer cooldown periods (30-60 minutes) to avoid alert fatigue. As you refine your strategy, you can shorten these intervals. Always test with small threshold differences first to ensure your notification channels work correctly before implementing real trading parameters.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the workflow into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file.
  2. Configure Google Sheets credentials in n8n and update the workflow node with your specific spreadsheet ID and range.
  3. Set up your watchlist in Google Sheets with at least 2-3 test cryptocurrencies and conservative price thresholds.
  4. Add notification credentials for at least one channel (Email is recommended for initial testing).
  5. Test with manual execution first, then activate the scheduler with a longer interval (5-10 minutes) for initial monitoring.
  6. Verify alerts arrive correctly and adjust thresholds/cooldowns based on your actual trading strategy.
  7. Expand to additional cryptocurrencies and notification channels once the basic workflow is stable.
