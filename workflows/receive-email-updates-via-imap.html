What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of manually monitoring email inboxes for important messages. Instead of constantly checking email for customer inquiries, support requests, order confirmations, or system notifications, this workflow automatically detects new emails via IMAP and triggers your chosen actions.

Businesses waste countless hours on repetitive email monitoring and manual data entry from messages. This template eliminates that manual work by connecting directly to any IMAP-compatible email service (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, custom domains) and processing incoming messages automatically. When a new email arrives, the workflow extracts key information like sender, subject, body content, and attachments, then routes it to your other business systems.

The solution provides a reliable, always-on email monitoring system that works with your existing email infrastructure. Unlike webhook-based services that require specific integrations, IMAP works with virtually any email provider, giving you flexibility without vendor lock-in.

How It Works

1. IMAP Email Trigger

The workflow starts with an IMAP Email node that connects to your email server using standard IMAP credentials. It polls your specified folder (typically Inbox) at regular intervals, checking for new messages since the last run.

2. Email Processing & Filtering

Each new email is parsed to extract structured data: sender address, recipient, subject, body (plain text and HTML), timestamps, and any attachments. You can add filters here to process only specific emails—for example, only those from your contact form or containing certain keywords.

3. Data Transformation

The email content is cleaned and formatted for your downstream systems. This might involve removing HTML tags, extracting specific information using patterns or AI, converting attachments to usable formats, or enriching data with additional context.

4. Action Execution

Finally, the processed email data triggers your business actions. This could be creating a ticket in your helpdesk, adding a lead to your CRM, posting a notification to Slack, saving attachments to cloud storage, or triggering a follow-up sequence.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance – Self-hosted or n8n.cloud account
  2. Email account credentials – IMAP access enabled with app password if using 2FA
  3. Destination system access – API keys or credentials for where emails should go (CRM, helpdesk, etc.)
  4. Basic understanding of your email provider's IMAP settings (server, port, SSL requirements)

Pro tip: Use app-specific passwords instead of your main email password for better security. Most email providers allow generating these in security settings.
