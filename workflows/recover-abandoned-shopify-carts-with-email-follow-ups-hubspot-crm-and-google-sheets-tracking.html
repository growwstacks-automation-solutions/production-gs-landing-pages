What This Workflow Does

Every abandoned shopping cart represents lost revenue and a frustrated customer. This automated workflow transforms those missed opportunities into recovered sales by systematically re-engaging customers who nearly purchased but didn't complete checkout.

The system monitors your Shopify store for new checkout events, identifies high-value abandoned carts, sends personalized recovery emails, updates your HubSpot CRM with detailed customer interaction data, and maintains a comprehensive tracking spreadsheet in Google Sheets—all without manual intervention.

Instead of losing 70% of potential sales to cart abandonment, you automatically convert 15-30% back into completed purchases while building richer customer profiles for future marketing campaigns.

How It Works

1. Shopify Triggers the Process

The workflow activates whenever a customer initiates checkout on your Shopify store but doesn't complete the purchase. It captures all cart details including products, quantities, prices, customer contact information, and timestamps.

2. Smart Cart Qualification

Not all abandoned carts deserve immediate follow-up. The system filters carts based on configurable rules—typically focusing on orders over a minimum value (like $50) and carts abandoned for a specific duration (like 12+ hours). This ensures you're prioritizing valuable opportunities.

3. Personalized Email Outreach

For qualified carts, the system sends a personalized email reminding customers about their pending purchase. The email includes specific product details, prices, and a direct link back to their cart to resume checkout seamlessly.

4. CRM Synchronization

The workflow checks if the customer exists in HubSpot, creates a new contact if needed, and updates their profile with their latest shopping behavior. This builds a complete customer journey record for future personalized marketing.

5. Activity Logging & Tracking

Every interaction is logged to Google Sheets, creating an audit trail of recovery attempts, outcomes, and customer responses. This data becomes invaluable for analyzing abandonment patterns and optimizing your recovery strategy over time.
