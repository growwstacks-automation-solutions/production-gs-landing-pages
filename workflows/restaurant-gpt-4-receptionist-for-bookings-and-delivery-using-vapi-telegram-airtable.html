What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the constant interruption of phone calls and messages that plague restaurant staff. It acts as a full-time, AI-powered receptionist that manages two critical revenue streams: table bookings and delivery/takeaway orders.

When a customer calls, the Vapi AI answers naturally, asks for party size, date, and time, checks real-time table availability in Airtable, and confirms the booking—all in a conversational voice. Simultaneously, customers can message your restaurant on Telegram. The AI greets them, fetches the live menu from Airtable, helps them build an order, validates their delivery address against your service area, calculates the total, and logs the complete order. All confirmed transactions are neatly recorded in Airtable, ready for your kitchen or host stand.

The system eliminates missed calls during rush hour, reduces order errors from miscommunication, and captures every customer interaction into a structured database. It turns chaotic, manual communication into a smooth, automated process that works 24/7.

How It Works

The workflow is a multi-channel orchestration engine connecting voice, chat, AI, and your database.

1. Customer Initiation

A customer interaction starts via a phone call (handled by Vapi's voice AI) or a Telegram message. The workflow detects the channel and routes the conversation appropriately, while maintaining a consistent customer experience.

2. Intent Recognition & Routing

Using OpenAI's GPT-4, the system analyzes the customer's first message to determine intent: "I'd like to book a table for 4" vs. "Can I order delivery?". It then branches into the dedicated booking or ordering flow.

3. Booking Flow Logic

For reservations, the AI asks clarifying questions, then queries the Airtable base to find available tables matching the party size and time window. It presents options to the customer, confirms details, and finally creates a new record in the 'Bookings' table with all details and a status of 'Confirmed'.

4. Ordering Flow Logic

For orders, the workflow fetches the current menu from Airtable, validates item selection and customization, calculates the total with tax, and collects the delivery address. It can use the Google Maps API to verify the address is within your delivery zone. If not, it automatically suggests takeaway. Once confirmed, a detailed order record is created.

5. Data Sync & Confirmation

All finalized bookings and orders are written to Airtable with timestamps, customer details, and status. A confirmation message is sent back to the customer via their original channel (voice response or Telegram). The workflow can also trigger secondary actions, like sending a summary to a staff Slack channel or updating inventory.

Pro tip: Use Airtable's "Calendar View" for the 'Bookings' table to give your staff a real-time, visual schedule of all AI-confirmed reservations.

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this automation in under 30 minutes.

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above to get the JSON file. In your n8n instance, go to Workflows > Import from File and select the downloaded JSON.
  2. Set Up Credentials: In the n8n "Credentials" section, create new credentials for Airtable, OpenAI, and the Telegram Bot. Add your API keys.
  3. Configure Airtable: Duplicate the provided base template into your Airtable account. Note the Base ID and Table Names, and update the corresponding nodes in the workflow.
  4. Connect Vapi: In your Vapi dashboard, create a new assistant. Copy the system prompt from the 'AI Agent' node in the workflow and paste it into Vapi. Connect the Vapi webhook node to your assistant's URL.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the workflow. Test the booking flow by calling your Vapi number and the order flow by messaging your Telegram bot. Verify that records appear correctly in your Airtable base.

Pro tip: Start with Telegram chat only for testing. It's faster to iterate on the conversation flow before setting up the voice phone line.
