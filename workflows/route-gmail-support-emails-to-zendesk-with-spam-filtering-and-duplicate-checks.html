What This Workflow Does

Customer support teams waste countless hours manually forwarding emails from shared Gmail inboxes into Zendesk. This workflow automates the entire process while adding critical quality controls.

The automation monitors your designated Gmail support address, applies spam filtering rules checks for duplicate inquiries, then creates properly formatted Zendesk tickets with all original email content and attachments preserved.

How It Works

1. Gmail Trigger

The workflow constantly monitors your specified Gmail inbox for new support emails matching your defined criteria (specific labels, senders, or keywords).

2. Spam Filter

Each email passes through configurable spam detection that evaluates sender reputation, content patterns, and blacklists. Suspected spam diverted to a review folder.

3. Duplicate Check

The system compares the email subject and content against recent tickets, preventing duplicate tickets for the same issue.

4. Zendesk Ticket Creation

Valid emails that pass all checks automatically convert into Zendesk tickets with proper categorization, priority assignment, and field mapping.

Pro tip: Configure additional validation steps like customer verification for VIP accounts or automatic escalation for urgent keywords.
