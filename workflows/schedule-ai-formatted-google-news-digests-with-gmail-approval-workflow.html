What This Workflow Does

Staying on top of industry news is crucial but time-consuming. This automation solves that by creating a hands-free news curation system. It automatically searches Google News for your chosen topics, uses AI to format the articles into a clean digest, and sends it to your Gmail for approval before distribution.

The workflow includes intelligent batching: if you decline the first set of articles, it automatically fetches the next batch until you find content worth sharing. This creates a continuous improvement loop, ensuring you only distribute high-quality, relevant news to your team, newsletter subscribers, or social media followers.

By automating the entire process—from discovery to formatting to approval—you save 5-10 hours per week on manual research while maintaining editorial control over what gets published.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence that mimics how a human researcher would curate content, but at scale and automatically.

1. Scheduled Trigger & News Collection

A schedule trigger kicks off the workflow daily, weekly, or at your preferred interval. It then uses the SerpApi node to search Google News for your predefined keywords or topics, returning the latest relevant articles with titles, snippets, and links.

2. AI-Powered Formatting & Summarization

The raw articles are sent to an AI model (like OpenAI) that transforms them into a professional digest format. The AI can summarize key points, apply consistent styling, and even add brief commentary or categorize articles by theme, creating a polished output ready for review.

3. Gmail Approval with Iterative Batching

The formatted digest is emailed to you via Gmail with clear approve/decline options. If you approve, the workflow proceeds to distribution. If you decline, it fetches the next batch of articles (managed by an Airtable counter) and repeats the formatting and approval process until you get content you like.

4. Distribution & Tracking

Once approved, the digest can be sent to various destinations: posted to a Slack channel, saved to a Google Doc, added to a content calendar, or formatted for a newsletter. The workflow logs all actions and maintains a record of what was sent when.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (self-hosted, as this template uses community nodes).
  2. SerpApi account for accessing Google News search results programmatically.
  3. Airtable account (free tier works) to create a base with WorkflowID and Counter columns for tracking.
  4. OpenAI API key for AI-powered formatting and summarization.
  5. Gmail account connected via OAuth to send and receive approval emails.
  6. Basic understanding of n8n node configuration and API key management.

Pro tip: Start with broad search terms and refine based on the quality of results. Use the Airtable counter to track which batches you've reviewed, preventing duplicate content in future runs.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automated news digest running in under 30 minutes:

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file.
  2. Create your Airtable base with two columns: "WorkflowID" (text) and "Counter" (number). The workflow will manage rows automatically.
  3. Connect your accounts in n8n: SerpApi, Airtable, OpenAI, and Gmail. For Gmail, set the recipient email in the node settings or use the $env.EMAIL_ADDRESS_ME variable.
  4. Configure the search query in the SerpApi node with your target keywords, location, and number of results.
  5. Customize the AI prompt in the OpenAI node to format the digest exactly how you want it (bullet points, summaries, tone of voice).
  6. Test the workflow manually first, then set the schedule trigger to your preferred frequency (daily, weekly, etc.).
