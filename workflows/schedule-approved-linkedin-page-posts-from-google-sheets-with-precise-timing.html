What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming problem of manually posting to your LinkedIn company page. Instead of logging in daily, creating posts, and scheduling them individually, this workflow lets you manage everything from a Google Sheets content calendar. Team members submit posts, managers approve them, and the system automatically publishes them at precisely scheduled times.

The workflow runs four times daily during business hours, checking your Google Sheet for approved LinkedIn posts scheduled for that day. It processes one post at a time to prevent conflicts, marks it as "scheduled," then uses n8n's Wait node to pause execution until the exact publication time. At the scheduled moment, it either downloads an image from Google Drive for visual posts or publishes an article link, then updates your sheet with the live LinkedIn URL.

How It Works

1. Content Planning in Google Sheets

Your team uses a shared Google Sheet as a content calendar with columns for scheduled date/time, platform, post type, caption, media URL, and approval status. Managers review and mark posts as "Good" when approved.

2. Automated Scheduling Check

The workflow triggers hourly during business hours (9:45 AM–12:45 PM) and scans the sheet for approved LinkedIn posts scheduled for today. It filters out other platforms and unapproved content.

3. Precise Time Control

Unlike basic schedulers that publish immediately, this workflow uses n8n's Wait node to pause execution until the exact scheduled time. It automatically converts timezones (Eastern to India time in the template) so posts publish at 10:00 AM sharp, not when the workflow runs at 9:45 AM.

4. Smart Post Type Routing

The system detects whether a post is a "Creative Post" (with image) or "Article" (with link). For creative posts, it downloads the image from Google Drive; for articles, it uses the URL directly.

5. LinkedIn Publishing

Using LinkedIn's Community Management API, the workflow posts to your organization page with proper authentication. It includes the caption, media, and tracks the resulting post URN for analytics.

6. Status Tracking & Audit Trail

After publishing, the workflow updates your Google Sheet—changing status from "Scheduled" to "Published" and adding the live LinkedIn URL. This creates a complete audit trail of what was posted when.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (self-hosted or n8n Cloud)
  2. Google Sheets with OAuth access for your content calendar
  3. Google Drive with OAuth access for image storage
  4. LinkedIn company page (not personal profile) with admin access
  5. LinkedIn Community Management API credentials (free developer account)

Pro tip: Use a separate "credentials" sheet within your Google Sheets document to store your LinkedIn Organization ID and other configuration. This keeps sensitive data organized and makes the workflow reusable across multiple company pages.

Quick Setup Guide

1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.

2. Set up credentials for Google Sheets, Google Drive, and LinkedIn Community Management API in n8n's credential manager.

3. Create your content calendar in Google Sheets with the required columns: Scheduled On, Platform, Post Type, Caption, Media URL, Approval Status, Post URL, and row_number.

4. Update the workflow nodes with your specific Google Sheet ID and LinkedIn Organization ID (found in your company page URL).

5. Test with a future-dated post scheduled 5 minutes ahead, mark it as "Good," and verify it publishes correctly.

6. Activate the workflow and let it run automatically during your business hours.
