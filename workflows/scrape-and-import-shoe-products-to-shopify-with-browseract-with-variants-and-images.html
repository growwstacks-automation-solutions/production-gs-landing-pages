Scrape & Import Shoe Products to Shopify with BrowserAct

Automatically scrape product data from any website and import complete listings with variants, images, and details directly to your Shopify store.

What This Workflow Does

This advanced automation solves a critical problem for e-commerce entrepreneurs and dropshippers: manually copying product data from supplier websites to your Shopify store is incredibly time-consuming and error-prone. For shoe businesses specifically, where each product has multiple sizes (variants) and numerous images, the manual process can take 30-45 minutes per product.

The workflow automates the entire product import pipeline. It reads a list of product URLs from your Google Sheet, visits each page using BrowserAct's scraping technology, extracts all relevant details including product names, prices, descriptions, available sizes, and image URLs, then creates fully configured products in your Shopify store with proper variants and uploaded images. What used to take hours now happens automatically while you focus on growing your business.

Pro tip: This template is optimized for shoe products but can be adapted for any product category with variants. The structured approach to handling sizes and bulk image uploads makes it particularly valuable for apparel, accessories, and other variant-heavy products.

How It Works

Step 1: Read Product URLs from Google Sheets

The workflow starts by connecting to your Google Sheet where you maintain a simple list of product URLs you want to import. This sheet acts as your product discovery database—you can add new URLs at any time, and the automation will process them on its next run.

Step 2: Scrape Product Data with BrowserAct

For each URL, the workflow uses BrowserAct (a specialized web scraping service) to visit the product page and extract structured data. It captures the product title, price, description, available sizes/variants, and all image URLs. The scraping is configured to handle modern e-commerce site structures.

Step 3: Transform and Structure the Data

A custom Code node processes the raw scraped data, cleaning and formatting it for Shopify's API. This includes converting size strings into proper variant arrays, ensuring image URLs are properly formatted, and creating unique SKU patterns for inventory tracking.

Step 4: Create Base Product in Shopify

The workflow creates the main product entry in your Shopify store with the core details: title, description, price range, and product type. This establishes the product foundation before adding variants.

Step 5: Add Product Options and Variants

Using Shopify's API, the automation dynamically adds product options (like "Shoe Size") and creates individual variants for each available size. Each variant gets its own pricing and SKU for proper inventory management.

Step 6: Upload Product Images in Bulk

The workflow uploads all product images from their original URLs to Shopify's CDN, attaching them to the newly created product. This maintains visual quality and ensures fast loading times for your customers.

Step 7: Send Completion Notification

Finally, a Slack notification confirms the batch has been processed, giving you visibility into what products were added without needing to check Shopify admin constantly.

  2. BrowserAct "Bulk Product Scraping" template configured for your target websites
  3. Google Sheets account with a spreadsheet containing product URLs to import
  4. Shopify store with API access enabled (private app with product write permissions)
  5. n8n instance (self-hosted recommended for this community node integration)
  6. Optional: Slack workspace for completion notifications

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance
  2. Configure BrowserAct credentials in the respective nodes with your API key and workflow ID
  3. Connect your Google Sheet by setting up OAuth credentials and specifying your spreadsheet ID
  4. Set up Shopify authentication using a private app token with product write permissions
  5. Test with a single product URL first to ensure all connections work correctly
  6. Schedule the workflow to run daily or weekly for automatic product imports

Web scraping for e-commerce is the automated process of extracting product data from websites, including prices, descriptions, images, and variants. It works by using software tools to visit product pages, identify the relevant data points in the HTML structure, and collect that information into a structured format like a spreadsheet or database.

This automation saves hundreds of hours compared to manual copy-pasting. Modern scraping tools like BrowserAct can handle JavaScript-rendered content, navigate through pagination, and adapt to different website layouts, making them reliable for most e-commerce platforms.

Scraping publicly available product data for personal business use is generally legal, but you must respect the website's terms of service and robots.txt file. It's crucial to avoid overloading servers with too many requests and to use the data ethically—never copy copyrighted descriptions verbatim.

Many successful dropshippers use scraping to source products, but always verify the legality for your specific use case and jurisdiction. When in doubt, consult with a legal professional familiar with e-commerce and data collection regulations in your country.
