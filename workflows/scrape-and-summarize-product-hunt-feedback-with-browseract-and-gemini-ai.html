What This Workflow Does

Launching a product is stressful, but understanding why a competitor's launch succeeded or failed is pure gold. Manually reading through hundreds of Product Hunt comments is a massive time sink. This automated workflow solves that by acting as your 24/7 competitive intelligence analyst.

It systematically scrapes user feedback from any Product Hunt launch page, processes the raw comments through Google's Gemini AI, and delivers a structured report. You get a concise summary, categorized positive and negative points, and actionable recommendations—all without spending hours reading.

This turns qualitative, messy feedback into quantitative, structured data you can use to inform your own product roadmap, marketing messaging, and feature prioritization.

How It Works

The workflow connects several powerful tools to create a seamless intelligence pipeline.

Step 1: Input & Trigger

You provide a list of competitor product names or URLs in a Google Sheet. The workflow can be triggered manually for a one-time analysis or put on a schedule (daily/weekly) for ongoing monitoring of new launches.

Step 2: Data Collection

A BrowserAct node performs the web scraping. It navigates to the specified Product Hunt page, extracts all public user comments, and passes this raw text data to the next stage. This handles the complexity of interacting with a dynamic webpage.

Step 3: AI-Powered Analysis

The core magic happens here. An AI Agent node, powered by Google Gemini, receives the scraped comments. Using a specialized prompt, it acts as a competitive analyst, distilling the feedback into key themes, sentiment, and insights.

Step 4: Structured Output

The AI's analysis is formatted into a clear structure: a one-paragraph summary, bullet-point lists of positive feedback and criticisms, and a set of strategic recommendations for building a successful product in the same space.

Step 5: Storage & Notification

The structured report is appended to a Google Sheet for easy review, tracking, and sharing with your team. Finally, a Slack notification is sent to confirm the job is complete and the insights are ready.

Pro tip: Use the Schedule Trigger to run this workflow automatically every Monday morning. You'll start each week with a fresh competitive intelligence report on the latest Product Hunt launches in your niche.

  2. BrowserAct Account & API Key: To power the web scraping step.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to import and configure this automation in your n8n instance.

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above and import the JSON file into your n8n canvas.
  2. Install the BrowserAct Node: In your n8n instance, install the community node package `n8n-nodes-browseract-workflows`.
  3. Configure Credentials: Set up credentials in n8n for BrowserAct, Google Gemini, Google Sheets, and Slack.
  4. Set Your Inputs: In the Google Sheets node, point to your spreadsheet containing the Product Hunt URLs or product names you want to monitor.
  5. Test & Schedule: Run the workflow once manually to ensure everything connects. Then, switch the trigger to a schedule (e.g., weekly) for hands-off monitoring.
