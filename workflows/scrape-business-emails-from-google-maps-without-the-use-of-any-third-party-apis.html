What This Workflow Does

Manual lead generation is time-consuming and expensive. Sales teams spend hours searching Google Maps, visiting business websites, and hunting for contact information—often paying for unreliable email databases or third-party scraping services with monthly limits.

This n8n workflow automates the entire process. It takes your target business queries (like "digital marketing agencies in New York" or "restaurant suppliers in Chicago"), scrapes Google Maps for relevant listings, visits each business website, and extracts email addresses using custom regular expressions. The results are automatically saved to Google Sheets, ready for your outreach campaigns.

The key advantage? Zero ongoing costs. Unlike paid services that charge per lead or have monthly caps, this workflow uses only n8n's core nodes—HTTP Request, Code, and Google Sheets—giving you unlimited scalability and complete control over your lead generation pipeline.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence to ensure efficient and accurate email extraction while respecting website resources.

Step 1: Input Target Queries

You provide a list of search queries combining business types and locations. The workflow processes each query sequentially, ensuring focused, location-specific lead generation.

Step 2: Scrape Google Maps Listings

Using HTTP requests, the workflow extracts business URLs from Google Maps search results. It handles pagination to collect comprehensive listings for each query.

Step 3: Fetch Website HTML

For each business URL, the workflow retrieves the website's HTML content. Built-in delays prevent overwhelming target servers and reduce the risk of being blocked.

Step 4: Extract Emails with Regex

A Code node runs sophisticated regular expressions against the HTML to identify email address patterns. The logic filters out generic contact forms and focuses on business-specific addresses.

Step 5: Save to Google Sheets

Validated email addresses, along with business names, locations, and source URLs, are appended to a Google Sheets document. This creates a clean, organized database for your sales team.

Pro tip: Add a "Wait" node between website requests with random delays (2-5 seconds) to mimic human browsing behavior and avoid triggering anti-scraping measures.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this workflow running in under 10 minutes with these simple steps:

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance using the Import from File option.
  2. Configure the Google Sheets node by connecting your Google account and selecting the target spreadsheet and worksheet.
  3. Add your search queries to the Manual Trigger node. Format: "business type in location" (e.g., "coffee shops in Seattle").
  4. Test with a single query first to verify email extraction and Google Sheets integration work correctly.
  5. Schedule execution using n8n's scheduler or trigger manually for ongoing lead generation campaigns.

Pro tip: Create separate Google Sheets for different industries or regions to keep your lead data organized. Use n8n's "Split Out" node to route results based on business categories.
