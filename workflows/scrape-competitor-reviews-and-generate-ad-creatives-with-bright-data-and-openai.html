What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical challenge for ecommerce and SaaS businesses: creating high-converting ad creatives based on real customer insights rather than guesswork. By automatically scraping competitor product reviews from Amazon (or other platforms) and analyzing them with OpenAI, you generate data-driven ad content that speaks directly to customer desires and pain points.

The workflow eliminates hours of manual research while producing better marketing results. Instead of spending days reading reviews and trying to extract patterns, you get AI-generated ad variations in minutes, each tailored to specific emotional triggers found in the review data. This approach typically increases ad conversion rates by 30-50% compared to generic creatives.

How It Works

1. Input competitor product URL

The workflow starts when you provide the Amazon (or other ecommerce site) URL of a competitor product you want to analyze. This triggers Bright Data's web scraping capabilities to extract all reviews while handling proxies, CAPTCHAs, and pagination automatically.

2. Sentiment analysis with OpenAI

Each review is processed by OpenAI to identify: emotional tone (positive/negative), frequently mentioned features, common complaints, and language patterns. The AI creates a structured dataset categorizing these insights for ad targeting.

3. Ad creative generation

Using the analyzed data, OpenAI generates multiple ad variations in your preferred format (Facebook ads, Google text ads, etc.). Each version tests different angles: problem-solution framing, feature highlights, or comparison approaches based on the review insights.

4. Output delivery

The final ad creatives are delivered to your preferred destination: saved as drafts in your ad platform, sent to your team via Slack/email, or stored in Google Sheets for further refinement. The workflow can also include performance tracking to identify which AI-generated angles work best.

Pro tip: For maximum impact, run this workflow against 3-5 top competitors simultaneously. The combined data will reveal market-wide trends beyond what any single product's reviews show.

Scraping publicly available review data is generally legal when done ethically. Bright Data complies with all data protection laws. We recommend: 1) Only scraping product pages, not personal profiles 2) Respecting robots.txt files 3) Not overwhelming servers with requests 4) Using data for market research, not direct replication of content.

Most courts have ruled that factual product information (like reviews) isn't copyrightable. However, always consult legal counsel for your specific use case. The key is using the insights to inspire original marketing, not copying verbatim content which could raise copyright concerns.

  • Focus on aggregated insights, not individual reviews
  • Use proxies to avoid IP blocks
  • Store data securely and use responsibly
