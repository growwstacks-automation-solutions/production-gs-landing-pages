What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow automatically scrapes the 20 most recent articles from TechCrunch, one of the most influential technology news platforms. It extracts key information including article titles, URLs, publication dates, authors, and summaries - saving you hours of manual research and data collection.

The automation is particularly valuable for content teams, market researchers, and competitive intelligence professionals who need to stay updated on the latest tech trends without constantly monitoring the site. The structured data output can be easily integrated with other tools for analysis, reporting, or content repurposing.

How It Works

1. Fetching TechCrunch's latest articles

The workflow begins by accessing TechCrunch's homepage or specific category pages to retrieve the list of most recent articles. It handles the website's pagination to ensure complete coverage of the latest 20 posts regardless of how they're distributed across pages.

2. Extracting article metadata

For each article found, the workflow extracts the essential metadata including the headline, permanent link, publication timestamp, and author information. This structured data forms the foundation for any subsequent analysis or processing.

3. Retrieving article summaries

The automation goes beyond basic metadata to capture the article excerpts or opening paragraphs that typically appear on listing pages. These summaries provide enough context for preliminary analysis without needing to scrape full article content.

4. Formatting and output

Finally, the collected data is cleaned, standardized, and formatted into a consistent JSON structure. This makes it ready for integration with databases, spreadsheets, or other business systems that might process the information further.
