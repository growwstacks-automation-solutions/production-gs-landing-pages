What This Workflow Does

Coming unprepared to sales calls is one of the biggest productivity killers in business development. Manual research about companies you're meeting with can take 15-30 minutes per call, and it's easy to skip when you're busy. This creates missed opportunities, generic conversations, and lost deals.

This n8n workflow automates company research by scanning your Google Calendar each morning, identifying upcoming meetings with companies, fetching their latest news from NewsAPI, and delivering personalized briefing emails via Gmail. You get curated news headlines, descriptions, and direct links to articles—all before your first meeting of the day.

The automation ensures you're always prepared with relevant talking points, demonstrates you've done your homework, and helps you build stronger relationships by showing genuine interest in your prospect's business developments.

Configuration panel for customizing the workflow parameters including date ranges and news sources

How It Works

1. Calendar Scanning

Each morning, the workflow connects to your Google Calendar and scans for meetings scheduled that day. It extracts company names from event titles or descriptions using pattern matching, creating a list of companies you'll be interacting with.

2. News API Integration

For each identified company, the workflow queries NewsAPI for recent articles. You can configure parameters like date range (last 7, 14, or 30 days), relevance filtering, and source preferences to ensure you get the most valuable insights.

3. Data Processing & Filtering

The workflow processes the news results, removing duplicates, sorting by relevance and recency, and extracting key information like headlines, descriptions, publication dates, and direct URLs to full articles.

4. Personalized Email Delivery

A customized email is generated for each company, containing the curated news list with clickable links. These emails are sent to your Gmail inbox with clear subject lines indicating which meeting they prepare you for.

5. Conditional Logic & Error Handling

Built-in conditions handle edge cases: if no meetings are found, the workflow stops gracefully; if no news is available for a company, it notes this in the email; if API limits are reached, it continues with available data.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and save the JSON file locally.
  2. Import into n8n by going to your n8n instance, clicking "Workflows" → "Import from file" and selecting the downloaded JSON.
  3. Configure credentials for Google Calendar, Gmail, and NewsAPI in their respective nodes.
  4. Adjust parameters in the "Setup" node to match your preferences for date ranges, email timing, and news filtering.
  5. Test the workflow with a single meeting in your calendar to ensure emails are delivered correctly.
  6. Activate the schedule trigger to run automatically each morning (or your preferred time).
