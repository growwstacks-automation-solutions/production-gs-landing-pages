Automate your prospect research by finding target companies on LinkedIn and syncing their data directly to your Airtable CRM

What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the tedious manual process of researching companies on LinkedIn and transferring their information to your CRM. Sales professionals waste hours each week copying data between platforms, risking errors and missing opportunities. The automation performs targeted LinkedIn searches based on your criteria (industry, location, size), extracts key company details, and creates clean records in your Airtable CRM.

By automating this process, you ensure your sales pipeline stays filled with qualified prospects while eliminating repetitive data entry. The workflow captures comprehensive company profiles including industry classification, employee count, headquarters location, and LinkedIn engagement metrics—all structured for easy filtering and segmentation in Airtable.

How It Works

1. Configure LinkedIn search parameters

The workflow starts by executing a LinkedIn company search using your predefined filters. You can specify criteria like industry verticals, geographic locations, company size ranges, or keywords in company descriptions. These parameters ensure you only capture relevant prospects matching your ideal customer profile.

2. Extract company profile data

For each matching company, the workflow collects detailed information including the company name, LinkedIn profile URL, industry classification, employee count range, headquarters location, and website URL. It also captures valuable engagement metrics like follower count and recent post activity.

3. Transform data for CRM consistency

The raw LinkedIn data gets processed to match your Airtable CRM structure. This includes standardizing industry classifications, converting employee ranges to numerical values, and extracting clean website domains. The transformation ensures all records follow your preferred formatting for consistent reporting and filtering.

4. Create or update Airtable records

The workflow checks if each company already exists in your Airtable base to prevent duplicates. New companies get added as fresh records, while existing ones receive updates with the latest LinkedIn data. The system maintains all historical information while refreshing key metrics for accurate pipeline management.
