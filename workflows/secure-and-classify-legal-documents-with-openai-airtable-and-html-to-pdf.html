What This Workflow Does

This enterprise-grade legal document management system solves the critical challenges law firms and corporate legal departments face with document organization, classification, and security. Traditional manual processes for sorting legal documents are time-consuming, error-prone, and often fail to meet compliance requirements across multiple jurisdictions.

The workflow automatically processes incoming legal documents, uses OpenAI's AI to classify them by type (contracts, briefs, filings etc.), extracts key metadata, securely stores them in Airtable with proper access controls, and generates PDF versions with watermarks or security features as needed. This creates a searchable, secure legal vault that maintains compliance with data protection regulations.

How It Works

1. Document Ingestion

The workflow begins by receiving legal documents through various channels - email attachments, cloud storage uploads, or direct submissions. The system accepts multiple file formats including Word, PDF, and scanned documents.

2. AI Classification

OpenAI's natural language processing analyzes each document to determine its type (contract, motion, brief etc.), jurisdiction relevance, and confidentiality level. The AI identifies key clauses, parties, and dates for metadata extraction.

3. Secure Storage

Classified documents are stored in Airtable with proper access controls based on classification. Sensitive documents are automatically flagged and restricted to authorized personnel only.

4. PDF Generation

The system converts documents to standardized PDF format with appropriate watermarks, security features, and metadata tags based on the classification and jurisdiction requirements.

5. Audit Trail

Every action in the system is logged with timestamps and user information (when applicable) to maintain a complete audit trail for compliance purposes.

Who This Is For

This solution is ideal for law firms handling sensitive client documents, corporate legal departments managing contracts and compliance materials, and legal tech companies building document management platforms. It's particularly valuable for organizations operating across multiple jurisdictions with varying data protection requirements.

Pro tip: For international firms, configure jurisdiction-specific rules in Airtable to automatically apply the correct security protocols based on the document's classification.
