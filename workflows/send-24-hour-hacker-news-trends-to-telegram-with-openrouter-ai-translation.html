What This Workflow Does

Manually checking Hacker News for trending discussions is time-consuming and easy to miss. This automation solves that by continuously monitoring the platform, identifying genuinely trending posts using a custom "Gravity Score" algorithm, and delivering clean, translated summaries to your Telegram channel or group.

Unlike basic RSS feeds that just show newest or highest-voted posts, this workflow calculates post velocity—how quickly engagement accumulates—to surface what's actually trending right now. It filters out low-quality "infancy" posts and noisy content, ensuring you only receive signal, not noise.

The optional AI translation layer makes global tech discussions accessible to non-English speaking teams, breaking down language barriers in real-time. You get a curated digest of the most relevant tech conversations delivered automatically, saving hours of manual monitoring each week.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Data Collection

The workflow triggers on a schedule (default: every 4 hours) and fetches Hacker News data from the past 24 hours using the Algolia API. This provides comprehensive coverage without overwhelming you with constant notifications.

2. Gravity Score Calculation

Each post receives a custom "Gravity Score" that combines points, comments, and time decay. This algorithm prioritizes posts gaining traction quickly rather than just those with high total engagement, ensuring you see what's trending now, not what was popular yesterday.

3. Intelligent Filtering

The system automatically filters out new posts with zero engagement ("infancy" posts) and applies configurable thresholds to ensure only quality content passes through. This eliminates noise and keeps your feed high-value.

4. Optional AI Translation

Using OpenRouter or other LLM services, the workflow translates post titles and summaries into your preferred language. The system prompt is customizable, allowing translation to Chinese, Spanish, French, or any other language your team needs.

5. Formatted Telegram Delivery

Finally, the workflow formats the curated content into clean HTML messages with clickable links, engagement metrics (points/comments), and time indicators, then sends them directly to your specified Telegram chat or channel.

Pro tip: Don't need translation? Simply delete the Translate node and connect the Filter node directly to the message formatting step. The workflow remains fully functional with English-only content.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your Hacker News monitor running:

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Configure Telegram credentials by creating a bot via @BotFather and obtaining your Chat ID
  3. Set up OpenRouter (or your preferred LLM) credentials if using translation
  4. Adjust the schedule trigger to match your preferred monitoring frequency
  5. Test the workflow with a manual execution to verify Telegram delivery
  6. Activate the workflow and let it run automatically

The workflow includes comprehensive error handling and will notify you of any issues with data fetching or delivery, ensuring reliable operation.

Pro tip: Start with the default 4-hour schedule, then adjust based on your needs. For real-time monitoring, you can reduce to hourly, but consider adding additional filtering to prevent notification overload.
