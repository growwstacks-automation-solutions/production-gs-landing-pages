What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the manual, time-consuming process of sending personalized outreach emails to leads. Instead of copying and pasting from spreadsheets or using expensive marketing platforms with complex setups, this workflow connects Google Sheets (where your leads live) directly to SendGrid (professional email delivery).

It reads lead data—business names, contact details, industries, locations—and merges them into predefined email templates you control. Each lead receives a customized email with their specific information inserted automatically. The system includes rate limiting to prevent spam flags and loops through your entire list without manual intervention.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger & Data Collection

The workflow starts manually or on a schedule. It connects to your Google Sheets document and reads the "Leads" tab containing all contact information and custom fields you've collected about each business or individual.

Step 2: Template Selection

Simultaneously, the workflow accesses your "Email Templates" tab in the same spreadsheet. It can select templates randomly or based on rules you define, ensuring variety in your outreach while maintaining brand consistency.

Step 3: Personalization & Merging

Using n8n's Set node, placeholders in your templates (like [BusinessName], [Location], [Industry]) are replaced with actual data from each lead's row. This creates a fully personalized email body and subject line for each recipient.

Step 4: Email Delivery

The personalized content is sent via SendGrid using your authenticated sender domain. Each email is tracked for delivery, and the system waits 1-2 seconds between sends to maintain good sending practices and avoid triggering spam filters.

Step 5: Completion & Logging

The workflow continues through all leads in your sheet, then completes. You can extend it to log which emails were sent, update lead statuses, or trigger follow-up sequences based on engagement.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with workflow import capability
  2. Google Sheets account with a spreadsheet containing leads and email templates
  3. SendGrid account with verified sender domain and API key
  4. Lead data organized with columns for email, name, company, and custom fields
  5. Email templates written in HTML or plain text with merge field placeholders

Pro tip: Start with 3-5 email templates that address different prospect scenarios (new lead follow-up, value proposition, case study share). Test them with small segments before full deployment to optimize open and response rates.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template file using the button above
  2. Import the JSON into your n8n instance (Workflows → Import from File)
  3. Connect your Google Sheets credentials (OAuth2) to the Google Sheets nodes
  4. Add your SendGrid API key to the SendGrid node
  5. Duplicate the sample spreadsheet and customize with your leads and templates
  6. Update the spreadsheet IDs in the Google Sheets nodes to point to your document
  7. Test with a single lead first, then execute the full workflow

Track these key metrics for email campaign success: delivery rate (percentage of emails actually delivered), open rate (who opened your emails), click-through rate (who clicked links), conversion rate (who took desired action), and unsubscribe rate.

Also monitor bounce rates to maintain list quality. These metrics help you refine templates, timing, and targeting for better results over time. Start by aiming for 20%+ open rates and 2%+ click-through rates as baseline targets.

To avoid spam flags: use a professional email domain with proper authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), maintain a clean email list by removing bounced addresses, include clear unsubscribe links, space out sends (1-2 seconds between emails), avoid spam trigger words in subject lines, and ensure your content provides genuine value.

Warm up new sending domains gradually with small volumes first. SendGrid's infrastructure helps with deliverability, but following these best practices ensures your messages reach the inbox rather than the spam folder.

The ideal frequency depends on your relationship with recipients. For cold outreach, start with 3-5 emails spaced 3-7 days apart. For nurturing existing leads, weekly emails often work well.

Always provide an easy unsubscribe option and monitor engagement metrics. Test different frequencies with segments of your list to find what generates the best response without causing fatigue. Quality content sent less frequently often outperforms frequent, low-value emails.
