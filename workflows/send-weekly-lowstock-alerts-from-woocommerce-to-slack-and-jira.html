What This Workflow Does

Running out of stock is more than an inconvenience—it's lost revenue and disappointed customers. This automation solves that by proactively monitoring your WooCommerce inventory levels and alerting your team before problems occur.

The workflow runs automatically every Monday, fetching all active products from your WooCommerce store. It categorizes items into "low stock" and "urgent low stock" based on customizable thresholds. For low stock items, it sends detailed alerts to a designated Slack channel. For urgent stock situations that require immediate attention, it automatically creates Jira issues with all relevant product details, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.

This transforms inventory management from a reactive, manual process into a proactive, automated system. Your team spends less time checking spreadsheets and more time taking action on what matters.

How It Works

1. Weekly Scheduled Trigger

The workflow activates automatically every Monday at midnight (configurable). This regular cadence ensures consistent oversight without requiring manual initiation.

2. Fetch WooCommerce Products

It connects to your WooCommerce store via API and retrieves all active products with their current stock levels, SKUs, and categories.

3. Categorize Inventory Levels

Using your predefined thresholds (e.g., low stock = under 20 units, urgent = under 5 units), the workflow separates products into different categories for appropriate handling.

4. Send Slack Alerts

For low stock items, it posts formatted messages to your chosen Slack channel with product names, remaining quantities, and direct WooCommerce links for quick review.

5. Create Jira Issues for Urgent Items

Products in the urgent category automatically generate Jira tickets with detailed descriptions, priority levels, and assignment to the appropriate team member.

6. Summary Notification

A final summary message goes to Slack showing total items flagged, with links to created Jira tickets for complete traceability.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with workflow execution capabilities
  2. WooCommerce store with REST API access enabled
  3. Slack workspace with permissions to create incoming webhooks or use Slack app
  4. Jira Software Cloud account with API credentials for issue creation
  5. Basic understanding of your inventory thresholds and reorder processes

Pro tip: Before setting thresholds, analyze 3 months of sales data to determine your actual stock velocity. This ensures alerts match your real reorder timeline, not just arbitrary numbers.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file
  2. Configure WooCommerce node with your store URL and API credentials
  3. Set up Slack connection by creating an incoming webhook for your desired channel
  4. Connect Jira node with your instance URL, email, and API token
  5. Adjust thresholds in the categorization node to match your business needs
  6. Test the workflow with a single product to ensure all connections work
  7. Activate the schedule to run weekly (default is Monday at 00:00)
