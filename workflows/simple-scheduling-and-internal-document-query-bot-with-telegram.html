What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of manual customer service and administrative overhead by creating an intelligent Telegram bot that handles three key business functions automatically. First, it manages scheduling—when a customer messages "schedule laptop maintenance tomorrow," the bot creates a Google Calendar event without human intervention. Second, it provides instant price quotes by searching through your updated price lists and service catalogs. Third, it answers complex questions by querying your internal documents, policies, and knowledge bases using AI-powered retrieval.

The workflow connects Telegram's messaging platform with your business tools through AI agents and RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) technology. Instead of customers waiting for email responses or phone calls, they get immediate, accurate answers 24/7. This reduces response time from hours to seconds while ensuring information comes directly from your authoritative sources, not generic AI hallucinations.

How It Works

Step 1: Telegram Message Reception

The workflow starts when a user sends a message to your Telegram bot. The Telegram Trigger node captures this message and passes it to the AI agent along with conversation history from Redis memory, maintaining context throughout the interaction.

Step 2: AI Intent Recognition

An AI agent (using Google Gemini or similar) analyzes the message to determine the user's intent. It classifies whether the request is for scheduling, price checking, document querying, or general assistance, then routes to the appropriate workflow branch.

Step 3: Action Execution

For scheduling requests, the bot connects to Google Calendar via MCP Client to create events with proper titles, descriptions, and times. For price checks, it searches through connected Google Sheets or databases. For document queries, it uses RAG to retrieve relevant information from your Google Docs, internal wikis, or knowledge bases before formulating a response.

Step 4: Response Delivery

The bot sends a clear, helpful response back through Telegram, confirming actions taken or providing requested information. Conversation history is updated in Redis memory to maintain context for future interactions with the same user.

What You'll Need

  1. Telegram Bot Token: Created via BotFather on Telegram with basic messaging permissions.
  2. Google Workspace Account: Access to Google Calendar, Docs, and Sheets for scheduling and document retrieval.
  3. AI Model Access: API key for Google Gemini, OpenAI GPT, or similar LLM service for natural language processing.
  4. Redis Database: Upstash Redis or similar for conversation memory storage (free tier available).
  5. n8n Instance: Self-hosted n8n or n8n.cloud account to run the workflow.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and Import: Click the download button above to get the JSON file, then import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Credentials: Set up credentials for Telegram Bot, Google Services, AI Model, and Redis in n8n's credentials management.

Pro tip: Start with a limited set of documents and scheduling permissions during testing. Gradually expand access as you refine the bot's accuracy and response patterns. Use the conversation history in Redis to identify common questions and improve your knowledge base.
