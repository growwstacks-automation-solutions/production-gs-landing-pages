What This Workflow Does

This AI Email Assistant transforms how businesses handle email communication by automating the entire processing pipeline. Instead of manually sorting through dozens or hundreds of daily emails, this workflow uses artificial intelligence to intelligently classify incoming messages, determine their priority and urgency, generate professional draft responses in your voice, and organize everything with smart labeling.

The system connects Gmail with GPT-4 (or compatible AI models) to create a sophisticated email management solution that learns from your communication patterns. It can distinguish between urgent client requests, routine inquiries, spam, and internal communications, ensuring that critical messages receive immediate attention while maintaining organized records of all correspondence.

Beyond basic filtering, this workflow provides actionable intelligence—it suggests when emails need team involvement, identifies messages requiring follow-up, automatically saves attachments to cloud storage, and can even send alerts to platforms like Telegram or Slack for truly urgent matters. This isn't just email filtering; it's complete email intelligence automation.

How It Works

1. Email Trigger & Initial Processing

The workflow activates whenever a new email arrives in your Gmail inbox. It first retrieves the complete message including headers, body content, attachments, and conversation history. The system checks for existing labels and processing status to avoid duplicate handling of previously managed emails.

2. AI-Powered Classification

Using GPT-4 or similar AI models, the email content is analyzed against multiple criteria: urgency keywords, sender importance, requested actions, time sensitivity, and required response complexity. The AI assigns priority levels (high/medium/low) and categories like "needs response," "urgent," "forward to team," or "follow-up required."

3. Intelligent Response Generation

For emails requiring replies, the AI generates contextually appropriate draft responses that match your communication style. It considers the entire email thread, maintains professional tone, and can provide multiple response options for complex inquiries. Placeholders are used for information requiring human input.

4. Automated Organization & Actions

The workflow applies relevant Gmail labels, marks emails as processed, saves attachments to Google Drive with intelligent naming, and creates draft responses in Gmail for your review. For urgent emails, it can trigger instant notifications to your preferred communication channels with direct links to the message.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud-based or self-hosted)
  2. Gmail account with API access enabled
  3. OpenAI API key or access to compatible AI service (Anthropic Claude, local LLM via Ollama, etc.)
  4. Google Drive account for attachment storage (optional but recommended)
  5. Telegram bot or Slack webhook for urgent alerts (optional)

Pro tip: Start with a dedicated Gmail label like "AI-Processed" to track which emails have been handled by the workflow. This makes it easy to review the system's performance and make adjustments to your classification criteria.

Quick Setup Guide

1. Download the template file using the button above and import it into your n8n instance via Templates → Import from File.

2. Configure Gmail OAuth2 credentials for all Gmail-related nodes: Email Trigger, Get Conversation Thread, Create Draft Response, and Label Management nodes.

3. Set up your AI model credentials in the AI Language Model node. You can use OpenAI's GPT-4, GPT-3.5-turbo, or alternative services like Anthropic Claude.

4. Customize the AI classification prompts in the "AI Email Classifier" node with your business-specific urgency keywords and priority criteria.

5. Create corresponding labels in your Gmail account (High Priority, Medium Priority, Needs Response, etc.) and update the label IDs in the workflow.

6. Test with sample emails, review classifications and draft responses, then activate the workflow for production use.
