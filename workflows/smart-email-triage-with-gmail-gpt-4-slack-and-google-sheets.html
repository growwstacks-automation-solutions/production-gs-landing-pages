Smart Email Triage with AI

Automatically analyze incoming emails, classify intent with GPT-4, send real-time Slack alerts, and log insights to Google Sheets.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

Email overload is a universal business problem. Important messages get buried, urgent requests are missed, and teams waste hours manually sorting and forwarding. This workflow solves that by turning your inbox into an intelligent, automated communication hub.

It continuously monitors your Gmail for new emails, extracts the sender, subject, and body content, then uses GPT-4 to analyze the intent and determine priority. Key insights are formatted and posted as structured alerts in Slack for immediate team attention. Every processed email, along with its AI classification, is logged into Google Sheets for tracking, analytics, and audit purposes.

Pro tip: This workflow is especially powerful for shared inboxes (like support@ or sales@) where multiple team members need visibility and quick action on high-priority messages.

How It Works

Step 1: Gmail Trigger & Email Extraction

The workflow starts by polling your Gmail account for unread or new incoming emails based on a configurable search filter (e.g., "is:unread"). It extracts the sender's address, subject line, and the full body content, preparing this data for AI analysis.

Step 2: AI Intent Classification with GPT-4

The email content is sent to GPT-4 (via Azure OpenAI or OpenAI API) with a prompt designed to classify intent. The AI determines categories like "Urgent Support Request", "Sales Inquiry", "General Feedback", or "Low Priority". It also generates a concise summary and assigns an urgency score.

Step 3: Slack Notification & Alerting

Based on the classification, a formatted message is constructed containing the sender, summary, intent, and urgency. This is posted to a designated Slack channel. High-priority emails can trigger @mentions or be sent to specific team channels for immediate action.

Step 4: Google Sheets Logging & Audit Trail

All processed data—timestamp, sender, subject, intent, summary, urgency—is appended as a new row in a Google Sheets spreadsheet. This creates a centralized, searchable record of all email communications and their AI-driven insights.

Step 5: Error Handling & Fallbacks

The workflow includes built-in error handling. If the AI API fails or data parsing encounters issues, error notifications are sent via Slack so the team knows the automation needs attention, ensuring reliability.

What You'll Need

  1. Gmail Account: With OAuth2 credentials configured for inbox read access.
  2. Slack Workspace: A Bot token with chat:write and channels:history scopes.
  3. Google Sheets: OAuth2 credentials for data logging; a prepared spreadsheet with column headers.
  4. GPT-4 Access: Azure OpenAI or OpenAI API credentials with GPT-4 capabilities.
  5. n8n Instance: Either n8n Cloud or a self-hosted n8n environment to run the workflow.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download & Import: Download the JSON template file and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure Credentials: Set up the Gmail, Slack, Google Sheets, and GPT-4 credentials as resources in n8n.
  3. Adjust Gmail Filter: Modify the Gmail trigger node's search query to match your needs (e.g., "is:unread", "label:Inbox").
  4. Set Slack Channel: Enter your target Slack channel ID in the Slack notification node.
  5. Specify Google Sheet: Provide your Google Sheet ID and tab name in the Sheets append node.
  6. Test & Activate: Run the workflow once manually to verify all connections and data flow. Then activate it for continuous execution.
