What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of helpdesk tickets getting lost in email inboxes or requiring manual checking of multiple systems. It creates a seamless bridge between Easy Redmine (your helpdesk system) and Microsoft Teams (where your team collaborates), with the added intelligence of GPT-4 to summarize complex tickets.

When a new ticket is created or updated in Easy Redmine, the workflow automatically posts a formatted message to your designated Microsoft Teams channel. The AI component analyzes the ticket description, extracts key information, and generates a concise summary that helps your team quickly understand the issue without reading lengthy descriptions.

How It Works

1. Ticket detection in Easy Redmine

The workflow monitors your Easy Redmine instance for new or updated tickets using webhooks or API polling. It captures all relevant ticket details including title, description, priority level, assignee, and custom fields.

2. AI-powered summarization

The full ticket description is sent to GPT-4, which analyzes the content and generates a 3-4 sentence summary highlighting the core issue, affected systems, and any urgent requirements. This step saves support teams from parsing lengthy technical descriptions.

3. Teams notification formatting

The workflow formats a rich Teams message card with the ticket title as the header, AI summary as the body, and key metadata (priority, category, due date) in a structured layout. Action buttons can be included to directly open the ticket in Redmine.

4. Conditional routing

Optional logic routes different ticket types to different Teams channels. For example, critical infrastructure issues might go to an #urgent-support channel while feature requests go to #product-feedback.

Pro tip: Configure the AI to extract specific technical details relevant to your systems, like error codes or server names, by providing examples in your workflow setup.
