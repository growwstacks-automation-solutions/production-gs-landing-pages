What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical problem for small e-commerce businesses and solopreneurs: providing instant, helpful customer support without being online 24/7 or investing in expensive helpdesk software. It creates a smart AI chatbot that acts as your first line of sales support.

The workflow listens to customer questions—like "Do you have the black leather iPhone case in stock?"—and automatically fetches real-time product information from a Google Sheet. It can answer queries, confirm availability, and even process an order by collecting customer details and updating your inventory, all through a conversational AI interface powered by GPT-4o.

Everything is managed through a single Google Sheet that serves as both your product catalog and order management system, making it incredibly simple to set up and maintain without any technical database setup.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to handle customer interactions from start to finish.

1. Customer Query Input

The workflow is triggered by a customer message, which can come from various channels you connect (like a website chat widget, Telegram, or WhatsApp). The AI agent receives this natural language query.

2. Intent Recognition & Data Fetching

GPT-4o analyzes the customer's intent. If they're asking about product availability, the workflow triggers the "Get Stock" node, which queries your Google Sheet to find the relevant product and current stock levels.

3. Intelligent Response Generation

Using the product data from Sheets and its conversational AI capabilities, the chatbot crafts a helpful, accurate response. It can show product details, images, and available quantities, just like a human support agent would.

4. Order Processing (If Applicable)

If the customer wants to buy, the AI guides them through providing necessary details (name, address, phone). The "Place Order" node then writes this new order to a separate tab in your Google Sheet for fulfillment.

5. Inventory Update

Once an order is confirmed, the "Update Stock" node automatically deducts the purchased quantity from your inventory sheet, keeping your stock counts accurate in real-time.

Pro tip: Use a single Google Sheet with two tabs (Stock and Orders) to keep everything simple. The AI agent can seamlessly switch between reading from one tab and writing to another based on the conversation context.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow.
  2. Google Sheets credentials set up in n8n with access to a spreadsheet for your product inventory and orders.
  3. OpenAI API key for GPT-4o access (or another compatible LLM provider).
  4. A Google Sheet structured with at least two tabs: one for product stock and one for customer orders.
  5. A trigger channel such as a website chat widget, Telegram bot, or webhook endpoint to receive customer messages.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this chatbot live in under 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Download and Import: Click the download button above to get the JSON file. In your n8n instance, go to Workflows → Import from File and select the downloaded template.
  2. Configure Google Sheets: Set up Google OAuth credentials in n8n if you haven't already. Then update the three Google Sheets nodes ("Get Stock," "Place Order," "Update Stock") with your specific spreadsheet ID and tab names.
  3. Connect Your AI: Add your OpenAI API key to the AI Agent node credentials. You can customize the system prompt in this node to match your brand voice and product specifics.
  4. Set Up Your Trigger: Connect the workflow trigger to your preferred channel. For testing, you can use the Manual Trigger node; for production, connect to a platform like Telegram, Discord, or a webchat widget.
  5. Test and Activate: Turn on the workflow and test it with sample queries like "What phone cases do you have?" or "I want to order the blue iPhone case." Verify that stock checks and order placements correctly update your Google Sheet.

Pro tip: Start with a limited product catalog in your Google Sheet for testing. Once everything works perfectly, expand to your full inventory. This makes troubleshooting much easier.
