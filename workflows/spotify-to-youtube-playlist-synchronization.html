What This Workflow Does

Manually maintaining YouTube playlists that match your Spotify favorites is time-consuming and error-prone. This automation solves that problem by creating a one-way synchronization system that automatically adds and removes YouTube videos based on changes to your Spotify playlists.

The workflow continuously monitors your specified Spotify playlist, identifies when tracks are added or removed, searches YouTube for the best matching videos, and updates your YouTube playlist accordingly. It includes smart matching algorithms that consider video length, content relevance, and popularity to ensure high-quality matches.

Beyond basic synchronization, this system includes auto-recovery features that detect when YouTube videos become unavailable and automatically find replacements. All matches are stored in a database for persistence, ensuring your playlists survive server restarts and maintain their synchronization history.

How It Works

1. Playlist Monitoring & Comparison

The workflow starts by fetching your current Spotify playlist and comparing it against the previously stored version in the database. It identifies new tracks that need to be added to YouTube and tracks that have been removed from Spotify that should be deleted from YouTube.

2. Smart Video Matching

For each new track, the system searches YouTube using optimized queries combining artist name, track title, and duration filters. It evaluates search results based on relevance, view count, and upload date to select the most appropriate video version.

3. YouTube Playlist Management

Selected videos are added to your specified YouTube playlist in the correct order. The system also removes videos corresponding to tracks that have been deleted from your Spotify playlist, maintaining perfect synchronization.

4. Database Persistence & Recovery

All matches between Spotify tracks and YouTube videos are stored in a Supabase database. This allows the system to recover from interruptions, track synchronization history, and quickly find replacement videos when originals become unavailable.

5. Scheduled Automation

The entire process runs on a schedule (default: hourly) to keep your playlists continuously synchronized. You can adjust the frequency based on how often your Spotify playlists change.
