What This Workflow Does

Manual startup funding research is time-consuming, inconsistent, and often misses critical opportunities. Investment teams, market researchers, and business development professionals spend hours each week scanning news sites, compiling spreadsheets, and trying to spot trends in fragmented data.

This intelligent automation solves that problem by creating a systematic pipeline that monitors funding announcements, extracts key details using AI, conducts deep research on promising companies, and organizes everything into a searchable Airtable database. It transforms scattered news into structured intelligence you can actually use for decision-making.

The workflow specifically monitors sources like TechCrunch and VentureBeat, but can be extended to any publication with consistent funding coverage. It's designed for scalability—what starts as monitoring a few sources can grow into a comprehensive market intelligence system.

How It Works

1. News Monitoring & Article Collection

The workflow begins by scraping sitemaps from selected news sources to identify recent funding announcement articles. It uses intelligent filtering to focus only on relevant content, avoiding general business news that doesn't contain actual funding data.

2. AI-Powered Data Extraction

Each article is processed through Claude AI to extract structured information: company name, funding amount, round type (Seed, Series A, etc.), lead investors, participating investors, and market vertical. The AI transforms unstructured article text into clean, consistent data fields.

3. Deep Company Research

For each funded company, the workflow triggers Perplexity AI or Jina AI to conduct comprehensive research. This goes beyond the press release to gather information about founding team, business model, total funding to date, employee count, headquarters location, and competitive positioning.

4. Database Organization & Enrichment

All extracted and researched data flows into two interconnected Airtable bases: one for funding round details and another for comprehensive company profiles. The system creates relationships between tables and enriches records with calculated fields and categorization.

5. Alerting & Reporting

Based on configured rules (specific investors, minimum funding amounts, or target markets), the workflow can trigger notifications via email, Slack, or other channels. It also generates summary reports of recent funding activity by sector, geography, or investor.

What You'll Need

  1. Airtable account with bases created for funding rounds and company research (template structure provided)
  2. Claude API access (Anthropic) for structured data extraction from articles
  3. Perplexity AI API access or Jina AI (generous free tier available) for deep company research
  4. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the automation
  5. News sources to monitor (initially TechCrunch and VentureBeat, easily extendable)

Pro tip: Start with 2-3 news sources and validate the data quality before expanding. The Jina AI free tier is excellent for initial testing and can handle substantial research volume without immediate cost.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the workflow into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file.
  2. Create your Airtable bases using the provided field structures or import the template base linked in the workflow documentation.
  3. Configure API credentials for Claude, Perplexity/Jina, and Airtable in n8n's credential management.
  4. Test with a single article to verify data extraction accuracy and adjust AI prompts if needed.
  5. Schedule the workflow to run daily or hourly based on your research needs.
  6. Set up alerting for specific criteria (e.g., companies in your target market raising over $5M).
