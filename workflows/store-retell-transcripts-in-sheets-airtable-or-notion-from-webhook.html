What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow automatically captures voice call transcripts from Retell and stores them in your preferred database platform - Google Sheets, Airtable, or Notion. It solves the problem of manually copying and pasting call transcripts, ensuring all customer conversations are systematically recorded and organized.

When integrated with Retell's webhook system, this workflow captures the full transcript of each call along with metadata like call duration, timestamps, and participant information. The data is then formatted and routed to your chosen destination where it can be searched, analyzed, or shared with team members.

Example of Retell transcripts automatically stored in Google Sheets

How It Works

1. Webhook Trigger

The workflow starts when Retell sends call transcript data via webhook after each completed call. The webhook contains the full conversation transcript along with call metadata.

2. Data Parsing

The raw webhook data is processed to extract key information like call participants, timestamps, and the conversation transcript. The workflow cleans and formats this data for optimal storage.

3. Destination Routing

Based on your configuration, the workflow routes the formatted transcript data to your chosen destination - Google Sheets, Airtable, or Notion. Each platform has specific formatting requirements that the workflow handles automatically.

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for customer support teams, sales organizations, and any business using Retell for voice conversations that need to maintain records of calls. It's particularly valuable for:

  • Compliance teams requiring call records

What You'll Need

  1. An active Retell account with webhook capabilities
  2. Access to n8n (self-hosted or cloud version)
  3. A Google Sheets spreadsheet, Airtable base, or Notion database
  4. API credentials for your chosen destination platform

Pro tip: Create separate sheets/tables for different call types (sales, support, etc.) to make searching and reporting easier.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure the Retell webhook URL in your Retell account settings
  3. Connect your destination platform (Sheets/Airtable/Notion) credentials
  4. Map the fields from Retell to your database columns
  5. Test with a sample call and verify the data appears correctly

Google Sheets is best for teams needing simple tabular data that's easy to share. Airtable offers more robust database features like linked records and multiple views. Notion provides the richest formatting options and wiki-like organization.

A sales team might prefer Airtable for its CRM-like capabilities, while a support team might choose Notion to combine transcripts with knowledge base articles. Sheets works well for teams already using Google Workspace who need basic reporting.

  • Sheets: Simple spreadsheet analysis
  • Airtable: Advanced filtering and relations
  • Notion: Rich text formatting and embedding
