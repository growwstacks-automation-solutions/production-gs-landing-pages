What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the challenge of manually monitoring and analyzing Apple App Store reviews across multiple apps. Product teams often struggle to keep up with the volume of feedback, missing critical insights buried in hundreds of reviews. The automation fetches new reviews daily, stores them in Pinecone's vector database for semantic search, then uses GPT-4 Mini to generate concise summaries highlighting common themes, sentiment trends, and urgent issues.

The processed insights are delivered to a designated Slack channel, enabling your entire team to stay informed about customer feedback without spending hours reading individual reviews. The system maintains historical context, allowing you to track how sentiment evolves after updates or identify recurring complaints that need attention.

How It Works

1. App Store Review Collection

The workflow begins by fetching the latest reviews from your Apple App Store listings using the App Store Connect API. It retrieves all new reviews since the last run, including ratings, text, and metadata like app version and device type.

2. Pinecone Vector Storage

Each review is converted into a vector embedding using AI and stored in Pinecone. This enables semantic search across all historical reviews, making it easy to find similar feedback even when phrased differently. Pinecone's indexing allows the system to efficiently compare new reviews against past feedback.

3. AI-Powered Analysis

GPT-4 Mini processes the batch of new reviews to identify common themes, sentiment distribution, and notable outliers. The AI groups similar feedback, detects urgent issues (like crashes), and highlights potential feature requests. It maintains context about your app's specific features and common pain points.

4. Insight Delivery to Slack

The final step formats the AI's findings into a clean, actionable summary posted to your chosen Slack channel. The message includes sentiment breakdowns, top themes with example quotes, and any critical issues requiring immediate attention. Team members can discuss findings directly in the thread.
