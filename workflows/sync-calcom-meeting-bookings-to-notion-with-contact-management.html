What This Workflow Does

For founders, consultants, and service providers, managing client meetings across multiple tools creates chaos. You schedule meetings in Cal.com, take notes elsewhere, and try to remember follow-ups—leading to missed details and unprofessional client experiences.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your scheduling tool and your client management system. Every time someone books, reschedules, or cancels a meeting in Cal.com, the workflow automatically updates your Notion database. It doesn't just log the meeting—it manages contacts, links relationships, and maintains complete client interaction histories.

The result is a centralized command center for all client interactions. You can see at a glance who you've met with, what was discussed, and what needs to happen next—without ever leaving Notion.

How It Works

The workflow listens for changes in your Cal.com calendar and intelligently syncs them to Notion while managing associated contacts.

1. New Booking Detection

When someone schedules a new meeting through your Cal.com link, the workflow captures all meeting details including time, attendees, and any custom questionnaire responses.

2. Contact Management

The system checks if the attendee already exists in your Notion contacts database using their email. If they exist, it links them to the new meeting. If not, it creates a new contact record first.

3. Meeting Creation in Notion

A new page is created in your Notion meetings database with all relevant details mapped to custom properties. This includes the Cal.com unique ID for future reference.

4. Reschedule Handling

When a meeting is rescheduled in Cal.com, the workflow finds the corresponding Notion page using the Cal.com ID and updates the date/time automatically.

5. Cancellation Processing

For cancelled meetings, the workflow archives the Notion page (keeping it accessible in trash for 30 days) while maintaining the record for historical purposes.

Pro tip: Use custom properties in your Notion meetings database to capture specific information from Cal.com's custom questions. This lets you tailor the sync to capture exactly what matters for your business—project type, budget range, or specific discussion topics.

What You'll Need

  1. Cal.com Account: A working Cal.com account with API access enabled. You'll need to generate an API key from your Cal.com settings.
  2. Notion Account: A Notion account with permission to create and modify databases. You'll need to create a Notion integration and share your databases with it.
  3. Notion Databases: Two databases in Notion—one for meetings and one for contacts—with specific properties configured (detailed in the setup guide).
  4. n8n Access: Either n8n.cloud account or self-hosted n8n instance to import and run the workflow.
  5. Database IDs: The unique IDs for your Notion meetings and contacts databases (found in their URLs).

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automation running in under 15 minutes:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Create Cal.com connection in n8n using your API key from Cal.com settings → Developer → API keys.
  3. Create Notion connection in n8n using your integration token from notion.so/profile/integrations.
  4. Prepare your Notion databases with the required properties: Meetings database needs Event Time (date), Cal ID (text), Contacts (relation), and Name. Contacts database needs Name, Email, and Meetings (relation).
  5. Configure the workflow nodes by entering your specific database IDs in the Notion nodes.
  6. Test the workflow by scheduling a test meeting in Cal.com and verifying it appears correctly in Notion.
  7. Activate the workflow and let it run continuously to automatically sync all future meetings.

Pro tip: Start with a test Cal.com event type and a separate test Notion database. Once everything works perfectly, switch to your production databases. This prevents any accidental data issues during setup.
