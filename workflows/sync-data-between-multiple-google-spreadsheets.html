What This Workflow Does

Manually copying and pasting data between Google Sheets is a tedious, error-prone task that wastes valuable time. This automation solves that by automatically syncing data from a master spreadsheet to one or multiple destination sheets on a schedule you set.

The workflow triggers every two minutes (configurable), reads specific columns (A to G in the example) from your source Google Sheet, and writes that data unchanged to two different destination spreadsheets. It ensures that your reports, dashboards, and team sheets are always up-to-date without any manual intervention.

Whether you're consolidating sales figures, distributing updated task lists, or maintaining a single source of truth for inventory, this template removes the friction of manual data management. It's a foundational automation that can be extended to include data transformation, filtering, or integration with other apps.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple but powerful sequence to keep your sheets in sync.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

A Cron node acts as the workflow's heartbeat, running the automation every two minutes. You can easily adjust this schedule to hourly, daily, or any interval that matches your business needs.

Step 2: Read from Source Sheet

The Google Sheets node connects to your designated source spreadsheet. It reads data from a specific sheet (e.g., "Sheet Data") and defined columns. This step establishes a reliable connection and fetches the latest data.

Step 3: Write to Destination Sheets

Two separate Google Sheets nodes (or more, if you add them) receive the data. Each writes the information into its target spreadsheet, maintaining the same sheet name and column structure. The data flows directly without alteration, ensuring perfect replication.

Pro tip: Use named ranges in your source sheet instead of static cell references (like A:G). This makes your automation more resilient if you add or move columns later.

What You'll Need

  1. A free or paid n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted).
  2. Google Cloud Platform project with the Google Sheets API enabled.
  3. Service account credentials (JSON key file) or OAuth 2.0 setup for n8n to access your Google Sheets.
  4. At least two Google Sheets: one source and one or more destination sheets.
  5. Edit permissions for the service account or OAuth user on all involved sheets.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this automation running in under 10 minutes.

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure Google Sheets connections in each node. Use your service account JSON key or OAuth credentials.
  3. Update the spreadsheet IDs and sheet names in the nodes to point to your actual Google Sheets.
  4. Adjust the Cron schedule if you need syncing more or less frequently than every two minutes.
  5. Test the workflow by clicking "Execute Workflow". Check that data appears correctly in your destination sheets.
  6. Activate the workflow to run automatically on your chosen schedule.

Pro tip: Before going live, run the workflow in test mode with a small sample of data. Verify the column mapping and data integrity in your destination sheets to prevent any accidental overwrites.
