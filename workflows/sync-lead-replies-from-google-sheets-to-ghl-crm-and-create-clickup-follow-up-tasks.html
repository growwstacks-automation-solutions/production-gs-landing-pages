What This Workflow Does

Manual lead tracking is a silent revenue killer. When a potential customer replies to your outreach, every minute counts. This n8n workflow eliminates the friction between communication, customer relationship management, and task execution by creating a seamless bridge between Google Sheets, GoHighLevel (GHL) CRM, and ClickUp.

The automation monitors a designated Google Sheet for new lead replies (marked as "Yes" in a Replied column). When a reply is detected, it instantly updates the corresponding contact record in GoHighLevel to reflect the new engagement level and simultaneously creates a structured follow-up task in ClickUp for your sales team. This ensures immediate action, perfect data synchronization, and complete auditability—all without a single manual click.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple but powerful trigger-action principle, ensuring data flows accurately between your sales tools.

1. Google Sheets Trigger & Filter

The workflow starts by polling your Google Sheet at regular intervals (configurable, typically every minute). It looks for rows where the "Replied" column changes to "Yes." An "If" node acts as a gatekeeper, processing only rows that meet this condition, preventing unnecessary API calls and ensuring clean data flow.

2. Update Contact in GoHighLevel

For each qualifying lead, the workflow extracts the unique GHL contact ID and updates the corresponding record in your GoHighLevel CRM. You can map custom fields—like "Last Contact Date," "Lead Status," or "Engagement Score"—to reflect the new interaction, keeping your CRM perfectly synchronized with real-time engagement.

3. Create Task in ClickUp

Simultaneously, the automation creates a new task in your designated ClickUp list. The task is populated with the lead's name, email, and relevant details from the sheet. You can set priority, assignees, due dates, and custom fields to ensure the right team member knows exactly what action to take next.

4. Merge & Log Results

A "Merge" node combines the successful outputs from both the GHL update and ClickUp task creation. This unified data is then appended to a separate "Sheet2" tab in your Google Sheet, creating a timestamped audit log of every sync operation. This log is invaluable for troubleshooting and reporting.

What You'll Need

  1. An active n8n instance (Cloud or self-hosted).
  2. A Google Sheet with at least these columns: Name, Email, GHL_ID, Replied.
  3. A second sheet within the same workbook (e.g., "Sheet2") to serve as the sync log.
  4. API credentials for:
    • Google Sheets (with read/write access)
    • GoHighLevel (OAuth2)
    • ClickUp
  5. Your ClickUp Team ID, Space ID, and List ID where follow-up tasks should be created.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated service account for Google Sheets API access in a production environment for better security and reliability than using a personal account.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this automation running in your environment in under 15 minutes.

  1. Prepare Your Sheet: Create your tracking sheet with the required columns. Ensure the "GHL_ID" column contains the correct contact IDs from GoHighLevel.
  2. Set Up Credentials: In your n8n instance, create and test credentials for Google Sheets, GoHighLevel, and ClickUp.
  3. Import the Template: Download the JSON file using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  4. Configure Nodes:
    • Update the Google Sheets trigger node with your spreadsheet ID and range.
    • In the GHL node, verify the field mappings match your custom properties.
    • In the ClickUp node, input your Team, Space, and List IDs.
  5. Test & Activate: Click "Execute Workflow" to test with one row. Check that the contact updates in GHL and a task appears in ClickUp. Once verified, activate the workflow to run automatically.
