What This Workflow Does

Manual data entry is a silent killer of sales productivity. Every minute your team spends copying leads from spreadsheets into your CRM is a minute not spent selling. This workflow solves that by creating a seamless, automated bridge between Google Sheets and HubSpot.

It automatically imports new contact information from a designated Google Sheet directly into HubSpot as contact records. The workflow intelligently checks for existing contacts by email address to prevent duplicates, updating records instead of creating new ones. This ensures your sales team always has the latest lead data without any manual effort.

Whether you collect leads through website forms, event registrations, partner referrals, or content downloads that land in a spreadsheet, this automation ensures they flow instantly into your primary sales tool, accelerating your response time and conversion rate.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to ensure clean, reliable data transfer.

Step 1: Trigger & Data Fetch

The workflow can be triggered manually or on a schedule. It connects to your specified Google Sheet and reads the latest rows of lead data, typically including fields like name, email, company, phone, and lead source.

Step 2: Data Validation & Cleaning

Before sending data to HubSpot, the workflow can perform basic validation—checking for valid email formats, removing empty rows, or formatting phone numbers consistently. This step ensures only high-quality data enters your CRM.

Step 3: HubSpot Lookup & Decision

For each lead, the workflow queries HubSpot to see if a contact with that email already exists. This is the crucial duplicate prevention mechanism. Based on the result, it decides whether to create a new contact or update an existing one.

Step 4: Create or Update Contact

If the contact is new, HubSpot creates a record with all mapped properties. If the contact exists, HubSpot updates the record with any new information from the sheet, enriching the contact profile over time.

Step 5: Logging & Error Handling

The workflow includes error handling for API issues or malformed data. It logs the outcome of each record processed (created, updated, or failed), providing transparency and making troubleshooting straightforward.

Pro tip: Add a "Date Synced" column to your Google Sheet. Use a simple formula to mark rows once they've been processed, giving you a clear visual audit trail of what's been imported.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google account with access to the spreadsheet containing your leads.
  2. A HubSpot account (Free, Starter, Professional, or Enterprise).
  3. A HubSpot Private App Access Token with permissions to read and write contacts (and optionally companies).
  4. Your lead data in a Google Sheet with consistent column headers (e.g., "Email", "First Name", "Company").
  5. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to import and run the workflow.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this automation running in your environment in just a few steps.

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Create HubSpot credentials: In HubSpot, go to Settings > Integrations > Private Apps. Create a new private app with scopes for crm.objects.contacts.read and crm.objects.contacts.write. Copy the access token.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: In the n8n workflow, configure the Google Sheets node by authenticating with your Google account and selecting your spreadsheet and worksheet.
  4. Map your fields: In the HubSpot node, map the columns from your sheet (like "Email", "First Name") to the corresponding HubSpot contact properties.
  5. Test and activate: Run the workflow once manually with a few test rows. Verify contacts appear correctly in HubSpot, then set it to run on a schedule (e.g., every hour).

Pro tip: Start with a test sheet containing 5-10 dummy leads. Run the workflow and confirm they appear in HubSpot correctly before connecting your main lead source. This prevents accidental data issues.
