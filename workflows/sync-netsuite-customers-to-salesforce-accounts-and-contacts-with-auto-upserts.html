What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical business problem: disconnected customer data between your ERP (NetSuite) and CRM (Salesforce). When customer information exists in separate systems, sales teams work with outdated records, finance can't see real-time customer status, and reporting becomes inaccurate.

The workflow automatically exports customer records from NetSuite and intelligently syncs them into Salesforce. It distinguishes between company records (creating/updating Accounts) and individual contacts (creating/updating both Accounts and linked Contacts). This eliminates manual data entry, reduces errors, and ensures both systems reflect the same customer information.

Beyond simple data transfer, the template includes pagination handling for large datasets, duplicate prevention through upsert logic, and proper relationship mapping between Accounts and Contacts. This creates a reliable, scalable solution that grows with your business.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to ensure data integrity and completeness:

1. Initialize & Fetch

The workflow starts by initializing pagination parameters, then fetches customer records from NetSuite in manageable batches via REST API. This approach handles large customer databases without overwhelming either system.

2. Process & Classify

Each retrieved record is processed individually. The workflow examines record attributes to determine whether it represents a company (Organization) or an individual person (Individual/Person). This classification determines how the data will be mapped to Salesforce objects.

3. Upsert to Salesforce

For company records, the workflow performs an upsert operation to the Salesforce Account object, using a unique external ID to prevent duplicates. For individual records, it first ensures a corresponding Account exists (creating if necessary), then upserts the Contact record linked to that Account.

4. Merge & Continue

Results from both processing branches are merged, and pagination parameters are updated. The workflow continues fetching and processing batches until all NetSuite customer records have been synchronized, then completes cleanly.

What You'll Need

  1. NetSuite Access: Administrator credentials or API access with permissions to read customer records.
  2. Salesforce Access: Administrator or integration user credentials with create/update permissions on Account and Contact objects.
  3. External ID Field: A custom field in Salesforce to store NetSuite's internal ID for upsert operations.
  4. n8n Instance: A running n8n environment (cloud or self-hosted) to import and execute the workflow.
  5. Field Mapping Document: Understanding of which NetSuite fields correspond to which Salesforce fields in your specific implementation.
