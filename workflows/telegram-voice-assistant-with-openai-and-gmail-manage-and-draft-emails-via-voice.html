What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the constant email overload that professionals face daily. Instead of typing replies or scrolling through your inbox, you simply send a voice message to your Telegram bot. The workflow transcribes your voice using OpenAI, understands your intent, and manages your Gmail accordingly—whether that's reading recent emails, drafting responses, or sending replies.

It transforms your messaging app into a personal email assistant that works while you're commuting, walking between meetings, or multitasking. The AI maintains conversation context, so you can discuss email threads naturally, ask for summaries, or request specific actions—all through simple voice commands.

How It Works

Step 1: Voice Command Reception

When you send a voice message to your Telegram bot, the workflow triggers immediately. The Telegram node captures the audio file and passes it to the next stage for processing.

Step 2: Speech-to-Text Conversion

OpenAI's Whisper model transcribes your voice message into accurate text. This happens in seconds, even with background noise or technical terminology.

Step 3: Intent Recognition & Routing

A switch node analyzes the transcribed text to determine what you want: "read my last 5 emails," "draft a reply to John," or "send an email about the meeting." The workflow routes to the appropriate action branch.

Step 4: Gmail Integration

Based on your command, the workflow interacts with Gmail via OAuth. It can retrieve specific emails, draft new messages with AI-generated content, or send pre-approved responses directly.

Step 5: AI-Powered Response

For complex requests like "discuss the budget email from yesterday," OpenAI's chat model engages in a conversational analysis, providing insights, suggestions, or draft responses that you can approve or modify.

What You'll Need

  1. Telegram Bot Token: Created via @BotFather on Telegram (free)
  2. OpenAI API Key: From platform.openai.com (pay-as-you-go)
  3. Gmail OAuth Credentials: Set up in Google Cloud Console
  4. n8n Instance: Self-hosted or n8n.cloud account
  5. Basic understanding of credential management in automation tools

Pro tip: Start with a dedicated Gmail account or label for testing. This lets you experiment safely without affecting your primary inbox while you refine the voice commands.
