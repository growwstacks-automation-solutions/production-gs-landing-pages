What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious manual process of copying content from your RSS feed to social media platforms. Content creators, bloggers, and news publishers often spend hours each week manually sharing their latest publications across different channels. This workflow automatically monitors your Tiny tiny RSS public feed and posts new content to your Mastodon account, ensuring your audience stays updated without constant manual intervention.

The automation runs every 10 minutes, checking for new published items in your RSS feed. When it detects new content, it formats the post appropriately for Mastodon and publishes it automatically. This ensures timely distribution of your content while freeing up your time for more creative work. The workflow handles authentication, content formatting, and error handling, providing a reliable hands-off solution for content distribution.

How It Works

Step 1: RSS Feed Monitoring

The workflow begins by connecting to your Tiny tiny RSS public feed URL. It checks for new published items at regular intervals (every 10 minutes by default). The RSS Read node extracts key information including titles, descriptions, publication dates, and links from your feed.

Step 2: Content Processing

New feed items are processed to ensure they meet Mastodon's requirements. This includes checking content length (Mastodon has character limits), formatting links properly, and extracting relevant metadata. The workflow can be configured to add hashtags, commentary, or custom formatting to make posts more engaging.

Step 3: Mastodon Authentication

The workflow authenticates with your Mastodon instance using your access token. This secure connection allows the automation to post content on your behalf without storing your password. Different Mastodon instances (like mastodon.social, fosstodon.org, etc.) are supported through configuration of your instance URL.

Step 4: Post Creation & Publishing

Finally, the formatted content is posted to your Mastodon account. The workflow includes error handling for network issues, rate limiting, or authentication problems. Successful posts are logged, and the workflow ensures duplicate content isn't republished by tracking previously shared items.

What You'll Need

  1. Tiny tiny RSS public feed URL - The publicly accessible RSS feed URL from your Tiny tiny RSS installation
  2. Mastodon instance URL - Your Mastodon instance (e.g., https://mastodon.social, https://fosstodon.org)
  3. Mastodon access token - Generated from your Mastodon account settings under Development → New Application
  4. n8n instance - Self-hosted n8n or n8n.cloud account to run the workflow
  5. Basic understanding of RSS feeds - Familiarity with your feed structure and content format

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your RSS to Mastodon automation running in minutes:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance
  2. Configure the RSS Read node with your Tiny tiny RSS public feed URL
  3. Set up the HTTP Request node with your Mastodon instance URL and access token
  4. Adjust the schedule - The default is every 10 minutes, but you can modify this based on your posting frequency needs
  5. Test with a single item - Run the workflow once to verify formatting and posting work correctly
  6. Activate the workflow - Once testing is successful, activate the workflow to run automatically

Pro tip: Before going live, test your automation with a private Mastodon account or use Mastodon's "Post to followers only" visibility setting. This lets you verify formatting and functionality without broadcasting test posts to your entire audience.
