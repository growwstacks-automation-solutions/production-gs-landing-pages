Automate Your Reading List: Tiny Tiny RSS to Wallabag

Never manually save articles again. This free n8n workflow automatically sends your starred RSS items to Wallabag for later reading.

What This Workflow Does

If you're an avid reader who uses Tiny Tiny RSS to follow numerous feeds and Wallabag to save articles for later, you know the frustration of constantly switching between apps to manually save content. This repetitive task interrupts your reading flow and wastes valuable time that could be spent actually consuming content.

This automation solves that problem completely. Every 10 minutes, the workflow checks your Tiny Tiny RSS account for newly starred articles. When it finds one, it automatically extracts the article URL, title, and any other relevant metadata, then creates a corresponding entry in your Wallabag account. The result is a seamless pipeline where starring an article in your RSS reader instantly adds it to your "read later" queue without any manual intervention.

The business value extends beyond personal productivity. For researchers, content teams, and knowledge workers, this automation ensures important articles are never missed and are systematically organized for future reference. It transforms a fragmented manual process into a reliable, hands-off system that works 24/7.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence that mimics what you would do manually, but with perfect consistency and speed.

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

A scheduler node activates the workflow every 10 minutes (configurable to any interval). This regular polling ensures new starred articles are captured promptly without requiring real-time webhooks from Tiny Tiny RSS.

Step 2: Fetch Starred Articles

The workflow connects to your Tiny Tiny RSS API and queries for articles marked with a star since the last check. It uses intelligent filtering to only retrieve new items, avoiding duplicates.

Step 3: Process Article Data

Each article's data is extracted and formatted. The workflow can handle various RSS feed formats and normalize the information into a consistent structure suitable for Wallabag's API requirements.

Step 4: Save to Wallabag

The formatted article data is sent to Wallabag via its API, creating a new entry in your reading list. The workflow includes error handling to retry failed attempts and log any issues for troubleshooting.

Step 5: Confirmation & Logging

Finally, the workflow logs successful transfers and can optionally send notifications (email, Slack, etc.) to confirm articles have been saved, providing visibility into the automation's operation.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automation running in under 30 minutes:

  1. Download the Template: Click the download button above to get the JSON workflow file.
  2. Import to n8n: In your n8n instance, go to Workflows → Import from File and select the downloaded JSON.
  3. Configure Credentials: Set up credentials for Tiny Tiny RSS and Wallabag in n8n's credentials management.
  4. Update Workflow Settings: Adjust the scheduler interval if needed (default is 10 minutes) and verify the API endpoint URLs match your services.
  5. Test the Connection: Star an article in Tiny Tiny RSS and manually trigger the workflow once to verify it appears in Wallabag.
  6. Activate the Workflow: Turn on the workflow and let it run automatically. Monitor the executions for the first few cycles to ensure everything works smoothly.
