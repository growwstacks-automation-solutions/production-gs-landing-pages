What This Workflow Does

Unexpectedly high energy bills create financial stress and make budget planning difficult. This automation solves that problem by giving you daily visibility into your PG&E energy consumption and costs before the monthly bill arrives.

The workflow automatically logs into your PG&E account using Airtop's browser automation, extracts your daily usage data, calculates the current costs based on your rate plan, and sends you a concise email summary. Instead of waiting 30 days to discover you've overspent, you get daily updates that empower immediate action.

This transforms energy management from a reactive monthly surprise to a proactive daily practice, helping households and businesses identify usage patterns, catch malfunctioning equipment early, and optimize consumption during peak pricing hours.

How It Works

1. Automated PG&E Login & Data Extraction

Airtop securely logs into your PG&E account using stored credentials, navigates to the energy usage dashboard, and extracts the latest consumption data. This bypasses the need for PG&E to provide a direct API, which they don't offer to most residential customers.

2. Cost Calculation & Processing

The workflow processes the raw usage data, applying your specific rate plan (including tiered pricing and time-of-use rates if applicable) to calculate accurate daily costs. It can also compare today's usage to previous days or similar periods.

3. Email Notification Generation

A formatted email is created with key metrics: daily kWh usage, estimated cost, comparison to previous day, and projected monthly total. The email is designed for quick scanning with clear highlights of any concerning spikes.

4. Scheduled Daily Execution

The entire process runs automatically each morning via n8n's scheduler, ensuring you start your day with fresh energy insights without any manual effort. Failed runs trigger error notifications so you never miss data silently.

What You'll Need

  1. Airtop account with API key for browser automation (free tier available)
  2. PG&E online account credentials for the property you want to monitor
  3. Email account to receive daily notifications (Gmail, Outlook, or any SMTP-compatible service)
  4. n8n instance (self-hosted or n8n.cloud account)
  5. Basic understanding of your electricity rate plan (tiered vs. time-of-use)

Quick Setup Guide

1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.

2. Configure credentials in the n8n credentials manager: Add your Airtop API key, PG&E login details, and email service credentials.

3. Adjust the schedule to run daily at your preferred time (default is 7 AM local time).

4. Test the workflow manually once to ensure all connections work and you receive the first email report.

5. Activate the workflow and let it run automatically. Review the first week's reports to establish your baseline consumption.

Pro tip: During initial setup, temporarily add a Google Sheets node to log historical data. After a month, you'll have valuable trend data that most utility portals don't provide easily.
