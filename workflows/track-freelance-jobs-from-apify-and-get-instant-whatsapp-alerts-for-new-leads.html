What This Workflow Does

For freelancers and agencies, finding new clients is a constant challenge that requires hours of manual browsing across multiple job platforms. This workflow solves that problem by automating the entire lead discovery process. It uses Apify to scrape job postings from platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, or Freelancer.com, processes the results to match your specific criteria, saves qualified opportunities to Google Sheets for tracking, and sends instant WhatsApp alerts directly to your phone.

The system ensures you're among the first to know about new opportunities that match your skills and budget requirements. Instead of checking job boards multiple times daily, you receive real-time notifications when perfect client leads appear, giving you a competitive advantage in responding quickly. The workflow also maintains a searchable database of all discovered opportunities, helping you track application rates and identify the most productive platforms for your niche.

The automation workflow connects Apify scraping with data processing and WhatsApp notification delivery.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to transform raw job data into actionable client leads delivered directly to you.

Step 1: Trigger & Job Scraping

The workflow begins either on a schedule (using a Cron trigger) or manually. It activates an Apify Actor configured to scrape your target job platform, extracting details like job title, description, budget, client rating, posting date, and required skills.

Step 2: Data Processing & Filtering

Raw job data passes through a JavaScript node where custom logic applies your filtering criteria. You can filter by minimum/maximum budget, specific keywords, client location, project duration, or any other parameters important to your business.

Step 3: Database Logging

Qualified opportunities are automatically appended to a Google Sheets spreadsheet. Each entry includes the job details, discovery timestamp, and a direct link to the posting. This creates a searchable lead database for tracking and analysis.

Step 4: Instant Notification

The workflow formats a concise job summary and sends it via WhatsApp Business API directly to your phone. The message includes the most critical details: job title, budget, client rating, and a direct link to apply immediately.

Pro tip: Configure your filters to match not just skills but also your ideal client profile. Include minimum budget thresholds, preferred project durations, and client rating requirements to ensure you only receive alerts for high-quality, well-paying opportunities.

What You'll Need

  1. Apify Account: Free tier available. You'll need an API token and may need to configure or select an appropriate Actor for your target job platform.
  2. Google Sheets: A Google account with a prepared spreadsheet for logging discovered opportunities. The workflow will need edit permissions.
  3. WhatsApp Business API Access: Either through Twilio, WhatsApp's own Business API, or a compatible service provider. You'll need API credentials and a verified phone number.
  4. n8n Instance: Either n8n.cloud (free tier available) or a self-hosted n8n installation where you can import and run the workflow.
  5. Target Job Platforms: Clear understanding of which freelance platforms you want to monitor (Upwork, Fiverr, Freelancer.com, etc.) and their specific URL structures or search parameters.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file above and import it into your n8n instance via the workflow import function.
  2. Configure Apify Credentials: Add your Apify API token in n8n credentials and select or configure the appropriate Actor for your target job platform.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: Authenticate with your Google account and specify the spreadsheet ID and worksheet where jobs should be logged.
  4. Set Up WhatsApp: Add your WhatsApp Business API credentials (from Twilio or another provider) and specify the destination phone number for alerts.
  5. Customize Filters: Modify the JavaScript node to include your specific filtering criteria—budget ranges, keyword matches, client requirements, etc.
  6. Test & Schedule: Run the workflow once manually to verify all connections work, then set the schedule trigger to run hourly or at your preferred frequency.

Pro tip: Start with broader filters and gradually refine them based on the quality of alerts you receive. Monitor which types of jobs actually convert to clients and adjust your criteria accordingly over the first few weeks.
