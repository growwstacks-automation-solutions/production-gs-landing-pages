Track Investor Data from Crunchbase to Google Sheets

Automate investor research and market analysis. Pull funding history, portfolio companies, and investment patterns directly into spreadsheets for strategic decision-making.

What This Workflow Does

Manual investor research is one of the most time-consuming tasks for founders, business development teams, and market analysts. Spending hours copying data from Crunchbase into spreadsheets is inefficient, error-prone, and quickly becomes outdated. This automation solves that problem by creating a live pipeline between Crunchbase's comprehensive database and your Google Sheets.

The workflow automatically collects and structures investor data including funding history, portfolio companies, investment focus areas, and contact information. It transforms raw API data into actionable insights, enabling you to track market trends, identify potential investors, and monitor competitive landscapes without manual data entry.

How It Works

Step 1: Data Extraction from Crunchbase

The workflow connects to Crunchbase's API using secure authentication. You can configure it to search for specific investors, VC firms, or track companies in particular sectors. The system pulls structured data including investment rounds, participant details, and portfolio updates.

Step 2: Data Transformation & Enrichment

Raw API data is processed to extract the most valuable insights. The workflow calculates metrics like investment frequency, average check size, and sector concentration. It can also enrich data with additional context, such as identifying lead vs. follow-on investors in funding rounds.

Step 3: Google Sheets Integration

Processed data is automatically written to designated Google Sheets. The workflow can create new rows for new investments, update existing records, or append to historical datasets. You can structure sheets by investor type, industry vertical, or timeframe based on your analysis needs.

Step 4: Scheduling & Automation

Set the workflow to run on a schedule—daily, weekly, or monthly—ensuring your investor database is always current. You can configure alerts for specific events, like when a target investor participates in a new funding round in your sector.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for startup founders preparing for fundraising, business development professionals tracking partnership opportunities, market research analysts monitoring industry trends, investment professionals conducting due diligence, and corporate strategy teams analyzing competitive landscapes. If you need to make data-driven decisions about investors or market opportunities, this workflow eliminates the manual research bottleneck.

What You'll Need

  1. Crunchbase API access: A Crunchbase account with API credentials (various tiers available).
  2. Google Sheets: A Google account with Sheets access (free with Google Workspace).
  3. n8n instance: Self-hosted n8n or n8n.cloud account.
  4. Basic spreadsheet structure: A Google Sheet with columns for the data points you want to track.
  5. Clear research objectives: Knowing what investor criteria or market signals matter most to your business.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template JSON file using the button above.
  2. Import it into your n8n instance (Workflows → Import from File).
  3. Configure the Crunchbase node with your API credentials.
  4. Set up the Google Sheets node with your spreadsheet ID and sheet name.
  5. Adjust the search parameters to match your target investors or companies.
  6. Test the workflow with a single investor search to verify data flows correctly.
  7. Set the schedule trigger to your preferred frequency (daily recommended).
  8. Activate the workflow and monitor the first few runs for any adjustments.

Pro tip: Start with a focused list of 10-20 target investors rather than broad market searches. This ensures manageable data volume and lets you refine your criteria before scaling up. Create separate sheets for different investor categories (Seed, Series A, Corporate VC) for cleaner analysis.
