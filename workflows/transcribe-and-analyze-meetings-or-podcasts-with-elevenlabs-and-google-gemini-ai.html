What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming process of manually transcribing meetings, interviews, or podcast episodes and extracting actionable insights. It combines ElevenLabs' industry-leading speech-to-text technology with Google Gemini's powerful AI analysis capabilities to create a seamless pipeline from audio to intelligence.

The workflow automatically processes audio/video files, generates accurate transcripts, saves them to Google Docs for easy sharing, and performs sophisticated analysis to highlight key points, action items, and decisions. This eliminates hours of manual work while creating searchable, organized records of every important conversation.

How It Works

1. Audio File Processing

The workflow begins when a new audio or video file is added to your designated storage location. It supports common formats like MP3, WAV, and MP4 from meeting recordings, podcasts, or interview sessions.

2. ElevenLabs Transcription

The file is sent to ElevenLabs' Speech-to-Text API which converts the audio into accurate text transcriptions. The system handles multiple speakers and can be configured to include timestamps or speaker identification.

3. Google Docs Storage

The raw transcript is automatically saved to a Google Doc in your designated folder, creating a permanent, searchable record. The document is formatted for readability with proper headings and spacing.

4. Gemini AI Analysis

Google Gemini processes the transcript to extract key insights. This can include meeting summaries, action items, decisions made, sentiment analysis, and topic clustering based on your specific requirements.

5. Output Delivery

The final analyzed output is delivered to your preferred destination - this could be as a summarized email, Slack notification, or appended to the Google Doc alongside the full transcript.

  1. Download the JSON template file

Common questions about meeting transcription and AI analysis

AI transcription services like ElevenLabs now achieve 90-95% accuracy for clear audio, rivaling human transcription for most business needs. The key advantage is speed - AI can process hours of audio in minutes versus hours for humans.

For technical terminology or accented speakers, some manual review may still be needed, but for routine meetings and podcasts, AI transcription delivers excellent results at a fraction of the cost.

  • AI excels at consistent quality regardless of volume
  • Human transcribers still better with heavy accents or poor audio
  • Combining AI with light human review offers best balance

Modern AI like Google Gemini can extract action items, summarize key points, identify decisions made, and even analyze sentiment or engagement levels. Advanced analysis includes detecting speaker patterns, highlighting controversial topics, and extracting follow-up tasks.

Some systems can create structured meeting minutes with categorized discussion points, making information retrieval much easier than scanning raw transcripts. This transforms passive documentation into active intelligence.

  • Sentiment analysis reveals unspoken tensions
  • Topic tracking shows recurring discussion themes
  • Decision logging creates accountability trails

Leading services like ElevenLabs and Google offer enterprise-grade security with options for on-premise processing, data encryption, and strict retention policies. For highly sensitive content, look for providers with SOC 2 compliance and the ability to process audio without storing it permanently.

Always review the provider's data handling policies and consider redacting sensitive portions before processing if needed. Many legal and healthcare organizations successfully use AI transcription while maintaining compliance through proper vendor selection and configuration.

AI transcription typically costs 80-90% less than human services. Professional transcription averages $1-3 per minute, while AI services charge $0.10-$0.30 per minute for comparable accuracy.

The savings multiply when you factor in faster turnaround times (minutes vs. days) and the ability to process multiple meetings simultaneously without scaling human labor. For organizations with regular transcription needs, the annual savings often reach tens of thousands of dollars.

Modern AI systems can identify and separate up to 10 distinct speakers with good accuracy, though overlapping speech remains challenging. The best results come from clear audio recordings with minimal background noise.

Some services allow you to 'train' the system by providing speaker names or sample audio to improve identification accuracy over time. For highly interactive meetings, consider using tools that create individual audio tracks for each participant.
