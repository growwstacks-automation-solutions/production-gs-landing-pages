What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming problem of manually editing long videos into short, engaging clips for social media. Content creators, marketers, and businesses often spend 10-20 hours per week cutting, editing, and scheduling content—a process that's both repetitive and inconsistent.

The workflow automatically processes any long video (webinars, podcasts, interviews, tutorials) and extracts 3-6 viral-ready short clips using AI. It intelligently selects the most engaging segments based on transcript analysis, generates platform-optimized titles and descriptions, and schedules the clips for consecutive daily posting across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

Unlike manual editing, this system ensures consistent quality, precise word-level cuts to avoid awkward mid-sentence breaks, and maintains a reliable posting schedule that social media algorithms reward with increased reach and engagement.

How It Works

1. Video Submission & Audio Extraction

The workflow begins when you submit your long video. It automatically extracts the audio track using FFmpeg processing, preparing it for accurate speech recognition while preserving the original video quality for later editing.

2. AI-Powered Transcription & Analysis

OpenAI's Whisper transcribes the audio with word-level timestamps, creating a precise map of the spoken content. Google's Gemini then analyzes this transcript to identify 3-6 high-potential segments (15-60 seconds each) based on engagement signals like hooks, questions, and value statements.

3. Intelligent Clip Creation

Using the identified timestamps, the workflow executes precise FFmpeg cuts at absolute-second boundaries to avoid mid-word edits. It intelligently handles both vertical and horizontal source videos, applying smart cropping and padding to create platform-optimized 9:16 outputs without degrading resolution.

4. Metadata Generation & Scheduling

For each clip, AI generates platform-specific titles, descriptions, and hashtags. The workflow then schedules posts for consecutive days (3 clips = next 3 days, 6 clips = next 6 days) at optimal posting times, ensuring consistent content flow that algorithms favor.
