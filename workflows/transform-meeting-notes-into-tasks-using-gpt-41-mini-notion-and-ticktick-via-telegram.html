What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of disorganized meeting notes that never turn into actionable tasks. Teams waste hours manually transcribing notes, identifying action items, and assigning tasks across different platforms. The workflow captures Telegram meeting messages, processes them with GPT-4.1-mini to extract structured tasks, then creates corresponding items in Notion (for documentation) and TickTick (for execution).

By combining AI processing with popular productivity tools, this system eliminates the gap between discussion and action. The AI understands context, identifies owners from mentions, and can even suggest deadlines based on phrases like "by next week" or "urgent". What typically takes 30+ minutes of manual work per meeting happens automatically in seconds.

AI processing transforms raw Telegram messages into structured tasks

How It Works

1. Telegram message capture

The workflow monitors specified Telegram channels or group chats for new messages containing meeting notes. It can be triggered by a specific hashtag (#meetingnotes) or run automatically after scheduled meetings.

2. AI processing with GPT-4.1-mini

The raw text gets sent to GPT-4.1-mini which analyzes the content to: identify action items, extract task descriptions, determine priorities (from words like "urgent"), recognize assignees (from @mentions), and estimate deadlines (from time references).

Pro tip: Include clear action verbs in your notes ("decide on", "create draft", "review proposal") to improve AI accuracy.

3. Task creation in Notion

The processed tasks get added to a Notion database with standardized properties: task name, description, assignee, due date, priority, and meeting reference. Notion serves as the permanent record of meeting outcomes.

4. Sync to TickTick

Action items simultaneously appear in TickTick for immediate execution. The integration maintains two-way sync so task completion in TickTick updates status in Notion, keeping both systems aligned.
