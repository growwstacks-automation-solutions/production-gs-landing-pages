What This Workflow Does

Creating high-quality video content from images traditionally requires manual editing, multiple software tools, and significant time investment. This workflow completely automates that process, transforming a single image URL into a cinematic, multi-shot professional video ready for publishing.

The automation runs in two coordinated phases. First, it generates an editorial contact sheet from your source image using advanced AI. Then, it creates multiple video segments, merges them into a final video, and publishes it to your chosen platform—all without any human intervention.

This solution is particularly valuable for businesses that need to scale their video content production but lack dedicated video editing resources. It turns what was once a multi-hour creative task into a fully automated, spreadsheet-driven process.

How It Works

Phase 1: Contact Sheet Generation

The workflow begins by reading an image URL from your Google Sheets document. It downloads the image, uploads it to a temporary hosting service, then uses NanoBanana Ultra's edit-ultra capability to create a 2×3 cinematic contact sheet. This ensures identity preservation, fashion/texture fidelity, and consistent lighting across all generated frames.

Phase 2: Video Creation & Publishing

The contact sheet is split into six individual keyframes. Each frame is processed through Kling AI to generate cinematic video segments with specific camera movements and pacing. These segments are then merged using FFmpeg into a single cohesive video, uploaded to Google Drive, and automatically published to YouTube via Blotato.

Status Tracking & Management

Throughout the process, the workflow updates your Google Sheets with real-time status indicators—tracking which images have been processed, which videos are being generated, and providing final video URLs once publishing is complete.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the automation
  2. Google Sheets document with your image URLs and tracking columns
  3. API keys for AtlasCloud (NanoBanana + Kling), tmpfiles.org, and Blotato
  4. Connected accounts for Google Drive, Google Sheets, and Blotato publishing
  5. Basic understanding of workflow automation concepts

Pro tip: Start with a small batch of test images to verify your API configurations work correctly before scaling to production volumes. This helps identify any authentication or quota issues early.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure your API credentials in the respective nodes for AtlasCloud, tmpfiles.org, and Blotato.
  3. Connect your Google account to the Google Sheets and Google Drive nodes.
  4. Prepare your spreadsheet with the required columns: image URL, status, and final video URL.
  5. Test with one image to verify the entire pipeline works from sheet to published video.
  6. Schedule the workflow to run automatically or trigger it manually for batch processing.
