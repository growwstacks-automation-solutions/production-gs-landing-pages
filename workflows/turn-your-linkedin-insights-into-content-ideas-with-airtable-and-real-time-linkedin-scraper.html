What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common challenge of losing track of valuable content inspiration from LinkedIn. Many professionals engage with posts but struggle to systematically capture and organize these insights for their own content creation.

The workflow automatically collects your LinkedIn post reactions, filters them by recency and engagement, then structures the data into Airtable where you can easily categorize, prioritize, and develop content ideas. It eliminates manual note-taking while building a searchable repository of proven content inspiration.

How It Works

Step 1: Capture LinkedIn Activity

The workflow connects to LinkedIn's API through RapidAPI to fetch your recent post interactions, including likes, comments, and shares.

Step 2: Filter Relevant Content

It applies date filters to focus on recent activity and excludes low-engagement posts, ensuring only quality content enters your database.

Step 3: Structure the Data

The workflow extracts key elements like post text, author information, and engagement metrics, formatting them for optimal organization in Airtable.

Step 4: Populate Airtable

Finally, the structured data flows into your Airtable base where you can tag, categorize, and develop content ideas with your team.

What You'll Need

  1. A LinkedIn account with engagement history
  2. RapidAPI account with access to LinkedIn API
  3. Airtable account with a prepared base
  4. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure the LinkedIn scraper node with your API credentials
  3. Connect the Airtable node to your base
  4. Set your preferred schedule (weekly recommended)
  5. Activate the workflow

Pro tip: Add a second workflow that notifies your team when high-value content is added to Airtable for immediate ideation.
