What This Workflow Does

Content creators and marketers spend hours manually reading YouTube comments to find inspiration. This process is slow, inconsistent, and often misses hidden patterns in audience feedback. This n8n automation solves that by turning raw YouTube comments into research-backed content ideas complete with hooks and outlines—automatically.

The workflow scrapes comments from any YouTube video, uses GPT-4.1-mini to filter which comments represent genuine content opportunities, enriches promising ideas with fresh web research via Tavily, and generates compelling hooks and structured outlines. Everything is organized in Google Sheets for easy review and execution.

Instead of guessing what your audience wants, you get data-driven content suggestions that address real questions and pain points expressed in your comments section. This transforms passive audience feedback into an active content pipeline.

How It Works

This workflow follows a logical six-step process that mimics how a strategic content team would analyze comments, but at scale and speed.

1. Trigger & Input

You paste a YouTube URL into the workflow's chat trigger. This could be your own video, a competitor's video, or any content in your niche. The workflow accepts the URL and prepares to extract all available comments and metadata.

2. Comment Scraping with Apify

The Apify YouTube Comments Scraper node fetches all comments, including the comment text, author name, like count, and timestamp. This gives you the raw material to analyze—often hundreds or thousands of data points that would be impossible to review manually.

3. AI-Powered Filtering

Each comment passes through a GPT-4.1-mini node that evaluates whether it represents a viable content idea. The AI looks for specific patterns: questions seeking answers, problems needing solutions, requests for tutorials, or expressions of confusion. Comments marked "Yes" proceed; "No" comments are logged but not developed further.

4. Initial Storage in Google Sheets

All comments—both accepted and rejected—are appended to a Google Sheet. This creates an audit trail and raw dataset. The sheet tracks the comment ID, text, author, likes, and the AI's initial "Is Idea?" verdict.

5. Research & Enrichment

For comments flagged as potential ideas, the workflow triggers two parallel actions. First, Tavily performs a web search to gather current information, statistics, and context around the topic. Second, GPT-4.1-mini analyzes the comment and research to generate a specific topic title, an engaging hook sentence, and a structured content outline.

6. Final Output & Organization

The enriched data—topic, research summary, hook, and outline—is written back to the same row in Google Sheets. You now have a prioritized list of content ideas, each backed by audience demand and current research, ready for your content calendar.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get this automation running in under 20 minutes:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure credentials for Apify, OpenAI, Tavily, and Google Sheets in n8n's credential management.
  3. Prepare your Google Sheet with these exact column headers in order: id, text, author, likes, isIdea, topic, research, hook, outline.
  4. Update the Google Sheets nodes to point to your sheet's ID and tab name.
  5. Test with a single video URL using the chat trigger to verify all connections work.
  6. Schedule the workflow to run weekly on your channel's latest videos for continuous idea generation.
