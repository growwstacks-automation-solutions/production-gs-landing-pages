What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical problem for businesses using HubSpot: maintaining clean contact data. When new contacts enter your CRM—whether from forms, imports, or integrations—their email addresses may be invalid, mistyped, or disposable. Invalid emails lead to bounced campaigns, wasted marketing spend, and inaccurate sales outreach.

The workflow automatically validates every new HubSpot contact in real-time using OneSimpleAPI's email verification service. It checks email syntax, domain validity, and mailbox existence before the contact becomes part of your marketing and sales processes. Invalid contacts are flagged immediately, allowing your team to take corrective action or exclude them from campaigns.

Beyond simple validation, this automation creates a systematic approach to data hygiene that scales with your business. It eliminates manual verification tasks that typically consume 5-10 hours per week for marketing operations teams, while improving email deliverability rates by 20-40%.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence that ensures comprehensive validation without disrupting your existing HubSpot workflows.

Step 1: HubSpot Trigger Detection

The workflow monitors your HubSpot instance for new contact creation events. Using HubSpot's webhook or API trigger, it instantly captures each new contact addition, whether from forms, manual entry, or system integrations.

Step 2: Email Extraction and Preparation

For each new contact, the workflow extracts the email address field and prepares it for validation. It handles various email formats and can process multiple email fields if your HubSpot setup includes secondary email addresses.

Step 3: OneSimpleAPI Validation

The extracted email is sent to OneSimpleAPI for comprehensive validation. The service performs multiple checks: syntax validation, domain existence verification, MX record checking, and mailbox existence testing. It also identifies disposable emails and role-based addresses.

Step 4: Result Processing and Decision Logic

Based on the validation results, the workflow applies conditional logic. Valid emails proceed normally through your HubSpot workflows. Invalid emails trigger specific actions: they can be tagged, moved to a separate list, or trigger notifications to your team.

Step 5: Notification and Reporting

The workflow sends real-time notifications about validation outcomes to your preferred communication channels. It can update HubSpot contact properties with validation status, create tasks for sales follow-up on questionable emails, and generate validation reports for data quality monitoring.

Businesses should implement three types of email validation: syntax checking, domain verification, and mailbox existence testing. Syntax checking ensures proper email format, domain verification confirms the domain exists and accepts mail, and mailbox existence checks if the specific address is active.

  • Domain verification prevents fake domain entries
  • Mailbox existence testing confirms active addresses
