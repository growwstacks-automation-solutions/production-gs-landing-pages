What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the critical problem of email bounce rates that plague sales and marketing teams. When you're reaching out to prospects, invalid email addresses waste your time, damage your sender reputation, and reduce campaign effectiveness. This workflow automatically verifies email addresses using Hunter's powerful verification API before they enter your outreach sequences.

The template connects to your lead sources—whether that's CSV imports, form submissions, or CRM entries—and runs each email through multiple validation checks. It identifies valid addresses ready for outreach, flags risky ones for manual review, and removes invalid addresses that would otherwise bounce. This ensures your sales team only spends time on prospects who can actually receive your messages.

Beyond simple validation, this workflow provides deliverability confidence scores, identifies disposable email addresses, and can integrate with your existing sales stack to automatically update lead records. It transforms email list management from a manual, error-prone process into an automated quality control system.

How It Works

Step 1: Input Email Collection

The workflow begins by collecting email addresses from your chosen source. This could be triggered by new form submissions on your website, CSV file uploads, scheduled list imports from your CRM, or real-time API calls from your sales tools.

Step 2: Hunter API Verification

Each email address is sent to Hunter's verification API which performs multiple technical checks: syntax validation, domain existence verification, SMTP server testing, and mailbox confirmation. The API returns a detailed result including validity status, risk score, and reason for any failures.

Step 3: Result Processing & Categorization

The workflow analyzes the verification results and categorizes emails into different groups: valid (ready for outreach), risky (needs review), invalid (should be removed), and unknown (requires manual checking). This segmentation allows for different handling strategies based on deliverability confidence.

Step 4: Integration & Action

Based on the categorization, the workflow takes appropriate actions. Valid emails can be added to your CRM or email marketing platform. Invalid addresses are removed from your lists. Risky emails can trigger notifications for manual review. The system can also update custom fields in your sales tools with verification status and confidence scores.

Pro tip: Schedule this workflow to run weekly on your entire contact database. Email deliverability changes over time as people change jobs or companies update their email systems. Regular verification maintains list quality and protects your sender reputation.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and save the JSON file to your computer.
  2. Import into n8n: In your n8n instance, go to Workflows → Import from file and select the downloaded JSON.
  3. Configure Hunter connection: Click the Hunter node, add your API key from your Hunter account, and test the connection.
  4. Set up your email source: Configure the trigger node (HTTP, Schedule, or app-specific) to match how you'll feed emails into the workflow.
  5. Configure destination actions: Set up the nodes that handle verified emails—whether that's updating CRM records, adding to email lists, or saving to databases.
  6. Test with sample data: Run the workflow manually with 5-10 test emails to ensure everything works correctly.
  7. Activate and schedule: Turn on the workflow and set appropriate scheduling based on your email volume needs.
