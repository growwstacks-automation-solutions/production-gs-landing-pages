What This Workflow Does

This n8n automation solves the costly problem of undeliverable mail by automatically verifying the deliverability of new contact addresses in Groundhogg using Lob's address verification API. When new contacts are added to your Groundhogg CRM, the workflow checks each address against USPS databases to confirm it's deliverable, standardized, and properly formatted.

The system flags undeliverable addresses immediately, allowing your team to follow up with contacts for corrected information before wasting resources on mail that won't reach its destination. Verified addresses are updated in Groundhogg with standardized formatting that improves deliverability rates for all future communications.

How It Works

1. New contact trigger

The workflow activates whenever a new contact is added to your Groundhogg CRM. It extracts the mailing address fields and prepares them for verification.

2. Address verification with Lob

The contact's address is sent to Lob's verification API, which checks it against authoritative postal databases. Lob returns a deliverability status, standardized address format, and confidence score.

3. Deliverability check

The workflow evaluates Lob's response to determine if the address is deliverable. Undeliverable addresses trigger an immediate notification to your team.

4. CRM record update

For verified addresses, the workflow updates the Groundhogg contact record with the standardized address format and adds a "Verified Address" tag for segmentation.

Pro tip: Combine this with a direct mail automation to only send physical mail to verified addresses, reducing wasted postage costs by 15-30%.
