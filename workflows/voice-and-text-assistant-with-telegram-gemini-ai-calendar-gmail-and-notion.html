What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of fragmented productivity. Instead of manually switching between your calendar, email, and notes app, you can simply tell your Telegram bot what you need. The workflow listens to your voice notes or reads your text messages, uses Google Gemini AI to understand your intent, and then performs the corresponding action in Google Calendar, Gmail, or Notion.

Imagine saying, "Schedule a meeting with Alex next Tuesday at 3 PM" while driving, and having the event appear in your calendar. Or whispering, "Email the team the project update" and having a draft composed and sent. This template bridges the gap between conversational input and concrete digital action, saving you significant time and mental energy each day.

The automation follows a logical, step-by-step process to transform your request into action.

Step 1: Trigger via Telegram

The workflow is activated when you send a message or voice note to your dedicated Telegram bot. The Telegram Trigger node captures this input.

Step 2: Authentication & Voice Processing

Step 1: Trigger via Telegram

Everything starts when you send a message or voice note to your dedicated Telegram bot. The workflow is activated instantly.

Step 2: Authentication & Audio Processing

The system first verifies you're an authorized user. If you sent a voice note, it automatically retrieves the audio file and transcribes it into text using a speech-to-text service.

Step 3: AI Intent Recognition

The transcribed text (or your original text message) is sent to the Google Gemini AI Agent. This agent, equipped with memory for context, analyzes your request to determine what you want to do (e.g., "create calendar event," "send email," "make a note").

Step 4: Smart Action Execution

Based on the AI's understanding, the workflow branches out to perform the task in the correct app. It can create or read Google Calendar events, draft and send emails via Gmail, or create new pages/notes in your Notion workspace.

Step 5: Confirmation & Reply

Finally, the Telegram bot sends you a message back confirming the action was completed or providing the information you requested, closing the loop.

Quick Setup Guide

Get your personal AI assistant up and running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above to get the JSON file. In your n8n instance, go to Workflows > Import from File and select the downloaded JSON.
  2. Configure Telegram Trigger: In the "Telegram Trigger" node, replace the placeholder credentials with your actual Bot Token and Chat ID.
  3. Connect Your Accounts: For each of the green nodes (Google Gemini, Google Calendar, Gmail, Notion), click the node and use n8n's OAuth or credential setup to connect your actual accounts.
  4. Set Up AI Agent Memory (Optional): The "Window Buffer Memory" node stores conversation context. Adjust the buffer size if you want the AI to remember more of your previous messages.
  5. Test & Activate: Turn the workflow ON in n8n. Send a simple text message like "Hello" to your Telegram bot. You should receive a reply. Then try a command like "Add a test meeting tomorrow at 2pm."
