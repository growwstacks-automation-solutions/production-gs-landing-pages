What This Workflow Does

Launching a new product or service? Building an engaged waitlist is crucial, but manual signup management is time-consuming and prone to fake emails. This automation solves that by creating a seamless, self-service waitlist with built-in email verification.

The workflow captures user information through a form, generates and sends a unique verification code via email, and only adds confirmed users to your Google Sheet. It keeps unverified users in a loop until they enter the correct code, ensuring your list contains only genuine, interested leads. All data—including verification status—is tracked automatically, giving you a clean, actionable database from day one.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical three-step process that mimics how a human would manage signups, but entirely automatically.

Step 1: Initial Signup Capture

A user submits their name, email, and other details through an embedded form. The workflow immediately records this entry in your Google Sheet with a "pending verification" status and generates a random 6-digit code.

Step 2: Email Verification Loop

The system sends the verification code to the user's email. They're then prompted to enter this code in a second form step. If the code matches, they proceed; if not, they remain in the verification loop until they provide the correct code or abandon the process.

Step 3: Data Storage & Confirmation

Once verified, the user's status updates to "confirmed" in your Google Sheet, and any additional information (like use case or company size) can be collected. The workflow can optionally trigger a welcome email or add the lead to your CRM.

Pro tip: Customize the verification email to match your brand voice and include a brief value proposition. This increases completion rates and starts building relationship equity immediately.

Quick Setup Guide

Get your verified waitlist running in under 30 minutes with these steps.

  1. Download the template using the button above and save the JSON file locally.
  2. Import into n8n by going to your n8n dashboard, clicking "Import from File," and selecting the downloaded JSON.
  3. Configure Google Sheets by updating the Google Sheets node with your spreadsheet ID and sheet name. Ensure your column headers match the template's expected structure.
  4. Set up email sending by configuring the "Send Email" node with your SMTP credentials or email service connection.
  5. Customize form text and email messages to match your brand and use case.
  6. Activate the workflow and embed the form URL on your website or share it directly with potential signups.

Pro tip: Test the entire flow yourself before going live. Submit the form with a personal email, complete verification, and check that data appears correctly in your Google Sheet. This catches configuration issues early.
